_(Council Member, Auralis — Expansion Faction)_

**Orla Fest** controls [[Auralis|Auralis's]] western shipping lanes through a network of family-held port interests that has been expanding for two generations. She is fifty-two, not ideological, and entirely predictable — which [[Tovan Ashce]] has noted in writing as her most useful quality from the perspective of anyone trying to model the council.

## Character

Orla is direct in the manner of commercial people who have spent their careers in environments where imprecision wastes time. She says what she wants, says why she wants it, and moves on. She does not dress positions in principle when they are interests and does not expect others to either, which has led to several memorable exchanges with [[Castor Helme]], whose performances she finds a waste of everyone's time.

Her support for expansion is entirely commercial. More territory under [[Auralis]] control means more ports, more trade routes, more negotiating leverage, and more opportunities for the Fest family network to extend its position. She has run this calculation explicitly and does not pretend it is about anything else. The Expansion Faction's more ideological members find this refreshing in theory and slightly uncomfortable in practice.

Her [[Flux]] ability is T2, used primarily for shipment verification and logistics sigil work — the standard commercial suite. She has never needed more than this and considers time spent on Flux development to be time not spent on the actual business.

## On the Council

Orla's position on specific votes is consistently predictable from her commercial interests, which makes her one of the few council members who can be modeled cleanly. [[Davan Rhyce]] accounts for her as a known variable. [[Tovan Ashce]] does the same from the other direction, which is why he has twice engineered situations where her commercial interests and an Expansion Faction vote were technically in conflict, producing abstentions at moments that suited him.

She is aware she has been outmaneuvered on both occasions. She has not adjusted her approach because her approach produces what she needs across the full range of her activity, and two lost votes in nine years on the council is an acceptable cost. She is not on the council to win every vote.
