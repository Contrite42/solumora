Sera Voss is the seventh-generation head of the [[The Voss Line|Voss line]] and the current leader of [[Wolfpoint]]. She is fifty-one years old, T5, and the first person in the Voss family's history to attempt to write down what they know in a form that someone outside the family could actually learn from.

This project is taking longer than she expected. She considers this appropriate.

## Character

Sera has the particular quality of someone who has spent decades becoming more precise rather than more certain. She knows more than she did at thirty and holds it more lightly, which is not the same as holding it loosely. She is direct without being blunt, patient without being passive, and has a specific kind of attentiveness in conversation that makes people feel they are being listened to at a level slightly deeper than they intended to speak.

She is not warm in the Terravellian social sense. She is genuinely interested in people, which is different and sometimes more unsettling.

Her T5 capacity is the highest the Voss line has produced in three generations. She does not display it. Within Wolfpoint this is understood as correct. Outside Wolfpoint, on the rare occasions she travels, it occasionally produces the particular miscalculation people make when they meet someone capable who doesn't perform capability.

## The Document

The project Sera has been working on for eleven years is a transmissible [[Hypertext]] record — a structured account of the Voss family's accumulated knowledge written in a form that can be copied, distributed, and learned without direct instruction from a Voss family member. Every previous generation transmitted [[Hypertext]] knowledge through apprenticeship and oral tradition. What Sera is building would make that unnecessary.

The technical challenge is significant. Hypertext is not a fixed system — it is a living practice, and capturing it in static form without losing the judgment that makes it work is the problem she has been solving, slowly, for over a decade. The document exists in multiple drafts. The most recent is the most complete. It is not finished.

She has thought carefully about what finishing it means. A completed transmissible Hypertext record is an object that [[Auralis]] would want, that [[Terravelle]] would want, that independent scholars cycling through Wolfpoint have been asking about for years. [[Mira Solv]], who handles [[The Council of Auralis|the council's]] correspondence with Wolfpoint on [[Drest]]'s behalf, has been more persistent than most. Sera has replied to every letter politely and without commitment.

What she has not told anyone is that the document's implications extend further than Hypertext. Examining the [[Watts|Watts ledger's]] underlying Flux architecture through the structural lens that seven generations of Hypertext work gave her, she has concluded that the ledger was built — deliberately, by someone who understood Flux encoding at a level that makes the current Scholar system look like an early draft. The encoding resembles Hypertext the way a master's completed work resembles a student's first attempt at the same problem. The ancient civilization that built the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] also built the ledger. Both kingdoms have been running on their infrastructure for centuries without knowing it.

She does not know what to do with this. She is finishing the document anyway. The decision of what it means can wait until the work is done.

## On Leadership

Sera inherited the Wolfpoint leadership at thirty-four when her father — the sixth Voss — died of a long illness that he worked through until two weeks before the end. The transition was uncontested. She had been functionally running much of Wolfpoint's day-to-day operation for three years before that and the family's assessment of her capability had been settled for longer.

She does not find leadership difficult in the way that people who were not prepared for it find it difficult. She finds it requiring in the way that anything requiring your full attention for decades is requiring. The difference matters to her.

Her two children — a daughter of twenty-three and a son of nineteen — are both developing Hypertext capability. Neither has been told they are being assessed for succession. Both are aware they are being assessed for succession. Sera considers this a reasonable foundation.

## Relationship with the Outside World

Sera's direct contact with either kingdom is limited and deliberate. She corresponds. She receives visitors. She does not travel to [[Solhaven]] or [[Valdenmoor]] and has declined every formal invitation to do so, politely and without explanation, for seventeen years.

What she knows about both kingdoms comes through the traders and scholars who pass through Wolfpoint, through the Voss family's own correspondence networks, and through the particular quality of attention she brings to everything she reads. She has a clearer picture of the current political situation between [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] than either kingdom's leadership would be comfortable knowing she has.

She has no intention of intervening in it. Wolfpoint's survival across seven generations has depended on being genuinely useful and genuinely independent, and she does not intend to trade that for involvement in a conflict that will resolve itself one way or another without her help.

The document, when it is finished, may change this calculation. She is aware of that. She is finishing it anyway.
