**Timekeeping Technology** describes the mechanical, astronomical, and [[Flux]]-based instruments used across [[Solumora]] to measure time with varying degrees of precision, from crude shadow estimation to expensive mechanical watches. Access to accurate timekeeping is deeply stratified by class and wealth — missing an appointment because "morning" is imprecise has different consequences for a laborer versus a merchant negotiating a 10,000 [[Watts]] trade contract. This inequality is not melodramatic injustice but practical reality: precise time costs money, and those without money make do with approximations that work adequately for most purposes but create disadvantages in contexts where exact timing matters.

## Astronomical Timekeeping

### Sundials

**Function:** Cast shadow indicates time by sun position.

**Cost:** 5-15 Watts (simple fixed sundial), 25-50 Watts (portable folding sundial with latitude adjustment)

**Accuracy:** ±15-30 minutes precision under clear skies.

**Availability:** Extremely common. Urban settlements have public sundials in market squares, guild halls, temple courtyards. Households with outdoor space often own simple sundials. Travelers carry portable versions.

**Limitations:**
- Useless during cloudy weather, rain, or nighttime (roughly 40% of hours unavailable [[Terravelle]] climate)
- Requires calibration for latitude (portable sundials have adjustment mechanisms, fixed sundials specific to installation location)
- Less accurate near dawn/dusk (shadow angles extreme, measurements imprecise)

**Social Stratification:**
- **Working-class:** Rely on public sundials when available, shadow estimation otherwise
- **Middle-class:** Households own fixed sundials, merchants carry portable versions for travel
- **Upper-class:** Sundials backup to mechanical clocks, not primary timekeeping method

### Lunar Phase Tracking

**Function:** 30-day lunar cycle visible across [[Solumora]] (single large moon with reliable period).

**Cost:** Free (observation requires no tools)

**Accuracy:** Month boundaries precise (new moon reliably marks month start). Day-within-month estimates accurate ±1-2 days for experienced observers.

**Availability:** Universal. Anyone can observe moon phase, though cloud cover interferes.

**Practical Use:**
- Agricultural planning ([[Maren Valley]] farmers plant specific days relative to moon phase)
- Maritime navigation (tidal prediction requires lunar tracking)
- Religious observances ([[Ascendant Path]] ceremonies aligned with lunar events)
- Commercial contract timing ("payment due new moon Harvestfall")

**Institutional Support:**
- [[Ascendant Path]] temples maintain formal lunar calendars, announce month boundaries
- [[Trade Guilds]] distribute printed lunar calendars annually (middle-class households purchase for 3-5 Watts)
- Rural communities maintain informal consensus (community elder or religious figure announces month change)

### Star Position Navigation

**Function:** Specific star patterns rise/set at predictable times, enabling time estimation at night.

**Cost:** Free (observation requires no tools), though knowledge/training required

**Accuracy:** ±30-60 minutes for trained navigators, worse for casual observers

**Availability:** Limited by knowledge and clear skies. [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] sailors, [[Desert Zakros]] expedition guides, rural travelers use star navigation routinely. Urban populations rarely learn detailed star positions (less need).

**Social Stratification:**
- **Maritime workers, expedition guides, rural travelers:** Essential skill, learned through apprenticeship
- **Urban populations:** Minimal knowledge ("that's the north star, that's the belt cluster")
- **Upper-class:** Educated individuals may learn astronomical observation as intellectual hobby (not practical necessity)

## Mechanical Timekeeping

### Water Clocks

**Function:** Controlled water drip marks hours through calibrated container filling or emptying.

**Cost:** 150-300 Watts (quality installation), 50-100 Watts (crude homemade version)

**Accuracy:** ±10-15 minutes per hour under ideal conditions (accuracy degrades with temperature changes affecting water flow)

**Maintenance:** Requires daily refilling, periodic cleaning (mineral deposits clog mechanisms), and recalibration against astronomical observations.

**Availability:**
- **Institutional:** Common in [[Ascendant Path]] temples, [[Trade Guilds]] halls, government administrative offices, wealthy merchant headquarters
- **Residential:** Rare. Only upper-class households (cost not just purchase but ongoing maintenance burden)
- **Public:** Some settlements have public water clocks (temple courtyards, market squares, visible to passersby)

**Practical Use:**
- Coordinating meetings ("meet at third hour past noon" when both parties can reference same water clock)
- Timing [[Flux Medicine]] treatments (precise dosing intervals)
- [[Law and Order#Court Proceedings|Legal proceedings]] (trial duration tracking, witness time limits)
- Guild work shifts (precisely timed rotations reduce disputes)

**Social Stratification:**
- **Working-class:** Interact with public water clocks when available, cannot afford private ownership
- **Middle-class:** Guild members benefit from institutional water clocks (coordination within guild)
- **Upper-class:** Private household water clocks common (status symbol and practical tool for managing complex schedules)

### Mechanical Clocks

**Function:** Spring-driven or weight-driven gears move clock hands, marking hours and sometimes minutes.

**Cost:** 800-2000 Watts (quality clock requiring skilled clockmaker)

**Accuracy:** ±5-10 minutes per day (requires weekly adjustment against astronomical observations)

**Maintenance:** Requires expert clockmaker visits (several times per year), lubrication, gear inspection, spring replacement. Maintenance costs 50-100 Watts annually.

**Availability:** Extremely rare. Urban elite, major institutions, government offices only.

**Notable Examples:**
- **[[Solhaven]] [[Crestward District]] Public Clock:** Visible tower clock with audible bell chimes each hour. Funded by [[The Council of Auralis]], maintained by contracted clockmaker (annual maintenance 150-200 Watts). Becomes informal time reference for upper terrace residents and merchants.
- **[[Valdenmoor]] Guild Council Chamber Clock:** Weight-driven clock in main guild hall. Symbolizes [[Trade Guilds]] institutional precision and control. Bell chimes during official guild meetings.
- **Major Temple Clocks:** Largest [[Ascendant Path]] temples ([[Solhaven]], main cities) have mechanical clocks supplementing water clocks. Used for coordinating multi-hour rituals, precise ceremony timing.

**Social Stratification:**
- **Working-class:** Never directly interact with mechanical clocks (too expensive, no access)
- **Middle-class:** Benefit indirectly from public clocks (hear bells, adjust personal schedules accordingly)
- **Upper-class:** Wealthy households and institutions own private mechanical clocks (major status symbol, indicates precision-demanding lifestyle)

### Personal Watches

**Function:** Miniature mechanical timepiece worn on chain or in pocket. Portable precision time reference.

**Cost:** 2000-5000 Watts (extreme luxury item)

**Accuracy:** ±10-20 minutes per day (worse than large clocks due to miniaturization compromises). Requires daily adjustment against reliable time source.

**Maintenance:** Fragile mechanisms require careful handling. Expert repair needed 1-2 times yearly (costs 100-200 Watts per repair). Watches fail frequently, ownership implies access to ongoing repair services.

**Availability:** Vanishingly rare. Only wealthiest merchants, high-tier [[Flux Users]], senior government officials, aristocratic families own personal watches.

**Social Function:**
- Extreme status symbol (wearing visible watch signals wealth comparable to owning country estate)
- Practical tool for merchant negotiations (arrive precisely on time signals professionalism and reliability)
- Demonstrates access to continuous high-skilled clockmaker services (watch useless without repair network)

**Contemporary Challenge:** Clockmakers increasingly conscripted for military applications (timing [[Flux]] artillery, coordinating troop movements). Civilian watch maintenance deteriorating, some wealthy owners find watches unrepairable.

## [[Flux]]-Assisted Timekeeping

### Flux-Oscillation Counters

**Function:** [[Control Tier|T3-T4]] practitioner creates stable oscillating flux field, counts oscillations to measure duration with extreme precision.

**Cost:** 50-100 Watts per hour (practitioner time and flux expenditure)

**Accuracy:** ±1-5 seconds per hour (far superior to mechanical methods)

**Availability:** Laboratory and specialized applications only. Not practical for civilian daily use.

**Practical Use:**
- [[Flux Medicine#Experimental Treatments|Experimental medical treatments]] requiring precise timing
- Alchemical processes with critical temperature/timing windows
- [[Desert Zakros]] expedition planning (calculating crossing duration, supply consumption rates)
- Astronomical observations (measuring celestial events, eclipse timing)

**Limitations:**
- Requires continuous practitioner attention (cannot walk away, limits scalability)
- Expensive (50-100 Watts hourly rate makes routine use prohibitive)
- Practitioner fatigue (maintaining stable oscillation mentally demanding, practical limit ~4-6 hours)

**Contemporary Pressure:** T3-T4 practitioners increasingly requisitioned for military applications. Civilian access to flux-oscillation timing declining sharply.

### Time-Binding [[Sigils]]

**Function:** Sigil activates after specified duration, triggering alarm, light, or other signal.

**Cost:** 10-30 Watts per sigil inscription (varies with complexity and duration)

**Accuracy:** ±5-15 minutes for durations under 24 hours. Accuracy degrades for longer durations (72-hour time-bind unreliable, may trigger hours early or late).

**Availability:** [[Uncommon Grimoire]] technique (T2-T3 practitioners can inscribe). Middle-class households occasionally commission time-bind sigils for specific needs. Working-class cannot afford routine use.

**Practical Use:**
- Medical timing (patient medication dosing, treatment interval alarms)
- Cooking (baking timers preventing overcooking expensive ingredients)
- Wake-up alarms (wealthy individuals commission morning wake sigils)
- Meeting reminders (professionals who must manage many appointments)

**Limitations:**
- Single-use (sigil consumed upon activation, must purchase new for next use)
- Duration limited (beyond 24 hours reliability poor)
- Environmental interference (strong flux activity nearby can trigger premature activation or suppression)

**Social Stratification:**
- **Working-class:** Never use (cost prohibitive for casual applications)
- **Middle-class:** Occasional use for important timing (expensive dinner party, critical business meeting)
- **Upper-class:** Regular use for lifestyle management (various appointments, household staff coordination)

### Flux-Preserved Time Markers

**Function:** Practitioner creates flux field that degrades at known rate. Field collapse indicates time elapsed.

**Cost:** 20-40 Watts per marker (inscription and field establishment)

**Accuracy:** ±20-40 minutes for 6-12 hour durations

**Availability:** Specialized technique (not widely taught, requires T3+ practitioner skill). Mostly used by expedition teams and remote workers.

**Practical Use:**
- [[Desert Zakros]] expedition timing (left at camp, field collapse signals return-by-deadline)
- Remote work coordination (logging operations, mining camps, field surveys)
- Military patrol timing (soldier carries marker, collapse signals shift-change)

**Limitations:**
- Visible flux field (emits faint glow, not subtle)
- Single-use, expensive for routine applications
- Environmental flux can interfere (strong rift activity accelerates degradation, making timer unreliable)

**Contemporary Pressure:** Military expands use of flux-preserved time markers (patrol coordination). Civilian access reduced as practitioners prioritize military contracts.

## Informal and Relative Timekeeping

### Shadow Length Estimation

**Function:** Observe shadow cast by vertical object (pole, building, person). Shadow length estimates time of day.

**Cost:** Free (requires no tools)

**Accuracy:** ±30-60 minutes for experienced individuals, worse for casual users

**Availability:** Universal. Anyone working outdoors learns shadow estimation.

**Practical Use:**
- Agricultural laborers (planting/harvesting schedules, meal timing, work coordinations)
- Travelers (estimating arrival times, planning rest stops)
- Outdoor markets (vendors estimate closing times)

**Limitations:**
- Requires sun visibility (cloudy weather, forests, urban canyons interfere)
- Northern latitudes experience extreme shadow variation ([[Wolfpoint]] shadows nearly horizontal in winter)
- Not usable at night

**Social Context:** Working-class primary timekeeping method. Middle and upper classes regard shadow estimation as crude but acknowledge practical utility.

### Work Rhythms and Meal Times

**Function:** Regular daily activities structure time implicitly.

**Cost:** Free

**Accuracy:** ±30-90 minutes (varies with household and workplace regularity)

**Availability:** Universal. Everyone organizes day around recurring events.

**Practical Examples:**
- **Meals:** Breakfast (shortly after sunrise), midday meal (noon-ish), evening meal (sunset-ish)
- **Work Shifts:** Guild rotations, watch changes, patrol schedules
- **[[Tavern Culture and Social Drinking|Tavern Hours]]:** Opening (mid-morning), peak (evening), closing (late night)
- **Market Activity:** Vendors arrive dawn, peak midday, close late afternoon
- **Religious Observances:** Morning prayers, midday gatherings, evening rituals

**Social Stratification:**
- **Working-class:** Meal times highly regular (dictated by work schedules, household rhythm)
- **Middle-class:** More flexible (can adjust meal times for social/business needs)
- **Upper-class:** Variable (dine when convenient, though formal dinners scheduled precisely)

### Natural Indicators

**Function:** Environmental cues indicate approximate time.

**Cost:** Free

**Accuracy:** ±1-2 hours (rough indicators, not precise)

**Availability:** Universal, though urban populations less attuned than rural.

**Examples:**
- **Bird Calls:** Dawn chorus (sunrise), evening calls (dusk), night-hunting birds (midnight)
- **Temperature:** Coolest (pre-dawn), warmest (mid-afternoon)
- **Tidal Cycles:** Coastal settlements observe twice-daily tides (predict time, though requires knowledge of lunar/tidal relationship)
- **Light Quality:** Morning light (cool, sharp shadows), afternoon light (warm, longer shadows), evening light (golden, fading)

**Social Context:** Rural populations, outdoor workers, sailors rely heavily on natural indicators. Urban middle/upper classes less practiced (buildings block views, artificial lighting disrupts circadian cues).

## Timekeeping and Class Stratification

### Working-Class Time Experience

**Primary Methods:**
- Shadow estimation (crude, adequate for agricultural and labor work)
- Public sundials (when available)
- Work rhythms and meal times (implicit structure)
- Natural indicators (dawn, dusk, temperature)

**Precision:**
- "Morning" accurate enough for coordinating with neighbors (planting together, communal labor)
- "Around noon" sufficient for meal planning
- "Before dark" adequate deadline for outdoor work completion

**Limitations:**
- Cannot reliably coordinate activities requiring 30-minute precision (missed appointments, delayed deliveries)
- Disadvantaged in disputes over contract timing ("did you deliver before noon?" becomes subjective without shared clock reference)
- Economic penalty: Late arrivals to day labor hiring (morning hiring happens specific time, approximation insufficient)

**Costs Avoided:**
- No clock purchase (savings 800-2000 Watts)
- No watch ownership (savings 2000-5000 Watts)
- No time-bind sigils (10-30 Watts per use)
- Working-class timekeeping budget: Essentially zero (public infrastructure and free observation methods only)

### Middle-Class Time Experience

**Primary Methods:**
- Household sundials (outdoor), portable sundials (travel)
- Guild-maintained water clocks (institutional coordination)
- Public mechanical clocks (urban settlements, audible bells)
- Printed lunar calendars (purchased annually)
- Occasional time-bind sigils (critical timing only)

**Precision:**
- Sundials provide ±15-30 minute accuracy (adequate for business appointments, merchant negotiations)
- Institutional clocks enable coordinated guild meetings (all members reference same time)
- Combination approach: Sundials primary, public clocks for adjustment, natural indicators backup

**Limitations:**
- Cannot afford personal mechanical watch (cost prohibitive)
- Cannot routinely use flux-assisted timing (expensive, reserved for critical needs)
- Precision suffers during cloudy weather (sundials useless, must rely on cruder methods)

**Costs:**
- Sundial: 25-50 Watts (one-time, lasts decades)
- Lunar calendar: 3-5 Watts annually
- Occasional time-bind sigils: 30-60 Watts annually (used sparingly)
- Middle-class timekeeping budget: ~5-10 Watts annually after initial sundial purchase

### Upper-Class Time Experience

**Primary Methods:**
- Private household mechanical clocks (ultimate status symbol)
- Personal watches (extreme luxury, minority even among wealthy)
- Private water clocks (backup to mechanical clock)
- Time-bind sigils (routine use for appointment management)
- Hired practitioners for flux-oscillation timing (specialized needs)

**Precision:**
- Mechanical clocks provide ±5-10 minutes daily accuracy (weekly adjustment required)
- Personal watches enable portable precision (ostentatious display of wealth)
- Time-bind sigils organize complex schedules (household staff coordination, multi-appointment days)

**Advantages:**
- Never late to important meetings (personal watch eliminates guesswork)
- Can coordinate time-sensitive business (large trade contracts specify hour-level precision, both parties have mechanical clocks)
- Social signaling (visible watch announces wealth more effectively than most other displays)

**Costs:**
- Mechanical clock: 800-2000 Watts initial, 50-100 Watts annual maintenance
- Personal watch: 2000-5000 Watts initial, 100-200 Watts annual maintenance
- Time-bind sigils: 200-500 Watts annually (routine use)
- Flux-oscillation timing: 100-300 Watts annually (specialized applications)
- Upper-class timekeeping budget: 300-800 Watts annually (excludes initial clock/watch purchase)

### Institutional Time Infrastructure

**[[Ascendant Path]] Temples ([[Auralis]]):**
- Maintain water clocks and mechanical clocks (largest temples)
- Announce month boundaries, significant astronomical events
- Provide public time reference (sundials in courtyards, visible clocks, audible bells)
- Funding: Temple tithes, [[Council Orders|Council]] grants (declining contemporary period)

**[[Trade Guilds]] ([[Terravelle]]):**
- Maintain water clocks in guild halls (shift coordination, meeting schedules)
- Distribute printed lunar calendars (annual guild service)
- Coordinate market timing (opening/closing bells, auction schedules)
- Funding: Guild dues, commercial transaction fees (pressured contemporary period but more stable than temple funding)

**Government Offices (Both Kingdoms):**
- Mechanical clocks and water clocks (record-keeping precision)
- Astronomical observations (official calendar maintenance)
- Public clock installations (urban settlements, high-visibility locations)
- Funding: Tax revenue (stable but increasingly diverted to military)

## Contemporary Timekeeping Crisis

### Practitioner Requisitions

**Impact:**
- T3-T4 practitioners conscripted for military applications (flux-oscillation timing for artillery, troop coordination)
- Civilian access to flux-assisted timekeeping collapsing (no practitioners available, costs skyrocketing)
- Time-bind sigil inscriptions declining (T2-T3 practitioners leave civilian practice for military work)

**Examples:**
- Alchemical laboratories closing (cannot obtain precise timing for temperature-critical processes)
- Wealthy households hoarding existing time-bind sigils (replacement uncertain)
- Medical treatments delayed (flux-assisted timing unreliable without practitioners)

### Clockmaker Conscription

**Impact:**
- Skilled clockmakers drafted for military work (timing mechanisms, artillery synchronization)
- Civilian mechanical clock maintenance deteriorating (fewer experts available, wait times months)
- Personal watches increasingly unrepairable (clockmakers prioritize military contracts)
- New clock construction essentially ceased (all skilled labor diverted)

**Examples:**
- [[Solhaven]] [[Crestward District]] public clock recently stopped (three-week delay finding clockmaker for repair)
- Wealthy merchant's personal watch broken (no clockmaker available, watch useless paperweight)
- Water clocks more common than mechanical (lower maintenance skill requirements, less military relevance)

### Institutional Funding Collapse

**Impact:**
- [[Ascendant Path]] temples receive less funding (public clock maintenance declining)
- [[Trade Guilds]] under pressure (maintaining guild clocks but cutting public services)
- Government-funded public clocks deteriorating (funds diverted to war preparation)

**Examples:**
- Smaller settlements lose public time infrastructure (sundials remaining but mechanical clocks silent)
- Lunar calendar printing reduced (fewer middle-class households can purchase)
- Astronomical observation coordination declining (less cross-border cooperation)

### Social Consequences

**Coordination Degrading:**
- Business appointments less reliable (parties arrive at different times, disputes increase)
- [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] schedules less predictable (port coordination relies on shared clocks, accuracy declining)
- Guild shift changes messier (water clock disruptions cause disputes over hours worked)

**Class Inequality Widening:**
- Middle-class loses access to institutional clock infrastructure (forced to cruder timekeeping methods)
- Upper-class hoards personal watches, mechanical clocks (maintenance expensive but still obtainable with connections)
- Working-class unaffected directly (already using crude methods, no infrastructure to lose)

**Economic Impact:**
- Contract disputes increase (contested delivery times, timing disagreements)
- Productivity declines (shift coordination harder, labor market efficiency drops)
- Maritime operations riskier ([[Maritime Storm Coordination]] requires shared time reference, coordination deteriorating)
