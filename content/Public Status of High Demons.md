# Public Status of High Demons

The public status of [[High Demons]] changed from suppressed folklore and archival taboo to acknowledged geopolitical reality following the Fifth Bell declaration in Auralis, where [[Teren Voss|Verath]] identified himself publicly during the corridor crisis.

This shift represents one of the largest conceptual ruptures in post-contact Solumora history.

## Pre-Disclosure Baseline

Before disclosure, High Demons existed in official discourse as:
- historical rumor tied to fragmented persecution-era records,
- cautionary mythology used in faction rhetoric,
- restricted archival references with low institutional confidence.

Most civic populations treated them as either extinct or fictional exaggerations.

## Disclosure Event Effects

Immediate effects across both kingdoms included:
- rapid collapse of prior certainty about magical hierarchy ceilings,
- emergency legal confusion over personhood and jurisdiction,
- spike in archival demand for persecution-era documentation,
- factional attempts to reframe disclosure as proof of rival-state conspiracy.

The declaration's practical credibility rested on two factors:
- witnessed dimensional-force demonstration beyond known human tiers,
- subsequent corridor stabilization outcomes that aligned with Verath's technical warnings.

## Current Policy Position (Provisional)

No unified trans-kingdom legal framework currently exists for High Demon civic status. Present posture is provisional and function-driven.

### Auralis
- Publicly acknowledges existence of at least one living High Demon.
- Internal policy split between containment advocates and technical cooperation advocates.
- Emergency exception language permits non-human technical participation under royal and civic oversight.

### Terravelle
- Publicly accepts operational reality of High Demon intervention in corridor stabilization.
- Civil authorities prioritize technical utility and risk governance over immediate ideological resolution.
- Guild and municipal bodies demand codified accountability mechanisms before normalization.

## Social Response Patterns

Observed response clusters:
- **Fear bloc:** believes disclosure signals imminent domination by immortal entities.
- **Pragmatist bloc:** accepts cooperation as necessary under active dimensional threat.
- **Revisionist bloc:** reframes persecution history and demands accountability for prior suppression.
- **Instrumentalist bloc:** seeks to weaponize disclosure for factional legitimacy battles.

These clusters cut across kingdom lines and do not map cleanly to existing political factions.

## Legal and Governance Questions

Open unresolved questions include:
- Are High Demons legal persons, strategic actors, or exempt entities?
- What oversight applies to Tier 9-equivalent intervention in public crises?
- Can non-human actors hold advisory or operational authority within civic frameworks?
- What reparative obligations exist concerning historical persecution and archival suppression?

## Long-Term Implications

The revelation permanently alters four domains:
- **Security doctrine:** threat and stabilization models now include non-human high-tier participation.
- **Magic education:** control-tier assumptions and upper-bound theory require revision.
- **Historical scholarship:** persecution-era archives move from marginal study to core governance relevance.
- **Political legitimacy:** institutions that denied or obscured demon-related evidence face trust erosion.

The central transition is from myth management to coexistence governance. Whether that governance stabilizes depends less on demon power than on institutional capacity to absorb reality without collapsing into panic or opportunism.
