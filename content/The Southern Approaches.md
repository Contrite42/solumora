The Southern Approaches is the collective name used by desert guides for the suite of southward entry routes departing from [[Halveth]] into [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]. It is not a single pass or a fixed trail — the routes shift seasonally as dune geometry changes, rift activity near ruins sites moves, and the [[Halveth Cooperative]]'s accumulated guide intelligence updates what is currently viable.

## Geography

The Approaches fan out from Halveth's southern edge across a roughly 40-kilometer front. The western branch follows terrain that sits slightly lower than the central approach, which offers marginal temperature benefit during the day at the cost of increased wind exposure from the coastal side. The central approach is the most documented — the most crossed, the most observed, the most recorded in official expedition literature — which means its risks are known and its seasonal variations are well-calibrated. The eastern branch runs closer to the first ruins cluster, which provides better reference navigation points but exposes crossing parties to higher rift-incidence zones from the first days of the crossing.

Most [[The Advancement Corps|Advancement Corps]] expeditions use the central approach. Most independent operators use whichever approach the guide recommends for current conditions, which varies.

## Halveth as Staging Infrastructure

The Southern Approaches function as well as they do because [[Halveth]] sits behind them. The settlement's entire economy has organized around the crossing trade — supply depot, guide network, equipment certification, last-accountable-point logistics. What this means practically is that parties departing into the Approaches have access to the best pre-crossing preparation infrastructure available anywhere on [[Solumora]].

It also means that the margin between well-prepared parties and poorly prepared ones is starkest at the Halveth departure point. [[Selt Orvn]] has watched parties leave that he knew would not return — not because of bad luck, but because they arrived with plans that did not reflect what the Approaches require. He does not have authority to stop anyone. He keeps records.

## Seasonal Conditions

The Southern Approaches have better and worse windows:

**Mid-autumn**: preferred by most expeditions. The summer heat peak has passed. Wind is predictable enough to navigate against. Rift activity near the first ruins cluster is at seasonal low.

**Early spring**: second preference. Workable conditions; slightly higher wind variance than autumn.

**Midsummer**: not recommended. The heat band that every crossing must traverse is at its widest and most hostile. Crossings happen. The statistics are what they are.

**Late winter**: occasionally used for urgent crossing needs. Cold on the Halveth side. Significantly cold on the far side of the heat band. The temperature transition is faster and more extreme than at other times of year — thermal management in both directions requires more sustained effort.

## The Departure Point

Experienced guides say the desert announces itself within the first few hours south of Halveth. The transition is not abrupt on a map but is immediate in experience — the wind picks up, the landmarks of the settlement disappear behind terrain, and the environment stops operating by the rules of the settled world. Parties that are going to fall apart under the psychological weight of the crossing often show first signs within this initial window, before the genuine hardship has even started.

[[Avel Coss]] has specific observations about which expedition behaviors in the first day predict outcomes on day fourteen. He does not share these observations with the expedition members themselves, because parties that know they are being assessed do not provide accurate behavioral data.

## Flux Entry Reality (Addendum)

Departure checks at [[Halveth]] treat tier floor as a hard condition. [[Control Tier|Tier 0]] and [[Control Tier|Tier 1]] cannot break into the active desert Flux zone south of the settlement, so they are not counted toward required crossing coverage.

Guides may still accept T0 or T1 travelers in larger parties, but only when the party already has sufficient T2+ and guide-tier capacity before departure.
