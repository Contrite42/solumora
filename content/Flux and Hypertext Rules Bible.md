# Flux and Hypertext Rules Bible

This page consolidates operational rules, limits, and edge-case rulings.

## Core Principles

- [[Flux]] is universal; output depends on training, tier, and context.
- [[Control Tier]] measures control ceiling, not moral worth.
- Costs compound with complexity, duration, and concurrent demands.

## Casting System Boundaries

- [[Sigils]]: stable geometry, predictable output, slower adaptation.
- [[Hypertext]]: conditional logic, expressive branching, higher failure propagation risk.
- [[Hand Signs]]: rapid constrained execution, lower compositional headroom.

## Shared Constraints

- Channeling cost remains physically and mentally material.
- Environmental stress amplifies instability and error rates.
- Multi-caster coordination increases throughput and coordination risk.

## Edge-Case Rulings (Phase 1)

- Sequential conditioning can reduce peak strain but increases exposure time.
- Emergency overrides trade reliability for speed and must be logged.
- Apparent low-cost outcomes often externalize risk to maintenance burden.
