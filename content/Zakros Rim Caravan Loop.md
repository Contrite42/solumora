The Zakros Rim Caravan Loop is the seasonal overland circuit that runs around the northern and eastern operational edge of [[Equatorial Desert]], linking supply points near [[Ashford]] with expedition staging at [[Halveth]] and returning through secondary market nodes before the next crossing window closes.

It is a caravan loop because one-way routing is too brittle. Operators need predictable return loads to keep pricing stable and crews employed between major expedition pulses. Outbound movement concentrates on water systems, protective materials, food preservation stock, and replacement equipment for desert wear. Return movement carries salvageable gear, field notes, and high-margin specialty goods that can survive rough handling.

The loop is governed by risk bands, not fixed dates. Sand movement, rift-adjacent instability, and local security conditions can shift route viability week to week. Experienced caravans maintain alternate legs and switch points rather than forcing a failing plan, which is why veteran houses outperform cheaper entrants even with higher rates.

Administrative oversight is partial by design. Too much formalization slows dispatches; too little increases loss disputes. The current equilibrium is contract-heavy at departure and trust-heavy at handoff, with arbitration usually happening back in city offices after the season closes.
