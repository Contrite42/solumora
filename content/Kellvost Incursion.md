# The Kellvost Incursion

The Kellvost Incursion was a catastrophic [[Flux Anomalies and Rifts|rift event]] that occurred in the [[Northern Agricultural Districts]] of [[Terravelle]] on the morning of the 47th day of autumn, approximately sixty days before the present timeline. A major dimensional breach opened less than two kilometers from the settlement of Kellvost—a farming community of roughly three hundred residents—and remained active for seventeen minutes before collapsing.

Fifty-three confirmed dead. Twelve missing, presumed consumed by the rift or killed by entities that emerged during the breach. Eight survivors suffered permanent Flux contamination requiring long-term institutional care. The settlement itself was rendered uninhabitable; structural collapse from dimensional instability destroyed approximately 60 percent of buildings within the breach radius.

The incursion was not random. Within six days of the event, documentary evidence surfaced proving that [[The Council of Auralis]] had been tracking heightened rift activity in the region for at least eleven months and had delayed issuing formal warnings to [[Terravelle]]'s border coordination offices. The delay was attributed to ongoing research into rift mechanics being conducted under Council authority—research that required observing rift formation patterns without intervention.

## The Event

The breach occurred at approximately 6:20 in the morning, during the period when most of Kellvost's agricultural workforce was either beginning field rotations or tending livestock near the settlement perimeter. Initial reports describe a localized distortion visible as "air folding into itself," followed by rapid expansion of the rift boundary to approximately 180 meters in diameter at peak.

Entities emerged within the first three minutes—classifications remain incomplete, but surviving witnesses describe at least three distinct forms: a bipedal predator approximately 2.5 meters tall (designated "Shrike-form" in subsequent investigation documents), airborne swarm entities (designated "Splinter-cloud"), and at least one large amorphous mass that appeared to absorb organic matter on contact (designated "Maw-form"). 

The settlement's [[Border Guards]] detachment—four soldiers stationed as part of routine agricultural-district coverage—responded immediately but were insufficient to contain entities operating across a 180-meter breach radius. Two of the four guards died within the first eight minutes. The remaining two established a defensive perimeter around the settlement's central meeting hall and successfully evacuated approximately forty residents before the rift collapsed.

By the time reinforcements arrived from [[Brend Point]]—approximately thirty-five minutes after the breach opened—the rift had closed. What remained was structural devastation, fifty-three confirmed bodies (twenty-two intact, thirty-one partially consumed or dismembered), and a settlement population in shock.

## The Council's Knowledge

Six days after the incursion, a Terravelle diplomatic courier intercepted correspondence between two Auralis scholars attached to the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins research program]]. The correspondence, dated fourteen months before the Kellvost event, explicitly referenced "elevated dimensional instability readings in northern cross-border zones" and noted that Council leadership had authorized "observation without regional advisory" to preserve research integrity.

Follow-up investigation by Terravelle's [[Border Coordination Offices]] uncovered additional documentation: three separate reports filed by Auralis rift-monitoring stations showing progressive escalation of anomaly frequency in the Kellvost region over an eleven-month period. All three reports included recommendations for population advisory or temporary evacuation. None were forwarded to Terravelle authorities.

The rationale, as articulated in internal Council memoranda later obtained by Terravelle intelligence operatives, was that issuing warnings would "compromise observational fidelity" and that the Council assessed the likelihood of a major breach as "statistically improbable over the monitoring window." The assessment was incorrect.

[[Queen Cassia]] was not informed of the rift monitoring program or the decision to withhold warnings. Her first knowledge of the program came when [[Eddan Voss]] presented the intercepted correspondence to her directly during a formal diplomatic accusation delivered twelve days after the incursion. Cassia's response—expressing shock and promising immediate investigation—was read by Terravelle as either complicity or incompetence. [[Drest]], as head of the [[Expansion Faction]] and Council vote coordinator, was directly implicated in authorizing the monitoring protocol.

## Diplomatic and Military Consequences

The Kellvost Incursion transformed the existing border friction between Terravelle and Auralis from strategic competition into a crisis with moral clarity. Eddan Voss, previously restrained in public rhetoric about Auralis policy, described the Council's actions as "institutional murder through negligent observation." His address to Terravelle's governing councils three weeks after the incursion explicitly framed the event as grounds for demanding accountability "through whatever means Terravelle's security requires."

Auralis's formal response—delivered by Cassia under Council pressure—expressed regret for the loss of life but stopped short of admitting culpability, instead characterizing the monitoring delay as "a research judgment error constrained by incomplete predictive models." Terravelle rejected this framing, and within thirty days, both kingdoms had begun military mobilization.

The incursion also fractured Auralis internally. Cassia's limited authority and evident exclusion from Council decision-making became a public scandal. Reform-minded factions within Auralis society began questioning whether the Council's research ambitions had become disconnected from basic moral governance. [[The Council of Auralis]]'s defense—that rift research serves long-term civilizational stability—found diminishing support as casualty documentation from Kellvost circulated.

For the [[Border De-Escalation Protocols]] that had previously managed cross-kingdom incidents, the Kellvost Incursion represented a category failure. There was no administrative correction window. The dead could not be un-killed. The evidence of foreknowledge eliminated any possibility that the event was accidental or unforeseeable. The protocols, designed to preserve correction space before narrative hardening, had no mechanism for addressing institutional negligence at this scale.

## Ongoing Status

As of the present timeline, Kellvost remains evacuated. Dimensional stability in the breach zone has not fully normalized; monitoring stations report intermittent "echo distortions" suggesting residual rift activity. Terravelle has declared the area under military quarantine and prohibited Auralis observers from entering the site.

The fifty-three dead have been interred in a memorial site outside [[Brend Point]]. Survivors, numbering approximately 190 after accounting for casualties and those requiring institutional care, have been resettled across northern Terravelle districts. The settlement itself is expected to remain uninhabitable for at least five years, possibly permanently depending on whether residual Flux contamination can be mitigated.

The broader question—whether the Kellvost Incursion will precipitate open war or serve as the foundation for eventual treaty negotiation—remains unresolved.
