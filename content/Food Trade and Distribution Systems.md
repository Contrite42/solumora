**Food Trade and Distribution Systems** encompasses the networks, infrastructure, and economic arrangements that move food from production sites to consumers across [[Solumora]]. This is not abstract logistics — it determines who eats what, when populations face shortages, where settlements can survive, and how political power is exercised through food access control. Food distribution failure means famine. The systems that prevent this are physical (roads, storage facilities, transport animals), institutional ([[Trade Guilds]], allocation boards, merchant networks), and technical ([[Flux Food Preservation|Flux preservation]], standardized measures, quality verification).

## Primary Food Production Regions

### [[Maren Valley]] (Terravelle Agricultural Core)

The principal grain-producing region supplying [[Terravelle]] and exporting to [[Auralis]].

**Production Characteristics:**
- Wheat and barley dominant crops (well-suited to climate, soil conditions)
- 60-70% of harvest consumed within [[Terravelle]]
- 15-25% exported to [[Auralis]] via [[Ashford-Halveth Trade Road]]
- 10-15% held as strategic reserve (managed by [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]])

**Transport Infrastructure:**
- [[Maren Freight Chain]] coordinates grain movement to [[Valdenmoor]]
- Overland wagon transport to [[Ashford]] (staging point for Auralis exports)
- [[Trade Guilds]] manage bulk shipments (reduce costs through coordinated transport)

**Contemporary Pressure:**
- [[Terravelle]] debates whether to continue [[Auralis]] grain exports during escalating tensions
- [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] increases strategic reserve (war preparation reduces available export volume)
- Some agricultural producers advocate embargo (use food as political weapon)

### [[Upland Herding Network]] (Northern Terravelle)

Sheep and goat operations providing meat, dairy, and textiles.

**Production Characteristics:**
- 40-50 families operating in northern [[Terravelle]] highlands
- Primary products: wool and leather (60% of revenue), meat and dairy (40% of revenue)
- Seasonal production (spring lambing, fall slaughter)

**Distribution:**
- Direct sales to [[Valdenmoor]] markets
- Preserved meat shipped via merchant networks
- Dairy consumed locally (short shelf life limits distribution range)

**Economic Model:**
- Herders maintain independence (avoid guild incorporation)
- Informal coordination among families (share grazing ranges, coordinate market timing)
- Some tension with [[Trade Guilds]] (guilds want to control pricing, herders resist)

### [[Mountain Game Collective]] (Wolfpoint Region)

Coordinated hunting operation producing preserved game meat.

**Production Characteristics:**
- 60% of harvest exported to [[Terravelle]] via [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]
- 40% consumed within [[Wolfpoint]] and nearby coastal settlements
- Quality stratification: premium grades for export, standard grades for local consumption

**Preservation and Transport:**
- [[Flux Food Preservation#Chemical Stabilization|Chemical stabilization]] for export grades (extends shelf life for transport)
- Traditional smoking for local grades (cheaper preservation, shorter shelf life acceptable)
- Ren Caldst's operation handles premium preservation ([[Famous Food Establishments#Ren Caldst's Preserved Meats]])

**Economic Significance:**
- Game meat is [[Wolfpoint]]'s primary export commodity
- [[Terravelle]] [[Trade Guilds]] coordinate purchases (bulk buying, standardized pricing)
- Contemporary concern: border tensions threaten transport routes

### Coastal Fishing Networks

Distributed fishing operations along [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] coasts.

**Production Characteristics:**
- Daily catches consumed fresh in coastal settlements
- Surplus preserved (salt-curing, smoking) for inland transport
- Deep-sea catches preserved for luxury markets (higher-value species)

**Distribution:**
- Fresh fish transported overland reaches inland settlements within 2-3 days (limited range)
- Preserved fish ("saltfish") reaches entire interior (bulk commodity, working-class protein source)
- Luxury fish shipped to [[Solhaven]], [[Valdenmoor]] upper-class markets

**Economic Model:**
- Independent fishermen sell to dockside buyers
- Buyers coordinate preservation and onward transport
- [[Trade Guilds]] less dominant in coastal fishing (more independent operators than agricultural sector)

## Distribution Infrastructure

### Overland Transport

Road networks moving bulk food shipments between settlements.

**Primary Routes:**
- [[Ashford-Halveth Trade Road]] — [[Terravelle]] grain to [[Auralis]], salt and manufactured goods return
- [[Maren Freight Chain]] — [[Maren Valley]] grain to [[Valdenmoor]]
- Coastal roads connecting fishing settlements to interior markets

**Transport Methods:**
- Wagon convoys (bulk grain, preserved foods)
- Animal pack trains (smaller loads, rougher terrain)
- Individual porter transport (smallest scale, highest cost per unit weight)

**Costs:**
- Wagon transport: 0.5-1.5 Watts per 100 pounds per 50 miles (varies by road quality, season, security)
- Pack animal: 1.5-3 Watts per 100 pounds per 50 miles
- Human porter: 5-10 Watts per 100 pounds per 50 miles (viable only for highest-value goods)

**Seasonal Variation:**
- Spring mud reduces transport capacity (roads impassable, delays common)
- Winter snow closes northern routes (coastal [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] shipping only option for [[Wolfpoint]])
- Summer optimal for bulk transport (dry roads, extended daylight)

**Contemporary Disruption:**
- Border crossing delays increasing (customs inspections intensify)
- Merchants stockpile inventory (anticipate future transport interruption)
- Transport animals requisitioned for military use (reduces civilian capacity)

### Maritime Transport ([[Outer Coast Packet Line]])

Coastal shipping connecting [[Wolfpoint]] to [[Terravelle]] via ocean routes.

**Operations:**
- Regular packet ships (scheduled routes, known departure/arrival times)
- Chartered vessels (larger shipments, irregular schedules)
- Small coastal boats (short-range transport between nearby settlements)

**Cargo:**
- [[Wolfpoint]] game meat to [[Terravelle]] markets
- [[Terravelle]] manufactured goods and grain to [[Wolfpoint]]
- Passengers traveling between settlements

**Economics:**
- Maritime transport cheaper per pound than overland (for distances over 150-200 miles)
- Weather risk higher (storms cause delays, occasional cargo loss)
- Limited capacity winter months (reduced shipping frequency)

**Contemporary Concern:**
- Naval tensions between [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] threaten maritime routes
- Both kingdoms expanding naval presence (merchant shipping vulnerable to interdiction)
- Coastal settlement populations concerned about supply disruption

### Storage Facilities

Infrastructure enabling food stockpiling and seasonal smoothing.

**Types:**
- **Household storage:** Root cellars, [[Flux Food Preservation#Temperature Control (Coldbox Seal)|coldbox containers]] (individual families, minimal capacity)
- **Commercial storage:** Merchant warehouses, tavern inventories (moderate capacity, supports daily operations)
- **Institutional storage:** [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] reserves, guild warehouses (large capacity, strategic function)

**Preservation Integration:**
- Flux coldbox storage extends perishable food life (enables larger inventories)
- Traditional preservation (smoking, salt-curing, fermentation) allows multi-month to multi-year storage
- Combination strategies optimize cost and shelf life

**Capacity Constraints:**
- Limited by physical infrastructure (warehouse space, preservation facilities)
- Economic constraints (storage has costs — rent, renewal, labor)
- Political priorities (military preparations requisition storage capacity)

## Market Coordination

### [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] (Terravelle)

Institutional body managing grain supply within [[Terravelle]].

**Functions:**
- Purchase surplus grain from [[Maren Valley]] producers (provides stable market)
- Maintain strategic reserve (buffer against harvest failures, enables war supply)
- Subsidize bread pricing during shortages (prevents social unrest)
- Coordinate export approvals (regulates grain sales to [[Auralis]])

**Operations:**
- Contracts with agricultural producers (negotiated prices, quality standards)
- Warehouse network across [[Terravelle]] (regional distribution centers)
- Price monitoring (detects local shortages, coordinates redistribution)

**Contemporary Role:**
- Increasing strategic reserve (war preparation priority)
- Reducing [[Auralis]] export approvals (food as political lever)
- Managing internal distribution as labor shortage affects harvest (military conscription removes agricultural workers)

### [[Trade Guilds]] (Terravelle)

Merchant confederations coordinating commercial food trade.

**Functions:**
- Bulk purchasing negotiations (reduce costs through volume)
- Quality standardization (establish grades, verify products)
- Transport coordination (shared convoy costs)
- Market intelligence (price information, supply conditions, demand forecasts)

**Structure:**
- Guild managers coordinate overall strategy
- Individual merchants operate within guild framework
- Membership dues fund shared infrastructure (warehouses, transport capacity)

**Relationship with Basin Grain Allocation Board:**
- Complementary roles (Board handles strategic reserve, guilds handle commercial trade)
- Occasional tension (Board interventions affect guild commercial operations)
- Communication channels (Board consults guilds on market conditions)

### [[Auralis]] Market Deregulation

[[Auralis]] relies more on independent merchant networks than institutional coordination.

**Characteristics:**
- Independent merchants compete (less coordination than [[Terravelle]] guilds)
- Price competition emphasized (consumers benefit from merchant rivalry)
- Less strategic reserve capacity (merchants maintain commercial inventories only)

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Supply shocks create price spikes (no institutional buffer)
- Coordination failures during crises (individual merchant responses may conflict)
- Political instability risk (working-class populations vulnerable to price increases)

**[[Auralis]] Response:**
- Council monitors food prices (intervenes when spikes threaten social stability)
- Emergency grain purchases from [[Terravelle]] (via [[Ashford-Halveth Trade Road]])
- Contemporary pressure: [[Terravelle]] reducing export availability forces [[Auralis]] to develop alternatives

## Pricing and Economic Access

### Staple Food Prices (Contemporary [[Solumora]])

Typical costs for common foods (measured in [[Watts]]):

**Grains:**
- Wheat flour: 2-4 Watts per 10 pounds (seasonal variation, quality grades)
- Barley: 1.5-3 Watts per 10 pounds
- Coarse grain bread: 1-1.5 Watts per 2-pound loaf
- Quality bread: 3-5 Watts per loaf

**Preserved Proteins:**
- Saltfish: 5-8 Watts per pound
- Preserved game meat (standard grade): 10-15 Watts per pound
- Preserved game meat (premium grade): 20-35 Watts per pound
- Cured pork: 8-12 Watts per pound

**Fresh Foods:**
- Fresh fish (coastal settlements): 3-6 Watts per pound
- Seasonal vegetables: 1-3 Watts per pound
- Root vegetables (winter storage): 0.5-2 Watts per pound
- Fowl: 8-15 Watts per bird

**Dairy:**
- Fresh milk: 1-2 Watts per quart (available near production only)
- Cheese: 6-10 Watts per pound
- Butter: 8-12 Watts per pound

### Household Food Budgets

Typical annual food costs by social class:

**Working-Class Household (4 people):**
- Total annual income: 800-1200 Watts
- Food costs: 400-600 Watts (50% of income)
- Diet composition: Grain-heavy (bread, porridge), preserved fish/meat supplement, minimal fresh foods, limited dairy

**Middle-Class Household (4 people):**
- Total annual income: 2000-4000 Watts
- Food costs: 800-1200 Watts (30-40% of income)
- Diet composition: More diverse grains, regular meat/fish, seasonal fresh foods, moderate dairy, occasional luxury items

**Upper-Class Household (4 people + staff):**
- Total annual income: 8000+ Watts
- Food costs: 2000-4000 Watts (20-30% of income)
- Diet composition: Wide variety, premium grades, luxury imports, extensive fresh foods, advanced Flux preservation

### Price Volatility and Food Security

**Seasonal Variation:**
- Fresh foods expensive in winter (limited supply, high preservation costs)
- Grain prices peak before new harvest (old stock depleting, uncertainty about coming yield)
- Fresh fish prices lowest summer (peak fishing season, abundant supply)

**Regional Variation:**
- Coastal settlements: Cheap fish, expensive grain
- Interior agricultural: Cheap grain, expensive fish
- [[Wolfpoint]]: Cheap game meat, expensive grain (import dependency)

**Crisis Response:**
- Harvest failures trigger price spikes (reduced supply meets steady demand)
- [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] releases reserves (reduces price increases in [[Terravelle]])
- [[Auralis]] improvises (emergency imports, rationing, price controls)

## Cross-Border Food Trade

### [[Ashford]] as Trading Hub

Border settlement facilitating [[Auralis]]-[[Terravelle]] food exchange.

**Primary Flows:**
- [[Terravelle]] grain to [[Auralis]] (bulk commodity, wagon transport)
- [[Auralis]] salt to [[Terravelle]] ([[Wolfpoint]] depends on this for preservation)
- Smaller specialty trades (preserved fish, luxury foods, processed goods)

**Infrastructure:**
- Warehouses near border crossing (staging areas for customs inspection)
- Merchant lodging (traders wait during processing)
- Inspection facilities (customs, quality verification, documentation)

**Contemporary Strain:**
- Customs delays increasing (inspections intensify, paperwork requirements expand)
- [[Terravelle]] export restrictions reducing grain flow
- Merchants stockpile inventory in [[Ashford]] warehouses (anticipate border closure)

### Economic Interdependence

Both kingdoms benefit from food trade despite political tensions.

**[[Auralis]] Imports from [[Terravelle]]:**
- Grain (particularly to [[Halveth]] and other interior settlements with limited agricultural land)
- High-grade preserved game meat ([[Wolfpoint]] exports via [[Terravelle]] merchants)

**[[Terravelle]] Imports from [[Auralis]]:**
- Salt (critical for preservation — [[Ashford Supply Contract]] negotiations)
- Deep-sea fish (luxury markets in [[Valdenmoor]])
- Some manufactured food containers (ceramic ware for storage)

**Mutual Vulnerability:**
- Border closure would harm both kingdoms
- Food embargoes would cause civilian suffering before political concessions
- Military planners in both kingdoms assess food supply security

## Contemporary Challenges

### War Preparation Impacts

**Labor Shortages:**
- Agricultural workers conscripted ([[Auralis]] more aggressive than [[Terravelle]])
- Harvest yields decline (fewer workers for planting, weeding, harvesting)
- Transport capacity reduced (animals and workers requisitioned)

**Strategic Stockpiling:**
- Both kingdoms increase reserves (diverts food from civilian consumption)
- [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] requisitions higher percentages of harvest
- Civilian populations face reduced variety, increased prices

**Supply Chain Militarization:**
- Food transport increasingly serves military priorities
- Civilian food shipments delayed or diverted
- [[Auralis]] establishes military logistics corridors

### Border Crossing Deterioration

**Customs Intensification:**
- Inspections take longer (every wagon searched thoroughly)
- Documentation requirements increase (merchants face bureaucratic burdens)
- Arbitrary enforcement (officials use discretion as political tool)

**Merchant Adaptation:**
- Some merchants cease cross-border trade (risk-reward no longer favorable)
- Remaining traders charge premium (pass increased costs to consumers)
- Smuggling increases (informal routes avoid customs, operate illegally)

### Price Increases and Social Strain

**Working-Class Impact:**
- Food costs rising faster than wages
- Households reduce consumption (smaller portions, less variety, skip meals)
- Social unrest potential (hunger is politically destabilizing)

**Middle-Class Pressure:**
- Reduced disposable income (food costs consume larger budget share)
- Status anxiety (can no longer afford previous consumption patterns)
- Economic behavior changes (reduce tavern dining, eliminate luxury foods)

**Upper-Class Insulation:**
- Wealthy maintain access to food supply
- Differential impact increases class resentment
- Political pressure for state intervention (prevent total breakdown)

### Alternative Supply Development

**[[Auralis]] Grain Production:**
- Attempts to increase domestic grain cultivation (reduce [[Terravelle]] dependence)
- Limited success (less suitable agricultural land, labor shortage)
- Long-term strategy (requires years to establish infrastructure)

**Coastal Fishing Intensification:**
- Both kingdoms increase fishing effort (compensate for reduced trade)
- Sustainability concerns (overfishing may deplete stocks)
- Processing bottleneck (preservation capacity limits how much fish can be distributed inland)

**Preservation Technology Investment:**
- Both kingdoms research improved preservation methods (extend shelf life, reduce waste)
- [[Auralis]] [[Crestward Flux Institute]] prioritizes this (food security is national priority)
- Incremental improvements (no breakthrough, but gradual efficiency gains)

## Future Scenarios

### Border Closure Impact

If [[Auralis]]-[[Terravelle]] border closes completely:

**[[Auralis]]:**
- Grain shortage in interior settlements (particularly [[Halveth]])
- Price spikes trigger working-class unrest
- Emergency rationing implemented (Council priority)
- Intensified domestic production (emergency measures, potentially unsustainable)

**[[Terravelle]]:**
- Salt shortage threatens [[Wolfpoint]] preservation (export economy collapses)
- Reduced grain export market means lower agricultural prices (farmer income declines)
- [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] manages surplus (prevent political instability from price collapse)

**Both Kingdoms:**
- Civilian populations suffer
- Political pressure on leadership to resolve crisis
- Risk of escalation (desperation increases war likelihood)

### Open Conflict

If kingdoms actually go to war:

- Food production collapses (fields become battlegrounds, workers become soldiers)
- Supply chains destroyed (transport routes interdicted, warehouses burned)
- Famine becomes weapon (siege tactics targeting civilian food supplies)
- Long-term agricultural recovery required (multi-year process after hostilities end)
