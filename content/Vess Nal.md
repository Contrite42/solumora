**Vess Nal** is a senior dock worker at [[Wolfpoint Harbor]], part of the eight-person permanent crew that handles all maritime cargo operations for the settlement. He is T1, 52 years old, and has worked the harbor for fifteen years — longer than the current harbormaster, longer than most of the Packet Line captains, long enough to have institutional knowledge about ship tolerances, cargo handling, and loading efficiency that keeps Wolfpoint's limited port capacity functional.

## Expertise

Vess can estimate cargo weight from visual inspection. He knows which ships in the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] rotation can handle late manifest additions and which captains will refuse anything not pre-arranged. He knows the loading sequence that maximizes hold space, which crates can be double-stacked without damage, and how to distribute weight to keep ships stable in rough coastal waters. This knowledge is not documented in manuals — it exists in Vess's judgment, built from fifteen years of loading ships in wind and cold.

The [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] maintains detailed cargo records, but actual loading operations depend on Vess's real-time decisions. When [[Bren Hask]] says a shipment needs to depart that day, Vess is the person who determines whether the available hold space can accommodate it safely. When storms delay arrivals and multiple ships come within days of each other, Vess coordinates dock space allocation and crew scheduling to keep operations moving without errors.

He trains new dock workers, which means he trains every person who handles Wolfpoint's export cargo and survival imports. The training is practical: how to recognize weight limits from crate dimensions, how to secure loads against ship movement, how to identify damage that makes cargo unsuitable for maritime transport, and — critically — how to maintain cargo security so valuable [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] metalwork is not substituted or stolen during loading.

## Operations

Dock work at Wolfpoint is physically demanding in ways that go beyond typical port labor. The constant 45-degree cold means hands numb quickly without heating [[Grimoires]], and the wind off open water makes unprotected loading dangerous. The harbor's exposure means cargo handling happens in conditions where most southern ports would shut down operations entirely.

Vess works in these conditions year-round. He wears layered wool from [[Upland Herding Network]] producers and keeps a personal heating grimoire that provides steady low-level warmth without the Flux cost of high-intensity spells. His T1 assessment means he can maintain this consistently — enough control to run simple grimoires reliably, not enough to attempt anything complex. This position is deliberately chosen. Vess could have pursued T2 assessment, but opted not to. Dock work does not require higher-tier capability, and T1 status keeps his life stable rather than pushing him toward roles that demand more Flux expenditure than he prefers.

The dock crew operates as a coordinated unit during ship arrivals. Vess directs cargo flow from warehouse to pier, assigns workers to specific loading tasks based on their strength and experience, and maintains the loading pace that keeps ships on schedule without rushing workers into mistakes. [[Bren Hask]] manages overall harbor operations and external relationships; Vess runs the actual cargo handling that makes those operations possible.

## Position in Settlement

Vess is paid above standard Wolfpoint wages — [[Bren Hask]] maintains premium compensation for dock workers specifically to reduce economic pressure that might create security risks. A dock worker who feels underpaid has access to valuable cargo and relationships with ship captains who could facilitate theft. Vess earns enough to live comfortably, save modest reserves, and avoid the financial stress that creates temptation.

He lives in the lower settlement near the harbor, in a stone house built for wind resistance with a small workshop where he repairs dock equipment during winter months when ship arrivals slow. He is not part of Wolfpoint's elite circles — he does not attend [[The Wolfpoint Scholarium]] gatherings or participate in [[Hypertext]] development conversations — but he is respected within the working population as someone who has mastered a difficult job and maintained reliability for fifteen years.

When practitioners arrive from the south to study, Vess is often the first working-class Wolfpoint resident they encounter. He loads their baggage, provides practical orientation to the settlement's climate and logistics, and demonstrates through competent labor that Wolfpoint's isolation has not prevented its people from developing professional expertise. This is not a formal ambassadorial role, but it functions as one. Visitors who arrive expecting a frontier outpost often revise their assessment after watching Vess coordinate a complex loading operation in conditions that would paralyze southern ports.

## Strategic Awareness

Vess understands that his work is strategically relevant. The harbor is Wolfpoint's survival interface with the southern kingdoms, and cargo handling errors could threaten the settlement's economic independence. He has seen [[Keln Varost]] inspect outbound shipments to verify [[Terravelle]] trade relationships remain balanced. He has watched [[Auralis]] merchants ask careful questions about cargo volumes while pretending to make casual conversation.

He does not discuss these observations publicly, but he reports unusual patterns to [[Bren Hask]]. When a trader spends excessive time examining [[Mountain Game Collective]] preserved meat as if assessing preservation techniques rather than product quality, Vess notes it. When a ship arrives with cargo manifest discrepancies that suggest mid-transit tampering, he documents it. His reports are brief, factual, and contribute to the intelligence stream that [[Renn Taska]] uses to maintain Wolfpoint's economic balance.

Vess has spent fifteen years ensuring ships depart on schedule with correct cargo in good condition. This is not glamorous work. It is, however, one of the systems that allows Wolfpoint to remain independent rather than becoming a territory controlled by southern kingdoms with better resources and larger populations.
