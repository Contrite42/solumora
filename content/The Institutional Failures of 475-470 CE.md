_(Historical Context, Institutional Failure Attribution)_

**The Institutional Failures of 475-470 CE** describes the systematic dysfunction patterns that [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] authorities blamed on [[High Demons|demon]] interference, the actual causes of institutional failures (most were unrelated to supernatural interference), and how institutional attribution of natural failures to demonic causation enabled mobilization of persecution framework that wouldn't have been politically viable if causes were acknowledged as ordinary institutional stress. The failures are critical context because they demonstrate how institutions use supernatural threat narratives to avoid accountability for governance failures, how searching for demonic explanation provides politically acceptable justification for violence against scapegoat populations, and how persecution apparatuses become self-reinforcing through mechanism of blaming continued institutional failure on supernatural enemy rather than on institutional incompetence.

## Pre-Persecution Institutional State

Before 475 years ago, both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] maintained stable if unremarkable governance. Agricultural production was adequate, trade networks functioned normally, military operations were routine and adequate. No extraordinary institutional crises requiring explanation.

Around 475 years ago, pattern of dysfunction began that institutions couldn't immediately explain through normal causes.

## Documented Dysfunction Patterns

### Economic Crisis

**Observed Pattern**: Commodity pricing became unstable. Grain prices fluctuated 15-25% month-to-month, when historical baseline oscillation had been 3-5%. Tool prices spiked unpredictably. Livestock values became impossible to predict. Merchants couldn't maintain stable margins because they couldn't predict costs or values.

**Actual Causes** (identified through historical analysis centuries later):
- Regional drought affecting multiple territories simultaneously, reducing grain supply
- Trade network disruption from distant conflict reducing caravan efficiency
- Monetary inflation from previous military campaign depleting metal reserves
- Labor disruption from seasonal worker migration patterns changing
- Climate weather pattern shifting (later identified through agricultural records)

**Institutional Attribution**: "Demons are disrupting trade patterns and causing price chaos to destabilize our economy and create social stress."

**Why Attribution Made Sense Institutionally**: Acknowledging actual causes would require acknowledging institutional economic management failure, admitting external climate conditions were beyond institutional control, and explaining why trade networks couldn't be stabilized through policy intervention. Blaming demons required no institutional accountability.

### Political Conflict

**Observed Pattern**: Council members and institutional leaders developed sudden bitter conflicts over issues that should have been resolvable. Long-time allies would erupt into antagonism. Disagreements that were normally handled through consensus suddenly became intractable disputes. Multiple simultaneous conflicts between different institutional factions.

**Actual Causes** (identified through historical analysis centuries later):
- Resource scarcity from economic crisis putting institutional factions in competition
- Personality conflicts between specific leaders that happened to manifest during this period
- Legitimate policy disagreements about how to manage economic crisis
- Shift in political power structures as some institutional leaders gained influence and others lost it
- Normal institutional political conflict amplified by stress conditions

**Institutional Attribution**: "Demons are sowing discord among our leaders, turning allies into enemies, creating internal division to weaken us."

**Why Attribution Made Sense Institutionally**: Acknowledging actual causes would require acknowledging that institutional leaders couldn't maintain unity under stress, that underlying political structures were inherently fragile, that resource scarcity was creating legitimate conflicts of interest. Blaming demons gave institutional leaders excuse for conflict: "It's not our failure, we're being influenced by supernatural enemy."

### Military Operations Failure

**Observed Pattern**: Several military operations conducted during this period failed unexpectedly. Battles were lost despite favorable positioning or superior force. Commanders made tactical errors that seemed incomprehensible after-action analysis. Supply lines were disrupted in ways that seemed intentional but attribution was unclear.

**Actual Causes** (identified through historical analysis centuries later):
- Commander inexperience (some commanders lacked prior battle experience)
- Improved opponent intelligence and counter-tactics from advancing military-tactical knowledge
- Supply line vulnerabilities from trade network disruption
- Miscommunication and information failures between military units
- Overconfidence from previous military successes creating tactical laziness
- Weather disruption blocking supply delivery at critical moments

**Institutional Attribution**: "Demons are disrupting our military operations, influencing commanders into tactical errors, sabotaging supply lines."

** Why Attribution Made Sense Institutionally**: Acknowledging actual causes would require acknowledging that commanders were inexperienced, that military institutions needed reform, that opponent military capacity had improved. Blaming demons gave military leadership excuse for failures without requiring institutional change.

### Social Instability

**Observed Pattern**: Communities fractured into hostile groups. Neighbors who had coexisted peacefully became hostile. Religious disputes became violent. Social cooperation structures broke down. Theft and violence increased. Community institutions (guilds, village councils, religious congregations) had difficulty maintaining order.

**Actual Causes** (identified through historical analysis centuries later):
- Economic stress creating resource competition between community members
- Migration patterns as desperate people moved seeking work, disrupting community stability
- Emergence of new religious movement that competed with established religion for community allegiance
- Social mobility disruption as economic crisis made traditional advancement pathways unavailable
- Normal human response to widespread economic crisis: community stress increases hostility
- Generational transition creating value differences between older and younger community members

**Institutional Attribution**: "Demons are sowing discord in our communities, turning neighbor against neighbor, creating hostile environment."

**Why Attribution Made Sense Institutionally**: Acknowledging actual causes would require acknowledging that economic crisis creates social stress that institutions cannot prevent, that communities fractured due to legitimate resource competition, that new religious movements represented genuine alternative worldview attracting followers. Blaming demons provided institutional explanation without requiring institutional accountability.

## Why Supernatural Attribution Became Dominant

Given that all documented institutional failures had natural explanations, why did supernatural attribution become dominant institutional narrative?

**Accountability Avoidance**: Blaming demons avoided institutional accountability. Institutions that failed to maintain economic stability, prevent political conflict, maintain military success, or preserve social order could claim they were victims of supernatural enemy rather than incompetent.

**Political Convenience**: Supernatural threat unified both kingdoms around common enemy. It allowed institutional leaders to mobilize population support for security measures that would have been controversial if implemented for ordinary governance failure reasons.

**Threat Legitimacy**: Supernatural threats are by definition beyond normal institutional control. Institutional leaders could claim: "We're doing everything we can against supernatural enemy" without being required to propose how to succeed.

**Resource Justification**: Blame demons to justify increased military spending, increased security force size, increased investigation authority. Resources spent hunting demons were resources not available for addressing actual institutional failures.

**Violence Authorization**: Blaming demons enabled violence against identified scapegoats. If institutional failures were ordinary, violence would require ordinary justification. Blaming supernatural threat authorized extraordinary violence without normal legal constraints.

## The Self-Reinforcing Pattern

Once supernatural attribution became dominant:

**Institutional Failure → Blame Demons**: Institutions experienced crisis/failure, blamed demons.

**Blame Demons → Mobilize Against Them**: Institutional response: identify and execute suspected demons.

**Execute Suspected Demons → Continued Institutional Failure**: Institutional failures didn't resolve through demon-hunting because failures had natural causes not affected by executing humans.

**Continued Failure → Must Be More Demons**: Institutional logic: "We're executing demons but failures continue, therefore demons must be more numerous/hidden than suspected."

**Must Be More Demons → Intensify Persecution**: Increase identification standards sensitivity, execute more suspects, expand investigation scope.

**Expansion → Accelerate False Positives**: As persecution intensifies, killing more innocent humans (and occasionally actual demons by accident) while not addressing underlying institutional failures.

**Failures Still Continue → Institutional Investment**: Institutions become committed to demon-hunting framework because admitting failure would require admitting persecution was pointless.

## The Institutional Psychology

Why didn't institutional leaders simply stop blaming demons when persecution didn't improve institutional conditions?

**Sunk Cost Fallacy**: Institutions had invested enormous resources, executed thousands of people, destroyed property. Admitting demon-blame was false would mean admitting all that violence was pointless.

**Authority Preservation**: Religious and secular authorities had built power structure around demon-hunting and identification. Stopping would mean losing that authority and resources.

**Cognitive Dissonance**: Many institutional leaders genuinely believed demon-blame narrative. Contradicting the narrative would require accepting that they had authorized massive violence for nothing.

**Institutional Self-Protection**: Admitting institutional failure would create liability and accountability pressure. Maintaining demon-narrative protected institutions from accountability.

**Actual Institutional Improvements**: Interestingly, some institutional failures DID improve during persecution era, but not because demons were being eliminated. Improvements came from:
- Economic crisis naturally resolving after 10+ years as trade networks adapted
- New leaders emerging who had better governing skills
- Community adaptation to resource scarcity creating new normal
- Religious innovation accommodating both old and new religious movements

When improvements occurred, institutions credited demon-elimination rather than natural resolution. This "confirmation" of supernatural narrative reinforced institutional commitment to persecution framework.

## Why This Matters for Current Crisis

The Persecution Era demonstrates that institutional authorities will:

1. **Blame Supernatural Causes for Governance Failures**: If current [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] constitutional crisis has roots in institutional mismanagement, authorities might attribute it to demon interference
2. **Use Supernatural Threat to Authorize Violence**: Demon revelation gives authorities supernatural threat to blame for multiple ongoing crises
3. **Mobilize Against Supernatural Enemy**: Persecution framework becomes readily available and politically attractive
4. **Resist Correction When Persecution Fails**: If demon-hunting doesn't solve crises, institutions will intensify rather than reassess

If [[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] reveal demon existence during current [[The Constitutional War Path|escalating crisis]], they will be handing institutional authorities a supernatural scapegoat for institutional failures that persecution apparatus will enthusiastically use.

The demons won't be revealed for their own sake. They'll be revealed precisely because they provide institutional authorities explanation for failures that don't otherwise require institutional accountability.

That's why revelation without preventative framework means persecution becomes not just possible but probable.
