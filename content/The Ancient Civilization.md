The civilization that built the ruins in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] is not named in any surviving record. The settlements that became [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] have no oral tradition reaching back to it — the gap between its end and the earliest human settlements is long enough that continuity of memory was lost entirely. What remains is physical: structures, inscriptions, and three [[Ancient Ruins|anomalous Flux sites]] that are still active.

## What the Ruins Show

The structural scale of the ruins suggests a civilization that was both large and long-running. The buildings that have been surveyed are not the ruins of a young culture that rose quickly and fell before maturing — the construction techniques, the material choices, the evidence of layered modification over time, all suggest a civilization that built and rebuilt its infrastructure across generations. The survey records from [[The Advancement Corps]] expeditions, when assembled and compared across decades, suggest something that endured for at least two thousand years, possibly significantly longer.

The inscriptions, where they survive in legible condition, are in a notation system that partially maps to the standard [[Spell Variables|Scholar symbol framework]] — but only partially. The same symbols appear, but the structural logic is different. More variables, more conditional structures, more sequences that appear to encode interdependencies between spells rather than individual effects. [[Orva Dresk]]'s recent work on ruins-artifact Hypertext reconstruction represents one approach to reading these inscriptions; the Scholarium at [[Wolfpoint]] has approaches of its own that it has not published.

One possibility — not yet formally advanced in published scholarship but discussed in private — is that the ancient civilization's casting system was something like [[Hypertext]] at a scale and sophistication that current practitioners cannot replicate: not because the knowledge is lost, but because the required [[Control Tier]] simply does not exist in the current population at sufficient numbers.

## The Three Anomalous Sites

The [[Ancient Ruins|three anomalous Flux sites]] in the desert are different from the other ruins. They are not decayed. They are active. The Flux behavior in and around these sites does not match what should be present in an abandoned structure — it matches an ongoing process, something that is still running after the people who built it are gone.

What the process is doing is the central question. The [[The Culmination Faction|Culmination Faction]]'s position — that the ancient civilization "culminated" rather than died, that the anomalous sites represent the success of some final achievement rather than the cause of collapse — is a minority view in formal scholarship but a coherent one given the evidence. Something happened at these sites. The civilization is gone. The sites are still operating.

The ruins near the anomalous sites also produce [[Rift Incursions|Flux rifts]] at elevated rates. Whether this is a consequence of the ongoing process at the anomalous sites, a separate phenomenon, or the mechanism by which the ancient population was affected is not established.

## The Watts Connection

The [[Watts]] ledger — the currency system that both kingdoms use and that both kingdoms assume to be a natural feature of the world's Flux field — is the ancient civilization's construction. The ledger is built into the ambient Flux at a depth and with a sophistication that implies either extraordinary technical capability or extraordinary duration or both.

[[Sera Voss]] discovered this through analysis of [[Hypertext]] sequences in ruins artifacts. She has not published the finding. The implications — that both kingdoms are operating on infrastructure built by a civilization whose nature and end remain unknown, and that the [[Watts]] economy is not a natural phenomenon but a constructed one that could, in principle, be modified or terminated — are significant enough that she has not decided what to do with the knowledge.

The ancient civilization's construction of the Watts ledger suggests they understood Flux at a systemic level that current practitioners approach only in theory: not just casting individual effects but building persistent infrastructure that runs without ongoing maintenance, distributed across the ambient field of an entire continent.

## What Ended Them

No one knows. This is the honest answer, and it is unsatisfying enough that scholarship has produced several theories in its place.

The desert itself is the most obvious fact. The [[Equatorial Desert]] is where the ruins are most densely concentrated. The desert is also where the anomalous sites are. Whether the desert caused the civilization to end or the civilization's end caused the desert — or whether both are effects of a third cause — is unresolved.

The absence of catastrophe markers in the ruins makes invasion or conquest implausible. There is no evidence of mass death, no burned cities, no weapons-damage on a scale consistent with military defeat. The civilization simply stopped being present.

The Culmination Faction's theory — that the anomalous sites represent a successful achievement, that the civilization chose to leave or transform rather than dying — is impossible to rule out on current evidence. It is also impossible to confirm.
