Watts is the currency unit of [[Solumora]], used in both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] for wages, trade, and taxation. A single Watt is the base unit. Larger denominations exist as multiples — tens, hundreds, thousands — and the same terms are used in both kingdoms without distinction.

Watts are not physical objects. There are no Watt coins, no Watt notes, no physical representation of value that changes hands. A Watt is an entry on the [[Birth Runes|birth rune ledger]] — a transfer of recorded value from one registered individual to another, written into the distributed [[Flux]] field and verified by every other transaction in the network simultaneously.

You cannot hold a Watt. You can only have them, transfer them, and verify that you have them.

## How Transactions Work

A transaction between two registered individuals requires the consent of both parties — one initiates, one confirms, and the ledger writes. The process takes moments for anyone with basic Flux sensitivity and slightly longer for those without, who rely on a [[Flux Users|Scribe]] or trained intermediary to facilitate the read and confirm. In practice, most urban market transactions are fast enough that they do not feel meaningfully different from handing over a physical object.

The ledger cannot be altered retroactively. A transaction confirmed is a transaction permanent. Disputed transactions are resolved through the same social and legal mechanisms as any other contract dispute — the ledger provides the record, but interpreting what that record means in context is still a human problem.

## Origin

The Watts ledger predates both kingdoms. The earliest records of birth rune transactions in either kingdom's archives trace back further than coherent written history — the system was already running when the first settlements that became [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] began keeping records.

The [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] confirm what neither kingdom has formally concluded: the ledger is the work of the civilization that built those ruins. Inscription fragments recovered from confirmed ruin sites include ledger architecture — structural Flux encoding that matches the distributed network's underlying logic at a level that cannot be coincidental. The anomalous Flux behavior at the three active ruin sites is not entirely a consequence of ancient casting failures. Some of it is the ledger's own infrastructure, still running, embedded in the ambient field at those locations because that is where the original anchor points were built.

The ancient civilization built Watts as a currency system. The current world inherited it without realizing what they had inherited, and has been running their economies on it ever since.

This connection has not been made in either kingdom's scholarship. The [[Flux Users|Scholars]] at [[Emberfall]] studying ruin fragments and the administrators managing birth rune registration have never been in the same conversation. [[Drest]]'s faction is closer than anyone — they are investigating the ruins' Flux technology without knowing that technology never stopped operating — but they have not arrived at the ledger as a conclusion.

[[Sera Voss]] has. Working on the [[Hypertext]] document gave her a structural angle on Flux encoding that most Scholar-trained researchers do not have, and when she examined the ledger's underlying architecture through that lens she recognized it as written rather than natural — and written in something that resembles an extremely sophisticated, extremely old precursor to what the Voss line has been independently developing for seven generations. She has not shared this observation with anyone. It has significantly complicated her feelings about what to do with the document when it is finished.

## Physical Representations

Some people carry physical tokens stamped with their birth rune mark as a convenience — not because the token holds value, but because presenting it speeds up the consent verification process in busy commercial environments. These tokens are not currency. They are identification shortcuts. A token without the corresponding live birth rune behind it is worthless, and any trained [[Flux Users|Scribe]] can tell the difference immediately.

The practice of tattooing a birth rune onto the body, common among some [[Auralis]] casters, makes physical tokens unnecessary. The rune is always present and always readable with consent.

## Backing and Value

Watts are fiat currency in the strict sense — they are backed by nothing except the network's integrity and the collective agreement to treat them as valuable. There is no gold reserve, no Flux commodity, no state guarantee underwriting the system. What makes Watts work is that the ledger cannot be cheated, which means a Watt transferred is a Watt received, always, without possibility of counterfeiting or double-spending.

Neither kingdom controls the ledger. Neither kingdom issued the currency. Both kingdoms tax in it, pay in it, and regulate trade denominated in it, but the underlying system predates both of them and operates independently of any institutional decision. This is a fact that receives occasional academic attention and no political attention, because neither kingdom has found a way to make it actionable.

## The Unregistered Economy

Watts require [[Birth Runes|birth rune]] registration. People without runes do not participate in the Watts economy directly — they cannot initiate or receive ledger transactions. This excludes a meaningful portion of [[Solumora]]'s population, concentrated in rural communities and frontier settlements where registration is optional and often declined.

These communities are not economically inert. They trade in goods, labor, and relationship-based credit systems that predate Watts and function independently of the ledger. In [[Terravelle]] this parallel economy is treated as unremarkable. In [[Auralis]] it carries social stigma in proportion to how urban and Flux-integrated the surrounding community is — in [[Solhaven]]'s upper terraces, operating without Watts is practically inconceivable; in a southern frontier village, it is simply how things have always been done.

The two economies interact at their edges. A village that produces something a city wants will eventually find a registered trader willing to intermediate, converting goods into Watts on one side and Watts into goods on the other. [[Halveth]] runs exactly this kind of mixed economy — permanent residents on both sides of the registration line, trading freely with each other and with the expedition traffic that passes through.

## Scale

What a Watt buys varies significantly by location and context. A day's unskilled labor in [[Valdenmoor]] runs in the low single digits. A skilled [[Flux Users|Formulist]]'s daily rate in [[Solhaven]] runs in the hundreds. A desert crossing expedition outfit from [[Halveth]] runs in the thousands. The kingdoms do not fix prices and do not regulate wages beyond minimum standards in guild-registered trades. The ledger records what people agree to. It does not have opinions about whether the agreement was fair.
