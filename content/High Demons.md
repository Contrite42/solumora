High Demons are the apex form of [[Flux Demons]] — fully humanoid in appearance, possessed of genuine intelligence, capable of communication, and functionally immortal. They are the oldest, most capable, and least understood threat currently present in Solumora. No one in either kingdom is aware they exist.

## Appearance

A High Demon in its natural state is indistinguishable from a human being except for a single feature: horns. The horns vary in shape and size between individuals but are always present and cannot be fully concealed without deliberate effort. Beyond this, High Demons present with normal human variation in height, build, and coloring. They do not register as unusual to casual [[Flux]] detection — they can mute their signature sufficiently to pass unnoticed by anyone not actively looking for anomalies.

They are capable of passing as human in extended social contact. Several have done so for decades or centuries at a time.

## Nature

High Demons are native to the Flux dimension. They did not originate in Solumora, do not consider it home, and do not have loyalty to either kingdom or any institution within them. They are curious, patient, and possessed of a time horizon that no human institution can match. They do not plan in seasons or years. The oldest ones have been present long enough to have watched the current kingdoms form from their foundations, and they have had that entire span to develop whatever goals, habits, or ways of living suit them.

They are intelligent enough for complex reasoning, fluent in language after sufficient exposure, and fully capable of understanding the political and social structures of Solumora. What they do with that understanding varies entirely by individual. Their goals are their own, shaped by centuries of particular experience, and not well understood even by other High Demons.

They do not typically coordinate with each other. Encounters between High Demons are rare and tend toward careful mutual acknowledgment rather than alliance or conflict. What they share is not a common agenda but a common awareness: their presence in this world is something the world would respond to badly if it knew.

## The Hunting Period

Approximately four hundred years ago, a young High Demon who had been in Solumora for less than a decade lost control of their spell in a populated area. The incident killed a horse outright, killed one person, and left a third with a crushed leg. Scholars who investigated the aftermath identified the effect as a massive downward Force compression centred on a sigil point — an application far beyond anything a human caster at that age could produce, and distinctive enough that it did not resemble any known casting tradition.

The young demon was found and killed. This was the worst possible outcome for every other High Demon on the continent.

What followed was not an organised purge. There was no kingdom declaration, no formal bounty system, no coordinated authority directing the effort. What there was, for approximately two hundred years, was fear operating at the community level — a decentralised, persistent, slow-burning hunt driven by the knowledge that something that looked human could do what had been done in that square, and that the one responsible had not been unique. Communities turned on people who seemed too capable too young, or who could not account for their origins, or who simply had the wrong kind of strangeness about them. High Demons were not the only ones who died in this period. They were the intended targets.

[[Sorath]] and [[Fennick]] had both been in Solumora long enough by this point to have established identities, some degree of integration, and the experience to go quiet. Both withdrew. Sorath has said, to no one, that the two decades he spent in an isolated location waiting for the worst of it to pass were the most tedious of his existence. Fennick found the situation darkly interesting from a safe distance and has never fully admitted how close it came to not being a safe distance.

The hunting period did not end cleanly. It exhausted itself. The trail went cold, the incidents stopped, and without new fuel the fear gradually became history rather than present concern. No one declared it over. No one wrote down that it had stopped. It simply receded, and within a generation the specific knowledge of what had been hunted blurred into folk memory and then into nothing.

The three younger High Demons — [[Selvane]], [[Mave]], and [[Wren]] — all arrived after the hunting period had ended. None of them experienced it directly. Sorath has declined to discuss it in the one conversation where it was adjacent to the topic. Fennick has mentioned it twice, lightly, in contexts that suggested he found the historical irony more interesting than the trauma.

## Flux Ability

Each High Demon has exactly one spell — a single discipline that is effectively genetic, determined at origin rather than learned. They have no ability to cast outside this discipline. Within it, they have effectively unlimited control. A High Demon does not misfire. It does not have a practical ceiling on application. The only limit is imagination.

This is not a modest ability. A human [[Flux Users|Conduit]] at T9 has one area of total mastery alongside a lifetime of general casting development. A High Demon has spent centuries — sometimes eight of them — finding every possible use of a single spell with nothing else to think about. The results are not comparable to human casting in the same discipline. They have found angles that have not been imagined.

High Demons reach the equivalent of T9 with their spell type after more than 600 years of existence. Younger individuals — those who have been in Solumora for less than that — are still climbing toward that ceiling and are commensurately less capable, though still dangerous by human standards from the moment they arrive.

Their Flux detection is significant. A High Demon can sense other demons within a wide radius even when those demons are actively suppressing their signature. They cannot fully hide from each other.

## Current Population

There are five High Demons currently present in Solumora. Each has its own history, goals, and chosen method of existence. None of them are coordinating. All of them are aware, at some level, that others exist on the continent.

The oldest arrived before the current kingdoms consolidated. They have watched Terravelle and Auralis form, develop, and reach their current state of tension from positions embedded within both societies or moving through the spaces between them.

Their individual identities, locations, and agendas are not documented anywhere in either kingdom. This is partly because no one knows to document them, and partly because several of the oldest ones have been careful about what gets written down.
