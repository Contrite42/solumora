[[Selvane]] operates inside [[Solhaven]] as a long-horizon institutional parasite: never central, always adjacent to systems that matter. His daily routine is built around proximity windows to administrative infrastructure rather than social status.

He maintains a moving triad of access points: one trade-facing office, one records-handling corridor, and one logistics-adjacent site with predictable staff traffic. He cycles these every six to nine weeks so no single workplace pattern can be statistically profiled against recurring failures.

His interference method is low-amplitude and cumulative. Rather than causing obvious outages, he introduces timing faults in [[Sigils]] and token-linked processes: delayed message registration, intermittent archive checksum errors, and synchronization drift in shared ledgers. Any single event looks mundane. The aggregate effect is policy friction at moments that benefit his information priorities around [[Ancient Ruins]] expeditions.

Selvane avoids same-day disruption clusters. He caps visible anomalies per district and allows cooling intervals that match normal bureaucratic malfunction rates. He treats "plausible incompetence" as a stronger mask than perfect reliability or dramatic sabotage.

His risk protocol includes weekly route randomization between district corridors and strict avoidance of repeat contact with high-memory social actors. If a clerk begins recognizing him too quickly, he rotates identity layer and abandons that corridor for one quarter.
