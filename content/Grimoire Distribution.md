The physical movement of grimoires from [[Grimoire Copyists|Scribe]] to end user is a supply chain problem that both kingdoms have solved differently, with the same result: [[Common Grimoire|Common]]-tier texts reach most of the population reliably, and the distribution cost is embedded in the price difference between what Scribes charge and what end users pay.

## Terravelle's Distribution Network

In [[Terravelle]], grimoire distribution follows the same infrastructure as general commercial goods. The [[Trade Guilds]] operate an authentication and certification system, but they do not run the distribution directly — that is handled by booksellers, market traders, and the traveling merchant circuits that serve rural areas.

The supply chain typically runs: Scribe produces certified copies → bookseller buys in bulk for resale → market or shop sale to end user. In cities like [[Valdenmoor]] and [[Ashford]], this chain is short and competitive. Rural areas rely on traveling merchants who include certified Common copies in their mixed cargo because the demand is reliable and the weight-to-value ratio is favorable.

[[Sable Morn]]'s route journal documents the commercial calculus: Common grimoire copies are not the highest-margin item in a traveling merchant's cargo, but they are among the most consistent performers. Almost every settlement needs replacements for worn copies and occasionally wants new spells. The markup covers the carrying cost with margin to spare.

## Auralis's Distribution Network

[[Auralis]] runs a more differentiated system. Common and [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]] texts circulate through commercial channels similar to Terravelle's, though without the guild certification layer. Quality signaling relies on seller reputation and informal Scholar-network vouching rather than a certification mark.

The institutional channel is more significant in Auralis than in Terravelle: the [[The Advancement Corps]] and affiliated institutions distribute Common copies to practitioners in their networks as a patronage benefit, which creates a parallel supply chain alongside the commercial market. For practitioners with institutional affiliation, grimoire access is partly managed through that relationship rather than purchased independently.

Rural distribution in [[Auralis]] is less developed than in Terravelle, partly because Auralis's population is more concentrated in urban centers and partly because the institutional channel does not extend well to rural areas. Rural practitioners in Auralis often rely on the same traveling merchant circuits as rural Terravelle, just accessing different goods.

## The Desert Route Problem

Cross-kingdom grimoire distribution faces the obstacle of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]. Moving texts from Terravelle to Auralis or vice versa requires a desert crossing, which adds cost and time that makes formal commercial distribution economically unattractive for most Common-tier spells. The result is that the two kingdoms' grimoire markets are largely independent — spells developed in one kingdom are available in the other primarily through the [[Black Market Grimoires|informal channel]], not through formal commercial distribution.

The [[Relay Compact]] handles some inter-kingdom grimoire movement as part of its general cargo service, without asking questions about the contents. The [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]] carries expedited courier cargo that occasionally includes texts whose provenance is not examined at either end.

## Physical Format

Grimoires range from single-spell cards — a tile or small piece of treated paper with one sigil, the cheapest distribution unit — to bound multi-spell collections. The card format is dominant in the Common market because it allows practitioners to acquire exactly the spells they need without paying for a bound collection of which they only want three spells.

Bound collections are sold as curated sets: household utility packs, workshop specialty sets, expedition survival sets. [[WrittenWorks/The Zakros Crossing Compendium|Avel Coss's crossing compendium]] represents the expedition end of this market — a curated selection of desert-specific spells packaged for a specific use case. The value is in the curation, which substitutes for the buyer's knowledge of what they will actually need.
