Grimoire Rights and Restrictions is the legal and contractual framework governing who may copy, own, and distribute specific [[Grimoires]]. Not all grimoires are freely reproducible. Some are held under restrictive agreements that limit who may create copies, where those copies can be sold, or who is permitted to purchase them. Rights and restrictions exist at multiple levels: institutional, guild, authorial, and governmental.

## Institutional Control

Many [[Rare Grimoire|higher-tier]] grimoires are held in institutional libraries that control copying rights. A [[Flux Users|Scholar]] in [[Auralis]] seeking to copy a specific [[Rare Grimoire]] text must negotiate with the holding institution — often the [[Council of Auralis]] or a major research academy — for permission. Permission is usually conditional: the copy must remain within the institution, or the Scholar must agree not to reproduce it further, or copies may only be made for approved research purposes.

In [[Terravelle]], institutional control is less stringent but still present. The [[Trade Guilds]] maintain authenticated source libraries in [[Valdenmoor]] and restrict copying of certain texts to guild-registered scriveners. Private buyers can commission copies, but the scrivener must be guild-affiliated and the copy must meet inspection standards.

## Authorial Rights

When a [[Flux Users|Flux user]] develops a new spell and documents it in a grimoire, they hold the initial rights to its distribution. They may sell outright ownership, license the grimoire for copying under specific terms, or retain exclusive control. Authorial rights are enforceable in both kingdoms, though enforcement mechanisms differ.

In [[Terravelle]], authorial rights are treated as property and enforced through [[Trade Guilds|guild]] arbitration. A scrivener caught copying a rights-protected grimoire without permission faces fines and potential suspension. In [[Auralis]], enforcement is more inconsistent — wealthier authors can pursue legal action through the [[Council of Auralis]], but smaller practitioners often lack the resources to stop unauthorized copying.

Most authorial rights agreements expire after a set period (commonly 10-20 years) or upon the author's death. After expiration, the grimoire becomes freely reproducible unless the rights were sold to an institution or guild.

## Guild Restrictions

The [[Guild Scrivener Network]] uses restrictions to control which texts circulate at what tiers. A scrivener must hold certification to copy [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]] or higher-tier texts. Certification is granted after demonstrating sufficient [[Control Tier]] capability, clean work history, and payment of annual fees.

This creates a bottleneck: even if a buyer can afford a [[Rare Grimoire]] copy, they may struggle to find a certified scrivener willing to produce it. Higher-tier certifications are expensive to maintain, and most guild scriveners focus on [[Common Grimoire]] work where volume is higher and risk is lower.

## Governmental Restriction

Both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] maintain short lists of grimoires that are legally restricted. In [[Terravelle]], restrictions are pragmatic: spells that pose significant public safety risk (e.g., large-scale destructive sigils) or are associated with historical misuse. Restricted grimoires can still be copied and owned with special permits, usually granted for institutional research.

In [[Auralis]], restrictions are broader and include certain mind-affecting spells, soul-targeting sigils, and grimoires deemed politically sensitive. Ownership is not illegal, but copying and distribution require approval from the [[Council of Auralis]]. Enforcement is inconsistent — wealthy families often possess restricted texts through private inheritance, and enforcement focuses on commercial distribution rather than personal ownership.

## Violations and Enforcement

Violations typically involve unauthorized copying rather than possession. A scrivener copying a rights-protected grimoire without permission faces guild penalties (fines, suspension) and potential legal action from the rights holder. Buyers commissioning unauthorized copies are rarely prosecuted unless the grimoire is also governmentally restricted.

Cross-border enforcement is harder. A grimoire with protected rights in [[Terravelle]] may be freely copied in [[Auralis]] if Auralis law doesn't recognize the restriction. Guild agreements attempt to harmonize enforcement, but gaps exist, and some scriveners exploit them intentionally.

## Black Market Interaction

Rights and restrictions drive much of the [[Black Market Grimoire Circulation]]. Buyers who can't obtain a grimoire legally — either because no certified scrivener will copy it, or because the cost is prohibitive, or because the text is entirely restricted — turn to underground networks. Violations are common but rarely prosecuted unless the grimoire is used in a crime.
