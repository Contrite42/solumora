[[Geometry Pattern]] defines the spell’s _spatial footprint_—how its effect occupies space when it manifests. Pattern does not decide what the spell does (that comes from [[Hook]], [[Mode]], [[Discipline]], and [[Output Mode]]); Pattern decides **where and in what shape** the effect exists.

When Geometry Pattern is not specified by the spell’s [[Shape]], it defaults to Plane.

## Patterns (Scholar Standard)

- Plane (default)  
   The effect fills the written space of the sigil (the page/surface area of the rune). Plane spells are stable and common in [[Grimoires]] because the geometry is anchored to something physical.
  ![[Pattern__PLANE 1.png]]
- Point  
   The effect manifests at a single spot. Often used for precision work, marking, ignition, and “needle” effects.
  ![[Pattern__POINT 1.png]]
- Beam  
   A continuous line of effect in one direction. Common for cutting, pushing, heating, illumination, and line-of-force applications.
  ![[Pattern__BEAM 1.png]]
- Cone  
   A spreading fan of effect. Used for dispersal, crowd control, scanning, and broad short-range influence.
  ![[Pattern__CONE 1.png]]
- Sphere  
   A 3D ball of effect centered on a target or point. Used for bursts, area denial, and “bubble” zones.
  ![[Pattern__SPHERE 1.png]]
- Cylinder  
   A column or tube of effect (vertical or horizontal depending on casting context). Used for pillars, corridors, containment tubes, and directed zones.
  ![[Pattern__CYLINDER 1.png]]
- Ring  
   A perimeter boundary with an inside/outside distinction. Used for wards, thresholds, containment circles, and “do-not-cross” effects.
  ![[Pattern__RING 1.png]]
- Field  
   A general area of influence across a region. Used for sustained zones, rules-based regions (especially with Ward or Filter), and environmental control.
  ![[Pattern__FIELD 1.png]]

## Pattern and anchoring

- If [[Reach]] and [[Target Spec]] are omitted (low-control shapes), Pattern tends to manifest **at Where Written** by default.
- As more variables are explicitly controlled (larger [[Shape]]), Pattern can be placed more deliberately: centered on an Individual, projected to a location, or linked to a Marked point.

## Practical notes

- Plane is the most common pattern for mass-produced spells because it is easy to test, safe to mount on objects, and hard to mis-aim.
- Sphere and Field become dangerous quickly without clear [[Target Spec]] or [[Reach]]—they’re powerful but require better control.
