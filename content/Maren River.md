The Maren River is Terravelle's primary inland water artery, running from the elevated northern belt through [[Valdenmoor]] and onward toward eastern export channels linked to [[Ashford]].

## Function

The river is the logistical backbone for bulk movement that would be prohibitively expensive by overland cart at Terravelle scale. Grain, timber, fabric feedstock, and bonded mixed cargo all move through river staging coordinated by the [[Maren Freight Chain]].

Seasonal variation drives planning discipline. Spring surge expands capacity but increases hazard; late-summer shallows reduce throughput and force tighter dispatch windows.

## Political and Economic Weight

Control and maintenance of Maren infrastructure is one of the reasons [[Valdenmoor]] holds disproportionate administrative influence in Terravelle's commercial policy. Flood-wall sigils, crossing points, and bonded storage along the river determine whether the kingdom experiences price stability or abrupt scarcity.

Rural communities in [[Maren Valley]] depend on river continuity for both income and supply resilience. Urban districts in Valdenmoor depend on the same continuity for labor demand and food security.

## Cultural Note

Terravellian attitudes toward proportionality and reliability are reinforced by river life. Overcasting to solve one immediate river problem can create a downstream failure with higher total cost; founding-era institutions learned this repeatedly, and modern guild norms still reflect that memory.
