**Wolfpoint** is a settlement in the far north of [[Solumora]], situated well beyond the cultural and political reach of either [[Terravelle]] or [[Auralis]]. It sits in a region of constant mild cold — never freezing, never warm — where the temperature holds near 45 degrees year-round and the wind comes off open terrain with nothing to slow it. It is not a hospitable place. It is, however, a productive one.

Wolfpoint is the only known center of [[Hypertext]] development, and has been for seven generations.

## The Voss Line

Wolfpoint was founded by the Voss family — a lineage of soldier scholars who had spent careers inscribing [[Sigils]] onto armour, blades, and field equipment, and grown deeply frustrated with what the sigil system could not do.

The problem was geometric. A Circle sigil requires six precise outer points and a clean center. It assumes a flat surface, adequate light, and time. None of those conditions reliably exist on a battlefield. Curved pauldrons, riveted chainlinks, sword grips wrapped in leather — surfaces that resisted clean inscription and punished any deviation in the geometry. The Shape system had been designed for pages and stone, not for tools that moved and flexed and got hit.

The Voss founders did not abandon the Scholar symbol vocabulary. They abandoned the shape. If the variables could be encoded in a fixed geometric form, they reasoned, they could also be encoded in a sequence — a line of instructions read left to right, written in the same symbols, requiring no outer points, no center, no shape at all. Just logic, written directly onto whatever surface was available.

## Culture

Wolfpoint retains its military heritage in everything. The settlement is organized around discipline and function — buildings are low and wind-resistant, layouts are practical, nothing is built for display. The Voss line still leads, though leadership is earned within the family through demonstrated mastery of Hypertext rather than birth order alone. A Voss who cannot write a functioning Hypertext sequence does not lead Wolfpoint. This has produced, over seven generations, a remarkably competent ruling family.

[[Flux]] use here is intensely practical. The constant cold means Heat discipline work is embedded in daily life — armour sigils, building seals, workshop heating — and this has made Wolfpoint's practitioners unusually precise with thermal [[Spell Variables|variables]]. The military tradition means Force and Binding discipline work is equally mature. Mind and Soul disciplines are studied but regarded with the particular caution of people who have seen what happens when experimental spellwork goes wrong in a confined settlement far from help.

The population is small — a few hundred permanent residents, with a rotating body of practitioners who come to study and eventually leave, taking partial knowledge of Hypertext back to wherever they came from. The Voss line has never tried to prevent this. The spreading of Hypertext is considered a success condition, not a threat.

## Relationship with the Kingdoms

Wolfpoint knows [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] exist. It trades with both, maintains occasional correspondence, and has no interest in being absorbed by either.

[[Terravelle]] is aware of Wolfpoint and has filed it, characteristically, as a thing that is not yet their problem. The pragmatic assessment is roughly correct — Wolfpoint produces nothing Terravelle needs urgently, and the distance makes administration impractical. If [[Hypertext]] ever becomes a trade commodity, that assessment will change.

[[Auralis]] is considerably more interested. The existence of a casting method that operates outside the Shape system, developed independently over seven generations by a family of soldier scholars, is exactly the kind of thing Auralis [[Flux Users|Scholars]] want access to. There have been formal correspondence attempts. The Voss line has been polite, unhurried, and noncommittal. Wolfpoint has survived seven generations in the far north by being careful about what it gives away and to whom.

## Hypertext Development

What Wolfpoint has built over seven generations is not a finished system. [[Hypertext]] is still being developed — new symbol sequences are being tested, failure modes are being catalogued, and the limits of what a linear instruction set can express are still being mapped. The Voss line treats this as normal. A system that took seven generations to get here will likely take seven more to mature.

The current head of the Voss line, [[The Voss Line|Sera Voss]], is the first to formally document Hypertext sequences in transmissible form — a [[Grimoires|grimoire]] equivalent that can be copied and taught rather than passed down through direct instruction. Whether this document ever reaches [[Auralis]] is a question the current Voss head has not yet answered.

## Geopolitical Position

Wolfpoint's independence rests on being more valuable as an independent knowledge center than as a territory belonging to either kingdom. Both kingdoms know this. Both kingdoms manage their relationships with the settlement accordingly — [[Terravelle]] through the standing envoy relationship that [[Keln Varost]] manages from [[Greyveil]], [[Auralis]] through the research relationships and the indirect connection that [[Drest]] maintains with [[Mira Solv]].

The strategic dimensions of this situation — why both kingdoms want Hypertext access, what they have tried, what Wolfpoint fears — are examined in [[The Wolfpoint Question]].

What Wolfpoint actually sends out into the world — physical exports through the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]], and the knowledge exports that are its primary currency — is documented in [[Wolfpoint Exports]].

## People in Motion

Wolfpoint's geopolitical weight is carried by a small number of highly connected people rather than by population size. [[Sera Voss]] controls pace and scope of formal Hypertext disclosure. [[Ysel Voss]] and [[Mira Solv]] shape internal interpretation and selective external release. [[Keln Varost]] translates Terravellian priorities into a sustained envoy relationship that avoids direct pressure but preserves access.

Outside elite channels, practitioners and craftspeople such as [[Vel Sorvn]] and [[Renn Cald]] keep the settlement operational under climate and distance constraints. Their continuity work is what allows the Scholarium layer to focus on long-horizon development.
