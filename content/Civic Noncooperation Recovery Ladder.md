# Civic Noncooperation Recovery Ladder

The **Civic Noncooperation Recovery Ladder** is a staged response model used when communities remain formally nonviolent but suspend functional cooperation with coexistence institutions (permit flows, school reporting, grievance channels, district data exchange). Adopted thirty months after the [[Stories/The First Casualty|first casualty crisis]], it addresses legitimacy erosion that occurs without open confrontation.

## Problem Statement

After major incidents, many districts did not riot or rebel. They simply stopped participating. This produced:
- Data blindness for enforcement and mediation systems
- Silent service denial through unattended administrative queues
- Escalation risk when institutions interpreted silence as hidden threat

The Ladder treats noncooperation as an early-warning governance failure rather than immediate criminal signal.

## Ladder Stages

**Stage 0 - Baseline Drift**
- Participation decline under threshold levels
- Response: local outreach and metric watch only

**Stage 1 - Procedural Silence**
- Multi-channel participation drops beyond continuity floor
- Response: open acknowledgment hearing + temporary service continuity desk

**Stage 2 - Structured Refusal**
- Coordinated withholding of filings, permits, or compliance reports
- Response: joint mediation teams + protected complainant channels + published resolution timelines

**Stage 3 - Institutional Freeze Risk**
- Critical civic systems (schools, transit licensing, guild attestations) no longer interoperable
- Response: emergency continuity authority with independent observers and mandatory public dashboard updates

**Stage 4 - Destabilization Window**
- Cross-district cascading refusals threaten treaty-level operations
- Response: Review Board threshold case and temporary cross-border support deployment

## Design Principles

- Noncooperation is information, not proof of malice.
- Repair capacity must scale faster than institutional suspicion.
- Public timelines reduce rumor-driven escalation.
- Coercive tools are delayed until repair lanes are demonstrably exhausted.

## Field Use

[[Jess Maren]]'s Ashford teams used Stage 1 and Stage 2 responses repeatedly during post-sweep trust dips, showing faster recovery than districts that escalated directly to sanction posture.

The Ladder is now paired with the [[Emergency Reintegration Mandate]] in training curricula for Commission and Tribunal operators.
