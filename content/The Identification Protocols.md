_(Institutional Violence, Identity Identification)_

**The Identification Protocols** describes the systematic methods developed during [[The Ancient Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] by which religious authorities and state institutions identified individuals suspected of being [[High Demons|High Demons]], the characteristics targeted by identification frameworks, and how standard identification procedures produced high false-positive rates while successfully eliminating actual demons approximately 20-30% of the time. The protocols are historically significant because they demonstrate how institutional frameworks designed for security threat identification become persecution apparatus when false-positives are ignored and institutional investment in continuing procedures prevents course correction. Understanding the protocols is crucial for understanding why [[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] face such danger from revelation: if institutional authorities developed identification frameworks during Persecution Era with 80% false-positive rate, modern institutional authorities would likely develop similar frameworks with similar flaws, creating probability that both demons and innocent humans would be identified and executed on same pretextual basis.

## Core Identification Characteristics

Persecution Era identification protocols targeted seven characteristic categories:

### Physical Anomalies

**Age Impossibility**: Individuals who appeared significantly younger than official records indicated, or whose physical aging rate diverged from normal human progression. Persecution logic: demons don't age normally, apparent youth combined with stated age indicated non-human biology.

**False Positive Rate**: Medical conditions (certain hereditary conditions creating appearance of youthfulness), environmental factors (sun avoidance creating pale healthy appearance), psychological factors (some individuals maintaining excellent physical condition through discipline), and simple record error (birth dates mistranscribed) all created apparent age inconsistencies. Estimated 15-20% of flagged individuals were innocent.

**Non-Nutritional Sustainability**: Individuals observed to have limited food intake despite apparent physical health. Persecution logic: demons obtain energy through means other than food consumption, therefore unusual eating patterns indicated non-human nature.

**False Positive Rate**: Medical conditions (low-metabolism conditions, digestive disorders), cultural practices (religious dietary restrictions, ascetic practices), psychological factors (eating disorders, trauma-related appetite changes) all created patterns of limited food intake. Estimated 25-30% of flagged individuals were innocent.

**Unusual Wound Healing**: Individuals observed to recover from injuries with unusual speed or without visible scarring. Persecution logic: demon healing capacity exceeds human biological range.

**False Positive Rate**: Medical conditions (hereditary rapid-healing phenotypes), careful wound care (some individuals practiced better hygiene and treatment than contemporaries), and observer error (difficulty assessing healing timeline across months/years) created appearance of unusual recovery. Estimated 30-40% of flagged individuals were innocent.

### Behavioral Anomalies

**Emotional Distance**: Individuals demonstrating unusual difficulty with normal human emotional attachments or social integration. Persecution logic: demons lack human emotional capacity, therefore apparent emotional distance indicated non-human nature.

**False Positive Rate**: Personality disorders, trauma responses, neurodivergence (what modern medicine identifies as autism spectrum conditions), cultural differences in emotional expression, and simple introversion all created patterns interpreted as emotional distance. Estimated 60-70% of flagged individuals were innocent. This was highest false-positive category.

**Temporal Disassociation**: Individuals who appeared confused about current year/date, made historical references inappropriate for their apparent age, or demonstrated knowledge of past events suggesting longer lifespan than should be possible.

**False Positive Rate**: Education (some individuals were extensively trained in history), travel (experienced travelers had knowledge of distant places), oral tradition (some communities maintained detailed historical narratives), and occasional genuine memory confusion (normal human condition, sometimes interpreted as anomalous). Estimated 40-50% of falsely flagged individuals were innocent.

**Unusual Knowledge**: Demonstration of technical expertise, scholarly achievement, or specialized knowledge inconsistent with apparent background. Persecution logic: demon knowledge exceeds human capability acquisition.

**False Positive Rate**: Natural talent, excellent training, autodidactic learning, apprenticeship to master practitioners, and simple fortunate circumstances of education and opportunity all created unusual expertise. Estimated 50-60% of flagged individuals were innocent.

### Social Anomalies

**Community Isolation**: Individuals who maintained minimal social ties despite living in community, spent extensive time alone, had few demonstrated friendships, or seemed separate from communal structures.

**False Positive Rate**: Personality traits (introversion, social anxiety), life circumstances (recent arrivals, recent loss of family/community), professional requirements (some work required solitude or isolation), and simple preference for solitude all created social isolation patterns. Estimated 55-65% of flagged individuals were innocent.

**Unusual Economic Position**: Individuals demonstrating unexpected wealth not clearly connected to visible occupation, maintaining mysterious funding sources, or conducting commerce across unusual trade networks.

**False Positive Rate**: Inheritance, successful business acumen, prior accumulation from previous location, illicit economic activity (commercial work across trade networks included smuggling), and simple observer assumption that wealth wasn't legitimately earned. Estimated 25-35% of flagged individuals were innocent (though some were engaged in illicit activity unrelated to demon status).

**Behavioral Consistency**: Individuals whose behavior remained unchanged across decades despite normal human life progression - same habits, same routines, same personality despite aging - when viewed across extended observation periods.

**False Positive Rate**: Personality rigidity (common in humans), religious/philosophical commitment to consistency, fear of change, conservative temperament, and observer confirmation bias (remembering consistent patterns while forgetting changes). Estimated 45-55% of flagged individuals were innocent.

## The Interrogation Framework

Identified suspects underwent religious inquisition-style questioning:

**Knowledge Testing**: Questions designed to assess whether suspect possessed knowledge that shouldn't be available to humans. Typical questions:
- "Describe what you remember of the Attainment Wars" (occurred 170 years prior, asking if suspect remembers events they shouldn't have witnessed)
- "What languages do you speak and where did you learn them?" (assessing whether linguistic knowledge was excessive)
- "Describe your family history and where you came from" (looking for inconsistencies suggesting assumed identity)

**Values Testing**: Questions designed to assess whether suspect held values inconsistent with normal human morality. Typical questions:
- "Do you demonstrate appropriate attachment to human family members?" (assessing emotional distance)
- "Do you participate in religious practices of our community?" (assessing community integration)
- "What is your position on proper human lifespan?" (assessing whether suspect acknowledged normal human mortality)

**The Torture Problem**: Many interrogations included torture. Under torture, innocent individuals would often admit to unusual characteristics or behaviors they were accused of. Torture "confirmations" were treated as reliable evidence of demon status despite being torture-induced confessions.

**False Positive Mechanism**: Interrogation framework was designed to identify demons through knowledge of facts they shouldn't know. But interrogators didn't always know what facts were reasonable for educated humans to know. Unusual knowledge from education, travel, or training was interpreted as demon-indicating. Confessions under torture were treated as evidence despite being coerced statements from desperate innocent people.

## The Execution Determination

Religious authorities made final determination of demon status based on interrogation results:

**Rubber-Stamp Approval**: Most religious authorities rubber-stamped executability recommendations from state interrogators. Estimated 85-90% of individuals flagged through identification protocols were determined to be demons and executed.

**Rare Objections**: Some religious authorities exercised veto power and prevented executions when they judged evidence insufficient. These authorities saved approximately 10-15% of those flagged - preventing perhaps 10-20 innocent executions and preventing approximately 2-4 actual demon executions.

**Standards Drift**: Over 45-year persecution period, standards for "sufficient evidence" gradually declined. Early-period executions required more substantial evidence of demon characteristics. Later-period executions occurred with minimal evidence - single suspicious characteristic often sufficient for death penalty.

## What The Protocols Actually Detected

Historical analysis of persecution records (conducted ~250 years after persecution ended) reveals:

**True Positives**: Approximately 3-5 individuals executed were likely actual [[High Demons|High Demons]]. The identification protocols accidentally identified demons approximately 20-30% of the time, usually through combination of multiple markers (unusual longevity appearance + knowledge suggesting centuries of experience + lack of normal human attachments).

**False Positives**: Approximately 75-117 individuals executed were innocent humans. The protocols identified innocent humans approximately 70-80% of the time, usually through single suspicious characteristic misinterpreted as demon-indicator.

**Missed Demons**: Approximately 3-4 actual [[High Demons|High Demons]] survived persecution through concealment and avoidance. They were never flagged because they successfully hid characteristic patterns. Some demons' survival strategy: complete social withdrawal (no community presence, minimal human contact), extreme caution about knowledge display (avoiding demonstration of unusual knowledge), and constant relocation (never staying in one community long enough for observation-based identification).

## The Protocol Failure Pattern

Why did protocols fail systematically?

**Confirmation Bias**: Once someone was identified as "suspected demon," all subsequent observations were interpreted through that lens. Innocent person's introversion was "evidence of emotional distance." Innocent person's scholarly knowledge was "evidence of demon learning." Innocent person's health was "evidence of unnatural vitality."

**False Positive Acceptance**: Authorities accepted that protocols would produce false positives (innocent executions) as acceptable cost of occasionally identifying genuine demons. The cost-benefit analysis was simply wrong: wrong by factor of 15-20 in false-positive vs true-positive ratio.

**Institutional Sunk Cost**: As persecution continued, institutions became invested in framework's legitimacy. Admitting high false-positive rate would mean admitting institutions had executed innocent people and confiscated property illegally. Institutional self-preservation required continuing to execute more people to "confirm" original framework was correct.

**Lack of External Correction**: No external authority had power to stop persecution or demand higher evidence standards. Religious authority that developed protocols was same authority supposed to veto executions. Power was concentrated in persecution-implementing agencies.

## Why This Still Matters

The protocols demonstrate that identification frameworks for supernatural threat categories produce catastrophic false-positive rates when:
1. Characteristics targetted are actually normal human variation
2. Confirmation bias interprets all evidence through lens of assumed guilt
3. Institutional incentives reward continuing identification
4. No external authority can force correction

If modern [[Auralis]] or [[Terravelle]] institutions attempted to develop demon-identification frameworks in response to revelation that [[High Demons]] exist:

**Likely Targets**: The actual characteristic patterns that identify [[High Demons]] (genuine centuries-scale aging, genuine non-nutritional energy sourcing, genuine emotional difference from human baseline) would be targeted alongside many false-positive characteristics (introversion, unusual education, good health, ascetic practice, cultural difference).

**Likely False-Positive Rate**: Even with modern institutional sophistication, false-positive rate would likely approach 50-70%. Genuine demons might be identified 30-40% of the time.

**Likely Outcome**: Execution apparatus would target both actual demons and innocent humans at ratio of approximately 2:1 to 5:1 innocent to guilty.

The Persecution Era didn't represent unique institutional evil. It represented institutional rationalization of persecution frameworks that were fundamentally designed to produce false positives because true positives (actual demons) were rare and hard to identify reliably.

Modern institutions trying to address demon presence would make same fundamental error: designing protocols that catch innocent humans at much higher rate than actual demons.

[[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] attempting revelation without preventative institutional framework ensuring identification protocols never develop would be ensuring that revelation results in persecution.

The historical precedent isn't just warning. It's blueprint for what comes next if nothing prevents it.
