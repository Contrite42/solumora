_(Archaeological Field Technician, Ancient Ruins)_

**Kess Vorn** is a T3 field technician at the [[Solden Archive]], age 29, who has spent six years doing the hands-on work that makes archaeological research possible: excavation prep, structural stabilization, material transport, equipment maintenance, and the thousand practical details that scholars don't think about. She knows the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] through touch and repetition — which stones bear weight, which chambers echo with Flux resonance, which inscriptions were carved versus which were formed through some process no one's figured out yet. When the war makes the ruins irrelevant to everyone except as strategic positions, she's one of the few people who realizes the ancient site might hold literal answers about what's worth fighting over.

## Background

Kess came to ruins work by accident. She was working salt extraction in the northern desert edge after dropping out of [[Crestward Flux Institute]]'s Applied Studies program (couldn't afford continued tuition, couldn't stomach competitive academic culture). A [[Solden Archive]] expedition needed local guides familiar with desert conditions. She took the job for three months' wages. She stayed because the work was interesting and the ruins were strange in ways that kept unfolding.

She's not a scholar. She doesn't have theoretical training in pre-modern civilization or advanced [[Flux]] artifact analysis. What she has is six years of direct physical contact with structures built by people who understood something fundamental that current society has forgotten. She's mapped collapsed sections, reinforced unstable chambers, transported fragmentary inscriptions that scholars said were too damaged to move without destroying them (she moved them anyway, successfully, using techniques she developed through trial).

The scholars publish papers. Kess keeps the site accessible. The scholars get institutional recognition. Kess gets paid enough to keep doing work she finds meaningful. It's not an equal arrangement, but she's made peace with it.

Until the war starts and the scholars evacuate, leaving everything behind because [[Auralis]] needs their Flux expertise for military applications and the ruins are suddenly low-priority.

## The Abandonment

Three days after the [[Thel Reach]] incursion, [[Solden Archive]] receives an institutional directive: all T3+ personnel are to report to [[Crestward Flux Institute]] for reassignment to military support roles. Research operations at non-strategic sites are suspended indefinitely. The [[Solden Archive]] site manager, [[Trell Hess]], reads the directive during morning briefing and then spends six hours arguing with [[Crestward Flux Institute|Crestward]] administration before accepting that the order isn't discretionary.

The scholars pack essential documents and samples. The equipment stays behind — too massive to move quickly, not critical to war effort. Kess is offered evacuation transport because her T3 certification makes her technically eligible for military reassignment. She refuses. Someone needs to maintain the site or six years of stabilization work will collapse, and no one's offering to pay her a maintenance wage to stay behind when everyone else is leaving.

[[Trell Hess]] offers to pay her personally — he has research grant savings and feels guilty about abandoning the site. Kess accepts six months' wages and a promise that if the war ends, there will still be a position for her when research operations resume. Then the scholars leave and she's alone with the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] and approximately two hundred tons of excavated material that no one's coming back for anytime soon.

She spends the first week doing routine maintenance. She spends the second week going through the scholars' hasty cataloging to see what they left unprocessed. She spends the third week realizing the scholars never actually understood what they were excavating.

## What She Knows

Kess has physically handled material that scholars only examined through controlled settings:

- She knows the [[Flux]] resonance in the central chamber changes pattern every forty-three days on a cycle no one's documented because scholars only visit quarterly
- She knows certain inscription sequences generate heat when touched in specific order — not dangerous heat, but measurable, which means they're active Flux constructs, not decorative carvings
- She knows the structure's geometry isn't random; it's organized around a central axis that points toward [[Vren Waystation]]'s deep research bore, which means ancient civilization and contemporary anomalous sites are connected spatially
- She knows the fragmented inscriptions in Archive Chamber Seven describe something that translates poorly but seems to be instructions for "recursive currency stabilization" — which might be related to how the [[Watts|Watts ledger]] still functions without maintenance
- She knows the scholars were obsessed with finding a completion mechanism that would let them activate something, when the actual structure seems designed around termination protocols

Most importantly, she knows the ruins. Not as an intellectual puzzle, but as a physical space with behaviors and patterns. When she's alone in the central chamber at night, conducting routine resonance checks, she can feel the structure doing something — subtle [[Flux]] activity that doesn't register on standard detection instruments because it's too distributed and too efficient to create the waste-heat signatures instruments look for.

She's started documenting this herself. Not in scholarly format — she doesn't have that training. In field notes with measurements, timestamps, and her own observations about what the structure feels like it's doing versus what scholars assumed it was for.

## Motivation

Kess doesn't have grand political objectives. She's not trying to stop the war single-handedly or make a statement about institutional priorities. What she wants is for someone to understand that the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] might actually be relevant to what's happening, and walking away from them during a crisis caused by [[Flux]] and institutional failure is potentially the worst possible timing.

If the ancient civilization disappeared because they solved something, or because they failed to solve something, either answer matters right now. If the ruins contain functional infrastructure that still operates (like the [[Watts]] system demonstrably does), then maybe there's other functional infrastructure that could help. Or could warn. Or could explain why rift incursions are intensifying in ways that correlate with military Flux usage patterns.

She's not qualified to make those arguments to people in power. But she can document what she's seeing, maintain the site integrity, and hope that someone who is qualified eventually has reason to pay attention.

Also: she's spent six years here. This is her work. Walking away because institutions decided it's not strategic feels like abandoning something that matters just because it's inconvenient to care about.

## Personal Stakes

Kess's father [[Olan Vorn]] is a T2 fishing boat captain operating out of [[Keln Harbor]]. Her younger sister [[Nessa Vorn]] is a T1 apprentice at a [[Valdenmoor]] textile workshop. Both depend on economic stability — fishing relies on trade routes, textile work relies on material imports. War disrupts both.

[[Olan Vorn|Her father]] thinks she should come home, get a normal job, stop "playing in ruins" while the world falls apart. [[Nessa Vorn|Her sister]] thinks what she's doing is important even if no one's paying attention, and keeps asking whether the ruins really hold answers like the stories say.

Kess doesn't know if the ruins hold answers. She knows they hold something, and she knows no one else is positioned to find out because everyone qualified is being conscripted for military support or evacuating to safety.

Someone needs to stay. She's staying. If it matters, she'll have the documentation. If it doesn't matter, at least the site won't collapse from neglect while everyone's distracted.

## Current Status

Kess remains at [[Solden Archive]], conducting solo maintenance and unauthorized research using equipment the scholars left behind. She's been documenting the central chamber's Flux resonance patterns, cross-referencing inscription fragments, and developing a working theory about what the structure was actually designed to do (which is not what the official research literature assumes).

She has no way to communicate this to anyone who could do something with it — the site's communication link was disconnected when research operations suspended, and she doesn't have authorization to access military channels. She's essentially isolated at a desert ruin with possibly crucial information and no way to get it to people who need it.

She's been maintaining the site and waiting to see if anyone comes back. Or if circumstances change enough that someone realizes the ruins might be relevant. Or if she figures out a way to make contact with someone who can understand what she's found.

In the meantime, she's documenting everything. If nothing else, when this is over, someone will have a record of what the structure does during extended unsupervised observation. That's worth something, even if no one knows it yet.
