**Ossan Treml** is a [[Flux]] physician based on the upper periphery of [[Solhaven]]'s [[Crestward District]], certified through the [[Ascendant Path]]'s medical track. He is fifty-one, T4, and has practiced in Solhaven for twenty-two years.

## Practice

Flux medicine at the T4 level covers what lower-tier practitioners cannot reach: deep tissue Flux pathway mapping, complex wound repair that standard healing sigils cannot close, diagnostic work for cases where something has gone wrong with a patient's Flux response itself. These are not common problems, but they are serious when they occur, and the small number of practitioners who can address them commands significant fees and professional respect in roughly equal measure.

Ossan's primary patient population is Crestward. He maintains accounts with a dozen of the district's major households — attending physicians arrangements where a family retains his services for routine assessments, emergencies, and the kind of regular monitoring that Crestward families with political exposure sometimes require. This is the work that pays well and requires discretion.

He is occasionally called into [[The Middling Steps]] for complex cases: industrial Flux exposure injuries from craft workshops, children with unusual Flux pathway development that lower-tier practitioners have flagged, cases where something is clearly wrong and nobody in the Steps' standard medical network has been able to identify what. These calls pay less and require him to work in less well-equipped settings, and he accepts them at about the rate they arrive because he finds the diagnostic variety professionally useful.

He has had indirect contact with the palace medical staff three times in the past decade — not treating Cassia directly, but consulted on staff cases and once on a specialist question that the palace's own physicians wanted an outside opinion on. He knows slightly more about the palace's internal medical arrangements than the palace would prefer any private practitioner to know, and has treated this knowledge with complete discretion.

## Political Position

Ossan holds careful political neutrality as a professional principle. Physicians who treat Crestward families necessarily encounter the fault lines of council politics at close range — patients mention things while under treatment, household staff communicate through implications, and an observant practitioner sees patterns in who is under stress and what kind of stress it is. He has accumulated a working model of the council's current tensions that he keeps entirely private, partly from professional ethics and partly from a practical calculation about what is survivable in his position.

His private view — shared with no patient and no colleague — is that the council's long-term trajectory worries him more than the individual political conflicts that fill the Crestward's social calendar. He has seen the Expansion Faction's pressure on Terravelle expressed in his patients' stress responses over many years, and has come to the conclusion that this is not a situation moving toward resolution.

## Character

Ossan is a careful listener and a slow speaker. He has the particular social quality of people who spend their working lives in rooms where what goes unsaid matters as much as what is said: he is attuned to pauses, to what questions patients avoid answering, to the physical signals of anxiety that patients often believe they are concealing. He applies the same attention in social settings, which means he is considered an excellent dinner guest and a somewhat uncomfortable one.

He has no strong theological relationship with the Path, despite his Path certification. The Path's medical track is a competence credential in Solhaven; it certifies his ability rather than his beliefs, and the beliefs were never particularly strong. He attends the ceremonies that his Crestward patient relationships require and has nothing specific against Path theology. It simply does not touch him.
