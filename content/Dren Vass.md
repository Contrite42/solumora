# Dren Vass

**Dren Vass** coordinates [[Mountain Game Collective]], managing the logistics that convert [[Wolfpoint]]'s northern hunting grounds into reliable preserved meat exports. He is forty-four years old, [[Control Tier|Tier 3]], and has run the Collective for nine years. He is not a hunter — he is a logistics manager who ensures continuous flow: rotating teams stay supplied, field stations maintain processing capacity, and preservation timing prevents bottlenecks.

The Collective generates approximately 25-30% of [[Wolfpoint]]'s physical export revenue and provides the bulk of the settlement's internal meat supply. Dren's job is making sure both functions succeed without one compromising the other.

## Logistics Over Hunting

Dren runs population management protocols refined over seven generations: tracking game health, enforcing seasonal harvest limits, rotating hunting pressure across territories to prevent local depletion. These protocols are why Wolfpoint's meat supply remains consistent while other northern settlements experience boom-bust cycles.

Three hunting teams (4-6 people each) operate year-round on week-long rotations. Dren schedules rotations, manages field station supplies, coordinates transport from upland processing sites to Wolfpoint's central preservation facility, and adjusts harvest quotas seasonally based on population data.

His constraint is ecological: the Collective can only harvest what the hunting grounds sustainably produce. Dren does not push for maximum short-term yield. He manages for consistent multi-year output because ecological collapse would end both export operations and internal food security.

## Coordination with Other Operations

Dren coordinates closely with [[Morra Keld]], who runs preservation operations and depends on salt imported through [[Ashford Supply Contract]]. When packet delays reduce salt availability, Morra operates at reduced capacity. Dren adjusts hunting schedules accordingly  — no point harvesting game the Collective cannot preserve.

He also maintains range-sharing agreements with [[Upland Herding Network]] families. Both operations use overlapping upland territory. Coordination prevents conflict: sheep overgrazing degrades hunting habitat, excessive hunting disrupts grazing patterns. Informal agreements mediated by [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] keep both operations productive.

## Population Data and Strategic Calculation

Dren maintains detailed records: seasonal game population estimates, harvest volumes by territory, reproduction rates, predator pressure signals. This data serves dual purpose:

**Operational:** Guides next season's harvest decisions and territory rotation plans.

**Strategic:** Documents that Collective operations are ecologically sustainable, evidence Wolfpoint can present if either kingdom questions whether the settlement is over-harvesting resources or operating unsustainably.

These records matter because [[Wolfpoint]]'s independence depends partly on demonstrating responsible resource management. Dren's documentation provides that demonstration.

## What Makes This Work

The Collective succeeds because:
- Seven generations of population management built sustainable harvest protocols.
- Wolfpoint's constant 45-degree cold provides climate-based preservation advantages southern settlements cannot replicate.
- Dren manages for long-term consistency rather than short-term maximum yield.
- [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] ensures salt imports remain reliable enough to support preservation operations.

He reviews the population logs. He schedules next week's harvest rotation. He confirms field station supply levels. The meat travels south or enters internal consumption. The cycle continues.
