The Terravelle Crown Service is a small external intelligence and diplomatic support apparatus operating under direct Crown authority. It is not a spy network in any theatrical sense — it is a carefully maintained information-gathering capability that gives [[Terravelle]]'s Crown an independent picture of what is happening in [[Auralis]], along the desert border, and in cross-kingdom commercial channels.

## What It Does

The Service's primary product is intelligence reporting: analysis of [[Expansion Faction]] activity patterns, tracking of unusual Auralis military or logistics movements, commercial intelligence on cross-kingdom trade shifts that might signal political pressure being applied through economic channels. Most of this work is done through human contacts — merchants, travelers, people with commercial relationships on both sides of the desert — rather than through any elaborate technical means.

Secondary function: diplomatic back-channel support. When [[Eddan Voss]]'s formal diplomatic apparatus cannot or should not be the party making contact — when the message is informal, deniable, or exploratory — the Service provides the capacity to communicate without the communication appearing on any official record.

## Structure

The Service is small enough that its entire operational staff could fit in a medium-sized room. A director who reports to the Crown's foreign affairs advisor, a small number of analysts who aggregate reporting from field contacts, and a network of paid informants and contracted intermediaries who are not part of the Service's formal structure and do not know each other.

Field contacts are typically people with legitimate commercial or personal reasons to move between kingdoms. They are recruited carefully, paid reliably, and are under no illusion that they are doing something the other kingdom would approve of. The Service does not ask them to take significant risks. It asks them to notice things and report accurately.

## What It Knows

Currently: the [[Expansion Faction]]'s pressure on Terravelle's border commerce is more organized than it appears from the outside. The documentation requirements that [[Doss Varn]] has noticed as a tone shift at the Ashford command post trace back to a coordinated Auralis regulatory push that has fingerprints consistent with Expansion Faction influence on Council administrative functions. The Service has a working model of this. The Crown has been briefed.

What the Service does not know: [[Drest]]'s current operational timeline. The Service can see the direction of movement without knowing the destination or the schedule. This is a significant gap, and filling it is the active priority.

## Relationship to Other Institutions

The Service has a careful arm's-length relationship with the [[Halveth Cooperative]] — useful contacts for border intelligence, but the Cooperative's political neutrality means it cannot be relied on for Auralis-specific reporting. The [[Bone Road Consortium]] is a more useful channel for commercial intelligence but must be handled carefully, since the Consortium's loyalty is to its own continued operation rather than to either kingdom.

The Service is aware of [[Cassia]]'s position but has no reliable contact with her. Getting accurate information out of the Auralis Council complex is the hardest part of the job.
