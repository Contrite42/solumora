The Ruins Circuit is the informal name for a loose network of independent ruins researchers who work outside [[The Advancement Corps]] sponsorship. It is not an institution — it is a coordination pattern among people who share site data, warn each other about hazardous conditions, and occasionally collaborate on documentation projects, without having any formal relationship or shared governance.

## Who It Includes

The Circuit draws from practitioners who have compelling reasons to study the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins sites]] and equally compelling reasons not to do so through the Corps:

- Independent academics whose theoretical frameworks the Corps has rejected or ignored
- Practitioners from [[Terravelle]] who have no access to Corps sponsorship but have legitimate academic interest in pre-civilization sites
- Former Corps operatives who left and retained their field knowledge
- Collectors and patrons who fund documentation work for private purposes
- The occasional independent traveler who encountered ruins incidentally and developed an interest

[[Tessel Dran]] represents the scholarly end of this distribution — a researcher whose relationship with the Corps is complicated by the fact that her findings have not consistently matched what Corps coordination offices expected to receive. She shares data with Circuit contacts informally, outside the official reporting structure.

## What They Produce

The Circuit's output is inconsistent by nature. Individual researchers produce documentation to their own standards with their own equipment. Some of this is rigorous; some is not. The network's value is in aggregation: observations from a dozen independent sources sometimes reveals patterns that no single researcher's data would show, and the Corps's own reporting is too shaped by institutional priorities to serve this function.

The Circuit maintains an informal archive — not a single physical location, but a distributed set of records, copies of copies, that move through correspondence and personal relationships rather than formal collection. [[Cossa Vrend]], the Wolfpoint archive assistant who specializes in preservation, has organized one node of this informal archive without being explicitly aware of what it is part of.

## Relationship with the Corps

The [[The Advancement Corps|Corps]] regards the Circuit with a mixture of useful contempt and quiet attention. Contempt because the Circuit's lack of institutional structure means it produces inconsistent work that the Corps can dismiss when convenient. Attention because Circuit researchers occasionally find things the Corps missed or find them faster, and Corps coordination offices have been known to quietly incorporate Circuit observations into their own reports without attribution.

The Circuit has no mechanism to demand credit. This is accepted as the cost of operating without institutional backing.

## What They Want

Most Circuit members want the ruins to be studied honestly — without the institutional filtering that shapes what the Corps reports and what gets buried in coordination-office language. They do not have a unified theory of what the ruins mean. They are a research community in the early stages of a problem, not a faction with a conclusion to defend.

The [[The Culmination Faction|Culmination Faction]]'s academic arm, [[Aldric Mourne]], attempts to cultivate Circuit contacts as sources of raw data that can be interpreted through the Culmination framework. Some Circuit researchers find this arrangement useful. Others find it irritating. The Circuit's lack of formal structure means there is no authority to regulate these relationships.
