**Korvel Deep Station** is a research outpost positioned sixteen days south of [[Halveth]] at the deepest surveyed point of kingdom-funded [[Equatorial Desert]] exploration. It exists to study [[Ancient Ruins]] concentration patterns, monitor [[Flux Demons|rift]] activity at desert interior conditions, and test whether permanent human habitation is viable at distances beyond practical rescue range. After four years of operation, the answer to the viability question remains "technically yes, strategically questionable."

## Purpose

The Station was established by [[Auralis]] [[Flux Users|Scholar]] initiative following discovery that ruins density increases toward desert interior rather than decreasing as initial survey models predicted. Someone built more extensively the further south you go, which contradicts the assumption that desert periphery regions were more heavily populated than interior zones. Understanding why requires sustained observation at interior sites, which requires permanent staffing, which requires infrastructure that can sustain staff who cannot retreat to [[Halveth]] if conditions deteriorate.

Korvel Deep Station provides that infrastructure. The facility supports 3-5 researchers on rotating four-month assignments funded through [[The Council of Auralis]] research allocations. Primary research focuses include:
- Ruins cartography and structural analysis at interior concentration zones
- Rift frequency mapping and dimensional boundary stability patterns
- Flux density measurement at distances where settled-region baselines no longer apply
- Long-term environmental monitoring (temperature extremes, wind patterns, particulate composition)

The research output has been valuable enough to justify continued funding despite operational costs that exceed three times the budget for equivalent facilities at desert edge locations.

## Location and Infrastructure

The Station occupies ruins site KD-14, selected for unusual structural preservation: three partially intact interior chambers with ceiling sections that provide overhead cover rare at other surveyed sites. The chambers are reinforced with modern stone construction and integrated [[Grimoires|grimoire]] infrastructure that maintains habitable conditions through continuous [[Flux]] expenditure.

Water system: vapor condensation grimoires feeding sealed underground cisterns (8,000-liter capacity, sufficient for six weeks at full staffing without resupply)

Thermal management: permanent heating/cooling sigils inscribed directly into chamber walls (not portable grimoires — reduces maintenance burden and Flux cost through optimized permanent installation)

Power generation: experimental sustained-channel grimoires drawing directly from desert's elevated ambient Flux (reduces dependence on staff Flux reserves but requires continuous monitoring to prevent channel destabilization)

Supply storage: three months of preserved food, medical supplies, spare equipment rated for interior conditions

Communication: runelight relay system connecting to [[Vren Waystation]] (6-day travel distance) and ultimately to [[Halveth]] (16-day total)

The infrastructure represents significant capital investment deliberately designed for long-duration operation. Early research outposts used portable equipment that failed rapidly under interior conditions. Korvel Deep's permanent installation approach costs more initially but reduces maintenance labor and extends operational lifespan.

## Operational Reality

Staff at Korvel Deep Station experience isolation that makes [[Vren Waystation]] rotations seem socially comfortable by comparison. The nearest human presence is six days' travel, rescue response time from [[Halveth]] is measured in weeks, and evacuation during emergency conditions is not possible — if something goes catastrophically wrong, staff shelter in place and hope reinforcements arrive before supplies run out.

Four-month rotations were determined through trial: shorter rotations don't justify the logistics cost of staff replacement at sixteen-day one-way travel distance; longer rotations produced psychological strain indicators (withdrawal, poor judgment, conflict escalation) that threatened research quality and safety. Current rotation length represents optimized balance between operational efficiency and human limitations.

Staff selection emphasizes technical capability and demonstrated tolerance for isolation. Researchers at Korvel Deep are T3+ minimum (T2 cannot sustain the Flux loads required for independent work in interior desert), experienced with extended field deployments, and psychologically screened for indicators that predict successful adaptation to isolated high-stress environments. Despite screening, roughly 25% of first-rotation staff request early departure or decline subsequent assignments, which creates continuous staffing pressure.

The research work itself provides structure. When you are surrounded by ruins that predate recorded history in density patterns that shouldn't exist, curiosity functions as psychological anchor. Staff maintain detailed observation logs, conduct systematic site surveys, and debate theoretical models of pre-collapse civilization with the intensity of people who have limited entertainment options and unlimited questions.

## Discoveries

Korvel Deep Station's four years of operation have generated findings that justify its existence:

Ruins density patterns indicate interior zones were population centers rather than periphery margins. Whatever civilization built these structures valued desert interior access rather than treating it as barrier to cross. This inverts current assumptions about pre-collapse geography.

Rift frequency at interior locations is 40-60% higher than edge zone measurements, supporting models that dimensional boundary weakness correlates with ambient Flux density rather than being evenly distributed across the desert surface.

The deepest surveyed chambers at KD-14 contain inscriptions using geometric forms that don't match known [[Sigils|sigil]] shapes but show systematic repetition patterns suggesting they are notation rather than decoration. Translation attempts have failed, but the existence of systematic unknown magical notation is itself significant finding.

Desert fauna at interior zones exhibit behavioral differences from edge-zone populations — less opportunistic, more territorial, suggesting ecological stratification where interior zones represent different evolutionary pressure than periphery regions.

These findings feed academic publications, inform crossing route intelligence, and occasionally provoke debates within [[Auralis]] [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] circles about whether more aggressive interior exploration should be funded. The Station's research director sends quarterly reports to [[Solhaven]] academic institutions with enough substance to maintain funding authorization without revealing findings that might trigger territorial control attempts.

## Personnel

**[[Osven Keld]]** has completed six four-month rotations as Station research director — unusual duration indicating either exceptional tolerance for isolation or personality structure that prefers desert interior to civilized society. He is T4, 51 years old, specialist in pre-collapse archaeological analysis,and someone who values the clarity of ruins research over the political complexity of [[Auralis]] Scholar institutional life.

Support researchers rotate more frequently. [[Vella Morn]], T3, has served four rotations focusing on rift stability measurements. [[Cren Tasv]], T3, completed two rotations before requesting permanent reassignment due to isolation strain. Staffing is persistent challenge — maintaining continuity in research program while accommodating the reality that most people cannot or will not tolerate repeated deep desert deployments.

## Survival Incidents

Korvel Deep Station has faced three critical incidents over four years:

Year 2, supply run from [[Vren Waystation]] failed to arrive on schedule due to guide illness. Station implemented rationing protocols and sent emergency signal. Resupply arrived seventeen days late. Staff survived on reduced rations and emergency reserves with eleven days of food remaining when delivery reached the site.

Year 3, sustained-channel grimoire destabilized during maintenance procedure, causing thermal system failure during peak daytime heat. Staff retreated to deepest underground chamber and relied on portable grimoires for 36 hours while lead researcher conducted emergency repair. Repairs succeeded. The incident revealed single-point failure vulnerability in experimental power system design.

Year 4, three-demon rift emergence within station proximity forced full evacuation to emergency shelter site 8 km north. Staff remained at shelter site for five days until demons dispersed. Return to station revealed minor structural damage but no equipment loss. This incident prompted [[Halveth Cooperative]] recommendation that deep interior stations maintain permanent two-site contingency infrastructure rather than relying on rapid evacuation assumptions.

Each incident updated operational protocols and informed future facility design. Korvel Deep Station's operational continuity depends on learning from near-failures before they become total failures.

## Strategic Questions

The Station's existence raises questions [[Auralis]] has not publicly answered: What justifies sustaining deep interior operations at costs that exceed ten times the research value measured by conventional academic metrics? Why maintain presence at locations beyond practical military support range? What does [[Auralis]] expect to find at interior ruins that justifies this investment?

Official position: pure research pursuit, archaeological significance, expanding human knowledge of pre-collapse civilization.

Operational reality: [[Auralis]] funds Korvel Deep Station because someone in [[The Council of Auralis|the Council]] believes understanding what came before both kingdoms matters more than the budget committee admits, and deep desert research is better funded quietly through Scholar allocations than explained publicly where [[Terravelle]] would feel obligated to respond with equivalent investment.

The researchers don't care about the strategic reasoning. They care that funding arrives, supplies reach the station, and they can continue documenting a civilization that shouldn't have existed where it did but manifestly did exist because the ruins are right there to be measured.
