**Miren Thask** is thirty-seven years old and keeps the [[Watts]] ledger accounts for a commercial district credit house in [[Solhaven]]. [[Control Tier|T2]]. Her work is the detailed daily movement of the ledger — verifying transaction records, identifying discrepancies, maintaining the documentation that the credit house's operations produce and that guild review and [[Auralis]] commercial oversight require. She has done this work for eleven years at two institutions, the first smaller and quieter, the current one larger and more complicated.

The [[Watts]] ledger is distributed — no single institution controls it, no record can be altered after the fact, and the history of every transaction is permanent and accessible. Miren's work does not modify the ledger. It interprets it. The difference matters to her in ways she has difficulty explaining to people who have not done this work: the ledger is perfect; the documents that are supposed to match the ledger are made by humans and are therefore imperfect; finding where the documents lie is the actual job, and the ledger is the only reliable reference.

What she has learned over eleven years: most discrepancies are accidents. Scribes transpose numbers; documentation is filed out of order; cargo weights are rounded in ways that accumulate into apparent shortfalls. These are recoverable. A small category of discrepancies are not accidents — they are documentation that was designed to create distance between what happened and what the ledger records. These are also recoverable, but require a different approach, and Miren has developed a working model of what deliberate discrepancy looks like versus accidental discrepancy, which she could not fully articulate but applies accurately.

She has flagged four deliberate discrepancies in eleven years. In two cases, the credit house's senior management agreed with her assessment and acted on it. In two cases, they thanked her and did not act. She has continued to do the work. The ledger records what happens regardless.
