# Trel Vann

**Trel Vann** runs [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]], managing the metalworking operation that produces [[Wolfpoint]]'s premium blade and tool exports. He is fifty-one years old, [[Control Tier|Tier 4]], and has spent twenty-three years refining cold-forge technique — a temperature-differential annealing process that creates harder, longer-lasting edges than standard smithing can match.

He is not a Voss. He leads the Works through demonstrated mastery of the technique, not through political lineage. This is typical of [[Wolfpoint]]'s military heritage: competence determines position, regardless of family connection.

## The Job

Trel's role is production management across multiple competing pressures:
- **Quality maintenance** — every piece that leaves the Works must meet Wolfpoint's reputation standard for edge retention and durability.
- **Capacity constraint** — six smiths working on weeks-long production cycles can only generate approximately 200 finished pieces per year.
- **Market demand** — buyers in [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] would purchase three times current output if the Works expanded.
- **Strategic limitation** — controlled output maintains premium pricing and keeps Wolfpoint's metalwork valuable without being strategically threatening.

Trel does not expand production. Rarity sustains value. Controlled output keeps both kingdoms interested in trade without making [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] worth conquering.

## Cold-Forge Mastery

Cold-forge inverts standard smithing: instead of heating metal to forge temperature and quenching to harden, it cycles pieces repeatedly between Wolfpoint's constant 45-degree ambient cold and precision reheating at lower temperatures. Each cycle compresses the metal's grain structure in ways conventional forging cannot replicate.

Trel knows this technique as embedded practice knowledge — temperature curves for different alloy types, optimal cycle counts for various blade geometries, how to read surface texture that signals micro-fractures before they propagate. His expertise is not written. It is demonstrated through twenty-three years of consistent quality output.

He trains the Works' six smiths through direct instruction: observing their cycle-management decisions, correcting thermal timing errors before they become failures, and gradually building the judgment required to work metal at Wolfpoint's unique climate-enabled process scale.

## Production Decisions

Every quarter, Trel meets with [[Renn Taska]] at [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] to review market demand and pricing signals. Renn provides data: which blade types sold most reliably last quarter, which buyers are requesting commissioned work, what pricing adjustments southern markets might support.

Trel translates market intelligence into production decisions:
- **Catalog allocation** — how many standard blade types to produce for general sale versus commissioned custom work.
- **Output timing** — scheduling production cycles so finished pieces reach [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] cargo windows without saturating packet capacity.
- **Quality thresholds** — determining which imperfect pieces get downgraded to internal-use inventory versus scrapped entirely.

He maintains the Works at steady output: approximately 200 pieces per year, split roughly 60% catalog work / 40% commissioned pieces. This allocation has held stable for six years. Market demand has increased. Trel's output has not.

## Why He Limits Production

Trel could expand the Works: hire additional smiths, increase forge capacity, raise annual output to 400-500 pieces. Market demand supports it. Revenue projections justify it.

He does not expand because he understands the strategic calculation. [[Wolfpoint]]'s independence depends on being valuable enough to trade with, not valuable enough to conquer. If the Works became [[Ashford]]'s largest supplier of premium metalwork, or if [[Auralis]] military forces began depending on Cold-Forge blade supply, Wolfpoint's value would shift from "useful trade partner" to "strategic resource worth controlling."

Volume control maintains safety. The Works produces enough to matter economically, not enough to matter geopolitically.

Renn Taska has explained this logic explicitly during their quarterly reviews. Trel agrees but would limit production anyway — quality control degrades faster than people expect when operations scale beyond what experienced staff can sustain through direct oversight. Expanding the Works would risk reputation damage even if strategic concerns did not exist.

## Relationship with Wolfpoint's Broader Economy

The Works generates approximately 35-40% of Wolfpoint's physical export revenue. [[Renn Taska]] uses this revenue to fund [[Ashford Supply Contract]] relationships that provide grain, salt, and tools Wolfpoint cannot produce internally.

Trel's production decisions directly affect Wolfpoint's import capacity: if cold-forge output declines or quality failures damage market reputation, export revenue drops and supply imports become harder to sustain. He operates with this awareness. Every piece that leaves the Works carries economic weight beyond its individual sale price.

The six smiths working under him also understand: their daily cycle-management decisions aggregate into whether Wolfpoint maintains independent settlement status or becomes economically vulnerable to kingdom control. This creates sustained motivation beyond standard craftwork pride.

## What He Knows About Cold-Forge

Trel has twenty-three years of production data documenting what cold-forge can and cannot do:
- Optimal cycle counts for various alloy compositions (documented in temperature logs maintained at the Works).
- Failure modes when thermal protocols are violated (micro-fracture patterns, brittleness thresholds, surface degradation signatures).
- Theoretical limits on what surfaces cold-forge can work (flat blades succeed reliably, curved armour pieces fail consistently, small precision tools show mixed results).

This knowledge is not secret. It is also not published. A few [[Auralis]] researchers have attempted to reverse-engineer cold-forge by analyzing finished pieces. Their attempts produce partial insights but no successful replication — observing a finished blade does not teach you the seven-week production cycle that created it.

Trel has been asked, twice, by Auralis intermediaries whether he would teach cold-forge technique in exchange for payment or research collaboration. He declined both times, politely. The technique stays in Wolfpoint by design. If southern settlements replicate it, the Works loses its commercial advantage and Wolfpoint loses export revenue required for survival.

## Daily Reality

Trel spends most days at the Works: reviewing pieces in-progress, adjusting forge schedules, resolving technical problems when smiths encounter unexpected material behavior, and maintaining the temperature logs that track every piece's thermal history.

The logs are precise, methodical, and essential: without documented cycle counts and reheat temperatures, quality control becomes guesswork. Trel reviews every log entry personally, catching errors before they propagate into batch failures.

He is not particularly social. His relationships center on work: the six smiths he trains, Renn Taska's quarterly strategy reviews, and occasional coordination with [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] captains about cargo manifests and timing. Wolfpoint's small population makes extensive socializing unnecessary — everyone knows everyone through daily proximity.

He signs the production manifests. He confirms next quarter's catalog allocation. He inspects the forge schedules. The blades travel south. Wolfpoint remains independent.
