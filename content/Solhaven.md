**Solhaven** is the capital of [[Auralis]] and the seat of Queen [[Cassia]]'s nominal rule. It sits on the western coast, built up a series of natural terraces that rise from the harbor, and it is the most vertical city in [[Solumora]] — towers and multi-story structures climbing the terraces, connected by staircases and elevated walkways, the skyline a demonstration of everything [[Auralis]] believes about ambition and upward reach.

It is, genuinely, a remarkable place to look at. Whether it is a comfortable place to live depends almost entirely on your [[Control Tier]].

## Layout and Architecture

Solhaven is organized by elevation in ways that are officially geographic and practically social. The harbor district at the base is commercial — trade, warehousing, transport, the practical work of a port city. As you move up the terraces, the buildings become more elaborate, the [[Sigils]] more complex, the residents more accomplished. The uppermost terrace, the Crestward, is where the palace, the council chambers, and the residences of Auralis's most influential families sit.

The city is dense, loud, and layered. Navigation for newcomers is genuinely difficult — the street grid does not translate across terrace levels, and the elevated walkways have their own numbering system that locals navigate by instinct and outsiders find maddening.

## The Palace

[[Cassia]]'s palace occupies the Crestward's eastern face, designed to catch morning light and be visible from the harbor. It is large, ornate, and functions as both a residence and a performance space — the formal halls are used for the kind of diplomatic and ceremonial events that require a queen to appear capable of commanding a room.

Cassia is capable of commanding a room. This has occasionally caused the council difficulty.

## Population and Character

Solhaven's population approaches a million. It is the largest city in Solumora and the most [[Flux]]-saturated — the density of active [[Sigils]] in the upper terraces is high enough that visitors with [[Control Tier]] sensitivity sometimes describe a faint ambient pressure, a sense of the city's magical infrastructure running constantly underneath everything.

Low-tier casters in Solhaven are not persecuted. They are simply, quietly, at the bottom. The harbor district is where they live and work. The upper terraces are where people who can demonstrate ability accumulate. This has been true for as long as the city has existed and is considered by most Solhaven residents to be simply how things work.

## The Ascendant Path

[[Religion and Belief|The Ascendant Path]] runs deeper in Solhaven than anywhere else in [[Auralis]]. The Luminants — the Path's formal priesthood — operate out of three major temples in the city, the largest of which sits on the Crestward two blocks from the palace. Formal ceremonies mark every significant life event for registered citizens: birth rune inscription, tier certification, marriage, death. The Path's theology — that [[Flux]] mastery is the highest spiritual pursuit and that the tiers of casting represent genuine states of advancement — maps comfortably onto the city's social structure, which is part of why that structure feels like natural order to most Solhaven residents rather than a hierarchy that was constructed.

[[Hadren Cosse]] manages the council's relationship with the Luminant hierarchy. The three senior Luminants whose theological work most closely aligns with the Culmination Faction's goals are people he knows well. Whether the theological and the political alignment preceded or followed these relationships is a question neither party has found useful to examine closely.

[[Fennick]] has attended several significant Solhaven social events in the past decade, under different names, with his horns covered for exactly as long as it took to get past the door. He has found the Crestward gatherings specifically interesting. He has not yet noticed that two of the beings in the rooms are not human.
