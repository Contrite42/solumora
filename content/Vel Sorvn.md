**Vel Sorvn** is thirty-four years old and has been at [[Wolfpoint]] for nine years. He came from a river-trade family in western [[Terravelle]], assessed [[Control Tier|T3]] at eleven, trained in standard sigil work through his teens, and arrived at Wolfpoint at twenty-five after a year of correspondence with a practitioner there who had read a short paper he'd written about sequence logic as an alternative encoding for multi-conditional sigil effects.

## Appearance

Vel is thirty-four with the lean, slightly disheveled appearance of someone who has spent nine years at Wolfpoint working on practical [[Hypertext]] applications — not physically imposing, but carrying the restless energy of someone whose mind is constantly testing sequences even when they're not actively casting. His dark hair is kept at medium length and often shows the disarray of someone who runs their hands through it while working through logic problems, and he dresses in the practical style of Wolfpoint practitioners: simple tunics and trousers suitable for both workshop testing and archive research, everything showing ink stains and the occasional burn mark from sequence failures.

His hands are remarkably steady for someone whose work involves testing unstable sequences — a necessity he developed deliberately after early failures taught him that small errors propagate dramatically in conditional logic. The fingertips show light scarring from nine years of Hypertext development work, and he has the habit of sketching invisible sequence patterns in air while thinking, tracing the branching logic of conditionals with small precise gestures. He moves through Wolfpoint's workshop spaces with the focused absorption of someone who is mentally tracking multiple parallel problems, often pausing mid-conversation as though checking a logic branch that just occurred to him.

When explaining his work his manner is detailed and methodical, speaking in the technical language of sequence development while simultaneously showing what he means — the communication style of someone who thinks by building and tests by breaking. His workspace is organized with systematic thoroughness: sequences documented incrementally, test results catalogued by failure mode, seven years of practice notes that track thinking-on-paper about why things behaved unexpectedly. Off-duty he has the quiet, persistent focus of someone genuinely absorbed by work that most of the world doesn't take seriously, and colleagues note that he seems more energized by interesting failures than successful results — the sequences that don't work as expected reveal things the working ones conceal.

## Background

The paper was not sophisticated by Wolfpoint standards. It was, the practitioner told him when he arrived, interesting in the way that work is interesting when it reaches the right conclusion through an approach nobody at Wolfpoint had tried. He stayed.

His work is primarily applied: developing Hypertext sequences for practical field applications, testing their behavior across a range of conditions, documenting what fails and how. [[Hypertext]] in the early stages of a new sequence is not reliable in the way that a well-tested sigil is reliable. The same logic that makes it more expressive — branching conditionals, nested triggers, behavior that changes mid-cast based on what it encounters — also means a single error in sequence propagates in ways that a sigil's fixed shape contains. Vel has developed a practice of building sequences incrementally, testing each clause before adding the next, which is slower than the approach most Wolfpoint practitioners use and produces cleaner results.

He keeps practice notes. Not the formal research logs that the Voss-led archive requires — those he maintains separately, in the appropriate format. The practice notes are his: observations about what sequences did that he did not expect, notes on failure modes, thinking on paper about why a particular conditional behaved as it did. He has seven years of practice notes. He has occasionally considered whether they contain something worth formalizing, and has not yet done so.
