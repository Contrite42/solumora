Keld Dast is thirty-eight and has worked the northern mine shafts outside [[Greyveil]] for nineteen years, following his father's practice and his grandfather's before that. His [[Control Tier|tier assessed at T1]] when he was ten. He uses it for structural soundings — a technique that tells him whether a section of shaft needs bracing before the rock announces that opinion more dramatically. He learned it from his father, who learned it from his. It has never been formally taught anywhere he is aware of.

## Appearance

Keld is thirty-eight with the compact, powerful build of someone who has spent nineteen years working mine shafts — not tall, but heavily muscled through the shoulders and arms from extraction work and shaft maintenance. His dark hair and beard are kept short for practical reasons, and both show the perpetual dust-staining that marks miners who spend most of their working life underground. His face is weathered beyond his years from alternating between shaft work and surface conditions, and his skin has the particular grayish tinge that comes from extended time in the low-light environment of the mines.

His hands are densely calloused and scarred from nineteen years of rock work — old impact injuries from equipment handling, burn marks from tool maintenance, the accumulated damage of someone whose work environment is fundamentally unforgiving. He moves with the careful economy of someone who understands precisely how much energy the shaft work requires and never wastes motion unnecessarily. When assessing shaft conditions his posture changes noticeably — becoming still and focused, head tilted slightly as though listening to something others can't hear, the physical concentration of someone performing T1 structural soundings that might determine whether the next shift is safe.

He wears the practical mining clothing of northern Greyveil operations: heavy canvas work clothes reinforced at stress points, thick leather gloves worn through and repaired countless times, heavy boots with reinforced toes. Everything he owns is functional rather than decorative, and the gray ore dust is so thoroughly worked into the fabric that it's become part of the color. Off-duty he has the quiet, self-contained manner of someone who is genuinely content with work that others might find claustrophobic or oppressive, and the few times he speaks about the shafts his voice carries a familiarity that borders on affection — these are spaces he knows better than any surface environment.

## Work

The shafts his family works run east of the city into the rock formations that feed Greyveil's ore economy. They are not deep by any standard that would impress a mining operation further south — the richest seams ran out two generations ago — but they produce consistently and the family knows this particular ground in a way that newer crews do not. Keld brought in two outside teams three years ago to assist with the more labor-intensive extraction work. He pays them fairly and expects them to follow his directions on shaft conditions without discussion. So far they have.

He has been to [[Valdenmoor]] once, for a guild licensing dispute that was resolved in his favor. He found the city interesting in the way that a large enclosed space is interesting — pressing inward in a manner he does not associate with the mine shafts, which are small but legible. He came home satisfied and has not left since.

The travelers passing through Greyveil on the northern road are part of the city the way the wind off the rock formations is part of the city — present, not particularly interesting, not worth organizing around. He has given directions to [[Wolfpoint]] more times than he has counted. He could not tell you anything about it.
