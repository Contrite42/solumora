[[Sorath]] does not hold a fixed base in [[Solumora]]. His operational pattern is a long movement circuit that alternates between the [[Equatorial Desert]] margin, secondary inland roads in both kingdoms, and short-duration stays in coastal nodes such as [[Hedun]].

The circuit is not random. Sorath avoids repetition windows shorter than six months in any one settlement. He treats recurrence itself as detection risk: if the same innkeeper can place his face in memory inside one season, he has stayed too long. Most of his stops are one to three nights, with occasional seven-night pauses only when weather or trade congestion makes immediate movement more visible than delay.

His cover discipline is minimal but precise. He uses ordinary traveler identities, pays in full, and keeps interactions deliberately forgettable. Unlike [[Fennick]], he avoids social gatherings where memory density is high. Unlike [[Selvane]], he does not maintain institutional access. His concealment work centers on optical uncertainty and recall drift: people remember a traveler, but not details they can report consistently.

Sorath's movement windows align with rift seasons around desert-adjacent corridors, not because he intervenes in incursions directly, but because elevated Flux noise lowers his signature contrast against background conditions. This allows him to pass through monitored routes with less adjustment overhead.

His only durable geographic preference appears to be route optionality. He favors road systems with at least three plausible branch exits, reducing the chance that anyone can predict his next position from his current one.
