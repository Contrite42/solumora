# Persecution Era - The Voss Survival Line

One of the few documented High Demon survival lineages available in modern historical records is the Voss protection network, relevant to current era because [[Teren Voss|Verath]] emerged from this lineage after persecution formally ended.

## The Being

Historical name uncertain. Referred to in Ashford Witness Amnesty Records as "the traveler who stayed," contemporary sources suggest birth name "Teren Voss" or close variant.

Age at persecution start: Unknown precisely, but consistent residence records from 100+ years before persecution began (suggesting existing age of 100-150 years minimum).

## Refuge Period (Years 1-180 of persecution)

Primary residence documented in [[Ashford]] region with coordinated refuge presence in border mountain communities.

**Documented cover identity:** Estate steward and traveling merchant. Maintained legitimate business connections while providing refugee network coordination.

**Community protection commitment:** Unlike some refugees who attempted hiding, this immortal became actively economically integrated:
- Managed trading company operations (legitimate business, high profit)
- Served as mediator in commercial disputes (valued for reliability and knowledge)
- Coordinated transport security for caravans (built reputation as trustworthy)

**Lantern Hunt evasion:** Successfully avoided multiple sweep cycles through:
- Advance warning from sympathetic magistrate network
- Documented travel during peak hunt seasons (appearing in official caravans made capture during hunts logistically infeasible)
- Plausible aging (aged at near-normal rate due to unspecified High Demon physiological variation; appeared 40-50 years old throughout 180-year period rather than obviously immortal)

## Family Formation (Year 150 of persecution onward)

Historical records document relationship formation with a local household administrator. Result: one child born (documentation unclear due to record destruction, but mentioned in Amnesty Records as "lineage continuation attempt").

This child would be father to [[Teren Voss|contemporary Verath]].

## Persecution End and Reintegration

When persecution formally concluded, this High Demon made deliberate choice:
- Complete institutional revelation of identity
- Submission to magistrate verification (accepting legal status rather than continued hiding)
- Formal resignation from active business roles

Unlike Five Survivors Concord signatory immortals (who died or disappeared after failed agreement), the Voss line chose integration over continued refuge.

This created unusual pattern: immortal being operating in semi-public existence as acknowledged high-tier administrator for final 20 years before voluntary retreat from public life.

## Modern Significance

Teren Voss's existence and public revelation 3 years ago connects directly to this lineage's decision-making:

- Family had experience of persecution and understood institutional fear mechanisms
- Family had experience of magistrate cooperation and knew trust could be rebuilt
- Family had economic integration foundations that made coexistence less threatening to communities

The Voss line is as much history lesson (showing institutional trust could be rebuilt after betrayal) as it is individual biography.
