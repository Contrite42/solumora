**Sova Mast** is fifteen years old and in her second year of apprenticeship in [[Valdenmoor]], learning structural sigil application under a guild-certified practitioner in the residential trade.

She assessed [[Control Tier|T2]] at eleven. This was expected — her parents both assessed T2, her older brother assessed T1, and T2 is the range where most of Valdenmoor lands. The assessment result settled into family conversation with the comfortable lack of drama of information that mostly confirmed what everyone had been preparing for. She started the apprenticeship at thirteen.

The work is currently theory-heavy. She knows the load-bearing applications well enough to describe them in detail and not well enough to perform them without supervision. Her training master has told her this is normal for year two and that year three is when the gap between knowing and doing begins to close for most apprentices. She believes this and also finds the gap frustrating.

The frustrating part is not the gap itself but the quality of the gap: she can see when a sigil placement is wrong and she cannot yet fix it with confidence. She can read a structural problem in a test space and explain it accurately and feels the imprecision in her corrections when she attempts them. She is living, at fifteen, in the specific experience of competence that has outrun the body's ability to execute it, which is the most common form of frustration in any skilled trade and is not something her training master finds unusual enough to remark on.

She has not thought much about what T2 means for the shape of her adult life. She thinks mostly about the work in front of her, and which of the day's corrections was closer.
