# Five Survivors Concord - Early Peace Attempt

The [[Five Survivors Concord]] was a formal accord signed approximately 200 years into the [[The Persecution Era|Persecution Era's]] 200-year span. It represents one of the only documented moments when immortal beings attempted institutional negotiation with kingdoms that were actively hunting them.

## Context of Negotiation

By year 200 of persecution, both kingdoms faced a paradox:
- Lantern Hunts had become ritualized performance rather than operational success
- Refugee networks had become semi-institutionalized through magistrate protection
- Kingdoms could not sustain indefinite persecution without depopulation-scale violence
- High Demons remained alive despite two centuries of active threat

The Five Survivors Concord emerged from recognition of mutual exhaustion.

## Documented Signatories

Historical sources identify five High Demons willing to negotiate:
1. **Kel'mantis variant** — The original incident trigger, now negotiating terms of continued existence (name and history confirmed unreliably)
2. **The Border Protector** — Immortal being with Terravelle guild network connections
3. **The Archivist** — Documented as maintaining historical records throughout persecution; academic value made negotiation possible
4. **The Merchant Principle** — High Demon with deep economic integration into trader networks
5. **The Mountain Keeper** — Refugee network operator coordinating sanctuary across both kingdoms

Fifth identity remains officially unconfirmed in both kingdom records.

## Terms

The Concord's language remains fragmentary (original documents were destroyed during subsequent hunts), but surviving quotes indicate:

- High Demons would accept **restricted residence locations** (specific designated zones)
- High Demons would accept **regular verification reporting** to kingdom magistrates
- High Demons would **cease active participation in governance** (though most had never been publicly involved)
- In exchange: **Formal cessation of active Lantern Hunts** and **legal recognition of protected residence rights**

## Why Agreement Failed

The Concord held for approximately 15 years. It collapsed when:

1. **New political faction opposed to peace emerged** in [[Auralis]] Council arguing that any immortal presence constituted permanent threat
2. **A High Demon's death** (circumstances unclear—possibly natural aging anomaly, possibly hunter discovery) triggered panic that agreement had failed to eliminate beings
3. **Hunt memorialization movement** in both kingdoms provided political pressure to resume visible persecution as proof of institutional strength

The formal Concord was repudiated. Its existence was struck from institutional records that weren't destroyed entirely.

## Historical Significance

The Concord's failure demonstrated that institutional persecution couldn't be negotiated away while public fear remained. By year 200, populations had internalized High Demons as existential threat rather than manageable policy problem.

Final persecution intensity actually *increased* after Concord failure, suggesting that the agreement's collapse reinforced rather than resolved fundamental institutional paranoia.

This history informs modern coexistence frameworks: revelation of immortals' existence alone isn't sufficient for safety. Institutional structures must be rebuilt from foundation to recontextualize threat as manageable rather than existential.
