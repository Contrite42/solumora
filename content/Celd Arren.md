_(Council Member, Auralis — Expansion Faction)_

**Celd Arren** does not need a strategic reason to support [[Auralis|Auralis's]] expansion northward. His family has been nursing a specific grievance with [[Terravelle]] for two generations, and he has one anyway — several, in fact, all of them economically legible and none of them sufficient to explain the emotional register with which he pursues them.

## Character

Celd is sixty-one, old money, and carries his family's history with Terravelle the way someone carries a portrait of a dead ancestor — prominently displayed, occasionally consulted, slightly incongruous with the actual furniture of the present. He is not consumed by it. He is simply organized around it in a way that predates any strategic analysis he has applied since.

The grievance: approximately forty years ago, during one of the three near-wars of the Contact Period, a trade route arbitration went against the Arren family in a settlement that Terravellian administrators managed to their advantage. The economic impact, by the time Celd's father was finished calculating it, was significant. By the time Celd inherited the calculation, it had acquired the particular solidity of things that have been explained many times in terms of principle rather than money.

He is not irrational about Terravelle. He does not dislike all Terravellians. He simply considers the question of Auralis's expansion northward to be one whose correct answer is obvious and whose opponents are either uninformed or acting against Auralis's interests. He holds this with the particular conviction of someone who arrived at a conclusion through grievance and then found the strategic arguments that supported it, rather than the other way around.

His [[Flux]] ability is T3, which he considers a private embarrassment in a culture that links ability to worth. He does not discuss it. He compensates with money, which is more reliably useful in the rooms he operates in.

## On the Council

Celd votes reliably with the Expansion Faction. He is not, in the council's current configuration, one of its more dangerous members, because his motivations are so legible that everyone in the room can model him. This is the particular disadvantage of people whose positions are emotionally grounded: they are easy to predict and difficult to surprise.

[[Davan Rhyce]] finds him a useful vote and an unreliable thinker. [[Castor Helme]] finds him useful in public presentations — there is something convincing about a man of old money and long grievance speaking plainly about what Auralis deserves — and irrelevant in actual strategy sessions. Celd is aware of the latter and considers it a reasonable division of labor.
