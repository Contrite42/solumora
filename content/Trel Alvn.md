**Trel Alvn** is a bootmaker in [[Valdenmoor]]'s [[The Outer Wards|Outer Wards]], running a small shop with two apprentices at the same address for eighteen years. He is forty-six, T0.

## The Shop

Trel's shop produces general-purpose boots for Valdenmoor's working population: dock workers, farm laborers, craftspeople who need durable footwear. The work is entirely non-magical — his craft is in leather selection, fit assessment, and the accumulated technique of construction that comes from making several hundred pairs a year for nearly two decades.

He holds guild certification through the Cordwainers' Guild's non-magical track, which covers leather grading, construction standards, and the fit-testing process that gives his work its reputation for producing boots that hold up without causing injury. The non-magical track is a full guild certification; it provides the same professional recognition and access to the guild's commercial network as the T2+ track, without the Flux-application components that higher-tier shoemakers use for specialized waterproofing and reinforcement work.

The gap between his work and a T2 certified bootmaker's work is real but narrow for the market segment he serves. T2 techniques produce demonstrably better results for specialist commissions — waterproof boots for river workers, reinforced construction for heavy lifting, extended-lifespan work requiring preservation sigils. For general everyday boots, the quality difference is smaller, and the price differential is significant enough that his market — working-class Outer Wards residents who need functional footwear at reasonable cost — consistently prefers his product.

He is doing reasonably well by the standards of his neighborhood. Not building capital reserves, but not in debt, with a consistent customer base that returns because his work is reliable. He owns his tools. He does not own the building.

## The T0 Experience in Terravelle

Trel's T0 assessment was delivered without drama at age nine. The guild advising system redirected him toward non-magical guild tracks, he trained in bootmaking through an apprenticeship he found through guild placement, and he has been doing the work ever since. This is the version of the T0 story that [[Terravelle]] is designed to produce, and it is a real outcome — his life is functional, his work is valued, and the system treated him with the practical respect of giving him accurate information and access to useful tracks.

What the system cannot give him, and does not try to, is the experience of wondering what he might have done differently if he had assessed higher. He does not spend time on this. He is aware of it the way a person is aware of a door they have passed enough times that they no longer particularly think about what is behind it. The Covenant tradition's silence on this — there is no Path theology offering an interpretation of what T0 means about his spiritual progression, no consolation framework that his culture provides — is sometimes experienced as a gap and sometimes as a relief. He tends toward the latter.

His two apprentices: one T0 like him, on the same non-magical track; one T1, who has the option to take the Flux-application components and is three years into deciding whether to. The T1 apprentice has the technical skills for both tracks and has been offered advancement opportunities that Trel was not. He has advised her to take them when she is ready, without pressure about the timeline. What she decides about her own career is her decision, and providing accurate information rather than steering is what his version of mentorship looks like.

## Character

Trel is steady and private in the way that people who have built an uncomplicated life tend to be — not because there is nothing beneath the surface but because the surface has been stable long enough that examining it is not a daily habit. He has neighbors he has known for fifteen years and knows them in the specific, practical way that long-term proximity produces: their work patterns, their children's names, who can be asked for help with what.

He has been married, divorced ten years ago, and is on reasonable terms with his former wife who lives elsewhere in the Outer Wards and does her own work. Their settlement was practical and handled through guild arbitration, which is what the system exists for. He sees no particular need to characterize it further than that.
