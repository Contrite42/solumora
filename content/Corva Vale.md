Corva Vale is an orchard district in [[Auralis]]'s southeastern uplands, sixty miles inland from [[Solhaven]]. Population approximately 300 across eight holdings. It produces tree fruit (apples, pears, stone fruits) for urban markets and processes seasonal surplus into preserved products.

## The Land

The uplands around Corva Vale sit above the basin's flood zone, with well-drained soil, moderate rainfall, and a climate warm enough to support fruit cultivation without requiring intensive [[Flux]]-based climate control. The orchards here are generations old — some trees are sixty years into productive life cycles that can extend past eighty years with proper management.

Fruit cultivation at Corva Vale operates on a different timescale than annual crop farming. Decisions made today about tree planting, pruning, and variety selection will determine yield twenty years from now. Every holding maintains detailed records: tree age, yield history, variety performance, and pest pressure patterns that inform long-term orchard management.

## The Season

Orchard work at Corva Vale concentrates around specific events: spring flowering (pest management and pollination support), summer fruit thinning (controlling per-tree yield for quality), autumn harvest (coordinated labor mobilization), and winter pruning (shaping next season's growth).

Trel Orvn, who manages the northern holdings, coordinates timing across the entire district. Flowering happens within a two-week window — every holding needs pest-control casting and pollination support simultaneously, which requires pooling the district's [[Flux Users|practitioner]] capacity. Dren Saln, T2, casts Pestward coverage for six holdings during the critical flowering period, working fourteen-hour days to maintain protection across hundreds of trees.

The work is not optional. Lose the flowering period to pest damage and the season's yield disappears. The entire district's economy depends on getting this two-week window right.

## The Preservation Operation

Fresh fruit from Corva Vale supplies [[Solhaven]]'s premium markets during harvest season. But most of the district's output goes to preservation: dried fruit, fruit preserves, and pressed juice products that maintain market value through winter and spring when fresh fruit is unavailable.

The district operates two communal preservation facilities where holdings bring surplus fruit for processing. Mira Brendt manages the larger facility, coordinating processing schedules, quality standards, and distribution logistics that get preserved products to urban buyers before competing supply reaches market.

Preservation work is time-sensitive and labor-intensive. Fruit arrives at peak ripeness and must be processed within days to prevent spoilage. The facility runs continuous shifts during peak harvest, pulling labor from across the district to maintain processing throughput.

## The Market Structure

Corva Vale sells fresh fruit to urban buyers during harvest season at premium prices. The rest of the year, the district sells preserved products at lower margins but consistent volume. This two-tier market structure creates economic stability — harvest-season premiums provide cash flow spikes, and preserved-product sales provide base income that carries the district through off-season periods.

Nora Teld coordinates the district's market relationships. She visits Solhaven monthly to maintain buyer connections, negotiate pricing, and track market conditions that affect demand. Her work is part sales, part logistics, part economic intelligence — knowing which products are moving, which buyers are reliable, and which market opportunities are worth pursuing versus which are traps.

The district does not have a unified sales organization. Each holding maintains its own buyer relationships for fresh fruit. But the preserved-products market operates collectively through Nora's coordination, which gives individual holdings more negotiating leverage than they would have operating independently.

## The Practitioners

Corva Vale has five active [[Control Tier|Tier 2]] practitioners distributed across the holdings. During spring flowering they coordinate as a district-wide team, providing comprehensive pest control. The rest of the year they work independently, managing holding-specific problems: tree health, soil fertility, preservation facility temperature control.

The district's Flux use is seasonal and strategic. Spring flowering protection is intensive and critical. Summer growth management is minimal. Harvest-season preservation work concentrates around facility operations rather than field casting. Winter has almost no active casting — the orchards rest, and so do the practitioners.

This seasonal rhythm shapes how Corva Vale integrates Flux into its economy. Practitioners are orchard workers first, casters second. Their Flux capacity is a tool they apply during specific high-stakes periods, not a continuous operational requirement.

## The Pressure

Urban buyers have been requesting year-round fresh fruit supply, which is not naturally possible given Corva Vale's seasonal production cycle. Some holdings have begun experimenting with [[Flux]]-enhanced cold storage that extends fruit shelf-life through winter months.

The technology works but requires sustained [[Control Tier|Tier 3]] casting to maintain storage conditions, which is more expensive than traditional preservation methods. Two holdings are currently running cold-storage trials, tracking costs and market returns to determine if year-round fresh supply is economically viable.

The district has not reached consensus. Cold storage creates market opportunities but increases operational complexity and Flux dependence. Some holdings see it as necessary adaptation to changing urban demand. Others see it as overextending into markets that do not justify the investment.

The experiments continue. The crop continues. The conversation continues.
