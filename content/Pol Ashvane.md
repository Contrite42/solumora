_(Council Member, Auralis — Culmination Faction)_

**Pol Ashvane** rarely speaks in full council sessions. When he does, it is to ask a question — usually one that has already been answered, from someone who has not yet realized the answer was wrong the first time. He has been a member of [[The Council of Auralis]] for nine years and has never given a speech. Three significant council decisions in the last five years went the way he wanted them to without anyone clearly identifying why.

## Character

Pol is fifty-one, precise, and entirely without the need for credit. This last quality is unusual enough on the council that it took most of his colleagues several years to properly account for it. People who want credit for things are predictable: they advocate visibly, stake positions publicly, and can be tracked and managed. Pol doesn't want credit. He wants outcomes. The gap between those two orientations is where he does his work.

He operates through private conversation — meeting people individually, before sessions, after sessions, sometimes weeks before a vote that has not yet been announced. He does not lobby. He informs. He shares something relevant that he happens to know, asks whether it changes anything, and leaves. In most cases it changes something. He does not follow up. The vote records the result.

His [[Flux]] ability sits at T3 — enough to be functional, not enough to matter to anyone monitoring caster strength. He uses it rarely and practically. He is not someone anyone watches for [[Control Tier|Flux]] reasons.

## What He Wants

Pol has never stated a position that summarizes his goals in any public or semi-public context. The Culmination Faction's stated purpose — recovering whatever the ancient civilization built — aligns with his voting record, but whether he believes in the goal or simply finds it strategically correct to support is not something his colleagues have agreed on.

What is clear: he is positioning himself. For what, specifically, he has not said. His conduct suggests a very long time horizon — patience that makes [[Drest]]'s twenty-year project look near-term. He is the person on the council who is most explicitly thinking about what comes after whatever comes next.

[[Tovan Ashce]] has noticed him. [[Pol Ashvane|Pol]] has noticed that [[Tovan Ashce|Tovan]] has noticed him. They have had one conversation about something unrelated, conducted with the particular precision of two people who are aware they are not talking about the thing they are talking about.
