_Extracts from case documentation maintained by [[Rell Hadv]], guild master, Valdenmoor certification practice. The following concern apprentice assessment procedures for high-tier candidates._

---

**Opening note, first month of current apprenticeship:**

The standard apprenticeship documentation was designed with a ceiling in mind — specifically, that the supervising certified practitioner should be capable of demonstrating, at minimum, the same tier of application as the apprentice is being trained toward. This assumption holds for the majority of cases. The current case does not.

Current apprentice tier: [[Control Tier|T5]], confirmed by second assessor. Supervising master tier: T4, self.

Procedural question, first: Does guild certification require that a supervising master be capable of demonstrating all exercises included in apprenticeship training? The current charter language states that the master must be "competent to evaluate" apprentice work. I interpret this as distinct from "capable of performing" the same work. A T5 completing T4 exercises can be evaluated by a T4 master competent in T4 work. Whether the same logic extends to T5 exercises is less clear. I will consult the regional guild chair before the apprentice reaches that stage.

Procedural question, second: The journeyman evaluation requires demonstration before a certified assessor. For T4 certification, T4 assessors are standard. For T5, there is no separate assessor tier — assessment continues to be performed by certified masters. Whether a T4 master may assess a T5 journeyman application is not addressed in current documentation. Flagging for network consultation.

---

**Third month note:**

The apprentice is technically excellent and learns procedural requirements quickly. The T5 range of application has not yet appeared in training exercises — the first two years of formal work are spent within T1-T3 range regardless of assessed tier, to ensure foundational competence before expansion. This is correct pedagogically. It also means the procedural complications have not yet been practically relevant.

The apprentice's question rate is high and the questions are good ones. This is, in my experience, a reliable indicator.

I am writing this note before the questions become practically relevant so that the documentation begins now rather than at the point of urgency.

---

**Note added, seventh month:**

Consulted with regional guild chair. Her reading of "competent to evaluate" aligns with mine. She is less certain about the journeyman assessor question and has referred it to the documentation committee. This means it will take longer than I would like.

The apprentice has not asked about any of this. Either they have not thought about it yet, or they have thought about it and decided that asking is not the right move at this stage. Given what I have observed over seven months, it is probably the second.
