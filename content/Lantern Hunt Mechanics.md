# Lantern Hunt Mechanics

The **Lantern Hunts** were the operational persecution infrastructure—systematic sweeps designed to locate, identify, and eliminate High Demons and suspected sympathizers. The name derived from the lantern-light formations used in coordinated night operations across multiple districts, creating visual and psychological spectacle alongside practical search function.

## Organization Structure

Hunts operated under dual-kingdom coordination with local magistrate execution:
- Auralis [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] set strategic targets and authorized multi-week campaigns
- Terravelle [[Terravelle Guild|Guilds]] coordinated information sharing with border magistrates
- Local town militias and hired mercenaries conducted sweep operations

No unified central authority managed hunts across both kingdoms. This fragmentation made coordination inefficient but also made resistance harder to target systematically—survival networks developed locally without kingdom-level surveillance capacity.

## Sweep Tactics

Standard hunt operations included:
- **Lantern formations**: Teams moving in geometric patterns at night with high-visibility torches to flush targets from hiding
- **Loyalty interrogation**: Residents questioned about acquaintances, suspicious relationships, unusual financial patterns
- **Trace elimination**: Destroying records of birth, death, family relationships for suspected sympathizer lineages
- **Relocation sweeps**: Forcing populations of suspected high-demon-supporting areas to relocate to interior districts where movement could be monitored

## Failure Mechanisms

Hunts were episodically successful at locating individuals but systematically failed at eliminating High Demon threats because:
- Immortal beings could outlast single hunts and wait out regional panic cycles
- Coordinated multi-kingdom hunts were rare (maximum 3-4 times per century)
- Local magistrates often covertly protected networks that benefited economically from clandestine trade
- Sympathizer populations were large enough that total elimination would require depopulation-scale violence

By year 150 of persecution, institutional recognition emerged that Lantern Hunts created psychological terror without achieving permanent threat elimination. Some magistrates began deliberately conducting symbolic hunts while maintaining refugee networks.

## Psychological Function

The true operational success of Lantern Hunts was social fragmentation:
- Communities learned to distrust neighbors who might report them
- Trust institutions dissolved if they were perceived as hunt-coordinating
- Paranoia about hidden immortals replaced rational threat assessment

This fragmentation persisted long after organized hunts were formally ended, embedding institutional paranoia that current coexistence frameworks struggle to overcome.
