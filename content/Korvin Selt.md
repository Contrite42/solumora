**Korvin Selt** is forty-seven years old and works as a dock team lead in [[Solhaven]]'s [[Harbor District]]. [[Control Tier|T2]]. He has worked on the docks for thirty years — started as a laborer, moved into supervision at thirty-two, and has held his current position for fifteen years. He is reliable, understands complex cargo movements, and younger dock workers respect his judgment on practical problems.

## Appearance

Korvin is a broad-shouldered man with the build of someone who spent two decades doing heavy labor before transitioning to supervisory work. He's weathered in the way dock workers become — sun-darkened skin, permanent squint lines from years working in harbor glare, hands calloused and scarred from rope work and cargo handling. His hair has gone mostly gray and he keeps it short for practical reasons.

He dresses in the standard dock worker's uniform — heavy canvas trousers, work tunic in faded blue, boots that have been resoled multiple times. As a team lead he wears a red armband indicating supervisory status, though he only puts it on when absolutely necessary for identifying himself to merchants or inspectors. He moves with the deliberate economy of someone who knows how to work efficiently without wasting energy, and his voice carries across a noisy dock without him needing to shout.

At forty-seven he's in the physical condition of someone who still does manual work when needed but has learned to pace himself. His hands are steady and capable, and younger workers notice that he can still demonstrate any task he asks them to do.

## Background

His family was harbor-born; his father was also a dock worker (T0, unusual for this region and this work), and his mother worked in provisioning. He expected to be T0, assessed as T2, and spent his early years taking jobs that used his tier but did not require significant technique. He learned Flux work on the job, developed some skill, and found that moderate magical ability combined with years of practical knowledge made him more valuable than either alone.

Korvin has neither ambition to rise further nor concern that he has not. He earns enough to live in his own small room in the residential quarters, supports an adult daughter who works in the textile trade, and attends the Temple of Ascendant Understanding occasionally without particular devotion. He appreciates the temple's low-pressure approach; the sermons about work as spiritual practice resonate with his experience of thirty years of honest dock work.

He is aware that the [[Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run]] and [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] are both vulnerable to disruption, and watches trade flow patterns closely for warning signs. He has mentioned to colleagues that the seasonal variation seems to be shifting — more volatility in otherwise stable routes. He has not escalated this observation to anyone in authority; it is the kind of thing dock people know and discuss among themselves.

Korvin expects to do dock work for another fifteen to twenty years, retire with modest savings, and spend his later years in the quieter quarters further up the terrace, possibly in a partnership with someone else also aging out of working life. This is a realistic plan and not dependent on luck or advancement.
