**Rella Dost** is an assessment preparation tutor based in [[Solhaven]]'s [[The Middling Steps|Middling Steps]], specializing in preparing children ages seven through ten for [[Control Tier]] assessment. She is thirty-six, T3, and has run her practice for eight years.

## The Practice

Assessment preparation is a contested service in Solhaven. The [[Ascendant Path]]'s official position is that tier assessment measures innate Flux capacity and that preparation cannot meaningfully alter results. This is largely true and largely irrelevant to the families who hire people like Rella. What preparation provides is not a higher score — it is a child who knows what to expect, who has been taught the basic breathing and attention exercises that reduce assessment anxiety, who has practiced the geometric visualization tasks the assessment process uses, and who arrives at the ceremony in a condition to perform clearly rather than in a state of stress-induced interference.

The difference between a child who performs clearly and a child in peak stress is, by Rella's professional estimate, about a half-tier in borderline cases. This is not nothing.

She takes ten students per assessment cycle. Her waiting list is eighteen months. Her fees are at the top of what the middle-terrace market will bear. Families from the lower Steps and the harbor district occasionally approach her; she keeps two sliding-scale slots per cycle for families who cannot pay full rates, which she does not advertise and which generates the most consistent referrals in her practice.

## What She Knows

Eight years of assessment preparation has given Rella a detailed private map of upper-Steps family anxiety. She knows which households have T4+ parents who are quietly terrified their child assessed T2 at early indicators. She knows which families are putting everything into a T3 child they believe will go higher with the right training. She has sat with parents immediately after their child's assessment results came back — the ones who got what they hoped for and the ones who didn't — and has become very good at the particular kind of professional presence that is required in those moments.

She maintains strict confidentiality about individual families. She has never discussed a specific family's situation with another family. The social consequence of a breach would end her practice immediately, and the professional ethic behind the rule is real as well — she knows things about the private anxieties of Crestward-adjacent families that those families would consider intimate disclosures. The knowledge carries weight.

## Character

Rella is precise in her speech and careful in her professional relationships. She has a tendency to say exactly what she means without a great deal of social softening, which the families who hire her generally appreciate because it tells them she will be equally honest about their children. She has assessed thousands of children's baseline Flux response well enough that she is rarely surprised by a certification result, which she considers one of the less comfortable aspects of her expertise.

Her own T3 assessment sits at the upper edge of what guild certification for her kind of work requires. She has not pursued Path advancement tracks — the Path's educational hierarchy is not where she wants to be, and the independence of private practice is something she values more than the institutional credential would provide.
