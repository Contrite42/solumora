**Sem Aldt** is twenty-nine years old and crews a coastal trading vessel that runs the northern [[Terravelle]] coastline between the river ports south of [[Valdenmoor]] and the harbor settlements north of [[Greyveil]]. [[Control Tier|T1]]. He has been working this route for six years. Before that, two years on a longer ocean run out of a southern Terravellian port, which he left because the pay was marginally better on the coastal route and the time away from port was shorter, and at twenty-three those things mattered in the order he weighted them.

## Appearance

Sem is twenty-nine with the lean, weathered build of someone who has spent eight years working maritime trade on the northern Terravelle coast — fit from constant sailing work, sun-and-wind damaged beyond his years from extended exposure to coastal conditions. His light brown hair is kept short for practical reasons and often shows the salt-stiffness that's permanent for coastal sailors, and he dresses in the functional style of northern maritime crews: heavy wool layers, oiled canvas weather gear, everything showing the wear and salt-staining of years at sea.

His hands are heavily calloused from rope work and sail handling, and he moves with the unconscious balance of someone whose body has adapted to working on shifting decks in variable weather. When performing T1 weather readings his expression becomes focused and slightly distant, the look of someone sensing atmospheric conditions beyond what's visibly apparent — though he treats these readings as one input among several rather than relying on them exclusively. His face has the permanent squint of someone who spends working days scanning horizons and reading water conditions, and there's a watchfulness in his eyes this past year that wasn't there before — the visible mark of someone who has noticed that their routine work has become more complicated without understanding why.

He moves through northern ports with the easy familiarity of someone who has run the same seasonal routes for six years, understanding harbor rhythms and building relationships with operators during winter maintenance seasons. When cargo manifests require more documentation than usual his manner is professionally compliant without being curious, the practiced neutrality of someone who has learned that asking why things have changed is less useful than adjusting to the new requirements. Off-duty he has the practical, straightforward presence of someone who chose coastal work for tangible reasons — better pay, shorter time away from port — and continues with it because those reasons remain valid even as other aspects of the work have shifted in ways he can't quite model.

## Work

Maritime work on the northern coast is seasonal in ways the river trade is not. The summer months have reliable traffic and reasonable weather. The shoulder seasons are the skilled-sailor months — the runs that require good judgment about when to go and when to stay — and the deep winter is the maintenance season, when the vessels are in port and the crew does repair work and the work of building relationships with the harbor operators that smooth the summer season's logistics.

His T1 casting is used for weather reading — a standard maritime application, giving sailors a short-range forecast of the actual conditions rather than the general seasonal pattern. T1 weather reading is limited in range and certainty. Sem treats it as one input among several: his own experience of how this stretch of coast behaves, the harbor operators' local knowledge, the other sailors' observations. He does not rely on it more than it warrants.

He is aware that the northern runs have become more complicated in the past year — more documentation required at the ports near [[Greyveil]], more inspection of cargo manifests, more questions about origin and destination that the cargo has always had clear answers to. He does not know why this has changed. He notes it as a change and adjusts accordingly.
