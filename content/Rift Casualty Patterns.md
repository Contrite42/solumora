_(Medical Assessment, Dimensional Trauma)_

**Rift Casualty Patterns** are the documented medical, psychological, and institutional consequences of [[Flux Demons|flux demon]] exposure and dimensional breach proximity during [[Rift Incursions|rift incursion]] events. Standard military casualty assessment classifies rift victims using conventional combat trauma metrics (KIA/wounded ratios, injury severity scales, recovery timeline projections), but [[Seln Morra]]'s analysis of [[The Thel Reach Incursion|Thel Reach]] casualties demonstrates that dimensional exposure produces fundamentally different pathology requiring 3-4 times more medical resources per patient than conventional trauma and generating long-term complications that military planning doesn't account for. The discrepancy between official casualty projections and actual treatment requirements represents a catastrophic medical planning failure that will overwhelm institutional healthcare systems as rift exposure events intensify during military operations.

## Conventional Classification

[[Auralis]] military documentation classified [[The Thel Reach Incursion|Thel Reach]] incident casualties as:
- **17 KIA** (killed in action)
- **26 wounded** (requiring medical treatment)
- **Total casualty count: 43**
- **Casualty ratio: 1:1.5 (fatalities:wounded)**
- **Projected resource requirement: Standard trauma protocols, 3-7 day treatment cycle for wounded**

This classification follows conventional military medical metrics designed for combat injury assessment. The framework assumes:
- Fatalities are immediate and final
- Wounded require acute treatment followed by recovery or discharge
- Resource requirements scale linearly with casualty counts
- Treatment protocols exist for injury types encountered
- Recovery timelines are predictable based on injury severity

When [[Seln Morra]] — T0 trauma response coordinator with 23 years crisis management experience — received the [[Thel Reach]] casualties at [[Greyveil]] field clinic 31 hours after the incursion, she recognized immediately that none of those assumptions were accurate for dimensional exposure.

## Actual Pathology

The "26 wounded" classification concealed fundamentally different medical realities:

### Dimensional Trauma (8 patients)

Flux exposure from proximity to active rift breach produces progressive tissue degradation that doesn't stop when initial injury occurs. Eight [[Thel Reach]] survivors presented with ongoing cellular breakdown at microscopic level — living tissue slowly converting toward unstable energy states incompatible with biological function.

**Symptoms**: 
- Visible Flux luminescence beneath skin (tissue emitting light as cellular structures destabilize)
- Progressive pain intensification as nerve tissue degrades
- Organ function decline measurable hour-by-hour
- Cognitive impairment as neural tissue disrupts

**Treatment**: No established medical protocols exist. [[Seln Morra]] improvised using Flux suppression techniques normally applied for magical overexertion, combined with aggressive tissue stabilization interventions. Five of eight patients stabilized after 36-48 hours intensive intervention. Three required ongoing specialist care in [[Valdenmoor]] because field clinic capacity was insufficient.

**Prognosis**: All eight face permanent Flux sensitivity — reduced tolerance for magical energy exposure, chronic pain from residual tissue damage, elevated risk for secondary complications if exposed to future rift events. None will fully recover to pre-incursion health baseline.

**Resource requirement**: 40-60 times higher than standard trauma care during acute phase. Ongoing specialist monitoring required indefinitely.

### Flux Burn Penetration (12 patients)

Direct demon contact produces injuries that superficially resemble thermal burns but involve magical energy penetration through multiple tissue layers. Unlike thermal burns that damage from surface inward, Flux burns disrupt tissue at all depths simultaneously.

**Symptoms**:
- Apparent surface wounds with underlying tissue damage disproportionate to visible injury extent
- Infection resistance failure (damaged tissue can't mount normal immune response)
- Healing process dysfunction (standard wound recovery mechanisms don't function properly for Flux-damaged tissue)
- Pain management resistance (conventional analgesics ineffective for magical nerve damage)

**Treatment**: Combination of physical wound management and Flux-based healing techniques. Requires medical practitioners with both conventional training and T2+ Healing discipline capacity. [[Greyveil]] clinic had one qualified practitioner; [[Seln Morra]] had to coordinate emergency referrals to [[Valdenmoor]] for specialist intervention.

**Prognosis**: 6-8 week recovery cycle for moderate cases, permanent scarring and reduced function common. Severe cases face amputation risk when tissue damage exceeds healing capacity.

**Resource requirement**: 8-12 times higher than thermal burn treatment due to specialist practitioner scarcity and extended treatment cycles.

### Psychological Trauma - Geometry Violation Exposure (6 patients)

Witnessing dimensional breach events exposes human nervous systems to perceptual experiences they're not designed to process. Spatial geometry violations, non-Euclidean entity behavior, physics-defying motion — the cognitive load of processing impossible sensory input produces psychological damage distinct from conventional trauma.

**Symptoms**:
- Persistent perceptual disturbances (visual processing errors, spatial disorientation)
- Intrusive recollection that's visceral rather than memory-based (brain attempting to re-process unprocessable experience)
- Reality anchoring difficulty (uncertainty about whether current perception is reliable)
- Acute anxiety triggered by normal Flux usage (any magical activity feels like precursor to dimensional breach)

**Treatment**: Conventional psychiatric trauma protocols prove inadequate. [[Seln Morra]] coordinated specialist psychiatric referral to practitioners experienced with anomalous exposure cases (rare specialty, limited capacity).

**Prognosis**: Extended recovery timeline (months to years), high risk for permanent psychological impairment, some patients never regain full reality-processing confidence.

**Resource requirement**: Specialized psychiatric care over extended timelines — far exceeding standard trauma counseling capacity.

## The Hidden Casualties

Beyond the documented 26 wounded, [[Seln Morra]]'s analysis identified additional casualty categories that military classification didn't capture:

**Latent Deterioration Cases**: Four survivors who initially presented as uninjured began showing delayed-onset dimensional trauma symptoms 48-72 hours post-exposure. Their proximity to the rift during active phase was marginal — not close enough for immediate injury — but sufficient for tissue-level Flux disruption that manifested later. 

Military casualty count: 0 (not injured during incursion)  
Actual medical reality: 4 additional patients requiring intensive intervention

**Psychological Impact - Broader Population**: All 43 survivors experienced the incursion. The 6 classified with acute psychological trauma were the most severe cases requiring immediate psychiatric intervention. [[Seln Morra]]'s assessment: 20-25 survivors face clinically significant psychological impairment requiring professional support over months.

Military casualty count: 6 (acute cases only)  
Actual medical reality: 20-25 requiring extended care

**Caregiver Trauma**: Survivors who provided emergency medical response during the 9-hour institutional response delay witnessed severe injuries, performed improvised treatment under crisis conditions, and held dying neighbors. [[Merra Teld]], who maintained Force barrier at storage hall entrance after [[Kel Dorst]] died, experienced both direct rift exposure and extreme psychological stress from responsibility for 43 lives. She received no psychiatric assessment because she functioned too competently during crisis — competence concealed trauma until delayed symptoms emerged.

Military casualty count: 0 (functional capacity maintained)  
Actual medical reality: 8-10 survivors providing crisis response face caregiver trauma requiring professional support

**Community Dissolution**: [[Thel Reach]] was a functional community of 400 residents. Seventeen died. The settlement's social fabric — trust, cooperative relationships, institutional confidence — suffered catastrophic damage. Survivors face extended grief processes, community reconstruction challenges, and institutional betrayal trauma from recognition that the incursion was preventable.

Military casualty count: Uncategorized (not medical metric)  
Actual social reality: 383 survivors face long-term community trauma impacts

## The Projection Error

[[Seln Morra]] performed detailed resource requirement analysis comparing official military planning assumptions against actual treatment needs for [[Thel Reach]] casualties:

**Official Projection** (per 100 rift casualties):
- 60 fatalities, 40 wounded
- Resource requirement: 120-180 practitioner-hours total
- Treatment timeline: 5-7 days acute phase
- Recovery: 80% wounded return to function within 30 days
- Specialist capacity: Standard trauma protocols, no specialist shortage anticipated

**Actual Reality** ([[Thel Reach]] data scaled to 100 casualties):
- 40 immediate fatalities
- 60 wounded/exposed (broader classification required)
- Resource requirement: 2,400-3,600 practitioner-hours total (20x official projection)
- Treatment timeline: 30-90 days acute phase, extended care indefinitely
- Specialist requirement: Flux-healing practitioners (regional capacity ~150 total across both kingdoms)
- Long-term impacts: 80% face permanent health impairment or psychological damage

The discrepancy is catastrophic for medical planning. If [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] military operations generate rift exposure casualties at scale, available medical capacity will be overwhelmed within weeks, not months.

[[Seln Morra]]'s analysis: Current regional medical systems can handle approximately 200 rift casualties per month before specialist capacity depletes and treatment quality collapses. If military operations generate sustained incursion frequency of 3-4 events monthly (plausible given [[Emberfall Probability Maps|historical probability patterns]] and increased Flux usage density), casualty generation will exceed treatment capacity by factor of 3-5.

The result: exponentially increasing mortality as untreated dimensional trauma progresses to fatality, medical system collapse as practitioners burn out under impossible caseload, and societal breakdown as populations lose confidence in institutional capacity to protect them.

## Institutional Response Failure

[[Seln Morra]] documented her analysis and submitted it through official channels to military medical command. The response:

**Day 6**: Acknowledgment of receipt, notation that analysis under review  
**Day 9**: Request for additional supporting documentation  
**Day 12**: No substantive response, generic statement that "medical planning incorporates best available data"  
**Day 15**: [[Seln Morra]] directly contacted military medical planning coordinator, provided detailed breakdown  
**Day 16**: Coordinator thanked her for input, stated that operational planning reflects multiple data sources  

No change to official casualty projections occurred. No adjustment to medical resource allocation was implemented. No specialist practitioner mobilization beyond standard military medical staffing took place.

[[Seln Morra]] realized the institutions weren't ignoring her analysis because they had better information — they were ignoring it because acting on it would require admitting that current military operations planning was based on catastrophically flawed assumptions. Changing the projections would mean acknowledging that the war they were planning would overwhelm medical systems and produce casualties far beyond what leadership had communicated to populations.

Institutional credibility required maintaining the official projections even when evidence demonstrated they were wrong. Political necessity overrode medical reality.

[[Seln Morra]] stopped submitting reports through official channels. She started preparing for the medical collapse she knew was coming by pre-positioning supplies, training additional field medics in dimensional trauma protocols she'd developed, and building underground coordination networks that could function when official systems failed.

She's not trying to stop the war anymore. She's trying to keep as many people alive as possible when the war makes her predictions come true.

Official military casualty projections remain unchanged. [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] military medical planning continues operating on assumptions that [[Seln Morra]]'s analysis demonstrates are catastrophically wrong.

The [[Thel Reach]] casualties remain in various treatment stages:

- 8 Flux burn patients recovering (4-6 weeks remaining on treatment cycles)
- 6 acute psychological trauma patients in psychiatric care (months-years timeline)
- 20+ broader population facing delayed psychological impacts (minimal institutional support)
- 383 survivors continuing to inhabit [[Thel Reach]] under military administration

[[Seln Morra]] maintains detailed documentation of every treatment intervention, resource requirement, timeline, and outcome. The documentation is precise, defensible, and demonstrates unambiguously that military medical planning is based on false assumptions.

She has no way to force institutional leadership to acknowledge the evidence. What she has is preparation for the systemic failure she's certain is coming, and the professional competence to document exactly who knew what when the casualties start overwhelming capacity.

Someone needs to make institutional leadership understand that medical logistics determine whether war produces manageable casualties or societal collapse. If no one in authority will listen before the crisis, at least there will be documentation proving the warning was provided and ignored.

The seventeen dead at [[Thel Reach]] were preventable. The thousands dead to come from medical system collapse will be just as preventable, and just as ignored, unless someone forces accountability before catastrophe becomes inevitable.
