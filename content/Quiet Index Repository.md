The Quiet Index Repository is a restricted archival subsection maintained inside Terravelle Guild of Letters holdings in [[Valdenmoor]], dedicated to preserving dispersed records tied to [[The Persecution Era]] without exposing current communities to unnecessary reputational harm.

It is not a public museum and not a moral tribunal. It is a working archive for controlled historical reference, cross-checking fragmentary testimony, and supporting present-day policy work where rumor-driven social panic remains a documented risk.

## Why It Exists

For most of the Contact period, persecution-era memory survived unevenly through family ledgers, route logs, and oral recitations. Formal institutions held fragments but avoided consolidation, partly from neglect and partly from concern that naming historic perpetrators by settlement could trigger defensive politics that overshadowed practical lessons.

The Quiet Index was created as a compromise framework:

- Preserve records before they vanish through household turnover and material decay
- De-identify sensitive location/person details in general references
- Maintain full-detail versions under restricted access for qualified researchers
- Prioritize methodological lessons (how panic spread, how records failed, what prevented escalation)

The repository's founding charter language emphasizes prevention over blame.

## Collection Scope

Current holdings include:

- Family memory ledgers from lineages such as [[Brendt Lineage]] and partial extracts from [[Cors Lineage]] route books
- Site survey reports from [[Narrowglass Cellar]] and [[Rimewell Loft]]
- Comparative incident tables built from settlement records in both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]
- Copies of threshold recitation notes associated with [[Seln Lineage]] oral practice
- Administrative correspondence showing how later guild systems replaced informal suspicion controls with certification structures

Each item receives two entries: a public-facing summary record and a restricted-detail record. The summary entry is enough for general historical orientation. The restricted entry preserves names, route details, and contextual annotations that could be misused if circulated casually.

## Stewardship

The repository is managed by a small records team under guild oversight. The lead curator is Dren Saln, [[Control Tier|Tier 2]], previously a rural ledger clerk in [[Corva Vale]] known for clean cross-ledger reconciliation under sparse source conditions.

Dren's role is technical and procedural:

- Verify provenance of incoming materials
- Separate transcription from interpretation
- Flag uncertainty explicitly instead of smoothing contradictions
- Control access tiers based on research purpose and harm-risk review

He is not a public historian and does not publish narrative syntheses. His team provides materials to scholars and policy analysts who do that work.

## Access Model

Access is stratified:

- **Open reference layer**: chronology summaries, methodological notes, de-identified case patterns
- **Research layer**: controlled access for accredited scholars with supervised note handling
- **Restricted layer**: full-detail records available only with dual authorization (guild plus review panel)

The review panel includes one external observer from [[Auralis]] archive networks on rotating term, intended to reduce kingdom-bias in access decisions for cross-border material.

## Present-Day Use

The Quiet Index supports three active use cases:

- Historical scholarship in [[History of Solumora]] and related teaching material
- Administrative training for incident documentation in rural and border communities
- Risk-analysis support for institutions handling high-volatility claims around [[Control Tier]] disparities

In practice, the repository is most valuable when modern systems begin to repeat old failure modes: anonymous accusation chains, preventive exclusion language, and policy responses built on unverified capability assumptions.

## Critiques

Some critics argue the Index is too cautious and should publish full names for accountability. Others argue the repository should not exist at all because revisiting persecution records can reopen local grievance. The stewardship position is procedural: selective memory creates repeated mistakes; careless disclosure creates new harm. Controlled access is the least-worst option.

The archive does not claim to settle moral arguments. It claims to keep evidence available so present institutions cannot pretend the pattern was unknowable.

## Why It Matters

The Quiet Index Repository is the institutional memory keeper for a period that mostly survived outside institutions. Its value is not symbolic closure. Its value is continuity of evidence and method.

When fear rises, people still reach for fast stories. The repository's purpose is to ensure slower, documented alternatives remain possible.
