**Border Coordination Offices** facilitate informal cooperation between [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] law enforcement agencies when crimes cross kingdom boundaries. They are not treaty organizations with formal legal authority but rather administrative contact points enabling information sharing and cooperative investigation when both kingdoms have shared interest in resolving cases.

## Purpose and Function

Cross-border crimes create jurisdictional problems neither kingdom can solve independently. A merchant defrauded in [[Auralis]] by a [[Terravelle]] practitioner cannot be compensated unless Terravelle cooperates. A fugitive fleeing from [[Valdenmoor]] to [[Solhaven]] cannot be arrested unless both kingdoms coordinate. Border Coordination Offices exist to manage these coordination needs without requiring formal diplomatic negotiation for every case.

**Information Sharing:** Offices exchange investigation details when cases involve parties or evidence in both kingdoms. An [[Auralis Enforcement Bureau]] investigator pursuing a fraud case can request information from Terravelle offices about suspects, witnesses, or transactions occurring in Terravelle territory.

**Witness Coordination:** When trials require testimony from witnesses in the other kingdom, offices facilitate contact and voluntary witness travel. They cannot compel witnesses to testify but can arrange logistics and safe passage.

**Evidence Exchange:** Physical evidence located in one kingdom but needed for prosecution in the other can be transferred through coordination offices, maintaining documented chain of custody acceptable to both kingdoms' courts.

**Fugitive Notifications:** When suspects flee across borders, offices notify the other kingdom's enforcement agencies. There is no formal extradition, but informal cooperation sometimes results in fugitives being located and voluntarily returned.

**Case Status Updates:** Offices track ongoing cross-border investigations and provide status updates to counterpart agencies, enabling both kingdoms to remain informed about cases affecting their citizens or territories.

## Organizational Structure

Each kingdom maintains 3-4 coordination offices in border regions and major ports:

**Auralis Offices:**
- [[Solhaven]] office (western coast, maritime coordination)
- Helven office (eastern border, overland routes)
- [[Halveth]] office ([[Equatorial Desert|desert]] crossing coordination)

**Terravelle Offices:**
- [[Valdenmoor]] office (central administrative hub)
- [[Ashford]] office (eastern port, maritime coordination)
- [[Wolfpoint]] office (northern trade corridor)

Each office employs 4-8 personnel: administrative coordinators, translators, legal specialists familiar with both kingdoms' systems, and investigators with cross-border case experience. Personnel are typically [[Control Tier|T2-T3]] practitioners with communication skills, diplomatic temperament, and understanding of institutional differences between kingdoms.

Offices report to their respective kingdom enforcement agencies ([[Auralis Enforcement Bureau]] or Terravelle civic enforcement structures) but operate with significant autonomy because rapid coordination requires local decision-making authority.

## Limitations

Border Coordination Offices cannot:

**Compel Cooperation:** Neither kingdom is obligated to honor coordination requests. If Terravelle declines to share evidence or locate witnesses, Auralis offices cannot force compliance. Cooperation is voluntary and depends on perceived mutual benefit.

**Conduct Enforcement:** Offices have no arrest authority, cannot conduct investigations independently, and cannot impose legal consequences. They facilitate cooperation between agencies that do have enforcement authority.

**Override Jurisdictional Boundaries:** When cases involve legal questions about which kingdom has jurisdiction, offices cannot resolve them. Jurisdictional disputes escalate to diplomatic negotiation or remain unresolved.

**Handle Sensitive Cases:** Politically sensitive cases, espionage accusations, or crimes involving kingdom officials are typically excluded from coordination office involvement because neither kingdom trusts informal cooperation channels for high-stakes situations.

**Guarantee Outcomes:** Even when offices successfully facilitate cooperation, courts in either kingdom may still fail to convict, victims may not receive compensation, or fugitives may evade capture. Offices improve coordination but cannot control final outcomes.

## Cultural Differences in Operation

[[Auralis]] coordination offices operate formally. They document all requests, track case status systematically, and follow established procedures for information exchange. Requests are written, communication is official, and accountability is institutional.

[[Terravelle]] coordination offices operate pragmatically. They prefer direct personal communication, adapt procedures to specific cases, and prioritize speed over documentation. Requests are often verbal, relationships between office personnel matter more than formal protocols, and accountability is personal rather than institutional.

These cultural differences create friction. Auralis offices want documented records for every exchange. Terravelle offices view extensive documentation as bureaucratic waste that slows practical cooperation. Neither approach is wrong, but the mismatch complicates coordination.

Over time, personnel in coordination offices develop working relationships that bridge cultural gaps. An Auralis coordinator who has worked with specific Terravelle coordinators for years learns to accept verbal agreements and trust personal accountability. A Terravelle coordinator working with Auralis offices learns to provide written documentation when requested. These personal relationships make border coordination function despite systemic cultural incompatibility.

## Success Rate

Border Coordination Offices successfully resolve perhaps 20-30% of cross-border cases brought to them. Successful cases typically involve:
- Clear evidence in both kingdoms (no disputes about facts)
- Voluntary cooperation from suspects or witnesses
- Cases where both kingdoms have shared interest in resolution
- Low political sensitivity

Failed cases typically involve:
- Jurisdictional disputes neither kingdom will concede
- Suspects who refuse voluntary cooperation and cannot be compelled
- Evidence located in territory the other kingdom will not access
- Political sensitivity causing either kingdom to block cooperation
- Resource constraints preventing thorough investigation

This success rate is not impressive compared to domestic law enforcement, but it is significantly better than zero cooperation. Without coordination offices, cross-border crimes would almost never be prosecuted.

## Notable Cases Handled

**The Ashford Maritime Fraud:** A shipping operator defrauded clients in both kingdoms through false delivery documentation. Coordination offices shared evidence from both sides, enabling [[Auralis Civic Courts]] to convict and Terravelle guilds to expel the operator from Maritime Guild. Full restitution to victims in both kingdoms.

**The Valdenmoor Fugitive Recovery:** An Auralis merchant fled to Valdenmoor after embezzling 80,000 [[Watts]] from business partners. Coordination offices located the fugitive, facilitated voluntary return to Auralis, and enabled prosecution and asset recovery. Fugitive was incentivized to return through Terravelle guild pressure — remaining in Valdenmoor without guild standing was not viable long-term.

**The Desert Salvage Dispute:** [[Terravelle Guild System|Terravelle guild]] members and Auralis salvage operators disputed claim rights to abandoned equipment in [[Equatorial Desert]]. Coordination offices mediated (not legally binding but accepted by both parties), establishing shared salvage rights and preventing conflict escalation.

## Political Context

Border Coordination Offices exist in context of broader [[Auralis]]-[[Terravelle]] tension. Both kingdoms value the offices because they reduce friction and provide diplomatic channel for managing routine cross-border issues. However, offices are always vulnerable to political decisions.

If diplomatic relations worsen, coordination offices face pressure to restrict cooperation or cease operation entirely. During [[The Three Near-Wars]] periods, coordination offices reduced activity significantly, processing only the most routine requests and avoiding anything politically sensitive.

Currently, offices operate at moderate capacity. Relations between kingdoms are tense but not openly hostile. Offices process routine cases, share non-sensitive information, and maintain professional relationships. Both kingdoms recognize that eliminating coordination offices would make border management harder without providing any strategic advantage.

## Future Trajectory

Border Coordination Offices will continue functioning as long as diplomatic relations between kingdoms remain stable enough to value cooperation. If relations deteriorate into open conflict, offices will cease operation immediately. If relations improve substantially, offices might expand into formal treaty organizations with binding authority — though neither kingdom has proposed this expansion, and both seem content with current informal arrangement.

The offices represent pragmatic middle ground: cooperation without formal commitment, coordination without sovereignty compromise, and justice provision for cross-border crimes without requiring either kingdom to trust the other completely.
