_(Council Member, Auralis — Culmination Faction)_

**Oren Vael** has never been to [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] and has no intention of going. He has also, over eleven years, routed more financial support to desert expedition work than any other individual in [[Auralis]] outside of the council's formal budget, through a network of merchant intermediaries that keeps his name off the documentation. He considers this a reasonable division of responsibilities: some people go into the desert, and some people make it possible for those people to go into the desert. He is emphatically the latter.

## Character

Oren is fifty-eight, commercially minded in the particular way of someone whose family has been commercially minded for three generations, and entirely practical about the question of why he supports the Culmination Faction. The council provides access. Access provides contracts. Contracts generate returns. The Culmination goal itself — the recovery of whatever the ancient civilization left behind — is, if it ever produces results, potentially the most commercially significant event in the history of [[Solumora]]. He wants to be positioned for that. He does not need to believe in it to recognize it as a legitimate investment thesis.

He is not cynical about this. He finds the ruins research genuinely interesting, in the way he finds any long-duration high-risk high-reward proposition interesting. The true believers among the Culmination Faction sometimes find his pragmatism off-putting. He finds their need for everyone to share their conviction equally off-putting. They have reached an accommodation.

His [[Flux]] ability is T2, used almost entirely for transaction verification — reading [[Birth Runes|birth runes]], confirming ledger entries, the practical commerce applications that a merchant house trains into everyone regardless of ability. He has not needed more than this and does not particularly want it.

## The Proxy Network

The merchant intermediaries through whom Oren funds expedition equipment are not a secret in any formal sense — several council members are aware that Vael money moves through certain channels — but the documentation does not trace back to him directly, and that distance is intentional. It is not primarily for concealment from the council. It is for concealment from [[Terravelle]], whose trade monitors he has reason to believe are more attentive to money moving toward desert expedition work than the official relationship between the kingdoms would warrant.

He has mentioned this concern to [[Drest]] once, in passing, without detail. Drest noted it without apparent interest. Oren has not raised it again.
