The Terravelle Desert Trade Office is a small semi-official body operating under the Guild Council structure of [[Terravelle]]. It manages the rare authorized crossing attempts initiated from the Terravelle side of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] — issuing crossing permits, coordinating guide arrangements, and maintaining the northern staging infrastructure that Terravelle has never invested in as heavily as [[Auralis]] has invested in [[Halveth]].

## Function

Terravelle treats the desert as a problem to manage, not a resource to exploit. The Trade Office reflects this: it exists not to enable crossings but to ensure that the crossings that happen anyway are documented, survive at a rate high enough to not become a political embarrassment, and return goods or information that justify the cost.

An authorized crossing permit from the Trade Office requires:
- Statement of purpose and declared cargo (both ways)
- Proof of minimum Flux capability (T2 for any crossing party member; T3 recommended for lead caster)
- Bonding deposit against Terravelle emergency extraction costs if applicable
- Route plan reviewed against current Trade Office conditions data

The Office's conditions data is less current than the [[Halveth Cooperative]]'s. The Cooperative is on the ground. The Office works from guide returns and the occasional contracted intelligence purchase from Compact-adjacent operators who sell crossing condition reports without disclosing how they know what they know.

## Northern Approaches

The Office maintains informal infrastructure at two northern staging points — supply depots and basic shelter, not the built-up logistics operation that Halveth represents. These are the entry points for [[The Northern Narrows]], the collection of Terravelle-side crossing routes used when southern approaches are unsuitable or unavailable.

Guides for northern-side crossings are primarily sourced from the [[Halveth Cooperative]] under cross-booking arrangements, paid at standard Cooperative rates. There are no Terravelle-native desert guides operating at the Cooperative's qualification level. This is a function of Terravelle's historical indifference to the crossing trade; it produces competent navigators for its own terrain, not desert specialists.

## Political Reality

The Trade Office's actual significance is less in the crossings it enables and more in what it represents: Terravelle's acknowledgment that the desert cannot be ignored entirely. The [[Expansion Faction]] in Auralis has pressed for years on Terravelle's comparative under-investment in desert access, using it as evidence of strategic passivity. The Trade Office is partly a response to this pressure — a visible institutional presence that allows Terravelle to point at something when the question is asked.

Whether it does anything useful is a question the staff are not encouraged to examine closely.
