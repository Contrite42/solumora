**Drev Oln** is fifty-two years old and runs a mid-sized inn on [[Ashford]]'s main commercial street. [[Control Tier|T1]]. He has operated the inn for fourteen years, having purchased it from the previous owner with savings from fifteen years working in the city's provisioning trade. The inn is not the most expensive establishment in Ashford and not the cheapest. It occupies the band where travelers who have been on the northern road for weeks will pay for a room that is reliably clean and food that is reliably adequate, which in practice is most travelers.

An innkeeper in Ashford sees everything that moves through the city without being interesting enough to be observed in return. Drev has been watching this for fourteen years. He has developed a working taxonomy of traveler types — not formal, not recorded anywhere, just a practiced reading of who books which room and what they order and how they arrange themselves in the common room and whether they want conversation or privacy. Most travelers want what they present as wanting. A small category want something else, or are trying not to be visible about what they actually want, and these are the ones he finds interesting to read without acting on.

He has not repeated anything he has heard in his common room to anyone official and has no intention of doing so. He has not refused business to anyone who could pay, and has no intention of doing so. These two policies have served him adequately for fourteen years and he does not expect them to stop serving him adequately.

What he has noticed in the past year: the ratio of travelers coming south from northern Terravelle has increased. The documentation they carry is standard but their questions about Ashford's Auralis border crossing are more specific than casual travelers' questions usually are. He has answered them accurately and continued making their tea.
