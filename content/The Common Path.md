The Common Path is not a faction in the organized sense. It is a name that has attached itself, over about thirty years, to a persistent current of political advocacy among T0–T2 practitioners in [[Auralis]] who believe the kingdom's tier-based social hierarchy is a policy choice, not a natural order, and one that deserves to be challenged.

## What They Argue

[[Auralis]]'s social structure assigns worth roughly according to [[Control Tier]]. A T5 practitioner commands different economic opportunity, social standing, and institutional access than a T1 practitioner. This is not formally codified in law — there is no statute that says T0 citizens have fewer rights. But the [[Ascendant Path]]'s theological framework, which treats tier advancement as spiritual progress and tier ceiling as a measure of a person's relationship with [[Flux]], provides a legitimizing narrative that the social hierarchy exploits.

The Common Path's core argument is that tier is a random biological distribution, not a moral measurement. A T0 person who lives well, contributes to their community, and participates in the [[Watts]] economy is not in a lower spiritual state than a T5 practitioner who happens to have been born with higher Flux capacity. The Ascendant Path's framing has been weaponized to justify material inequality, and the weaponization should be named and resisted.

## What They Actually Do

Most Common Path activity is local and unglamorous: petitions to City Councils for tier-neutral civic access, challenges to institutional policies that explicitly require tier thresholds for things unrelated to Flux practice, advocacy for T0 practitioners in the [[Watts]] ledger disputes that sometimes arise when their birth rune registration is questioned.

The network operates through community houses in the lower districts of [[Solhaven]] and several other Auralis cities. There is no central structure. Local groups coordinate loosely and share printed materials — short texts making the Common Path's arguments, written to circulate without attribution.

## What They Have Achieved

Less than they want and more than their opponents are comfortable acknowledging. Several City Council policies in [[Solhaven]] that had explicit tier minimums for civic positions have been quietly revised in the past decade. The Advancement Corps was forced to publicly clarify that field contractor roles did not require tier certification. Neither of these victories was dramatic, and neither was publicly attributed to Common Path pressure. This is how their wins tend to arrive: as administrative revisions, not acknowledgments.

## Who Opposes Them

The [[Expansion Faction]] regards the Common Path as a distraction from serious governance — and also, privately, as a threat to the social stability that keeps low-tier Auralis practitioners too occupied with local disputes to focus on Council-level politics. The Ascendant Path establishment regards them as a theological problem. [[Cassia]] has read their arguments and found them largely correct, which she has not said to anyone in the Council.
