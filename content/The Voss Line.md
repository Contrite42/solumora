The Voss Line is the founding and ruling family of [[Wolfpoint]], now seven generations old. They are soldier scholars by origin and by ongoing identity — the military heritage of the founders has not faded into mere tradition but remains a functional part of how Wolfpoint operates and how the Voss family leads it.

## Origins

The first Voss — the name used for the founding generation collectively, though the individual members had their own names — were a group of soldiers and [[Flux Users|Scribes]] who had served in [[Terravelle]]'s northern campaigns. They were not deserters or exiles. They left with their service completed and their reputations intact, and they went north because the problem they wanted to solve required distance from institutional constraints.

The problem: [[Sigils]] did not work reliably on battlefield equipment. Curved surfaces, field conditions, time pressure, the physical damage that combat inflicted on inscribed objects — all of it combined to make standard sigil geometry an unreliable tool for the application they cared most about. They had spent careers working around this limitation. Going north was the decision to solve it instead.

## Succession

Leadership of Wolfpoint passes within the Voss family, but not by birth order. The criterion is demonstrated [[Hypertext]] mastery — the ability to write, test, and teach functional Hypertext sequences at a level that reflects genuine understanding rather than memorized patterns. This test is administered informally and collectively: the family assesses, discusses, and reaches consensus. There have been three cases in seven generations where the eldest child did not inherit because a sibling was more capable. None of these transitions produced lasting conflict. The Voss family is, among its other qualities, pragmatic about itself.

## The Current Voss

The current head of the Voss line is **Sera Voss**, fifty-one years old, the seventh-generation leader of [[Wolfpoint]]. She is the first Voss to formally document [[Hypertext]] sequences in transmissible form — the project that will, if completed, transform Hypertext from an oral and apprenticeship tradition into something that can be copied, distributed, and learned at a distance.

She has not decided what to do with that document when it is finished. [[Auralis]] has made its interest clear. [[Terravelle]] has not officially asked but would almost certainly want access. The independent scholars who cycle through Wolfpoint have their own ideas.

Sera Voss is in no hurry. The document is not finished yet, and the decision of what to do with it can wait until it is.

Her [[Control Tier]] is T5 — the highest in the Voss line in three generations. She does not display this, consistent with the family's tradition, but it is known within Wolfpoint and has contributed to the respect she commands there.

## The Voss Approach to Flux

The Voss family does not discuss [[Flux]] in spiritual terms. They discuss it in technical terms. It is a medium, a resource, a language — and the purpose of work is to speak it more precisely, waste less of it, and understand more of what it can say.

This is not coldness. The Voss family is, by all accounts of people who have spent time in [[Wolfpoint]], warm and hospitable within the bounds of their particular culture. It is simply a different orientation — one that treats mysticism about Flux the way a skilled craftsperson treats mysticism about their tools. Respectful of what they do not know. Uninterested in romanticizing the not-knowing.
