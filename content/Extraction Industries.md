# Extraction Industries

The extraction industries constitute the foundational resource production segment of [[Solumora]]'s economy, converting geological deposits and biological populations into raw materials that supply manufacturing, preservation, and institutional operations across both kingdoms. Operating at a 5-10% economic contribution by Watt volume, extraction remains disproportionately critical to other sectors' functionality — metalwork cannot proceed without ore, preservation cannot proceed without salt, craftswork cannot proceed without specialized materials.

The sector is deliberately fragmented across small-scale independent operators (40% of production), medium-scale commercial operations (40%), and large institutional extraction (20%), creating competitive markets where pricing pressures and accessibility favor institutional buyers. Contemporary military demand is collapsing this balance, with institutional requisitions and conscription systematically eliminating independent and medium-scale operators.

## Categories of Extraction

**[[Mining Operations]]** — Underground and surface mineral extraction producing metal ores, Flux-reactive materials, and specialty minerals. Primary locations: mineral-dense regions accessible to established settlements. Production scale: 8-15 tons annual output at major sites, 2-5 tons at medium operations, <1 ton from small independent efforts. High capital requirements and resource-intensive operations concentrated in institutionally-backed sites.

**[[Logging Operations]]** — Timber harvest and forestry management producing construction lumber, specialized woods for metalwork, and fuel stock. Primary locations: accessible forest belts surrounding settlements (Maren Valley, northern territories, coastal regions). Production scale: 100-200 cords annual output at major sites, 20-50 cords at medium operations. Seasonal cycle with autumn/winter concentration and spring-summer processing. Environmental degradation from acceleration represents emerging sustainability concern.

**[[Salt Extraction]]** — Coastal salt harvest operations producing preservation salt for food processing and industrial applications. Primary locations: marine-accessible coastal regions (Hedun district primary supplier, secondary operations in scattered coastal settlements). Production scale: 40-80 tons annually at major Hedun operations, 5-20 tons at secondary sites. Non-renewable in some regions; sustainable in others with managed extraction.

**[[Creature Harvesting]]** — Systematic hunting and harvesting of high-value creatures (Bloodshrike venom, Flux Scorpion carapace, mountain game meat) producing pharmaceutical materials, craftwork components, and food products. Primary locations: biologically active regions (mountain territories, desert periphery, coastal zones). Production scale: highly variable by creature type; Bloodshrike venom represents premium commodity with sustained shortage. Contemporary military demand for creature venom approaching unsustainable 120-130% of sustainable yield.

**[[Ruin Salvage]]** — Systematic recovery of pre-collapse artifacts, construction materials, and salvaged components from ancient sites. Primary locations: desert interior ruins, scattered archaeological sites in inhabited regions. Production scale: highly irregular; dependent on expedition capability and discovery frequency. High-value materials (Flux-reactive metals, pre-collapse ceramics) command premium pricing despite irregular supply.

## Market Structure

Extraction markets stratify by commodity type:

**Bulk commodities** (grain, basic timber, standard ore) sell through institutional purchasing channels or merchant networks, with pricing set by supply volume and buyer leverage. Institutional buyers command 30-40% discounts through procurement authority and volume aggregation.

**Specialty commodities** (creature venom, Flux-reactive materials, salvaged pre-collapse artifacts) operate in premium markets with stable high pricing, limited supply, and institutional or high-tier practitioner buyers. Military demand for specialty materials has created persistent shortage conditions.

**By-products** (sawdust for fuel, bone and hide from processed creatures, mining tailings for construction material) supply secondary markets with minimal oversight. Price volatility in by-product markets sometimes exceeds primary commodity pricing.

## Labor Structure

Extraction operations employ three labor tiers:

**Field operators** (T0-T2 practitioners, non-practitioners) performing physical labor — mining, timber falling, creature processing, salvage operations. Comprise 60-70% of operational workforce. Conscription disproportionately affects this tier.

**Site management** (T2-T3 practitioners) coordinating workflow, equipment maintenance, safety protocol enforcement, and production monitoring. Comprise 15-20% of workforce. Institutional consolidation increasingly divert these practitioners toward military-support roles.

**Specialist operations** (T3-T4 practitioners) performing Flux-enhanced extraction (rapid ore processing, creature venom extraction with containment), quality control, and resource evaluation. Comprise 5-15% of workforce depending on commodity type. Military conscription creates critical shortage in specialist tier.

## Contemporary Pressure

**Military demand override:** Institutional requisition quotas and conscription are systematically converting peacetime extraction operations toward military supply focus. Non-military materials production declining 20-30% as operations redirect capacity toward military-priority materials (metals for weapons, creature venom for defensive applications, Flux-reactive minerals for military Flux infrastructure).

**Environmental degradation:** Extraction acceleration over past 3-5 years approaching sustainability boundaries in multiple commodity categories. Timber depletion visible in formerly reliable logging regions, creature populations in Bloodshrike and Flux Scorpion showing reproduction decline suggesting population stress, mineral operations expanding into marginal geological zones with lower-grade output and higher processing requirements.

**Institutional monopolization:** Large institutional operations are consolidating commodity categories through preferential access to military purchasing, exclusive claims on high-value materials, and capital deployment that undersells independent operators. Medium-scale and independent operations are experiencing margin compression and consolidation pressure.

**Labor conscription and voluntary departure:** Field and specialist-tier practitioners are conscripted for military operations or voluntarily departing extraction work for perceived safety advantages of non-extraction employment. Replacement recruitment from T0-T1 populations is inadequate to maintain operational capacity, creating operational understaffing across the sector.
