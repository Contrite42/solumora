The current period — the last thirty years of [[Eddan Voss]]'s reign and the last twenty of [[Cassia]]'s — is the most politically tense in the Contact era. Not because the kingdoms have never been this close to conflict before, but because the mechanisms that have historically prevented conflict are showing signs of strain that previous periods have not tested in the same way simultaneously.

## What Has Changed

Three things have changed in the current period that were not true in previous tense periods.

**The crossing capability gap has narrowed.** When [[Auralis]] first developed reliable crossing technology, the asymmetry in desert capability was a stabilizing factor — [[Terravelle]] could not easily project force south, which removed the motivation to try, and Auralis's crossing capability gave it access to [[Terravelle]]'s northern territory only in the limited logistical sense that it could send diplomatic and trade expeditions. Over two centuries, the gap has narrowed considerably. Terravelle now has crossing capability. The desert still makes military force projection enormously difficult for both sides, but "enormously difficult" has replaced "effectively impossible" as the constraint.

**Auralis has something it didn't have before.** The ruins research, the anomalous sites, the [[The Culmination Faction|Culmination Faction]]'s belief that the ancient civilization's achievement is recoverable — this is not a new aspiration, but the corps expeditions of the last two decades have produced results that have moved it from theoretical to practical. The [[The Advancement Corps|Advancement Corps]] has been operating at an intensity that suggests something has been found, or is close to being found. What it is, and what finding it would mean for the strategic balance, is the specific uncertainty that Eddan's advisors cannot assess from outside.

**The [[Expansion Faction]] has been building for forty years.** The [[The Three Near-Wars|Ashford Blockade]], approximately forty-five years ago, was the Expansion Faction's overreach in the third near-war, and it failed when Terravelle rerouted trade. [[Drest]], then a junior faction member, drew a specific lesson from that failure: economic pressure without military capability to back it is reversible. The current period of Expansion Faction activity — the trade route disruptions, the border friction, the slow accumulation of strategic positioning — is being conducted with a patience and consistency that suggests it is preparation rather than an end in itself.

## The Constraints That Remain

Neither of these changes has removed the constraints that have prevented actual war.

The desert is harder to cross than it used to be for large forces, but it is still an enormous logistical problem. Neither kingdom has demonstrated the capability to project meaningful military force across it. Guerrilla operations, intelligence operations, and the kind of slow strategic pressure that does not require armies are still more practical than invasion.

Economic interdependence continues to provide both kingdoms with reasons to stop short of open conflict. The trade routes that carry [[Terravelle]]'s goods to [[Solhaven]] and [[Auralis]]'s high-tier products to [[Valdenmoor]] generate enough revenue that disrupting them is a cost both sides feel.

Eddan's personal pragmatism is a constraint that does not appear in official strategic assessments but is real: he has consistently declined to escalate beyond what he calculates is productive, and the escalation threshold he sets is high. How much longer he remains in a position to set that threshold, and whether his successors would set it at the same level, is an uncertainty that Auralis's council has certainly factored into its planning.

## The Specific Risks

The risks are not abstract. Several specific mechanisms could convert the current tension into something more serious.

A ruins discovery that gives [[Auralis]] a decisive military or strategic advantage would change the calculation immediately. Eddan would face a situation where the usual "manage quietly and wait" approach produces a progressively worse outcome, creating pressure for a response that the usual approach is not designed to produce.

An Expansion Faction miscalculation — pushing too hard on a trade disruption or border friction that produces a Terravellian response that requires Auralis to either back down publicly or escalate — could trigger the kind of commitment spiral that the near-wars have narrowly avoided.

A high-tier practitioner incident — rift incursion in a populated area, a [[High Demons|High Demon]] making a visible mistake, something that produced the kind of panic the [[The Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] was built on — would be more destabilizing now than in a lower-tension period, because both kingdoms' governments are already watching each other for signs of escalation.

None of these risks are certain. They are the reasons the current period is the one that people paying attention are most concerned about.
