**Sella Veld** is forty-five years old and works in cargo handling at a warehouse in [[Valdenmoor]]'s river district. [[Control Tier|T1]]. Eight months ago, a Flux channel malfunction during a routine preservation casting at her worksite exposed her and three colleagues to an uncontrolled discharge. Two of her colleagues recovered without lasting effects. One sustained a permanent reduction in Control Tier. Sella sustained Flux pathway damage — a structural disruption to the channels through which she accesses her T1 capacity.

She can still cast. The capacity is intact. The pathways are not. What was previously automatic — a trained gesture she could perform without conscious effort — now requires deliberate reconstruction on each attempt, and the reconstruction is unreliable. Some days it works. Some days it produces the beginning of the cast and then fails. She has not found a consistent correlation between what she does and which outcome she gets, which is the specific thing she finds most difficult.

She is receiving treatment from a guild-certified Flux practitioner — [[Flux Medicine|Flux medicine]] at T3, the standard level for pathway assessment and basic remediation work. The practitioner can read the damaged pathways clearly and reports that the structural disruption is stable rather than progressing. Whether it is reversible is a question the practitioner gives cautious answers to, which Sella has learned to read as probably not, but no one wants to say that directly.

The warehouse has accommodated her reduced capacity within the work's requirements. Cargo handling at her level does not require reliable T1 casting — the T1 application was marginal to the job before the incident, useful rather than necessary. The accommodation is genuine and she does not take it for granted.

What she has lost is not the ability to do her job. It is the clean relationship between intention and result. She had never noticed having it until it was disrupted.
