**Hypertext** is a method of [[Flux]] casting developed in and around [[Wolfpoint]], a settlement in the far north of [[Solumora]]. Where [[Sigils]] arrange [[Spell Variables]] into a fixed geometric form — a shape with defined slots — Hypertext writes them in sequence as a line of logic. It is, in the words of those developing it, less like filling out a form and more like writing the instructions the form was built from.

Both systems use the same symbols. The difference is what those symbols are doing.

In a standard [[Sigils|sigil]], a symbol occupies a position in a shape and defines a variable: this spell uses Heat, this spell reaches [[Reach|Medium]], this spell targets [[Target Spec|Individual]]. The shape is the structure. The symbols fill it.

In Hypertext, the symbols are operators. They define *what happens*, *when it happens*, *under what conditions*, and *what triggers what next*. A line of Hypertext reads less like a recipe and more like a conditional statement — IF this, THEN that, WITH this output, WHILE this condition holds, UNLESS this threshold is crossed.

## Why It Matters

The [[Sigils|sigil]] system, for all its power, is bounded by what a shape can express. A Circle has six outer points. That means six variables, defined once, firing in one configuration. It cannot natively represent branching behavior, sequential effects, nested conditions, or spells that change their own behavior mid-cast based on what they encounter.

Hypertext can represent all of these things. Its ceiling — measured in [[Control Tier]] — is the same as the sigil system. A Tier 4 Hypertext line does not exceed what a Tier 4 Circle can hold. What changes is the *expressiveness*. Hypertext can describe effects that no current sigil shape has the geometry to capture.

A sigil is a powerful tool. Hypertext is the language the tool is written in.

## Structure

Hypertext is written as a linear sequence of symbols, read left to right. Each symbol in the sequence serves one of three roles:

**Condition** — defines when or whether the next effect fires. Uses symbols from [[Persistence]] and [[Target Spec]] as gatekeepers.

**Action** — defines what [[Flux]] does at this step. Uses [[Hook]] and [[Mode]] symbols as operators.

**Parameter** — modifies the action at this step. Uses [[Discipline]], [[Output Mode]], [[Geometry Pattern]], and [[Reach]] symbols to shape the output.

A line of Hypertext can be as short as three symbols or extend into sequences that take minutes to activate fully, chaining through dozens of conditional branches. The [[Control Tier]] required to hold a line of Hypertext scales with its length and complexity — a long chain with many nested conditions demands more sustained Flux precision than almost any standard sigil.

## Learning Hypertext

Hypertext can be taught and learned. It requires no special innate ability beyond what any [[Flux Users|Formulist]] already has — the aptitude for Hypertext is the same aptitude for sigil use, applied differently. What it demands instead is a different way of thinking about [[Flux]]: not as something you shape into a form, but as something you instruct step by step.

Most practitioners describe learning Hypertext as similar to learning to read music after a lifetime of playing by ear. The underlying skill was always there. The notation is new.

## Wolfpoint

[[Wolfpoint]] is the only known center of Hypertext development. Situated in the far north, well beyond the cultural reach of either [[Terravelle]] or [[Auralis]], it operates outside both kingdoms' magical traditions. There is no Terravellian efficiency doctrine shaping what gets developed. There is no Auralis prestige structure rewarding or gatekeeping advancement. Wolfpoint is a frontier settlement, and Hypertext reflects that — raw, unpolished, and not yet fully systematized.

Whispers of Hypertext have reached both kingdoms. Neither has formally responded. [[Auralis]] has noticed; it would be unlike them not to. [[Terravelle]] has filed it under things that are not yet their problem.

## Relationship to the Sigil System

The [[Flux Users|Scholar]] community has not yet formally classified Hypertext within the standard [[Spell Variables]] framework because Hypertext does not fit cleanly inside it. It uses the same symbols. It obeys the same [[Control Tier]] limits. But it does not use Shape — there is no Triangle, Square, Pentagon, or Circle. The geometric structure that defines how many variables a sigil controls simply does not exist in Hypertext. Instead, length and logical complexity determine scope.

Whether Hypertext represents an evolution of the sigil system or a parallel path that will eventually diverge entirely is a question [[Wolfpoint]] is still in the process of answering.
