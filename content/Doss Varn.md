**Doss Varn** is twenty-eight years old and serves as a guard at the [[Auralis]] border crossing point near [[Ashford]]. [[Control Tier|T1]]. He has held this posting for four years, having gone through the standard Auralis border authority training at twenty-three. The work is mostly procedural: verifying traveler documentation, checking cargo manifests against [[Watts]] ledger records, maintaining the border station's daily log, and occasionally managing situations that the procedures do not cover.

## Appearance

Doss is twenty-eight with the lean, alert build of four years in border authority work. His light brown hair is kept military-short, and he wears the standard gray-blue border uniform with practiced precision. His hands are calloused from constant document handling and inspections, and his posture is the calm readiness of someone trained to spot what does not fit.

He carries a worn personal notebook in addition to official logs. It is not regulation issue, but he keeps it because patterns become visible only when you record enough detail.

## Work

Most days are routine verification and traveler management. Doss is competent and patient, with good judgment about when a technically valid file still requires closer review.

Over the past year, reporting requirements have tightened without clear public explanation. More Terravelle papers are crossing his checkpoint, and command language has shifted from routine throughput to documentation precision.

## The Shift

Doss has observed a consistent pattern: additional forms, denser logging requirements, and repeated instruction from experienced contacts to "log everything carefully." None of these changes are dramatic alone. Together, they are directional.

He does not know what the data is being prepared for. He does know the pattern is not random, and that careful records are his best protection if routine procedures become retroactive evidence.

## Relationships

- `dependent`: [[Ashford]] - checkpoint function depends on command guidance and cross-office processing reliability.
- `ally`: [[Collen Mast]] - shared border observations align across fixed and mobile unit work.
- `unknown-link`: [[Expansion Faction]] - receives downstream procedural pressure without policy context.

## Use In Session

- Players can use Doss as a grounded checkpoint source to validate whether a border anomaly is isolated or systemic.

## Complications

- Official forms are complete, but informal handling patterns imply hidden triage criteria.
- Reporting pressure raises the risk of punishing honest ambiguity.
- Doss can help only within procedures unless players create protected leverage.

## Secrets

- Doss keeps a private pattern log that captures what official summaries omit.
- He can identify exactly when checkpoint behavior shifted and by which protocol revision.
- A future audit could force him to choose between command loyalty and factual disclosure.
