**Vessa Oln** is forty-six years old and teaches Flux application at a mid-tier academy in [[Solhaven]]'s middle terraces. [[Control Tier|T4]]. She has been teaching for sixteen years, before which she spent eight years in commercial Flux work that she found increasingly uninteresting. The decision to move into instruction was partly practical — the academy position paid adequately and offered stability — and partly a recognition that explaining how casting worked engaged her more than performing it for clients who were primarily interested in the result.

She teaches the T2-T3 curriculum, which in Solhaven's tiered academy system is the range that most working students occupy. The instructional challenge is making the intermediate range intelligible to people who have been told, through various channels, that intermediate is something you move through rather than something you occupy. She has stopped explaining the curriculum this way. She has started explaining it as a body of work that produces competence, and letting the students discover which of them will take it further.

She has taught several T5 students over the years. They pass through the standard curriculum quickly. She is attentive to the ones who pass through quickly and remain — students who could move ahead and choose not to, because there is something in the foundational work they want to understand more fully. These are the students she finds interesting. They are also, in her experience, the ones least represented in the institutional pathways the academy feeds.

The [[Ascendant Path]]'s involvement in Solhaven's academy system is administrative and financial rather than pedagogical — the Path provides funding, approves the curriculum structure, and receives progress reports on high-tier students. The Path does not teach. Vessa finds this arrangement acceptable. The Path's curriculum philosophy, as she understands it from the approval documentation, is less about what students learn than about which students the institution is oriented toward. She teaches everyone in her section.
