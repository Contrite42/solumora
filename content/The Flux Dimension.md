The Flux Dimension is the dimensional space adjacent to [[Solumora]] from which [[Flux Demons]] originate and through which [[Rift Incursions|rifts]] occasionally open. It is not a void and it is not a mirror of the physical world. It has structure — persistent features, stable regions, something that functions like geography — but the organizing principles of that structure have no equivalent in Solumoran experience and have never been successfully described by anyone who has attempted it.

No human has entered the Flux Dimension and returned. Everything known about it is inferred from what comes through the rifts, what those creatures carry in their biology, and the Flux signature characteristics of rift openings themselves.

## Structure

The Flux Dimension has regions. This much can be inferred from the variation in creatures that emerge through different rifts — the types, sizes, and Flux densities of what comes through are not random, and the patterns suggest that different rift locations are opening onto different areas of the same space rather than randomly sampling a uniform environment.

What those regions are organized around is unknown. There is no evidence of terrain in any conventional sense — no indication that the creatures emerging are adapted to heat, cold, altitude, or moisture in ways that would suggest a physical landscape. What they are adapted to is Flux density, which varies significantly across the dimension in ways that appear stable over time. The densest regions produce the most capable creatures. The [[High Demons]] are presumed to originate from or mature within the highest-density areas.

The dimension has no known edge. Rifts have opened in every part of the [[Equatorial Desert]] that has been surveyed, and the variation in what emerges does not map cleanly onto desert geography. Whatever the Flux Dimension's structure is, it does not correspond to Solumora's physical layout.

## Time

Time in the Flux Dimension does not move at the same rate as in Solumora. The direction of the difference — whether time there runs faster or slower — cannot be determined from external observation, and the creatures that cross through do not carry any reliable indicator of subjective elapsed time.

What can be observed is that rift openings show Flux signature characteristics inconsistent with a space experiencing the same temporal flow as the physical world. [[Flux Users|Scholars]] at [[Emberfall]] who have studied rift signatures describe a particular quality of temporal discontinuity at the boundary — a hesitation in the Flux field that suggests the two spaces are not synchronized.

The practical implications of this are unknown. Whether a creature that spends what feels to it like a decade in the Flux Dimension has experienced a decade, a century, or a year by Solumoran reckoning has never been established. The [[High Demons]] have not volunteered this information, and no one in either kingdom knows to ask them.

## Ecology

The Flux Dimension has a full ecology. The variety of creature types that have emerged through rifts over the period of documented incursions is sufficient to suggest predator and prey relationships, specialist and generalist niches, and the kind of evolutionary pressure that produces genuine biological diversity. Creatures observed emerging through rifts have included forms with no earthly reference point, forms that are recognizably animal-like in silhouette but wrong in ways that suggest convergent evolution under different pressures, and the [[High Demons]] themselves at the apex.

The lower-order creatures that emerge through rifts are not the full range of what the Flux Dimension contains. They are what is capable of surviving the crossing. The rift opening process appears to impose its own filter — creatures with insufficient internal Flux density do not make it through intact. What this means for the actual population distribution of the Flux Dimension is unclear, but it suggests that Solumora's experience of Flux Demon incursions represents a heavily filtered sample of what the dimension actually holds.

## Relationship to Solumora's Flux Field

The Flux Dimension is not separate from Solumora's ambient [[Flux]] field — it is adjacent to it, and the two spaces interact at the boundary. Areas of high Flux density in Solumora correspond to areas of thin boundary between the two spaces, which is why the [[Equatorial Desert|desert's]] anomalous Flux concentrations produce rifts and ordinary terrain does not.

The [[Ancient Ruins|anomalous ruin sites]] in the desert represent the thinnest boundary points currently known. Whether this is a consequence of the original Flux events at those sites or whether the sites were built there because the boundary was already thin is one of the unresolved questions of [[Auralis]] expedition research — though the researchers asking it are not framing it in terms of dimensional boundaries, because the Flux Dimension is not a concept that exists in current Solumoran scholarship.

The boundary can apparently be made thinner by sustained high-Flux activity, which is the most likely explanation for the increasing rift frequency near sites of active expedition work. Whether it can be made permanently thinner — or opened deliberately — is not known. It is the kind of question that [[Drest]]'s faction would find very interesting if anyone thought to ask it.
