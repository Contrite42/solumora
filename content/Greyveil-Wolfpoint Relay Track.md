The Greyveil-Wolfpoint Relay Track is the overland northern relay system used for correspondence, compact goods, and specialist movement between [[Greyveil]] and [[Wolfpoint]]. It is less a paved road than a maintained sequence of way markers, shelter points, and handoff stations calibrated to harsh ground conditions.

The track is run by relay crews rather than single-endurance carriers. Teams rotate at fixed intervals to reduce weather attrition and keep message reliability high during long cold stretches where one injury can end a run. The relay model costs more than single-rider dispatch and fails less often, which is why both Terravellian administrators and independent operators keep paying for it.

Most traffic is informational: legal packets, technical correspondence, procurement instructions, and copied research fragments moving in both directions. Physical cargo is limited to items where volume is low and replacement time is high, including precision tools and treated materials that Wolfpoint workshops cannot source locally on demand.

Risk management on the track is practical and conservative. Dispatch houses maintain dual-copy protocols for critical packets, and receiving offices in Greyveil log expected arrival bands rather than single timestamps so delays can be triaged without immediate escalation.
