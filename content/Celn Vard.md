**Celn Vard** is fifty-one years old and has worked as a livestock trader in the communities that edge the northern [[Equatorial Desert]] in southern [[Auralis]] for twenty-six years. [[Control Tier|T2]]. The work takes him through a circuit of small settlements that have more connection to the desert's margin than to the administrative centers of Auralis proper, and he has developed the working knowledge of desert-edge conditions that this circuit produces over time.

Three years ago, he shared a roadside fire with a traveler for one night between settlements. He has told the story perhaps fifteen times since — not seeking it out, but responding when the occasion is right. The traveler gave his name as Sorath. He was going south, toward the desert. He asked questions about the settlements Celn had recently been through — specific questions, about particular individuals Celn had dealt with, in a way that suggested he already knew some of the answers and was checking the rest. He contributed nothing about himself except presence, which was considerable. When Celn woke at dawn, the traveler was gone.

What Celn tells, when he tells it: the fire burned lower than it should have given the fuel, the traveler did not appear to sleep, and the questions were too specific to be coincidence. What he does not tell, because he cannot account for it: the feeling, sitting across the fire, of being weighed against a scale he could not see.

He has not encountered [[Sorath]] again. He has not stopped thinking about whether he will.
