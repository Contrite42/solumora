**Seld Norn** is forty years old and works as an independent research consultant in [[Valdenmoor]], analyzing site documentation for guild-contracted projects. [[Control Tier|T3]]. She spent eight years as a field assessor for [[The Advancement Corps]] before leaving the institution's service at thirty-two. She does not describe the reasons for leaving to people who ask in general terms. She has described the reasons to two people in her life, specifically and accurately, and does not plan to add to that number.

## Appearance

Seld is forty with the precise, self-contained appearance of someone who has spent eight years doing independent consulting work after leaving an institutional career she doesn't discuss. Her dark hair is kept in a practical short cut, and she dresses in the professional-but-not-formal style of independent practitioners: well-made tunics and trousers that work equally well for site visits and documentation review, everything chosen for function without institutional affiliation markers.

Her hands show the mixed evidence of two careers — old calluses from eight years of Corps field assessment work, newer ink staining from eight years of documentation analysis. She moves with the efficient economy of someone who learned to work in institutional structures and then chose to work outside them, and there's a guardedness in her posture that wasn't there when she was younger, the visible mark of someone who has clear boundaries about what they will and won't discuss. When reviewing site documentation her focus is absolute, eyes moving across reports with the trained pattern-recognition of someone who knows what institutional distance looks like and how truth gets filtered in the transition from field notes to formal records.

Her expression is professionally neutral but notably careful — she has developed the consultant's skill of being helpful without being familiar, competent without inviting questions about her background. When she speaks it's in brief, specific statements that answer what's asked without volunteering additional context, the verbal habit of someone who has learned that fewer words mean fewer complications. Off-duty she is notably more relaxed but still maintains clear social boundaries, and the two people who know why she left the Corps can see the difference between her public reserve and the actual person underneath it in ways that others simply read as professional distance.

## Background

Her current work is straightforward: she reviews site documentation for construction and infrastructure projects, evaluates whether existing Flux assessments are accurate, and produces reports that clients can use to make decisions. She is good at this. She has developed, over eight years of Corps field work and another eight of independent consulting, a thorough working methodology for site assessment that she can execute without thinking about where the methodology came from.

What she knows from Corps work: how institutional documentation creates distance between what was observed and what was reported, which categories of observation tend to disappear in the transition from field notes to formal record, and what absence in a document means versus what presence means. This knowledge is directly applicable to evaluating whether anyone else's site documentation can be trusted, which is the core skill her current clients pay for.

She does not have ongoing contact with the Corps. She does not have contact with [[Renne Osl]], who was a junior assessor when Seld was in her last field year and who she suspects has developed into exactly the kind of competent senior assessor the Corps needs. She does not think about this often.
