**Food & Drink** encompasses the production, preservation, distribution, and cultural practices surrounding food and alcohol consumption across [[Solumora]]. This is not peripheral to worldbuilding — food security determines settlement viability, class hierarchies manifest through consumption patterns, and taverns function as information exchanges and social infrastructure. What people eat, where they drink, and how food moves from production to consumption reveals economic relationships, political tensions, and social identities.

## Institutional Food Operations

Large-scale organized provisioning:

**Celde Morv's Provisioning Business** — [[Celde Morv]] (T1, 38yo, [[Solhaven]] Middling Steps) operates mid-scale provisioning including coldbox storage, multi-supplier sourcing from [[Terravelle]] via [[Maren Freight Chain]], spoilage detection services (part-time T2 practitioner), variety at multiple price points serving merchant negotiations and middle-class dining. Revenue 18,000-22,000 Watts annually.

**Mountain Game Collective** — [[Wolfpoint]] coordinated hunting producing 60% export to [[Terravelle]], 40% internal consumption. Premium grades chemically stabilized (Ren Caldst operations), standard grades traditionally smoked. Primary [[Wolfpoint]] export commodity distributed by [[Trade Guilds]] via [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. Contemporary concern: border tensions threaten transport routes.

**Basin Grain Allocation Board** — [[Terravelle]] institutional grain management purchasing surplus from [[Maren Valley]] producers, maintaining strategic reserves (buffer against harvest failure, enable war supply), subsidizing bread pricing during shortages (prevent social unrest), coordinating export approvals to [[Auralis]]. Contemporary role: increasing reserves, reducing [[Auralis]] exports, managing distribution despite labor shortages.

## Class Stratification Through Food

Consumption patterns signal and reinforce social hierarchies:

**Working-Class Diet:**
- Grain-heavy (bread, porridge, coarse meal)
- Preserved fish/meat supplement (saltfish standard protein)
- Minimal fresh foods (limited preservation access)
- Food costs 50% of household income (400-600 Watts annually for 4 people)
- Tavern consumption: ale in rough ware, minimal food accompaniment

**Middle-Class Diet:**
- More diverse grains (wheat bread, barley dishes)
- Regular meat/fish (both preserved and seasonal fresh)
- Moderate dairy, occasional luxury items
- Food costs 30-40% of income (800-1200 Watts annually)
- Tavern consumption: wine and ale, food expected with drinking, moderate portions

**Upper-Class Diet:**
- Wide variety, premium grades, luxury imports
- Extensive fresh foods (coldbox storage, chemical preservation)
- Flux-enhanced flavor profiles (T3 manipulation, controversial)
- Food costs 20-30% of income (2000-4000+ Watts annually)
- Formal dining: multi-course meals, imported wines, elaborate preparation

## Supply Chain Dependencies

Food security relies on functioning networks:

**[[Auralis]] Dependencies:**
- Grain imports from [[Terravelle]] [[Maren Valley]] via [[Ashford-Halveth Trade Road]]
- Salt production for preservation (exported to [[Terravelle]])
- Coastal fishing (working-class protein source, preservation for inland distribution)
- [[Wolfpoint]] game meat exports through [[Terravelle]] trade networks

**[[Terravelle]] Dependencies:**
- Salt imports from [[Auralis]] ([[Ashford Supply Contract]] negotiations, [[Wolfpoint]] preservation economy)
- [[Maren Valley]] grain distribution to interior settlements
- Coastal fish for inland protein (particularly northern [[Wolfpoint]])
- Cross-border luxury food markets

**Mutual Vulnerability:**
- Border closure would harm both kingdoms' civilian populations
- Food embargoes cause suffering before political concessions
- War preparation stockpiling reduces civilian food access now
- Supply chain militarization diverts resources from commercial distribution

## Contemporary Food Crisis Pressures

Multiple converging factors strain food systems:

**Labor Shortages:**
- Agricultural workers conscripted ([[Auralis]] aggressive, [[Terravelle]] increasing)
- Harvest yields declining (fewer workers for planting/harvesting)
- Transport capacity reduced (animals, workers requisitioned)
- Tavern staff conscripted (service quality decreases, family members substitute)

**Strategic Stockpiling:**
- Both kingdoms increasing reserves (diverts from civilian consumption)
- [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] requisitions higher harvest percentages
- Merchants stockpile anticipating border closure
- Civilian populations face reduced variety, increased prices

**Border Crossing Deterioration:**
- Customs inspections intensify (longer delays, arbitrary enforcement)
- [[Terravelle]] reduces grain export approvals (food as political weapon)
- Merchants cease cross-border trade (risk-reward unfavorable)
- Smuggling increases (informal routes, illegal operations)

**Price Increases:**
- Working-class households reduce consumption (smaller portions, skip meals, less variety)
- Middle-class pressure (food consuming larger budget share, eliminate luxuries)
- Upper-class insulated (differential impact increases class resentment)
- Social unrest potential (hunger is politically destabilizing)

**Flux Practitioner Requisitions:**
- Higher-tier practitioners diverted to military preparation
- Civilian preservation access decreases (fewer practitioners available, higher costs)
- Establishments revert to traditional methods (adequate but less efficient)
- [[Famous Food Establishments|Coldbox renewal costs increase]]

## Future Scenario Analysis

**Border Closure Impact:**
- [[Auralis]]: Grain shortage particularly [[Halveth]], price spikes trigger unrest, emergency rationing, intensified domestic production
- [[Terravelle]]: Salt shortage threatens [[Wolfpoint]] preservation economy, reduced grain export market lowers agricultural prices, [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] manages surplus
- Both: Civilian suffering, political pressure on leadership, risk of escalation

**Open Conflict:**
- Food production collapses (fields become battlegrounds, workers become soldiers)
- Supply chains destroyed (routes interdicted, warehouses burned)
- Famine as weapon (siege tactics targeting civilians)
- Multi-year agricultural recovery required post-conflict
