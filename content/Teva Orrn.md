**Teva Orrn** is twenty-five years old and works at her family's grain processing operation in a rural community in central [[Terravelle]]. [[Control Tier|T2]]. Her older brother [[Cadell Mors]] has been at a northern border garrison for four years. He writes every two months, or used to.

## Appearance

Teva is twenty-five with the practical, capable appearance of someone who has spent her entire adult life working her family's grain processing operation in rural Terravelle. Her light brown hair is kept in a long braid for practical reasons — grain dust is a constant presence and loose hair is a liability around processing equipment. She dresses in the functional style of agricultural workers: sturdy tunics and trousers that can handle mill work, heavy aprons during processing hours, everything showing the permanent dusting of grain residue that comes with the work.

Her hands are work-worn from years of processing labor — calluses from equipment handling, the slight flour staining that never quite washes out, strong from hauling grain sacks and managing millstone maintenance. She moves through the family operation with the confident efficiency of someone who grew up learning this work, understanding seasonal rhythms and equipment maintenance without conscious thought. Her face has the healthy complexion of someone who works outdoors regularly but not exclusively, and there's a watchfulness in her eyes that wasn't there a year ago — the visible mark of someone worrying about things they can't control.

When she speaks about the grain work her manner is straightforward and knowledgeable, the easy competence of someone who knows their trade thoroughly. But colleagues and family members have noticed that she's become quieter this past year, more prone to distraction, and she checks the mail with a frequency that suggests anxiety rather than anticipation. She carries her brother's letters in a worn leather folder that she keeps in her personal belongings rather than leaving them out, and the careful way she handles them reveals how much weight those worn pages carry. Off-duty she has a warm, steady presence and maintains the strong family and community ties that characterize rural Terravelle life, though the worry about Cadell sits visibly just beneath her everyday interactions.

## Background

The letters have changed. The change is gradual and probably invisible to someone who has not been reading his letters for four years. Early letters were about the work — what the garrison does, who is there, what the northern road looks like in winter. The recent letters are still about the work, but they describe it in the past tense more often, as though cataloguing what things used to be, and they contain more observations about things he has noticed without telling her what he makes of them. He is not a person who withholds things. He is a person who tells her what he is thinking. The recent letters tell her what he is seeing.

She has not written to ask directly what is happening. She does not know how to ask it in a way that would reach him rather than the garrison's letter review process, which she is not certain exists but which she is not certain does not. She has also considered that if she knew what he was seeing, she would not know what to do with it, and she is not certain that knowing is better than the version she has now, which is being worried about something she can't name.

The grain processing work continues. The harvest was adequate. The community's flux practitioner — a traveling assessor on circuit — came through in the fall. She mentions these things in her letters too.
