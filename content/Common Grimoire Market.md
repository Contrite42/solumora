The Common grimoire market is the economic layer that makes [[Flux]] use genuinely distributed across [[Solumora]] rather than confined to high-tier practitioners. Because T1–T2 spells are within reach of the majority of the population, and because professional [[Grimoire Copyists|Scribes]] can reproduce them at volume, Common grimoires circulate at prices that most working households can afford with genuine saving rather than exceptional wealth.

## Price Points

A single-spell Common copy — a single [[Sigils|sigil]] on a small card or tile, produced by a certified Scribe — runs at the low end of the Watt market for skilled work. A bound collection of six to twelve Common spells, guild-certified in [[Terravelle]] or Scholar-vouched in [[Auralis]], costs more but is the standard household purchase and within reach of most families who participate in the [[Watts]] economy over a saving horizon of weeks to months.

The price structure rewards volume purchase. Scribes who sell individual spells to walk-in customers charge more per sigil than booksellers who buy in bulk and distribute through market networks. Most Common grimoires that reach rural households or small workshops have passed through at least two middlemen between the Scribe who produced them and the person who activates them.

## What Moves Through the Market

The most commercially successful Common spells are the ones with the clearest, most repeatable utility in daily working life: lighting, heating, cold preservation, surface maintenance, basic security. [[Hearthlight]], [[Coldbox Seal]], [[Lockward]], [[Dustsweep]], [[Rustblock]] — these are not glamorous spells. They are the spells that save time, prevent spoilage, and reduce physical labor in ways that compound over a working life.

The market for combat or protection spells at Common tier is smaller than people expect. [[Hardenskin]], [[Forcepulse]], [[Shockloop]] — these exist and sell, but they are niche purchases compared to utility applications. Most Common buyers are not thinking about threat response. They are thinking about whether their supply of [[Hearthlight]] cards will last through winter.

## Distribution Channels

**Market stalls**: the most accessible channel. Scribes or resellers operating in city markets sell individual spell cards, bound collections, and replacements for common household spells. Quality varies; there is no guarantee on a market stall purchase except the seller's reputation, which in larger cities is largely anonymous.

**Guild-certified booksellers** (Terravelle): shops that have [[Trade Guilds]] certification to distribute authenticated grimoire copies. Higher price than market stalls, higher reliability. The certification mark on the cover is supposed to indicate the copy traces to an authenticated master.

**Traveling merchants**: in rural areas, much of the Common grimoire supply arrives through traveling trade circuits. [[Sable Morn]] and similar circuit traders carry copies as part of a mixed cargo because grimoires are light, non-perishable, high-margin relative to weight, and reliably demanded in every settlement they pass through.

**Institutional supply** (Auralis): the [[The Advancement Corps]] and affiliated Scholar institutions supply Common grimoires to practitioners in their networks, often at below-market prices as a patronage benefit. This is part of how Auralis maintains its Flux-as-social-mobility narrative — the Common tier is accessible through institutional channels even to practitioners who cannot afford market prices.

## Price as Access

The Common market is the primary mechanism by which grimoires spread power without creating a pure caste. A T1 practitioner in rural [[Terravelle]] with a saved set of Common cards has a meaningful working spell set. A T2 practitioner in [[Auralis]] with access to an institutional supply network has more — but the difference is degree, not kind.

What the Common market cannot do is provide access above its tier. A T1 practitioner can buy every Common grimoire available and will still not be able to use an Uncommon spell, because the [[Control Tier]] ceiling is biological. The market distributes what is accessible; it does not change what is accessible.
