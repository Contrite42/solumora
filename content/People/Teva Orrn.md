Teva Orrn is the most essential person in a fifty-mile radius of wilderness that most [[Terravelle]] administrators have never heard of. Assessed at T3 with a specialization in food creation [[Sigils]], she provides nutritional support for six rural families scattered through the deep forest northwest of [[Valdenmoor]] — communities too isolated to rely on conventional supply chains and too small to sustain themselves through hunting and gathering alone during the harsh winter months.

## The Ability

Teva's [[Flux]] work centers on biological transformation — converting available organic matter into concentrated, nutritionally complete food sources that can sustain human life through extended periods when hunting and foraging are insufficient. This is not magic that creates something from nothing. It is sophisticated manipulation of existing biological material, restructuring plant matter at the molecular level to maximize caloric density, protein content, and essential nutrients.

Her most common application is the conversion of wood pulp, bark, and forest debris into edible concentrates that taste better than they have any right to and provide complete nutrition for adults and children. A day's work for Teva can produce enough concentrated food to sustain a family of four for a month. The process requires her to understand both the [[Flux]] mechanics of biological transformation and the nutritional requirements of human physiology — knowledge she has developed through fifteen years of practice and careful attention to the health outcomes of the families she serves.

She also works with preservation enhancement — taking meat from successful hunts and extending its nutritional lifespan far beyond what conventional preservation [[Sigils]] can achieve. Her preserved meat retains full nutritional value for up to a year and can be prepared in ways that make it indistinguishable from fresh game. This capability allows rural families to make the most of successful hunting seasons and maintain protein intake through lean periods.

## The Territory

Teva operates from a homestead built into a natural clearing where three streams converge, approximately forty miles from the nearest [[Terravelle]] settlement with guild presence. The location was chosen for its centrality to the families she serves and for the natural [[Flux]] concentration that occurs where moving water intersects — a phenomenon that reduces the energy cost of her biological transformation work and allows her to sustain higher-tier applications for longer periods.

Her homestead includes a workshop specifically designed for food creation work — a building with carved [[Sigils]] that maintain optimal temperature and humidity for biological transformation, storage areas with preservation enchantments that keep raw materials stable until they can be processed, and living quarters that accommodate the families who travel to her for extended food preparation sessions during the pre-winter preparation period.

The families she serves — the [[Solen Carr|Carrs]] who provide lumber, the Ossves who hunt and trap, the Calns who maintain the region's only trading post, and three other households scattered through the forest — form a loose network of mutual dependence. Teva's food creation work is the foundation that makes their isolated lifestyle sustainable. Without her, the population density the forest currently supports would be impossible to maintain.

## The Process

Food creation at T3 requires careful preparation and sustained concentration. Teva typically works in day-long sessions, processing raw materials in batches that maximize efficiency while maintaining the precision necessary for safe biological transformation. The work is physically and mentally demanding — she must hold complex [[Flux]] structures stable for hours while monitoring multiple variables to ensure the transformation produces safe, nutritionally complete results.

Her raw materials come from the forest itself and from the families she serves. Wood waste from [[Solen Carr]]'s lumber operation. Plant matter gathered during seasonal foraging. Bones and less desirable cuts from successful hunts. Material that would otherwise be waste or marginal nutrition becomes the foundation for food that can sustain families through winter isolation.

The transformation process requires her to understand the molecular structure of what she is working with and the nutritional requirements of what she is creating. A mistake in the biological transformation can produce food that is inedible, nutritionally incomplete, or actively harmful. Teva has never had a failure that endangered anyone, but she has had near-misses that reinforced her understanding of how precise the work must be.

## The Community Role

Teva is not just a service provider — she is the central figure around which the region's rural community organizes itself. Families plan their seasonal activities around her availability. The timing of hunts, the scheduling of lumber operations, the coordination of trading expeditions all take into account when Teva will be available for food processing work.

She also serves as the community's connection to [[Flux Users|flux]] knowledge that extends beyond practical applications. When young people in the rural families show signs of flux sensitivity, they are brought to Teva for initial assessment and basic training. She cannot provide the formal tier assessment that requires guild certification, but she can determine whether a child's sensitivity is worth the journey to [[Valdenmoor]] for official evaluation.

Her workshop serves as a gathering place during the pre-winter preparation period, when multiple families converge on her homestead for intensive food processing sessions. These gatherings are not just work — they are the primary social events of the rural calendar, opportunities for families to share information, coordinate plans, and maintain the relationships that sustain their isolated communities.

## The Knowledge

Teva's expertise extends far beyond the technical mechanics of biological transformation. She understands the nutritional requirements of people living in harsh conditions — what nutrients are most critical during winter isolation, how to balance caloric density with digestibility, how to create food that provides psychological as well as physical sustenance during the months when fresh options are unavailable.

She also understands the forest ecosystem in ways that complement and extend the knowledge of the hunters and loggers she serves. Her work requires her to know which plants are available in which seasons, how to identify and process materials that are safe for transformation, how to predict and adapt to variations in the forest's productivity from year to year.

This knowledge was not acquired through formal training. Teva was assessed at T3 when she was eleven years old, during one of the periodic tier evaluation visits that [[Terravelle]] administrators make to rural areas. Her family sent her to [[Valdenmoor]] for two years of basic [[Flux]] education, where she learned the theoretical foundations of biological transformation. But the practical application — the specific techniques that make food creation work in an isolated forest environment — she developed through fifteen years of experimentation, failure, and gradual refinement.

## The Burden

Teva is thirty-four years old and has never been more than a day's travel from her homestead for longer than a week. Her work ties her to the region in ways that make extended absence impossible — the families she serves depend on her availability during critical seasonal periods, and the knowledge she has developed is specific to this forest, this climate, these particular challenges.

She has no apprentice. No one in the current generation of rural children has been assessed at a tier high enough to learn biological transformation work, and the families she serves cannot spare anyone for the years of training that would be required to develop her capabilities. When she retires or dies, the network of rural families that currently sustains itself in this region will have to contract significantly or relocate to areas with better access to conventional food sources.

This reality weighs on her in ways that the families she serves do not fully understand. She is not just providing a service — she is the single point of failure for a way of life that has sustained multiple generations of rural [[Terravelle]] families. The responsibility is both a source of pride and a form of isolation that she has never found a way to resolve.
