Veld Dorv is a T3 field documentation specialist stationed in [[Emberfall]] as part of [[The Advancement Corps]]'s ongoing monitoring of ruins research activities. She has held this position for four years, long enough to develop genuine competence in the administrative aspects of expedition oversight and long enough to notice patterns in what gets documented versus what gets reported up the chain of command.

## Role and Responsibilities

Veld's official duties center on maintaining accurate records of all research activities conducted in Emberfall's vicinity. She logs expedition departures and returns, catalogs recovered artifacts before they are transferred to [[Auralis]] institutions, and files regular reports on the progress of independent researchers like [[Tessel Dran]] and [[Aldric Mourne]]. The work requires attention to detail and a working knowledge of [[Ancient Ruins]] research protocols, both of which she has developed through practical experience rather than formal training.

Her position sits at the intersection of academic research and institutional oversight. She attends briefings with expedition leaders, reviews preliminary findings from ruins sites, and coordinates with [[The Council of Auralis]]'s research priorities as they filter down through Corps hierarchy. The role demands discretion—much of what she documents is considered sensitive by the researchers who produce it and the administrators who direct it.

What makes Veld valuable to the Corps is her ability to assess the significance of research findings without being a researcher herself. She can identify when an expedition has recovered something potentially important, when a researcher's work has taken an unexpected direction, and when the gap between stated objectives and actual activities suggests something worth closer attention. This assessment capability is what qualified her for the Emberfall posting despite her relatively modest [[Control Tier]].

## Growing Concerns

Over her four years in Emberfall, Veld has developed an increasingly detailed picture of how information flows through the research oversight system. Expedition reports are filed with her office, reviewed by her supervisors, and forwarded to Auralis institutions for final analysis. Artifact transfers follow documented protocols. Research findings are supposed to be preserved in their entirety for institutional review.

The system works as designed in most cases. Where it does not work is in the small percentage of cases that produce findings considered especially significant. Veld has noticed that certain expedition reports are edited between her initial filing and their final archived versions. Specific artifacts disappear from transfer manifests without explanation. Research findings that she personally documented as recovered are later listed as "inconclusive" or "requiring further study" in official summaries.

These discrepancies are not random. They cluster around particular types of discoveries—inscriptions that show unusual complexity, artifacts that exhibit anomalous [[Flux]] behavior, findings that suggest the [[Ancient Ruins|ancient civilization]] possessed capabilities beyond current understanding. The pattern is subtle enough that it took Veld years to recognize it, but consistent enough that she can no longer dismiss it as administrative error.

## The Dilemma

Veld joined the Corps with genuine belief in its stated mission: advancing human understanding of Flux through careful study of ancient achievements. She has spent four years working within a system she believed was designed to preserve and share knowledge for the benefit of both kingdoms. The discovery that this system includes deliberate information suppression has forced her to reconsider both her role and her loyalties.

She has not yet decided what to do with her observations. Raising concerns through official channels would require her to accuse her supervisors of systematic deception, which would end her career regardless of whether the accusations were substantiated. Going outside official channels would violate her oath to the Corps and potentially compromise ongoing research efforts that she still believes have value.

The middle path—continuing to document everything accurately while saying nothing about the discrepancies—feels increasingly like complicity. Veld understands that her silence enables whatever process is removing information from the official record. She also understands that her position gives her access to information that would be valuable to independent researchers who might be able to act on it more freely than she can.

## Daily Life in Emberfall

Veld lives in Corps-provided housing near Emberfall's administrative center, a practical arrangement that keeps her close to her work and integrated into the town's small community of permanent residents. She takes meals at the common dining hall most evenings, where she encounters the mix of researchers, suppliers, and local workers who keep the town functioning. These interactions have given her a ground-level perspective on how the research community actually operates, as distinct from how it appears in official documentation.

Her relationships with the researchers she monitors are necessarily complex. [[Tessel Dran]] treats her with professional courtesy but obvious wariness—he knows she reports on his activities and has learned to be careful about what he discusses in her presence. [[Aldric Mourne]] is more forthcoming, perhaps because his [[The Culmination Faction|Culmination Faction]] connections give him confidence that his work aligns with institutional priorities. Independent researchers vary in their approach to her, from open cooperation to polite evasion.

Veld has developed genuine respect for the quality of work being done in Emberfall, which makes her growing awareness of information suppression more troubling rather than less. The researchers she monitors are competent, dedicated, and producing findings that advance understanding of the ancient civilization. That some of their most significant discoveries are being quietly removed from the official record suggests purposes she was not told about when she accepted her posting.
