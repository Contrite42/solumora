

## Daily Existence

Mave's visible social position is not professional but social. She appears in [[Selvane]]'s circle — loosely described as an associate or friend of long standing, though no one can say how they met or when the attachment formed. She orbits Selvane socially, like a younger sibling to an eldest. This is the central fact of how others perceive her presence in [[Solhaven]]. Neither Mave nor Selvane knows the other is a demon. The social relationship is genuine.

Mave has no independent professional identity. She appears at Selvane's functions, handles peripheral tasks, and is simply present in Selvane's orbit without clean explanation. When pressed for details about her background or work, people who interact with her regularly find they cannot provide specifics. The attachment exists. The history behind it does not hold in memory.

[[Avel Tors]] has had four encounters with Mave, each adjacent to Selvane's world. The first was at a function Selvane hosted — Mave handled arrangements at the periphery, spoke briefly with several guests, was present without being central. The second came through a referral via Selvane's network — someone mentioned Mave might have insight on a crafting question, though the conversation that followed was less helpful than expected. The third was a chance meeting near [[Crestward]], where Mave was conducting some errand that seemed connected to Selvane's household. The fourth was similar — another peripheral encounter, another ordinary explanation.

Each encounter was slightly wrong in ways Avel cannot articulate. Each had an ordinary explanation that satisfied at the time. The pattern is invisible because the connective tissue — Selvane — is not something Avel tracks systematically. The encounters exist as separate incidents rather than a sequence.

The quality that makes Mave hard to remember is not a [[Flux]] effect. It is a quality of presence that fades. Details don't hold. Conversations with her are remembered as having happened, but the content blurs. Arrangements she makes are completed successfully, but the process of making them becomes unclear afterward. She is difficult to remember precisely, and this difficulty is consistent across everyone who interacts with her.

[[Essa Rolt]] has noticed that Mave visits Selvane's household with a frequency that matches no professional relationship Essa can identify. No business is conducted that she can see. Mave arrives, spends time with Selvane, leaves. The visits follow no schedule Essa can determine. They serve no function she can account for. Essa cannot hold the details afterward — not the duration of the visits, not the substance of any conversations, not the specific arrangements that bring Mave to the household. She has noted this pattern without acting on it. The noting itself is something she has to work to maintain.

## Daily Existence

Mave's position in [[Solhaven]] is social rather than professional. Those who encounter Mave describe an associate of [[Selvane]]'s — someone present at functions, handling peripheral tasks, simply there in Selvane's orbit without clean explanation. No one can say precisely how they met or when the attachment formed. The relationship appears to be of long standing, though the details consistently fail to hold in memory afterward.

[[Avel Tors]] has had four encounters with Mave, each adjacent to Selvane's world. The first was at a council-adjacent function where Mave was managing some aspect of the arrangements — not hosting, not presenting, but present and handling things. The second came through a referral in Selvane's network, a craftsperson recommended for work that required particular discretion. The third was a chance meeting near Crestward, Mave walking with the unhurried pace of someone who belonged in that district. The fourth was similar — Mave emerging from a building Avel knew to house several trade offices, moving with the same quiet presence.

Each encounter felt slightly wrong in ways Avel could not name. The conversation was ordinary, the business conducted normally, but something about the interaction refused to settle properly in memory. The pattern is invisible because the connective tissue — Selvane — is not something Avel tracks systematically. The encounters register as separate incidents involving someone whose name and face fade between meetings.

This quality of presence that fades is not a [[Flux]] effect. It is something inherent in how Mave occupies space, how attention slides away from details that should be memorable. People remember the interaction occurred. They remember conducting business or exchanging pleasantries. The specifics — what Mave looked like, what exactly was said, the particular texture of the encounter — do not hold.

[[Essa Rolt]] has noticed that Mave visits Selvane's household with a frequency that matches no professional relationship she can identify. No business is conducted that she can see. Mave arrives, spends time with Selvane, leaves. The visits follow no pattern she can discern — not weekly, not monthly, not tied to any calendar of events or obligations. Essa cannot hold the details of these visits afterward. She has noted this without acting on it, filing the observation in the category of things about Selvane's affairs that she manages without fully understanding.
