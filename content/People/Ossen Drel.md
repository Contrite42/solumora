Ossen Drel has crossed the [[Equatorial Desert]] seven times and survived all of them, which makes him one of the most experienced guides currently working out of [[Halveth]]. He is forty-three years old, has been guiding for sixteen years, and charges rates that reflect both his success record and his unwillingness to take contracts from people he considers insufficiently prepared to survive what he will lead them into.

## Background

Ossen came to [[Halveth]] as part of an [[Auralis]] expedition twenty years ago — a junior researcher on a team investigating rift patterns near the desert's northern edge. The expedition was adequately funded, poorly planned, and led by someone who had read extensively about desert conditions without experiencing them. Three people died on the crossing. Ossen was not one of them, but the experience taught him that academic preparation and practical survival were different categories of knowledge, and that the difference could be measured in lives.

He stayed in Halveth after the expedition returned to [[Solhaven]]. Initially this was recovery — the crossing had left him with heat damage that took months to heal and a reluctance to immediately attempt the journey back south. By the time he was physically ready to travel, he had begun working with local guides and discovered that his [[Control Tier|T3]] assessment and his recent experience with expedition failure made him useful in ways the formal research structure had not.

## Guiding Philosophy

Ossen guides expeditions that he believes can succeed. He does not guide expeditions that he believes cannot, regardless of what they offer to pay him. This is not moral squeamishness — it is practical experience. A failed crossing reflects on the guide's reputation, and reputation is the only currency that matters in Halveth's guide trade. Better to turn down lucrative contracts than to accept ones that end with dead clients and damaged credibility.

His assessment process is thorough and non-negotiable. He interviews every expedition member individually. He examines their [[Grimoires|grimoire]] collections and their [[Sigils|sigil]] preparation. He requires demonstration of thermal regulation under controlled conditions. He asks detailed questions about their experience with extreme environments, their [[Flux]] reserves, and their understanding of what the crossing will demand from them physically and mentally.

Most importantly, he observes how they respond to being told no. An expedition leader who accepts his refusal to guide them and asks for specific recommendations about what they need to improve is someone he might work with in the future. An expedition leader who argues, offers more money, or suggests that his standards are unnecessarily conservative is someone he will not work with under any circumstances.

## Desert Knowledge

Ossen's seven crossings have been spread across different seasons, different routes, and different group compositions. He has guided formal [[Auralis]] research expeditions, independent treasure hunters, and on one occasion a [[Terravelle]] diplomatic party that needed to reach [[Solhaven]] faster than the sea route would allow. Each crossing has added to his understanding of how the desert behaves and what it requires from the people who enter it.

He knows the better routes — paths that reduce crossing time without increasing risk, water sources that can be relied upon, landmarks that remain visible even in the wind-driven dust that makes navigation difficult. More importantly, he knows the signs that indicate when conditions are deteriorating beyond what a prepared group can handle, and he is willing to abort a crossing if those signs appear.

His [[Flux]] discipline is Heat, which makes him exceptionally valuable for desert work. At T3, he can maintain thermal regulation for an entire expedition group for extended periods, and his sixteen years of practice have refined his efficiency to the point where he can sustain this work through a complete crossing without depleting his reserves. This is not common among guides. Most expeditions require multiple thermal specialists to maintain coverage.

## Relationship with Halveth

Ossen is one of the village's permanent residents and one of its most reliable sources of expedition-season income. His success rate means that groups he has guided return to Halveth to organize future crossings, and his recommendations carry weight with other guides and suppliers. When he endorses an expedition's preparation, other village services become more available and more reasonably priced.

He has a functional working relationship with [[Wren]], though they do not socialize extensively. She has never interfered with an expedition he was guiding, which he considers professional courtesy. He has never brought an expedition through Halveth that she considered problematic, which she considers competent screening. Their mutual respect is based on the understanding that they both take the desert seriously and both have reasons to want people to survive it.

## Current Situation

Ossen is currently contracted to guide an [[Auralis]] research expedition scheduled for the next crossing season — a group investigating the anomalous [[Flux]] readings near the [[Ancient Ruins|deep desert sites]]. The expedition is well-funded, properly equipped, and led by researchers who have accepted his preparation requirements without argument. He considers it one of the more promising contracts he has taken in recent years.

He has also been approached by two independent groups seeking guides for the same season. One he has declined — their thermal preparation is inadequate and their leader responded poorly to his feedback. The other he is still evaluating. Their equipment is excellent and their [[Control Tier|tier]] distribution is strong, but they have been evasive about their specific objectives in the desert, which makes him cautious about the kind of attention their expedition might attract.
