Selt Orvn runs the Crosswind Inn, the largest of [[Halveth]]'s three expedition-season accommodations and the one most likely to have space available when the village is full of researchers, guides, and treasure hunters preparing for [[Equatorial Desert]] crossings. She has been the inn's proprietor for twelve years, took over the business from her uncle when he decided that dealing with expedition groups was more exhausting than profitable, and has since built it into the most reliable lodging option in the village.

## The Crosswind Inn

The inn occupies a converted warehouse near Halveth's eastern edge — a large, practical building that was never designed for comfort but has been modified over the years to provide it anyway. Selt has divided the main floor into common sleeping areas with curtained alcoves for privacy, and the upper floor into smaller private rooms for expedition leaders, high-tier casters, and anyone willing to pay extra for walls and a door that closes.

The inn's common room serves as Halveth's unofficial expedition planning center during the busy season. The large tables accommodate map-spreading, equipment inventorying, and the kind of detailed logistical discussions that determine whether a crossing succeeds or fails. Selt provides these services deliberately — she has learned that expeditions which plan thoroughly in her common room are more likely to return and recommend the inn to future groups.

The kitchen operates at capacity during expedition season, serving preserved foods designed for travelers who need high nutrition and reliable storage. Selt sources ingredients from [[Auralis]] suppliers when possible, but she maintains relationships with local hunters and foragers to supplement the menu with fresh options that most expedition members have not eaten in months.

## Management Philosophy

Selt runs the inn on the principle that expedition groups are temporary but their reputations are permanent. A group that stays at the Crosswind and speaks well of their experience will bring future business. A group that leaves dissatisfied will cost her more than their room fees were worth. This makes her attentive to the specific needs of different expedition types and willing to accommodate requests that other innkeepers might consider unreasonable.

Research expeditions need quiet spaces for documentation work and secure storage for equipment that cannot be replaced if damaged. Independent treasure hunters need discretion about their objectives and flexibility about payment timing. Diplomatic parties need privacy and the kind of service that reflects well on Halveth's hospitality. Selt provides all of these, and she charges accordingly.

She does not, however, extend credit indefinitely or house groups that create problems for other guests. Her policy is clear: payment in advance, no violence on the premises, and any damage to the building or its contents is the responsibility of whoever caused it. Groups that cannot meet these standards are referred to the village's other accommodations.

## Relationship with [[Wren]]

Selt and [[Wren]] have a working arrangement that benefits both of them and the village as a whole. Wren occasionally directs expedition groups toward the Crosswind when she considers them worth accommodating. Selt provides Wren with information about which groups are planning what kinds of crossings, when they expect to depart, and whether their preparations seem adequate for their stated objectives.

This information flow is not formal surveillance — it is practical cooperation between two people who understand that Halveth's economy depends on successful expeditions and that failed expeditions reflect poorly on everyone involved. Selt's observations about expedition preparation are often more detailed than what Wren can gather from brief road encounters, and Wren's assessment of which groups are likely to cause problems helps Selt make decisions about accommodation availability.

Neither of them discusses this arrangement openly, but most of Halveth's permanent residents understand that it exists and consider it sensible village management.

## Experience with [[Rift Incursions]]

The Crosswind Inn's location near the village's eastern edge means it sits closer to the [[Rift Incursions|rift zones]] than most of Halveth's buildings. Selt has dealt with three separate incidents of lower-order [[Flux Demons]] reaching the inn's vicinity during her twelve years of operation. In each case, the demons were more interested in the [[Flux]] signatures of the expedition groups staying at the inn than in the building itself, but the encounters were close enough to require evacuation and careful management.

Selt's response protocol is practical and well-rehearsed: guests move to the inn's interior rooms, expedition groups with high-tier casters take responsibility for detection and defense, and she coordinates with other village residents to ensure that the incident is contained quickly. She does not panic, does not close the inn during periods of elevated rift activity, and does not charge extra for demon-related disruptions — all of which expedition groups appreciate and remember when making recommendations.

Her experience with these incidents has made her attentive to the signs that indicate when rift activity is increasing. She notices when the village feels different in ways that are hard to articulate, when [[Wren]]'s road work becomes more active, when the local guides start discussing route modifications. This awareness helps her prepare the inn and its guests for periods when demon encounters become more likely.

## Current Season

The upcoming expedition season is shaping up to be one of the busiest Selt has managed. She has confirmed reservations from two [[Auralis]] research expeditions, three independent groups, and a [[Terravelle]] party that has been deliberately vague about their objectives. The inn is booked to capacity for the peak crossing months, and she has had to turn away several groups that contacted her too late to secure space.

She is particularly interested in one of the independent groups — well-equipped, well-funded, and led by someone who has stayed at the Crosswind before and left a good impression. Their stated objective is ruins investigation, but their equipment list suggests they are prepared for more than archaeological work. Selt has mentioned this discrepancy to [[Wren]] but has not pressed for details. In her experience, groups that are secretive about their plans usually have reasons that are none of her business as long as they pay their bills and follow inn policies.
