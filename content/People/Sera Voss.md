## The First Test

Sera designs a minimal [[Flux]] signal structured to match the pattern she identified in the [[Watts]] ledger infrastructure. The signal is deliberately simple — a basic query formatted according to the notation fragments she has documented, channeled through a standard transaction node in [[Wolfpoint]]. She is not attempting to access anything, alter anything, or extract information. She is testing whether the pattern recognition she has developed through months of ledger analysis corresponds to something that can respond.

Something responds. Not a voice, not a message — a change in the ledger entry format that was not there before the signal was sent. The change is brief, structured, in a notation she does not fully recognize but that resembles [[Ancient Ruins|ancient sigil]] fragments from her research. The response appears in the transaction record itself, embedded in the standard Watts transfer documentation as additional data that should not exist within the current ledger system's architecture.

She tells no one. The test result is recorded separately from her other research, in notes that are not filed with the formal [[Wolfpoint]] archive system. The response suggests that the Watts infrastructure contains active components — not just automated transaction processing, but something capable of recognizing and responding to structured queries. This possibility had been theoretical until now.

She is designing a second test. The second test will be larger, more complex, structured to probe whether the response was automated recognition or something approaching communication. The design process is careful and methodical — she cannot predict what a more complex signal might activate, and the first response demonstrated that the infrastructure can modify its own output in ways the current kingdoms do not understand.

[[Ysel Voss]] does not know about the test or its results. [[Cael]] does not know. Sera has not discussed this development with anyone at Wolfpoint, including the senior practitioners who have been following her research with interest. The response raises questions about the nature of the ancient civilization's infrastructure that extend beyond academic curiosity into practical implications for both kingdoms' economic systems. Until she understands what she has found, she is keeping it contained.

## The First Test

Sera designs a minimal [[Flux]] signal structured to match the pattern she identified in the [[Watts]] ledger infrastructure. The signal is deliberately small — barely above the threshold of detection, encoded with the simplest possible query structure she can construct while maintaining the pattern characteristics that appear to be significant. She channels it through a standard transaction node in [[Wolfpoint]], routing it as if it were a routine ledger verification rather than an experimental probe.

Something responds. Not a voice, not a message — a change in the ledger entry format that was not there before. The response appears in the transaction log as a brief notation, structured and precise, written in symbols that resemble the ancient sigil fragments from her research but arranged in sequences she does not fully recognize. The notation appears for exactly long enough for her to record it, then disappears from the log entirely, leaving no trace that it was ever present.

She tells no one. The response is recorded separately from her other research, in notes that are not filed with the formal [[Wolfpoint]] archive system. She does not discuss it with [[Ysel Voss|Ysel]]. She does not mention it to [[Cael]]. The implications of what just happened — that something in the Watts network is capable of recognizing and responding to structured queries, that it can modify ledger entries in real-time, that it appears to be using notation systems that predate the current kingdoms — are too significant to share before she understands them better.

She is designing a second test. The second test will be larger — not dramatically so, but structured to probe whether the response was automated or deliberate, whether it can handle more complex queries, whether it will respond consistently to repeated contact. The second test will also be more dangerous. If something in the network is actively monitoring for the kind of signal she sent, a larger probe may attract attention she is not prepared to handle.

The work proceeds carefully. Sera has spent her adult life studying the intersection between current [[Flux]] practice and the remnants of what came before. She knows the difference between a discovery that can be shared immediately and one that requires preparation before it becomes public knowledge. This is the latter.
