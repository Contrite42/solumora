## The Notification Problem

Mira received word through an academic contact in [[Valdenmoor]] — a passing mention in correspondence about research resources that someone in [[Wolfpoint]] has been making inquiries about the [[Watts]] ledger infrastructure. Specifically, whether the network has historical documentation predating the current guilds. The contact thought it might interest her, given her work on ancient systems.

Mira does not know who is asking. The inquiry came through standard academic channels, the kind of cross-kingdom scholarly communication that happens routinely without institutional oversight. Someone researching economic history, perhaps, or infrastructure development. Nothing that would normally warrant attention.

Her concern is timing. If someone in [[Terravelle]] independently approaches the questions she has been sitting on, [[Drest]] may hear about it before she has decided whether to tell him. Drest's interpretation of "Terravelle poking at ancient infrastructure" will not be the same as hers. He will see strategic implications where she sees scholarly curiosity. He will want to know why she did not bring this to his attention immediately.

What she does is nothing, for now. She notes the contact, files the information separately from her other research, and decides to let two weeks pass before deciding whether to act. She is managing information and buying time — both for herself to determine what the inquiry actually represents, and for whoever is asking to either pursue it further or let it drop.

She does not know the person asking is [[Sera Voss]]. She does not know that the inquiry represents the first external probe of discoveries that could reshape both kingdoms' understanding of their own infrastructure. She knows only that someone is asking questions adjacent to work she has kept private, and that privacy may not be sustainable much longer.
