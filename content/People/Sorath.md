

## Current Movement

Sorath has come north from the desert interior and entered [[Hedun]]. This is unusual for Sorath — his patterns over the past several decades have favored the open road, small settlements, and the spaces between kingdoms rather than established port cities. His presence in Hedun represents a deviation from established behavior that he has not explained to anyone, because there is no one to explain it to.

The errand, from the outside, appears mundane: someone buying supplies at harbor district merchants, making a quiet inquiry at a counting house near the docks, lingering in the areas where cargo moves through the city. What the errand actually concerns is not visible to anyone Sorath interacts with. The merchants remember a customer who paid in full and asked no unusual questions. The counting house clerk remembers processing a routine transaction. The dock workers remember someone who moved through their workspace without disrupting the flow of work.

Sorath's presence in Hedun carries the same quality of careful unremarkability that has characterized his movement through [[Solumora]] for centuries. He moves through the city like someone who has been in worse places — not [[Fennick]]'s practiced social smoothness, not [[Selvane]]'s calculated institutional positioning, simply present without drawing attention to the fact of presence. Harbor districts are full of travelers. One more traveler, even one whose details seem to slide away from memory afterward, registers as normal variation rather than anomaly.

[[Fennick]] and Sorath have not crossed paths during Sorath's time in the city. The harbor district is large enough and busy enough that this represents scale rather than coincidence. Two individuals moving through the same area without encountering each other is unremarkable when the area processes hundreds of people daily. The proximity will not last — Sorath's patterns suggest he will move on within days, and Fennick's attention is currently directed toward social opportunities rather than systematic observation of harbor traffic.

[[Tolla Rend]], the harbor supervisor, has seen Sorath twice during routine oversight of dock operations. Both encounters were brief and professional — Sorath asking a practical question about cargo scheduling, Tolla providing the requested information. Tolla has noted something was wrong about both interactions but cannot name what. The wrongness is not behavioral — Sorath's questions were appropriate, his manner was unremarkable. It is something else, a quality that registers below conscious analysis. Tolla has not acted on this observation because there is nothing specific to act on.
