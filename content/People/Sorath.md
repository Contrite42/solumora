

## Current Movement

Sorath has come north from the desert interior and entered [[Hedun]]. This is unusual for Sorath — his patterns over the past several decades have favored the open road, small settlements, and the spaces between kingdoms rather than established port cities. His presence in Hedun represents a deviation from established behavior that he has not explained to anyone, because there is no one to explain it to.

The errand, from the outside, appears mundane: someone buying supplies at harbor district merchants, making a quiet inquiry at a counting house near the docks, lingering in the areas where cargo moves through the city. What the errand actually concerns is not visible to anyone Sorath interacts with. The merchants remember a customer who paid in full and asked no unusual questions. The counting house clerk remembers processing a routine transaction. The dock workers remember someone who moved through their workspace without disrupting the flow of work.

Sorath's presence in Hedun carries the same quality of careful unremarkability that has characterized his movement through [[Solumora]] for centuries. He moves through the city like someone who has been in worse places — not [[Fennick]]'s practiced social smoothness, not [[Selvane]]'s calculated institutional positioning, simply present without drawing attention to the fact of presence. Harbor districts are full of travelers. One more traveler, even one whose details seem to slide away from memory afterward, registers as normal variation rather than anomaly.

[[Fennick]] and Sorath have not crossed paths during Sorath's time in the city. The harbor district is large enough and busy enough that this represents scale rather than coincidence. Two individuals moving through the same area without encountering each other is unremarkable when the area processes hundreds of people daily. The proximity will not last — Sorath's patterns suggest he will move on within days, and Fennick's attention is currently directed toward social opportunities rather than systematic observation of harbor traffic.

[[Tolla Rend]], the harbor supervisor, has seen Sorath twice during routine oversight of dock operations. Both encounters were brief and professional — Sorath asking a practical question about cargo scheduling, Tolla providing the requested information. Tolla has noted something was wrong about both interactions but cannot name what. The wrongness is not behavioral — Sorath's questions were appropriate, his manner was unremarkable. It is something else, a quality that registers below conscious analysis. Tolla has not acted on this observation because there is nothing specific to act on.

## Current Movement

Sorath has come north from the desert interior and entered [[Hedun]]. This is unusual for Sorath — his patterns over the past several decades have kept him to the smaller settlements and the roads between them, places where a quiet traveler attracts no particular attention and leaves no lasting impression. Hedun is larger than his typical stops, busier, with more institutional presence and more people whose job it is to notice things that don't fit.

The errand that brought him here appears, from outside, as someone buying supplies, making a quiet inquiry at a harbor counting house, lingering near the docks without obvious purpose. What the errand actually concerns is not visible to anyone Sorath interacts with. The merchant who sold him travel provisions remembers a customer who paid in full and asked no questions. The clerk at the counting house remembers processing a standard inquiry about shipping schedules. The dock workers who have seen him near the waterfront remember someone who was present but not in the way.

Sorath's presence in Hedun has the quality of someone who has been in worse places. Not [[Fennick]]'s smoothness, not [[Selvane]]'s prominence. Simply present. He moves through the harbor district like someone for whom cities are a temporary condition to be endured rather than engaged with. He does not avoid attention so much as fail to generate it — another traveler in a port city full of travelers, unremarkable enough that the effort of remembering him specifically does not seem worth making.

[[Fennick]] and Sorath have not crossed paths. The harbor district is large, Fennick's current interests lie elsewhere in the city, and proximity is not the same as encounter. The scale of Hedun is sufficient that two people can operate in the same general area for days without their movements intersecting. This proximity will not last — Sorath's business in the city appears to be concluding, and his pattern suggests he will move on soon.

[[Tolla Rend]], the harbor supervisor, has seen Sorath twice in the past week and noted something was wrong without being able to name what. Not wrong in the sense of criminal activity or immediate threat, but wrong in the sense of a detail that doesn't fit the pattern she expects from travelers passing through her section of the port. She has not acted on this observation — there is nothing specific to act on, and her responsibilities do not extend to investigating feelings about people who have committed no offense. She has noted it, filed it in the mental category of things to remember if they become relevant later, and continued her work.
