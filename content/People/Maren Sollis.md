_(Council Member, Auralis — Reform Faction)_

Maren Sollis is the most isolated member of [[The Council of Auralis]] and the only one who consistently advocates for [[Cassia]]'s actual authority as queen. She has been fighting this fight for six years, since she joined the council, and losing it incrementally every session. What makes this particularly frustrating is that she is usually right about the policy issues, demonstrably competent at the administrative work, and completely unable to translate either fact into political influence.

She is thirty-eight years old, assessed at T6, and represents what [[Auralis]] claims to value — [[Flux]] mastery in service of the kingdom's advancement — while being systematically marginalized by the people who control how that advancement actually happens.

## Character

Maren is direct in the way that people who have never learned to be otherwise tend to be. She says what she thinks, asks the questions other people are avoiding, and responds to political maneuvering as though it were a problem to be solved rather than a game to be played. This approach has made her effective at identifying real issues and completely ineffective at building the coalitions necessary to address them.

Her [[Flux]] discipline is Detection — specifically, the analysis of complex systems and the identification of structural problems before they become visible failures. At T6, her range and precision allow her to assess everything from the integrity of a building's foundation to the health of a trade network to the stability of a political arrangement. She can see the stress fractures in [[Solhaven]]'s social hierarchy, the resource bottlenecks in the kingdom's expansion plans, and the growing contradictions in the council's approach to [[Cassia]]'s role. What she cannot see is how to fix any of it without the cooperation of people who benefit from the problems continuing.

## The Reform Agenda

Maren's political position is straightforward: [[Cassia]] is the queen, the council exists to advise her, and the current arrangement where the council makes decisions and Cassia signs them is both constitutionally wrong and practically unsustainable. She has been making this argument for six years with increasing urgency as her Detection work shows her the structural stress building in the kingdom's political system.

Her proposed reforms are moderate by any reasonable standard. Restore the queen's authority over major policy decisions. Require council proposals to be presented to her for genuine consideration rather than pro forma approval. Establish regular private meetings between the queen and individual council members so she can develop independent relationships with them. Create formal mechanisms for the queen to initiate policy rather than only responding to council initiatives.

These proposals have been voted down consistently, usually by margins that suggest the other council members consider them somewhere between naive and treasonous. [[Castor Helme]] treats them as amusing distractions. [[Drest]] treats them as irrelevant to the real work of the kingdom. [[Tovan Ashce]] votes against them while expressing sympathy for their underlying logic, which Maren finds more infuriating than outright opposition.

## Relationship with Cassia

Maren has tried twice to warn [[Cassia]] about [[Castor Helme]]'s manipulation and the broader pattern of council control. Both attempts failed, but not for the reasons she expected.

The first attempt was direct: a private meeting where Maren explained what she was observing about the council's treatment of the queen and suggested that Cassia had more authority than she was being allowed to exercise. Cassia listened politely, thanked her for the concern, and gave no indication that she understood the warning or intended to act on it. Maren left the meeting convinced that Cassia was either too naive to recognize the manipulation or too intimidated to resist it.

The second attempt was more careful: Maren used her Detection ability to document specific instances of council members making decisions without consulting the queen, then presented this analysis as a procedural concern rather than a political one. Cassia reviewed the documentation thoroughly, asked several intelligent questions about the constitutional precedents, and then filed it away without taking any action. This time Maren left convinced that Cassia understood the situation perfectly but had decided that challenging it was not worth the risk.

Both interpretations were wrong. Cassia understood the warnings, appreciated the concern, and recognized that Maren was the only council member genuinely advocating for her interests. What Cassia also understood was that Maren lacked the political power to protect her if she moved against the council's control and failed. The warnings were accurate, but they were not actionable given the resources available.

Maren has not attempted a third warning. She continues to advocate for the queen's authority in council sessions, but she has stopped expecting Cassia to advocate for it herself.

## Detection Work

Maren's T6 Detection ability makes her the most informed person on the council about the kingdom's actual condition, which is both her greatest asset and her greatest source of frustration. She can see problems developing years before they become crises, but she cannot convince her colleagues to address them until the damage is already done.

Her analysis of the kingdom's expansion into [[Terravelle]]'s territory shows clear signs of overextension — supply lines that are too long, administrative structures that are too thin, local populations that are more resistant than the reports indicate. She has presented this analysis to the council three times. Each time, the Expansion Faction has dismissed it as excessive caution, and the vote has gone against her.

Her analysis of the [[Emberfall]] research program shows resource allocation patterns that suggest diminishing returns — more funding producing proportionally less progress, research directions that have been pursued past the point of likely success, personnel assignments that prioritize political loyalty over technical competence. She has presented this analysis twice. Both times, [[Drest]] has responded with classified information about recent breakthroughs that justify continued investment, and the council has voted to maintain current funding levels.

Her analysis of [[Solhaven]]'s social stability shows increasing stratification, growing resentment in the lower terraces, and political pressure that is building toward some kind of release. She has not presented this analysis to the council because she knows exactly how it would be received, and she is not prepared to be dismissed on an issue where the consequences of being right are measured in civil unrest.

## The Isolation Problem

Maren's political isolation on the council is not accidental. Her positions are inconvenient to everyone else's agenda, her Detection work produces information that contradicts their preferred narratives, and her advocacy for the queen's authority threatens the arrangement that allows them to govern without meaningful oversight.

[[Castor Helme]] considers her a nuisance — someone who asks uncomfortable questions and proposes solutions that would interfere with his research priorities. He has never moved against her directly, but he has consistently worked to ensure that her proposals receive minimal consideration and her concerns are framed as excessive worry rather than legitimate analysis.

[[Drest]] considers her useful for identifying technical problems but naive about political realities. He respects her Detection ability and occasionally consults her about implementation details, but he dismisses her broader political concerns as a distraction from the real work of advancing the kingdom's capabilities.

[[Tovan Ashce]] considers her the most dangerous person on the council, not because of her current influence but because of her potential influence if the political situation changes. If [[Cassia]] ever moves to assert real authority, Maren will be her natural ally and most competent advisor. Tovan has been quietly working to ensure that Maren remains isolated until he can determine whether supporting her or opposing her better serves his long-term interests.

## What Maren Wants

Maren wants [[Auralis]] to function the way it claims to function — with genuine royal authority, competent administration, and policy decisions based on accurate information rather than factional politics. She wants the council to serve the kingdom rather than their own agendas. She wants her Detection work to be used to prevent problems rather than ignored until they become crises.

She also wants [[Cassia]] to succeed as queen, not because of personal loyalty but because she believes the alternative — continued council control with no meaningful oversight — will eventually damage the kingdom beyond repair. Her Detection work shows her the stress fractures building in the current system. She knows that something will eventually break, and she would prefer it to be the political arrangement rather than the kingdom itself.

This makes her both the most principled member of the council and the most frustrated one. She is fighting for outcomes that would benefit everyone in the long term, but she is fighting alone against people who benefit from the current system in the short term. She is usually right about the technical issues, but she is consistently wrong about the political feasibility of addressing them.

The tragedy of Maren's position is that she represents exactly what [[Auralis]] claims to value — competence, integrity, and the use of [[Flux]] mastery in service of the kingdom's genuine interests — while being systematically excluded from the decisions that determine how those values are actually implemented.
