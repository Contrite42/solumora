In [[Solumora]], those who interact with [[Flux]] are not simply called magic users — their title reflects their _relationship_ to it, whether that means copying it, practicing it, studying it, or embodying it at a level the kingdom's systems cannot meaningfully measure.

Both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] share the same titles and broadly the same language around Flux use, though the _weight_ each kingdom places on these roles differs considerably.

## Scribes

Scribes copy and reproduce existing [[Sigils]] and [[Grimoires]] for mass distribution. Their work is exacting — a misdrawn [[Sigils|sigil]] fails or misfires — and a skilled Scribe is commercially valuable regardless of their personal [[Control Tier]]. A Tier 6 caster who prefers the desk to the field is as welcome in a scriptorium as a Tier 1. The role carries no stigma; in [[Terravelle]] particularly, Scribes are considered essential tradespeople.

## Formulists

Formulist is the general term for anyone actively practicing [[Flux]] use in daily life. Most working casters fall here — the blacksmith with a [[Common Grimoire#Warmstone|Warmstone]] sigil on his forge, the courier carrying a [[Uncommon Grimoire#Whisperlink|Whisperlink]] token, the soldier with a [[Common Grimoire#Shove|Shove]] sigil on their shield. Being a Formulist implies nothing about power level, only that [[Flux]] use is part of how a person operates in the world.

## Channelers

Channeler is reserved for those who can operate safely at [[Control Tier]] 5 and above. At this threshold, the exponential nature of [[Flux]] scaling means a Channeler is not simply a stronger Formulist — they are categorically different in what they can hold, shape, and survive. Most people will never meet a Channeler. In [[Auralis]] they attract fame and attention almost automatically; in [[Terravelle]] they are respected but quietly regarded as outside the normal social contract.

## Scholars

A Scholar is anyone whose primary pursuit is the creation and development of new [[Sigils]] and spell structures. [[Control Tier]] ceiling is irrelevant — a Tier 1 caster can spend a lifetime designing [[Sigils|sigils]] intended for Channelers, and the work is no less valuable for it. Scholars are the architects of the spell ecosystem; without them the [[Grimoires]] stagnate. Most operate within academic institutions or under private patronage, and [[Auralis]] funds Scholar work far more aggressively than [[Terravelle]].

## Conduits

Conduits exist beyond the kingdom classification system. The standard [[Control Tier]] scale was not built to contain them — at the apex of exponential [[Flux]] scaling, the difference between a Tier 8 and a Tier 9 caster is not a step, it is a gulf. With only approximately 500 in all of [[Solumora]], most people will live and die without ever encountering one. They are not unregistered out of rebellion or secrecy — the system simply has no honest way to rate them. Conduits tend toward isolation, not by law but by the quiet reality that operating at their level in a civilized setting is considered deeply antisocial at best, and catastrophic at worst.

## Education and Training

Flux competence is not innate. Practitioners learn through formal education, guild apprenticeship, or family transmission — each pathway with distinct costs, cultural contexts, and outcomes.

**[[Aldrane Academy]]** is [[Auralis]]'s largest institutional training facility, teaching structured curriculum across three tracks: Scribal (2 years), Formulist (4 years), and Scholar (6 years). Admission is merit-based, and tuition ranges from 40,000 to 180,000 [[Watts]]. Students who cannot afford costs may enter through bonded enrollment, receiving subsidized education in exchange for years of post-graduation civic service. Academy graduates are trained, reliable, and capable within documented procedures — though they often struggle with improvisation compared to guild-trained practitioners. [[Vessa Korst]], a T3 Formulist instructor with 22 years at Aldrane, exemplifies the Academy's emphasis on safety, discipline, and producing practitioners who will not kill themselves through ignorance.

**[[Terravelle Guild System]]** organizes instruction through specialized trade guilds rather than centralized schools. Prospective apprentices gain entry through family connection, demonstrated work, or direct sponsorship from existing guild members. Apprenticeships last 3-7 years, involve unpaid labor in exchange for embedded instruction, and advance based on demonstrated competence rather than fixed timelines. Guild training produces practitioners who excel at adaptive problem-solving and field improvisation but often lack broad theoretical grounding. [[Dren Velt]], a T4 Thermal Guild master in [[Solhaven]], has trained eleven apprentices across 26 years — eight became journeymen, two advanced to master status, and one was expelled for persistent carelessness.

**[[Family Flux Transmission]]** is the most common and least visible education pathway. Children born into Flux-working families learn through ambient observation, practical assistance, and supervised casting embedded in household work. This pathway costs no tuition, begins years before formal programs, and teaches Flux use in live professional contexts. However, quality depends entirely on teaching parents' competence, produces specialists rather than generalists, and reinforces inter-generational class barriers by excluding children from non-Flux families.

**[[Grimoire Apprenticeship Contracts]]** structure the legal and social relationships between apprentices and masters. In [[Auralis]], contracts are formal legal documents enforced through civic courts, specifying service obligations, termination clauses, and financial penalties for breach. In [[Terravelle]], contracts are social compacts enforced through guild reputation structures — breaking a contract does not trigger legal penalties but causes professional reputation collapse that ends careers.

**[[Flux Education Costs]]** create substantial barriers to access. [[Aldrane Academy]] Formulist Track tuition (120,000 Watts) represents 5-8 years of average working-class family income. [[Terravelle Guild System|Guild apprenticeships]] require families to support non-earning members for years plus cover material costs. Geographic barriers add further expense for rural families. Wealth stratification means most Flux practitioners come from families who could already afford multi-year educational investments. Merit scholarships and bonded enrollment provide limited social mobility but do not eliminate class barriers.

**[[Scholar Training]]** prepares practitioners who design new [[Sigils|sigils]] rather than execute existing ones. [[Aldrane Academy]]'s 6-year Scholar Track teaches Flux theory, sigil geometry, experimental design, and safety protocols for untested spell structures. [[Control Tier]] ceiling does not determine Scholar capability — T1 Scholars design T4 spells they cannot personally cast. However, higher-tier Scholars can test their own prototypes, giving them research independence. [[Terravelle]] has no formal Scholar training equivalent; Terravelle Scholars learn through family mentorship or self-directed study, producing fewer but highly specialized practitioners.

The result is tier-stratified, culturally divergent, and class-reinforcing education infrastructure. [[Auralis]] optimizes for scale, standardization, and meritocratic access. [[Terravelle]] optimizes for quality control, professional continuity, and gatekept expertise. Both systems function. Neither is equitable.
