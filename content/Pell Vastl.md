**Pell Vastl** is forty-eight years old and has spent twenty-two years as a transaction scribe at a merchant house in [[Ashford]] — the person whose work is verifying [[Birth Runes|birth runes]], confirming [[Watts]] ledger entries, documenting cargo transfers, and maintaining the paper trail that formal commerce requires. He assessed [[Control Tier|T1]]. T1 is adequate for this work and nothing more, which is exactly the relationship he has developed with his tier: it does the job, and the job is sufficient.

## Appearance

Pell is forty-eight with the precise, unassuming appearance of someone who has spent twenty-two years as a transaction scribe — present but unremarkable, observant without drawing attention. His dark hair is thinning and kept short for practicality, and he dresses in the neutral professional style of merchant house staff: simple tunics in browns and grays, always neat but never memorable. He wears reading glasses constantly — thin wire frames that have become part of his face after decades of document review work.

His hands are the most notable thing about him: perpetually ink-stained from verification work, with the distinctive callus on his right index finger from years of birth rune authentication and ledger confirmation sigils. He holds documents with the careful precision of someone who knows that misreading a single line can invalidate an entire transaction, and his eyes move across manifests with practiced efficiency, catching inconsistencies that others miss. He has developed the peculiar stillness of someone who spends their work life sitting in the same chair reviewing the same kinds of paperwork, and the slight stoop in his shoulders speaks to decades of hunching over ledgers.

His expression is professionally neutral — neither curious nor incurious about the transactions he processes, simply attentive to accuracy. When he speaks it's in brief, precise statements, the verbal habit of someone who has learned that fewer words mean fewer complications. Off-duty he has a dry wit and a tendency to notice the structural patterns in merchant house operations that everyone else accepts as given, though he rarely comments on them unless directly asked. The only time his face shows genuine engagement is when discussing the Watts system — the elegant distributed infrastructure underlying everything he does — and his eyes actually light up when explaining how the ledger's verification protocols work.

## Work

He is not particularly interested in the larger economic patterns his work feeds into, except in the way that a person who has seen the same kinds of transactions for twenty-two years develops a working model of what they mean. He knows when a transaction structure is unusual without being able to articulate exactly why. He knows when a cargo's documentation is technically complete but organized in the way that documentation is organized when the people doing the organizing are hoping no one looks closely.

He has worked for three different merchant houses over twenty-two years. All three have had operations that included transactions structured in this way. He has not reported any of them to the guild review office. He has assessed, at each house, whether the transaction structures involved would create problems for him specifically, and whether the problems were ones the review office would actually address. His assessments have been accurate.

He finds the [[Watts]] system remarkable and says so to anyone who asks. The ledger that underlies all of it — distributed, impossible to forge, no institution in control — is the most elegant piece of infrastructure he works with daily, and nothing about his twenty-two years has diminished this. The people using it are exactly as clever and dishonest as people have always been. The ledger records all of it perfectly. He finds this, somehow, reassuring.
