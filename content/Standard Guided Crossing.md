The standard guided crossing is the dominant method for traversing [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] — used by [[Auralis]]-funded research expeditions, commercial parties carrying cross-kingdom cargo, and any individual who has been adequately advised about what the desert does to the unprepared.

## Parameters

A standard guided crossing runs 14 to 21 days depending on route, season, and party composition. The variance is real: a lean party with an experienced guide in optimal weather is different from a larger group with mixed Flux capability navigating an early rift season. Expedition leaders who plan for 14 days and do not build in buffer are statistically over-represented in the incident record.

Minimum viable party composition:

- One [[Halveth Cooperative]]-vetted guide (T3 minimum; T4 preferred for any crossing involving ruins proximity)
- Two casters with thermal discipline coverage (the guide typically provides one coverage set; parties should not assume the guide absorbs all thermal load)
- One caster with navigation capability — not map-reading, but the ability to dead-reckon in featureless terrain under wind conditions and maintain a corrected heading when landmarks disappear

Party size above six introduces coordination costs that reduce speed without improving survival odds proportionally, unless the additional members add specific coverage the party lacked (e.g., a dedicated rift-response caster for ruins-approach expeditions).

## Grimoire Requirements

The standard grimoire kit for a guided crossing covers:

- **Thermal regulation**: heat band survival during day transit; cold protection for the temperature collapse at night. Both are required. Neither can be deprioritized in favor of the other.
- **Air filtration**: particulate protection for lungs and sigil surfaces in constant wind. Without this, grimoire pages degrade within hours of open exposure.
- **Navigation aids**: heading-hold and landmark-substitute sigils for low-visibility traversal.
- **Fauna detection**: presence-of-Flux-signature sensing for early warning on creature approach.
- **Emergency shelter**: rapid deployment shelter sigils rated for full weather conditions.

Parties from [[Auralis]] that pass through [[Halveth]] typically arrive with kits assembled per their home-institution standard. The [[Halveth Cooperative]] gear certification review exists because those standards are calibrated for the theoretical crossing, not the actual one. Selt Orvn's depot maintains updated inventory of the configurations that have worked in practice.

## Failure Profile

Standard guided crossings fail most commonly through thermal reserve exhaustion — parties that use Flux faster than projected and arrive at the critical heat band with insufficient capacity to manage the temperature. The second most common failure is navigation loss, typically occurring when wind conditions exceed the party's ability to maintain a corrected heading and they are not aware of the cumulative deviation until they have moved too far off route to correct without extending the crossing time past their supply limits.

Guide-led parties that follow the guide's instructions closely have substantially better survival outcomes than parties where expedition leaders override guide judgment. This is well-documented. It does not stop expedition leaders from overriding guide judgment.

## Seasonal Timing

Optimal crossing windows:

- **Mid-autumn**: after the peak heat season, before the worst winter winds. Preferred by most Auralis expeditions.
- **Early spring**: temperature margins are adequate; rift activity near ruins sites tends to be lower than late-season.

Crossings during midsummer are not recommended. They happen anyway, for cargo that cannot wait, and the survival rate reflects the timing.

## Non-Casters and Low-Tier Party Members

[[Control Tier|T0]] and [[Control Tier|T1]] travelers can complete a guided crossing but cannot contribute to the party's Flux coverage — they are passengers within the protection envelope that higher-tier casters maintain. Each T0 or T1 member is a person whose thermal and environmental survival load sits entirely on the rest of the party.

The practical implication: party composition reviews by [[Halveth Cooperative]] guides assess total Flux coverage capacity against total party size. A five-person party with two T0 or T1 members and three T3 casters needs the three casters to cover five people's protection load, not three. Guides who conduct accurate assessments before departure account for this explicitly. Expedition leaders who assume low-tier members can handle some portion of their own desert protection needs are reliably wrong.

## Access Floor Constraint (Addendum)

At the Zakros entry zone, [[Control Tier|Tier 0]] and [[Control Tier|Tier 1]] cannot break into the active Flux field without higher-tier support. Standard guided manifests therefore treat them as non-channel coverage for thermal stabilization, navigation correction, and emergency response.

The practical planning rule is unchanged but now explicit: crossing-critical casts are assigned to T2+ practitioners, with T3+ guide coverage preferred for route control under stress.
