_(Voss Line — Wolfpoint)_

**Ysel Voss** is twenty-three years old, the elder of [[Sera Voss|Sera Voss's]] two children, and the person in [[Wolfpoint]] most likely to inherit its leadership. She is not certain of this. Sera has not told her. [[Doran Voss|Doran]] has not been told either. Ysel is aware that she and her brother are both being assessed and that the assessment is ongoing, and she considers this a reasonable way to run a succession and a mildly uncomfortable thing to live inside.

## Character

Ysel has her mother's precision and something her mother does not have in quite the same form: urgency. Where Sera is patient with long work, Ysel is patient with long work and wants to know what it is for. She has grown up watching the Hypertext document exist in draft — eleven years of drafts, each more complete than the last, none of them finished — and the question of what happens when it is finished has been present in her mind for most of her adult life. Not anxiously. Just present, the way a stone is present at the bottom of a clear lake.

She thinks in branches — in conditional structures, in if-then frameworks — more naturally than anyone else in Wolfpoint, including her mother. Her Hypertext sequences tend to be longer and more complex than the exercises require, because she finds the constraint of a short line boring and the problem of a long conditional chain genuinely interesting. Sera has told her twice that complexity that is not necessary is a form of dishonesty. Ysel has heard this and is not sure she agrees about all cases.

She is also the person in Wolfpoint most curious about the world outside it. She has read the correspondence archives extensively — every letter from [[Solhaven]], [[Valdenmoor]], the independent scholars who cycled through, the long exchange with [[Mira Solv]] — and has a picture of the current political situation between the kingdoms that is detailed, current, and built entirely from secondary sources. She has never left Wolfpoint.

## On the Document

Ysel has read more of the current draft than [[Sera Voss|her mother]] knows. She found the working copies in the document room during a period when Sera was traveling the settlement on other business, and she read them with the careful attention of someone who understood that she was doing something her mother had not explicitly invited. She has not mentioned this.

What she found in the later sections — the structural analysis of the [[Watts|ledger's]] underlying [[Flux]] architecture, the comparison to Hypertext encoding at a deep level — she has thought about at some length. She has not arrived at the same conclusion as Sera. She has arrived at a question: if the ledger was built by someone who understood Flux the way the document's analysis suggests, and if those builders are the same people who left the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]], then the ruins are not the only thing they left, and the document is not the only way to look at what they built.

She has written this question down in her personal notes and not shared it with anyone.
