# Kess Rolv

**Kess Rolv** is [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]]'s Ashford relations coordinator, managing the [[Ashford Supply Contract]] portfolio that provides [[Wolfpoint]] with grain, salt, tools, and specialty goods the settlement cannot produce internally. She is forty-six years old, [[Control Tier|Tier 4]], and has held the position for twelve years. Her job is maintaining relationships with multiple [[Ashford]] merchant houses, balancing supply diversification against relationship maintenance, and ensuring [[Wolfpoint]]'s import flow remains reliable enough to sustain independent settlement status.

Wolfpoint's survival depends on southern supply access. Kess is the person who makes that access work.

## The Contract Portfolio

Kess manages standing relationships with approximately 8-12 Ashford merchant houses, each specializing in different supply categories. She deliberately avoids single-supplier dependency: no merchant controls more than 40% of any critical supply category (grain, salt, tools).

**Grain contracts:** Three merchant houses supply grain through rotating annual agreements. Diversification protects against individual merchant failure, political disruption, or price manipulation.

**Salt contracts:** Coastal extraction operations near [[Hedun]] supply salt, transported to Ashford, then north through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. [[Morra Keld]] at [[Mountain Game Collective]] communicates preservation needs directly to Kess, who negotiates contracts 20% above projected requirements.

**Tool and metal stock:** Multiple metalworking suppliers provide replacement tools and raw stock. These relationships are more flexible — tools degrade on multi-year timelines, allowing opportunistic purchasing when suppliers offer favorable terms.

## Annual Negotiation Cycle

Kess spends approximately one month per year in Ashford conducting face-to-face contract negotiations. The rest of the year, she manages relationships through correspondence carried by packet ships, responding to price adjustments, delivery delays, and merchant inquiries.

The negotiation cycle:
- **Winter assessment** — reviewing previous year's supply performance and export revenue projections.
- **Spring contracting** — negotiating volume guarantees, pricing, delivery schedules, payment terms.
- **Execution phase** — monitoring deliveries spring through autumn, adjusting as disruptions occur.
- **Settlement** — final payments, dispute resolution, preliminary discussions for next cycle.

Payment structure is hybrid: partially in watts (currency transferred through Ashford banking), partially through commodity exchange (Wolfpoint exports delivered in exchange for import allocations). This reduces currency requirements and gives merchants direct access to premium cold-forge metalwork and preserved game.

## Trust Distance Management

Ashford merchants are profit-seeking entities in competitive southern markets. Wolfpoint is a remote settlement with limited options if contracts fail. Kess maintains leverage through:
- **Diversified relationships** — no single merchant can hold Wolfpoint hostage through supply control.
- **Multi-year reputation** — twelve years of consistent contract performance establishes Wolfpoint as low-risk partner.
- **Measured responses** — when merchants violate terms (late deliveries, short quantities), Kess adjusts future allocations gradually rather than terminating relationships immediately.

This measured approach prevents sudden supply disruptions while creating long-term accountability pressure. Merchants who consistently underperform lose business share to more reliable competitors.

## Crisis Management

Kess's most critical function is managing supply disruptions before they threaten settlement survival:

When **maritime disruptions** delay deliveries, Kess:
- Activates grain and salt reserves to buffer immediate consumption.
- Renegotiates delivery schedules and adjusted quantities with merchants.
- Communicates with [[Renn Taska]] about export timing if delayed imports affect production operations.

When **merchant failures** occur (business collapse, southern supply constraints), Kess:
- Sources alternative suppliers while reserves buffer the gap.
- Adjusts payment terms with remaining suppliers to incentivize increased allocations.
- Escalates to Renn if failures threaten multiple supply categories simultaneously.

She has managed both crisis types multiple times during twelve years. The pattern is consistent: early detection through monitoring merchant correspondence, rapid activation of contingency protocols, sustained communication with affected parties.

## What She Knows About Kingdom Dynamics

Through Ashford merchant relationships, Kess maintains informal awareness of:
- [[Terravelle]] guild policy shifts affecting trade relationships.
- [[Auralis]] political factional movements that might disrupt supply chains.
- Market conditions and economic trends that could affect Wolfpoint's export demand or import costs.

This intelligence supplements what the Voss line learns through formal channels. Kess reports patterns to Renn quarterly. The information helps [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] anticipate disruptions before they become crises.

## Strategic Dependencies and Vulnerabilities

Kess operates with full awareness of Wolfpoint's structural vulnerabilities:
- **Maritime dependence** — all imports travel through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]].
- **Ashford concentration** — all supply funnels through Ashford before traveling north.
- **Payment capacity** — Wolfpoint must generate sufficient export revenue to fund imports.

She cannot eliminate these vulnerabilities. She manages them by maintaining diversified relationships, negotiating buffer provisions into contracts, and ensuring Renn Taska has accurate intelligence about supply-security trends.

## Daily Work

Outside the annual Ashford negotiation month, Kess spends most days at Registry offices: managing merchant correspondence, tracking delivery schedules against contractual commitments, coordinating with Wolfpoint production operations about import-timing dependencies, and updating the contract portfolio documentation that tracks twelve years of relationship performance data.

She reviews correspondence from three Ashford grain suppliers about next quarter's delivery schedules. She confirms salt contract terms with the Hedun extraction operation's representative. She drafts responses to two merchants requesting adjustment to specialty-goods pricing. The letters travel south with the next packet ship. The grain flows north. Wolfpoint continues.
