Nessa Keld is operations planning director for [[Crestward Field Company]] in [[Emberfall]], responsible for translating council research goals into field plans that can survive actual [[Equatorial Desert]] conditions.

She is [[Control Tier|Tier 4]], 42 years old, and is known for reducing mission scope early when risk projection diverges from sponsor assumptions.

## Role

Nessa's work starts where ambition becomes logistics. She receives expedition briefs from council-backed research programs and reconstructs them into executable architecture:

- objective ladders (primary/secondary/minimum viable outputs)
- staffing profiles matched to route conditions
- cache, withdrawal, and communication thresholds
- conditions under which mission continuation becomes irresponsible

Her planning model assumes that desert work is dynamic and that fixed plans fail unless they include pre-authorized down-scope paths.

## Reputation

Sponsors describe her as difficult when schedules are political. Field teams describe her as the reason they came back.

She has a standing policy that no mission departs with unresolved contradictions between route intelligence and load assumptions. This often delays launch windows and creates friction with faction offices seeking visible progress.

## Relationship to Institutions

Nessa coordinates directly with [[Emberfall Expedition Registry]], [[Halveth Guide Bureau]], and analysts from [[Zakros Loss Table]]. She treats these offices as one operating chain rather than separate bureaucratic layers.

Her influence is procedural, not political. She does not advocate policy in council sessions. She builds plans that force policy pressure to pass through documented operational constraints.

## Why She Matters

Nessa represents a recurring pattern in Auralis expedition culture: mid-level experts who absorb strategic ambition and output survivable practice.

Without planners like her, council goals remain declarations.
