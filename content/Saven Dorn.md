**Saven Dorn** is forty-one years old and supervises extraction operations for a mining group in the rock formations northeast of [[Greyveil]]. [[Control Tier|T3]]. He started as a shaft worker at eighteen, moved into crew lead work at twenty-six, and has been running the group's northern section operations for six years. The group owns three active shafts and two that are being assessed for viability. His job is the middle layer of this: translating the operational reality of what the shafts can produce into the commercial commitments that the group makes to buyers.

He works regularly with the ore factors who operate between the extraction operations and the southern markets. [[Brenne Kalt]] handles his group's primary long-term contracts. The working relationship is seven years old and productive in the way of professional relationships where both parties have learned what the other person is actually good at and adjusted their expectations accordingly. Saven is good at knowing what the shafts can do on any given timeline. Brenne is good at turning that information into agreements that hold. Neither of them pretends to be good at the other's part.

The extraction work is physically demanding and has a straightforward relationship to competence that Saven finds preferable to the alternatives he has observed. A supervisor who overestimates the shaft capacity commits the group to contracts that cannot be fulfilled. A supervisor who underestimates it leaves production and income on the surface. Both mistakes are immediately visible. He has made both, early in his tenure. He made them once each.

His T3 casting is used for structural assessment — reading stress patterns in the rock to determine shaft stability and identify where reinforcement is needed. Standard extraction application. He is good at it without finding it especially interesting. The interesting part is the logistics. The casting is a tool.
