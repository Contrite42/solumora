**Saven Dorn** is forty-one years old and supervises extraction operations for a mining group in the rock formations northeast of [[Greyveil]]. [[Control Tier|T3]]. He started as a shaft worker at eighteen, moved into crew lead work at twenty-six, and has been running the group's northern section operations for six years. The group owns three active shafts and two that are being assessed for viability. His job is the middle layer of this: translating the operational reality of what the shafts can produce into the commercial commitments that the group makes to buyers.

## Appearance

Saven is forty-one with the weathered, solidly built appearance of someone who spent fifteen years as a shaft worker before moving into supervision six years ago. His dark brown hair is kept short and shows significant gray — premature aging that comes from years of physical extraction work and the stress of running operations. He dresses in a hybrid style between field supervisor and commercial operations: heavy work clothing suitable for shaft inspections but clean enough for meetings with ore factors, everything practical and showing the ore dust that never completely washes out of mining work.

His hands show the full scope of his career — heavy callusing and scarring from eighteen years of extraction work, newer ink staining from six years of contract documentation, the mixed evidence of someone who still spends time in the shafts but whose primary work is now coordination rather than labor. He moves with the economical efficiency of someone who learned energy management through physical labor and carries that discipline into administrative work, and his posture shows the slight stiffness of old injuries from shaft work that he doesn't mention.

When discussing operations his manner is precise and practical, speaking in the direct language of someone who has made visible mistakes early in his supervisory tenure and learned from them. He has developed the supervisor's particular skill of assessing shaft capacity accurately — neither overcommitting the group to contracts they can't fulfill nor leaving production on the surface — and his expression when reviewing production reports shows the focused calculation of someone translating rock extraction into commercial timelines. Off-duty he has the steady, straightforward presence of someone who prefers work with clear competence metrics, and his seven-year working relationship with factors agent Brenne Kalt is characterized by mutual respect for what each person actually does well.

## Work

He works regularly with the ore factors who operate between the extraction operations and the southern markets. [[Brenne Kalt]] handles his group's primary long-term contracts. The working relationship is seven years old and productive in the way of professional relationships where both parties have learned what the other person is actually good at and adjusted their expectations accordingly. Saven is good at knowing what the shafts can do on any given timeline. Brenne is good at turning that information into agreements that hold. Neither of them pretends to be good at the other's part.

The extraction work is physically demanding and has a straightforward relationship to competence that Saven finds preferable to the alternatives he has observed. A supervisor who overestimates the shaft capacity commits the group to contracts that cannot be fulfilled. A supervisor who underestimates it leaves production and income on the surface. Both mistakes are immediately visible. He has made both, early in his tenure. He made them once each.

His T3 casting is used for structural assessment — reading stress patterns in the rock to determine shaft stability and identify where reinforcement is needed. Standard extraction application. He is good at it without finding it especially interesting. The interesting part is the logistics. The casting is a tool.
