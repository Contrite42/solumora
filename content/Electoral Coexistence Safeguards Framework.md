# Electoral Coexistence Safeguards Framework

The **Electoral Coexistence Safeguards Framework** represents the first deliberate attempt to prevent [[Public Status of High Demons|coexistence governance]] from becoming a campaign wedge issue during routine political transitions. Established four years after the [[The Three Near-Wars#The Kellvost Incursion|Rift Catastrophe Revelation]], the framework emerged not from crisis but from anticipation — both kingdoms recognized that electoral pressure could unravel institutional durability faster than emergency conditions.

## Institutional Context

The framework was developed jointly by [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] electoral administrators, oversight commissioners, and magistrates with historical memory of [[The Three Near-Wars#Historical Foundations: The Persecution Era (~400-200 Years Ago)|persecution-era]] mobilization patterns. Primary development occurred in Ashford coordination sessions during Year 4 after revelation, with final ratification completed before first major electoral campaigns began.

Unlike crisis-reactive frameworks established in the immediate post-revelation period, the Electoral Safeguards were designed explicitly for *ordinary governance rhythms* — the assumption that coexistence would need to survive not just emergency conditions but routine political competition, partisan advantage-seeking, and leadership turnover.

## Core Safeguard Mechanisms

### Campaign Speech Constraints

The framework establishes narrow procedural limits on campaign messaging regarding coexistence status:

- **Protected Status Language:** Candidates may not campaign on platforms advocating for removal of High Demon protections or return to prosecution-based enforcement. This is treated as advocacy for systematic violence, legally equivalent to campaign platforms calling for ethnic persecution.
- **Policy Critique Permitted:** Candidates *may* criticize specific operational implementations (Protected Observation zone boundaries, enforcement resource allocation, commission oversight procedures) without triggering safeguard violations.
- **Evidence Standards:** Campaign claims regarding coexistence "failures" must meet documented evidence standards established by [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal]] precedent. Fabricated incidents or conspiracy theories regarding High Demon threats face electoral censure.

The constraints apply to formal campaign communications, public rallies, and guild-distributed materials. Private beliefs and informal speech remain protected — the framework targets institutional mobilization rather than individual opinion.

### Pre-Election Review Periods

Starting ninety days before any major election, the [[Ashford Coexistence Oversight Commission]] enters elevated review posture:

- **Campaign Material Review:** Electoral administrators submit proposed campaign materials involving coexistence references for advance review. Materials violating safeguard provisions receive formal notice; repeated violations trigger candidate disqualification procedures.
- **Public Incident Acceleration:** Coexistence enforcement incidents during the review period receive faster resolution timelines to prevent campaign weaponization of unresolved cases.
- **Cross-Kingdom Coordination:** [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] oversight bodies meet weekly during review periods to synchronize messaging and prevent one kingdom's election from destabilizing the other's coexistence institutions.

The review mechanisms were deliberately designed with procedural friction — disqualification requires three documented violations plus magistrate review, preventing partisan weaponization while maintaining enforcement credibility.

### Post-Election Transition Safeguards

The framework establishes specific protocols for peaceful power transfer when anti-coexistence factions gain electoral victory:

- **Policy Implementation Delays:** New leadership cannot modify coexistence frameworks for minimum ninety days after assuming office. This allows opposition mobilization and prevents sudden policy reversals during transition confusion.
- **Treaty Continuity Clauses:** International coexistence commitments ([[Corridor Stabilization Treaty]] provisions, [[Joint Rift Monitoring Cell]] operations) remain binding regardless of electoral outcomes. New leadership inherits obligations rather than choosing whether to honor them.
- **Commission Independence:** Oversight commissioners serve fixed terms that do not align with electoral cycles. New leadership cannot immediately replace commissioners, preserving institutional memory across transitions.

## Electoral Testing Events

The framework survived its first major test during Terravelle's Year 4 legislative elections, when northern district candidates campaigned on "Protected Zone Overreach" platforms arguing that rural communities faced excessive enforcement burdens. The campaign remained within safeguard boundaries — criticizing implementation rather than paradigm — and won several seats.

The new legislators attempted to restrict Protected Observation zone boundaries shortly after taking office. The ninety-day implementation delay allowed counter-mobilization from civil society groups and cross-border treaty partners. By the time the delay expired, political support for boundary changes had collapsed, and the framework remained intact.

Auralis's Year 5 Council elections presented higher-risk testing: candidates affiliated with pre-revelation [[Expansion Faction]] remnants won council majority in three southern districts. Post-election transition procedures prevented immediate policy changes, and the commission independence provisions ensured oversight continuity. The new council members discovered that coexistence governance had become operationally embedded in trade, labor, and civic infrastructure — reversing frameworks would require dismantling functional systems that constituents relied on daily.

## Practical Operation Patterns

Electoral administrators report that campaign speech constraints function primarily through pre-clearance cultural norms rather than enforcement. Candidates understand that materials flagged during review become opposition research material; most campaigns self-censor to avoid the political cost of safeguard violations.

The framework creates political space for *competence competition* around coexistence governance — candidates compete on who can manage oversight more effectively, reduce enforcement errors faster, or improve cross-kingdom coordination — without relitigating whether coexistence should exist.

## Documented Limitations

The framework cannot prevent:

- **Cultural Backlash:** Electoral safeguards constrain campaign messaging but do not address underlying community resentment or fear. Populations can elect candidates who comply with safeguards while privately opposing coexistence entirely.
- **Gradual Erosion:** The ninety-day implementation delay prevents sudden reversals but cannot stop sustained multi-term efforts to weaken frameworks through accumulated marginal changes.
- **Cross-Border Desynchronization:** If one kingdom reliably elects pro-coexistence leadership while the other reliably elects skeptical leadership, the framework preserves operational continuity but does not resolve the underlying political asymmetry.

Oversight commissioners emphasize that electoral safeguards buy time and maintain procedural legitimacy but cannot substitute for ongoing social acceptance. Frameworks survive elections because enough constituencies benefit from coexistence's continuity — not because legal constraints prevent change.

## Integration with Historical Precedent

The framework's design explicitly references [[The Three Near-Wars#Historical Foundations: The Persecution Era (~400-200 Years Ago)|persecution-era]] patterns where public panic was systematically mobilized through institutional messaging. Campaign speech constraints exist because persecution history demonstrates that organized violence requires official sanction and coordination — limiting institutional mobilization capacity reduces recurrence risk even when popular sentiment remains hostile.

The [[Succession Safeguards Charter]] and [[Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum]] provide complementary durability layers: succession protocols ensure competent leadership continuity, curriculum provisions ensure new generations understand why coexistence matters, and electoral safeguards ensure routine political competition doesn't undo institutional commitments.
