_(Knowledge Preservation, Mobile Archives)_

**The Failsafe Vault Migrations** describe the late-era movement of high-value records, instruments, and encoded legal memory from exposed archive complexes to distributed fallback vaults during the final crisis of [[The Ancient Civilization]]. The pattern matters because it shows preservation strategy depended on mobility and redundancy, not fortress defense.

## Migration Doctrine

Recovered indexing marks suggest four rules:

- Split critical records into independently survivable bundles
- Route bundles along non-overlapping corridors
- Separate decoding keys from primary records
- Limit full-context access to trained custodian pairs

The design prevented single-point loss and reduced capture value if any route failed.

## Evidence in Ruins

Material signatures around several secondary sites indicate staged transfer rather than ordinary storage:

- Temporary packing floors and transport abrasion patterns
- Short-duration occupancy traces near vault chambers
- Partial inventories with deliberate omission markers

These align with emergency extraction, not long-term settlement use.

## Link to Present Arc

This framework parallels modern evidence management behavior by actors like [[Osten Vess]] and [[Merra Teld]]: multiple copies, separated chains, and controlled disclosure. The ancient pattern implies that in legitimacy crises, truth survives through distribution more than through institutional trust.
