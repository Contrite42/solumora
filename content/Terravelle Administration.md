[[Terravelle]] has a king, but the kingdom is not run by the king. This is not a constitutional arrangement or a formal delegation of authority — it is the practical result of a political culture that evolved over centuries in the absence of strong central institutions and has never required them.

## The Crown

[[Eddan Voss]] rules from [[Wolfpoint]], which is a long way from anywhere. The position carries genuine authority in three domains: external affairs and treaty negotiation, final appellate jurisdiction in disputes that cannot be resolved at lower levels, and military command in the event of a declared emergency. Outside those domains, day-to-day governance of the kingdom proceeds without systematic royal involvement, and has for as long as anyone can document.

This is not regarded as a weakness in Terravellian political culture. The Crown's remove from ordinary governance is understood as appropriate — the king manages what cannot be managed locally, and leaves local management to local institutions. The arrangement functions because local institutions exist to fill the space.

## The Guild Network

The [[Trade Guilds]] are the primary administrative layer of Terravelle's settled belt. Every city of significant size has a Guild Council — the representatives of the major trade guilds operating in that city — that handles the functions that in another political system might be handled by a city government: licensing, standards enforcement, labor dispute resolution, infrastructure maintenance allocation, and inter-city trade negotiation.

Guild Council authority derives from charter rather than royal appointment. The Crown issued founding charters to the major guilds in the early Founding Period and has issued revisions since, but the charters confer authority that the guilds exercise independently. The Crown can revoke a charter, but has done so twice in the past century and both times created more problems than it solved.

What this produces in practice: a kingdom where the governing institutions are horizontally organized around economic function rather than vertically organized around territorial hierarchy. [[Valdenmoor]]'s Guild Council does not report to the Crown; it reports to the guilds whose members elected it. Disputes between city Councils are resolved through inter-guild arbitration, not royal authority, unless they escalate to something the arbitration mechanism cannot handle — which is rare, because the guilds have strong incentives to resolve disputes before they require outside intervention.

High-tier practitioners in Terravelle tend to hold positions in the guild structure rather than in any royal apparatus. A T4 or T5 specialist does not rise toward the king; they rise toward guild leadership, where their Flux competence is a professional credential rather than a mark of personal elevation.

## City Administration

The major cities — [[Valdenmoor]], [[Ashford]], [[Greyveil]] — each operate under a hybrid system. The Guild Council handles trade and economic regulation. A separately elected City Council handles civic matters: roads, water, public buildings, the registration systems that track population and Flux practitioner certifications. The two bodies coordinate, conflict occasionally, and share jurisdiction in ways that produce arguments that are never fully resolved and never need to be.

Smaller cities and towns do not typically maintain this separation. In most of Terravelle's mid-sized settlements, a single council handles both economic and civic functions, with guild representatives holding the dominant positions. Villages below a certain population threshold elect a headman by community consensus and manage their own affairs with almost no outside involvement.

## The Covenant's Role

The [[Covenant of Measure]] has no formal governmental authority. Covenant practitioners are not officials; the Covenant is not a state institution. What it functions as, in practice, is a community-level mediation layer for disputes that do not fit cleanly into guild jurisdiction — interpersonal conflict, inheritance disputes, questions of community obligation that predate or fall outside the guild framework.

In rural areas, the Covenant practitioner who serves a community may be the only person with consistent connection to any institutional structure beyond the immediate community itself. This gives the Covenant significant informal authority in rural Terravelle that it does not have in the cities, where multiple institutions compete for the same space.

## What This Produces

Terravelle's administrative structure is resilient and slow. It is resilient because no single institution's failure collapses the whole — the guild system, city councils, and Covenant function in parallel rather than hierarchy, and the kingdom continues operating when any one of them stops. It is slow because significant change requires the guilds to agree, which is a deliberate process involving organizations that have different interests and no central authority to override them.

This is in direct contrast with [[Auralis]], where [[The Council of Auralis|the Council]] can make rapid decisions that apply across the southern kingdom's administrative structure. Terravelle's diffused governance means it cannot move quickly, but it also means that no single faction can redirect the kingdom's institutions with a council vote. This has frustrated Auralis strategists for as long as both kingdoms have been paying attention to each other.
