A personal working grimoire is a practitioner's own annotated reference text — distinct from a commercially published copy in that it reflects the specific practitioner's experience with the spells it contains, their modifications to standard variables, their notes on failure modes, and their adjustments for their own casting characteristics. It is not produced for sale and would not be particularly useful to most buyers if it were.

## What It Contains

The commercial copy of a spell contains the sigil and its variable table. Nothing more is required to activate the spell for a practitioner who meets the tier requirement. A personal working grimoire contains that, and also:

- Margin notes on which variable combinations the practitioner has tested and what they produced
- Watt cost observations from the practitioner's own activation attempts, which may differ from published cost estimates because individual Flux efficiency varies
- Notes on activation conditions that affect performance — surface quality, ambient Flux density, time-of-day effects that the practitioner has noticed
- Modified sigil sketches for variant configurations that the practitioner is developing or has verified
- Failure records: what happened when the spell misfired, what the practitioner believes caused it, what they changed

Over years of use, a working grimoire becomes a practitioner-specific map of the spell space. [[Ostal Mrev]]'s consultation notes represent this kind of accumulated expertise — the sigil tables are public knowledge, but the field observations and variant configurations are his alone.

## Commercial vs. Personal Value

A working grimoire's commercial value is low relative to its personal value for the practitioner. The annotations are written for the person who made them, in shorthand that makes sense in context and often nowhere else. A Scribe trying to extract publishable spell variants from a working grimoire needs someone who can interpret the practitioner's notation, which in practice means the practitioner themselves or someone who worked closely with them.

The exceptions are working grimoires that contain documented original spells — variant sigils that the practitioner developed and did not separately register. These have publishing value, and when practitioners die with undocumented originals in their personal notes, institutions tend to move quickly on the estate.

## The Development Record

Working grimoires are also where new spell development is visible as a process rather than a finished product. [[Ossal Meln]]'s workshop notes show iterative development — failed attempts, cost calculation errors, revised configurations — that commercial publications do not include. The publication presents the finished sigil; the working grimoire shows how the practitioner got there.

This development record is undervalued commercially and overvalued academically. [[The Advancement Corps]] has standing interest in acquiring working grimoires from practitioners whose field work involved ruins-site observations or anomalous Flux behavior, because the raw notes sometimes contain observations that the practitioner did not recognize as significant and that the official reports did not capture.

## Relationship to Published Copies

Most practitioners maintain both: a personal working grimoire for active development and reference, and commercial copies of spells they use regularly. The commercial copies are the ones they activate in the field — cleaner, verified, produced by someone else. The personal grimoire is the one they write in.

[[Vel Sorvn]]'s practice notes represent the working grimoire of a T3 practitioner who is actively testing hand sign variants against equivalent sigil configurations — the kind of personal research that no commercial publisher would produce and that the practitioner is doing primarily because they find it interesting.
