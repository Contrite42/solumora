_(Council Member, Auralis — Expansion Faction)_

Castor Helme is the loudest member of the Expansion Faction on [[The Council of Auralis]] and the most visible obstacle between [[Cassia]] and anything resembling actual authority. He is not the most powerful person on the council. He is the most present, which in a body of twenty has proven to be nearly as effective.

He introduced himself to [[Cassia]] twice at her own coronation banquet. He has never acknowledged this. Most of what he does to her, he never acknowledges.

## Character

Castor is theatrical in the specific way of people who have discovered that performance works and have never been given sufficient reason to stop. He is large in every sense — loud voice, broad gestures, opinions on everything delivered with the confidence of someone who has never been seriously wrong in their own estimation. He is charming in the way that makes people laugh and then feel obscurely that they should not have.

He is also, underneath the performance, genuinely competent. This is what makes him dangerous rather than merely irritating. The theatrics are not a cover for incompetence — they are a cover for the quieter work he does when nobody is watching him because they are too busy watching the performance.

His [[Flux]] ability sits at T2, which he does not advertise. What he uses it for, in the small and careful ways that T2 allows, is information. Charged [[Sigils]] on correspondence he handles. Faint detection work at the margins of social gatherings. Subtle application of pressure where pressure is needed. Nothing that would be considered a violation if examined directly — everything calibrated to stay just inside the line. Every use is instrumental. Every decision serves the same goal.

## The Obsession

Castor Helme is fifty-seven years old, and he is terrified of dying.

This is the fact that structures every decision he makes, every faction position, every piece of leverage he accumulates. He has spent nineteen years on the council, and every year he has felt the physical reality of aging — the slowness returning to his body, the recovery time that grows longer, the knowledge that the probability of his death increases with each passing season.

The [[Ancient Ruins]] in the [[Equatorial Desert]] held something. [[Drest]] and the Culmination Faction call it the source of knowledge about [[Flux]] mastery. Castor knows better. Somewhere in those ruins is proof that the ancient civilization discovered — or built — a mechanism for extending human life. The [[Watts]] system itself is only one piece of their technology. There are others.

He pursues Expansion not out of ideological conviction or desire for Auralis's territorial aggrandizement. He pursues it because control of resources, populations, and knowledge is the pathway to immortality research. Every trade route secured is potential access to rare materials. Every territory added increases the pool of researchers and mages who might unlock the mechanism. Every increment of power is leverage toward the one question that matters: how did the ancients defeat death?

## On the Council

Castor leads the Expansion Faction's public voice. He argues for territorial consolidation, trade route control, and the kind of strategic pressure on [[Terravelle]] that keeps the border situation quietly uncomfortable without tipping into open conflict — framed always as strategic necessity, never revealing the true aim.

Behind the council facade, he directs research. He has influence over who gets assigned to [[Emberfall]]. He receives regular reports from its director on progress toward the immortality sigil — the key that will unlock the ancient mechanism or create it anew. He monitors every discovery that comes from desert expeditions. When Sera Voss discovered [[Watts]] as ancient technology, Castor received word before most council members. His first action was not to analyze its political implications but to determine whether the secret might contain information about life extension.

He and [[Drest]] have a functional alliance that goes deeper than either truly admits. Drest seeks the ancient civilization's knowledge as ascension — understanding reality at its deepest level. Castor wants that knowledge as survival. They both want [[Emberfall]] funded, both want desert expeditions supported, both want the best researchers directed toward ancient secrets. The motivation differs. The agenda aligns.

Drest considers Castor useful and predictable. Castor considers Drest useful and focused on the wrong goal. Between them they have ensured that the council's resources flow toward immortality research under the guise of advancement.

## Relationship with Cassia

Castor is not cruel to [[Cassia]]. He is incapable of thinking of her as a person to be cruel to. She is infrastructure. She is legitimacy. She is the signature on the documents that direct research, the face that justifies funding, the authority through which he operates.

He treats her the way you treat a piece of furniture that has been in the room so long you have stopped seeing it — present, functional in a narrow sense, not something you consider when making decisions.

She is nineteen years younger than him. Sometimes this fact troubles him slightly, in a way he does not examine closely: she will likely outlive him, and he will have spent his life ensuring others can live forever while he dies. This is an unacceptable outcome. He works toward the sigil with the particular intensity of a man aware of his own finitude and running out of time.

He has been surprised, on three occasions, to discover that she was paying more attention than he assumed. He has not updated his behavior in response to any of these occasions, which [[Tovan Ashce]] considers the most useful thing about him.

[[Maren Sollis]] finds him insufferable and suspects, without clear evidence, that he is using the queen for purposes she does not fully understand. She has tried twice to warn [[Cassia]]. [[Cassia]] has not understood the warning, which is perhaps the largest tragedy of her kingship — that the one council member advocating for her autonomy lacks the political power to do anything about it.

## What Castor Wants

He wants to live forever. Everything else — the Expansion Faction, the strategic pressure on [[Terravelle]], the council influence, the careful orchestration of research — is the machinery he has built to get there. He is willing to sacrifice [[Cassia]] to that goal. He is willing to sacrifice [[Auralis]] if necessary. He is willing to sacrifice most things except his own continued existence.

This makes him a specific kind of dangerous: not malicious from cruelty, but ruthless from absolute clarity of priority. The immortality sigil is not one goal among many. It is the only goal. Everything else is merely the path to walking along.
