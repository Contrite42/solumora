A **Target Specification** defines exactly what or who a spell acts upon when it fires. Without an explicit Target Spec encoded in the sigil's [[Shape]], the spell defaults to **Where Written** — the sigil itself becomes the point of effect.

Target Spec is one of the six outer variables controlled by [[Shape]], and one of the first dropped when a sigil is compressed to a Triangle — meaning most mass-produced [[Grimoires]] rely heavily on the default.

**Where Written** _(default)_
The spell activates from and acts upon the surface or object the sigil is inscribed on. The most common target type in everyday and commercial spellwork — the sigil _is_ the delivery mechanism.

## Self

The spell targets the caster directly. Reliable and low-drift since the caster is always the closest known anchor point for [[Flux]].
![[Target__SELF 1.png]]

## Individual

The spell locks onto a _specific_ person. Requires the caster to have a clear, deliberate focus on that individual at the moment of activation. Vague intent produces vague results or misfire at higher [[Control Tier|Tiers]].
![[Target__INDIVIDUAL 1.png]]

## Object

The spell acts upon a specific physical object. Common in utility and crafting applications — hardening tools, heating vessels, sealing containers.
![[Target__OBJECT 1.png]]

## Surface

The spell spreads across a flat plane. Used for area treatments, floor or wall effects, and environmental prep work. Pairs naturally with Plane in [[Geometry Pattern]].
![[Target__SURFACE 1.png]]

## Group

The spell targets a collection of individuals simultaneously. Requires higher [[Control Tier]] to hold cleanly — the more targets, the more Flux demand. Poorly controlled Group targets tend to clip unintended bystanders.
![[Target__GROUP 1.png]]

## Filter

Rather than targeting something specific, the spell searches for targets that meet a defined variable — material type, Flux signature, temperature, intent. Used in detection, sorting, and conditional effect work. Pairs naturally with the Filter [[Hook]].
![[Target__FILTER 1.png]]

## Marked

The spell seeks out anyone or anything bearing a corresponding sigil mark. The mark acts as a beacon — range and reliability depend on [[Reach]] and the quality of the mark itself. Commonly used for communication spells, tracking, and keyed access wards.
