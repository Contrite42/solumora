[[Fennick]] moves through [[Auralis]] and northern ports as a social wake operator. He does not build durable institutions; he engineers short-lived emotional weather systems in rooms where decisions are made.

His circuit prioritizes repeatable gathering types instead of fixed venues: merchant dinners, post-auction drinking rooms, guild arbitration celebrations, and council-adjacent patron evenings. He appears under shifting names, often with intentionally inconsistent biography details, because he relies on immediate charisma and biochemical mood steering rather than long-term identity integrity.

Fennick's concealment is timing-based. He enters late, peaks influence quickly, and exits before analytical participants can reconcile his anomalies. Hosts remember the evening as unusually successful or unusually volatile, but memory attribution diffuses across the crowd.

When horns become a visibility risk, he reframes them as theatrical affectation, travel custom, or status eccentricity depending on class context. He prefers explanations people repeat because they sound clever.

His operational hazard is recurrence compression. If the same social cluster encounters him across too many contexts within one season, pattern recognition pressure rises. He counters this by switching between [[Hedun]], [[Solhaven]], and inland waypoints where guest lists only partially overlap.
