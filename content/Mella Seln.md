**Mella Seln** is thirty-six years old and works as a licensed Flux practitioner in a small town in central [[Terravelle]], serving the community's general casting needs. [[Control Tier|T3]]. She trained in Hand Sign casting rather than [[Sigils]], a choice that was practical at the time — the guild-adjacent school available to her when she began training at fourteen specialized in Hand Signs, and she proved competent. She has been using the method for twenty-two years and has not found a reason to retrain.

Hand Signs and Sigils produce equivalent results at equivalent tier levels for most practical applications. The distinction is methodological: Sigils are geometric constructions that act as external scaffolding for the casting, allowing precise documentation and reproducibility; Hand Signs are gestural sequences executed by the practitioner's body, producing the same result through internalized structure rather than external form. Guild certification accommodates both, though the documentation standards differ — a Sigil-based practitioner can photograph their work, while a Hand Sign practitioner's certification requires demonstration in front of assessors who can verify the internal structure is correct.

The practical consequence for Mella's work: she cannot subcontract easily. A Sigil practitioner can prepare a casting template that another practitioner can execute later. A Hand Sign practitioner's work lives in the practitioner. She does all the community's certified work herself, which is the equivalent of a single general practitioner in a medical context — she has seen everything in the normal range and occasionally something outside it.

What she finds: people assume Sigil-users are more precise and Hand Sign-users are more instinctive, and people are wrong about this in exactly the way they would be if they confused the tool for the practitioner. She is precise. The precision lives in her hands rather than in a geometric diagram, and this has not made it less reliable across twenty-two years.
