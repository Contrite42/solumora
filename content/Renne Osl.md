**Renne Osl** is thirty-three years old and works for [[The Advancement Corps]]'s eastern coordination office, in a role titled Senior Field Assessor. The work involves traveling to sites, evaluating what the sites contain, and producing reports that satisfy the documentation requirements of the formal expedition classification system.

She is good at this. She has been good at it since her third year, when the senior officer she was working under told her she had better instincts for the gap between what a site showed and what a report needed to say than anyone he had trained. She took this as the compliment it was intended to be.

Her [[Control Tier|T3]] casting is used for site analysis — structural assessment, Flux density mapping, documentation verification, and the other components of field assessment that require an active caster rather than someone who can read an instrument. She is efficient with it. The assessments she completes in a given quarter are forty percent above the Corps's expected output for her grade.

The sites she has assessed in five years of field work range from catalogued ruins to contested territory to a small number of active anomalies that the formal classification system has no exact category for. She has written accurate reports about all of them. The reports have satisfied review each time.

She does not keep a private journal. She is aware that she does not keep one, in the specific way you are aware of a thing you have decided and then not examined the decision.
