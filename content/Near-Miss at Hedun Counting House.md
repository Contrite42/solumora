In late winter, a ledger clerk at a [[Hedun]] harbor counting house logged the same traveler fee token twice under two slightly different names. The token signature should have matched one person. Instead, the light-read trace looked "split" for less than a second and then normalized.

The clerk elevated it to shift lead review as probable instrument drift. Shift lead review flagged no theft pattern, no cargo discrepancy, and no repeat anomaly from that booth in the next ten days. The incident was closed as a calibration fault.

[[Tolla Rend]] heard about it because the names matched a quiet traveler she had already noticed near dock approach routes. She considered escalating to port watch, then declined. The report lacked actionable detail and she had no rule basis to hold someone on "felt wrong" impressions.

The traveler left the city before dawn two days later. No one could agree on his coat color when asked afterward.

The record remained in Hedun's archive under routine reconciliation notes. No one connected it to [[Sorath]].
