Working notes maintained by [[Ossen Drel]], inter-kingdom trader. Logistics, pricing, contacts, and observations from twenty-one years on the crossing route.

---

**On the differential — standing notes, updated as needed**

A given luxury good in [[Solhaven]] costs between 1.4 and 1.9 times its [[Valdenmoor]] equivalent depending on category and season. The crossing cost needs to fit inside this differential after guide fee, Halveth staging, and the carrying contingency. At the low end of the differential, the crossing only makes sense for high-density cargo. At the high end, the margin is comfortable enough to take on larger volumes if a guide is available with sufficient capacity.

The differential compresses when the [[Equatorial Desert|crossing]] season is active and multiple operators are running the route. It expands when conditions close the crossing for a quarter or longer. I have been running this route long enough to have seen both. The compressed season is the busier season but not the more profitable one.

**On the two kingdoms — differences that affect the work**

[[Terravelle]] prices things in terms of what it costs to make and move. [[Auralis]] prices things in terms of what it costs to have and be seen having. These are different economies and I operate in both of them, which means I carry the same cargo with different pitches.

The guild documentation in Terravelle adds two days to any cargo transaction that involves certification questions. This is the cost of the Terravelle system working as intended. On the Auralis side, certification questions are resolved faster and with more flexibility, which has its own costs, but the costs are not paid in time at the dock.

The people I deal with in each kingdom are different in ways that took me years to identify precisely. The Terravelle contacts want to know that a transaction is square — that the numbers add up, that the documentation is clean, that there will be no complications. The Auralis contacts want to know that they are getting something the other party does not have access to. These are the same want expressed differently, and once I understood that I stopped trying to give the Auralis contacts clean numbers and started giving them the information that the next buyer along the chain would pay for.

**On [[Avel Coss]]**

I have crossed with Coss twice, on the fourth and sixth crossings. I have also crossed with two other guides.

The two other guides were competent. The crossing was harder with each of them than it was with Coss, in ways that are difficult to attribute to a single factor. The party moved differently. The decision-making in uncertain conditions was slower and more visible. One of them explained his reasoning at a point when I would rather he had just made the decision.

I have crossed with Coss on the seventh crossing as well, which was the most recent. The pattern holds. I do not know exactly what makes the difference and I have been watching carefully for three crossings.

---

**On [[Halveth]]**

Halveth is a staging point and not a place I have thought much about as a place. Over fourteen years of passing through, this has begun to feel like an error of some kind, but I have not corrected it.

The permanent residents here know how to exist in the desert's margin in a way I do not. I bring expertise about moving goods between the kingdoms. They have expertise about the territory in which that movement is possible. These are complementary and not overlapping, and the arrangement works.

The person who runs things in Halveth — Wren — I have dealt with on logistical questions twice. Both times the outcomes were practical and efficient. Beyond this I have no useful observations.
