# Desert Signal Network

The Desert Signal Network is an unofficial communication system operating across the [[Equatorial Desert|Zakros]] frontier. It consists of signal towers, fire relays, and safe houses operated by a loose coalition of guides, expeditions, and settlement residents who need to communicate rapidly when official courier routes are impractical.

The network is not licensed. It is tolerated because both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] have discovered that rapid communication about Rift Incursions, expedition emergencies, and security threats benefits both sides equally. An expedition that activates a signal at one of the desert edge relay stations alerts [[Halveth]] watch posts within hours. A Rift Incursion that threatens a settlement boundary is communicated to both kingdom authorities through parallel signal chains, creating the impression of dual concern even when official positions diverge.

## Operation

The core system runs on line-of-sight signal fires, maintained by 40-50 volunteer operators distributed along the desert rim. Messages are encoded in fire patterns, color, duration, and timing. Complex communications are broken into brief segments repeated until acknowledged or until the network decides the message has had sufficient time to propagate.

Effectiveness is weather-dependent. Clear nights allow messages to travel 15-20 relay stations across the frontier. Storm seasons reduce range dramatically. Winter disruptions are significant enough that institutions switch to courier routes or delayed communication when predictable timing matters.

## Unofficial Status

The network operates in a deliberately ambiguous legal space. Both kingdoms publicly maintain that they do not endorse the signal system. Both kingdoms maintain listening posts where trained operators monitor signal traffic. Neither kingdom officially responds to signals, but both kingdoms' frontier garrisons are manned by people who live within sight of the signal towers and who understand that a timely response to an incoming signal protects their own settlements.

The safety houses that provide rest and resupply for signal operators, guides, and travelers caught in emergency situations are scattered across the frontier. They are not officially part of any government institution. They are staffed by people who maintain dual citizenship or no official citizenship, creating a community that answers to the desert itself as much as to any kingdom authority.
