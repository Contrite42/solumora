**Wolfpoint Harbor** is the settlement's only maritime connection to the southern kingdoms — a wind-exposed stone pier complex where the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] delivers imports and collects exports that sustain Wolfpoint's independence. The harbor operates year-round despite the constant 45-degree cold and the wind that comes off open water with nothing to slow it before it reaches the docks.

## Layout

The harbor consists of three stone piers extending into deep water, built low and massive to survive winter storm surge. Each pier has reinforced mooring points and covered loading zones where cargo can be staged out of wind and spray. The piers were built by the Voss founders using Force discipline [[Sigils]] that have been continuously maintained for seven generations — the same family that developed [[Hypertext]] applied similar precision to creating infrastructure that would not fail.

Behind the piers, a stone warehouse complex provides bonded storage for both incoming supplies and outgoing exports. The warehouses are built into the hillside for wind protection and thermal stability — the constant cold means [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] metalwork and [[Mountain Game Collective]] preserved meat can be stored without additional refrigeration [[Grimoires|grimoires]]. Salt from [[Ashford Supply Contract]] deliveries is kept in sealed chambers with moisture-control sigils that prevent clumping in the sea air.

The harbormaster's office sits at the pier junction, a low stone building with shuttered windows and a heating grimoire that runs continuously through winter months. This is where [[Bren Hask]] coordinates ship schedules, cargo manifests, and the logistical reality that all of Wolfpoint's survival imports arrive through a harbor that can only handle two ships at once.

## Operations

The [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] runs scheduled deliveries every 6-8 weeks during summer months, extending to 10-12 weeks in winter when storms make coastal navigation dangerous. Ships arrive with grain, salt, replacement tools, specialty goods, and occasional passengers — mostly practitioners coming to study at [[The Wolfpoint Scholarium]]. They depart with cold-forge metalwork, preserved meat, wool textiles, and the knowledge exports that are Wolfpoint's actual strategic currency.

Loading and unloading is done by a permanent crew of eight dock workers, all T0-T1 [[Flux Users]], supplemented by rotating labor from the settlement when large shipments arrive. The work is physically demanding and paid better than most Wolfpoint occupations — [[Bren Hask]] maintains premium wages as an insurance policy against the temptation to skim valuable cargo or sell information about shipment contents to southern merchants.

The harbor operates on a just-in-time arrival rhythm that leaves minimal margin for error. [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] maintains 6-8 months of grain reserves specifically because a single missed delivery or storm-delayed ship can threaten the settlement's food security. When ships are overdue, the entire settlement feels it — ration planning begins, preservation operations slow to conserve salt, and [[Renn Taska]] starts sending inquiry messages south through whatever merchant contacts have working coastal signal networks.

## Security

The harbor is the point where Wolfpoint is most vulnerable. A maritime blockade by either [[Terravelle]] or [[Auralis]] would collapse the settlement's economy within months. A coordinated raid could seize high-value exports or disrupt the supply chain long enough to force Wolfpoint into formal alignment with one kingdom or the other.

This vulnerability is why [[Bren Hask]] runs the harbor with the intensity of someone managing a military installation rather than a commercial port. Cargo manifests are verified against [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] records before ships are unloaded. Outbound shipments are sealed and witnessed to prevent mid-transit substitution of genuine cold-forge work for inferior copies. Loading schedules are coordinated so no ship carries export cargo from only one economic operation — every departure includes mixed goods that make theft less valuable and ensure multiple Wolfpoint operations track whether shipments reach destination.

The harbor also handles occasional inspection requests from kingdom envoys. [[Keln Varost]] arrives every few months from [[Greyveil]] to verify Wolfpoint's [[Terravelle]] trade relationships remain balanced. [[Auralis]] sends less formal observers — traders who ask careful questions, practitioners who spend time at the Scholarium and report back through channels Wolfpoint knows exist but does not formally acknowledge. [[Bren Hask]] cooperates with these inspections to the extent required to maintain trade access while revealing nothing about actual [[Hypertext]] development or internal Wolfpoint strategy.

## People

[[Bren Hask]] has run the harbor for nine years. He is T2, 38 years old, and came to Wolfpoint from [[Ashford]] as a ship's mate before deciding the settlement's climate and isolation suited him better than coastal city life. He knows southern merchant houses, coastal shipping schedules, and the specific ways traders try to extract information or favorable contract terms from isolated northern settlements. His operational posture is conservative: maintain schedule predictability, keep cargo secure, report anything unusual to [[Renn Taska]] immediately.

The dock crew includes [[Vess Nal]], T1, who has worked the harbor for fifteen years and knows the loading tolerances of every ship in the Packet Line rotation. [[Vess Nal|Vess]] can estimate cargo weight from visual inspection and knows which captains will accept late additions to manifest and which will refuse anything not pre-arranged. This institutional knowledge is what keeps loading operations efficient despite Wolfpoint's limited dock capacity.

When ships arrive, the settlement shifts energy toward the harbor. Merchants come to verify their export shipments departed, [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] staff cross-check incoming supplies against standing contracts, and [[The Wolfpoint Scholarium]] checks for any books, documents, or materials ordered through southern academic networks. The harbor becomes, for a day or two, the center of Wolfpoint's attention — the moment when the settlement's isolation becomes real connection to the wider world, and everyone remembers they survive because ships keep coming.
