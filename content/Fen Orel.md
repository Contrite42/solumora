_(Formulist, Valdenmoor)_

**Fen Orel** does cargo preservation work in [[Valdenmoor]] — sealing sigils for river transport, cold-packing for perishables moving upriver, the occasional contract job for a merchant house that needs someone reliable and discreet. He is twenty-six, T3, and has hit his [[Control Tier|ceiling]] firmly enough to have stopped arguing with it. He has been one of [[Cael|Cael's]] close friends for several years, and did not change how he treated Cael when the succession shifted.

## Character

Fen is practical and blunt in the way of someone who works with his hands and thinks with his hands, which is to say he takes things apart and looks at what's inside them, whether the thing is a cargo seal that isn't holding or an argument that isn't working. He is not academic. He has no formal Scholar training and does not particularly want it. What he has is the accumulation of ten years of practical [[Flux]] work that has built a set of intuitions about how Flux actually behaves under field conditions rather than how it's supposed to behave according to the system.

This is where he and [[Cael]] found each other. Cael attends the gatherings where theory comes up naturally. Fen attends them because he likes the company and because arguments about how [[Sigils]] work are more interesting than most of the available conversation in the cargo district after a long day. They met when Cael was still the spare, at a small gathering in the warehouse district where a retired Channeler was holding court about cost theory, and Fen was the person who said the thing the theorist was dancing around.

He did not know who Cael was at first. By the time he found out, their dynamic was already established — Cael asks questions, Fen answers honestly, and if the answer is "I don't know" or "I think you're wrong about the specific part" that is the answer — and Fen saw no reason to change it when the succession made the question of who Cael was more complicated.

## On Cael's Theory

Fen's working assessment of [[Cael|Cael's]] theory about channeling cost is that it is probably right about the direction and wrong about several specifics, and that the specifics Cael is wrong about are wrong in ways that will eventually matter if he pursues this seriously. He says this. Cael finds this useful in a way he finds most people's agreement not useful.

What Fen contributes from his own experience: at T3 in the preservation trade, he hits his ceiling on long jobs — sustained cold-seals for large cargo volumes push against his limit over a full work day, and he has spent more time than most people thinking carefully about what that feels like from the inside and what it costs in the hours after. He does not have a theory. He has a body of practical observation about the cost curve that nobody has asked him to formalize, and that [[Cael]] is the only person who has ever treated as worth careful attention.
