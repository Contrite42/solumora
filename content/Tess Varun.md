_(Council Member, Auralis — Culmination Faction)_

**Tess Varun** was appointed to [[The Council of Auralis]] three years ago at twenty-five, making her the youngest member in the council's twenty-three-year history. She is [[Drest|Drest's]] most reliable echo and the most efficient way to know what Drest is going to say before he says it, because she will have already said it, slightly louder and with slightly less qualification.

## Character

Tess is not Drest's puppet. This distinction matters to her and is partially true. She has her own positions, she has studied the Culmination Faction's history and goals with genuine diligence, and she arrived at her convictions before she arrived at the council seat. The problem — and she would not call it a problem — is that her convictions are substantially Drest's convictions, developed through two years of attending his informal gatherings as an observer before the appointment, consuming his intellectual framework the way someone who arrives at a tradition young consumes a tradition.

She is twenty-eight. She has the energy that comes from still believing that saying the right things clearly enough will produce results. The older council members — [[Corda Lenn]] in particular — watch this with the particular attention of people who remember what it looked like on someone else twenty years ago.

Her [[Flux]] ability is T5, Force discipline, which makes her one of the council's more capable active casters in absolute terms. In the context of a political body she uses it sparingly, on [[Drest|Drest's]] implicit advice, having been told early that display was not how the council worked. She has followed this advice even as she has quietly noted that [[Ilse Dorn]] does not need to display anything either.

## The Bellwether Function

[[Tovan Ashce]], [[Pol Ashvane]], and at least two Expansion faction members have independently developed the habit of reading Tess to know which way Drest is leaning before the formal vote. This is not a deception on Tess's part — she advocates sincerely and is not performing a telegraph function she is aware of. It is simply that her timing in taking positions reliably follows Drest's conversations with her by several days, which, for people who notice patterns, is information.

She does not yet know she is being read this way. When she finds out — and she will find out; she is not unobservant — it will produce an interesting recalibration.
