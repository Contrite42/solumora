**Valdenmoor Food Economy and Agricultural Pressure** documents how [[Valdenmoor]] — [[Terravelle]]'s capital and [[Maren Valley]]'s market center — integrates agricultural production into urban food security. Unlike [[Solhaven]] (entirely import-dependent), [[Valdenmoor]] sits amid grain production, creating both advantages (direct supply access) and vulnerability (being center of requisition pressure when war mobilization diverts agricultural output).

## Food Supply Structure

[[Valdenmoor]] (population ~25,000-35,000) derives food from proximate [[Maren Valley]] agriculture (primary), inter-city trade, and local food production (small-scale urban agriculture, hunting).

### Primary Supply: [[Maren Valley]] Grain (Immediate Region)

The fundamental advantage: [[Valdenmoor]] sits at center of grain production region. Farmers within 5-10 day wagon distance supply directly. Harvest flows immediately to city upon completion (no multi-week transport delay like supplies reaching [[Solhaven]]).

**Supply characteristics:** Post-harvest (Harvestfirst through Coolward), daily grain arrival 600-1000 pounds as threshing completes throughout valley. Farmers sell directly to [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] depots and merchant houses. Retail flow relatively steady (unlike [[Solhaven]]'s dependent supply arriving in batches).

**Seasonal variation:** Abundant Harvestfirst-Coolward post-harvest flow; declining through winter as stored grain shipped out for inter-regional trade and institutional supply. Spring supply bottleneck (stored grain depleted, waiting for new growth).

**Price advantage:** Direct farmer supply reduces merchant markup (no multi-week transport, wholesale aggregation at market source). Grain retail prices in [[Valdenmoor]] typically 10-20% lower than equivalent quality in [[Solhaven]] (transport and middleman costs eliminated).

### Secondary Supply: Inter-Regional Trade

Grain exported from [[Maren Valley]] to [[Auralis]] (via [[Ashford]] export route) and to [[Wolfpoint]] (via [[Maren Freight Chain]] north). Trade surplus normally re-imports specialty goods (salt from [[Auralis]], game from [[Wolfpoint]], spices from coastal). Net food: small (exported value ~equal to imported specialty value).

### Tertiary Supply: Local Food Production

[[Valdenmoor]] maintains some local agriculture: suburban vegetable gardens (supporting ~10-15% of fresh vegetable needs seasonally), livestock (chickens, goats) in outlying areas. Hunting in nearby forests provides game (supplementary protein, 5-10% of meat consumption).

**Advantage:** Reduces absolute import dependence. Seasonal vegetable buffer allows population to consume fresh produce rather than entirely preserved diet.

**Limitation:** Contributes only 10-20% of food calories; insufficient for self-sufficiency.

## Agricultural Pressure and Requisition

[[Valdenmoor]]'s location creates acute wartime vulnerability: it is military logistical hub, and surrounding agricultural productivity is military asset.

### Military Requisition Pressure

War mobilization (623 FE) creates Crown requisition quotas: farmers required to provide 15-20% of harvest to military (beyond normal tax). Direct effect: grain normally flowing to [[Valdenmoor]] civilian market diverted to military supply.

**Documented impact:** Harvestfirst-Coolward 623 FE requisition orders reduced civilian market receipts 20-30%. River barge transport capacity commandeered for military grain shipment (supporting barracks, camps, supply depots). Transport bottleneck: civilian merchants cannot ship grain (no barge availability); overland wagon transport more expensive and slower.

**Population consequence:** Reduced grain availability and price increase 30-50%. Working-class purchasing power drops; dietary reduction noticeable within 4 weeks.

### Military Camp Location Effect

Temporary military installations (training camps, supply depots, garrison expansion) positioned near [[Valdenmoor]] create high-volume institutional demand. Example: Harvestfirst 623 FE, Crown established 3000-4000 person training camp adjacent to city (consolidating recruits for western frontier deployment). Camp food requirement: 400-600 pounds daily grain. Sourced directly from regional supply, reducing civilian market availability 15-25%.

**Multiplier effect:** Camp procurement disrupts normal merchant supply chains. Farmers sell to military procurement agents (guaranteed bulk purchases, no haggling) rather than to civilian merchants. Civilian merchant suppliers lose suppliers. Prices spike as civilian merchants compete for remaining available grain (limited supply).

### Harvest Labor Conscription

War conscription claims estimated 15-25% of able-bodied harvest workers. [[Maren Valley]] harvest Harvestfirst-Coolward 623 FE conducted with reduced workforce, resulting in estimated 10-20% harvest volume loss. Labor shortage prevented full harvest of standing grain; some harvest rotted in fields (unharvested due to insufficient labor).

**Combined effect:** Requisition (20-30% diversion to military) plus harvest labor loss (10-20% reduced yield) combines into 28-44% effective reduction in civilian food supply availability.

## Market Structure and Distribution

[[Valdenmoor]] food distribution operates through formal and informal networks.

### [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]] System

Formal government institution managing grain supply, maintaining reserves, distributing to populations via retail markets and relief programs.

**Operations:** Procurement from farmers post-harvest (central depot receives grain deliveries, pays farmers directly), storage in city granaries (~4 weeks of supply maintained as strategic reserve), retail release to merchant licensed retailers (controlled quantities at state-set prices preventing extreme civilian price gouging).

**Wartime adjustment:** Requisition quotas dramatically increase Board's grain acquisition (feeding military), reducing available civilian supply. Board attempts to maintain price ceiling (prevent panic purchasing, limit profiteering), but undersupply eventually breaks price controls.

**Personnel:** Board employs ~30-50 people (administrators, warehouse workers, verification staff, decision makers). Director reports to Crown authority. Wartime expansion adds military procurement officers (military representatives on Board managing military quantity allocation).

### Independent Merchant Retail

Separate merchant houses operate independently, purchasing grain, selling at market prices. Compete with Board system for supply (Board prioritizes military, then civilian relief, then retail market; independent merchants source remaining supply or attempt direct farmer purchases).

**Volume:** Estimated 20-40% of civilian consumption sourced through independent merchants; 40-60% through Board system; 10-20% through relief and informal distribution.

**Vulnerability:** Independent merchants highly sensitive to supply disruption. Requisition pressure reduces available supply they can acquire; prices spike as limited supply commands premium.

## Seasonal Food Pressure Points

[[Valdenmoor]] experiences distinctive seasonal pattern (different from [[Solhaven]]).

### Harvest Season Peak (Harvestfirst-Coolward, 8-10 weeks)

Summer abundance: grain arriving daily, prices lowest of year, food security maximum. Populations consume well, store reserves. Working-class populations experience genuine abundance (rare state) — wheat bread accessible, meat more frequent, fresh vegetables at garden peak.

**Institutional activity:** Harvest season is processing peak — threshing, milling, storage. Food labor intensive work. Working-class harvest labor earns 15-25 Watts daily (peak income season). Labor-dependent households maximize income during this period.

### Winter Supply Decline (Coolward-Colden-Thawmark-Darkmoon, 12-16 weeks)

Stored grain declines through winter as consumed. Spring storage exhaustion creates pre-spring scarcity (4-6 weeks before new crop yields). Prices rise 40-80% above harvest lows. Populations reduce consumption (shift to less desirable preserved foods, grain substitution). Working-class malnutrition emerges late winter (Darkmoon-Colden boundary).

### Spring Scarcity (Thawmark-Firstgreen, 5-6 weeks)

Most acute: stored grain depleted, new crop not yet yielding. Prices peak (80-120% above harvest baseline). Institutions implementing relief. Some populations (vulnerable dependents) face food insecurity.

### Summer Transition (Firstgreen-Brightward, 5-6 weeks)

New crop beginning producing edible yield (late Firstgreen) — provides relief. Transition from stored to fresh grains. Prices declining. Food security returning.

## Agricultural Worker Dynamics

[[Valdenmoor]]'s unique position creates distinctive labor conditions for agricultural workers.

### Harvest Labor Market

Post-harvest threshing employs 5000-7000 temporary seasonal workers for 8-10 weeks (Harvestfirst-Coolward). Wages 15-25 Watts daily (among best wages available to working-class). Income concentrated in single season creates uneven annual distribution: harvest worker earns 800-1200 Watts in 8-10 weeks, must budget 52 weeks.

**Wartime impact:** Conscription claims harvest workers (14-18 year old males particularly targeted). Estimated 15-25% harvest labor loss due to military conscription. Remaining workers have highest demand and increased bargaining power (wages spike to 20-30 Watts as shortage drives laborers scarce). But overall harvest reduction (cannot hire wage increases away) means total harvest income distributed across fewer workers — aggregate income to working-class actually declines.

### Farmer Requisition Pressure

Individual farmers requisitioned 15-20% of harvest by Crown decree. Economically devastating for small farmers (margin operation: just enough to survive). Losing 15-20% of grain for household consumption means loss of survival margin. Farmers leverage informal arrangements (hiding portion of harvest, bribing requisition inspectors, understating actual yield).

**Documented: 618-623 FE, estimated 20-30% of farmers engaged in requisition evasion (hiding harvest from official accounting). Severe penalty (imprisonment, seizure of lands) if caught — yet risk accepted given production impact. Indicates wartime stress exceeds institutional control authority.**

## Class Food Access Differences

[[Valdenmoor]]'s agricultural proximity creates graduation in food access, different from [[Solhaven]].

### Working-Class Food Access

Harvest season: abundant, wages peak, food security high. Post-harvest through spring: constrained. 50-60% of working-class household income dedicated to food. Winter supply decline forces reduction in consumption (move to cheaper grains, preserved fish reduction, meat elimination).

**Farmer-class workers:** Farm-dependent workers (day laborers on farms) have some access to farm production directly (perquisite of employment, sometimes formal part-wages). Harvest season provides temporary access to fresh grain at source prices (below market). Still insufficient for full year; off-season employment required.

### Middle-Class Food Access

Merchant and guild-class populations (~25-30% of population) medium-stable food security. Merchant-house contracts stabilize prices. Access both wholesale (bulk grain purchase) and retail. Storage adequate (merchant warehouse shares, personal cellaring). Harvest season price leverage and off-season storage provide comprehensive security.

### Elite Access

Government, guild leadership, prominent merchants: complete insulation. Food supply non-constraint. Requisition authority or institutional access ensures supply. Food security guaranteed.

## Contemporary Vulnerability (623 FE)

Current wartime context creates compounded pressure more severe than [[Solhaven]]:

- **Military requisition:** 15-20% of harvest diverted (direct grain loss)
- **Harvest labor conscription:** 15-25% of harvest workers recruited, reducing actual harvest 10-20%
- **Military camps:** Additional demand 15-25% of normal civilian consumption
- **Farmer stability:** Requisition-pressured farmers reducing spring plantings (risk calculation: why plant if harvest requisitioned?), potentially reducing next year's production

**Integrated effect:** 40-65% effective reduction in long-term food supply availability through combined mechanism. Severe shortage risk 623-624 FE. Vulnerable populations (working-class, dependents) at moderate-to-severe malnutrition risk.

**Critical difference from [[Solhaven]]:** [[Valdenmoor]] has institutional agricultural connections (Board system, direct farmer relationships). This creates both mitigation capacity (can negotiate with farmers, can implement targeted relief) and vulnerability (requisition authority directly extracting resource).
