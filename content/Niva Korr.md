Niva Korr is a [[Control Tier|T0]] contract verifier in [[Ashford]] specializing in cross-border documentation quality for trade and grimoire-adjacent shipments.

She is 41 years old and works primarily with merchant houses that cannot afford delays caused by malformed paperwork at inspection points.

## Non-Caster Niche

Niva's work sits in a Flux-dependent economy but does not require Flux use:

- reconcile conflicting ledger entries before customs review
- identify forged sequence patterns in copied certifications
- map document chains to actual custody paths
- prepare dispute packets when enforcement actions rely on weak evidence

She cannot authenticate sigils directly. She can detect when the paperwork around a claimed sigil history is incoherent.

## Adaptation Strategy

Her adaptation is evidentiary depth.

Niva built reference libraries of genuine and fraudulent document structures from two decades of casework. She works with trusted T2-T3 auditors only for narrow technical checks and keeps the rest of the analysis in plain-document logic to avoid overreliance on magical validation.

Because she is T0, clients initially underestimate her in high-tier circles. She uses that bias to gather clearer statements early, then locks disputes with precise contradiction tables that are hard to dismiss once filed.

## Relationship to Underground Pressure

With expanded black-market circulation in the Ashford corridor, Niva's workload increased. She does not work for smuggling networks, but her verification methods are now used by legitimate houses trying to avoid becoming accidental fronts for forged-authentication cargo.

She regularly interfaces with compliance teams linked to [[Trade Guilds]] and occasionally with offices tracking patterns associated with [[Midden Exchange]] methods, always from the legal side of document review.

## Why She Matters

Niva shows a third T0 pattern: in systems that prize magical capability, non-caster professionals can dominate where disputes are won by consistency, memory, and proof structure.

When everyone argues from power, she argues from records that hold up in the morning.
