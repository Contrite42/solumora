[[Reach]] defines how a spell connects from its casting origin to its point of effect. It determines whether a spell acts on the caster, the written sigil, something in contact, or something at distance.

When Reach is not specified by the spell’s [[Shape]], it defaults to Self.

## Reach Options (Scholar Standard)

- Self (default)  
   The spell anchors to the caster’s own body/space. Effects originate from, center on, or apply to the caster.
  ![[Reach__SELF 1.png]]
- Anchored  
   The spell is fixed to a location or object as its origin (often Where Written). It does not travel; it waits, radiates, or triggers from its anchor.
  ![[Reach__ANCHORED 1.png]]
- Touch  
   The spell connects through direct contact. The caster (or the sigil) must physically touch the target at the moment of activation.
  ![[Reach__TOUCH 1.png]]
- Short — up to **10 ft**  
   A near-field projection. Reliable, low drift, commonly used for practical work and close combat.
  ![[Reach__SHORT 1.png]]
- Medium — up to **50 ft**  
   A stable working distance. Requires more control than Short and is more sensitive to interruption or obstructed lines.
  ![[Reach__MEDIUM 1.png]]
- Long — up to **200 ft**  
   A high-demand projection. Small errors in sigil, aim, or Flux feed become noticeable at this range.
  ![[Reach__LONG 1.png]]
- Line-of-Sight  
   The spell can reach any point the caster can directly see (within normal visibility). Environmental conditions matter; fog, glare, darkness, and obstructions reduce reliability.
  ![[Reach__LOS 1.png]]
- Linked  
   The spell reaches through an established link—such as a paired mark, token, keyed sigil, or known signature. Linked reach is limited by the quality of the link rather than simple distance.
  ![[Reach__LINKED 1.png]]

## Practical notes

- Reach does not decide what shape the spell takes—that’s [[Geometry Pattern]]. Reach decides _where_ that geometry is allowed to manifest.
- In low-control shapes, many spells behave as Anchored or Self effects by default because those are the safest and most reliable connections.
