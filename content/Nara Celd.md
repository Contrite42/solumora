Nara Celd has worked [[Ashford]]'s eastern docks for thirty-four years. She is fifty-two, undocumented, and has not had a reason to leave the city. She was born two streets back from the waterfront in a boarding house that no longer exists — the building was taken down for warehouse expansion when she was nineteen, and she moved two streets further back, and has been moving further back in small increments ever since, which is how the waterfront works.

She loads and unloads cargo. She has opinions about which dock masters run clean operations and which ones pocket the difference between what arrives and what gets recorded. She does not share these opinions unless asked, and even then only with people she has known long enough to have a working read on. The docks have their own information economy. Nara understands it the way she understands the tides — not something she learned, something she absorbed over thirty-four years of standing in the same place watching the same patterns move through.

Her [[Control Tier|T0]] status closes her off from the preservation contracts that make up a significant portion of the better-paying work. She has managed around this for three decades through a combination of seniority, reliability, and a specific kind of usefulness that does not show up in guild certifications — she knows which cargoes are handled more carefully when a specific foreman is on shift, which of the informal warehouses operates under an arrangement with the harbor master that everyone accommodates, which ships' captains will hire without documentation and which ones will not risk it regardless of how well you work.

She is not particularly interested in the social life of the docks, which is extensive and heavily dependent on drinking. She has two people she would describe as close friends. One of them has been dead for six years. She has not replaced him.

She attends no ceremonies. She observes nothing formally. She has spent her life using exactly what she needs and not reaching for anything she cannot hold.
