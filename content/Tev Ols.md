**Tev Ols** is ten years old and lives in [[Solhaven]]. The [[Tier Assessment|assessment ceremony]] is in six weeks.

Tev has known about the ceremony for two years — Solhaven parents tend to begin the conversation early, because Solhaven understands what tier assessment means and prefers that children arrive prepared rather than surprised. Prepared means: knowing what the examiner will ask you to do, knowing that it does not hurt, knowing that the result is final and will be confirmed by a second assessor if it comes back high. Tev knows all of this. Tev has also spent two years noticing how often the people around them return the conversation to the ceremony in ways that suggest the preparation is partly for the parents.

Their father assessed [[Control Tier|T3]] and works in commercial verification. Their mother assessed T2 and is active in the [[Ascendant Path]]'s local community work. Both of them have been careful to say, in several different conversations, that the result does not matter and that any tier is a good tier. Tev believes this and also understands that it is not the complete truth of what either parent thinks.

The specific things Tev thinks about: a friend two streets over who assesses next year, and what happens to their friendship if the tiers are very different. Whether the examiner will be the same one who assessed Tev's older cousin — the cousin said the examiner was kind and methodical. Whether a T0 result is possible in a family with a T3 and a T2, which Tev has tried to ask about twice and received answers that did not actually address the question.

What Tev does not think about much: what tier they will actually get, or what it will mean for the future. That part feels too large and too far away. The ceremony itself feels close. The six weeks feel manageable, each one on its own.
