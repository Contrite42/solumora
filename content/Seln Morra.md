_(Trauma Response Coordinator, Terravelle)_

**Seln Morra** is a T0 trauma response coordinator for [[Terravelle]]'s distributed medical network, age 47, who has spent twenty-three years managing casualty responses to mining accidents, expedition failures, and natural disasters across the northern kingdom. She's the person who organizes triage when institutions collapse, who routes supplies when systems fail, who keeps people alive through competence and improvisation when the official response isn't fast enough. When the [[Thel Reach]] incursion casualties arrive at [[Greyveil]] field clinic and she sees the injury patterns combined with the institutional response delay, she realizes the war's casualty projections are catastrophically underestimating what's actually going to happen when supply chains fail.

## Background

Seln has no [[Flux]] capability. Zero. In a society where magical power determines social position, she's functionally invisible to anyone operating on prestige assumptions. She's also indispensable to anyone who's ever watched medical systems collapse under surge capacity pressure and realized that Flux technique doesn't matter when you don't have enough bandages, clean water, or experienced hands applying pressure to wounds.

She started in medical work at twenty-four, apprenticed to a T2 physician in [[Ashford]] who taught her triage protocols, wound management, infection prevention, and the brutal arithmetic of resource allocation during mass casualty events. The physician died during a dock accident when Seln was twenty-six; Seln took over coordination of the response, kept fourteen people alive through a critical six-hour window before additional support arrived, and discovered she was better at crisis management than anyone expected.

She's been doing it ever since. Twenty-three years coordinating trauma responses across [[Terravelle]]. She knows every regional clinic, every equipment cache, every physician and field medic who's competent versus who panics under pressure. She knows how to assess casualty volume against available resources and route the overflow before the primary site gets overwhelmed. She knows how to keep supply chains moving when official channels break down.

She's saved hundreds of lives through logistics competence in situations where magical power was useless. The institutions barely notice she exists. The people who've bled out in clinics she's coordinated know exactly who she is.

## The Incursion Response

When [[Thel Reach]] incursion casualties arrive at [[Greyveil]], thirty-one hours after the rift attack, Seln is the first person to see them. She's at [[Greyveil]] coordinating a supply redistribution exercise (routine quarterly positioning of medical caches for seasonal mining accident patterns) when the [[Auralis]] military transport pulls up with seventeen body bags and twenty-six survivors requiring immediate treatment.

The injury patterns are wrong. Not wrong like she's never seen them — wrong like the official casualty projections fundamentally misunderstand what [[Rift Incursions|rift incursions]] do to human bodies. The [[Auralis]] military documentation says "seventeen fatalities, twenty-six injured" like it's a workplace accident. What Seln sees is dimensional trauma, Flux burn penetration that doesn't stop at skin layer, tissue degradation from proximity exposure, and psychological damage from witnessing geometry violations that human nervous systems aren't designed to process.

Eight of the "survivors" won't survive thirty days without specialist intervention that border clinics don't have equipment for. Three are going to need permanent institutional care. The military response planning assumes casualty ratios based on conventional combat. The actual casualties from rift exposure are multiples higher than the documentation acknowledges.

Seln documents everything: injury types, progression patterns, treatment resource requirements, specialist referral needs. She routes the eight critical cases to [[Valdenmoor]] with emergency priority. She coordinates psychiatric support for the trauma exposure cases. She burns through three weeks of regional medical supplies in thirty-six hours keeping people alive.

Then she sits down and does the arithmetic for what happens when rift incursions become a regular occurrence during sustained military operations, and the numbers are horrifying.

## What She Knows

Seln has twenty-three years of crisis response data in her head and in meticulously maintained field documentation:

- She knows [[Terravelle]]'s distributed medical system can handle approximately 200 serious casualties per month before regional supplies deplete faster than replacement shipments arrive
- She knows rift exposure casualties require 3-4 times more resources per patient than conventional trauma
- She knows military casualty projections are based on combat injury ratios that don't account for dimensional exposure effects
- She knows [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] combined probably have specialist capacity for maybe 150 dimensional trauma cases before institutional care collapses
- She knows supply chain disruption from war will degrade medical logistics by 40-60% based on historical trade disruption patterns
- She knows the kingdoms are about to experience casualty volumes that will overwhelm every medical system simultaneously

Most critically, she knows that the people making military decisions have never coordinated a mass casualty response and don't understand the difference between planning capacity and operational reality under surge pressure.

## Motivation

Seln doesn't have political opinions about the war. She has operational opinions about casualty projections that are wrong by an order of magnitude and will get thousands of people killed through institutional incompetence rather than enemy action.

She wants someone in authority to understand what's actually going to happen when medical systems fail across both kingdoms simultaneously. She wants supply chain protection prioritized over offensive operations. She wants rift exposure protocols developed before more people get exposed without proper treatment infrastructure. She wants military planners to consult with people who've actually managed trauma surge responses instead of using theoretical models.

Failing that, she wants to prepare for the collapse she knows is coming. Pre-position supplies in locations that won't be accessible once war logistics dominate transport networks. Train more field medics in rift exposure treatment. Establish communication channels for medical coordination that don't depend on military authorization. Build redundant capacity before it's needed instead of scrambling to improvise when systems are already overwhelmed.

She's one person with no institutional authority. But she knows how to coordinate distributed responses when central authority breaks down. If the institutions won't prepare, maybe she can organize something parallel that survives the collapse.

## Personal Stakes

Seln's son [[Vren Morra]] is a T2 soldier in [[Terravelle]]'s border patrol, age 24, stationed at [[Dren Outpost]]. If the war escalates, border patrol becomes front-line military service, and [[Vren Morra|Vren]] will be experiencing exactly the kind of dimensional trauma exposure Seln just documented at [[Greyveil]].

Her daughter [[Kess Morra]] is a T1 apprentice scrivener in [[Valdenmoor]], age 21. If war disrupts the economy and [[Valdenmoor]]'s trade networks collapse, [[Kess Morra|Kess]]'s apprenticeship income disappears and she's left with no livelihood and no fallback position.

Seln's husband died six years ago in a mining accident — one of the disasters she coordinated response for. She knows what it's like to lose someone to institutional failure that could have been prevented with better safety protocols and faster response. She spent six years making sure other families didn't experience the same loss.

Now she's watching the kingdoms march toward a casualty event that will make every mine disaster she's ever coordinated look minor, and nobody with authority is listening to the people who actually know how to keep trauma victims alive when systems are failing.

## Current Status

Seln has returned to her coordination base in [[Greyveil]], but she's stopped following official protocols. She's been quietly redirecting medical supplies to pre-positioned caches, training volunteer medics in rift exposure treatment protocols she's developed from the [[Thel Reach]] cases, and establishing underground communication networks with physicians and coordinators who recognize that official channels are going to fail.

She's also been documenting everything she knows about actual casualty patterns versus official projections. The documentation is detailed, precise, and demonstrates that military planning is based on assumptions that are catastrophically wrong. She has no way to get it to anyone with authority who would act on it — military medical command isn't interested in hearing from a T0 civilian coordinator that their projections are flawed.

She's waiting for someone to make contact who understands that medical logistics matter more than tactics for determining whether this war becomes a catastrophe or an extinction event. If someone comes looking for expertise in keeping people alive when institutions fail, she'll have answers. If no one comes, she'll do what she's always done: coordinate distributed response with whatever resources she can access and keep as many people alive as possible until the crisis ends.
