The tier assessment is the process by which an individual's maximum safe [[Control Tier]] is determined. In both kingdoms, it is conducted between the ages of eight and twelve, and its result is, for most people, the most consequential single fact about their life that they did not choose and cannot change.

The assessment does not measure what a child can do with Flux. It measures what they can safely hold. A T3 child who has never cast a sigil still assesses at T3. The capacity is structural, present from birth, and does not require practice to exist — only practice to develop. The assessment reveals a ceiling. What happens under that ceiling is the work of a lifetime.

## How It Works

The assessment is conducted by trained assessors — [[Flux Users|Channelers]] at minimum, since reading another person's Flux capacity accurately requires casting at T5 or above. In cities, assessors are typically attached to the [[Ascendant Path]] chapter (in [[Auralis]]) or the regional guild office (in [[Terravelle]]). In rural areas and small settlements, a traveling assessor covers a circuit of communities on a fixed schedule, sometimes annually, sometimes less often. In disputed territory and remote settlements like [[Halveth]], assessors arrive through whatever institutional arrangement can be arranged, and the gap between when a child is ready and when an assessor arrives is sometimes years.

The assessment itself takes between thirty minutes and two hours depending on the child and the result. The assessor asks the child to perform a sequence of increasingly demanding exercises — not spells, but controlled Flux handling tasks designed to probe where the child's capacity begins to strain. A T1 child will complete the first several exercises without difficulty and show clear resistance at the threshold that marks their ceiling. A T6 child will pass through several exercises that would halt most adults before the assessor sees the resistance appear.

When the result is in a normal range — T1 through T3, where most people fall — a single assessor completes the session, records the result, and provides the standard documentation to the family and the relevant institution. When the result is T4 or above, common practice in both kingdoms is to bring a second assessor to confirm before the result is finalized. This is partly rigor and partly institutional habit: high results carry significant implications for the child's life trajectory, and a second confirmation has historically reduced the number of disputes.

When the result is T0, the assessment is brief. There is nothing to read above the baseline.

## In Auralis

In [[Auralis]], the tier assessment is inseparable from [[Ascendant Path]] ceremony. The assessment is conducted by a Luminant-affiliated assessor or a senior Luminant themselves, and the result is delivered in a ceremonial context that frames the number as a spiritual revelation rather than a technical measurement. The Path teaches that tier is "where you begin on a path you will walk for the rest of your life" — and the ceremony is designed to embed this framing in the moment the result is received.

The ceremony for T3 and above involves formal registration with the chapter's advancement track, the beginning of instruction in the Path's theology of mastery, and notification to the institutional networks that have an interest in high-tier children. The first contact from a scholarship institution or a senior Luminant's office may arrive within weeks of a high result.

The consolation ceremony for T0 results is a separate rite, conducted with the child and immediate family. It emphasizes contribution through witness, contemplation, and support of others' advancement. Senior Luminants who have performed this ceremony many times have developed complex and sometimes difficult relationships with the gap between what the ceremony says and what the Path's social structure delivers to T0 individuals in practice. The ceremony is not dishonest. It is insufficient.

## In Terravelle

In [[Terravelle]], the tier assessment carries less formal ceremony and more immediate practical weight. The result is a fact that determines which guild tracks are available, what training paths make sense, and where a family should direct their hopes for a child's working life. High results attract attention from scholarship institutions and, for results at T5 and above, from interests that extend beyond the guild system.

The [[Covenant of Measure]] has no formal ceremony for the tier assessment. The birth rune inscription is the Covenant's primary life rite. The assessment result is received as information. What you do with it is your own to determine — though the guild system, the scholarship institutions, and the social expectations of the community will all have opinions about this, and those opinions are not nothing.

For T0 results in Terravelle, there is no ceremony at all. The child is redirected toward trades that do not require casting capacity and given practical information about their options. This is done with competence and, usually, with kindness. It sometimes lands with neither.

## What Happens After

The weeks following a high-result assessment have a characteristic texture: institutional letters, family conversations, the particular weight of being a child who has become suddenly legible to systems that had not previously noticed you. A T4 or T5 result in a rural community means the community does not have a path for you. The path exists elsewhere. Whether you take it is theoretically a choice and practically shaped by everything your life has been until that moment.

A T0 result has its own characteristic texture, quieter and more lasting: the feeling of a door that did not open, and the work of building a life in a world organized around the assumption that the door was the important thing.

Most people — the T1s and T2s who make up the bulk of any community — experience the assessment as a transition into a defined track, a confirmation of what was probably expected, a fact that settles into the background of a life. For most people, this is exactly what it is.
