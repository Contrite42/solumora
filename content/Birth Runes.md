A birth rune is a personal [[Flux]] sigil that registers an individual within the distributed economic and identity network known as the Watts ledger. It is not a magical awakening, not a measure of ability, and not a religious rite in any formal sense — though both kingdoms have layered cultural meaning onto it over time. At its core it is a record: proof that a specific person exists, tied to a specific sigil, connected to every transaction that person has ever made.

## What It Does

A birth rune does two things. It establishes identity — a unique Flux signature registered to a specific individual that cannot be duplicated — and it connects that identity to the Watts ledger, allowing the holder to participate in the formal currency economy.

The rune itself does not store value. It is an address. The ledger, which exists distributed throughout [[Solumora]]'s ambient [[Flux]] field rather than in any physical location or institution, holds the record. Every transaction made against a birth rune writes simultaneously to every other transaction in the network. Forging a transaction would require rewriting the entire ledger simultaneously — a task that has never been accomplished and is not considered practically possible. The currency works because the ledger cannot be cheated, and the ledger cannot be cheated because it lives in the Flux field itself, distributed and without a central point of failure.

No institution controls it. No kingdom owns it. Both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] operate their formal economies on top of it, but neither created it. Its origins are not documented in either kingdom's records, which is a fact that receives less attention than it probably should.

## The Object

A birth rune can be inscribed on any object the holder chooses. The sigil requires only [[Control Tier|T1]] casting to create and any trained caster can perform it, which keeps the ceremony accessible to essentially anyone who wants it. The technical barrier is low. The social and economic incentives vary by location.

Most people who opt in choose a thin card of local material — wood, pressed fiber, treated leather, fired clay — carried on the person. The card is not the rune. The rune is the Flux signature. The card is simply the most convenient surface to inscribe it on, readable by anyone with consent and portable enough to use in daily transactions.

Some casters, particularly those deeply embedded in [[Auralis]]'s magical culture, have the rune tattooed directly onto their skin — a permanent body inscription that cannot be lost or stolen. This practice is regarded with discomfort by more conservative casting traditions, who consider permanent Flux inscription on living tissue an unnecessary intimacy with the field. In [[Terravelle]] this discomfort is cultural. In the Covenant of Measure tradition it carries mild ethical weight — the birth rune as a skin mark sits close to the line of ostentatious Flux use, though it is generally considered to fall just inside acceptable.

Carrying the rune on an unusual object — a piece of furniture, a weapon, a large stone — is technically permitted and occasionally done as an eccentricity or a statement. It is impractical enough that it remains rare.

## Reading and Privacy

A birth rune holder can read their own ledger at any time — balance, transaction history, registered identity. Reading another person's rune requires their consent. The consent mechanism is built into the sigil structure and cannot be bypassed at T1. Higher-tier casters have, in theory, the capacity to attempt forced reads, and this is treated in both kingdoms as a serious violation — practically equivalent to theft in [[Terravelle]] and both a legal and spiritual offense in [[Auralis]].

[[Flux Users|Scribes]] in both kingdoms are trained in rune verification as a standard part of their practice. A merchant, guild, or administrator can request consent-read of a rune to verify identity and confirm transaction capacity. This is routine in any formal economic context.

## Who Has One

In cities and large settlements, birth rune registration is the default. Participating in the formal economy — guild membership, property ownership, formal employment, access to institutional services — effectively requires one. The social pressure in [[Solhaven]] and [[Valdenmoor]] to be registered is strong enough that most urban residents do not think of it as a choice.

In villages and rural communities, the calculation is different. The formal Watts economy is less relevant to daily life when most exchange happens in goods and labor. Registration is available — any passing caster with T1 can perform it — but it is genuinely optional, and many rural communities function entirely on barter and relationship-based credit with no Watts changing hands at all. Joining the formal economy means choosing to participate in a larger system, and that choice is made deliberately when it is made at all.

[[Halveth]] is a useful example. The village's permanent population is mixed — some registered, some not — and both groups trade freely with each other. [[Wren]] is registered. Whether the information on her ledger accurately reflects her actual history is a separate question.

## Status Without a Rune

In [[Terravelle]], the absence of a birth rune is unremarkable provided the person is not trying to do something that requires one. The kingdom's administrative apparatus does not actively pursue the unregistered. A person without a rune exists outside the formal economy and outside the formal record, which in Terravelle reads as unusual but not suspicious. [[Ashford]] in particular has a significant unregistered population that the city has a practical policy of not examining too closely.

In [[Auralis]], the absence of a birth rune carries social weight that goes beyond the practical. The Ascendant Path's theology ties Flux participation to spiritual standing, and a person who has declined the rune is, in the Path's framework, someone who has declined to participate in the world's deeper structure. This is not a formal legal disadvantage in most contexts, but the social inference is real and the treatment that follows from it is consistent enough to be a meaningful deterrent. In [[Solhaven]]'s upper terraces, being unregistered is essentially a class marker.

The people [[Mave]] has replaced are registered. Their ledgers continue to transact normally. The Flux signature on their rune, which should be unique to them, is now Mave's. This is the one known mechanism by which the birth rune system can be compromised, and no one in either kingdom is aware it is possible.
