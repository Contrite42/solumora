# Death Ledger Claim Adjudication

**Death Ledger Claim Adjudication** is the review process that determines whether expedition death-linked payouts are valid, partial, denied, or escalated for dispute handling.

It connects field casualty records to contractual payout obligations in underwriter, guild, and banking systems.

## Adjudication Pipeline

A standard adjudication run has five stages:

1. intake validation (identity, policy, filing window)
2. event reconstruction from available logs and witness packets
3. cause-class assignment under contract language
4. payout computation and exception checks
5. settlement release or dispute escalation

Delays at stages 2-3 drive most downstream conflict.

## Evidence Inputs

Adjudicators rely on layered evidence:

- institutional or private death documentation
- route logs and escort attestations
- recovery service records from [[Rescue and Recovery Services]]
- contract riders and prior amendment history

The weakest link is often chain-of-custody integrity, especially when documentation markets are overloaded.

## Common Dispute Triggers

Frequent dispute triggers include:

- cause-of-death category disagreement
- missing or contradictory witness packets
- filing after deadline due to delayed recovery
- policy language conflict between base and rider clauses

These disputes are amplified when linked to known patterns in [[Expedition Insurance Fraud Patterns]].

## Throughput vs Accuracy Tension

Adjudication systems face constant tension between speed and certainty.

Fast decisions reduce household hardship and market panic. Slow decisions reduce fraud leakage but increase social and political pressure. Most institutions oscillate between these priorities instead of resolving them.

## Operational Outcome

Adjudication quality directly affects trust in [[Crossing Risk Underwriters]] and in survivor-facing finance commitments.

When adjudication is opaque or inconsistent, sponsors raise premiums, survivors distrust settlement terms, and informal claim markets expand outside formal channels.
