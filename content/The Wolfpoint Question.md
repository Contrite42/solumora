The Wolfpoint Question is the informal name that diplomatic and intelligence circles in both kingdoms use for a specific strategic problem: [[Wolfpoint]] controls the only known development center for [[Hypertext]], a Flux casting method that neither kingdom can independently replicate, and neither kingdom has found a way to change this situation without costs that exceed the benefit of trying.

## Why It Matters

[[Hypertext]] is not, by most current assessments, a decisive military advantage. A Hypertext practitioner of equivalent tier to a sigil practitioner is not dramatically more dangerous in direct confrontation. The advantage is expressiveness: Hypertext can describe effects that sigil geometry cannot represent, and in a conflict where those effects matter — where the ability to create conditional, sequential, or self-modifying Flux behavior is the difference between a working solution and an impossible one — Hypertext practitioners would have capabilities their opponents lack.

Both kingdoms' military analysis has reached approximately the same conclusion: Hypertext is currently a research and engineering advantage, not a combat advantage. If [[Auralis]] developed Hypertext-capable military practitioners at scale before [[Terravelle]] had comparable access, that gap would matter primarily in the medium-term strategic picture — in how quickly Auralis could develop new capabilities, how flexibly it could respond to novel problems, how effectively its researchers could build on the ancient ruins findings that both kingdoms are pursuing.

This medium-term frame is what makes the Wolfpoint Question urgent now and manageable now. It is not a crisis. It is a slowly shifting advantage that both kingdoms are watching.

## Auralis's Position

[[Auralis]] wants access and has been trying to get it for approximately fifteen years through a combination of formal correspondence, individual recruitment, and the relationship that [[Drest]] maintains with [[Mira Solv]]. None of these have produced the systematic access the Council desires. The Voss family has been consistently polite, occasionally useful in specific research areas, and has never offered the Scholarium access arrangement at scale that would give Auralis genuine Hypertext capability.

The constraint on Auralis's approach is that taking Wolfpoint by force would produce neither the cooperation nor the continuing development that makes Wolfpoint valuable. The Voss family's Hypertext knowledge is embodied, not purely documented. Occupying the settlement and removing its leadership would leave Auralis with a research facility whose key practitioners have either fled or declined to work. The knowledge would degrade faster than the occupation could extract it.

Drest understands this. His approach with Mira Solv is structured around creating a relationship of mutual benefit rather than extraction — giving Wolfpoint enough of what it wants (research results from Auralis-sponsored ruins expeditions, access to Auralis's sigil theory documentation) to make continued engagement more attractive than disengagement. Whether this approach will eventually produce the access he wants, or whether Wolfpoint will continue to manage the relationship without ever crossing the threshold, is the current state of the question.

## Terravelle's Position

[[Terravelle]] has filed Wolfpoint as a managed concern rather than an urgent problem. The Crown's current assessment — maintained through the relationship that [[Keln Varost]] manages — is that Wolfpoint's independence is strategically beneficial as long as it remains genuinely independent. A Wolfpoint that has been absorbed by Auralis is a threat. A Wolfpoint that is independent provides a buffer and a potential source of access on terms that Terravelle could negotiate if Auralis ever pressed hard enough to make the negotiation necessary.

This is a passive strategy, and the Crown knows it. Eddan's administration has not committed resources to actively building the kind of research relationship with Wolfpoint that would give Terravelle genuine Hypertext capability. The practical reasoning is that the relationship cost would be significant and the timeline to meaningful capability is long — long enough that the political situation may have changed by the time it mattered.

## What Wolfpoint Fears

Wolfpoint's strategic position depends on remaining more valuable as an independent resource than as a territory belonging to either kingdom. This balance has held for seven generations, but the people in [[The Voss Line|the Voss line]] are aware that it is not guaranteed.

What Wolfpoint fears is not military conquest — the logistics of projecting force into the far north are genuinely difficult, and neither kingdom has found a compelling enough immediate reason to try. What it fears is irrelevance: the possibility that one or both kingdoms will develop alternative access to Hypertext capability through the ruins sites, through independent practitioner development, or through the partial knowledge that Scholarium alumni carry, such that Wolfpoint's exclusive position erodes without any single confrontation that the settlement could have seen coming.

[[Sera Voss]]'s unfinished documentation project — the systematic Hypertext text that could theoretically be distributed broadly — is, from one angle, a hedge against irrelevance. If Wolfpoint publishes the foundation, it loses its gatekeeping position but gains credit for the system's spread and potentially gains a different kind of centrality: as the acknowledged origin and advanced development center of a method that has become generally available. She has not decided whether this trade is worthwhile, and has not decided at all.
