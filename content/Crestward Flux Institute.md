# Crestward Flux Institute

The Crestward Flux Institute is [[Auralis]]' primary research institution dedicated to studying [[Flux]] mechanics, optimization, and practical applications. It operates under a joint arrangement between [[The Council of Auralis]] and independent scholars, maintaining official university status while conducting research that serves council strategic interests.

The Institute employs approximately 80-100 researchers and technical staff, organized into departments focused on: cost optimization (reducing Watts required for spell execution), delivery mechanisms (improving range and precision), durability studies (making effects persist longer), and cross-discipline interactions (understanding how spells from different magical traditions can be combined or modified).

## Research Focus

The dominant research direction at Crestward is **cost reduction and scaled execution**. Most Flux research assumes single practitioners casting single spells. Crestward's researchers focus on how to execute larger effects through coordinated teams, how to reduce per-practitioner cost requirements, and how to make Flux-based systems work for T0 and T1 practitioners who currently have limited magical agency.

This focus satisfies multiple strategic interests simultaneously: it appears to be humanitarian (expanding access to Flux ability), it serves merchant house interests (cheaper Flux solutions improve commercial viability), and it directly supports [[Auralis]]' geographic expansion (coordinated low-cost spells become force multipliers in frontier settlement building).

The research environment is externally collaborative but internally competitive. Teams compete for funding and publication prominence, creating rapid innovation cycles but also territorial disputes over credit, methodology, and priority access to materials. This competition is tolerated by council overseers because it drives output velocity.
