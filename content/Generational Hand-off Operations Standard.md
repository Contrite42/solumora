# Generational Hand-off Operations Standard

The **Generational Hand-off Operations Standard** is a three-year governance operations framework defining how coexistence institutions transfer authority, tacit knowledge, and failure-memory from founding actors to successor cohorts without losing procedural safeguards.

## Need for Standardization

Early coexistence durability relied heavily on a small set of high-trust operators: [[Merra Veld]], [[Jess Maren]], [[Kess Tarren]], and allied founders in Commission, Tribunal, and monitoring networks. By year three, burnout and role turnover made personality-dependent governance a systemic liability.

The Standard requires institutions to preserve operational memory in forms that survive individual departure.

## Required Components

**Dual-Cycle Shadowing**
- Incoming operators must shadow outgoing leads for two full reporting cycles.
- Shadow logs are auditable and must include exception-handling decisions.

**Failure-Case Library**
- Each institution maintains indexed casebooks of major errors, corrective actions, and unresolved risks.
- Casebooks are integrated into [[Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum]] modules for advanced cohorts.

**Red-Team Continuity Drills**
- Quarterly simulation of rollback attempts, noncooperation waves, and false-positive cascades.
- Success criteria prioritize detection speed and transparent correction, not flawless prevention.

**Succession Risk Register**
- Required publication of high-risk role vacancies and transition milestones.
- Prevents silent authority gaps in critical review and mediation roles.

## Relationship to Existing Charters

The Standard operationalizes the intent of the [[Succession Safeguards Charter]] with measurable practice requirements and cross-institution verification.

Where the Charter defines governance commitments, this Standard defines how those commitments are executed under real staffing turnover and political stress.

## Early Outcomes

Pilot deployments showed:
- Lower remediation lag after personnel transitions
- Reduced dependence on founder intervention during district disruptions
- Improved consistency in application of the [[Civic Noncooperation Recovery Ladder]]

The Standard is now considered mandatory for institutions operating in Full Integration and Transitional Compliance zones, with adapted versions available for Protected Observation contexts.
