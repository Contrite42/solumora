**Orre Cavlt** is forty-nine years old and runs a mid-sized trading house in [[Ashford]] that has operated cross-border commerce between [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] for twenty-two years. [[Control Tier|T2]]. He took over the business from his aunt at twenty-seven, after spending his twenties learning route reliability, documentation handling, and relationship-driven customs flow.

## Appearance

Orre has the weathered, practical look of a long-haul trade operator: graying temples, ink-stained hands, and the alert expression of someone always recalculating schedule risk. He dresses to pass between warehouse floor and negotiation table without changing profile.

He carries a worn case of manifests, contact notes, and contingency plans. People read him as calm; people who know him read the jaw tension when systems stop behaving as modeled.

## Background

Cross-border trade is his entire professional life. He built success by reading informal indicators better than most: wait-time drift, inspection tone, and candor levels among processing contacts.

This year, those indicators have shifted in ways he cannot map cleanly to declared policy.

## What He Cannot Model

Multiple trusted Auralis-side contacts have become formally careful. Conversations that used to include context now remain transaction-narrow. A one-day clearance became four days with no formal hold and no actionable explanation.

Orre knows the difference between friction and signal. Friction is episodic and local. Signal is directional and persistent. What he sees now has directional features, but not enough transparency to identify intent.

His response has been operational, not rhetorical: wider schedule buffers, diversified contacts, and denser private records of crossing behavior.

## Relationships

- `ally`: [[Ashford]] - relies on city-side operators and arbitration channels to keep contracts executable.
- `dependent`: [[Auralis]] - throughput depends on relationships inside systems he does not control.
- `rival`: [[Expansion Faction]] - bears downstream cost of policy pressure that is never openly priced.

## Use In Session

- Players can engage Orre to trace whether a specific delay is noise, targeted pressure, or systemic transition.

## Complications

- Official paperwork appears valid while informal clearance behavior changes.
- Key contacts provide answers without context, reducing predictive power.
- Contract penalties arrive faster than policy explanations.

## Secrets

- Orre's private logs can reconstruct shift timing across multiple checkpoints.
- He can identify which cargo classes began receiving silent scrutiny first.
- One delayed shipment links to a network that should not appear in routine lanes.
