[[Filter]] is the most selective form of [[Target Spec]] in the sigil framework. Instead of aiming at one named target, the caster defines criteria and the spell applies only to targets that match.

A filter can be physical (wearing a marker, carrying a tagged object), positional (inside a marked boundary), or procedural (part of a known group definition). Broad identity-level filtering is possible in theory but usually too expensive or risky for ordinary work.

Filter targeting is difficult because the spell must evaluate candidates in real time. This increases [[Flux]] cost and failure risk, especially when combined with large [[Pattern]] areas or long [[Persistence]] durations.

In daily practice, filter logic is used for checkpoints, controlled access, expedition safety controls, and mixed-crowd operations where indiscriminate effects would be unacceptable.
