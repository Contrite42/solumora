# Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works

The **Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works** is the metalworking operation at the heart of [[Wolfpoint]]'s physical export economy. It produces blades, tools, and specialty components using cold-forge technique — a temperature-differential annealing process developed over seven generations that creates harder, longer-lasting edges than standard smithing can match.

The Works is not a large operation. Six permanent smiths, rotating through twelve-hour shifts, producing perhaps 200 finished pieces per year across blades, specialty tools, and commissioned work. But what it produces commands premium pricing in both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]], and generates enough revenue to justify [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]'s regular stops at [[Wolfpoint]].

## The Cold-Forge Technique

Standard smithing heats metal to forge temperature, shapes it while hot, then quenches to harden. Cold-forge inverts this: rough-shape the piece at standard forge heat, then cycle it repeatedly between Wolfpoint's constant 45-degree ambient cold and precision reheating at lower temperatures. Each cycle compresses the metal's grain structure in ways conventional forging cannot replicate.

The technique requires:
- **Controlled ambient cold** (Wolfpoint's year-round 45-degree temperature provides this naturally).
- **Precise reheating cycles** (smaller, carefully managed heat applications between cold cycles).
- **Extended production time** (weeks instead of days for high-quality pieces).
- **Deep material knowledge** (each metal alloy has different optimal cycle counts and temperature curves).

The Voss family discovered the technique three generations ago when attempting to create better-performing weapon edges for military applications. What they learned was that Wolfpoint's constant cold was not an obstacle to metalwork — it was an advantage no other settlement possessed.

## What the Works Produces

**Primary Products:**
- **Blades** (knives, short swords, tool blades) — the Works' signature export. A Wolfpoint blade holds edge 40-60% longer than standard equivalents, making it valuable to hunters, craftspeople, and professionals who depend on sharp tools daily.
- **Specialty tools** (precision chisels, scribing instruments, surgical implements) — where edge retention matters more than raw strength.
- **Commissioned work** (custom pieces for buyers willing to pay premium rates and wait extended production times).

**What It Does Not Produce:**
- Armor (too large, too time-intensive, market too small).
- Bulk tools (cold-forge advantage doesn't justify production cost for generic metalwork).
- Weapons above specialized knife/sword scale (military contracts would attract kingdom attention the Works prefers to avoid).

## Operations and Staffing

The Works operates from a low-profile stone building at Wolfpoint's southern edge, positioned to catch minimal wind while maintaining access to the settlement's main workshop district.

**[[Trel Vann]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 4]], 51 years old) runs the Works. He is not a Voss, but he trained under one and has spent twenty-three years refining cold-forge technique. His role is production management: cycle scheduling, quality control, maintaining the temperature logs that track each piece's thermal history, and determining which commissioned projects the Works will accept.

Trel's constraint is capacity. The Works could sell three times what it produces if he expanded operations. He does not expand. Rarity maintains premium pricing. Controlled output keeps Wolfpoint's metal exports economically significant without being strategically threatening. Both kingdoms value Wolfpoint cold-forge work. Neither kingdom considers it worth controlling by force.

Six smiths work under Trel, divided into three two-person teams that rotate shifts. Cold-forge requires sustained attention across weeks — tracking cycle counts, managing reheat temperatures, identifying micro-fractures before they propagate. Team continuity prevents mistakes.

## Economic Position

The Cold-Forge Works generates approximately 35-40% of [[Wolfpoint]]'s physical export revenue. The other major contributors are preserved game through [[Mountain Game Collective]] and wool textiles from [[Upland Herding Network]].

This revenue proportion is deliberate. Wolfpoint's economic independence depends on diversified physical exports that give the settlement legitimate trade justification without creating kingdom dependency on any single product. If cold-forge metalwork became Wolfpoint's dominant export, buyers in [[Terravelle]] or [[Auralis]] would have stronger incentive to control the source directly.

Trel knows this. He manages output to maintain value without creating strategic vulnerability. The Works produces enough to matter economically, not enough to matter geopolitically.

## Market Access

Finished pieces travel south through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]], typically in mixed cargo with preserved game and wool textiles. [[Ashford]] serves as primary distribution point for [[Terravelle]] buyers. [[Auralis]] buyers receive pieces through [[Ashford]] intermediaries or, occasionally, through direct purchase arrangements managed by [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]].

Pricing is set per-piece based on:
- Blade/tool type and size.
- Commission complexity (custom work costs 40-60% more than standard catalog pieces).
- Buyer relationship (repeat buyers with demonstrated reliability get pricing preference).
- Market demand signals (Trel adjusts catalog pricing yearly based on previous year's order patterns).

Average catalog blade: 18-24 watts. Custom work: 30-50 watts depending on specifications. For context, this positions Wolfpoint cold-forge work in the "premium craftwork" tier — accessible to professionals and well-resourced practitioners, priced above commodity metalwork but below rare specialist equipment.

## What Buyers Know

Buyers know cold-forge produces better edges. Most buyers do not know the technique details — temperature cycles, timing protocols, the role of Wolfpoint's specific climate conditions. The Works does not teach its methods to external buyers or accept apprentices from outside Wolfpoint.

This knowledge control is strategic. If cold-forge technique spreads and other settlements replicate it (assuming they can engineer equivalent cold-management infrastructure), Wolfpoint loses its metalwork advantage. The technique stays internal by design.

A few [[Auralis]] researchers have attempted to reverse-engineer cold-forge results by analyzing finished pieces. The attempts have produced partial insights but no successful replication. Metallurgy is embedded practice knowledge as much as it is theory. Observing a finished blade does not teach you the seven-week production cycle that created it.

## Relationship with Wolfpoint's Broader Economy

The Cold-Forge Works exists within Wolfpoint's deliberate economic strategy: maintain physical exports valuable enough to justify regular maritime trade access, diversified enough to prevent kingdom dependency, specialized enough to command premium pricing, and controlled enough to avoid becoming a strategic target.

Trel Vann's job is not just producing quality metalwork. It is producing exactly enough quality metalwork to keep Wolfpoint economically independent without making the settlement worth conquering.

He tracks his output logs. He manages his cycle schedules. He turns down commissioned work that would expand the Works beyond safe operational scale. He signs the manifests that go south with the packet ships.

The blades travel. Wolfpoint remains north, cold, and independent.
