Reld Marsh is a reed and salt-harvesting settlement in [[Auralis]]'s eastern coastal lowlands, fifteen miles south of [[Solhaven]]. Population approximately 120. It supplies woven reed products, preserved marsh plants, and sea salt to basin markets through direct trade relationships.

## The Environment

The marsh at Reld sits where tidal estuaries meet freshwater runoff from the basin's eastern drainage. The water is brackish, the land is intermittently flooded, and the ecosystem produces resources that upland agriculture cannot: dense reed stands for weaving, edible marsh plants, and naturally concentrating salt deposits in the tidal pools.

The settlement does not farm in the traditional sense. It harvests. Reed cutting happens in late summer when water levels drop and stands are accessible. Marsh plant collection runs year-round, following seasonal availability. Salt harvesting concentrates during the dry season when tidal pool evaporation reaches maximum efficiency.

## The Reed Economy

Reed products from Reld Marsh supply [[Solhaven]]'s basket-weaving workshops, mat production, and thatching operations. The settlements' weavers cut, bundle, dry, and deliver raw reed in consistent quality grades that urban workshops depend on for predictable output.

[[Lorn Seld]], who coordinates reed operations, has been managing harvest schedules and buyer relationships for twelve years. She knows which stands produce the best weaving-grade reed, which cutting methods preserve future growth, and which urban buyers pay fair prices for consistent quality. Her reputation for reliable delivery has made Reld Marsh the preferred reed supplier for multiple Solhaven workshops.

The work is seasonal, physically demanding, and requires knowledge of marsh ecology that cannot be taught from books. When to cut, where to cut, how to manage stands for long-term yield — these are details learned through years of direct practice, and Reld's economic viability depends on maintaining that expertise across generations.

## Salt Production

Salt harvesting at Reld Marsh is small-scale but profitable. During the dry season, tidal pools concentrate to brine strength naturally through evaporation. Settlement workers guide the brine into shallow collection pans, allow further evaporation, then harvest and process salt crystals for sale to inland communities that lack coastal access.

The operation is not mechanized. Workers move brine by hand using buckets and channels, rake forming salt from pans, and pack the product for transport. [[Flux]] use concentrates around evaporation management — Heatward sequences to accelerate drying on overcast days, and Sealward protections to prevent spoilage during storage.

Nev Kort, T1, manages the settlement's salt operation. His casting is minimal — seasonal, situational, and always in service of predictable problems. The salt business at Reld does not depend on Flux. It benefits from it at the margins.

## Daily Life

Reld Marsh is small, isolated, and stable in a way that urban [[Auralis]] communities are not. Most families have worked the marsh for generations. The settlement has no formal governance structure — decisions happen through informal consensus among the core families who manage the major operations.

Social cohesion at Reld comes from shared logistics. Reed cutting requires coordinated labor across multiple family groups. Salt harvesting during the dry season pulls every available hand. Marsh plant collection routes are shared knowledge that no one monopolizes. The settlement functions because everyone participates in maintaining the collective resource base.

Children at Reld learn marsh work from an early age. Most stay to continue family operations. Those who leave typically move to Solhaven, where they maintain trade relationships that benefit both the settlement and their urban households. Reld's integration into broader Auralis markets depends on those connections persisting across generations.

## The Market Position

Reld Marsh occupies a niche market position that protects it from direct competition. Reed products from the marsh have quality characteristics that urban workshops prefer: flexibility, durability, and consistent fiber length. Competing reed sources exist, but none match Reld's combination of quality and reliable supply.

Salt production is more competitive — multiple coastal settlements harvest salt — but Reld's proximity to Solhaven gives it transport-cost advantages that offset lower production volume compared to larger operations farther south.

The settlement's economic stability comes from maintaining both product lines. Reed harvesting alone would make Reld viable. Salt production alone would be marginal. Together they create a diversified income base that keeps the settlement functional through seasonal variations and market fluctuations.

## What Changed

Urban buyers from Solhaven have been requesting larger reed volumes over the past two seasons. [[Lorn Seld]] has declined most expansion requests. Increasing harvest volume would require cutting younger stands before they reach optimal maturity, which would reduce long-term yield quality and risk damaging the marsh's regenerative capacity.

The buyers do not understand the constraint. From their perspective, Reld is choosing to limit supply when demand exists. From Lorn's perspective, maintaining sustainable harvest practices is what keeps Reld economically viable across decades, not seasons.

The tension has not escalated to conflict. But it has created a gap — urban workshops want more volume now, and Reld wants to preserve the resource base that will sustain future generations. Neither position is wrong. Both reflect legitimate economic pressures operating on different timescales.
