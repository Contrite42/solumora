Rift incursions are the term used in Terravelle's border documentation for the ongoing emergence of [[Flux Demons]] through dimensional tears in the [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] region and the subsequent movement of those creatures into inhabited territory.

The term is Terravelle's. Auralis does not have an official term for it.

## The Nature of Rifts

Rifts are not an artificial problem and do not have an artificial cause. They form where the ambient [[Flux]] density is high enough to thin the membrane between Solumora and the [[The Flux Dimension|Flux dimension]] — a process that predates both kingdoms and has been ongoing since before the desert took its current form. The anomalous sites in the deep desert represent the zones of highest density and consequently the most active and persistent tear formation, but minor rifts open and close throughout the high-Flux regions along Zakros and toward the poles, without pattern or warning.

Rifts can be closed. The [[Sigils|sigil]] work required to dispel a dimensional tear is understood in principle by [[Flux Users|Scholar]] institutions in [[Emberfall]] and has been tested in limited conditions. The practical barrier is tier: effective rift closure requires sustained casting at T5 or above, and T5 casters are rare enough that mounting a systematic closure effort against dozens of active sites in hostile terrain is not a realistic operational proposal. Individual closures by high-tier expedition members have occurred. No organized program exists.

## Geography of the Problem

Rifts form in zones of extreme Flux density, concentrated around the anomalous sites in the deep desert. The creatures that emerge move outward from those zones and, because they carry their own Flux density and do not require high-Flux environments to survive, they range freely. The natural direction of range expansion is north and south along the desert's edges — north into the unpopulated deep desert, south into the sparse frontier territory at the northern edge of [[Auralis]].

Terravelle's southern reaches are far enough from the primary rift zones that incursions reaching Terravelle proper represent creatures that have traveled significant distance, which in practice means they have been ranging for long enough to have acclimated, fed, and become more capable than they were at emergence.

## Auralis's Position

The [[The Council of Auralis|Council of Auralis]] is aware of the incursions. The affected territory is frontier — low population density, economically marginal, politically underrepresented on the council. The casualty rate is real but not dramatic by the standards of a continental power managing multiple competing priorities.

The council's functional position, never formally stated, is that the incursion rate represents an acceptable ongoing cost. Individual council members hold this view for different reasons — some genuinely prioritize the research value of active rift sites over the defensive cost of closing them, some are focused elsewhere and simply do not engage with the question, and some have run the numbers and concluded that intervention would cost more politically than the current situation costs in lives.

[[Drest]] is aware of the full scope of the problem. His position is consistent with the Culmination faction's broader interest in the desert sites: the rifts are adjacent to the ruins, active rift zones are zones of extreme Flux density, and that density is relevant to what the ruins were built to do. Closing the rifts is not straightforwardly in the Culmination faction's interest.

No one on the council has stated this publicly.

## Terravelle's Position

[[Terravelle]] considers the incursion situation a border crisis. The creatures that range far enough north to reach Terravelle proper are a genuine military and civil problem, and Eddan's administration has been managing it with border documentation, quiet military deployment, and deliberate understatement.

Formal escalation — treating the incursions as a matter requiring joint response from both kingdoms — requires Auralis's acknowledgment that a problem exists at a scale worth addressing. That acknowledgment has not been forthcoming. Pushing for it publicly would require Terravelle to make claims about conditions in Auralis's own territory that Auralis can simply deny, in a political environment where the two kingdoms are already managing significant tension.

The result is a crisis that Terravelle cannot name as a crisis, managed with resources Terravelle cannot publicly justify, against a threat that Auralis is officially unconcerned about.

[[Eddan Voss]] has not found a way to force the issue without either starting a diplomatic confrontation he cannot win or revealing the extent of Terravelle's border intelligence, which would raise its own questions about how long and how closely Terravelle has been watching Auralis's frontier.

## What Is Not Known

Neither kingdom has any documentation of [[High Demons]]. The incursion problem, as both kingdoms understand it, refers entirely to lower-order creatures. The possibility that something categorically more capable has been present in Solumora for centuries, operating at a scale that makes the lower-order incursions look like noise, has not occurred to anyone in a position to act on it.
