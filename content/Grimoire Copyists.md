Grimoire copying is a skilled trade, not a simple reproduction task. A poorly copied [[Sigils|sigil]] does not fail cleanly — it misfires, underperforms, or produces effects that bear only a partial relationship to the intended spell. The demand for accurate copies is constant, and the practitioners who can deliver them reliably occupy a well-defined position in the economy of [[Flux]] access.

## The Copyist Trade

A professional copyist — called a Scribe in both kingdoms — specializes in precision sigil reproduction. The core competency is geometric accuracy at small scale: sigil variables are encoded in the exact proportions, angles, and weights of the drawn symbol. A sigil that is close is not the same as a sigil that is correct, and the difference is not always visible to an untrained eye until the spell fires.

Most professional Scribes work from master copies — high-quality source grimoires authenticated by their originator or by institutional review. The quality of the output is directly bounded by the quality of the source; a copy from a degraded or uncertain master is a degraded copy, regardless of the Scribe's skill.

The [[Common Grimoire|Common]]-tier market drives the bulk of copying work. Most Scribes spend the majority of their working hours on T1–T2 spell reproduction, which is high volume, relatively low margin, and requires precision but not unusual talent. [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]]-tier work commands higher fees and requires more experienced copyists because the sigil geometry is more complex and the margin for error narrower.

## Copymark

The spell [[Common Grimoire#Copymark|Copymark]] (T2, Chemical/Binding discipline) is the professional Scribe's primary tool — it transfers the exact surface impression of a sigil from one object to another on contact, using the source itself as the template. It does not substitute for skill; the spell is only as accurate as the contact it makes, and inconsistencies in surface, ink quality, or pressure produce inconsistencies in the copy. But it removes the hand-tremor variable and is fast enough to make volume work viable.

Scribes who work without Copymark — using freehand technique only — are rarer and more expensive. Their work is considered more authoritative in [[Auralis]] scholarly circles, where Copymark output is sometimes viewed as second-generation reproduction rather than true copying.

## Certification

In [[Terravelle]], Scribe work is certified through the [[Trade Guilds]]. A certified Scribe has passed a quality-verification process for their tier specialty: Common, Uncommon, or above. The certification is not a license in the legal sense — an uncertified Scribe can still sell copies — but it is a credibility marker that affects where they can sell and at what price. Guild-certified grimoires carry an authentication mark.

In [[Auralis]], Scribe certification is informal but operates through the [[The Advancement Corps|Scholar]] networks. High-quality Scribes are vouched by practitioners whose names carry institutional weight. The vouching system is more opaque than Terravelle's guild certification and more susceptible to personal patronage relationships.

## Error Rates

Honest Scribes track their error rates, because a reputation for clean copies is the only durable asset in the trade. A copy that fails in the field comes back to the Scribe who sold it, either as a direct complaint or as damaged reputation. Most professional Scribes guarantee their work for Common tier and charge a modest remake fee for documented misfires rather than refund the full price.

The industry standard distinction is between a transcription error (geometric inaccuracy in the copy — Scribe fault) and an activation error (caster did not meet tier requirement, insufficient Flux, poor surface conditions — not Scribe fault). Disputes over which category applies are common, particularly for buyers who are near the tier threshold for a given spell.
