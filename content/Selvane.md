Selvane is the elder of the two [[High Demons|High Demons]] currently operating within [[Auralis]], approximately three centuries old and operating at the upper range of T4 approaching T5. He has been in [[Solhaven]] for forty years under a succession of identities, and his current cover — a mid-tier trade advisor with unremarkable credentials and a reputation for quiet competence — has held for eleven of them.

## Appearance

Selvane presents as a lean man in apparent middle age, composed and precise in manner, the kind of person who is always slightly better dressed than the occasion requires. His horns are small and close-set, easy to disguise under styled hair, and he has not had a significant close call in over a decade. He maintains his cover with the particular discipline of someone who has lost a cover before and learned from it.

He does not smile often. When he does it does not reach his eyes, and most people who interact with him regularly have noticed this and decided it means he is simply a serious person. This interpretation is convenient and he does nothing to correct it.

## Discipline

Selvane's spell is Electric — specifically, his control over electrical charge, conductivity, and the disruption of systems that depend on them. At his current tier this extends beyond the physical: he can disrupt [[Sigils]] mid-activation, interfere with Flux channels at short range, cut the invisible lines of communication that run between people through shared grimoire networks and linked tokens. He is not the most powerful demon in [[Solumora]]. He is possibly the most dangerous one to have near a functioning institution, because what he does best is make things stop working in ways that are very hard to trace back to a cause.

The applications to political manipulation are not subtle in concept but are entirely invisible in execution. A message that doesn't arrive. A meeting where the Flux-linked documentation fails to record correctly. A council member whose personal sigil system develops intermittent faults at critical moments. Selvane does not need to be in the room. He needs to be in the building.

## Goals

Selvane wants a permanent passage between Solumora and the [[The Flux Dimension|Flux dimension]] — not a rift, which opens and closes on its own and cannot be directed, but a constructed hallway that holds. This has been the organizing purpose of his time in Solumora. He does not know yet whether it is achievable. He knows that the anomalous sites in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] are the most promising terrain he has found: the membrane between dimensions is thinnest there, the ambient [[Flux]] density is unlike anything else in Solumora, and the ancient civilization was doing something at those sites significant enough that the Flux field has not normalized in the centuries since.

What exactly they were doing is what he needs to know. The council's expeditions are the only organized research effort that produces real data from the deep desert, and forty years of making himself quietly indispensable to the trade infrastructure that funds that work has given him access to expedition reports before most of the council itself sees them.

He does not know whether the ancient civilization succeeded at anything adjacent to what he wants. He does not know whether the T9-scale Flux events at the anomalous sites were failures in the direction he cares about or something else entirely. He is positioned to find out.

## Relationship with Mave

[[Mave]] arrived in [[Solhaven]] already knowing he was there. He ran a series of disruptions against her early work — not to drive her out, but to establish clearly that he was aware of her and that she was operating in territory he had spent decades preparing. She acknowledged this and stayed.

The accommodation that has developed is not a partnership. He has not told her what he is working toward. She has access he does not — the identities she acquires move through spaces and relationships that would take him years to reach through conventional means, and she has, on occasion, positioned information in ways that were useful to him without being asked. He accepts this without commenting on it. Whether she understands what she is helping with, he cannot tell.

He is also, though he would not frame it this way, slightly concerned that she is getting better faster than he expected.
