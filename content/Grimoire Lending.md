Grimoire lending is the practice of providing temporary access to a text without transferring ownership. It operates through institutional libraries, commercial rental operations, and informal private arrangements — three parallel systems with different terms, different clients, and different risks.

## Institutional Lending

Both kingdoms' major institutions lend grimoires to qualified members. In [[Terravelle]], the [[Trade Guilds|guild]] libraries in [[Valdenmoor]] and [[Ashford]] lend [[Common Grimoire|Common]] and [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]] masters to certified members for reference copying. The terms are practical: a fixed loan period, a deposit against damage or loss, and a copying fee if the member intends to make working copies rather than simply reference the text. Rare-tier lending requires a purpose statement and approval from the library supervisor.

In [[Auralis]], institutional lending through the [[The Advancement Corps]] and affiliated Scholar institutions functions as a patronage benefit. Members in good standing can access texts held by the institution; access to higher-tier material depends on standing, stated purpose, and the institution's assessment of the requester's reliability.

## Commercial Rental

Commercial grimoire rental — paying a weekly fee for temporary possession of a copy — is most prevalent in cities with high practitioner density and insufficient purchasing power to support full ownership. [[Solhaven]] and [[Valdenmoor]] both have established rental operations serving T2–T4 practitioners who need access to an Uncommon spell for a specific job but cannot justify owning a full Uncommon collection.

The economics of rental rely on the same text serving many clients serially. Rental copies experience heavy use and degrade faster than privately owned texts, which is why commercial rental operations are one of the primary customers for [[Grimoire Repair and Preservation]] services — a rental copy that fails in the field is a reputational and liability problem for the rental operator.

## Private Lending

In rural areas and small communities, private lending is often the primary mechanism of access to grimoire texts above what the local market carries. A practitioner who holds an Uncommon copy may lend it to a neighbor who needs access to a specific spell, relying on social obligation and community relationships rather than formal terms.

Private lending creates an ambiguous situation around copying. Most private lenders understand that borrowers may copy from the text — a freehand or [[Common Grimoire#Copymark|Copymark]]-assisted copy for personal use is generally tolerated. A borrower who copies and then sells or lends further is taking something the original owner did not intend to give, but there is no formal mechanism to prevent it and social consequences are the primary deterrent.

## Cross-Kingdom Lending

Cross-kingdom lending — an [[Auralis]] Scholar lending a text to a [[Terravelle]] practitioner or vice versa — is rare and operates entirely through personal relationship rather than any institutional framework. The text crosses [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] via courier or carried personally, the terms are whatever the parties agree, and there is no enforcement mechanism if the arrangement goes wrong. Most practitioners who engage in cross-kingdom lending have established trust relationships built over years of correspondence or direct contact.
