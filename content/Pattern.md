[[Pattern]] defines the spatial geometry of a spell's effect: where the result exists in physical space after casting. It does not define discipline or intent by itself; it defines footprint.

Common pattern choices include point, plane, beam, cone, ring, cylinder, sphere, and field. Simpler patterns are cheaper and easier to aim. Complex patterns are expensive because the caster must hold more boundaries and edge conditions at once.

Pattern selection is usually a logistics decision:
- Point and plane effects are favored when precision matters more than coverage.
- Beam and cone effects are favored when directionality matters.
- Sphere and field effects are favored when area control matters.

Pattern always interacts with [[Reach]] and [[Target Spec]]. A wide pattern with long reach and selective targeting can quickly become cost-prohibitive for low-tier casters.
