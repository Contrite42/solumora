Fennick is the second oldest [[High Demons|High Demon]] in [[Solumora]], somewhere in his seventh century, and by a significant margin the most visible one. Where the others conceal themselves as a matter of survival, Fennick conceals himself as a matter of taste — selectively, strategically, and only when the alternative would be less entertaining.

## Appearance

Fennick presents as a man in his apparent thirties: sharp-featured, expressive, with the particular energy of someone who has just arrived at a party and immediately made it better. His horns are a genuine problem for maintaining a human identity and he has solved this by not maintaining one. He moves. He arrives somewhere, causes a significant amount of social disruption, and leaves before anyone has time to ask too many questions about the horns. The working assumption he has cultivated across a century of this approach is that anyone strange enough and confident enough to walk into a gathering with horns and immediately begin holding court is probably someone you don't want to question directly.

This has worked more often than it hasn't.

## Discipline

Fennick's spell is Chemical — specifically, his relationship to the chemistry of living things. At upper T7 and with six centuries of practice, his control over what a body produces, absorbs, and processes is extraordinary. He does not need to touch a person. He does not need them to consume anything. He can alter the chemical state of everyone in a room from across it, and has done so routinely for as long as he can remember.

The applications he favors are social: lowered inhibition, elevated mood, the particular warmth that makes strangers feel like old friends and confidences flow easily. He does not consider this manipulation, exactly. He considers it hospitality at a level most hosts can't manage. The conversations he has facilitated, the alliances he has nudged into existence, the feuds he has defused or inflamed depending on which seemed more interesting — these are his legacy, scattered across six centuries of gatherings he has crashed and left better or worse than he found them.

He is also capable of less pleasant applications. He simply finds them less interesting.

## Nature

Fennick is, genuinely, having a good time. This is not performance. He arrived in [[Solumora]] young and curious and found a world full of creatures who live fast, feel intensely, and build things that don't last — and he has found this delightful for six hundred years running. He does not have an agenda in the way [[Selvane]] has an agenda. He has preferences: he prefers interesting situations to boring ones, honest chaos to managed stability, and creatures who say what they mean to creatures who don't.

He gives guidance freely to anyone he can communicate with — humans, the occasional [[Equatorial Fauna|desert creature]] that has enough Flux sensitivity to register him, whatever comes through the rifts that seems worth talking to. His advice is usually good. It is also usually destabilizing, because Fennick's threshold for what constitutes a reasonable thing to suggest is calibrated to someone who cannot die of consequences.

He is aware of [[Sorath]] and finds his withdrawal a waste. He is aware of [[Selvane]] and [[Mave]] and finds their council maneuvering ambitious for their age. He has visited [[Wren]]'s village twice, purely socially, and considers Wren the most sensible of the younger three.

## Current Situation

Fennick follows interesting things. Currently the most interesting things are in [[Auralis]] — the political tension with [[Terravelle]], the council's obsession with the [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]], the young queen who is smarter than her situation. He has attended three separate council-adjacent social events in [[Solhaven]] in the past decade under three different names, horns covered for exactly as long as it took to get inside and no longer.

He has not noticed that [[Selvane]] and [[Mave]] are both embedded there, or rather he has noticed the Flux signatures and has not yet placed them. When he does it will complicate his next visit considerably.
