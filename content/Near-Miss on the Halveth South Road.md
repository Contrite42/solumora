A mixed expedition team departing [[Halveth]] ignored staging advice and pushed onto the south road one day earlier than local guides recommended. Within six miles they encountered layered Binding resistance: markers that should have held direction drifted, pack animals balked at ordinary crossings, and two route sigils refused to stabilize.

Team leadership accused a rival guide company of sabotage and returned to Halveth to file complaint. [[Selt Orvn]] reviewed the route notes and found no tampering signatures. The complaint converted to a "poor timing and field fatigue" advisory.

One survivor, already shaken by prior incursion exposure, insisted someone had "closed the road" to force them back. No supporting witness could identify who, and no one reported seeing a caster operating in the area.

The same team departed three days later under revised timing and completed its outer survey without further anomalies. In village discussion, the first failure became a cautionary story about arrogance and weather.

No one treated it as evidence of [[Wren]]'s control lattice.
