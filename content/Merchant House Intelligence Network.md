# Merchant House Intelligence Network

The Merchant House Intelligence Network is an informal system by which mid-tier and large merchant houses operating across the [[Terravelle]]-[[Auralis]] boundary share commercial information to reduce mutual risk and coordinate strategy. It is not a formal coalition. It is a recurring pattern of conversation between trusted counterparts spanning decades.

## Function

Merchant houses operating cross-border commerce maintain senior representatives who meet irregularly in neutral cities ([[Solhaven]], [[Valdenmoor]]) or maintain written correspondence that is carefully worded to be plausibly about commerce while actually communicating strategic intelligence.

Information shared typically includes:
- **Economic forecasting** — predictions about demand fluctuations, seasonal trade flows, and supply disruptions.
- **Currency and payment system changes** — shifts in how kingdoms are managing cross-border payments, which can signal broader economic policy changes.
- **Personnel and institutional shifts** — knowledge about which administrators are being promoted, rotated, or removed, and what these changes suggest about institutional priorities.
- **Regulatory pressure** — early warnings about documentation requirement changes, new tariff structures, or inspection intensity shifts.
- **Market access threats** — awareness when smaller competitors are being squeezed, when new trade corridors are opening, or when established routes are being closed.

## Strategic Value

Cross-border merchant houses are in a unusual position. They operate under both kingdom authorities simultaneously. They observe policy changes in real time across the boundary. They notice when official positions are about to shift before official announcements, because they see the ground-level effects first.

A merchant house that suddenly finds documentation requirements in one direction tightening while the other direction remains stable has noticed something about institutional intent that official channels may not yet have articulated. A company that watches regulatory attention shifting toward a particular type of trade route has recognized pattern change. A merchant tracking garrison rotation patterns in [[Halveth]] may notice acceleration before any official announcement.

## Relationship to Official Authority

Neither kingdom officially recognizes the network as a distinct entity. Both kingdoms are aware that it exists. The relationship is deliberately asymmetrical — merchant houses operate with sufficient legitimacy that neither kingdom wants to push too hard, but with sufficient gray-market character that neither kingdom trusts them completely.

This creates a system where merchant houses are simultaneously tolerated as a useful source of early warning about commercial disruption and monitored as potential security risks. The arrangement persists because merchant houses control information that both kingdoms want access to, and because merchant houses benefit from continued independent operation across the boundary.

## Known Participants

Major merchant houses maintaining cross-border operations include Valdenmoor Trade Consortium, houses based in [[Ashford]], [[Solhaven]] shipping consortiums, and independent merchant traders operating personal networks across the frontier. The scale is probably 15-25 significant actors with enough capital and continuity of operation to participate meaningfully in intelligence exchange.
