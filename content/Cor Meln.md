**Cor Meln** is thirty-eight years old and has lived in [[Halveth]] his entire life. His parents came through on a crossing when he was not yet born and stayed, which is the origin story for most permanent Halveth residents and is not a story he finds particularly interesting to tell.

His work is equipment. He checks, repairs, and maintains the gear that expedition parties bring in and sometimes leave behind — rope, load-bearing frames, water storage, the various Flux-adjacent tools that desert guides rely on and that need consistent inspection to stay reliable. He also maintains the settlement's emergency stock, which is a separate inventory kept separate for reasons that become obvious the first time an expedition comes back short a guide and the remaining party members are not equipped to wait out a return.

His [[Control Tier|T1]] casting ability is useful for diagnostic work — detecting structural failure in materials that look sound, finding the compromised weave in a rope before it breaks under load. He does not use it for anything else. The diagnostic application is sufficient for what the work requires.

He knows the rift zone proximity the way a person who has spent their life near something knows it: not as a subject of study but as a condition of existence that shapes what you do on a given day. When the ambient [[Flux]] shifts at the margins, he knows before he checks the instruments. He has never found this remarkable.

[[Wren]] governs the settlement's significant decisions. Cor Meln follows Wren's guidance with the same unsentimental acceptance he brings to weather patterns and equipment schedules. The guidance is generally sound. He has not needed to develop a theory about why.
