The fauna of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] is unlike anything found elsewhere in [[Solumora]]. Its ecosystem is built almost entirely on insects and arachnids — creatures that have adapted over unknown generations to the desert's temperature extremes and, critically, to its unusually dense ambient [[Flux]] field.

## Flux-Fueled Biology

Zakros fauna draw ambient [[Flux]] directly into their biological processes in ways that no creature outside the desert does. They do not use [[Sigils]] — they have no capacity for deliberate spellwork — but they have evolved bodies that channel Flux as a supplementary energy source alongside conventional food intake. This makes them partially independent of food availability, which is how they survive an environment where conventional food chains are barely functional.

The mechanism is not fully understood. [[Flux Users|Scholars]] in [[Emberfall]] have studied preserved specimens for decades without arriving at a complete model of how the biological Flux channeling works. The best current theory is that Zakros fauna have evolved something analogous to a low-tier [[Solumora|birth rune]] as a biological structure — a persistent Flux-holding capacity in their body chemistry that tops up from ambient field density rather than deliberate inscription.

## Behavior

Zakros fauna are not aggressive by default. They understand, in whatever way desert fauna understands things, that a prepared human group is not easy prey. A party moving with purpose, with adequate thermal [[Sigils]] and mutual awareness, will typically not be approached.

What they respond to is weakness. An injured traveler, an isolated one, someone who has lost their grimoire in the wind or whose Flux reserves are depleted — these are different calculations. Zakros fauna are patient and numerous. They can afford to wait.

The most dangerous fauna are not the largest. The largest visible species — a broad-bodied arachnid roughly the size of a large dog — is territorial but avoidable. The more significant threat is the swarm fauna: small insects that travel in dense groups, responsive to Flux expenditure as a signal, and capable of overwhelming a caster who has drawn too heavily on their reserves and cannot sustain defensive sigils.

## Notable Species

**The Glasswing** — a large translucent insect with wings that refract light unpredictably, making it difficult to track visually. Not aggressive but territorial around its nesting sites, which are marked by clusters of partially buried material that can be mistaken for ruins fragments from a distance.

**The Ashcrawler** — the large territorial arachnid. Brown-grey coloration, heavily armored, Flux-sensitive in a way that makes it respond to active casting near its territory. Experienced desert crossers learn its territorial patterns and route around them.

**The Flicker Swarm** — not a single species but a complex of several small insect species that travel together and respond collectively to Flux expenditure. The name comes from the shimmer effect their Flux-channeling bodies produce in the heat. A Flicker Swarm on the horizon is a reason to conserve casting resources immediately.

**The Deepwalker** — large, slow, found primarily near the [[Ancient Ruins|anomalous Flux sites]]. Not known to have attacked a human. Not known to do much of anything. [[Flux Users|Scholars]] who have observed them describe a persistent ambient Flux disturbance that follows the creature without any visible casting mechanism. What it is doing with that Flux, if anything, is unknown.

## Relationship to Flux Demons

The native fauna of Zakros are not [[Flux Demons]] and should not be confused with them. Fauna evolved within Solumora's Flux gradient over generations. Flux Demons are dimensional interlopers with no evolutionary relationship to the desert ecosystem. The fauna treat them as they treat most things: with opportunistic patience. Flux Demons are not easy prey and are not typically targeted. In areas near active rift zones, the fauna distribution shifts — the largest territorial species tend to vacate the immediate vicinity of known rifts, possibly due to the Flux disturbance associated with rift activity.
