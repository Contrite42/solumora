# Border Settlement Coexistence Protocol

The **Border Settlement Coexistence Protocol** is a specialized framework developed by the [[Ashford Coexistence Oversight Commission]] for settlements along the [[Auralis]]-[[Terravelle]] border, where coexistence policies intersect with jurisdictional ambiguity, resource competition, and living memory of the near-war. Implemented eighteen months after the [[Stories/The First Casualty|High Demon revelation crisis]], it represents the first formal acknowledgment that border communities face distinct integration challenges requiring tailored approaches.

## Geographic Scope

The Protocol applies to settlements within thirty miles of the border, including:
- Desert crossing waypoints ([[Ashford]], [[Breck Tor]], [[Kellen's Reach]])
- Mining outposts with cross-border resource claims
- Agricultural communities along the [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]]
- Temporary expedition staging camps

These communities share characteristics making standard coexistence frameworks inadequate:
- Mixed jurisdictional authority (both kingdoms claim some oversight)
- Economic dependence on cross-border trade requiring institutional cooperation
- Population fluidity (seasonal workers, expedition crews, traders)
- Recent trauma from near-war mobilization

## Core Provisions

**Dual Observer System**: Both kingdoms maintain Commission observers in border settlements; neither has unilateral enforcement authority. Disputes escalate to joint Commission panels rather than kingdom courts.

**Seasonal Compliance Windows**: Integration requirements scaled to settlement population cycles. Full compliance expected only during peak-population months; reduced requirements during winter/low-activity periods when permanent residents predominate.

**Resource Neutrality Clause**: High Demon involvement in cross-border resource management must be disclosed to both kingdoms. Prevents scenarios where one kingdom leverages High Demon expertise for competitive advantage.

**Emergency Cascade Prevention**: If coexistence incident occurs in border settlement, automatic cooling-off protocols activate before either kingdom can deploy enforcement. Designed to prevent single incidents from triggering diplomatic crises.

## Implementation Challenges

**Ashford** provides the Protocol's most complex test case:
- Population varies 300% between expedition season and winter
- [[Ashford Dock Compact]] already creates jurisdictional complexity
- Community has expedition-rescue culture valuing competence over institutional compliance
- [[Merra Veld]]'s presence (Protocol architect + local resident) creates bias perception

Other settlements face different challenges:
- **Mining outposts**: Workers fear High Demon geological expertise makes human labor obsolete
- **Agricultural border areas**: Generational families with [[The Persecution Era|persecution memory]] resist outsider-imposed integration
- **Temporary camps**: Compliance enforcement impossible when population turns over every three months

## Strategic Tensions

The Protocol embodies a fundamental trade-off:

**Flexibility** enables functional coexistence in complex border environments where rigid rules would fail. Communities appreciate scaled requirements matching their actual capacity.

**Weakness** creates legal gray zones where discrimination can hide behind "local conditions" justifications. Critics note Protected Observation Zone classification grants cover for communities wanting to avoid integration entirely.

The [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal]] has limited border jurisdiction under the Protocol, frustrating Tribunal staff who see it as systematic enforcement evasion. [[Kess Tarren]], representing Tribunal interests in Commission negotiations, argued the Protocol "trades justice for stability."

[[Jess Maren]], working at the [[Joint Rift Monitoring Cell]] [[Ashford]] station, describes the Protocol's practical reality: "We have three High Demons on staff during expedition season, zero in winter. The city shifts between Full Integration and Transitional Compliance depending on the calendar. It's messy, but we're solving rift predictions—the framework bends to let us work."

## Long-term Trajectory

The Protocol's eighteen-month review revealed:
- Zero persecution incidents in Protocol-governed settlements (vs. three in comparable non-border towns)
- Higher High Demon participation in border institutions than in some core cities
- Jurisdictional gray zones enable cooperation both kingdoms would struggle to authorize formally

These outcomes suggest the Protocol's flexibility, while frustrating to principled advocates, may produce better practical coexistence than rigid universal standards. The question remains whether it represents a permanent accommodation to border complexity or a transitional framework toward eventual uniform integration.
