**Oryen Veld** is thirty-nine years old and runs a mid-sized import brokerage in [[Solhaven]]'s commercial district. T2. Raised in [[Solhaven]], assessed at eleven, trained in the standard commercial Flux track, and spent his twenties building enough capital and enough relationships to operate independently rather than as a subordinate in someone else's house.

He has done this. The brokerage handles cargo from the coastal routes, matches incoming shipments to buyers, earns a percentage on each transaction. The operation is neither failing nor growing significantly, and Oryen has made a considered decision that this is acceptable. He is not building toward anything larger. He is maintaining something viable.

In [[Solhaven]]'s implicit social architecture, T2 sits below the threshold where advancement ambition is culturally expected and above the threshold where its absence is considered a practical acceptance of limits. It sits in a band where the people around you are watching to see what you do with what you have. He is aware of this watching. He has been aware of it since he was twelve and understood what T2 meant in a city organized around the belief that tier should correspond to trajectory.

The watching has shaped his professional style more than he would prefer. He presents himself as a person of measured ambition rather than as a person who has chosen adequacy, because the latter framing does not land well in Solhaven and the former is accurate enough to maintain. The distinction matters to him in ways he does not talk about.

His T2 casting is used entirely for transaction verification and ledger work — the standard commercial application. He is good at it in the specific way of someone who has done it daily for fifteen years, and finds it neither interesting nor unpleasant.
