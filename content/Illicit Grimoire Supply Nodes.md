# Illicit Grimoire Supply Nodes

**Illicit Grimoire Supply Nodes** are the recurring points where unauthorized grimoire material enters black-market circulation before distribution through brokers and transport networks.

They are not single hidden warehouses. They are functional roles inside otherwise legitimate systems.

## Core Node Types

Black-market circulation depends on five node classes:

- source-library leakage (institutional copy room access)
- overrun copying (extra copies beyond authorized orders)
- salvage recirculation (estate and expedition text diversion)
- cross-border substitution (declared packet swap during transit)
- authentication staging (seal/provenance preparation)

These nodes connect directly to [[Black Market Grimoire Circulation]] through [[Midden Exchange]], [[Solhaven Copymark]], and related broker channels.

## Node Stability Patterns

Nodes remain stable when they are low-volume and embedded in high-volume legitimate traffic.

They fail when:

- throughput expands faster than trust networks
- the same source appears repeatedly in seizure reports
- documentation anomalies cluster around one courier class

Most collapses happen from pattern visibility, not single betrayal.

## Economics of Node Participation

Node operators are paid for access asymmetry, not for battlefield risk.

- low-tier overrun copy nodes earn steady small margins
- high-tier source leaks earn infrequent, high-value payouts
- staging nodes earn premium pricing when verification pressure rises

This creates an economy that rewards discretion and repeatability over spectacle.

## Enforcement Exposure

Enforcement rarely reaches all nodes in one operation.

Typical seizure action hits transport and broker layers first. Source and staging layers adapt by rotating channels and shortening provenance chains. This is one reason suppression appears cyclical rather than terminal.

## Strategic Relevance

Understanding node structure changes policy design.

If enforcement targets only final sellers, system regeneration is rapid. If policy aligns seizure triggers with [[Counterfeit Grimoire Verification Failures]] and provenance-stage signatures, disruption lasts longer and raises replacement cost.
