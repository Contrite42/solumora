Pale spells occupy the same [[Control Tier]] ceiling as [[Mythic Grimoire|Mythic]] — T9, Conduits-only — but they are not simply powerful. They are spells that touch something beyond the normal boundaries of [[Flux]]. Where Mythic spells demand everything a Conduit has, Pale spells demand something more and give something back that does not feel entirely like [[Flux]].

Most Scholars who have studied Pale-class effects agree on two things: they should be documented, and they should not be cast lightly. The effects of Pale spells do not merely affect the target. They leave marks on the casting site, on the caster, and in some recorded cases, on the fabric of local Flux itself — residual distortions that persist long after the spell has ended.

The name comes from older scholarship. The word *pale* was used historically to describe light that comes from no identifiable source. The analogy was considered apt.
