**Drest** is the Archduke of [[Auralis]], the loudest voice on [[Cassia|Cassia's]] council, and the man who has shaped the kingdom's direction for the past twenty-three years. He did not plan any of this. That is, depending on how you look at it, either the most damning thing about him or the most human.

## Background

Drest was close to the queen who preceded [[Cassia]] — close enough that when she died in childbirth, he was the person who moved first. Whether that movement came from grief, from love, from a sense of obligation, or from something more calculated is a question that has never been answered cleanly. Possibly because the answer is all of those things at once, in proportions that shifted over the years until Drest himself may no longer be certain.

He swept the power vacuum before the old council could consolidate, installed the infant [[Cassia]] on the throne, and set himself up as the de facto steward of [[Auralis]]. His stated intention was to hold the kingdom intact until she was old enough to rule it.

He was not cut out for it. He is a man of conviction and loyalty and considerable personal force, none of which are the same thing as political competence. [[The Council of Auralis]] that now runs [[Auralis]] did not defeat him. They simply outmaneuvered someone who had never been trained for the space he was occupying, one slow degree at a time, until the kingdom's direction belonged to them and Drest's loudness was the primary thing he had left.

He is still the loudest. He has not stopped believing that means something.

## The Question

[[Cassia]] suspects Drest is her father. She has never confirmed it. She will never confirm it — there is no path to confirmation that does not cost more than the answer is worth, and the answer would not change anything that matters about her situation either way.

Drest has never said anything directly. He has, over twenty-three years, made choices that are difficult to explain except through that lens — small protections, certain interventions, the particular quality of his attention when she is in the room. Whether this constitutes evidence or simply the behavior of a man who feels responsible for a child he helped put on a throne, [[Cassia]] cannot say.

She has stopped trying to decide. The ambiguity is uncomfortable but stable. Certainty in either direction would be worse.

## On the Council

Drest leads the Culmination Faction within [[The Council of Auralis]] — the faction pursuing the recovery of whatever the ancient civilization in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] built. He believes, with the particular intensity of someone who has organized his life around a belief for twenty years, that the ruins hold the key to something that defeats natural decline. He funds expeditions personally when the council budget runs short. Several have not returned. He considers this the cost of the work.

His [[Flux]] tier is T6 — high enough to have genuine capability, not high enough to be unsettling. He uses it practically and without display, which is unusual for someone operating in [[Auralis]]'s prestige-oriented culture and has occasionally led people to underestimate him.

The [[Rift Incursions|incursion problem]] at the northern frontier is known to him. His position is consistent with the faction's broader priorities: the rifts are adjacent to the anomalous sites, active rift zones represent Flux densities relevant to what the ruins were built to do, and closing them is not straightforwardly in the Culmination faction's interest. He does not discuss this position openly. He does not need to. The council's inaction speaks clearly enough without anyone having to explain it.

## Relationship with Cassia

Drest is one of three council members who back [[Cassia]] — and he is the loud one, which means his support is visible and therefore complicated. When he advocates for her in council chambers it draws attention to the fact that she needs advocating for. When he overrides other members on her behalf it reminds everyone present that the override comes from him, not from her.

He believes he is helping. He is not entirely wrong. He is also not entirely right, and [[Cassia]] understands this with a clarity she has never been able to express to him directly.

They have a relationship that neither of them has the vocabulary for. He attends every formal occasion she hosts. He is always the last to leave.
