Reth Caln is fifty-one and has been working the territory between [[Greyveil]] and the northern reaches since he was seventeen. He traps the winter circuit, guides parties into the far north when hired, and does contract clearing work for settlements attempting to establish themselves in the forest margins above the settled belt. He is [[Control Tier|T1]], assessed at ten, used in his trapping practice without instruction, continued without instruction for thirty-four years. The application is his own — a form of extended environmental awareness that lets him read sign at range, which is not a technique he has seen described in any formal casting curriculum.

His reputation on the northern run is specific: he knows the terrain between Greyveil and the approaches to [[Wolfpoint]] better than anyone currently working it. He is reliable in the conditions that end other guides' willingness to continue. He has opinions about weather, route conditions, the decisions that get people into trouble above the treeline, and the reliability of various accounts of the far north that circulate in Greyveil's wayhouse circuit. He shares these opinions without being asked. The opinions are frequently accurate.

He has taken five parties to Wolfpoint over his career. Each time the arrangement has been the same: he brings them to a point two days south of Wolfpoint's approaches, gives them everything he knows about what follows from that point, and turns back. What that line represents — practical boundary, personal rule, something else — he has not said, and people who have spent extended time in the field with him tend not to ask.

He winters in a settlement two days north of Greyveil. In spring he moves with the trapping season. He maintains no permanent address in any city, which is less unusual in the northern reaches than it would be further south.
