# Care Triage Workflows

**Care Triage Workflows** are district-level rules used by clinics and infirmaries to prioritize treatment when demand exceeds practitioner throughput.

In practice, these workflows determine who is treated today, who is deferred, and who is redirected to lower-capacity follow-up channels.

## Decision Layers

Most triage systems in [[Clinical Care Systems]] now operate across three layers:

- immediate stabilization (high-risk injury, acute infection, respiratory failure)
- continuity protection (workers in critical infrastructure roles, dependent-care households)
- deferred management (chronic but non-acute cases with scheduled return)

The framework is operational, not moral. It is designed to preserve minimum district function under load.

## Inputs Used in Triage

Triage officers combine clinical and civic inputs:

- symptom severity and near-term survivability
- current medicine stock constraints
- queue pressure from [[Queue Management Offices]]
- household vulnerability indicators (children, elder dependents, isolation risk)

This blending of medical and social logic improves throughput but can produce inequity when documentation quality varies across districts.

## Throughput Techniques

Common techniques used during surge periods:

- short-cycle stabilization visits with scripted follow-up windows
- rotating practitioner lanes for predictable complaint clusters
- delegated monitoring through trained assistants and temple volunteers
- rapid referral packets for cases likely to fail local treatment

These techniques keep systems moving but increase probability of delayed complications.

## Failure Patterns

Workflow quality degrades when any two conditions overlap:

- staffing loss among T2-T3 practitioners
- stock substitution across multiple medicine classes
- policy volatility in ration and queue rules

At that point, triage shifts from prioritization to delay management.

## Link to Civilian Continuity

Care triage is tightly coupled to [[Ration Substitution Practices]].

When nutrition quality drops and labor demands remain fixed, clinics receive more low-acuity but high-volume cases that consume capacity needed for acute care. This is a key mechanism by which ordinary strain becomes system strain.

## Current Status

Operators describe current workflows as functional but thinning:

- queues are still processed
- high-risk cases are still stabilized
- deferred case return rates are worsening

That pattern signals a system that still works day to day while losing resilience across longer cycles.
