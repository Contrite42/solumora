**Ostal Mrev** is fifty-three years old and has spent thirty years working in infrastructure sigil application, most of it in [[Valdenmoor]] and the surrounding [[Terravelle|Terravellian]] settlements. [[Control Tier|T3]]. He was certified at twenty-two in general structural Sigil work, spent eight years as a journeyman practitioner on mid-scale construction, and then narrowed his practice to the specific category of work where standard approaches fail: sigil arrays in unusual environmental conditions, load distribution configurations that exceed standard documentation parameters, structural work that has to function across temperature ranges or under stress patterns that guild training does not cover.

The distinction he draws is between tier and depth. T3 is his ceiling — he has known this for decades — but within the range of what T3 can do, he has gone further than most T4 practitioners working in general application. This is not a point of pride so much as a practical description. Guild certification tracks tier as a proxy for capability because it is an efficient proxy for most practitioners and most situations. Ostal's situation is simply not most situations.

He is consulted by other practitioners when they encounter work they cannot resolve. He has a set of working principles about consultation: the consultant is there to solve the problem, not to explain why the practitioner should have solved it themselves; the documentation of the solution matters as much as the solution, because someone else will maintain this work in twenty years; the right answer is the one that works in the conditions that actually exist, not the one that would work in the conditions the original specification assumed. He has said versions of these things so often that he has stopped noticing when he says them.

He keeps detailed records of every consultation case he has worked. The records are not for the guild — guild documentation requirements are separate and he meets them separately. The records are for himself, organized by the category of problem rather than by client or date. He has, over thirty years, developed a taxonomy of structural sigil failures that does not exist anywhere in the guild's formal literature. He has not published it because the guild's publication process is not designed for this kind of practical synthesis, and he has not found another venue.
