# One Hundred Twenty Days After the First Casualty - Commission Docket

Esten Morv arrives at the Commission hall with two ledgers, one sealed complaint packet, and the practiced expression of someone who has learned to distrust rooms designed for accountability.

The first public session of the [[Ashford Coexistence Oversight Commission]] starts late because no one can agree where the observer benches begin and where the witness floor ends. The argument is procedural and therefore political.

On today's docket: a municipal detention request from North Ashford filed under "probable concealment risk" against three dock workers accused by rumor of "pattern-consistent non-human behavior." No physical evidence. No incident trigger. No technical referral.

Esten presents the packet anyway.

"If this request is lawful," she says, "your safeguards are decoration."

The Auralis legal delegate asks whether emergency context should allow lower evidentiary thresholds given recent public fear volatility. The Terravelle delegate answers by reading aloud one paragraph from [[The Identification Protocols]] and replacing old terminology with the wording in the new detention request.

The room gets very quiet.

Jess Maren, serving as technical seat this quarter, points to the operative line.

"They're not asking about a breach risk," she says. "They're asking for authority to detain people because their neighbors are anxious. That's not technical governance. That's panic outsourcing."

Vote result: request denied, referral issued for review of municipal drafting office.

The denial matters less than the language attached to it:

**"Rumor-convergent behavioral profile is insufficient basis for detention under coexistence law."

Esten copies the sentence into both ledgers.

Outside, the queue of observers shifts from anger to confusion. Some wanted detentions. Some wanted proof that detentions were impossible. They got procedure, which satisfies no slogan and protects more people than slogans ever will.

By second bell, the Commission clerk pins the ruling to the public board beside shipping notices.

Esten watches a dock foreman read the ruling twice, fold his copy carefully, and walk back toward the river warehouses without speaking.

She marks her final note for the day:

**First safeguard survived first pressure test.**

In this season, survival is progress.
