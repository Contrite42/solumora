# Sixty Minutes After the First Casualty - Bulletin Runner

Harn Vel carried three statements and trusted none of them.

He worked the board circuit between the dock archive, the guild quarter, and the public wall by south market, where official notices went to become rumors with better handwriting.

At first bell after dawn, he was handed a sealed strip stamped urgent class C.

By second bell, the same office handed him a replacement strip with two words changed.

By third bell, a runner from another office tried to buy both copies.

Harn kept walking.

---

Statement one said: "Security exchange at border marker."

Statement two said: "Hostile exchange at border marker."

Statement three, unsigned but clearly drafted by someone with rank, said: "Unauthorized aggression from opposite side."

One word changed budget requests.

One word changed how mothers packed bags.

One word changed how quickly boys with uniforms started believing they would be heroes.

Harn pinned statement one at dock archive because it had signature and timestamp.

He held the other two in his satchel and waited for confirmation from [[Public Information Spaces]] channels.

---

At minute forty-one, an editor from the afternoon broadsheet cornered him outside the market wall.

"Which version is true?" she asked.

"The signed one," Harn said.

"That's not what people are saying."

"People say weather is politics when they need to be angry at sky."

She laughed despite herself.

"You're impossible, Vel."

"I'm employed," he said.

---

At minute fifty-eight, the cross-office confirmation landed:

"One confirmed casualty. Cause pending technical review."

Harn replaced the first notice with the final line and added a chalk marker beneath it:

NO VERIFIED RETALIATION ORDER.

He knew someone would erase that by nightfall.

He also knew a few hundred people would read it before then and decide to wait one more hour before doing something permanent.

For bulletin runners, that counted as success.
