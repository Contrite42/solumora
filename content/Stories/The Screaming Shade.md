# The Screaming Shade

By the time they reached the ruined fort north of [[Halveth]], nobody was speaking above a whisper.

Three days of bad trail and worse anticipation had done that. The fort itself existed in maps as a notation: "possible binding anomaly, survey incomplete, approach with caution." Dorvin, their guide, had walked this route twice before and had offered exactly three explanations across the march to prepare them:

"A binding anomaly," he'd said on day one.

"A screaming thing," he'd said on day two.

"You'll know when you feel it," he'd said on day three, which wasn't helpful but turned out to be accurate.

At the threshold, they felt it.

The scream was not sound. It was pressure. A thought jammed into the skull and twisted until your teeth wanted to rattle.

Kess, T2 [[Control Tier|sense-caster]], stumbled and caught the doorstone.

"Stars," she whispered. "What is that?"

The chamber looked empty for a heartbeat.

Then vision shifted and the center resolved into a bound shape suspended above a carved lattice: human enough to recognize, wrong enough to dread.

Shoulders too narrow.
Arms too long.
Face implied more than seen.

The voice struck all of them at once.

_I am still the strongest._

Malla, their T4, formed ward sigils with shaking fingers. "State identity."

_T9. Master-level. Self-bound. Twenty-three years. No rescue._

Dorvin swallowed hard. "It's genuine," he said, though no one had asked.

---

As they adjusted, details emerged.

Journals stacked against one wall. Burned candle stumps welded to stone. Calibration marks scratched into slate like someone had argued with mathematics until both were exhausted.

Whoever this had been came here as a scholar first.

Kess stepped closer, against instinct. "Can you release yourself?"

_No._

"Can we release you?"

The response was almost laughter and almost grief.

_You are children at a lock built for a king._

Malla extended full sensory range and went pale as old paper.

"It's self-reinforcing," she said. "The bind tightens with time. This is no temporary prison. This is a structure becoming permanent."

_I designed it that way,_ the voice said. _I feared being taken. I did not model being abandoned._

Kess forced herself to ask the obvious.

"Did you send for help? [[Auralis]]? [[Ascendant Path]]?"

The scream sharpened like a blade being drawn.

_I signaled in [[Watts]]. Named myself. Mapped my location. They know._

Silence followed.

Not because no one had words. Because everyone understood the same answer at once.

This was not undiscovered tragedy.
This was selected neglect.

---

They spent three hours trying everything ethical and available below T9 capability.

Probe for weak points.
Test sympathetic release.
Attempt harmonic destabilization.

Every method failed cleanly.

The bound presence explained its own doom with terrifying calm:

_If original caster does not release, release probability trends toward zero. I am original caster. My channels no longer reach exterior action._

Kess asked the question no one wanted answered.

"How long can this continue?"

_Indefinitely._

She almost wished for a finite number. Mercy by expiration would have been kinder than math.

---

When they left, they placed a small offering of [[Watts]] at the edge of the lattice.

Symbolic. Useless. Human.

At the doorway Kess looked back one last time.

The figure remained suspended, still almost-human, still calculating itself toward eternity. The scream continued at a lower register, like machinery operating in total dark.

No one spoke on the road back to Halveth about publishing what they had found.

They all knew that some stories become warnings, some become scandals, and some become burdens carried quietly because there is no version of telling that provides relief.

Behind them, in the ruined fort, a mind too powerful to die and too trapped to live kept counting.
