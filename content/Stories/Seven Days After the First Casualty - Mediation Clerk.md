# Seven Days After the First Casualty - Mediation Clerk

Pavel Ren had processed forty-two dispute filings before midday and rejected eleven for using war language where evidence belonged.

The waiting room in the guild mediation hall sounded like a market argument with uniforms.

Carriers accused inspectors of selective delay.

Inspectors accused carriers of concealment.

Two municipal clerks accused both of rerouting blame into paperwork they did not have staff to absorb.

Pavel stamped each packet with the same sequence code and called cases by number.

Names made people perform. Numbers made them listen for a minute.

---

By day seven, everyone wanted retroactive certainty.

They wanted a single date when normal ended and emergency began.

Pavel knew that was not how collapse worked.

It worked by changed defaults.

Old default: release unless risk was proven.

New default: hold unless innocence was documented twice.

The rule change had never been announced. It had arrived through habit.

He wrote that sentence into three case notes and expected all three to be deleted upstream.

---

At 14:20, a freight broker and a checkpoint officer sat across from each other at Pavel's desk, both furious and both exhausted.

The broker demanded compensation for six days of spoiled shipments.

The officer produced a stack of directives that contradicted one another but all carried legitimate seals.

Pavel did what mediation clerks did when everyone was technically right and operationally doomed.

He built a narrow agreement no one liked.

Partial release by risk class.

Shared verification witnesses.

No public statement assigning fault until [[Border Incident Ledger]] entries reconciled.

Both parties signed because the alternative was losing another week to accusation.

---

At closing bell, Pavel copied the day's settlements and sent summary lines to [[Terravelle Guild Mediation]] and [[Border Coordination Offices]].

He marked trend status:

"Dispute volume stable at crisis level. Compliance possible only with expanded clerical capacity."

Stable at crisis level was the phrase of the week.

It meant systems had not failed.

It also meant they were no longer recovering.
