# Thaw Season Transit

The [[Rift Incursions|rift activity]] forecast for Thawmark transit was listed as "elevated but manageable" which [[Selt Orvn]] knew from experience meant "worse than optimal but not quite bad enough to forbid."

He stood at the [[Halveth]] staging point with four clients waiting for his assessment. They'd paid for [[Standard Guided Crossing]] service during peak season rates, but peak season had filled completely and they'd been offered a Thawmark window instead — marginal timing, reduced rate, higher risk.

All four had accepted. Now they were looking at him to confirm whether accepting had been wise.

"Forecast is what it is," Selt said. "Rift encounters are more frequent this time of year. We'll have longer detection intervals and more route diversions. Crossing time extends from five days to potentially seven or eight."

[[Mira Kess]], [[Control Tier|T2]], asked the obvious question. "How much more dangerous?"

"Fifteen to twenty percent casualty rate variance compared to optimal window. That's historical average."

"From what baseline?"

"Five percent baseline in optimal season. Thawmark sits around twenty to twenty-five percent."

The four clients looked at each other. [[Vren Mast]], T1, spoke for the group. "We knew the season was marginal when we booked. If you're willing to guide, we're willing to cross."

Selt nodded. That was the answer he'd expected. Nobody paid crossing fees and traveled to Halveth staging only to cancel at the final assessment. "Then we leave in four hours. Final equipment check now. If your [[Grimoires|grimoire]] bindings are questionable, replace them. If your water seals are old, replace them. I'm not stopping mid-crossing for preventable equipment failure."

They scattered to handle final preparations. Selt reviewed his own gear — all maintained to reliability standards that assumed worst-case scenarios. Thawmark wasn't worst-case, but it was close enough that preparation mattered.

---

**Day Two.** The first rift manifestation appeared six hours into the second day's travel, earlier than optimal-season patterns but within Thawmark expectations.

Selt detected it at three hundred meters — a spatial distortion that registered on his [[Control Tier|T3]] [[Flux]] sensitivity as wrongness in the ambient field. He stopped the group immediately.

"Rift ahead. Three hundred meters, stationary or slow-moving. We divert east."

The diversion added ninety minutes to the day's route and put them behind the planned camp point. Not catastrophic, but not good. Each delay compounded, and Thawmark margins didn't tolerate much compounding.

They made camp at dusk, two hours late. [[Kess Vehl]], T1, asked the question everyone was thinking. "How many more diversions?"

"In Thawmark? Expect three to five major diversions over full crossing. We've had one."

"So we're still within tolerance?"

"For now."

---

**Day Four.** By the fourth day they'd encountered four rift diversions and one near-manifestation that had resolved before requiring route change. They were now sixteen hours behind optimal schedule, but still within the extended Thawmark timeline.

The weather had also degraded. [[Equatorial Desert|Desert]] weather during Thawmark was unstable — temperature swings, unexpected wind, occasional dust storms. They were experiencing all three.

During the afternoon, a dust front moved in fast enough that Selt had to make a shelter-or-push decision with incomplete information. Sheltering meant burning four to six hours. Pushing meant reduced visibility and higher rift-detection failure risk.

He chose sheltering.

They dug into a rock formation lee side and waited out the dust. It cleared after five hours. They lost the remainder of the day's travel schedule but avoided potential rift encounters in compromised visibility, which would have been worse.

Mira asked the updated question during evening meal. "Still on viable timeline?"

"Barely. We need the next two days to go well."

---

**Day Six.** The next two days did not go well.

Day five brought three additional rift diversions and a water container seal failure that cost them two liters — not catastrophic because Selt maintained reserve margin, but reserve margin was called reserve specifically because using it meant operating without safety buffer.

Day six brought a rift manifestation that appeared inside their detection perimeter, close enough that [[Vren Mast]] had to execute emergency [[Flux]]-displacement to avoid contact. He succeeded — barely — but the strain left him depleted and shaking for two hours afterward.

They were now twenty-eight hours behind schedule, through three-quarters of their water, and still a day and a half from southern staging.

Selt gathered the group during midday rest. "Current status: we're behind on timeline, into reserve water, and facing one more day-plus of rift-territory travel. Conditions are marginal. We can continue with elevated risk or we can emergency-signal for extraction and abort."

The four clients conferred. Vren, who'd been closest to actual rift contact, spoke first. "I vote continue. We're closer to completion than abort at this point."

Mira nodded agreement. Kess and the fourth client — [[Tann Moss]], T2, who'd been quiet most of the crossing — both confirmed.

"Continue," Selt said. "Reserve rations from here forward. We finish tomorrow or we don't."

---

**Day Seven.** They finished.

The southern staging point came into view at mid-afternoon on day seven — thirty-four hours behind optimal schedule, with reserve water at twenty percent and every member of the party exhausted past the point where exhaustion felt like a meaningful word.

But they emerged. All five people who'd entered the [[Equatorial Desert|desert]] from [[Halveth]] exited at [[Seln Watch]]. No casualties. No major injuries. Thawmark transit completed.

Selt filed the standard completion documentation, noting the extended timeline, the rift encounter frequency, and Vren's near-contact incident. The documentation went into the seasonal pattern analysis that [[Halveth Cooperative]] used to refine future Thawmark risk assessments.

The four clients collected their verified crossing certification and dispersed. Mira paid an additional completion bonus that Selt hadn't expected but accepted. Vren admitted that he'd underestimated Thawmark difficulty and wouldn't book marginal-season again. Kess said she'd do it again if she had to but preferred not to. Tann said nothing, paid on time, and left.

---

**Three days later.** Selt was back in Halveth coordinating the next expedition when he saw the seasonal casualty report. Thawmark crossings that season: twenty-three attempted, four with casualties, two fatalities.

His crossing had been among the nineteen that completed without casualty. That meant he'd performed within acceptable standards during elevated-risk conditions. It also meant that five other guides hadn't, and two people were dead because of rift encounters during a season that everyone involved had known was marginal.

The clients had known. The guides had known. The coordination staff had known. They'd all chosen to proceed anyway because Thawmark was marginal, not impossible, and marginal meant most people survived.

Most.

Selt filed his next expedition intake forms and waited for the next group of clients who'd accept marginal-season timing to save on fees. They'd come. They always did.

And he'd guide them, because that was the job. And most of them would make it.

Most.
