# Sixty-Seven Days After the First Casualty - Fifth Bell Declaration

By fifth bell, both commands have accelerated.

Terravelle pushes heavy ward units north of Ashford despite Jess's warning package. Auralis moves dimension specialists into the staging flats under emergency authority language so broad it reads like panic with seals attached. The corridor model closes its remaining uncertainty and lands exactly where the team predicted: overlap inside forty-eight hours.

Cassia stands in a side chamber of the Auralis civic hall with Merra, Jess, Kess, and Teren Voss, listening to aides argue in three adjacent rooms about legal posture while the world edges toward structural failure.

"If I issue a unilateral suspension order, the Council claims I exceeded ceremonial authority," Cassia says. "If I wait for formal vote, we run out of time before paper reaches the floor."

Jess answers first. "Then this is no longer a constitutional problem. It is a survival problem with constitutional paperwork attached."

Kess spreads the latest threshold chart across the table. "The next synchronized discharge cycle hits before dawn tomorrow. If corridor load spikes as forecast, first linked failure could open inside six hours of that."

Merra looks to Teren. "How much can you hold if it starts?"

Teren does not look away. "One breach, maybe two, temporarily. Not a cascade front across a live corridor."

Cassia exhales once, slow, then straightens. "Then we stop pretending this can be handled privately."

At fifth bell she walks onto the public balcony above the lower court, where municipal clerks, transport coordinators, and off-duty guards have gathered after rumors of emergency movement orders. The square below is not full, but it is enough. More importantly, it has scribes.

She does not read from prepared text.

"Auralis citizens," she says, voice carrying over rain and stone, "I am issuing immediate suspension of high-tier deployment through the northern corridor pending joint dimensional safety review with Terravelle. This is not negotiation weakness. This is casualty prevention."

The crowd murmurs, then sharpens into attention as she continues.

"You were not given full information about rift risk. I was not given full information either. That ends now."

She turns slightly and nods to Teren.

He steps into the open.

At first, nothing visible happens. Then the air around him bends in a narrow, controlled ring like heat over iron. Not spectacle. Proof. Enough for every trained practitioner in hearing range to feel the pressure differential and know it is not human-tier output.

Teren speaks plainly.

"My name is Verath. I am one of five surviving High Demons. If both kingdoms continue current movement, you will trigger a linked rift cascade that neither military can survive."

The square does not erupt. It freezes.

For two centuries, High Demons have been rumor, warning tale, suppressed footnote. Now one stands under municipal lanterns with a queen beside him and says the part no one can process fast enough.

Jess mutters, almost to herself, "Good. Shock buys minutes."

Merra is already moving. "Send the signal package to Terravelle now. Full release, no redactions."

Kess hands Cassia the pre-authored technical brief with two signatures added in wet ink: Kess Tarren and Jess Maren. Cassia signs beneath them and stamps emergency authority before anyone in the chamber can stop her.

Within nine minutes, copies are in three courier chains headed north.

Within twenty, Council members begin calling for containment and arrest.

Within thirty, every command office in both kingdoms receives the same message: suspend corridor deployment or accept immediate cascade probability above survivable threshold.

By sixth bell, no one can claim they were not warned.
