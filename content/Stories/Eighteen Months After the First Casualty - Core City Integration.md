# Eighteen Months After the First Casualty - Core City Integration

The [[Auralis]] [[Aldrane Academy]] lecture hall held two hundred students for [[Cassia]]'s public appearance, which the [[Ashford Coexistence Oversight Commission|Commission]] had carefully branded as "institutional dialogue" rather than royal visit. Full Integration Zone protocols meant High Demons were expected to participate in public institutional functions without special accommodation.

[[Teren Voss|Verath]] sat three rows back. Visible, but not prominent.

"The [[Corridor Stabilization Treaty|Treaty]] establishes mutual rift monitoring because neither kingdom's observation network was complete," Cassia explained to the assembled students. She'd gotten better at public speaking over eighteen months—less apologetic, more direct. "[[Auralis]] had depth of historical data. [[Terravelle]] had geographic coverage across their northern territories. Combining them required admitting both kingdoms had gaps."

A student raised her hand. "But the Treaty also requires disclosure about High Demon expertise contributions. Why does that matter?"

Cassia glanced toward Verath with the ease of eighteen months' practice at this exact dynamic. He stood.

"Because rift prediction accuracy improved forty-seven percent when [[Joint Rift Monitoring Cell|Monitoring Cell]] staff had access to long-term observation data," Verath said. The lecture hall's acoustics carried his voice clearly. "I've been watching rift patterns for roughly four hundred years. That's a useful data set. The Treaty requires that both kingdoms know when they're using it."

"So we're dependent on—" The student hesitated over the phrasing.

"On me? Yes, for that particular expertise. The same way you're dependent on [[Aldrane Academy]] instructors for runic theory, or [[Terravelle]] guilds for northern climate adaptation knowledge." Verath's tone remained level. "Expertise dependence is normal. The Treaty just makes it transparent."

Another student: "But you were _hiding_ for centuries. Why should we trust—"

"You shouldn't," Verath interrupted calmly. "Trust is earned. I'm earning it by showing up to lectures like this, publishing my rift observation methods, and letting both kingdoms verify my predictions against ground truth data. Eighteen months of that earns some credibility. Four hundred years of hiding costs it. The math is working itself out in public view."

Cassia let the silence hold for a moment. The lecture hall's audience—T0 through T4 students, mix of [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] citizens, average age nineteen—processed the exchange with visible uncertainty. Full Integration meant this was supposed to be normal. The students' expressions suggested it still felt strange.

"The [[Regional Enforcement Variance Doctrine|Variance Doctrine]] creates three implementation tiers," Cassia continued, pulling focus back to governing framework. "Full Integration, Transitional Compliance, and Protected Observation. This academy is in a Full Integration Zone. That means High Demon participation in institutional functions is expected—not special, not celebrated, just normal. Does anyone here find that actively uncomfortable?"

Three hands went up. Cassia nodded. "Thank you for being honest. The [[Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum|Coexistence Curriculum]] includes provisions for students who need processing time. You can access counseling resources or alternative formats for this material. The goal isn't forcing comfort—it's establishing that the institutional framework holds whether you're comfortable or not."

One of the students who'd raised their hand: "What if we're never comfortable?"

"Then you might choose to live in a Protected Observation Zone, where integration requirements are minimal." Cassia's tone remained matter-of-fact. "Or you might stay in a Full Integration Zone and remain uncomfortable while following the behavioral requirements anyway. The [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal|Tribunal]] cares about actions, not feelings."

"That seems..." The student trailed off.

"Harsh?" Cassia finished. "Maybe. The alternative is making feelings a requirement for institutional participation, which means segregation with official blessing. The Variance Doctrine already creates geographic segregation. The Commission won't extend that to feelings-based sorting."

Verath spoke again from his position in the audience. "I've lived through a two-century [[The Persecution Era|persecution cycle]]. Housing segregation, employment discrimination, organized hunts. All of it justified by how people felt about immortal beings existing near them. If the choice is between your discomfort and my safety, current governing framework picks my safety. You can disagree with that. You can't act on it violently."

The lecture hall's atmosphere shifted—not quite hostile, but testing boundaries. An older student, T4 tier by the control-density signature: "You're saying we have to accept immortal beings with power we can't match just...existing in our institutions?"

"Yes," Verath said simply. "The same way T0 citizens accept T4 casters like you existing in institutions without power-matching. Society works when institutional behavior requirements apply regardless of power tier. That's not new. What's new is applying it to immortals."

Cassia watched the dynamic carefully. Full Integration meant having these conversations publicly, repeatedly, until they stopped feeling revolutionary. Eighteen months had moved them from "absolutely not" to "uncomfortable but functional." The Commission projected another eighteen months to reach "normal with periodic friction."

The question was whether uncomfortable-but-functional was stable long-term, or just a transitional state masking deeper resistance.

"Final question before we move to institutional mechanics," Cassia said. "Does seeing High Demon participation in public governance make anyone here more or less likely to support the current framework?"

Four hands: more supportive. Seven hands: less supportive. The rest—nearly two hundred students—stayed neutral or uncertain.

"That distribution has held stable for six months," Cassia observed. "The Commission calls it progress. The [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal|Tribunal]] calls it disappointing. Both are probably right."

She spent the next ninety minutes walking through Treaty provisions, Commission oversight mechanisms, and Tribunal enforcement protocols. Verath answered technical questions about rift monitoring when called on. The lecture hall gradually shifted from testing boundaries to taking notes.

By the end, it looked like institutional education. Not revolutionary. Not inspiring.

Just the slow, repetitive work of making coexistence normal enough that the next generation might forget it was ever controversial.

Full Integration Zone protocols counted that as success. Whether it was justice remained the question nobody could answer at eighteen months.
