# The Three Principles

[[Rett Soln]] watched the three apprentices arrange their gear at the staging point north of [[Halveth]] and knew immediately which one would make a mistake first. Not because [[Kess Vorn]] was careless — she wasn't — but because she was methodical in exactly the way that led to overlooking the obvious when conditions changed.

"Packs ready?" Rett called.

All three confirmed. [[Vren Tass]], [[Control Tier|T1]], had his water secured and his [[Grimoires|grimoire]] satchel positioned correctly. [[Mira Holst]], T1, had tied off her secondary supplies with the quick-release knots he'd taught them. Kess had everything in the right place, checked twice, documented in her field log.

And her primary grimoire was still in the satchel instead of in her hand.

Rett walked over. "What's the first thing you do when you leave staging?"

Kess blinked. "Confirm heading and check weather."

"What's in your hand while you do that?"

She looked down at her empty hand, then at the satchel. "Oh."

"Not oh. Why?"

"Because if something happens in the first hundred meters, I need immediate access."

"And if your grimoire's in the satchel?"

"I lose time getting it out."

"How much time?"

"Four, five seconds."

"How long does it take a rift manifestation to close distance in open terrain?"

Kess's expression shifted. "Two seconds."

"So if you're in the satchel when you should be in your hand, you're already—"

"Dead," Kess finished. She pulled the grimoire out, checked the binding, and held it ready position. "Won't happen again."

"It will," Rett said. "But not today." He looked at the other two. "Same applies. Grimoires out, accessible, checked for damage. If your binding's compromised, you tell me now, not when we're three hours in and something tests it."

All three checked their grimoires. No damage. Good enough.

---

**Principle One: Distance Management.** They walked north into the boundary zone between settled land and the [[Equatorial Desert|desert proper]]. This wasn't the deep crossing lanes — those required specialist coordination and full expedition protocols. This was the accessible margin where apprentices could learning [[Flux]] discipline without committing to multi-week traverses.

Dangerous enough to matter. Controlled enough to survive mistakes.

Rett stopped them at the first marker cairn, two miles from staging. "Vren. How far back is Halveth?"

"Two miles, give or take."

"How long to return if we have to?"

"Thirty minutes fast walk, twenty if we run."

"How long do you have if you're injured and bleeding?"

Vren hesitated. "Depends on the injury."

"Worst case."

"Five, ten minutes before shock."

"So if you're hurt badly enough that you can't walk, what happens?"

"Someone has to carry me or I don't make it."

"Which means distance is now everyone's problem, not just yours. Principle One: every step you take away from staging is a step someone else might have to carry you back. You don't take that step unless the value justifies the risk."

Mira raised her hand. "How do we measure value?"

"You don't," Rett said. "You make the best assessment you can with the information available and accept that you might be wrong. That's why we practice — so your assessments improve."

He waved them forward. They continued north.

---

**Principle Two: Flux Conservation.** At the three-mile marker, Rett called a halt and pointed at a cluster of rocks fifty meters west. "Vren, move that rock pile ten meters south."

Vren looked at the pile, then at Rett. "Using [[Flux]]?"

"How else?"

"I could walk over and move them by hand."

"And?"

"That doesn't use Flux at all. Saves capacity."

"So do it."

Vren walked to the rocks, moved them manually, and returned. Rett nodded. "Principle Two: [[Flux]] is a limited resource. Your [[Control Tier|tier]] gives you a maximum capacity, but every casting depletes it. If you use Flux for something you can do without it, you have less Flux when you actually need it. What does that mean?"

Kess answered. "We conserve Flux for situations where nothing else works."

"Exactly. You don't cast light when you have a lantern. You don't move objects with Flux when you have hands. You don't waste capacity showing off. Out here, conservation is survival."

---

**Principle Three: Failure Recognition.** At the four-mile marker, Rett stopped them again and pulled a practice [[Sigils|sigil]] plate from his pack — a basic heat-generation design, deliberately flawed. "Kess, channel Flux into this and activate it."

Kess took the plate, examined it briefly, and began channeling. The sigil lit, flickered, and died. She tried again. Same result.

"It's not working," she said.

"Why not?"

"The...the binding's wrong? Or the Flux input is incorrect?"

"Try again."

She tried four more times. Each time the sigil failed. Finally she stopped and looked at Rett. "I can't make it work."

"Why are you still trying?"

"Because you told me to activate it."

"I told you to try. I didn't tell you to succeed. Principle Three: when something doesn't work after multiple attempts, recognize the failure and stop. Out here, persistence in the face of obvious failure doesn't make you determined. It makes you dead. If a [[Grimoires|grimoire]] won't cast, you switch methods. If a route won't clear, you find another one. If a situation won't improve, you retreat. Do you understand?"

Kess nodded slowly. "It's not about succeeding. It's about recognizing when you can't and adapting."

"Yes. And that's harder than it sounds, because adaptation means admitting you were wrong about your initial approach. Most people die still committed to the plan that stopped working three steps back."

---

**Return.** They walked back to staging point, slower than they'd left. Rett didn't push pace. The purpose of the exercise wasn't distance. It was principles.

At the staging point, he watched the three of them repack their gear. This time Kess kept her grimoire out until everything else was secured, then placed it in the satchel last, on top, easily accessible.

Small improvement. It would matter.

"Questions?" Rett asked.

Vren spoke first. "How long does it take to internalize these principles?"

"Depends. Some people get it in a season. Some never do."

"And the ones who never do?"

"They stop doing this work. One way or another."

The three apprentices looked at each other. Rett didn't elaborate. They were smart enough to understand what "one way or another" meant out here.

Mira spoke next. "When do we try an actual crossing?"

"When I stop seeing you make mistakes during practice runs. Could be three months. Could be a year. Could be never if you don't improve."

"That's direct."

"That's the job." Rett slung his pack. "Next session is in six days. Same time. If your grimoire needs maintenance, handle it before then. If your Flux capacity feels strained, rest. Don't show up unprepared and expect me to compensate."

He left them at the staging point and walked back toward [[Halveth]]. Behind him, the three apprentices began their own return journey, talking among themselves in the way apprentices did when they thought the instructor was out of earshot.

He heard Kess say: "I almost died having my grimoire in the wrong place."

"Almost," Vren said. "But you didn't."

"This time."

Rett kept walking. The not-dying-this-time part was the lesson. Eventually either it stuck or it didn't. His job was to teach the principle and watch what they did with it.

Most of them learned. The ones who didn't stopped showing up, one way or another.

That was how it worked out here. It always had been.
