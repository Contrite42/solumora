# Twenty-Four Hours After the First Casualty - Road Dispatcher

Varn Hesk ran the road board at Ashford East with chalk, string, and a temper famous enough to keep merchants honest.

By the second morning after the first casualty, his board looked like a broken loom.

Routes crossed out.

Convoys split.

Three departure lanes marked HOLD until escort status clarified.

Every driver in line said the same thing differently: "If we do not move now, we lose the contract."

Varn pointed at the red stripe under the clock.

"If we move blind now, we lose the drivers."

---

At 07:30 he issued stagger rules.

No convoy over six carts.

No mixed-origin cargo without precleared papers.

No night departures without corridor confirmation from [[Border Coordination Offices]].

The guild rep called it overreaction.

Varn slid a casualty notice across the desk and said, "Overreaction is writing apology letters to households from memory because your manifests burned in panic."

The rep signed.

---

By noon, [[Maren Freight Chain]] operators requested emergency corridor priority for grain and medical stock.

Varn approved half and deferred half.

He knew exactly what that meant: one district would complain about empty shelves tonight while another praised efficiency on tomorrow's bulletin.

Dispatch was choosing who felt the shortage first.

Nobody said that part out loud.

---

At 15:10 a courier arrived with two contradictory movement advisories, each marked urgent, each stamped by legitimate offices that no longer agreed on sequencing.

Varn pinned both to the board and wrote between them:

"Until reconciled, lowest-risk interpretation applies."

A young driver asked what that meant in plain words.

"It means you arrive late," Varn said. "It also means you arrive."

---

At 21:00 he reviewed closure count.

Seventeen runs delayed.

Five rerouted.

Zero confirmed convoy losses.

He accepted the insults from contractors as proof the system was still human enough to blame someone.

If the shouting stopped, that would mean no one expected roads to work at all.

Before locking up, he reset first departures for dawn and left one note for day shift:

"Protect continuity, not pride. Pride does not feed districts."
