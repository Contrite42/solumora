# Fifty-Eight Days After the First Casualty - Lantern Stacks

Greyveil's Lantern Stacks district is the wrong place for clean decisions. Too many narrow lanes, too many private archives, too many doors that do not open for strangers.

Merra walks in first. Jess follows with a satchel of ledgers she refuses to leave unattended. Kess follows last, scanning signage for old trade marks the way other people scan for exits.

The bookstore is smaller than Merra expected.

"Voss Antiquarian and Records," painted in fading cream on dark wood. Window glass rippled with age. Inside, shelves in disciplined rows and one man in a charcoal vest repairing a binding under yellow light.

"We're closed," he says without looking up.

Merra places the unsigned note from Dry Warehouse Nine on the counter.

"Then this can wait until morning," she says.

The man looks up, sees the note, and sets the binding needle down with precise care.

"You should not have brought that here," he says.

Kess steps forward, puts the Site Seventeen pre-edit copy beside the note, and watches his eyes.

"Neither should you," she says.

For five seconds, no one speaks.

Then he locks the front door, turns the sign to CLOSED, and leads them through a rear curtain into a narrow records room with a single table.

"My name is Teren Voss," he says. "For tonight, that is enough."

Jess opens her ledger to the failure window page and pushes it across.

"We need confirmation or correction. No riddles."

Teren scans the page once.

"Your window is optimistic," he says. "If either side accelerates under political pressure after the next casualty bulletin, subtract six days."

Merra feels cold along her spine.

"How do you know that?"

Teren does not answer directly.

"Because I have watched this pattern complete before."

Kess folds her arms. "Before when?"

"Before your institutions existed in their current form."

Jess leans back, expression flat. "That's either metaphor or a confession."

"It is a warning," Teren says. "And you are out of procedural time."

He lays three archival strips on the table: old monitoring traces, hand-copied centuries apart, same curve shape as Jess's modern corridor model. Kess reads one and pales.

"These dates... this is Persecution Era indexing."

Teren meets her gaze. "Yes."

Merra hears the room narrow to her own breathing.

"Who are you?"

Teren rests both hands on the table, as if deciding where to place weight.

"Someone who stayed hidden because that kept people alive. Someone now choosing the opposite because hidden won't hold through a cascade."

No glow, no dramatic reveal, no surge of theatrical power. Just a sentence that lands like structural stress.

Jess closes her ledger slowly.

"If you're telling the truth, we have one job," she says. "Keep the corridor from snapping long enough for whatever you can do to matter."

Teren nods once.

"Then we begin tonight."

Merra looks from Jess to Kess to the man who has just told them history is still walking around under another name.

She has led rescues in storms, collapses, and night crossings where the stars vanished under dust.

This feels the same.

Right people. Wrong hour. No replacement crew coming.

"Fine," she says. "No more waiting rooms. Give us the real map."

Outside, Lantern Stacks goes quiet under rain.

Inside, the first direct alliance forms between three exhausted humans and a survivor from the part of history everyone decided was finished.
