# Sixty Minutes After the First Name - Patrol Clerk

Teren Mall did not hear the shot.

He heard the radio cough, then the second voice speaking too evenly.

"Marker C confirmed one down. Repeat, one down."

Teren had spent nine years turning border chaos into lines on paper. He knew the tone. Calm voice, fast breathing. The kind people use when they are trying not to become the story.

He opened the incident ledger before command asked.

Time field first.

Then location.

Then status: UNVERIFIED CASUALTY REPORT.

He stared at the blinking cursor where cause belonged and left it blank.

---

At minute twelve, three versions arrived.

Version one: warning shot ricochet.

Version two: weapon discharge during reposition.

Version three: hostile fire from opposite embankment.

Each version came from someone reliable. Each version made a different future.

Teren attached all three and tagged them SEQUENCE HOLD under [[Border De-Escalation Protocols]].

The major wanted a single line for external distribution.

"Write defensive engagement," he said.

"Not yet," Teren answered.

"You are a clerk."

"I am a clerk who has to sign this after your mood changes in daylight."

The major swore and left.

Teren kept typing.

---

At minute twenty-eight, a liaison from [[Border Coordination Offices]] pushed through the office door with a courier slate and a face gone colorless.

"South side reports one casualty too," she said.

No one asked whether it was true.

No one asked whether it was connected.

In that room, everyone understood the same equation: one death could be procedure; two deaths became symmetry; symmetry became narrative; narrative became orders.

Teren copied both entries into the shared format and added the only sentence he trusted:

"Cause classification pending bilateral technical reconciliation."

He expected that sentence to be ignored by noon.

He wrote it anyway because records were the only thing slower than panic.

---

At minute fifty-six, command released a statement.

"Border security incident under review."

It did not include names.

By then, names were already moving through barracks, homes, and market lanes faster than official text could travel.

Teren closed the first packet and opened the second.

He changed the status line from UNVERIFIED to CONFIRMED CASUALTY, singular, then paused over the plural form waiting in the next row.

He had one hour of facts and a week of consequences.
