# The Crossing That Failed

They planned the crossing for eight months, and by every formal metric they planned it well.

Three [[Control Tier|T4]] practitioners. Two T3 support specialists. Seventeen sealed provision tubes. Two redundant route sets. One veteran [[Standard Guided Crossing|guide]] named Doren Pike, seven previous Zakros passages, no client fatalities.

When they left the last stone marker behind and stepped into the open burn of the [[Equatorial Desert|Zakros]], everyone carried the calm that comes from preparation.

By day six, that calm had hairline fractures.

---

The first [[Rift Incursions|incursion]] appeared as a pale distortion three kilometers east, low on the horizon, like heat shimmer that refused to move with the wind. Doren studied it through a brass sighting tube and said, "We keep course. Edge transit only. No heroics."

No one argued. Their models said they could pass before the growth arc reached their corridor.

Day seven: Leth Varn, lead environmental sensor, started checking bearings every eleven minutes instead of every hour.

Day eight: she recast the same perimeter scan four times in one watch and forgot she'd done it.

Day nine: she woke the camp before dawn because she was certain the sand under them had shifted by a full degree south. It had not.

"I can't stop hearing it," she told Rynn, the senior coordinator, voice raw and too fast. "It's like the rift is just behind my shoulder."

Rynn took Doren aside while the others pretended not to watch.

"Contamination," Doren said. "Partial channel intersection. She's still functional, but not for command calls." 

There was no good option. Retreat meant turning back through unstable margins. Continuing meant trusting a team with its strongest sensor mentally unraveling.

They voted by protocol.

Continue, slow pace, shared watch on Leth's casting authority.

Leth voted to retreat and then laughed at herself for voting at all. "Don't count mine," she said. "I wouldn't trust me either."

---

On day ten they found a dead practitioner from another route, half-buried, gear straps fused by heat.

No one touched the body. In Zakros, recovery decisions can kill the living. They marked coordinates, logged identifying details, and moved.

Doren said, "Not our concern," in the same tone he'd use for weather updates, because if he said it with feeling the team might stop.

That night Leth did not sleep. She sat inside the perimeter ward and whispered reserve calculations to herself until sunrise.

Day eleven they made good distance, enough that some optimism came back around the edges.

Day twelve killed that optimism in under five seconds.

---

They were moving through an apparently clean stretch, five kilometers from safer ground. Leth had just handed over scan duty to Tera Noll and stepped off-line for water.

Then she went still.

"There," she said, pointing at empty air.

"There's nothing there," Tera replied.

Leth took three steps forward anyway.

Doren shouted.

Rynn lunged and missed her sleeve by inches.

The rift opened where no signature had registered twenty minutes earlier: a vertical seam the color of old glass, thin as a door crack and wide enough to erase a person.

Leth Varn touched it with her left shoulder and disappeared as if the world had edited her out.

No scream. No blood. No body.

Just four people and a guide standing in sunlight that suddenly felt too bright.

---

They crossed out of Zakros with reserves still on the books, which meant the mission was logged as "successful transit with fatality incident." 

On paper, they had done nearly everything right.

Their report filled twenty-one pages:

- exposure window,
- contamination markers,
- voting protocol,
- route integrity,
- reserve usage,
- post-loss movement discipline.

The final line in Doren Pike's addendum became required reading for new teams:

_Competence does not remove risk. It only narrows the shape of your failure._

Doren refused new contracts for eight months. Rynn never crossed Zakros again. Tera kept crossing and, by her own admission, counted steps out loud any time she passed a silent stretch at midday.

Because once you've watched someone vanish where nothing was, "clear route" becomes a phrase you say for function, not belief.
