# Year 190 of Persecution - The Final Hunt

The last official Lantern Hunt commenced in year 192 of persecution. It was bureaucratically irrelevant: kingdoms had already stopped enforcing Decree prosecutions, magistrates were actively protecting refuge networks, and population consensus had shifted toward treating High Demons as coexistent rather than eliminatable.

But institutional momentum required one more hunt. One more demonstration of commitment to safety. One more grand coordinated sweep to prove the system still functioned.

The refugee known locally as "[[Teren Voss|the merchant prince]]" received warning three weeks before the hunt commenced. His protection network, by this point, was so extensive that early warning was built into magistrate communication itself.

He spent those weeks doing the thing hunters least expected: documentation.

Walked through [[Ashford]]'s markets with careful attention. Visited the estates where refugees worked as household staff. Greeted merchants he'd partnered with. Spent time with the magister who'd protected him for sixty years, having conversations that seemed routine but were actually structured farewells.

Most immortals would have fled. Moved to more secure refuges. Used the warning window to relocate everything.

He moved nothing.

When the Lantern formation swept toward the merchant house where he maintained public residence, they found him in the courtyard. Alone. Visible. Almost deliberately appearing there to be found.

The formation commander recognized him. That was the unusual part. Usually refugees either fled or hid. Didn't stand in courtyards waiting to be captured.

"You understand you're under arrest," the commander said. Not phrased as question.

"I understand there's performance expected," he replied. "Arrest me. Hold me. Conduct your interrogation showing institutional strength. Then release me when political optics no longer require the hunt to continue."

The commander stared at him for a long moment. Then she laughed. Just once. Brief.

"That might actually be true," she said. "You understand that I can't just let you walk."

"I understand completely. This hunt needs prisoners to succeed. Let me be your prisoner for thirty days, and then release me under magistrate mercy provision. Show restraint and institutional health. Everyone gets what they need."

They arrested him. He spent exactly twenty-eight days in holding. Released on magistrate order claiming "insufficient evidence for continued detention." Re-arrested three weeks later in a different jurisdiction, held for nineteen days, released again.

The dance continued for almost two years. Hunts that nominally pursued him but always managed to just miss. Institutional records showing active effort without actual threat.

Institutional theater reaching its final form.

When persecution formally ended, he was still in that cycle. The hunt cycle just stopped rather than reaching conclusion. No ceremonial capture, no resolution, just administrative silence.

He walked out of the last detention—year 200, persecution officially ended—and didn't get arrested again.

The Lantern Hunts hadn't killed the High Demons. They'd transformed into mutual performance. Everyone understood the script. Most understood it was unsustainable.

Nobody knew what came next.
