# One Hundred Eighty Days After the First Casualty - First Appeal

The first major appeal to the [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal]] arrives before the ink dries on the treaty register.

Case APCT-07 concerns a municipal directive authorizing "temporary identity verification sweeps" in two corridor-adjacent wards after a warehouse fire triggered rumor spirals. No technical evidence of non-human involvement. No named suspects. Broad authority to detain for "behavioral incongruity."

In another century, this directive would have become a policy template.
In this one, it hits a hearing bench in forty-six hours.

Jess testifies as technical witness.
"Fire cause was faulty lamp housing and overloaded storage rigging," she says. "No dimensional signature. No anomaly indicators."

Municipal counsel argues that fear volatility itself is a security threat requiring preventive tools.

The presiding chair asks one question:
"Preventive against what, specifically, and proven by which evidence?"

Counsel answers with neighborhood rumor density maps.

Kess, invited as historical-pattern expert, places two charts side by side:
- current directive language,
- archived wording from [[The Lantern Decrees]].

The overlap is too obvious for rhetoric to hide.

"This is not innovation," she says. "This is recurrence with cleaner formatting."

Verdict: directive enjoined, municipal office sanctioned, mandatory policy rewrite ordered under APCT supervision.

The published opinion includes a line that circulates across both kingdoms by sunset:

**"Collective anxiety is not evidentiary substance. Administrative fear cannot satisfy coercive threshold."**

At the dock tea stand, people read the line between price notices and weather updates.
Some call it elite legalism.
Some call it overdue civilization.

Merra calls it useful.

"First appeal held," she tells Jess that night. "Framework might actually survive first impact."

Jess closes her ledger and writes one sentence:

**Precedent established before panic normalized.**

That sentence may save more lives than any speech given this season.
