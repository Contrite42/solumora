# A Scrivener's Morning

The parchment today is good stock. Thick enough to hold ink without feathering, smooth enough that the nib glides without resistance. In the [[Valdenmoor]] scriptorum, on a morning when the light falls through the eastern windows at exactly the right angle, this is as close as a day gets to perfect.

Maren Kel has been copying spells for fourteen years. She is [[Control Tier|T1]], which determines her role absolutely: she reads existing spells, transcribes them with absolute fidelity, and does not deviate. She is very good at not deviating. Her certification record shows one error in fourteen years — a misplaced glyph in a [[Heat]] discipline spell that killed the practitioner who attempted to cast it. She does not think about that day anymore, or she tries not to.

This morning she is copying "Warmbloom," a common [[Heat]] spell from the [[Common Grimoire]], used primarily for agricultural applications and therapeutic warmth. It is the spell that appears in beginner's texts, taught to young practitioners as their first sustained casting. It is safe. Well-understood. 3 mistakes in ten thousand attempts, and the mistakes are almost always practitioner error rather than spell design.

The scriptorum is quiet. The other scribes are working at adjacent desks, the sound of their nibs on parchment creating a rhythm that Maren has learned to work inside. Copy, check, mark the margin with her certification sigil, move to the next page. The spell requires four pages. She will finish three pages this morning, one after the midday meal.

A [[Control Tier|T2]] practitioner enters the scriptorum looking for a copy of "Ghostpath," a [[Mind]] discipline spell for navigation through complex spaces. It is rare enough that stock is low. The apprentice who handles supply checks the inventory, shakes her head. Maren watches the practitioner's disappointment ripple across his face and watches it settle into quiet acceptance.

After the practitioner leaves, the apprentice brings a requisition to Maren's station. High priority. A practitioner in [[Life in Halveth|Halveth]] has specifically requested that Maren copy her "Ghostpath" commission. The request mentions her by name. The requisition includes overage payment for expedited work.

Maren has never been to Halveth. She has never met the practitioner. She knows the spell perfectly — she has copied it seventeen times — but this copy will be made specifically for someone who chose her work by reputation. The parchment is still good stock. The light is still falling at the right angle.

She sets aside "Warmbloom" and retrieves the prepared materials for "Ghostpath." This spell is more complex — eight pages, higher error rate. More attention required.

As she writes the first glyph, her hands move with nineteen years of certainty. Not fourteen years. Nineteen, counting the years before her certification when she was learning the craft from her mother, who was also a T1 scrivener in this same scriptorum, copying spells for people she would never meet.

The practitioner in Halveth will receive this copy in three weeks, if the courier road stays open. She will cast it, maybe successfully, maybe not. Maren will never know. But her work will have mattered to someone, in a place she has never seen, in a moment she will not witness.

She continues writing.
