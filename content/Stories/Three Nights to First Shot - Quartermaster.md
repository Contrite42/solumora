# Three Nights to First Shot - Quartermaster

Iless Varn balanced three ledgers on one crate and pretended this was still a warehouse problem.

Warehouse problems had ratios. Inputs, outputs, spoilage, delay. You could fix them with labor and time.

This week had neither.

The requisitions came stamped emergency but written like theater: enough detail to look accountable, not enough detail to survive audit.

He checked lot tags by hand and crossed out two requests for heat-ration kits destined for units that did not exist on last month's roster.

The clerk beside him looked panicked.

"If we reject these, they'll elevate," she said.

"Good," Iless said. "Elevation leaves signatures."

---

By midday, the line at the loading dock curved into the alley.

Supply officers from three commands, all with perfect urgency, all convinced their transfer deserved priority over everyone else's. Two had correction memos from [[The Council of Auralis]] signed within minutes of each other and contradicting each other on route authority.

Iless clipped both to the same packet and marked it HOLD: JURISDICTION CONFLICT.

Nobody thanked him for procedural purity.

One captain leaned across the desk and asked whether Iless understood what delay cost at the border.

Iless looked at the manifest and answered the only truth he trusted.

"I understand exactly what undocumented movement costs when someone dies and everyone wants a clean chain."

---

At evening bell, he reviewed outbound totals.

Food: down twelve percent from projected.

Medical bind packs: down eight.

Signal batteries: redirected twice in one day.

The numbers were not collapse. They were pre-collapse: the polite stage where systems still functioned, but only by cannibalizing tomorrow.

He drafted a variance report to the logistics board and copied a second version to a private archive channel that still reached one reviewer in [[Ashford]].

Officially, cross-border administrative sharing was suspended.

Unofficially, people who remembered the [[The Ashford Blockade Generation|Blockade Generation]] still traded facts when they could.

---

After dark, he locked the stores and found a fresh order waiting under the door.

Expedite transfer to forward depot. No unit code. No quartermaster counter-signature.

The seal was real. The procedure was not.

He wrote REJECTED in thick ink, then wrote the reason in thinner ink: "Missing accountability chain under emergency movement standard."

He expected a summons by sunrise.

He also expected that if first blood came, someone would ask where the crates moved, who signed, who verified.

Tonight, he had given his future self one answer that would still hold.
