# One Hundred Eighty Days After the First Casualty - Ratification Hall

The hall is too bright for a treaty vote and too crowded for a technical briefing.

Cassia signs first because protocol demands it. Eddan's delegate signs second because logistics demanded it yesterday. Everyone else signs in the order of least political damage.

On paper, the [[Corridor Stabilization Treaty]] is a legal instrument.
In the room, it is six months of fear, exhaustion, and reluctant arithmetic arranged into clauses.

Merra stands with Jess and Kess against the rear wall where technical staff are expected to look useful and not symbolic. Verath is present under agreed public conditions, not hidden and not central.

When the clerk reads Article I out loud, one Auralis hardliner calls it surrender by paperwork.
When she reads Article IV, one Terravelle firebrand calls it immunity theater.

Jess leans toward Merra and says, "If both sides hate the same paragraph for opposite reasons, it's probably load-bearing."

Vote totals pass in both chambers by narrower margins than anyone wanted and wider margins than anyone expected.

After signatures, no one cheers.

Instead, municipal runners carry certified copies to command posts, courts, guild offices, and market boards where the treaty will be interpreted, resisted, exploited, and occasionally obeyed by people who were not in this room.

Kess watches the packets leave and says, "Now comes the hard part."

Merra nods. "The hard part started months ago. This is just where it got names."

Outside, rain starts as bells mark evening.

Inside, the clerk stamps final registry line:

**Treaty active upon publication.**

For six months, the corridor has survived on emergency promises.
Tonight it survives on enforceable text.
