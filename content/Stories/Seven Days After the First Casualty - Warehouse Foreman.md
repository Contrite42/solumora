# Seven Days After the First Casualty - Warehouse Foreman

Dara Voss stopped calling it overflow on day seven.

Overflow implied a temporary mismatch between incoming loads and available hands.

What she had now was queue sediment: pallets that should have moved yesterday becoming tomorrow's base layer, then next week's obstruction.

At warehouse nine in Ashford east, she red-tagged three aisles and rerouted traffic through a side lane meant for emergency fire access.

She signed the waiver herself.

If something burned, it would be her name on the inquiry form.

---

The staffing board looked worse than the cargo board.

Six regular drivers reassigned to escort duty.

Two lift operators gone to family funerals.

One records clerk transferred to emergency liaison office and never replaced.

Dara split the remaining team into short shifts with mandatory breaks, not because policy required it, but because tired crews crushed more crates and started more fights.

She posted one line above the clock:

"Throughput without safety is delayed loss."

Nobody thanked her. They just worked slower and broke less.

---

At 13:05, a priority notice arrived for immediate military-adjacent loading.

At 13:11, a clinic requisition arrived marked urgent for staple foods and clean water barrels.

Both could not leave first.

Dara applied the rule she had adopted three days earlier:

life support before strategic reserve unless direct violence was active in district.

She cleared the clinic load.

The military liaison shouted, then requested written rationale.

Dara handed him the rationale already prepared and copied to [[Queue Management Offices]].

He left with the paper and less confidence than he arrived with.

---

At dusk, a junior loader asked whether things were getting better.

Dara looked at the floor map and answered in warehouse language.

"We are no longer falling behind by hour. Only by day."

The loader laughed once, then kept labeling barrels.

Progress was relative in a week like this.

Before lights out, Dara filed the daily note to [[Food Trade and Distribution Systems]] coordinators:

"Capacity holding at reduced safe baseline. Recovery requires staffing return, not new directives."

It was the seventh time she had written that sentence.
