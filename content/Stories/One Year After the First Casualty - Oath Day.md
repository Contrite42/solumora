# One Year After the First Casualty - Oath Day

The oath chamber smells like polished wood and old paper, which Merra has decided is the smell of institutions trying to look permanent.

Today, three incoming officials swear into roles that did not exist a year ago and now decide whether the next year resembles recovery or regression.

Jess sits at the side table with the handoff dossiers stacked by risk level. Kess has annotated each dossier with unresolved technical liabilities. Verath attends as observer under charter protocol, no speaking role unless asked.

Cassia reads the opening line of the new [[Succession Safeguards Charter]]:

"Authority transfers; obligations do not."

One incoming commissioner asks whether the continuity overlap period is truly mandatory or "advisory in spirit." Jess answers before anyone with ceremonial language can soften it.

"Mandatory. The corridor doesn't care about your onboarding preferences."

The room laughs once, then returns to seriousness.

Each oath now includes a clause absent from prior generations:

**"I acknowledge documented persecution mechanisms and accept duty to prevent their procedural recurrence."**

Merra watches the words land on faces too young to remember Ashford standstill week except as archived footage and inherited argument.

After signatures, outgoing officials pass sealed casebooks to incoming teams. No speeches, just documents: breach-near misses, sanction precedents, false rumor cascades that were stopped in time.

At the end of the ceremony, the clerk posts one line to the public docket:

**Continuity transfer certified: complete.**

Merra reads it and feels, for the first time in months, something close to relief.

Not because the system is safe.
Because the system has learned to assume it is never safe by default.
