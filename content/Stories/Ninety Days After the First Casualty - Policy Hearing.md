# Ninety Days After the First Casualty - Policy Hearing

The hall in Auralis Civic Courts is full by dawn, and no one pretends this is an ordinary hearing.

On the docket: emergency codification of non-human technical participation, oversight constraints for Tier 9-equivalent interventions, and constitutional authority disputes tied to the Fifth Bell declaration.

In plain language: who gets to decide what happens next now that everyone knows Verath exists.

Cassia enters without ceremonial canopy and takes the witness table instead of the raised dais. Council delegates object immediately. She acknowledges the objection and stays seated.

"Today is fact-finding," she says. "Not performance."

Kess testifies first, presenting the technical chain from corridor thresholds to near-breach indicators and stabilization outcomes. She is interrupted twice on methodology and once on "possible bias due to prior conflict with Council research bodies."

She answers each interruption with citation number and page reference.

Jess follows with logistics timelines and movement-risk dependency maps. She is asked whether her models can "guarantee" future safety under mixed command conditions.

"No model guarantees anything," she says. "It narrows stupidity windows. Use it or don't."

The room goes quiet long enough for recorders to catch every word.

Verath appears under negotiated terms: present, identified, no demonstration except if immediate threat emerges. Half the hall watches him. The other half watches everyone else watching him.

A Council legal advocate proposes emergency detention authority over non-human strategic entities until full legal framework is established.

Cassia asks one question.

"Under that proposal, would the corridor have held long enough for this hearing to exist?"

The advocate does not answer directly.

By afternoon, the court-adjacent committee releases provisional ruling language:

1. Non-human technical participation remains lawful under crisis oversight.
2. Any Tier 9-equivalent intervention requires dual civic-technical sign-off except imminent breach conditions.
3. Auralis and Terravelle must jointly publish intervention audit packets within ten days of any major event.
4. Emergency authority used in Fifth Bell remains contested politically but valid under immediate casualty-prevention doctrine.

No faction is satisfied. Which is how Merra later describes the ruling as "probably usable."

Outside the hall, demonstrators hold signs on both sides:

**NO IMMORTAL EXEMPTIONS**
**NO RETURN TO SILENCE**

Between them, clerks pin the provisional ruling to the public board.

It flaps once in late wind and settles flat.

In the old system, nothing this disruptive would have moved in one quarter.
In the new one, instability itself is now a legislative calendar.
