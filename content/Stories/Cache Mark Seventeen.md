# Cache Mark Seventeen

The supply cache at marker seventeen was supposed to contain enough water for six people for four days. [[Meln Vass]] found it half-empty, with two of the four containers showing signs of seal failure old enough that the water inside had long since evaporated into the [[Equatorial Desert|desert]] air.

He stood over the cache while the rest of the expedition approached behind him and tried to calculate how badly they were now compromised. The math wasn't encouraging.

"Problem?" [[Selt Orvn]] asked, reaching the cache site thirty seconds later.

"Containers three and four are dry. Lost at least eight liters total, maybe more."

Selt knelt beside the cache and examined the failed seals. "How old?"

"Weeks. Maybe longer." Meln pulled the expedition log from his pack and checked the maintenance schedule. "This cache was certified twenty days ago. Inspector signed off on full capacity and working seals."

"Inspector was wrong or lying."

"Doesn't matter which. We're sixteen liters short on a route that doesn't have supplemental sources."

Selt looked north toward the route they'd committed to — another forty-eight hours of travel through terrain hot enough to kill anyone who underestimated hydration needs. Six people, sixteen liters short, and the next cache at marker twenty-three.

"Can we reach twenty-three on what we have?" Selt asked.

Meln did the math again, hoping the numbers would shift. They didn't. "If we ration severely and accept degraded physical performance, maybe. If anyone's usage spikes due to exertion or heat stress, no."

"What about backtracking to marker twelve?"

"Adds eighteen hours and puts us on a timeline that makes the return window nonviable. We'd miss the seasonal schedule."

Selt stood and looked at the four remaining full containers. "So we go forward undermanned or we abort."

"That's the choice."

Behind them, the other four expedition members had reached the cache site and were waiting. [[Vren Tass]], [[Control Tier|T2]], looked from the cache to Meln and understood immediately what the silence meant. [[Kess Marr]], T1, was still catching up. [[Ossen Veld]] and [[Tella Morr]], both T1, stood together near the marker cairn.

Meln called everyone in. "Containers three and four failed. We're sixteen liters short. I'm giving you the assessment now: if we continue on current route, we'll likely reach marker twenty-three but margin is thin. If anyone experiences heat stress or higher-than-baseline usage, we won't make it."

Vren asked the logical question. "What's the abort option?"

"Return to marker twelve, take the eastern bypass, accept the timeline failure. We miss this season's window and go home."

"Or?" Kess asked.

"Or we continue with reduced safety margin and accept the risk."

The silence stretched. Desert wind moved across the marker cairn. The sun was three hours from peak intensity, which meant they needed to decide soon if they were moving or sheltering.

Selt spoke first. "I vote continue."

Vren nodded. "Agreed. We knew the risks coming in."

Kess looked at the cache, then at Meln. "If we ration hard?"

"Very hard. And if anyone shows heat stress symptoms, we reassess immediately."

"Then I vote continue."

Ossen and Tella conferred briefly, then Ossen spoke for both. "Continue."

Meln had expected it. Six people who'd invested weeks preparing for this expedition, paid the fees, arranged the seasonal clearance — they weren't going to abort over a sixteen-liter shortfall. That was human nature and also the problem: human nature consistently underestimated how quickly sixteen liters became survival-critical in desert conditions.

But it was their decision to make collectively. Meln's job was to give them accurate information and implement whatever they chose.

"We continue," he said. "Ration protocol starts immediately. Half standard intake until marker twenty-three. No exceptions."

---

**Thirty hours later.** Kess collapsed at the nineteen-mile mark, eight hours short of marker twenty-three.

She'd been showing heat stress symptoms for two hours — slowed pace, confusion, excessive sweating that indicated her body's cooling system was failing. Meln had caught it early, increased her water slightly, reduced her pack weight. It hadn't been enough.

Now she was down and they were fifteen hours from the next water source with one person who couldn't walk.

Selt and Vren rigged a carry harness while Meln administered what water he could justify from the rapidly-depleting reserve. Kess was conscious but incoherent. Bad sign.

"Can she make twenty-three?" Ossen asked.

"If we carry her and nothing else goes wrong, maybe."

"And if something else goes wrong?"

Meln didn't answer. The mathematics of survival were clear enough without verbal confirmation.

They carried her.

---

**Marker twenty-three.** They reached it eighteen hours later, six hours behind optimal schedule. The cache was intact. Full capacity. Good seals.

Kess was unconscious.

Meln had been monitoring her decline for the past six hours and knew what was coming. Heat stroke didn't reverse once cerebral symptoms appeared. They could hydrate her, cool her, do everything right — and she'd still likely die because they were too far from medical intervention to matter.

They tried anyway.

Two hours after reaching marker twenty-three, Kess Marr's breathing stopped. Vren attempted resuscitation using [[Flux]]-assisted cardiovascular grimoire techniques. No response.

She was dead.

Meln documented the time, the circumstances, and the decision chain that had led them to continue past marker seventeen despite the cache failure. The documentation would go into the [[Zakros Loss Table]] and be reviewed by [[Halveth]] coordination to determine if any systemic failure had contributed.

The systemic failure was obvious: a falsified cache inspection, a group decision to continue with inadequate margin, and the entirely predictable outcome when margin disappeared.

They buried Kess at marker twenty-three under rocks they carried from the surrounding terrain. There was no ceremony because there was no time. They had to reach the southern staging point before their own water ran out.

---

**Return.** Three days later, they emerged at [[Seln Watch]] on the southern edge. Five people instead of six.

The expedition filed its report. The cache inspection records at marker seventeen were reviewed and found to be fraudulent — inspector [[Kess Rold]] had signed off on maintenance he hadn't performed, likely to reduce workload during peak season coordination. Kess Rold was suspended pending investigation. New inspection protocols were implemented.

None of it brought Kess Marr back.

Selt, Vren, Ossen, and Tella collected their fee payments for a completed traverse and left [[Seln Watch]] within a day. Meln stayed longer, finalizing paperwork and meeting with coordination staff to provide detailed testimony.

On his last day in Seln Watch, he walked out to the desert edge and looked north toward where marker seventeen sat. The cache would be restocked by now, inspected properly, ready for the next expedition that needed it.

They would trust it because the system said it was trustworthy. And mostly the system would be right and mostly they would be fine.

Mostly wasn't everyone.

Kess Marr had been twenty-three years old, [[Control Tier|T1]], competent enough to earn expedition placement but not experienced enough to recognize when the group decision was wrong. She'd voted to continue because the others had, because aborting felt like failure, because sixteen liters didn't sound like much until it was everything.

Meln had given them the information. They'd made the choice. The outcome had followed.

That was how it worked. That was how it would keep working.

He turned away from the desert and walked back into Seln Watch. In two weeks he'd be leading another expedition. Different people, different route, different risks.

The caches would be there. He'd trust them because he had to.

And sometimes they'd fail.
