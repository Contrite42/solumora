# Twenty-Four Hours After the First Casualty - Duty Captain

Captain Elra Morn had slept fifty minutes in a chair and woke to find the map had changed color.

Not the terrain map. The incident map.

Yellow markers yesterday. Orange before dawn. Two red circles by noon where patrol lines overlapped and both sides now claimed defensive priority at once.

She stood over the board with cold tea and read the overnight summaries twice.

No formal retaliation order.

No formal withdrawal order either.

Which meant local commanders would interpret posture for themselves.

---

At 09:10 she convened the shift brief and spoke faster than protocol allowed.

"Today's rule is distance discipline. We gain nothing from proving courage at twenty paces."

One lieutenant objected that pulling back would be read as weakness.

Elra answered without looking up from the sheet.

"Being read as weak is cheaper than being right next to three funerals."

She assigned two senior observers to every mixed-sight segment and ordered mandatory log duplication to [[Border Incident Ledger]] format before noon.

Half the room hated the paperwork.

All of them knew paperwork was what survived after memory became accusation.

---

By midday, supply asked for priority movement of munitions to marker corridor C-D.

Elra returned the request with one line: "Denied pending bilateral spacing confirmation under [[Border De-Escalation Protocols]]."

The reply came back in six minutes, unsigned: "Command concern noted."

Command concern meant someone above her wanted deniability and someone beside her wanted speed.

She sent the denial again with her name in full.

---

At 16:40, a weather shift rolled grit across the low road and cut line-of-sight to half range.

Bad visibility had started smaller wars than this one.

Elra initiated fog posture: no advance, no warning shots, no pursuit outside marked lane, immediate relay through [[Border Coordination Offices]] if contact crossed sectors.

One patrol reported movement and held fire.

Another patrol reported movement and held fire.

No one cheered.

In her trade, no shots fired was not victory. It was borrowed time.

---

At 23:58 she filed the day-end entry:

"One day post-casualty. No confirmed secondary fatalities. Reciprocal hardening continues."

Then she added a private note she would delete before archive copy:

"We are no longer preventing war. We are invoicing it by the hour."
