The **Circle** is the highest Shape level in the [[Flux Users|Scholar]] sigil system, providing full explicit control over all six outer [[Spell Variables]].

A Circle [[Sigils|sigil]] has **6 outer points + center**, encoding:
- [[Discipline]]
- [[Output Mode]]
- [[Geometry Pattern]]
- [[Reach]]
- [[Persistence]]
- [[Target Spec]]

The center encodes [[Hook]], [[Mode]], and [[Control Tier]] as with all [[Sigils|sigil]] shapes.

## Why Circle Matters

Circle sigils are the most powerful and flexible sigil form available within the current Scholar system — but also the most demanding to draw, copy, and cast safely. Every variable is explicitly defined, leaving nothing to default behavior. This makes them maximally configurable but unforgiving of error.

Most [[Spells|spells]] in the [[Rare Grimoire]], [[Legendary Grimoire]], [[Mythic Grimoire]], and [[Pale Grimoire]] use Circle sigils because the effects they produce require precise control over every variable.

## The Oldest Form

The oldest documented versions of any given spell are almost always recorded as Circles. [[Flux Users|Scholars]] begin with a Circle to capture maximum information, then compress the design into smaller shapes as the spell becomes better understood and standardized for mass use in [[Grimoires]].

## The Limit of the Current System

A Circle has 6 outer points. This is the maximum the current Scholar system formally defines. Ruins found in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] have returned fragments of sigil geometry with more points than a Circle — geometry that does not conform to any Shape the system recognizes. What a 7-point, 8-point, or infinite-sided sigil would control is a question that remains formally unanswered, and that certain [[Flux Users|Scholars]] in [[Auralis]] are spending considerable resources trying to answer.
