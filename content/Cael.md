**Cael** _(Prince of Terravelle, Second Son of Eddan)_

Cael is nineteen, the younger son of King Eddan of Terravelle, and the heir to a throne he was never supposed to inherit. His older brother was the crown prince. Cael was the spare — loved, provided for, and not particularly burdened with expectation until the campaign east of the capital, after which every expectation in [[Terravelle]] landed on him at once.

He has not handled this gracefully. He is aware of this. He continues to handle it ungracefully anyway.

## Character

Cael is not stupid and he is not lazy. He is a tinkerer — someone who takes things apart to see how they work and puts them back together slightly differently to see if they work better. Applied to furniture or mechanisms this would be an endearing hobby. Applied to [[Flux]] manipulation in a kingdom that needs him to be learning statecraft, it reads as avoidance.

It is avoidance. It is also genuine. The two things are not in conflict.

His specific obsession is channeling cost — the question of why moving [[Flux]] through a person demands the physical and mental toll that it does, and whether that toll is fundamental to how [[Flux]] works or simply a limitation of how the current [[Sigils|sigil]] system asks people to use it. He is a T1 or low T2 caster, which means he hits his own ceiling constantly and has developed an intimate familiarity with the feeling of running out. This has made him more interested in efficiency than almost any high-tier caster would be. High-tier casters rarely think carefully about cost. Cael thinks about almost nothing else.

He does not have the formal Scholar training to pursue this properly. He has notebooks, a few sympathetic [[Flux Users|Formulists]] in the city who humor his questions, and a habit of spending his evenings at gatherings where the conversation eventually turns to magic if he steers it there.

## The Parties

Cael's social life looks, from the outside, like a young man avoiding his responsibilities. From the inside it is a young man who finds governance abstract and frightening and [[Flux]] tangible and interesting, spending time in the environments where the latter comes up naturally.

His close friends are not political figures or future advisors. They are people he actually likes — a small, loyal group who knew him before the succession shifted and have not treated him differently since. He values this more than he can articulate and protects it accordingly.

Eddan is aware of the distinction between what Cael's evenings look like and what they actually are. He has not found a way to leverage this into a productive conversation. They love each other in the way of people who are too similar to communicate easily.

## Flux Research

Cael's working theory — unproven, under-resourced, and probably several years from anything demonstrable — is that channeling cost is at least partially a product of sigil geometry rather than a fundamental property of [[Flux]] itself. The Shape system asks a caster to hold multiple variables simultaneously in a fixed geometric relationship. Cael suspects this simultaneous holding is where most of the cost lives, and that a sequential approach might distribute the load differently.

He has not heard of [[Hypertext]]. If he ever does, it will be one of the more significant moments of his life.

## The Queen

When Cael was eight years old he attended a trade gala in the capital at which the [[Auralis|Queen of Auralis]] was present as part of a diplomatic delegation. He remembers very little about the gala. He remembers her clearly.

He has never told anyone this. It is not a political position. It is not actionable. It is simply a fact about him that sits quietly in the background of his awareness of the southern kingdom — that his father's most dangerous adversary is someone he has carried a faint, embarrassing impression of since childhood, and that the impression has not faded the way it was supposed to.

He is nineteen. He is aware it is not a serious thing. He is also aware that awareness has not done much to resolve it.
