_(Border Settlement, Terravelle)_

**Merra Teld** is a T2 logistics coordinator at [[Thel Reach]], age 32, who has spent eight years managing supply distribution for the desert-edge settlement. She keeps four hundred people alive in a place where institutional support arrives slowly or not at all. When the [[Rift Incursions|rift incursion]] hits [[Thel Reach]] on what becomes known as the Breaking Day, she loses seventeen neighbors, her supply depot, and any remaining faith that the kingdoms understand what they're doing at the border.

## Background

Merra grew up in [[Valdenmoor]], daughter of a [[Maren Freight Chain]] dock supervisor. She learned logistics by watching cargo move — what fails, what works, what kills people when it breaks. At twenty-four, she volunteered for desert-edge posting because the pay was better and the work mattered. [[Thel Reach]] wasn't supposed to be dangerous. It was supposed to be boring.

She's been keeping the settlement functional through declining institutional support for three years. Shipments arrive late. Equipment breaks and replacement parts don't come. She's learned to improvate, barter, and strip failed systems for components. The settlement survives because she's better at logistics than the people pretending to manage it from [[Auralis]].

## The Incursion

The rift opens at dawn, three hundred yards from the settlement's eastern perimeter. Merra is the first person to see it — she's up early checking yesterday's grain delivery for moisture damage. The dimensional breach looks like heat distortion at first, then geometry starts bending wrong, then [[Flux Demons]] come through.

She gets forty-three people into the reinforced storage hall before the demons reach the settlement. Seventeen don't make it. She watches [[Kel Dorst]], the settlement's T3 security coordinator, hold the demons at the hall entrance for six minutes before his reservoir runs dry and they tear him apart. She holds the door herself after that, burning her own Flux reserve into a Force barrier she's never tested at combat intensity, until the incursion collapses on its own.

The [[Auralis]] military response arrives nine hours later. By then, Merra has organized the casualty documentation, the supply damage assessment, and the survivor interviews. She has numbers, names, and a timeframe. What she doesn't have is an explanation for why the incursion happened at a known anomalous site that [[Emberfall]] has been monitoring for two years.

## What She Knows

Three weeks before the incursion, [[Thel Reach]] received a supply shipment with a data case mis-routed from [[Emberfall]]. The case contained rift probability maps for the northern desert edge. The map showed [[Thel Reach]]'s eastern approach flagged as "elevated threshold event probability — timeline 12-18 days."

Merra filed a flag-and-forward report. No response came. She sent two follow-up requests through official channels. Still nothing. On day eleven she sent a priority escalation to [[Council Compliance Directorate]]. On day fourteen, the rift opened.

She has kept the data case. She has kept her flag reports. She has kept the timestamped queue receipts showing her escalation was received and logged but never processed. The [[Council of Auralis]] knew. They did nothing. And now seventeen people are dead.

## Motivation

Merra doesn't want revenge. She wants accountability, and she wants the system fixed before more settlements get erased because someone in [[Solhaven]] decided research data was more valuable than a warning. The war that's starting won't fix anything — it will make the border more dangerous, stretch logistics thinner, and kill more people in places no one with power is paying attention to.

She's going to make them pay attention. She has documentation. She has survivor testimony. She has a logistics background that lets her track exactly how institutional failure killed her neighbors. And she's willing to take that documentation wherever it needs to go to stop this from happening again.

## Skills

- **Logistics Expertise**: Can model supply chains, identify bottlenecks, predict failure cascades
- **Resource Management**: Keeps systems running with inadequate support
- **Crisis Response**: Organized survivors during incursion, documented everything methodically
- **Institutional Knowledge**: Knows how [[Auralis]] bureaucracy actually works (and fails)
- **Flux Capability**: T2 Force discipline, learned combat application under pressure
- **Desert Experience**: Eight years at border, knows harsh environment survival

## Personal Stakes

The seventeen dead include [[Bren Toss]] (T1 water engineer who kept the wells running), [[Nessa Veld]] (T0 medical assistant), and [[Kel Dorst]] (T3 security who died holding the line). Merra knew their families. She approved their supply requests. She shared meals with them. They were her responsibility, and the system failed them.

Her parents in [[Valdenmoor]] want her to come home. She's refused. There are three hundred and eighty-three people still alive at [[Thel Reach]], and if the war escalates, they'll be abandoned entirely. She can't leave until they're safe or evacuated. She won't leave until someone answers for what happened.

## Current Status

Merra remains at [[Thel Reach]], maintaining settlement operations while the military establishes a forward position nearby. She's been interviewed six times by [[Auralis]] officials collecting incident data. She's provided the mis-routed data case and her escalation documentation to exactly one person: a [[Council Compliance Directorate]] inspector who looked at the timestamps and went very quiet.

She's waiting to see if that documentation disappears or if someone actually does something with it. If it disappears, she has copies. If the war consumes everything before accountability happens, she's already decided she'll carry that documentation across the [[Equatorial Desert|desert]] herself if necessary.

She's never been to [[Terravelle]]. She's heard it's more horizontal than [[Auralis]], and things actually work there. She's willing to find out.
