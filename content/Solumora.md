[[Solumora]] is a vast supercontinent — the only known landmass in the world — home to nearly a quarter billion people. It is a world of striking balance, yet divided by nature's extremes.

The equator is dominated by [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]], an inhospitable wasteland that only those equipped with [[Grimoires]], sufficient [[Flux]] ability, or extreme preparation can survive crossing. The [[Kingdoms]] of Solumora flourish in two belts on either side: the northern region is ruled by [[Terravelle]], a realm of structure and resilience, while the south is governed by [[Auralis]], a land of ambition and magical advancement. Between them, [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] acts as both barrier and crucible — shaping two cultures that developed in near-total isolation from one another and arrived at fundamentally different answers to the same world.

The equilibrium of Solumora is not just a balance of power. It is a constant negotiation with the world's harshest extremes.

## Economy

The economy of Solumora follows a pattern familiar to anyone who has tried to survive in it: the things you need to stay alive are within reach, and everything that makes life worth living will cost you dearly.

### Currency — [[Watts]]

The currency of Solumora is the **Watt**. [[Watts]] are not coins, notes, or physical objects. They are entries on a distributed ledger written into the ambient [[Flux]] field itself, verified by every transaction in the network simultaneously. There is no bank, no mint, no physical backing. No institution controls the ledger and no kingdom created it. Both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] run their formal economies on top of it, but neither owns it.

Transactions require the consent of both parties. One initiates, one confirms, and the ledger writes. The record is permanent and cannot be altered retroactively. This is what makes Watts work: the ledger cannot be cheated, because it lives in the Flux field distributed across Solumora with no central point of failure.

### The Birth Rune

A [[Birth Runes|birth rune]] is a personal [[Sigils|sigil]] that registers an individual within the ledger and serves as their financial identity. It is an address, not a container — the rune identifies who you are within the system, and the ledger maintains the record of what that identity holds. Birth runes are traditionally inscribed by the family within the first days of life, a low-[[Control Tier|tier]] sigil that any [[Flux Users|Formulist]] can manage.

Having a birth rune is common but not universal, and being without one carries no legal consequence in most contexts. It is simply how most people choose to participate in the Watt economy. Those without runes use barter, physical goods, favors, and informal debt — a parallel system that functions perfectly well and in some regions is the dominant one.

In [[Terravelle]], registration is common out of cultural habit — record-keeping fits the Terravellian preference for predictability. In [[Auralis]], participation in the ledger carries social and spiritual weight, and declining it is considered a statement whether or not it was intended as one.

### Price Structure

The Solumoran economy splits cleanly along one line: survival is cheap, comfort is not.

Basic food, rough clothing, common shelter, standard tools, and [[Common Grimoire|Common-tier]] [[Sigils|sigil]] work sit at prices within reach of almost anyone participating in the Watt economy. The floor is low by design and by long economic habit — neither kingdom has ever found instability at the bottom of society to be worth the savings.

The moment goods or services cross into quality-of-life territory the price climbs sharply and keeps climbing. A meal is cheap. A good meal is not. Shelter is affordable. Warm, warded, comfortable shelter is not. A [[Common Grimoire|Common]] [[Grimoires|grimoire]] is within reach of most working families. An [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]] one requires genuine saving. A [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] text, if it surfaces at all, is the kind of purchase that marks a turning point in a household's financial life.

[[Flux Users|Channeler]]-tier [[Sigils|sigil]] work — anything at [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] and above — is not really priced like a service. It is priced like access to something that most of the world will never be able to replicate, because that is exactly what it is.

## How People Move

Movement in Solumora is not one network. It is layered systems built for different risk and volume profiles.

Bulk goods in [[Terravelle]] move most efficiently through the [[Maren Freight Chain]], with [[Valdenmoor]] coordinating schedules and [[Ashford]] converting river volume into export flow. High-priority overland communication and compact critical cargo between border commerce and desert staging move through the [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]]. Seasonal expedition-linked heavy movement around the desert edge is handled by the [[Zakros Rim Caravan Loop]], where timing and local route knowledge matter more than nominal distance.

Maritime movement ties the coasts together on two different scales. The [[Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run]] provides high-frequency west-coast circulation inside [[Auralis]]. The [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] carries slower long-haul mixed cargo and correspondence linking northern settlements near [[Wolfpoint]] to eastern trade infrastructure near [[Ashford]] and onward buyers.

Information usually arrives before volume, and systems are designed around that fact. When a route destabilizes, dispatchers reroute messages first, then cargo. Most institutions in both kingdoms survive disruption not by preventing breaks, but by preserving movement continuity across at least one channel.

## Religion and Belief

Solumora has no unified religion. What it has is [[Flux]] — real, permeating, accessible to anyone with the capacity to touch it — and five distinct cultural traditions that interpret what this means spiritually.

The northern kingdom of [[Terravelle]] is shaped by the [[Covenant of Measure]], a tradition emphasizing proportionality and ethical Flux use. It has no clergy and no formal theology, but its influence runs deep: the suspicion of display, the trust in demonstrated competence, the belief that excess reveals character flaws. A Terravellian city dweller lives this tradition without thinking about it as tradition.

The southern kingdom of [[Auralis]] centers on the [[Ascendant Path]], an institutional religion claiming that Flux mastery is the highest form of spiritual development. The Path is thoroughly embedded in Auralis's government, schools, and social hierarchy. Its priests (Luminants) are skilled casters who officiate at every significant life event. The Path offers a theology that makes Auralis's tier-based society feel like cosmic order.

Between and beyond these institutional traditions lie three other practices: [[The Quiet Work]] of the northern territories, which treats Flux as a precise language; [[The Returners]], who believe the ancient civilization persists in the ruins and waits for contact; and [[The Ancestral Current]], a folk tradition present everywhere but formalized nowhere, which holds that Flux presence lingers in places where practitioners worked.

Most people practice some blend of their region's dominant tradition and elements from the others. A Terravelle merchant honors Covenant contracts and leaves food for local spirits. An Auralis scholar speaks in Ascendant Path theological language while quietly maintaining Ancestral Current practices in their family's home. The traditions do not demand exclusivity, and boundaries blur in lived practice.

The exception is the Ascendant Path, which is explicit enough in its theology that orthodox Luminants do distinguish between Path-consistent and Path-inconsistent beliefs. This creates an undercurrent of tension — the Path's institutional power in Auralis contrasts with the Covenant's institutional invisibility in Terravelle, a difference that reflects deeper divergence in how the two kingdoms understand authority, truth, and what Flux is for.

## History

The full timeline of Solumora spans an unknown pre-modern period, a founding era approximately six hundred years ago, four centuries of isolation between the kingdoms, and two centuries of Contact-era development that has produced the current political situation.

The **ancient civilization** that built the ruins in Desert Zakros is not named in any surviving record. What they left behind — the ruins, the [[Watts]] ledger, the [[Ancient Ruins|anomalous Flux sites]] — is still active. What ended them is unknown. The leading theories are documented in [[The Ancient Civilization]] and [[The Desert Barrier]].

The **founding period** produced two kingdoms that developed independently across the [[The Isolation Period|Isolation Period]] — four centuries in which the [[The Grimoire Tradition|grimoire tradition]] emerged separately in both kingdoms (arriving at nearly identical systems), the [[The Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] burned through both kingdoms' communities, and the cultures diverged in ways documented in [[The Cultural Divergence]].

**Contact** began approximately two hundred years ago when Auralis developed reliable crossing technology. The relationship since then has included [[The Three Near-Wars]] and the slow accumulation of the current tensions described in [[The Fraying Peace]].
