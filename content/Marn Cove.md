Marn Cove is a coastal fishing village on [[Auralis]]'s western shore, forty miles south of [[Hedun]]. Population approximately 150. It supplies preserved fish to inland markets and provides stopover services for vessels running the [[Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run]].

## The Fishery

The waters off Marn Cove are productive year-round: deep-water fish migrations in spring and autumn, nearshore catches during summer, and winter bottom-fishing when surface waters are too rough for small boat operations. The village operates a fleet of twelve boats, each crewed by extended family groups that have fished these waters for generations.

Most fishing at Marn Cove is traditional: nets, lines, traps, and knowledge of seasonal patterns passed down through family practice. [[Flux]] use concentrates around storm warning, boat repair, and fish preservation. Voss Maren, T2, casts Weathersense sequences before every major fishing expedition and maintains Sealward protections on the village's boat hulls to extend service life in salt water.

The work is not glamorous. Fishing here means long days, physical labor, and constant exposure to weather that shifts faster than casting can manage. What keeps Marn Cove functional is not advanced technique — it is accumulated knowledge about where fish move, when storms arrive, and how to read water conditions in ways that no [[Sigils|sigil]] can encode.

## The Preservation Operation

Fresh fish from Marn Cove moves to nearby coastal settlements for immediate consumption. The village's primary economic output is preserved fish: salted, smoked, and dried products that can be transported inland without spoilage.

The preservation operation runs year-round in a communal facility where catch that exceeds fresh-sale volume is processed into shelf-stable products. Kess Brolen manages the facility, coordinates processing schedules, and maintains quality standards that inland buyers expect. Her work is part chemistry, part logistics, part quality control — and she has been running the operation for sixteen years without a significant contamination incident.

Marn Cove's preserved fish moves inland via the overland trade routes that connect the coast to [[Auralis]]'s basin settlements. The product is not premium — it is working-class protein sold to communities that do not have direct coastal access. The volume is consistent, the price is stable, and the market relationship has sustained the village's economy through multiple fishing season variations.

## The Coastal Run

Marn Cove serves as an alternate stopover point for vessels running the [[Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run]] when weather or scheduling makes the primary route unpredictable. The village provides sheltered anchorage, emergency repair supplies, and provisioning services to coastal traffic that needs a safe harbor between major ports.

This is not a major revenue source — most vessels complete the coastal run without stopping — but it keeps Marn Cove integrated into the maritime network in ways that matter during disruptions. When a storm closes the main route or a vessel needs urgent repair, Marn Cove is the known alternative. That reputation has value beyond immediate economic return.

## The Practitioners

Marn Cove has three active [[Flux Users|practitioners]], all [[Control Tier|Tier 2]], all fishers who cast as part of their normal work rather than as specialists. Their collective capacity handles the village's Flux needs: storm prediction, equipment maintenance, preservation-facility temperature control, and occasional emergency casting when fishing operations encounter unexpected problems.

The village does not have a dedicated high-tier practitioner, and it does not need one. The problems Marn Cove faces are predictable, seasonal, and manageable with mid-tier casting applied by people who understand the context intimately.

## What the Village Tracks

Coastal Run traffic this season has been lighter than historical patterns. Fewer vessels are stopping at Marn Cove, which suggests they are completing runs without needing alternate harbors. This could mean improved weather prediction, better vessel maintenance, or simply a quieter shipping season.

Voss Maren has been comparing his Weathersense readings to historical records. The storm frequency this season is not unusually low. The coastal run's reduced stopover rate reflects something other than weather — possibly changes in shipping schedules, route optimization, or fleet composition.

The village is not concerned yet, but the pattern is being watched. Marn Cove's economic stability does not depend on coastal run stopover traffic, but the village's integration into broader maritime systems does. If the traffic pattern continues for multiple seasons, the village will need to adjust its relationship to the coastal trade network.
