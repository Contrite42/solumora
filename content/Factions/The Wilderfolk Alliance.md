The Wilderfolk Alliance is not a kingdom, not a guild, and not a formal political entity in any sense that [[Auralis]] or [[Terravelle]] would recognize. It is a network of rural communities, family groups, and individual practitioners who have chosen to maintain their independence from both kingdoms' administrative structures while sharing knowledge, resources, and mutual aid across the wilderness regions that neither kingdom fully controls.

The Alliance exists primarily in the deep forests of northern [[Terravelle]], the mountain valleys beyond [[Wolfpoint]], and the scattered settlements along the western coast that are too remote for effective royal administration. Its members are united not by geography or formal agreement but by a shared commitment to self-sufficiency, ecological balance, and the preservation of knowledge that predates both kingdoms.

What makes the Alliance significant is not its political influence — it has none — but its mastery of healing arts that surpass anything available through official channels. The Alliance has preserved and developed medical techniques that combine conventional [[Flux]] practice with botanical knowledge, ritual work, and approaches to human health that treat the body as part of a larger natural system rather than as an isolated mechanism to be repaired.

## Origins and Philosophy

The Wilderfolk Alliance emerged gradually over the past two centuries as rural communities found themselves increasingly pressured by the expanding administrative reach of both kingdoms. As [[Terravelle]] formalized its guild structures and [[Auralis]] developed its tier-based social hierarchy, people who preferred older, more localized ways of organizing their lives began to coordinate their resistance to incorporation.

The Alliance's core philosophy is built around the [[Covenant of Measure]], but interpreted more radically than either kingdom would accept. Where Terravelle treats the Covenant as a principle of sustainable resource use, the Alliance treats it as a complete framework for human existence — the understanding that human communities are part of natural systems rather than separate from them, and that health, prosperity, and survival depend on maintaining that integration.

This philosophy extends to their approach to [[Flux]] use. Alliance practitioners do not organize their magic around the [[Discipline]] framework that both kingdoms use. Instead, they work with what they call "life-weaving" — the understanding that Flux flows through living systems in patterns that can be supported, redirected, or healed, but not controlled in the way that conventional [[Sigils]] attempt to control it.

The result is a magical tradition that is less powerful in terms of raw capability but more sustainable in terms of long-term practice. Alliance healers can work for hours without the exhaustion that affects kingdom-trained practitioners. Their effects tend to be gentler but more permanent. They can address conditions that conventional medicine cannot touch, particularly those involving the interaction between Flux exposure and living tissue.

## Healing Arts

The Alliance's medical capabilities are built around three integrated approaches: botanical medicine, Flux-weaving, and what they call "whole-system healing" — the treatment of illness as a disruption in the patient's relationship to their environment rather than as an isolated biological problem.

**Botanical medicine** involves the cultivation and preparation of plants whose chemical properties interact with human physiology in ways that support natural healing processes. Alliance communities maintain extensive gardens of medicinal plants, many of which are not found in kingdom herbalism texts. The knowledge of which plants to use, how to prepare them, and how to combine them for specific conditions is passed down through apprenticeship and family teaching rather than formal documentation.

**Flux-weaving** is the Alliance's approach to magical healing. Rather than using conventional [[Heat]] or [[Chemical]] disciplines to directly manipulate the patient's body, Alliance practitioners work with the natural Flux patterns that exist within living tissue. They can sense disruptions in these patterns — areas where injury, disease, or Flux exposure has created blockages or imbalances — and use gentle applications of their own Flux to restore normal flow.

This approach is particularly effective for conditions that conventional medicine struggles with: chronic pain from old injuries, the lingering effects of dimensional exposure, and the kind of systemic illness that affects multiple organs without clear cause. Alliance healers can often restore function that kingdom physicians have declared permanently lost.

**Whole-system healing** addresses the patient's entire life situation rather than focusing solely on their symptoms. An Alliance healer will consider the patient's diet, living conditions, work patterns, social relationships, and spiritual state as part of the diagnostic process. Treatment often involves changes to all of these areas rather than just medical intervention.

This approach produces results that seem miraculous to people accustomed to kingdom medicine, but it requires time, patience, and the patient's active participation in ways that conventional treatment does not. Alliance healing is not something that happens to a patient — it is something the patient learns to do with guidance and support.

## Organization and Structure

The Alliance operates through a network of autonomous communities that maintain contact through traveling practitioners, seasonal gatherings, and a system of mutual aid that activates during crises. There is no central authority, no formal leadership structure, and no unified policy on most issues. Individual communities make their own decisions about how to organize their internal affairs and how to relate to the broader world.

What unifies the Alliance is shared knowledge and shared values rather than shared governance. A healer trained in one Alliance community can practice effectively in any other because the underlying principles are consistent even when the specific techniques vary. A family fleeing persecution in one region can find sanctuary in Alliance communities hundreds of miles away because the commitment to mutual aid transcends local boundaries.

The Alliance maintains this coherence through a practice called "knowledge-walking" — the tradition that experienced practitioners spend part of each year traveling between communities, sharing techniques, learning new approaches, and maintaining the personal relationships that hold the network together. Knowledge-walkers serve as informal ambassadors, teachers, and coordinators, but they have no authority to make decisions for the communities they visit.

The seasonal gatherings — typically held at remote locations during the summer months — provide opportunities for larger-scale coordination, the resolution of disputes between communities, and the formal recognition of new practitioners. These gatherings also serve as marriage markets, trade fairs, and cultural exchanges that strengthen the bonds between geographically separated groups.

## Relationship with the Kingdoms

The Alliance's relationship with both kingdoms is carefully managed to avoid direct confrontation while maintaining independence. Alliance communities typically comply with basic administrative requirements — they register births, pay taxes when collectors reach them, and provide information about local conditions when requested — but they resist deeper integration into kingdom systems.

This resistance takes the form of passive non-compliance rather than active rebellion. Alliance communities do not send their children to kingdom schools, do not participate in guild structures, and do not seek official recognition for their healing practices. They trade with kingdom settlements when it serves their purposes, but they maintain their own economic networks that operate independently of royal oversight.

The kingdoms tolerate this arrangement because the Alliance provides services that the official systems cannot match. Kingdom officials know that Alliance healers can treat conditions that defeat conventional medicine, and they occasionally arrange for discrete consultations when their own resources prove inadequate. The Alliance's knowledge of wilderness survival, botanical medicine, and Flux anomalies is also valuable for expeditions, border patrols, and other activities that take kingdom personnel into remote areas.

The tolerance has limits. If the Alliance were to actively oppose kingdom policies, harbor fugitives, or engage in activities that threatened royal authority, the relationship would change quickly. Alliance leaders understand this and have structured their practices to remain valuable enough to be tolerated while avoiding activities that would require suppression.

## People in Motion

**[[Zara Greenhaven]]** serves as one of the Alliance's most respected knowledge-walkers, traveling between communities throughout the northern wilderness to share healing techniques and coordinate responses to regional challenges. She has spent the past year investigating reports of unusual illnesses among communities near the [[Equatorial Desert]], trying to determine whether the symptoms are related to increased dimensional activity or represent a new disease pattern that requires different treatment approaches.

**[[Thrain Stonefist]]** operates as a bridge between Alliance communities and kingdom settlements, using his blacksmithing skills to create tools and equipment that Alliance healers need while maintaining trade relationships that provide access to materials not available in the wilderness. His workshop in a border settlement serves as an informal embassy where kingdom officials can arrange consultations with Alliance practitioners without officially acknowledging the arrangement.

**[[Aria Moonwhisper]]** travels throughout Alliance territory as a storyteller and cultural historian, preserving the oral traditions that maintain the network's shared identity while adapting those traditions to address current challenges. Her performances at seasonal gatherings and community celebrations serve both entertainment and educational functions, ensuring that younger generations understand the principles and history that guide Alliance practices.

## The Healing Reputation

The Alliance's medical capabilities have created a reputation that extends far beyond their actual territory. Stories of miraculous healings, of conditions cured after kingdom physicians gave up hope, of techniques that work where conventional medicine fails — these stories travel through trade networks, military units, and social connections throughout both kingdoms.

The reputation is both accurate and exaggerated. Alliance healers can indeed treat conditions that defeat conventional medicine, particularly those involving chronic pain, systemic illness, and the aftereffects of Flux exposure. Their whole-system approach often produces improvements in patients' overall health and quality of life that go beyond addressing specific symptoms.

However, Alliance healing is not magic in the sense that kingdom residents often expect. It requires time, patience, and the patient's active participation in lifestyle changes that may be difficult to maintain. It works best for conditions that have developed gradually and respond to gradual correction. It is less effective for acute trauma, infectious diseases, and conditions that require immediate intervention.

The exaggerated reputation has created problems for the Alliance. People with unrealistic expectations travel long distances seeking cures that may not be possible, sometimes abandoning conventional treatment that could help them in favor of Alliance approaches that may not be appropriate for their condition. Alliance healers have had to develop protocols for managing these situations while maintaining their commitment to helping anyone who seeks their aid.
