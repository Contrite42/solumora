# Keln Ridge Mines

Keln Ridge Mines is the largest centralized mining complex in [[Auralis]], located in the eastern highlands sixty miles from [[Solhaven]]. The site extracts iron ore and Flux-reactive minerals from deep underground deposits, employing 200-250 workers across multiple shafts under direct [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] management. The operation exemplifies institutional resource control, accelerated wartime extraction, and labor conditions characteristic of large-scale mining under military-supply priority.

The mines have operated for forty-three years, initially as independent merchant-house operation before [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] acquisition twenty years ago. Current director is [[Voss Keld]], [[Control Tier|T3]], managing operations, labor relations, and military requisition compliance for the past eight years.

## Site Geography and Infrastructure

**Location:** Eastern highlands, elevation 800-1000 meters, accessible via maintained road connecting to [[Solhaven]] sixty miles west. Terrain is rocky hillside with limited forest cover (timber scarcity creates fuel-cost challenges for on-site smelting operations).

**Active Shafts:** Six primary vertical shafts descending 40-120 meters depth, connected by horizontal tunnels accessing ore deposits. Shaft Alpha (deepest, 120 meters) produces highest-grade iron ore but requires continuous pumping operations preventing water infiltration. Shaft Beta (70 meters) accesses Flux-reactive mineral deposits — restricted access, specialist-practitioner supervision required.

**Surface Infrastructure:** Administrative buildings housing site management and records, worker barracks (capacity 180 beds, overcrowded due to wartime workforce expansion), processing facilities (ore sorting, initial crushing), equipment maintenance workshops, and supply storage. Water source: mountain spring 2 kilometers upslope, piped to site via gravity-feed system.

**Transport:** Ore wagons transport processed material to [[Solhaven]] for shipment to refineries. Return trips carry food supplies, equipment, and fuel. Transport coordination managed through contract with merchant house operating dedicated fleet (six heavy wagons, 24 draft animals, rotating crews).

## Extraction Operations

**Iron Ore Production:** Primary output. Current accelerated schedule: 12-15 tons raw ore weekly (approximately 600-750 tons annually). Pre-war production: 8-10 tons weekly. Ore grade: 35-40% iron content requires smelting for usable metal extraction. Pricing to [[The Council of Auralis|Council]]: 4 Watts per ton raw ore (below market rate of 8-10 Watts per ton).

**Flux-Reactive Mineral Extraction:** Secondary operation in Shaft Beta. Output: 8-15 kilograms monthly of crystalline Flux-responsive materials used in [[Grimoires|grimoire]] production and high-tier spell applications. Extremely hazardous — material occasionally releases uncontrolled Flux discharge during extraction. Requires [[Control Tier|T3-T4]] supervision. Pricing: 80-120 [[Watts]] per kilogram to military procurement (compare civilian market 150-200 Watts per kilogram when available).

**Limestone Quarrying:** Tertiary operation. Surface quarry produces construction stone for local use and occasional export to [[Solhaven]]. Output variable: 30-50 cubic meters monthly.

## Labor Structure

**Field Miners ([[Control Tier|T0-T1]]):** 140-160 workers performing physical excavation, ore hauling, tunnel support installation. Twelve-hour shifts, six days weekly. Wage: 16 Watts weekly (below regional average of 18-22 Watts for comparable work). Housed in on-site barracks with deductions for food and shelter (3 Watts weekly total). Casualty rate: 9 deaths annually current conditions versus historical 4-5 deaths annually.

**Safety Practitioners ([[Control Tier|T2-T3]]):** 12-15 practitioners managing ventilation, structural assessment, emergency response. Critical roles but undersized — wartime conscription removed several senior practitioners. Remaining practitioners work extended hours attempting coverage. Wage: 45 Watts weekly (suppressed from market rate of 60-70 Watts). High turnover — practitioners leave for better conditions elsewhere when contracts expire.

**Shaft Foremen:** Mid-level supervision coordinating daily operations in each shaft. Combination of experienced miners and [[Control Tier|T2]] practitioners. Wage: 35-40 Watts weekly.

**Site Director ([[Voss Keld]]):** Overall operations manager reporting to [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] logistics directorate. Manages personnel, production scheduling, requisition compliance, and merchant coordination. Salary: 120 Watts weekly plus performance bonuses tied to output targets (creating incentive misalignment — director benefits from accelerated extraction regardless of safety consequences).

## Operational Challenges

**Water Infiltration:** Shaft Alpha continuously fights groundwater seepage. Pumping operations require two [[Control Tier|T2]] practitioners maintaining Flux-driven pumps in eight-hour rotating shifts. System failure would flood shaft within 48 hours, destroying months of development work. Equipment strain creates persistent maintenance demands.

**Ventilation Failure Risk:** Deep operations require continuous air circulation. Recent practitioner understaffing means ventilation systems sometimes operate below safe capacity. Documented incidents: 3 worker-incapacitation events in past six months from inadequate air circulation versus historical 1 event every 18-24 months.

**Material Volatility:** Flux-reactive mineral extraction occasionally triggers uncontrolled discharge. Recent incident (four months ago): spontaneous energy release injured two workers, collapsed tunnel section. Investigation identified understaffed supervision as contributing factor. [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] response: accelerated extraction continues with "enhanced protocols" (undefined).

**Supply Chain Constraints:** Timber for tunnel supports increasingly scarce due to competing military construction demand. Current supply 30% below operational requirements. Operations reduce support-structure density to maintain production schedules, increasing collapse risk. [[Voss Keld]] has formally requested additional timber allocation; requests pending with no response timeline.

## Labor Relations and Tensions

**Below-Market Wages:** Workers aware that Keln Ridge pays 20-30% below comparable mining operations elsewhere. Minimal turnover only because alternative employment similarly constrained by wartime conditions. Underground discussion of collective work-refusal developing but not yet coordinated action.

**Safety Protocol Degradation:** Experienced miners observe safety-practitioner understaffing and reduced maintenance intervals. Informal worker complaints to foremen increase; formal complaints to management rare (workers fear retaliation or termination). [[Voss Keld]] aware of safety concerns but trapped between [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] production demands and operational reality.

**Institutional Resentment:** Workers view Keln Ridge as exploitative institutional operation enriching [[Auralis|Auralis]] military capacity while offering inadequate compensation and dangerous conditions. No organized resistance yet but cultural resentment building.

## Production Targets and War Pressure

**Current Output Requirements:** [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] requisition: 750 tons annual iron ore (50% above pre-war production), 150 kilograms annual Flux-reactive materials (double pre-war output). Meeting targets requires extended shifts, reduced maintenance intervals, and reduced safety margins.

**Price Suppression:** Institutional purchasing at 4

 [[Watts]] per ton iron ore versus market rate 8-10 Watts. [[Voss Keld]] repeatedly advocated for market-rate compensation; told requisition pricing non-negotiable under military emergency authority.

**Casualty Acceptance:** [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] logistics directorate maintains internal casualty threshold: acceptable annual loss rate 15 workers per 1000 (versus current 36 per 1000). Keln Ridge operates above threshold but continues operation under military-priority exemption.

**Environmental Impact:** Tailings waste accumulating adjacent to shafts; groundwater contamination evident in downstream settlements. Long-term ecological consequences secondary to immediate military supply priority.

## Future Trajectory

Keln Ridge exemplifies unsustainable extraction under wartime acceleration. Three likely outcomes:

**Operational Collapse:** Current accelerated schedule creates increasing probability of catastrophic failure (major flooding, structural collapse, toxic-gas incident). Such failure would remove largest [[Auralis]] iron-ore source from production for 6-18 months recovery period.

**Labor Revolt:** Accumulated resentment over wages, safety, and institutional exploitation could trigger organized work-refusal or exodus. Even partial workforce departure would cripple production.

**Post-War Reckoning:** Assuming operations continue without catastrophic failure, post-war assessment will reveal depleted deposits, environmental degradation, and institutional distrust requiring decades to remediate.

[[Voss Keld]] privately recognizes operations are unsustainable but lacks authority to overrule [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] directives. His operational reports document safety concerns and casualty rates, creating documentary record for future accountability — or self-preservation should institutional scrutiny eventually arrive.
