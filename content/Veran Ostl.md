_(Council Member, Auralis — Culmination Faction)_

**Veran Ostl** controls a significant share of [[Auralis|Auralis's]] eastern port trade and supports the Culmination Faction because [[Drest]] told him to and the arrangement has been profitable. He would switch factions if the math changed. [[Drest]] knows this and keeps the math from changing.

## Character

Veran is the council's most transactional member, which is a quality he holds without apparent embarrassment. He does not pretend to believe in the Culmination goal. He does not pretend to care about the character of the people he votes with. He attends sessions, votes in the way that has been established as correct, and leaves. He is fifty-five, punctual, and has not arrived late to a session in nine years.

He is also, by most measures, the council's most honest member — not morally, but epistemically. He knows what he wants, knows why he wants it, and acts accordingly. There are no hidden depths to his position, no long-horizon calculation running beneath the visible one. What Veran does is what it appears to be. This transparency is disorienting in a body that runs primarily on indirection.

His [[Flux]] ability is T2, exactly sufficient for the transaction verification work that his trade operations require. He has occasionally mused, in private, that higher ability would not have made him better at what he does; money moves through the ledger at the same speed regardless of the tier of the person holding the rune.

## The Arrangement with Drest

The mechanism by which [[Drest]] keeps the math favorable for Veran is not a single arrangement but a pattern of small advantages accumulated over fourteen years: contract access, favorable arbitration outcomes, quiet diplomatic support for Ostl shipping routes that were in contested territories, introductions that opened relationships that would otherwise have been difficult. None of these required Drest to do anything that looked like a quid pro quo. The favor economy of a council is diffuse enough that the connections remain politely unclear.

Veran has thought about this arrangement from multiple angles and has not identified a clean exit that improves his position. He has therefore not looked for one with much urgency.
