The Maren Valley is the agricultural and river-transport hinterland of [[Valdenmoor]] — the broad, fertile floodplain surrounding the upper and middle [[Maren River]] that produces most of the food, fiber, and raw materials the capital consumes. It is not a defined administrative unit but a geographic and economic concept: the communities, farms, and river-transport infrastructure within roughly three days' travel of Valdenmoor that together constitute the city's supply base.

## Geography and Scale

The Maren River rises in the elevated terrain north of [[Greyveil]] and runs southeast through the settled belt before reaching Valdenmoor and continuing east toward [[Ashford]] and the coast. The valley floor on either side of the river's middle section — the reach between the farming communities upstream and the city itself — is flat, fertile, and subject to the spring flooding that the river's behavior and Valdenmoor's flood-control infrastructure are both designed around.

The valley contains several dozen farming communities, ranging from villages of a few hundred people to small towns of a few thousand. Each community has its own mix of agricultural production, but the overall pattern is grain-heavy: wheat and rye as the primary crops, with the river's seasonal flooding providing the soil replenishment that makes the land so consistently productive. Specialty crops, livestock, timber from the valley's northern edges, and wool from the upland communities above the floodplain add variety to what would otherwise be a monotonous trade profile.

## Supply to Valdenmoor

The valley's agricultural output reaches Valdenmoor through two main channels. Direct river transport — grain boats running downstream on the Maren's current, returning upstream with city goods, managed through the [[Maren Freight Chain]]'s dispatch scheduling — handles bulk commodity volume. Land transport by cart and team handles the smaller quantities of higher-value or time-sensitive goods that cannot wait for river schedule coordination.

The forward-contract system is central to how this supply chain functions. [[Noll Drenk]] and operators like him buy grain forward from valley communities during the growing season, providing early-season capital to farming communities that need operating funds before harvest, and taking on the price risk of whether the harvest will meet the contracted volume and quality. This transfer of risk is the service that factoring provides, and it is worth a margin that the system has sustained for long enough that the basic structure is stable.

Communities that sell forward get predictability. Factors who buy forward take variability. In good years, the factors do extremely well. In bad years — drought, flooding that exceeds the normal replenishment pattern, crop disease — the factors absorb losses that can, in severe cases, end careers. This distribution of risk means that the Maren Valley's agricultural economy is somewhat insulated from year-to-year volatility at the community level, with that volatility transferred up the supply chain to people who have enough capital to survive some years of loss.

## Labor Movement

The valley's seasonal labor dynamic shapes Valdenmoor's population in ways the city has adapted to over generations. Farming communities in the valley have different labor demands across the year — intensive during planting and harvest, significantly reduced in the winter months and the midsummer growing period when crops need maintenance but not intensive labor. People from these communities who need income during the slow periods come to Valdenmoor.

Valdenmoor absorbs this labor through the North Dock Market and similar daily hiring mechanisms in [[The Dock Wards]] and the outer manufacturing districts. The work is available, the rates are consistent, and the seasonal workers return to their communities when their agriculture demands it. The city has never tried to prevent this circulation and has, over time, built the infrastructure — boarding houses at appropriate price points, labor market mechanisms that work at daily scale, community networks that help seasonal workers find their footing quickly — that makes the circulation work efficiently.

## Rural Communities and the Capital

The Maren Valley communities are not simply Valdenmoor's agricultural appendage. They have their own social structures, their own guild arrangements adapted for agricultural rather than urban trade, and their own relationship with the [[Covenant of Measure]] that is somewhat different from Valdenmoor's — more informal, more integrated with seasonal farming rhythms, less organized around guild hall ceremonies and more around the practical community events of agricultural life.

Characters like [[Reth Caln]] and [[Solen Carr]] — rural Terravelle residents whose lives are defined by agricultural community rhythms and the particular pressures of rural tier assessment — represent the valley communities' lived experience as distinct from the capital's. The valley knows Valdenmoor without being Valdenmoor, and treats the city as the important market and administrative center it is without organizing life around it.

The flow of information through the valley to the capital is slower than commercial goods but more selective. News from [[Greyveil]] or the border regions reaches Valdenmoor first through travelers and then through institutional channels; the valley communities often know about events in the western farmlands before the capital's administrators do, simply because the communities are adjacent to the news while the capital is receiving reports.
