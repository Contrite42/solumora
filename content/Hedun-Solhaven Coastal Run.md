The Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run is the highest-frequency west-coast maritime corridor in [[Auralis]], moving passengers, perishables, and administrative dispatches between the harbor economies of [[Hedun]] and [[Solhaven]]. It is the route people use when they need arrival predictability more than they need absolute speed.

Unlike long-haul packet circuits, this run is built on repeatability. Captains operate short-turn vessels with fixed crew rotations, and dock authorities in both cities coordinate berth windows tightly enough that delays tend to stay measured in hours rather than days. The system works because both ports treat schedule credibility as part of their commercial identity.

Cargo profile drives route behavior. Hedun sends hospitality inputs, fish, and private contract traffic north; Solhaven sends administrative materials, certified tooling, and capital-linked procurement south. The line also carries a steady stream of workers moving seasonally between the two cities, which gives it social importance beyond freight value.

Weather is a known cost, not a surprise. Squall bands and surf conditions can force temporary holds, but the corridor has enough vessel depth that operators can stagger departures and preserve downstream commitments. Most large houses keep contingency clauses specifically calibrated to this run's historical delay pattern.
