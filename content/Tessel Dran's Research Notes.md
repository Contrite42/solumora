**On the scale problem**

The sites I have assessed personally and the site records I have reviewed from [[The Advancement Corps|Corps]] assessors share a feature that the formal literature describes as "scale anomalies" — installations whose construction required Flux work at volumes that cannot be explained by individual casters, even at the T8-T9 range, working sequentially.

The phrase "scale anomalies" is doing a great deal of work. What it means is: we don't understand how they built this.

The argument I keep making — the one the committee keeps requesting additional documentation for — is that the answer is not "more powerful casters" but "coordinated casting at a systems level." The [[Ancient Ruins|ancient civilization]] did not build these structures by having individuals with extraordinary capability. They built them by having multiple casters whose outputs were integrated into a single effect, held together by a coordination architecture that does not exist in either current kingdom's Flux theory.

The evidence for this is in the geometry. Ruins sites with the largest-scale installations show Flux residue patterns that are not the work of a single source. They are composite — multiple distinct casting signatures, phase-aligned in a way that requires deliberate timing and integration. Phase-aligned multiple-caster work at this precision is not something the current sigil system can achieve. It may be something that [[Hypertext]] is approaching a framework for, which is why I wrote to [[Mira Solv]] two years ago and received a polite reply that she was not currently in a position to share her ongoing work.

**On what the ruins are not saying**

The ruins do not show us the endpoint. They show us large, coordinated Flux installations — bridges, structural reinforcements, a handful of things that look like what I can only describe as Flux regulators, devices for managing ambient Flux density across a large area. They do not show us anything that resembles a weapon. They do not show us anything that resembles the kinds of applications the Culmination Faction seems most interested in.

I have said this at three review committee sessions. The committee has noted my concern.

The [[Equatorial Desert|Zakros desert]] anomalies are a different question. The three sites believed to be T9 failure remains — those are not installations. Those are catastrophic outputs. Whether they are failures from an attempt to build something large, or failures from an attempt to do something else entirely, the current evidence does not differentiate. I have a professional opinion about which is more likely. I have not written it in any document the committee will see.

**On the Corps field reports**

I receive Corps field assessment reports through the Academy's standard research feed. The reports are well-formatted, accurate in their measurements, and systematically incomplete in a way that has become more consistent over the past four years rather than less.

What is missing is not data. The measurement tables are thorough. What is missing is context — the surrounding conditions of the assessment, what else was present, what the assessor's actual observations were before they were translated into measurement categories. The categories have gotten more precise at the same time as the information available to fill them has narrowed.

I have met three Corps field assessors at Academy conferences. All three were considerably more observant and analytically capable than the reports they submitted. This is not a coincidence I have found an adequate explanation for that I am prepared to put in writing.
