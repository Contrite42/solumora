# Survivor Benefit Contracts

**Survivor Benefit Contracts** are payout agreements that specify what surviving dependents receive when an expedition member dies, disappears beyond recovery window, or is permanently incapacitated.

They are designed to reduce household collapse after expedition loss, but contract quality varies widely.

## Contract Structure

Most contracts define:

- covered relationship classes (spouse, children, dependents, designated kin)
- trigger conditions for payout eligibility
- payout schedules (lump sum, staged, or hybrid)
- exclusion clauses tied to misconduct or undeclared route deviation

The practical value of the contract is determined less by headline payout and more by trigger language precision.

## Payout Models

Three models are common:

- lump-sum death payout for immediate debt and housing stabilization
- staged support payments over 6-24 months
- mixed model with immediate base plus contingent supplements

Staged models reduce underwriter shock but expose households to adjudication delay risk.

## Link to Adjudication

All benefit contracts are downstream of [[Death Ledger Claim Adjudication]].

Even "automatic" payout contracts usually contain cause-class or documentation riders that allow partial delay. This creates a critical gap between household need timing and institutional release timing.

## Failure and Dispute Patterns

Frequent failure points include:

- beneficiary disputes from incomplete designation records
- exclusion invocation based on ambiguous route conduct
- payout reduction after cause reclassification
- documentation mismatch between recovery and claim files

These patterns often overlap with controls and false positives in [[Expedition Insurance Fraud Patterns]].

## Household Impact

Benefit contracts are one of the few mechanisms that can keep surviving households from immediate debt spirals.

When contracts perform as written, they preserve rent continuity, food access, and care follow-up. When they fail, households often enter emergency systems within weeks.

## Why They Matter

Survivor-benefit design is not only private finance detail. It is social stability infrastructure in high-casualty expedition economies.

If benefit performance falls, public systems absorb the difference through shelter, ration, and care pressure with higher long-term cost.
