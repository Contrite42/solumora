**Dess Tannor** is thirty-five years old and manages administrative operations for the [[Harbor District]] guild collective. T1. She is not a merchant herself but works as a coordinator and record-keeper for the dock workers' guilds, handling apprenticeship records, work schedules, wage disputes, and the administrative infrastructure that keeps the district's guild system functioning.

She was born in the [[Harbor District]] to a family of dockworkers and assessed as T1, which created several possible pathways. She could have done dock work enhanced by basic Flux capability. She could have migrated to other employment. She chose instead to develop administrative skills and sought positions where her organizational aptitude and tier capacity both had value.

The guild collective hired her twelve years ago to manage increasingly complex record-keeping. She has since expanded the role to coordinating training schedules, documenting disputes for resolution, and maintaining worker welfare records. She understands the political pressures that surround guild operations and has developed skill at representing worker interests without directly confronting merchant authority.

Dess is well-respected in the district but somewhat invisible to outsiders. The district functions smoothly partly because merchants can trust that disputes will be handled fairly and that workers' concerns are properly documented. Most of that smooth functioning is her work.

She lives with her long-term partner Kerim in shared quarters in the residential district. They have built a stable life around their income and their work. Desshas no ambition to leave the Harbor District, nor has she experienced pressure to do so. She is competent, valued, and secure in her position.

She has recently noticed an increase in complaint documentation related to seasonal work disruption — unusual patterns in the [[Hedun-Solhaven Coastal Run]] and [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] trading schedules. Other merchants have mentioned similar concern. She has recorded the data but has not escalated it beyond the guild level. It is the kind of thing that guild people track and worry about without necessarily involving authorities. If disruption becomes severe, she will have documentation ready.
