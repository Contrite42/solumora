**Ossen Drel** is forty-seven years old and has been running inter-kingdom trade for twenty-one years. He has crossed the [[Equatorial Desert]] seven times, always with a guide, twice with [[Avel Coss]].

## Appearance

Ossen is forty-seven with the weathered, road-worn appearance of someone who has spent twenty-one years running trade between kingdoms — lean from constant travel, sun-darkened from seven desert crossings and countless overland routes, carrying himself with the alert watchfulness of someone who moves between different legal jurisdictions regularly. His dark hair is graying and kept at a practical medium length, and his face shows the lines of someone who has learned to read border tensions and customs officials' moods before speaking.

He dresses in the practical style of inter-kingdom traders: neutral clothing that works in both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]], everything chosen to avoid drawing attention while still looking professional enough for merchant meetings. His boots show the particular wear pattern of someone who spends more time walking between contacts than sitting in offices, and he carries a worn leather case containing trade documentation, ledger notes, and twenty-one years of accumulated logistics observations. His hands are ink-stained from constant manifest work and show the calluses of cargo handling rather than manual labor — he manages shipments rather than lifting them himself.

When conducting business his manner is professionally neutral, revealing nothing about what he thinks of the goods being transported or why clients might prefer his services for particular consignments. He speaks in careful, measured statements that answer questions accurately without volunteering additional context — the verbal habit of someone who has learned that border crossings go more smoothly when you provide exactly what's asked for and nothing more. His expression when reviewing cargo requirements is analytical and practical, mentally calculating crossing logistics and cost variables rather than engaging with content questions.

Off-duty he has the slightly rootless quality of someone who maintains standing room arrangements rather than permanent residences, comfortable in transit spaces like Ashford's commercial districts but never quite settled anywhere. Contacts in both kingdoms note that he has an unusual ability to understand what each side wants and how to frame transactions accordingly — not political knowledge, but practical commercial intelligence accumulated through two decades of noticing differences between the kingdoms' priorities.

## Work

He carries luxury goods primarily — things with high value-to-weight ratios that justify the crossing cost. Specific commissions occasionally: items that travel better with someone who knows both sides of the desert and how questions get asked at each border. He does not ask detailed questions about the nature of these commissions. He asks about the cargo handling requirements and the destination contact, which are the things that affect whether the job goes smoothly.

His [[Control Tier|T2]] casting is used for practical trade work — reading [[Birth Runes|birth runes]], confirming ledger integrity, the standard commercial applications. Not exceptional. Adequate for the work, which is what T2 means in practice for most people who use it.

He keeps a trade notebook. The bulk of it is logistics: what things cost in [[Solhaven]], what the same things cost in [[Valdenmoor]], how the crossing timing affects prices on both ends, which contacts on each side have stayed reliable over time. Threaded through the logistics notes are observations about the differences between the kingdoms that he has found it useful to think about — not politically, but practically. The two kingdoms want different things. Knowing what they want is as useful as knowing what they're willing to pay, and sometimes these are not the same information.

He does not have a permanent residence in either kingdom. He has a standing room arrangement in [[Ashford]] and another in [[Halveth]] that serves the desert crossing logistics. He has owned these arrangements for fourteen years and considers them adequate.
