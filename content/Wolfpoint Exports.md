Wolfpoint produces three categories of export, and they are not equivalent in importance or in what Wolfpoint is willing to give away.

## Physical Exports

The settlement's primary physical exports are goods that its cold-region location and specialized skill set produce more efficiently than its trading partners can: cold-forge metalwork (a technique that produces harder blade and tool edges through temperature-differential annealing processes developed for Wolfpoint's climate), preserved game and dried fish from the northern hunting grounds that reach [[Ashford]] in better condition than coastal alternatives due to the natural preservation of the cold, and occasionally specialized wool textiles from the upland herding communities north of the settlement.

None of these are significant at the scale of [[Terravelle]]'s or [[Auralis]]'s full trade economies. They are enough to support the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]'s regular stops and to give Wolfpoint legitimate commercial justification for its presence in both kingdoms' trading records — a settlement with actual trade activity is different, politically, from a settlement that is purely a knowledge institution.

The metalwork is the most valued. Wolfpoint's cold-forge technique produces results that practitioners familiar with standard smithing find difficult to replicate, and there are buyers in both kingdoms willing to pay premium rates for blades and tools that hold edge better than typical production. This is not a large trade volume. It is a consistent one.

## Knowledge Exports

Wolfpoint's actual economic and political currency is [[Hypertext]] knowledge. The settlement does not sell it directly — there is no transaction in which a practitioner arrives with [[Watts]] and leaves with a Hypertext curriculum. What it exports is access: the opportunity to study at the [[The Wolfpoint Scholarium|Scholarium]], to work alongside Wolfpoint's researchers, and to leave with the partial knowledge that Scholarium access provides.

This access is priced in terms the settlement controls: the commitment of the visiting practitioner's time (typically one to three years minimum), a demonstrated research contribution during that time, agreement to the Scholarium's publication and teaching restrictions, and the ongoing relationship with Wolfpoint that those restrictions entail. A practitioner who has studied at Wolfpoint and left is not independent of Wolfpoint. They know partial sequences, they cannot teach freely, and they know that Wolfpoint can verify whether they have violated the access terms — because the settlement maintains relationships with enough practitioners in enough places to hear about unauthorized Hypertext teaching.

This export model means that Wolfpoint's Hypertext knowledge has propagated into both kingdoms without either kingdom having been able to extract and replicate it independently. There are practitioners in [[Solhaven]] and [[Valdenmoor]] who have been through the Scholarium and who apply Hypertext techniques in their work. None of them have the full system. All of them know that the full system lives in Wolfpoint.

## What Wolfpoint Does Not Export

Wolfpoint does not export the foundational documentation that [[Sera Voss]] is currently writing — the systematic account of Hypertext theory and structure that would, if published broadly, allow practitioners to learn the system without Scholarium access. This document exists in draft. Its publication status has not been determined.

Wolfpoint also does not export the results of its ruins-site research — the findings from expeditions that the Voss family and associated researchers have made into ruin sites north of the settled belt, findings that have led Sera Voss to conclusions about the relationship between [[Watts]], [[Hypertext]], and the ancient civilization that are not yet known outside a very small group. This information has not been offered to either kingdom, and neither kingdom knows how much of it exists.
