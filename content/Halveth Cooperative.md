The Halveth Cooperative is not a guild. It has no charter, no dues, no formal membership. It is a reputation network — a loose association of licensed desert guides, supply operators, and staging specialists centered on [[Halveth]] who share route intelligence, vet new practitioners, and maintain informal pricing standards for the crossing trade.

## Structure

The Cooperative's working unit is the verified guide. To be recognized within the network, a practitioner must have completed at least three successful desert crossings in either direction and be vouched by two existing members. These are not official requirements — there is no official body to set them — but an unvetted guide who attempts to book work through Cooperative contacts will find doors closed, and an expedition that hires outside the network takes on reputational and legal exposure if something goes wrong.

Day-to-day coordination happens through [[Selt Orvn]]'s supply depot, which serves as the informal clearinghouse. Selt does not run the Cooperative — no one does — but he holds more institutional memory than anyone else in Halveth, and operators tend to route through him when they need to match expeditions with available guides or check whether a new face has the credentials they claim.

## What They Sell

The Cooperative's primary product is survival probability. A guided crossing through the network has documented survival rates. A crossing without a Cooperative-vetted guide does not, and the record of those attempts is not encouraging.

Specific services:
- **Guide placement**: matching expedition parties to guides whose route specialization fits the specific crossing season and destination. [[Avel Coss]] handles a significant share of desert-edge and ruin-approach work.
- **Gear certification**: review and sign-off on expedition grimoire sets against current desert conditions. The Cooperative maintains an updated record of which thermal sigil configurations have worked in field conditions, distinct from what official expedition literature recommends. Selt's inventory tracks the gap.
- **Route intelligence**: current conditions on the major southern crossing approaches, updated from guide returns. Weather windows, rift activity near ruins sites, fauna movement patterns.
- **Emergency extraction**: the network maintains a standing informal agreement to redirect available guides toward rescue operations for a fixed fee, paid whether the extraction succeeds or not. This is the only formal-adjacent arrangement the Cooperative operates.

## Economics

Crossing guide fees are not cheap and are not expected to be. A full-expedition lead guide charges rates that reflect both the risk and the years of investment required to be competent. [[Auralis]]-funded expeditions pay without negotiating, because their budget lines assume the cost. Independent merchants and academic researchers negotiate harder and accept worse odds when they succeed.

The [[Advancement Corps]] has a standing arrangement with three Cooperative guides for annual expedition seasons. The arrangement is financially stable and quietly resented — Corps expeditions are managed affairs that do not reflect well in the post-season after-action reports, and guides who return from Corps work tend to have specific, careful things to say about what the official record claims happened versus what they observed.

## Relationship with Terravelle

[[Terravelle]] does not fund crossing expeditions through the Cooperative. Terravelle treats the desert as a problem to manage from a distance, not a resource to exploit. The [[Terravelle Desert Trade Office]] coordinates rare authorized crossings from the northern side and occasionally hires Cooperative guides for south-to-north passage, which requires a different route set than the reverse.

This is a cordial but arm's-length relationship. Cooperative guides have no fixed loyalty to either kingdom. They work for whoever is paying to cross and whoever has not previously gotten someone killed through preventable decisions.
