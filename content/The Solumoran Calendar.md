**The Solumoran Calendar** is the unified timekeeping system used across both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] for coordinating harvests, planning expeditions, scheduling commerce, and marking historical events. This is not arbitrary cultural convention — accurate shared timekeeping prevents trade failures, missed meetings, and logistical disasters when coordination spans hundreds of miles and weeks of travel time. The calendar emerged from pre-Contact astronomical observations that both kingdoms independently developed and later discovered were functionally identical, which enabled the immediate integration of time-based systems once [[The First Contact|Contact]] began.

## Time Units and Structure

### The Year

**365 days** — One complete orbital cycle around Solumora's sun.

**No leap years:** Orbital period is precisely 365 days (within measurement tolerance). Long-term astronomical drift has not been observed over recorded history.

**Notation:** Years counted from arbitrary historical origin point (see Historical Eras section below).

**Current Year:** 623 years since Foundation Era origin (abbreviated: 623 FE).

### The Month

**12 months of 30 days each, plus 5 transition days** — Months aligned with lunar cycle (Solumora has single large moon with 30-day period).

**Month Names:**
- **Colden** (mid-winter, coldest period in northern/southern latitudes)
- **Thawmark** (late winter, ice breakup begins northern settlements)
- **Firstgreen** (early spring, planting preparation)
- **Plantmoon** (mid-spring, planting season peak [[Terravelle]])
- **Brightward** (late spring, maximum daylight hours approach)
- **Highdry** (early summer, lowest rainfall temperate zones)
- **Harvestfirst** (mid-summer, first harvest early crops)
- **Peaklite** (late summer, longest days)
- **Harvestfall** (early autumn, main harvest grain crops)
- **Coolward** (mid-autumn, temperature declining)
- **Darkmoon** (late autumn, preparing winter storage)
- **Frostfast** (early winter, freeze-up northern waters)

**Transition Days (5 days between Frostfast and Colden):**
- Called "Turndays" — not part of formal month structure
- Traditional rest period (minimal work, settlement-focused activities)
- [[Festivals and Public Celebrations|Festival celebrations]] common during Turndays
- Used for calendar adjustments, year-end accounting, reflection

### The Week

**6-day weeks** — Five months contain 5 weeks, seven months contain 5 weeks (distributed evenly across year).

**Day Names:**
- **Firstday** — week beginning, traditional work start
- **Midweek** — middle of work period
- **Thirdday** — continuation
- **Longday** — longest traditional work day (agricultural societies)
- **Marketday** — traditional market/guild meeting day
- **Restday** — traditional rest (Ascendant Path observances, family time, minimal work)

**Cultural Variation:**
- [[Terravelle]]: Marketday and Restday more strongly observed (guild meetings reliably scheduled, rest enforced culturally)
- [[Auralis]]: Week structure looser (work continues when necessary, less ceremonial)

### The Day

**24 hours** — Divided into morning (6am-noon), afternoon (noon-6pm), evening (6pm-midnight), night (midnight-6am).

**Sunrise/Sunset Variation:**
- Equatorial region ([[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]): Minimal variation year-round (~12 hours daylight consistently)
- [[Terravelle]] northern latitude (35-50 degrees north): Moderate variation (9-15 hours daylight across year)
- [[Auralis]] southern latitude (35-50 degrees south): Moderate variation (9-15 hours daylight across year)
- [[Wolfpoint]] extreme northern latitude (60+ degrees): Significant variation (6-18 hours daylight, seasonal adjustment required)

## Seasonal Patterns

### Equatorial Climate ([[Desert Zakros]])

**Consistent intensity:** High heat year-round, minimal temperature variation (daily highs 40-50°C / 104-122°F).

**Dry and wet periods:** Not traditional seasons, but moisture availability cycles
- **Highdry through Darkmoon:** Lowest moisture, harshest crossing conditions
- **Frostfast through Firstgreen:** Occasional moisture, marginally better (still extremely hostile)

**Crossing Windows:** Determined by [[Rift Incursions]] patterns more than seasonal weather (moisture variation less significant than flux stability).

### Temperate Zone Climate ([[Terravelle]], [[Auralis]] Core)

**Colden through Thawmark (Winter):**
- [[Terravelle]]: Cold, snow common, river ice (Darmarkarian winters colder than southern [[Terravelle]])
- [[Auralis]]: Mild, occasional frost, minimal snow (more moderate than [[Terravelle]])

**Firstgreen through Brightward (Spring):**
- Temperature rising, rainfall increasing
- [[Terravelle]]: Planting season ([[Maren Valley]] agricultural cycle begins)
- [[Auralis]]: Coastal fishing season intensifies, maritime trade increases

**Highdry through Peaklite (Summer):**
- Warmest period, lowest rainfall (dry heat, not humid)
- [[Terravelle]]: Continued agricultural work, first harvests early crops
- [[Auralis]]: Peak maritime activity, desert expeditions (slightly more viable window)

**Harvestfall through Darkmoon (Autumn):**
- Temperature declining, harvest completion [[Terravelle]]
- [[Terravelle]]: Main harvest, preservation season, winter preparation
- [[Auralis]]: Maritime activity declining, stored goods accumulating

### Northern Extremes ([[Wolfpoint]], Arctic Territories)

**Harsh Winter (Colden through Firstgreen):**
- Extreme cold, heavy snow, maritime water freezing
- Isolation increases (coastal packet ships reduced frequency)
- Settlement focus on stored food, minimal external activity

**Brief Summer (Harvestfirst through Coolward):**
- Moderate warming, snow melt, maritime access restored
- Hunting season peak ([[Mountain Game Collective]] operations intensive)
- Expedition departures, supply ship arrivals, annual activity compression

**Transitional Periods (short):**
- Spring thaw rapid (weeks, not months)
- Autumn freeze-up rapid (minimal temperate transition)

### Coastal Maritime Patterns

**Storm Seasons:**
- **Thawmark through Plantmoon:** Higher storm frequency (spring storms, dangerous for fishing fleets)
- **Highdry through Peaklite:** Lower storm frequency (preferred [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] season)
- **Harvestfall through Colden:** Moderate storms (autumn cyclones occasional, winter storms intense but less frequent)

**[[Maritime Storm Coordination]]:** Operates year-round but most critical spring and autumn (transition period instability).

## Timekeeping Methods

### Astronomical Observation

Primary method for establishing absolute time across [[Solumora]].

**Solar Position:**
- Noon marked by sun zenith (highest point, shortest shadow)
- Sundials common in settlements (simple, accurate during daylight)
- Portable sundials for travelers (folding designs, adjustable for latitude)

**Lunar Phase:**
- 30-day lunar cycle visible (clear night skies most latitudes)
- Month boundaries aligned with new moon
- Lunar calendars maintained by [[Ascendant Path]] temples, guild  administrators, [[Covenant of Measure]] timekeepers

**Star Positions:**
- Navigational star patterns (maritime operations, desert crossing navigation)
- Seasonal star visibility (northern sky different from southern, equator sees both)
- Agricultural communities use star positions for planting/harvest timing

**Eclipse Tracking:**
- Lunar eclipses occasional (several per year, visible when moon aligned)
- Solar eclipses rare (every 2-3 years, narrow visibility path)
- Significant eclipses recorded in historical chronicles (used as era markers)

### Mechanical Timekeeping

Clocks and watches requiring regular maintenance and calibration.

**Water Clocks:**
- Controlled drip mechanisms marking hours
- Common in temples, guild halls, administrative offices
- Require refilling (maintenance burden) but reasonably accurate
- Cost: 150-300 [[Watts]] for quality installation

**Clockwork Mechanisms:**
- Spring-driven or weight-driven mechanical clocks
- Rare, expensive, require skilled craftspeople for construction/repair
- Urban elite households and institutions only
- Cost: 800-2000 Watts for quality clock
- [[Crestward District]] in [[Solhaven]] has public clock tower (visible/audible across upper terraces)

**Personal Watches:**
- Miniature mechanical timepie ces worn on body
- Extremely expensive (2000-5000 Watts), status symbol
- Accuracy variable (require frequent adjustment against astronomical observations)
- Only wealthy merchants, high-tier [[Flux Users]], senior officials own personal watches

### [[Flux]]-Assisted Timekeeping

Practitioners using Flux manipulation for precise time measurement.

**Flux-Oscillation Counters:**
- [[Control Tier|T3-T4]] practitioners create stable oscillating flux fields
- Count oscillations for precise duration measurement
- Used in laboratories, high-precision applications (expedition timing, alchemical processes)
- Not practical for daily civilian use (requires continuous practitioner attention)
- Cost: 50-100 Watts per hour of operation (practitioner time)

**Time-Binding Sigils:**
- Specialized [[Sigils|sigils]] that trigger at specified time intervals
- Used for medication timing, waking sleepers, event reminders
- [[Uncommon Grimoire]] technique (T2-T3 accessible)
- Duration limited (most reliable under 24 hours, accuracy degrades beyond that)
- Cost: 10-30 Watts per sigil inscription

### Informal and Relative Timekeeping

How ordinary populations track time without precise instruments.

**Shadow Length:**
- Estimating time by shadow position/length (works anywhere with sun visibility)
- Experienced individuals accurate within 30-60 minutes
- Agricultural workers, laborers, travelers use this constantly

**Work Rhythms:**
- Meals mark time (breakfast, midday meal, evening meal)
- Shift changes (guild work rotations, watch changes, patrol schedules)
- [[Tavern Culture and Social Drinking|Tavern opening/closing]] times

**Natural Indicators:**
- Bird calls (species-specific dawn/dusk vocalizations)
- Temperature patterns (coolest pre-dawn, warmest mid-afternoon)
- Tidal cycles (coastal settlements, twice daily rhythm)

**Approximate Language:**
- "Morning" (sunrise to noon)
- "Afternoon" (noon to evening)
- "Evening" (sunset to night)
- "Night" (darkness to dawn)
- "A week" (6 days)
- "A month" (30 days, roughly)
- "A season" (90 days, three months)

## Historical Eras and Dating System

### Foundation Era (Current, Year 1-Present)

**Era Origin:** Arbitrary administrative decision approximately 623 years ago.

**Historical Context:**
- Both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] were using different dating systems during [[The Isolation Period]]
- [[The First Contact|Contact Era]] required unified calendar for trade coordination
- Diplomatic negotiations established "Foundation Era" as neutral starting point
- Neither kingdom claims era commemorates their specific history (compromise solution)

**Notation:**
- **FE** (Foundation Era) — years since origin
- **Current year:** 623 FE
- Historical dates: Event in 580 FE, person born 595 FE, treaty signed 610 FE

### Pre-Foundation Dating (Relative)

For events before Foundation Era origin, both kingdoms use relative notation.

**"Years Before Foundation" (BF):**
- [[The Isolation Period]] began approximately 400 years before Foundation = ~-400 BF or 400 BF
- [[The Ancient Civilization]] collapse unknown date, estimated 1000+ BF
- Imprecise beyond 500 BF (conflicting chronicles, lost records, measurement errors accumulate)

**Kingdom-Specific Systems (Historical):**
- [[Terravelle]]: Used "Years Since Unity" (Terravelle kingdom formation)
- [[Auralis]]: Used "Ascendant Count" (religious dating system via [[Ascendant Path]])
- Both systems abandoned formally during Contact negotiations (records translated to FE retroactively, though conversion errors known to exist)

### Significant Historical Dates

**Approximate Timeline (all dates Foundation Era unless noted BF):**

**Pre-Foundation Era:**
- **Unknown** — [[The Ancient Civilization]] at peak (ruins in [[Desert Zakros]] constructed)
- **Unknown** — Ancient civilization collapse/disappearance ([[The Desert Barrier]] established)
- **~1000 BF** — Earliest evidence of human settlement [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] regions (archaeological dating imprecise)
- **~400 BF** — [[The Isolation Period]] begins ([[Desert Zakros]] becomes uncrossable barrier, kingdoms develop independently)
- **~250 BF** — [[The Grimoire Tradition]] emerges independently both kingdoms
- **~180 BF** — [[The Persecution Era]] peaks both kingdoms (witch-hunts, anti-Flux violence)
- **~50 BF** — Persecution Era ends, magical practice legally protected both kingdoms

**Foundation Era:**
- **1 FE** — Era origin established retroactively (administrative decision during Contact negotiations)
- **423 FE** — [[The First Contact]] between Auralis and Terravelle (first successful crossing by [[Auralis]] expedition)
- **430-480 FE** — Early Contact period (trade establishment, cross-border relations develop)
- **492 FE** — First Near-War ([[The Three Near-Wars|border tensions nearly escalate to conflict]])
- **540 FE** — Second Near-War (resource competition [[Desert Zakros]] expeditions)
- **580 FE** — Third Near-War (territorial disputes eastern coast)
- **590-610 FE** — Stable period (trade flourishes, cross-border cooperation peaks)
- **610-623 FE** — [[The Fraying Peace]] (current tensions escalating, war preparation increasing)
- **623 FE** — Present day

## Calendar Maintenance and Coordination

### Institutional Timekeepers

**[[Ascendant Path]] Temples ([[Auralis]]):**
- Maintain official lunar calendars
- Announce month boundaries, holy days, significant astronomical events
- Coordinate time-dependent rituals and ceremonies
- Archive historical records with precise dating

**[[Trade Guilds]] ([[Terravelle]]):**
- Maintain practical calendars for commercial coordination
- Schedule market days, harvest periods, shipping windows
- Coordinate seasonal labor across agricultural regions
- Less ceremonial than [[Ascendant Path]], more efficiency-focused

**Administrative Offices (Both Kingdoms):**
- Government record-keeping requires consistent dating
- Tax collection, legal proceedings, property transactions all date-stamped
- [[Council Orders Registry]] ([[Auralis]]) maintains chronological order archives
- [[Valdenmoor]] administrative offices track commercial contracts, guild agreements

**[[Covenant of Measure]] Community Coordinators ([[Terravelle]]):**
- Track agricultural cycles for planting/harvest timing
- Coordinate [[Festivals and Public Celebrations#Covenant of Measure Equinox Observations|equinox observations]] (precise astronomical timing matters)
- Maintain historical records emphasizing practical utility over ceremony

### Cross-Border Calendar Coordination

**Unified System Benefits:**
- Trade contracts specify delivery dates both kingdoms understand
- Expedition planning coordinates departure/arrival across kingdom borders
- [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]] schedules reliable (both kingdoms use same date notation)
- Historical events have consistent dating (reduces confusion, enables cooperation)

**Rare Discrepancies:**
- Isolated settlements sometimes lag behind calendar updates (month boundary errors)
- [[Wolfpoint]] extreme northern latitude creates practical challenges (seasonal variation enormous, formal calendar less relevant than local conditions)
- Some rural [[Terravelle]] communities use agricultural cycle as primary calendar (formal month names secondary to "planting time", "harvest time", "winter storage time")

## Practical Calendar Use

### Agriculture

**[[Terravelle]] [[Maren Valley]] Cycle:**
- **Firstgreen through Plantmoon:** Planting preparation and execution
- **Brightward through Harvestfirst:** Crop growth, maintenance, irrigation
- **Harvestfall through Coolward:** Main harvest, processing, storage
- **Darkmoon through Thawmark:** Winter storage, equipment repair, planning

**Calendar Critical:** Planting too early risks frost damage, too late reduces yield. Farmers track calendar precisely.

### Maritime Operations

**[[Outer Coast Packet Line]] Schedule:**
- **Highdry through Coolward:** Peak season (lower storm frequency, maximum trips)
- **Colden through Thawmark:** Reduced frequency (winter storms, ice hazards northern routes)
- **Thawmark/Plantmoon:** Storm season (dangerous, shipping reduced but not stopped)

**Calendar Critical:** Ships schedule departures around known storm seasons, cargo delivery dates contractually specified.

### Expedition Planning

**[[Desert Zakros]] Crossings:**
- Calendar less determinative than [[Rift Incursions]] patterns (flux activity varies unpredictably)
- **Thawmark through Brightward:** Historically better window (slightly lower rift activity)
- **Harvestfall through Colden:** Worse window (increased rift frequency observed)
- Expedition departure windows planned months in advance (supply staging, guide coordination, equipment prep)

**Calendar Critical:** Missing departure window delays expedition 30-60 days (next window waits for guide availability, supply refresh).

### Legal and Commercial

**Contract Specifications:**
- Delivery dates: "15 Harvestfall 623 FE" (precise, legally binding)
- Payment terms: "30 days from contract signing" (duration rather than absolute date)
- Lease agreements: "renewable annually 1 Colden" (predictable coordination)

**Calendar Critical:** Missed dates trigger penalties, defaults, legal disputes. Both kingdoms enforce time-stamped contracts.

### Personal and Social

**Life Events:**
- Births recorded by day/month/year (birth rune inscription includes date)
- Marriages often scheduled Brightward or Peaklite (traditional, pleasant weather)
- Deaths recorded precisely (legal, property transfer, family records)

**Festival Scheduling:**
- [[Festivals and Public Celebrations|Major festivals]] have fixed calendar dates
- Agricultural festivals float (depend on actual harvest completion, roughly Harvestfall)
- Religious observances precise (equinoxes, lunar events)

## Contemporary Timekeeping Challenges

### War Preparation Disruption

**Calendar Reliability Deteriorating:**
- Cross-border coordination declining (border tensions interfere with calendar maintenance cooperation)
- Some settlements prioritizing military schedules over civilian calendar (disrupts commercial predictability)
- Expedition windows politically contested ([[Auralis]] prioritizing military over civilian crossing access)

**Information Lag:**
- Calendar updates, astronomical observations, month boundary announcements travel slower (reduced courier frequency)
- Isolated settlements falling further behind official calendar (errors accumulating)

### Economic Pressure

**Institutional Timekeepers Strained:**
- [[Ascendant Path]] temples receiving less funding (resources diverted to war preparation)
- [[Trade Guilds]] calendar coordination reduced (fewer staff, budget cuts)
- Clock maintenance declining (skilled craftspeople conscripted, replacement parts scarce)

**Impact on Commerce:**
- Delivery date disputes increasing (calendar discrepancies between trading partners)
- Agricultural coordination harder (less reliable planting/harvest timing info)
- Maritime schedules less predictable (storm coordination degrading)
