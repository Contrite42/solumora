**Derin Olt** is twelve years old and lives in [[Greyveil]]. He was assessed six weeks ago. The result was [[Control Tier|T2]].

## Appearance

Derin is a twelve-year-old boy with the lanky build of someone in the middle of a growth spurt — all elbows and knees, not quite grown into his hands yet. He has light brown hair that his mother keeps trimmed short in the practical northern style, and the slightly uncertain posture of someone who recently became aware that people are paying attention to him in a new way.

He dresses in standard Greyveil youth clothing — sturdy trousers, work shirts in layers for variable northern weather, boots that are already getting too small and will need to be replaced soon. He carries his tier assessment documentation in a leather folder that his parents gave him with instructions to keep it safe, and he's developed a habit of checking that it's still in his bag more often than strictly necessary.

He has not yet developed the distinctive markers of any particular profession or guild track, though he's started unconsciously imitating the stance and mannerisms of various adults he encounters — trying on different possible futures. His face still has the open expressiveness of childhood, but there's a new thoughtfulness in the way he watches guild workers and practitioners conduct their business.

## Assessment Context

The assessment was conducted by a traveling assessor who came through Greyveil on the autumn circuit. It took forty minutes. The assessor ran through the exercises in the standard sequence, noted the result, asked Derin if he had questions, answered the questions accurately, provided the documentation to his parents, and left for the next settlement on the circuit. The whole thing was straightforward in a way Derin had not expected it to be, given how much everyone had talked about it.

What he was told afterward: T2 is a solid result. It means standard guild track eligibility. His parents are both T2, so this was in the expected range. He can pursue most of the trades that require Flux certification, with a tier ceiling that works fine for most practical applications. He should start thinking about which guild track makes sense given what he's good at.

This is accurate and complete information. He knows it is accurate and complete. He also knows that his friend in the year above him assessed T5 last spring and left for Valdenmoor in the fall, and the comparison exists in the back of his mind as a fact he has not figured out what to do with. Not that T5 was something he wanted. Just that T2 is now a specific number rather than the open range it was before, and the number has a specific location in the world's accounting of what people are, and he is inside it now rather than before it.

He has not said any of this to his parents. They are clearly relieved. He doesn't want to complicate the relief.
