Grimoire Repair and Preservation is the specialized practice of maintaining, restoring, and protecting [[Grimoires]] from physical degradation. Grimoires are working tools used repeatedly over years or decades, and constant handling, environmental exposure, and accidental damage all degrade their functionality. Because [[Flux]] casting requires precise [[Sigils|sigil]] geometry, even minor ink fading or page warping can cause spell failure. Repair and preservation services exist to extend grimoire lifespan and maintain casting reliability.

## Why It Matters

A [[Common Grimoire]] used daily by a farming household experiences significant wear: pages turn repeatedly, ink is exposed to humidity and temperature fluctuations, bindings loosen, and accidental spills or tears occur. After 5-10 years of use, sigils may fade to the point where they no longer function reliably, and the grimoire must either be repaired or replaced.

Replacement is straightforward for [[Common Grimoire|Common]] texts — commissioning a new copy through the [[Guild Scrivener Network]] or an [[Independent Scrivener]] costs 200-300 [[Watts]]. But for [[Rare Grimoire|higher-tier]] grimoires, [[Uncommon Grimoire|specialized texts]], or grimoires with personal annotations and margin notes accumulated over years, replacement is expensive or impractical. Repair becomes the preferred option.

## Ink Restoration

The most common repair task is sigil re-inking. Over time, ink fades due to light exposure, handling, and environmental conditions. A faded sigil may still function but with reduced reliability or increased [[Flux]] cost due to geometric imprecision. Restoration involves carefully tracing over the original sigil with fresh ink, matching the line weight and geometry exactly.

This requires significant skill. A restoration specialist must have sufficient [[Control Tier]] to understand the sigil structure, steady hand control to trace accurately, and access to high-quality inks that won't degrade quickly. Poor restoration — lines too thick, geometry slightly off, ink chemistry mismatched — can make a grimoire worse than it was before.

Most restoration specialists are T2-T3 [[Flux Users]] working within the [[Guild Scrivener Network]]. They charge per sigil restored, with pricing similar to original copying: 3-6W per [[Common Grimoire]] sigil, 10-20W for [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]]. Rush restoration costs more, as does work on higher-tier or fragile texts.

## Binding Repair

Grimoire bindings fail from repeated opening and closing, exposure to moisture, and general wear. A failed binding risks page loss or damage, rendering the grimoire partially unusable. Binding repair involves re-stitching pages, replacing damaged covers, or fully rebinding the text into new materials.

Rebinding is cheaper than re-commissioning a grimoire but still represents significant cost: 30-60W for a simple [[Common Grimoire]] rebind, 80-150W for larger or more complex texts. Some owners choose to rebind grimoires as part of inheritance or gift-giving, adding decorative elements or durable materials to signal the text's importance.

Binding repair doesn't require [[Flux]] capability — it's a bookbinding craft skill. Many restoration specialists partner with bookbinders who handle structural work while the specialist handles sigil restoration.

## Environmental Preservation

Long-term preservation involves protecting grimoires from degradation before it occurs. Institutional libraries in [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] use preservation spells like [[Permanentpreserve]] or [[Preserveseal]] to lock grimoire pages against environmental damage. These spells are expensive (T4-T5 sigils with ongoing [[Flux]] cost) but effective, and are typically reserved for [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] or [[Legendary Grimoire|Legendary]] texts held in permanent collections.

Private owners use simpler methods: storing grimoires in sealed boxes away from light and moisture, using [[Dryseal]] or similar low-tier spells to protect pages during transport, and handling texts with clean hands to reduce ink contact. These methods slow degradation but don't prevent it entirely.

Some preservation specialists offer enchanted storage solutions: boxes or cases with embedded [[Preserveseal]] sigils that maintain stable temperature and humidity. These are expensive (100-200W for a small case, more for larger storage) but extend grimoire lifespan significantly, especially in harsh climates like the [[Equatorial Desert]] border regions.

## Restoration Disputes

Restoration work carries risk for both specialist and owner. A poorly executed restoration can damage a grimoire irreparably, and disputes over whether restoration was done correctly are common. The [[Guild Scrivener Network]] handles arbitration for guild-affiliated specialists, testing restored sigils and comparing them to documented standards.

Independent restoration specialists face higher dispute risk. An owner claiming poor restoration has limited recourse beyond reputation damage and refusal to pay. Some specialists require signed agreements acknowledging restoration risk, especially for fragile or high-value texts.

Fraud is rare but exists: a specialist might claim to fully restore a grimoire while only partially re-inking faded sigils, or use low-quality inks that degrade faster than traditional materials. Buyers without [[Flux]] expertise often can't verify restoration quality immediately and only discover problems months or years later when sigils fail.

## Higher-Tier Grimoire Challenges

[[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] and [[Legendary Grimoire|Legendary]] texts require specialized restoration that few practitioners can perform. A T5 or T6 sigil restoration demands matching [[Control Tier]] capability, extensive knowledge of sigil theory, and access to authenticated source texts for comparison. Most higher-tier restoration is institutional: done by academy specialists or guild master restorers, not private practitioners.

High-tier grimoires also carry higher risk. A single error in restoration can render an expensive spell useless, and replacement may not be possible if the source text is restricted or lost. This makes high-tier owners cautious about when and how to pursue restoration, often delaying until absolutely necessary.
