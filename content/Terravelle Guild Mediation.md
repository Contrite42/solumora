**Terravelle Guild Mediation** resolves disputes within [[Terravelle]]'s guild-organized professional communities through elder panels, collective testimony, and resolutions focused on restoring function rather than punishing wrongdoing. It is not a formal legal system in the [[Auralis Civic Courts|Auralis sense]] but rather an embedded conflict resolution structure operating within professional networks.

## How It Works

When guild members have disputes — contract disagreements, professional misconduct, resource conflicts, fraud accusations — they bring the matter to guild elders rather than formal courts. Guild elders are experienced practitioners with long standing in their trade, typically 15-25 years into their careers with master status and reputations for fair judgment.

**Mediation Process:**

1. **Complaint Filing:** The aggrieved party requests mediation through their guild's administrative office. They provide written complaint describing the dispute, involved parties, and desired resolution.

2. **Elder Panel Assignment:** The guild assigns 3-5 elders to hear the case. Elders with direct conflicts of interest (family relations, business partnerships, personal feuds with involved parties) recuse themselves.

3. **Testimony Gathering:** Both parties present their accounts. Elders question them, request clarifying details, and call witnesses if needed. Testimony is verbal, not documented with formal transcripts. The focus is understanding what happened, not building formal evidence records.

4. **Deliberation:** Elders discuss privately, assess credibility, determine what likely occurred, and decide on resolution. Deliberation typically takes hours to days, not weeks. Speed is valued — prolonged disputes disrupt guild function.

5. **Resolution:** Elders announce their decision. Resolutions commonly include: financial restitution to wronged parties, public acknowledgment of fault, temporary suspension from guild activities, mandatory supervision for future work, or in serious cases, guild expulsion.

6. **Compliance:** Parties are expected to comply voluntarily. Refusal to accept mediation outcomes results in reputation collapse and guild expulsion. Non-compliance is rare because guild membership is economically essential for most practitioners.

## What Gets Mediated

Guild mediation handles:

**Contract Disputes:** A client claims a [[Flux Users#Formulists|Formulist]] performed substandard work. Mediation assesses whether work met guild standards, determines if refund or corrective work is appropriate.

**Professional Misconduct:** An apprentice accuses their master of withholding promised instruction or abusive behavior. Elders investigate, interview both parties and other apprentices, and determine if the master violated guild training standards.

**Resource Conflicts:** Two guild members dispute access to shared workshop space, equipment, or client bases. Mediation establishes fair allocation or recommends organizational changes.

**Fraud and Theft:** A guild member is accused of defrauding clients or stealing from other practitioners. Mediation assesses evidence, determines restitution, and decides whether expulsion is warranted.

**Inter-Guild Disputes:** When conflicts involve members of different guilds (Maritime Guild and Thermal Guild disputing who has authority over ship heating systems), cross-guild elder panels convene to resolve jurisdictional questions.

## What Doesn't Get Mediated

Guild mediation does not handle:

**Serious Violent Crime:** Murder, severe assault, or crimes causing permanent harm escalate to formal Terravelle courts. Guilds have no authority to impose imprisonment or handle cases where victims demand formal prosecution.

**Disputes Involving Non-Guild Members:** Mediation works within guild networks. A dispute between a guild member and a non-affiliated party requires formal court resolution.

**Flux Crimes Beyond Guild Detection:** If a case requires [[Control Tier|T4+]] detection capabilities and no guild elders possess that capacity, the case escalates to formal courts or remains unresolved.

**Cross-Border Crimes:** Guild mediation has no authority over [[Auralis]] parties or crimes committed outside Terravelle territory.

## Enforcement Through Reputation

Guild mediation's power derives from reputation consequences rather than institutional force. A practitioner who refuses to comply with elder resolutions cannot be physically compelled by guilds, but their professional life collapses.

**Guild Expulsion:** Expelled members lose guild affiliation, which means:
- No access to guild client referral networks (primary source of contracts for most practitioners)
- No ability to train apprentices (apprenticeship requires guild standing)
- No professional certification (guilds do not recognize independent practitioners)
- Loss of workshop access, shared resources, and collective purchasing arrangements

**Reputation Propagation:** Guilds communicate internally and across guild networks. A practitioner expelled from Thermal Guild in [[Solhaven]] cannot simply relocate to [[Wolfpoint]] and start over. Maritime Guild members in Wolfpoint will hear about the expulsion through professional networks. The expelled practitioner is known and avoided.

**Professional Death:** For guild-affiliated practitioners, expulsion effectively ends careers. Alternative income options are limited — unskilled labor, non-guild trades, or relocation to areas with weak guild presence. Most expelled practitioners never recover professional standing.

This reputation enforcement is more severe than formal imprisonment in many cases. A practitioner who serves two years in Auralis prison can resume their career afterward. A practitioner expelled from their guild cannot.

## Cultural Perspective

[[Terravelle]] views guild mediation as superior to [[Auralis]]'s institutional courts because:

**Speed:** Mediation resolves disputes in days or weeks. Formal court processes take months or years.

**Community Understanding:** Guild elders know the professional context, understand technical details, and can assess credibility based on personal knowledge of involved parties. Formal judges rely on abstract evidence without direct professional understanding.

**Restorative Focus:** Mediation prioritizes restoring community function and compensating victims. Formal courts prioritize punishing offenders, which does not always help victims.

**Flexibility:** Elders adapt resolutions to specific circumstances rather than applying standardized sentencing guidelines. This flexibility allows creative solutions that formal systems cannot manage.

[[Auralis]] views guild mediation as nepotistic, inconsistent, and lacking procedural protection. Outcomes depend on which elders hear the case, and elders with biases can impose unfair resolutions without formal appeal processes. Terravelle practitioners argue that professional reputation accountability prevents elder abuse — elders who make unfair rulings lose standing and are not invited to future panels.

## Limitations

Guild mediation cannot:
- Compel parties outside guild structures to participate
- Impose imprisonment or physical punishment
- Handle crimes requiring capabilities beyond elder detection capacity
- Function when guild networks are absent (rural areas with no guild presence)
- Prevent wealthy or politically connected practitioners from leveraging influence with elders
- Provide written precedent or documentation for future reference

These limitations are accepted trade-offs. Guild mediation is faster, more adaptive, and more embedded in professional life than formal courts. It sacrifices institutional consistency for practical speed and community understanding.

## Relationship with Formal Courts

Terravelle has formal courts handling cases guild mediation cannot address. When mediation fails (parties refuse to participate, elders cannot reach resolution, cases exceed guild authority), disputes escalate to courts operating similarly to [[Auralis Civic Courts]] but with less procedural formality.

Most Terravelle citizens never interact with formal courts because guild mediation resolves disputes within professional networks, and community-level conflicts are handled through informal neighbor mediation rather than institutional processes.

## Practical Example

**Case:** A Thermal Guild journeyman is accused of installing defective heating sigils in a client's warehouse. The sigils failed after two weeks, causing temperature-sensitive goods to spoil. Client demands full reimbursement (8,000 [[Watts]]) plus compensation for lost goods (12,000 Watts).

**Mediation:** Three Thermal Guild elders investigate. They inspect the failed sigils, interview the journeyman and client, review the installation contract, and assess whether failure was due to journeyman incompetence or client misuse (warehouse staff may have interfered with sigils).

**Finding:** Elders determine the journeyman used inadequate anchoring technique, which caused premature failure. Not deliberate misconduct, but professional incompetence.

**Resolution:** Journeyman must:
- Refund the 8,000 Watts installation fee
- Compensate client 6,000 Watts for spoiled goods (50% of claimed loss — elders assess client shares responsibility for poor monitoring)
- Work under supervision for six months to correct technique issues
- Publicly acknowledge the error at next guild meeting

Client accepts resolution (partial compensation is better than prolonged dispute and no relationship with other Thermal Guild members). Journeyman accepts resolution (refusal would mean expulsion and career collapse). Case resolves in ten days from complaint to final resolution.

Formal courts would take months, require extensive evidence documentation, and produce outcomes focused on legal liability rather than practical function restoration. Guild mediation prioritizes getting both parties back to work with damage compensated and professional standards reinforced.
