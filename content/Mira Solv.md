Mira Solv is the council's designated correspondent with [[Wolfpoint]], appointed to that role by [[Drest]] for her capacity to maintain methodical, patient engagement over long distances. She is forty-four, holds a seat in the [[Council Members|Culmination Faction]], and is considered one of the quieter members of [[The Council of Auralis]] — which is not the same as being unimportant.

## Character

Solv does not attract attention in full council sessions. She speaks rarely, asks careful questions when she does, and listens to other people's certainties with a neutrality that could indicate agreement, skepticism, or simple patience, depending on what she does afterward. Drest selected her for the Wolfpoint correspondence because she is organized, persistent, and reliably discreet.

She is all three of those things. She is also not entirely predictable in how she applies them.

## Role on the Council

Her formal function is to maintain the council's correspondence with Wolfpoint on [[Drest]]'s behalf — drafting letters, cataloguing responses, and relaying summaries as appropriate. The position was considered administrative when she was given it. Seven years of it has made it something more.

Seven years of correspondence with [[Sera Voss]] has produced a relationship that is not a friendship exactly, but is considerably more than an exchange of polite formal letters. Solv has developed a detailed picture of Wolfpoint — its culture, its capabilities, its priorities, and the particular way its current head thinks about [[Hypertext]] and what it might become. She has not shared all of this with Drest.

## What She Has Withheld

[[Sera Voss]] has been sharing more than status updates. Over seven years, the correspondence has drifted into the theoretical foundations of [[Hypertext]] — not the document itself, which Sera has not sent and will not send through a council channel, but the reasoning underneath it: why the linear sequence works where geometric shapes fail, what the symbol vocabulary encodes that sigils cannot, and what it would mean for Flux practice if the method became transmissible at scale.

Solv has not passed this to [[Drest]]. She understands enough of what she has been given to know that the underlying theory of Hypertext is not a small thing, and she does not trust what the Culmination faction would do with it. She has not deceived Drest in her summaries — she has simply not included everything. She has not yet found a framework that satisfies her for who should know, when, and why.

She is aware this position cannot hold indefinitely.

## Relationships

Drest regards her as reliable. He is not entirely wrong. She reports to him accurately within the scope of what she has decided to report, which is not the same as the scope of what she knows.

Her seven years of correspondence with [[Sera Voss]] have produced a degree of mutual understanding she has not described to anyone on the council. Sera's letters are careful and noncommittal on the subject of the document. They are considerably less guarded on other subjects.
