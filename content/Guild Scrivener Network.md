The Guild Scrivener Network is the primary institutional infrastructure for [[Grimoires|grimoire]] production and distribution across [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]. Most commercially available grimoires at the [[Common Grimoire]] and [[Uncommon Grimoire]] tiers are copied by guild-registered scriveners working under standardized contracts, quality benchmarks, and pricing schedules.

## How It Functions

Guild scriveners are [[Flux Users]] with sufficient [[Control Tier]] to reliably reproduce [[Sigils]] without error. They work from authenticated source texts maintained by guild libraries, producing copies on commission or for inventory. Each copy is inspected before release — poor sigil transcription results in financial penalties and, in repeat cases, suspension.

Payment is structured per page, with multipliers for tier complexity. A [[Common Grimoire]] page costs around 5-8 [[Watts]] to commission; an [[Uncommon Grimoire]] page may cost 15-25W depending on regional demand. Rush orders, custom bindings, and margin annotations increase the price further.

Most guild scriveners are T2 or T3 practitioners. Higher-tier work exists but is rarer and handled through separate contracts with institutional buyers rather than the public market.

## Regional Differences

In [[Terravelle]], the scrivener network operates through [[Trade Guilds]] and is tightly integrated with river logistics. [[Valdenmoor]] maintains the kingdom's largest authenticated source library, and copies circulate outward via [[Maren Freight Chain]] to regional guild halls in [[Ashford]], [[Greyveil]], and rural townships.

In [[Auralis]], the network is less centralized. Guild halls exist in major cities like [[Solhaven]] and [[Hedun]], but private workshops and [[Flux Users|Scholar]]-led copyist cooperatives handle a significant share of production. Quality variance is higher, and authentication disputes are more common.

## What It Controls

The guild network doesn't have a legal monopoly, but it controls access to authenticated source texts and maintains the reputation infrastructure that buyers rely on. Independent copyists can work outside the network, but they face higher scrutiny and lower trust unless they build individual reputations over years.

Cross-border text flow is filtered through guild agreements. A scrivener in [[Terravelle]] working from an [[Auralis]]-sourced grimoire must verify authenticity or risk copying a fraudulent version. The network maintains a shared authentication registry, though it's updated slowly and disputes take months to resolve.
