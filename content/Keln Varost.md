**Keln Varost** is the [[Terravelle]] Crown's standing envoy to [[Wolfpoint]], based primarily in [[Greyveil]] with regular travel to the far-northern settlement. He is fifty-two, T2, and has held the position for eleven years.

## The Role

Crown envoy to Wolfpoint is a narrow position in Terravelle's administrative hierarchy — not a prestigious posting, not a stepping-stone to advancement, and not a role that people in [[Valdenmoor]] think about frequently. Wolfpoint is far from anything, produces goods in modest volumes, and has consistently declined to become Terravelle's problem. The Crown's relationship with the settlement is managed rather than developed, and Keln's actual job is to maintain the channel through which communication flows without the relationship becoming either so close that Wolfpoint loses its independence or so distant that [[Auralis]] steps into the gap.

He meets with the Voss family representatives — typically [[Ysel Voss]] for external relations, occasionally [[Sera Voss]] herself — on a quarterly schedule, with additional contact when either side has specific information to convey. These meetings cover trade logistics, the Outer Coast Packet Line schedule adjustments that affect Wolfpoint's commercial access, and the occasional intelligence update that Terravelle shares on Auralis's current orientation toward the northern settlement.

The intelligence sharing is deliberate. Terravelle's Crown has made a calculated decision to give Wolfpoint enough information about Auralis's attention to keep the Voss family appropriately cautious about Auralis's intentions — without overstating the threat in ways that would make Wolfpoint feel it needs to choose sides. The goal is to maintain the situation where Wolfpoint treats both kingdoms as approximately equivalent alternatives, which keeps it genuinely independent.

## The Information He Has

Keln knows more about Wolfpoint's actual capabilities than almost anyone outside the settlement. Eleven years of quarterly meetings with people who are careful about what they share have still produced a cumulative picture — not the full technical picture of what [[Hypertext]] can do, but the strategic picture of where Wolfpoint's research is going and what the Voss family's priorities are.

He knows that Wolfpoint's interest in the ruins sites is more active than the settlement's public posture suggests. He knows that Mira Solv's relationship with Drest is something the Voss family is managing rather than endorsing. He knows that Sera Voss has been working on a document that would, if published, fundamentally change the Hypertext access situation. He has not reported this last item to the Crown in terms that convey its significance, because he has concluded — based on eleven years of relationship with the Voss family — that signaling the Crown's awareness of it would change Wolfpoint's behavior in ways that would be unpredictable and probably unhelpful.

This judgment about what to report and what to hold is the most significant decision his role requires. He makes it under no formal authorization and in the awareness that if he is wrong about the tradeoff, he will have withheld strategically important information from Terravelle's government. He considers this an acceptable professional risk.

## Character

Keln is comfortable with ambiguity and patient with timelines that most administrators find frustratingly slow. This is not indifference; it is the disposition his role has selected for over eleven years. A relationship managed across quarterly meetings and eleven hundred miles of northern terrain cannot be pushed faster than the parties on both ends decide to move, and pushing tends to produce the kind of defensive caution that makes the relationship less useful.

He likes Wolfpoint. He respects what the Voss family has built — the functional settlement, the genuine knowledge, the careful management of an independence that most larger powers would not have left undisturbed this long. This affection does not interfere with his work, because his work is to keep the relationship in balance rather than to advance any particular outcome. But it means he is not entirely neutral, and he knows it.
