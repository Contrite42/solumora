**Celde Morv** runs a food provisioning business in [[Solhaven]]'s [[The Middling Steps|Middling Steps]], supplying both harbor-level crews and middle-terrace households. He is thirty-eight, T1, and has operated the business for eleven years.

## The Business

Provisioning exists in the gap between the harbor's bulk trade economy and the Steps' household service economy. Celde buys volume from harbor-level wholesale suppliers — grain, salt goods, preserved fish, seasonal produce from the coastal traders — and redistributes it in two directions: to merchant ships and fishing vessels provisioning for departure, and to the households and small restaurants of the middle terraces who want consistent quality supply without negotiating directly with harbor-level suppliers.

The harbor-side work is high volume and low margin. Ship provisioning is competitive — there are several operators serving the same dock zone — and the contracts go to whoever can guarantee quantity, timing, and acceptable quality without creating complications. Celde has eight harbor-side accounts. He has held most of them for more than five years, which in the provisioning trade represents loyalty that was earned through not failing on timeline or specification.

The terrace-side work is lower volume and higher margin. A Middling Steps household account wants reliability, consistent quality above the harbor baseline, and the specific items that middle-terrace cooking uses that harbor wholesale doesn't prioritize. These accounts pay better and require more skilled sourcing. Celde has twelve terrace accounts. Two of them are in the upper Steps, close enough to the Crestward that he occasionally gets asked whether he supplies Crestward households. He does not. The quality standards and purchasing practices there operate through a different network, and the capital required to participate in it would change his business's structure in ways he has not decided whether to pursue.

## The Tier Question

T1 is functional for provisioning work — basic preservation sigils, routine quality assessment, standard documentation. It does not carry the social weight of T2-T3 professional certification in the Steps' working economy, and Celde is aware of this mismatch continuously. His clients are mostly T2-T4. His suppliers are mixed. The social assumption in the Steps is that a T1 running a business this size either had family capital behind the start or got lucky with a specific contract. Neither is true in his case, which he knows and does not correct when the assumption is made.

He has developed a specific kind of professional composure around operating below his clients' tier level while depending on their accounts. It is not performed deference — he does not defer on business questions where he knows he is correct — but it is a calibration of when to press a point and when to let the social hierarchy have its expected outcome. He finds this calculation tiring and has gotten very good at it.

## Character

Celde is organized and direct. His business requires managing simultaneous time pressures from two different economic zones with different expectations about communication frequency and response time, and he has built operational habits that keep him consistently reliable to both. He is not particularly interested in the social dynamics of the Steps beyond what they require him to understand for business purposes.

His family is in [[Hedun]]. He returned to Solhaven after an early career in coastal trade when the provisioning opportunity became available. The distance is not particularly emotionally charged — the family relationships are functional rather than close — but it means his Solhaven social network is professional rather than personal, which he considers a reasonable outcome for a person who works twelve-hour days.

He is aware that his business occupies a position that requires him to maintain good relationships across a social divide most Steps residents cross only by moving up. He has not romanticized this. It is useful positioning and it requires constant maintenance, and both of those things are simply true.
