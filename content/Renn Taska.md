# Renn Taska

**Renn Taska** directs [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]], the institution responsible for maintaining [[Wolfpoint]]'s economic independence through careful management of export sales, import contracts, and trade relationships with both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]. She is fifty-one years old, [[Control Tier|Tier 5]], and has held the director position for fourteen years. Her job is ensuring Wolfpoint remains valuable enough to trade with, not valuable enough to conquer, and diversified enough to avoid capture by either kingdom.

Wolfpoint's independence is not guaranteed by military strength or geographic isolation. It is maintained through economic strategy. Renn executes that strategy.

## The Independence Calculation

Renn tracks three metrics explicitly:

**Export revenue by buyer kingdom:** Target ratio is 55-65% Terravelle, 35-45% Auralis. This reflects Terravelle's geographic proximity while avoiding excessive dependency on either kingdom. When ratios drift outside target ranges, Renn adjusts: declining new contracts from over-represented kingdoms, seeking buyers in under-represented markets.

**Import supply by source:** No single [[Ashford]] merchant controls more than 40% of any critical supply category (grain, salt, tools). [[Kess Rolv]] manages these relationships under Renn's oversight. Diversification prevents merchant leverage from becoming settlement vulnerability.

**Annual trade balance:** Export revenue should cover 100-110% of import costs, maintaining modest currency reserves without accumulating wealth that might attract kingdom attention. Wolfpoint's strategic value depends on appearing productive but not wealthy.

These metrics are documented in internal ledgers Renn maintains personally. She reviews them quarterly and adjusts trade strategy when patterns indicate drift toward dangerous dependency.

## Strategic Decision-Making

Renn's fourteen years have built deep institutional knowledge of trade relationships and the political dynamics shaping them. Her role is strategic decision-making that affects Wolfpoint's long-term positioning:

**Contract negotiations:** When major buyers request exclusive supply agreements or expanded volume commitments, Renn evaluates whether accepting would create strategic vulnerability. Often, she declines profitable short-term deals that would increase dependency.

**Crisis management:** When trade disruptions occur (maritime delays, merchant failures, buyer relationship breakdowns), Renn coordinates response across [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]], [[Mountain Game Collective]], [[Upland Herding Network]], and [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] relationships.

**Voss line coordination:** When trade decisions intersect with [[Hypertext]] knowledge-export policy, Renn coordinates with [[Sera Voss]] to ensure economic strategy and knowledge-control strategy remain aligned.

## Relationship with Production Operations

Renn coordinates with but does not control Wolfpoint's export producers:

**[[Trel Vann]]** ([[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]]): Quarterly meetings to review market demand, discuss pricing adjustments, negotiate production allocation changes. Trel maintains operational autonomy. Renn provides market intelligence that informs his decisions.

**[[Dren Vass]]** ([[Mountain Game Collective]]): Monthly coordination about harvest volumes, preservation capacity, salt supply adequacy. Renn ensures [[Kess Rolv]] negotiates sufficient salt contracts to support preservation operations.

**[[Lenna Tors]]** ([[Upland Herding Network]]): Quarterly reviews of wool quality trends, supply projections, grain allocation priorities for herding families. Renn sets allocation policy. Lenna implements it.

These relationships are collaborative. Production operations existed before Renn's tenure and would continue if leadership changed. The Registry's value is coordination expertise and external relationship management, not control authority.

## What She Knows But Does Not Say

Fourteen years of trade coordination provide intelligence most Wolfpoint residents never see:

**[[Auralis]] interest in Hypertext:** Renn tracks which [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] factions probe most aggressively for expanded knowledge access, which intermediaries they use, how inquiry patterns escalate. This intelligence informs Voss line decisions about what to release and to whom.

**[[Terravelle]] complacency:** Renn monitors Terravelle's assessment that Wolfpoint is "not yet their problem." She knows this depends on Hypertext remaining unproven as large-scale trade commodity. If that changes, Terravelle's calculus shifts dangerous ly. The Registry ensures trade relationships do not become threatening enough to trigger pragmatic interest in direct control.

**Buyer network intelligence:** Through Ashford merchant relationships managed by Kess Rolv and correspondence with southern buyers, Renn maintains informal awareness of market conditions, political shifts, economic trends in both kingdoms. This supplements formal intelligence channels and helps anticipate changes before they become crises.

The Registry does not publish these observations. Renn uses them to inform decisions that keep Wolfpoint independent.

## Daily Reality

Most of Renn's work is documentation and correspondence: reviewing quarterly production reports, drafting buyer correspondence, coordinating with Kess about contract negotiations, tracking payment flows, updating trade balance ledgers, and analyzing patterns that might signal emerging risks.

She meets regularly with production managers (Trel, Dren, Lenna) to ensure coordination between Registry strategy and operational reality. She maintains direct communication with key packet-line captains about cargo priorities and schedule reliability. She coordinates with Sera Voss when Hypertext policy decisions affect trade relationships.

The work is methodical, sustained, and mostly invisible. Success means nothing breaks. Renn's fourteen years without settlement-threatening trade failures represent sustained competence that most residents take for granted because crisis prevention does not produce dramatic success stories.

## Why She Does This

Renn could retire. Fourteen years in the director position, sustained success, institutional respect. The Registry would promote one of the assistant coordinators or recruit from outside.

She stays because the alternative is worse. If someone less experienced runs the Registry, the independence calculation degrades: bad contracts get signed, dependency ratios drift, kingdom attention escalates before anyone notices. Wolfpoint survives through careful economic management. Renn provides that management.

Strategic planning is not heroic work. It is sustained attention to metrics most people never see, relationships that take years to build, and decisions that prevent problems before they require dramatic solutions.

She reviews next quarter's export projections. She confirms grain reserve levels with Kess. She drafts correspondence to an Auralis buyer requesting volume expansion (polite decline with alternative proposal attached). The ledgers update. The trade continues. Wolfpoint remains independent.
