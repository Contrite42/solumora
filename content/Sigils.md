Sigils are the deliberate, visual frameworks that shape [[Flux]] into a defined form. Each sigil is a precise geometric design — drawn, etched, or inscribed — representing a specific [[Spells|spell]] structure. To cast a spell using a sigil, a caster must have both knowledge of the sigil and sufficient [[Flux]] capacity to activate it, as measured by [[Control Tier]].

Each sigil encodes spell attributes within its geometry through [[Spell Variables]] — [[Hook]], [[Mode]], [[Control Tier]], [[Discipline]], [[Output Mode]], [[Geometry Pattern]], [[Reach]], [[Persistence]], and [[Target Spec]] are all encoded in the sigil's structure. The Shape of the sigil determines how many of these outer variables are explicitly controlled and how many fall back to defaults. Sigils are stored in [[Grimoires]] or carved onto objects, allowing for prepared spellcasting by anyone who meets the tier requirement.

## Creating a Sigil

Sigil creation is a meticulous process blending art, knowledge, and precision. A caster or [[Flux Users|Scholar]] first conceptualizes the desired spell effect, then encodes its attributes into geometric components — lines, vertices, and curves — each representing a specific variable from the [[Spell Variables|Scholar system]]. Each sigil must be drawn with accuracy, as small deviations can alter or fail the spell entirely.

[[Flux Users|Scribes]] copy and reproduce existing sigils for mass distribution. [[Flux Users|Scholars]] design new ones. The distinction matters — copying is craft, design is research, and the two roles are rarely held by the same person.

Once completed, a sigil serves as a stable conduit: when activated, it channels the exact [[Flux]] needed to manifest the intended spell.
