## Notes

Fluxhold (Conditional) stores [[Flux]] the same way [[Fluxhold]] does, but holds it behind a defined trigger rather than releasing it on demand. The condition is set at inscription — a temperature threshold, a physical impact, a proximity rule, a time window — and the reservoir discharges its full stored Flux the moment that condition is satisfied.

This makes it the standard component in trap sigils, delayed-release mechanisms, and any spellwork that needs a Flux burst at a specific moment rather than a steady draw. It is commonly paired with a Trigger [[Hook]] sigil on the same object, with the conditional reservoir feeding the trigger's activation.

The rune burns out completely on discharge regardless of how much Flux it had stored. A conditional reservoir that never triggers will hold indefinitely, slowly topping up from ambient [[Flux]] until the condition is finally met.
