# Emergency Reintegration Mandate

The **Emergency Reintegration Mandate** is a rapid-restoration protocol adopted after major coexistence enforcement failures, designed to stabilize affected communities before grievance cascades harden into long-term institutional refusal. It is the operational companion to the [[Coexistence Enforcement Failure Review Board]], translating review findings into immediate civic repair actions.

## Purpose

When enforcement overreach occurs, legal correction alone does not restore trust. The Mandate addresses the gap between:
- **Legal reset** (detentions voided, sanctions reversed)
- **Social reset** (residents willing to cooperate with institutions again)

The Mandate assumes that unaddressed humiliation, disruption, and reputational harm produce lasting non-compliance even when formal records are corrected.

## Activation Conditions

Triggered automatically when all are true:
- Review Board threshold event confirmed
- Tribunal voids or revises 25%+ of incident actions
- District-level participation metrics drop below continuity floor for two reporting cycles

Activation cannot be blocked by local officials under investigation.

## Core Measures

**72-Hour Community Repair Window**
- Public acknowledgment hearing with named institutional representation
- Temporary halt on non-critical enforcement operations
- Fast-track correction of civil records affected by false positives

**Reintegration Service Teams**
- Joint deployment from Commission civic mediators, Tribunal legal clerks, and local infrastructure coordinators
- Door-to-door remediation for licensing, school placement, guild access, and travel permissions disrupted by erroneous actions

**Civic Continuity Guarantees**
- Temporary safeguards for residents who challenge incident conduct
- Priority processing for cross-border work permits in affected districts
- Auditable compensation channels for documented loss of work or service access

**Trust Rebuild Monitoring**
- Weekly participation and grievance metrics for ninety days
- Public dashboard reporting whether recovery targets are being met

## Relationship to Existing Framework

The Mandate does not alter the three-tier model of the [[Regional Enforcement Variance Doctrine]]. It overlays emergency repair obligations onto whichever tier an affected district currently occupies.

In practice this means:
- Full Integration Zones receive intensified procedural transparency requirements
- Transitional Compliance Zones receive expanded mediation resources
- Protected Observation Zones receive non-intrusive remediation options that do not force cultural integration commitments

## Political Friction

Some Tribunal operators view the Mandate as incentivizing strategic victimhood: if communities can force enforcement pauses by amplifying grievance narratives, institutions may lose deterrence posture.

Counterargument from [[Jess Maren]] and border operators: deterrence without legitimacy decays faster than temporary procedural restraint. The Mandate treats trust as critical infrastructure, not optional sentiment.

## Two-Year Assessment

Initial implementation data indicates:
- Faster return to baseline cooperation in districts receiving full Mandate deployment
- Lower repeat-incident rates where reintegration teams operated jointly rather than agency-siloed
- Persistent resistance in communities with unaddressed persecution-lineage trauma, reinforcing need for [[Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum]] continuity

The Mandate is now considered a permanent safety layer within post-reveal governance architecture.
