# Upland Herding Network

The **Upland Herding Network** is the distributed sheep and goat herding operation north and east of [[Wolfpoint]], providing the settlement with wool textiles, supplementary meat, and dairy products that cold-zone agriculture cannot produce. It is not a formal institution with headquarters or centralized management. It is a network of family herding operations coordinated through seasonal agreements, range-sharing protocols, and trade relationships that have stabilized over multiple generations.

The Network sustains approximately 40-50 families across scattered upland holdings, collectively managing sheep and goat populations that provide [[Wolfpoint]]'s textile exports and a significant portion of its non-grain food supply.

## Why Herding Works Here

[[Wolfpoint]]'s constant 45-degree climate and open upland terrain create conditions where herding succeeds while crop agriculture mostly fails:
- **Year-round grazing** (no snow cover, consistent grass growth in wind-protected valleys).
- **Predator-manageable territory** (open sightlines make large predator detection easier than in forested regions).
- **Low disease pressure** (cold temperatures reduce parasite and pathogen survival compared to warmer pastoral zones).

Sheep and goats adapted to cold-region conditions thrive here. Grain crops do not. This makes herding the primary productive food system in Wolfpoint's surrounding territory.

## The Network Structure

The Network is not owned by [[Wolfpoint]] or governed by the Voss line. It exists independently, operating through family-based herding groups that maintain semi-permanent camps in upland valleys where grass quality and water access support sustained grazing.

**Range coordination** happens through seasonal meetings where herding families negotiate which valleys each group will use, how overlapping territories get shared, and how population health signals (disease outbreaks, predator pressure, grass degradation) propagate through the Network. These meetings have no formal authority structure. They function through reputation: families that violate range agreements or mismanage their herds lose access to shared resources and favorable trade terms with Wolfpoint buyers.

**Wolfpoint integration** occurs through trade relationships. Herding families bring wool, meat, and dairy products to Wolfpoint's trade posts, exchange them for grain, salt, tools, and cloth that the Network cannot produce internally, and return to upland camps to continue operations. The trade is sustained and reciprocal — Wolfpoint depends on the Network for textile exports and supplementary food, the Network depends on Wolfpoint for access to southern trade goods that only maritime connections can provide.

## What the Network Produces

**Primary Output: Wool**
Sheep herding dominates the Network because wool is Wolfpoint's textile export product. Cold-adapted sheep produce thick, dense wool that southern textile processors value for cold-weather garments and specialty fabrics. Annual wool production: approximately 8,000-10,000 pounds across the full Network, processed at Wolfpoint's textile workshop and shipped south through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]].

Wool quality varies by family and by seasonal conditions. The best-quality production comes from herding families with stable multi-year range access and experience managing breeding for fleece density rather than just herd size. [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] negotiates preferential pricing with families that deliver consistent high-quality wool, creating economic incentive for quality management.

**Secondary Output: Meat**
Goat herding provides supplementary meat that complements [[Mountain Game Collective]]'s preserved game supply. Goats adapt better than sheep to rougher upland terrain and provide more reliable meat yield per animal. Network families slaughter seasonally (autumn culls to reduce winter feeding burden) and bring preserved or fresh meat to Wolfpoint trade posts.

This meat serves mostly internal Wolfpoint consumption rather than export markets — volume is too variable and quality less premium than Mountain Game Collective's managed hunting operations.

**Tertiary Output: Dairy**
Goat milk and cheese provide protein supplementation for both Network families and Wolfpoint's internal consumption. Dairy production is highly seasonal (spring and early summer when grazing quality peaks) and does not travel well, making it primarily a local food source rather than trade commodity.

## Key Participants

**[[Lenna Tors]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 2]], 52 years old) coordinates wool trade between the Network and Wolfpoint's textile workshop. She is not a herding family member — she runs Wolfpoint's receiving operation, evaluating incoming wool quality, negotiating pricing with herding families, and managing the timing so textile processing capacity matches wool availability.

Lenna's role is preventing bottlenecks: if too much wool arrives simultaneously, processing capacity saturates and families wait days for payment. If wool delivery is too sparse, textile workers sit idle and export commitments risk delays. She adjusts pricing signals and delivery schedules to smooth flow across the seasonal wool cycle.

**Herding families** operate independently with minimal formal structure. The most stable operations are multi-generation family groups with documented range agreements and proven herd management skills. These families form the Network's core — they deliver the highest wool quality, maintain the most reliable trade relationships with Wolfpoint, and anchor range coordination during seasonal meetings.

Newer herding operations (families expanding into upland herding from other economic activities, or immigrants from southern regions attempting cold-zone pastoral work) face steeper learning curves. Some succeed and integrate into the Network. Many fail within 2-3 years and abandon herding for other work.

## Range Coordination with Mountain Game Collective

The Network and [[Mountain Game Collective]] share overlapping territory in Wolfpoint's upland zones. Both operations depend on range health — sheep overgrazing degrades hunting habitat, excessive hunting pressure disrupts grazing patterns.

Coordination happens through informal agreements mediated by Wolfpoint trade coordinators (primarily [[Dren Vass]] for the Collective and Lenna Tors for the Network). The two operations maintain communication about range use, adjust seasonal patterns to minimize conflict, and share population intelligence when disease outbreaks or predator pressure affect both herding and game populations.

This coordination is imperfect but functional. Both operations recognize that territorial conflict would damage the productivity both depend on. Cooperation is cheaper than competition.

## Economic Position

The Upland Herding Network generates approximately 20-25% of [[Wolfpoint]]'s physical export revenue through wool textiles, and provides significant internal food supply through supplementary meat and dairy production.

The Network's economic value to Wolfpoint is dual-purpose:
- **Export revenue** through wool that becomes finished textiles shipped south.
- **Food security** through meat and dairy that reduce Wolfpoint's import dependence for protein.

[[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] treats the Network as strategic asset, ensuring herding families receive consistent trade access, competitive pricing for quality wool, and priority allocation of imported goods (grain, salt, tools) needed to sustain upland operations. This preferential relationship maintains Network stability and prevents herding families from seeking alternative southern trade connections that would weaken Wolfpoint's economic position.

## Supply Dependencies

The Network's primary dependency is **grain**. Herding families cannot produce their own grain in cold upland zones. They depend on Wolfpoint trade access to grain imported from [[Ashford]] through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]].

When maritime trade disruptions reduce Wolfpoint's grain imports, herding families face immediate food security pressure. [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] manages this risk by maintaining grain reserves (funded through wool export revenue) and prioritizing herding families for grain allocation during shortage periods.

The Network is sustainable as long as [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] operates reliably. If maritime trade degrades significantly, the Network would face structural pressure — herding families might migrate south seeking more reliable food access, and Wolfpoint would lose both textile exports and supplementary food supply.

## What Makes This Work

The Upland Herding Network functions because:
- Cold-zone climate creates herding advantages that offset agricultural limitations.
- Wolfpoint provides trade access that herding families cannot replicate independently (maritime connections to southern markets).
- Network families maintain operational autonomy while coordinating through reputation-based agreements.
- Both Wolfpoint and herding families recognize mutual dependency and manage the relationship accordingly.

It is not a extractive relationship. It is a reciprocal one that has stabilized through seven generations of iteration.

Lenna Tors evaluates the wool. The herding families negotiate pricing. The textile workers process the fleece into finished goods. The packet ships carry it south.

The system continues.
