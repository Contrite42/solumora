A spell’s **[[Shape]]** is the outer geometry of its sigil. Shape does *not* describe the spell’s effect directly—Shape describes **how many variables the sigil explicitly controls**. The more sides, the more control points exist for encoding variables. Fewer sides means faster, safer, and easier-to-copy spellwriting, but with more attributes falling back to defaults.

Shape is paired with the center core: [[Hook]], [[Mode]], and [[Control Tier]].

## Shape Levels (Control Surface)

- **[[Circle]]** — 6 outer points + center  
  Full control of all outer variables:  
  [[Discipline]], [[Output Mode]], [[Geometry Pattern]], [[Reach]], [[Persistence]], [[Target Spec]]

- **Pentagon** — 5 outer points + center  
  One outer variable is omitted → it resolves to defaults.

- **Square** — 4 outer points + center  
  Two outer variables are omitted → they resolve to defaults.

- **Triangle** — 3 outer points + center  
  Minimal control. Only three outer variables are explicitly controlled (typically the most important), and the rest resolve to defaults.

## Defaults (when a variable is omitted)

When a shape does not include a variable, it resolves to the standard defaults:

- [[Discipline]] → Raw  
- [[Output Mode]] → Raw  
- [[Geometry Pattern]] → Plane  
- [[Reach]] → Self  
- [[Persistence]] → Immediate  
- [[Target Spec]] → Where Written

## Why cultures refine shapes over time

The oldest versions of a spell are often recorded as a **[[Circle]]** because it captures maximum control and is easiest to analyze, test, and correct. As the spell becomes better understood, scholars “compress” it into smaller shapes by standardizing assumptions and removing optional knobs—creating faster, safer, mass-producible versions suitable for [[Grimoires|grimoires]].

## Practical notes

- Larger shapes are more flexible and configurable.  
- Smaller shapes are easier to reproduce and harder to misuse.  
- A spell can be high [[Control Tier]] at any shape; Shape controls *how many knobs exist*, Tier controls *how hard the spell is to hold safely*.
