The Council of Auralis is the body of twenty that governs [[Solumora]]'s southern kingdom in the name of Queen [[Cassia]]. It is not a democratic institution, a meritocratic one, or an ancient tradition. It is the political entity that coalesced in the aftermath of the previous queen's death in childbirth — assembled by [[Drest]] in the chaotic weeks after Cassia's elevation, and gradually transformed, over two decades of internal maneuvering, into the actual power in [[Auralis]].

The council knows what it is. It does not discuss this openly.

## Structure and Factions

The twenty members divide into three factions with distinct and occasionally competing goals. Alliances shift on specific votes but the factions themselves are stable — they represent genuine differences in what council members want, not mere personality conflicts.

### The Culmination Faction
The largest faction, eleven members, organized around the council's defining long-term goal: the recovery and completion of whatever the pre-modern civilization in [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] was building. The faction's working belief — shared with varying degrees of sophistication across its members — is that the ancient civilization achieved or nearly achieved something that defeats natural decline, a [[Flux]]-based mechanism that the current system cannot replicate but the ruins still hold the key to. [[Drest]] leads this faction. He is its loudest voice and its most committed believer.

The Culmination Faction funds desert expeditions, supports the [[Emberfall]] research institutions, and pushes for territorial expansion primarily insofar as it secures resources and political stability for the long project. They are not warmongers. They simply have a goal that requires Auralis to remain powerful and intact for as long as the work takes.

### The Expansion Faction
Six members, organized around the straightforward goal of growing [[Auralis]]'s territory, population, and economic base. They are the most conventionally political faction — interested in trade routes, border security, military capacity, and the kind of kingdom-building that does not require ancient mysteries to justify. They support the Culmination Faction when the goals align and diverge when they don't.

The Expansion Faction is responsible for most of the active pressure on [[Terravelle]] — the trade route disruptions, the border friction, the slow accumulation of strategic advantage that Eddan has been managing for decades. They consider this normal statecraft.

### Cassia's Faction
Three members who back [[Cassia]] with genuine loyalty: [[Maren Sollis]], an older woman who served under the previous queen and transferred her allegiance to the infant who survived her; [[Tovan Ashce]], a mid-tier caster whose family has been part of Auralis governance for four generations and who considers the council's current direction a corruption of the institution; and [[Drest]] himself, whose support for Cassia is complicated by everything documented on his own page but is structurally real.

Maren Sollis supports Cassia openly and pays a social cost for it — she is the least influential faction member by most measures, and her advocacy for the queen draws attention to the queen's weakness rather than building her strength. Tovan Ashce's support is quieter and more useful — he votes strategically, withholds information from the other factions selectively, and has twice blocked council actions that would have materially reduced Cassia's formal authority.

Drest supports Cassia loudly in moments that cost him nothing and quietly in moments that cost him something. The ratio is imperfect. It is better than nothing.

## What They Want

The council's open goal, stated in its founding charter and repeated at formal sessions, is the advancement of [[Auralis]] toward a position of unmatched knowledge and capability in [[Flux]] mastery. The corollary — the thing that charter language is designed to describe without naming — is the completion of the ancient project: a mechanism that defeats death, or aging, or the finality of things. The exact parameters of what they are seeking are not agreed upon even within the Culmination Faction.

[[Cassia]] knows the goal is something like a fountain of youth. She does not know whether this is possible, likely, or simply the story twenty powerful people have told themselves to justify two decades of control. She suspects the council is not uniformly certain either, and that some of them have been sustaining the pursuit through political momentum as much as genuine belief.

She has not said this to anyone.

## Relationship with Cassia

The council does not hate [[Cassia]]. Several members are genuinely fond of her. The dynamic is more uncomfortable than hostility — she is the face of their project, the legitimacy they borrow every time they act in the kingdom's name, and the one variable in their system they have never fully controlled because she has never fully broken.

They gave her luxury and withheld power. This was the correct calculation for a four-year-old. At twenty-three, the calculation is less obviously correct, and the more perceptive council members know it.
