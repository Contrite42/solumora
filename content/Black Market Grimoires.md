The black market for [[Grimoires|grimoires]] operates in the space between what the official channels distribute and what practitioners actually want. It is not a unified organization — it is a collection of parallel supply chains, each operating around a different gap in the formal market.

## What Circulates Underground

**Out-of-tier texts**: the most commercially significant category. [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]]-tier and above grimoires are not freely sold in either kingdom — [[Terravelle]] holds them in institutional libraries, and [[Auralis]] circulates them through Scholar-network patronage arrangements that require institutional membership. A T5 practitioner who has no institutional affiliation and wants access to a Rare-tier text faces a choice between working within the patronage system, acquiring it through informal channels, or going without.

The informal channel exists. Rare-tier copies circulate through a network of traders, private collectors, and practitioners who hold the originals and are willing to sell or lend access for the right price. The copies are not certified and often are not high quality — they are produced from whatever master the seller had access to, under conditions that prioritize discretion over precision.

**Restricted discipline spells**: both kingdoms maintain lists of spell disciplines that are restricted in specific contexts — detailed mind-affecting spells, certain high-power Flux-manipulation tools, specific medical applications that are regulated in Auralis. These restrictions create demand for unauthorized copies. The supply follows the demand.

**Cross-kingdom access**: spells that are licensed and freely distributed in one kingdom but controlled in the other. During periods of political friction — and the current period qualifies — this category grows. An [[Auralis]] practitioner who wants a Terravelle-originated agricultural or engineering spell that has not been formally distributed south of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] will find it in the informal market at a significant markup over what a Terravelle guild copy would cost.

**Uncommon copies for below-threshold buyers**: practitioners who are close to a tier threshold but have not achieved it, who purchase Uncommon copies hoping repeated exposure or partial activation will accelerate development. Most practitioners report this does not work the way buyers expect. The market exists regardless.

## Supply Chains

The black market for grimoires is not primarily operated by criminals in the sense of people who specialize in crime. It is operated primarily by practitioners and traders who have access to originals and treat the informal market as a revenue supplement to their legitimate operations. A traveling merchant who carries authorized Common copies as their declared inventory and a few Uncommon or Rare copies in a sealed case is a common profile.

The [[Relay Compact]] handles some grimoire transport as part of its general cargo service across [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] — it does not ask about contents and does not specialize in grimoires, but the nature of the cargo that moves through undeclared channels includes texts that would not clear a formal inspection.

## Regulatory Interest

Both kingdoms are aware the informal market exists. Neither has invested heavily in suppressing it. The calculation is roughly the same on both sides: the Common tier is not worth suppressing because it doesn't need to be — it circulates freely through official channels already. The Rare-tier black market is small enough and serves practitioner needs that are real enough that aggressive suppression would generate more political friction than it resolves. The middle range — Uncommon, and cross-kingdom access — is the active regulatory area, but enforcement is episodic and targeted at specific actors rather than systematic.

[[Osten Reld]], the Expansion Faction's low-level operative in Solhaven, has used informal grimoire channels as a supplementary coordination tool — moving restricted texts as cover cargo for information transfer. He is not the only person doing this. He is one of the more careful ones.
