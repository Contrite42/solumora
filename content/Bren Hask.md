**Bren Hask** is the harbormaster of [[Wolfpoint Harbor]], the person responsible for coordinating all maritime arrivals, cargo handling, and the logistical interface between Wolfpoint's isolated economy and the southern kingdoms that keep it supplied. He has held this position for nine years. He is T2, 38 years old, and came to Wolfpoint from [[Ashford]] as a ship's mate before deciding the far north suited him better than coastal city life.

## Background

Bren worked the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] route for six years before taking the harbormaster role. This background gives him institutional knowledge that Wolfpoint values: he knows southern merchant houses, their reliability patterns, which captains can be trusted with sensitive cargo, and which traders will attempt to extract information or favorable terms from what they perceive asover-dependent northern settlement. He knows the difference between routine commercial inquiry and intelligence gathering masked as trade conversation.

This is not theoretical knowledge. Bren has watched [[Auralis]] merchants ask careful questions about [[Hypertext]] development while negotiating salt contracts. He has seen [[Terravelle]] envoys inspect cargo manifests with the thoroughness of people verifying treaty compliance rather than commercial accuracy. Wolfpoint's harbor is not just a port — it is the settlement's primary strategic vulnerability, and Bren runs it accordingly.

## Operations

Bren coordinates ship schedules, cargo manifests, loading operations, and security protocols. The [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] delivers supplies every 6-8 weeks in summer, extending to 10-12 weeks in winter when storms delay coastal navigation. When ships are overdue, Bren is the first person the settlement looks to for answers — whether the delay is weather, mechanical, or political.

He maintains premium wages for the eight-person dock crew specifically to reduce the temptation to skim valuable cargo or sell shipment information to southern traders. He verifies all cargo manifests against [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] records before authorizing unloading. He seals outbound shipments and requires witness signatures to prevent mid-transit substitution of genuine [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] metalwork for inferior copies.

Loading schedules are arranged so no ship carries export cargo from only one economic operation — every departure includes mixed goods from the Cold-Forge, [[Mountain Game Collective]], and [[Upland Herding Network]]. This ensures multiple Wolfpoint operations track whether shipments reach destination and makes theft less profitable. If a ship carrying only cold-forge metalwork disappeared, the loss would be concentrated. Mixed cargo spreads risk and creates overlapping monitoring.

## Strategic Role

Bren's operational conservatism is not personality — it is policy. The harbor is where Wolfpoint is most vulnerable to external pressure. A maritime blockade would collapse the settlement's economy within months. Coordinated raids could seize high-value exports or disrupt supply chains long enough to force Wolfpoint into formal alignment with one kingdom or the other.

He cooperates with kingdom inspection requests to the extent required to maintain trade access. [[Keln Varost]] arrives every few months from [[Greyveil]] to verify [[Terravelle]]'s trade relationships remain balanced. [[Auralis]] sends less formal observers — traders who ask careful questions, practitioners who spend time at [[The Wolfpoint Scholarium]] and report back through channels Bren knows exist but does not formally acknowledge.

His reports to [[Renn Taska]] are frequent and specific. When a merchant asks unusual questions, Bren documents it. When a ship arrives off-schedule, he tracks why. When cargo volumes from one kingdom drift significantly above or below target ratios, he flags it. [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] maintains economic independence through careful balance, and Bren provides the intelligence that makes that balance possible.

## Personal Position

Bren chose Wolfpoint deliberately. Ashford offered better pay, more comfortable climate, and proximity to larger commercial networks. Wolfpoint offered something he valued more: clarity of purpose. The harbor role is not ambiguous. Success means ships arrive on schedule, cargo moves efficiently, and no external party gains strategic leverage through the port. Failure means the settlement's survival becomes dependent on kingdom goodwill rather than mutual benefit.

He lives near the harbor in a wind-resistant stone house with heating sigils that run year-round. His salary is paid by the [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] from export revenue — his compensation is directly tied to the settlement's economic performance, which aligns his incentives with Wolfpoint's strategic interests. This is not accidental. [[Renn Taska]] structured compensation for all critical operations (harbor, Cold-Forge, preservation, trade coordination) so the people managing Wolfpoint's independence benefit materially from maintaining it.

Bren does not socialize extensively within the settlement. He spends working hours at the harbor and evenings reviewing shipping records, manifest discrepancies, and delivery timing patterns. When practitioners arrive from the south to study at the Scholarium, Bren is the first person they meet — the harbormaster who verifies their passage, confirms their stated purpose matches Registry records, and provides initial orientation to Wolfpoint's expectations about information security.

He is polite, efficient, and reveals nothing. This is not unfriendliness. It is the operational posture of someone who understands that every conversation at the harbor is potentially strategic, and casual disclosure can create vulnerabilities that take years to repair.
