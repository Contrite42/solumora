# Wolfpoint Trade Registry

The **Wolfpoint Trade Registry** is [[Wolfpoint]]'s trade coordination institution, responsible for managing the settlement's export sales, import contracts, and the economic balancing act that maintains Wolfpoint's independence from both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]. It is not a large operation — four permanent staff, seasonal clerks during peak trade periods — but it controls the commercial relationships that determine whether Wolfpoint survives as independent settlement or becomes economically captured by kingdom interests.

The Registry's mandate is strategic: ensure Wolfpoint generates sufficient export revenue to fund essential imports, diversify trade relationships to prevent single-kingdom dependency, and maintain the settlement's value as independent trade partner rather than conquest target.

## What the Registry Does

**Export coordination:**
- Negotiate pricing and delivery terms with buyers in [[Ashford]], [[Terravelle]], and [[Auralis]] for [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] metalwork, [[Mountain Game Collective]] preserved game, and [[Upland Herding Network]] wool textiles.
- Schedule [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] cargo allocations to match production cycles and buyer commitments.
- Track market demand signals to inform production adjustments (if cold-forge blade demand increases, Registry signals [[Trel Vann]] to adjust output allocation between catalog pieces and commissioned work).

**Import management:**
- Maintain [[Ashford Supply Contract]] relationships with multiple southern merchants, ensuring Wolfpoint receives grain, salt, tools, and specialty goods required for survival.
- Manage grain and salt reserves to buffer against maritime transport disruptions.
- Balance currency reserves against commodity exchange arrangements (paying for imports partially in watts, partially through direct export goods).

**Trade intelligence:**
- Monitor political and economic conditions in both kingdoms that might affect trade relationships (factional shifts in [[Auralis]], guild policy changes in [[Terravelle]], maritime route disruptions).
- Track competitor activities (other northern settlements attempting similar export strategies, southern producers developing alternatives to Wolfpoint-sourced goods).
- Assess buyer relationship health and adjust contract terms to prevent dependency on any single major customer.

## The Independence Calculation

The Registry's core strategic function is maintaining Wolfpoint's **economic independence** by ensuring the settlement is:
- **Valuable enough to trade with** — exports generate revenue that justifies [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]'s regular stops and gives both kingdoms economic incentive to maintain relationships.
- **Not valuable enough to conquer** — export volumes and revenue scales do not justify military intervention to control sources.
- **Diversified enough to avoid capture** — no single kingdom or buyer controls majority of Wolfpoint's export sales or import supply.

[[Renn Taska]], the Registry's director, tracks these metrics explicitly. She maintains internal documentation showing:
- Export revenue by buyer kingdom (target: 55-65% Terravelle, 35-45% Auralis to reflect Terravelle's geographic proximity while avoiding excessive dependency).
- Import supply by source (target: no single Ashford merchant controls more than 40% of any critical supply category).
- Annual trade balance (target: export revenue covers 100-110% of import costs, maintaining modest currency reserves without accumulating wealth that might attract kingdom attention).

When any metric drifts outside target ranges, Renn adjusts: declining new buyer contracts from over-represented kingdoms, shifting import allocations to alternative suppliers, adjusting export pricing to manage demand.

## Staffing and Operations

**[[Renn Taska]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 5]], 51 years old) directs the Registry. She has held the position for fourteen years, trained under the previous director, and has deep institutional knowledge of Wolfpoint's trade relationships and the political dynamics that shape them. Her role is strategic decision-making: contract negotiations that affect Wolfpoint's long-term positioning, crisis management when trade disruptions occur, and coordination with the Voss line when trade decisions intersect with [[Hypertext]] knowledge-export policy.

**[[Kess Rolv]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 4]], 46 years old) manages [[Ashford Supply Contract]] relationships, spending approximately one month per year in Ashford conducting face-to-face negotiations and the rest of the year managing correspondence with southern merchants. She is the Registry's primary supply-security specialist.

**Two assistant coordinators** handle day-to-day operations: manifests, correspondence, buyer inquiries, and coordination with [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]], [[Mountain Game Collective]], and [[Upland Herding Network]] about production schedules and cargo readiness.

**Seasonal clerks** (2-4 people during spring and autumn peak trade periods) assist with documentation surge when packet ships arrive with new contracts, payment settlements, and next-cycle planning.

## Physical Operations

The Registry operates from a two-story stone building near Wolfpoint's main workshop district, positioned for easy access to production facilities and maritime access points.

The ground floor houses documentation: contract archives, buyer correspondence, market intelligence reports, and the ledgers tracking Wolfpoint's annual trade balance. Everything is paper-based (no Flux-dependent record systems — power outages cannot disrupt core documentation).

The second floor contains meeting space where Renn conducts contract negotiations with buyers who travel north to Wolfpoint (rare but not unprecedented) and where Registry staff coordinate with production managers about output adjustments and delivery schedules.

The building is deliberately modest — functional workspace, minimal decoration. The Registry's job is maintaining Wolfpoint's independence through careful economic management, not projecting wealth or institutional power.

## Relationship with Production Operations

The Registry coordinates with but does not control Wolfpoint's export producers:

**[[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]]:** Renn meets with [[Trel Vann]] quarterly to review market demand, discuss pricing adjustments, and negotiate any changes to production allocation between catalog work and commissioned pieces. Trel maintains operational autonomy — he decides what the Works produces and how. Renn provides market intelligence that informs his decisions.

**[[Mountain Game Collective]]:** Monthly coordination with [[Dren Vass]] about harvest volumes, preservation capacity, and salt supply adequacy. The Collective operates independently but depends on Registry-managed salt imports. Coordination prevents bottlenecks.

**[[Upland Herding Network]]:** [[Lenna Tors]] manages wool trade relationships with herding families, reporting to the Registry about wool quality trends and supply projections. The Registry sets grain allocation priorities for herding families, using preferential access to sustain Network stability.

These relationships are collaborative, not hierarchical. Production operations existed before the Registry's current form and would continue if Registry leadership changed. The Registry's value is coordination expertise and external relationship management, not control authority.

## Crisis Management

The Registry's most critical function is managing trade disruptions before they cascade into settlement-threatening failures:

**Maritime disruptions:** When [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] experiences delays (storm damage, vessel loss, route disruption), the Registry:
- Activates grain and salt reserves to buffer immediate consumption needs.
- Communicates with Ashford merchants to reschedule deliveries and adjust quantities.
- Works with production operations to adjust output if export shipments cannot reach buyers on schedule.
- Monitors buyer relationships to ensure delayed deliveries do not permanently damage Wolfpoint's reputation for reliability.

**Buyer relationship failures:** When major buyers reduce orders or terminate contracts (market saturation, political shifts, competitor success), the Registry:
- Seeks alternative buyers in the affected kingdom to maintain geographic diversification.
- Adjusts pricing to attract new customers without triggering race-to-bottom dynamics.
- Communicates with production operations about output adjustments if demand decline is sustained.

**Supply contract failures:** When Ashford merchants fail to deliver (southern supply constraints, transport failures, merchant business collapse), the Registry:
- Activates reserves while sourcing alternative suppliers.
- Adjusts payment terms with remaining suppliers to incentivize increased allocations.
- Communicates with Wolfpoint's population and [[Upland Herding Network]] about rationing protocols if reserves approach critical levels.

Renn has managed all three crisis types during her fourteen years as director. The pattern is consistent: early detection through monitoring, rapid activation of contingency protocols, and sustained communication with all affected parties. Panic accelerates failures. Methodical response prevents them.

## What the Registry Knows But Does Not Say

The Registry maintains internal intelligence about kingdom-level politics that affect Wolfpoint's strategic position:

**[[Auralis]] interest in [[Hypertext]]:** Renn tracks which [[The Council of Auralis|Council]] factions are most interested in Hypertext access, which intermediaries they use to probe Wolfpoint's willingness to expand knowledge exports, and how aggressive their inquiry patterns become. This intelligence informs Voss line decisions about what Hypertext information to release and through which channels.

**[[Terravelle]] complacency about Wolfpoint:** Renn monitors Terravelle's assessment that Wolfpoint is "not yet their problem." She knows this assessment depends on Hypertext remaining unproven as large-scale trade commodity. If that changes, Terravelle's strategic calculus shifts. The Registry's job includes ensuring trade relationships do not become threatening enough to trigger Terravelle's pragmatic interest in direct control.

**Buyer network intelligence:** Through Ashford merchant relationships and correspondence with southern buyers, Renn maintains informal awareness of market conditions, political shifts, and economic trends in both kingdoms. This intelligence supplements what the Voss line learns through formal channels and helps Wolfpoint anticipate changes before they become crises.

The Registry does not publish these observations. It uses them to inform decisions that keep Wolfpoint independent.

## Why This Works

The Registry succeeds because:
- Renn Taska has fourteen years of relationship capital with Ashford merchants and kingdom buyers.
- Wolfpoint's export products are high-quality and supply-consistent, making the settlement a reliable trade partner.
- The Registry maintains diversification discipline even when concentrated relationships would be more profitable short-term.
- Wolfpoint's population understands that economic independence depends on careful management, not on maximizing immediate revenue.

It is fragile. If [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] fails, if Ashford supply chains collapse, if either kingdom decides Wolfpoint is worth conquering, the Registry cannot prevent strategic failure. What it can do — what Renn does — is maintain relationships, manage balances, and give Wolfpoint the best probability of surviving as independent settlement through careful economic stewardship.

She reviews the ledgers. She drafts next quarter's buyer correspondence. She confirms grain reserve levels. The trade continues. Wolfpoint remains independent.
