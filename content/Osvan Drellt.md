**Osvan Drellt** is forty-three years old and runs the water management operations for a mid-sized agricultural settlement in [[Terravelle]]'s interior, two weeks' travel from [[Valdenmoor]] and further still from anywhere the guild houses have regular presence. [[Control Tier|T4]]. He assessed at nine. The traveling assessor brought a second assessor back through the settlement two months later to confirm. The result has been his to manage ever since.

## Appearance

Osvan is forty-three with the weathered, capable appearance of someone who has spent fifteen years managing water systems in a remote agricultural settlement — lean and fit from constant outdoor work, sun-darkened from years of channel maintenance in all weather. His dark hair is kept short for practicality and is beginning to gray at the temples, and he dresses in the functional style of settlement infrastructure workers: practical work clothing that can handle everything from administrative meetings to emergency channel repairs, heavy boots for field work, the dust and water staining that comes with managing irrigation systems.

His hands show fifteen years of technical work — calluses from equipment handling, old scars from maintenance accidents, the steady precision of someone who performs T4-level casting regularly enough that it's become automatic. He moves through the settlement with the easy familiarity of someone who has lived in the same place their entire life, greeting neighbors by name, understanding the local terrain and seasonal patterns with the unconscious competence that comes from making consequential decisions based on them for years.

His expression is calm and thoughtful, the look of someone who is genuinely satisfied with their professional choices and doesn't require external validation to confirm them. When discussing water management his manner is detailed and practical, explaining technical problems in language that works for both educated practitioners and agricultural workers who simply need to know when water will reach their fields. He has developed the particular confidence of someone whose T4 level makes them locally exceptional but who has declined six external recruitment attempts because they understand what they're staying for.

Off-duty he has the warm, integrated presence of someone who is genuinely part of their community rather than serving it from outside, and settlement residents note that he treats the T4 assessment as simply one fact about him rather than the defining fact — useful for the work, but not more important than his knowledge of the land or his relationships with the people who depend on the channels he maintains.

## Background

He has received six formal recruitment approaches over the past twenty years: two from guild houses in Valdenmoor, one from a scholarship institution in Ashford, one from a Terravelle military adjutant who came through the settlement on a survey mission and noted his tier in the official report, one from an agricultural research collective he has never heard of before or since, and one, four years ago, from someone who did not fully identify their institutional affiliation but was clearly well-resourced. He declined all six. The letters he drafts in response are polite and brief.

He is genuinely suited to the water management work. It requires T3-range casting for standard channel maintenance and his T4 allows him to do work that would otherwise require two practitioners, which in a settlement this remote matters practically. He understands the local terrain and seasonal patterns with the precision of someone who has made consequential decisions based on them for fifteen years.

The settlement has four hundred residents. They know his tier. They also know he is the person who kept the channels running during the drought three years ago when the southern approach failed. These two facts coexist without hierarchy.

What he has found: declining external attention is easy when you know what you are staying for.
