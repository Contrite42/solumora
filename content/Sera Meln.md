**Sera Meln** is a T2 field healer based in [[Halveth]], age thirty-eight, trained through mixed apprenticeship: two years under a district practitioner in [[Ashford]], then eleven years of expedition-side medical work. She runs pre-departure medical checks and post-return stabilization for crossing teams moving through [[Equatorial Desert]].

## Practice

Sera's daily work has three lanes:

1. brief pre-departure screening for expedition members who would otherwise hide symptoms;
2. same-day treatment for return injuries in temporary camp clinics;
3. referral coordination for cases that need city-level specialist care.

She is known for refusing departures when basic risk thresholds are ignored. This has made her unpopular with some sponsors and trusted by most guides.

Her treatment style is conservative and documentation-heavy. She records hydration status, wound progression, and cognitive variance for every severe case. Teams dislike paperwork until one of her logs keeps a disputed death report from becoming a legal fight.

## Economics

Sera charges by exposure window instead of fixed visit count.

- pre-departure screening: 2-4 [[Watts]] per person;
- return stabilization package: 8-20 Watts;
- overnight observation during surge periods: 3 Watts per hour.

During peak crossing months she earns enough to remain independent. In off-season she subsidizes income by training local assistants in triage and infection monitoring.

## Limits

Her main limit is referral latency. She can stabilize many serious cases, but transfer to [[Ashford]] or [[Solhaven]] can take too long when roads are unstable or sponsors refuse extraction costs.

She also manages her own Flux exposure risk by capping high-intensity intervention blocks. Two years ago she developed tremor episodes after consecutive emergency shifts and now enforces mandatory downtime between complex interventions.

## Reputation

Among Halveth residents, Sera is considered practical rather than kind. She does not reassure when data does not support reassurance.

Among expedition leaders, she is "the one who tells you to turn back before the desert tells you harder." Some resent this. Most who survive thank her later.
