The Relay Compact is a small, informal network of independent operators who maintain pre-positioned supply caches at ruins sites across [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]. They do not guide crossings. They do not advertise. They provide the infrastructure that makes a specific class of crossing — longer-range, lower-tier, high-value cargo — survivable in ways that a standard guided expedition is not built for.

## Operations

The Compact's model is cache maintenance. At seven ruins sites across the desert interior, the Compact keeps sealed weather-resistant depots: preserved food, sealed water containers, pre-charged thermal grimoire supplements, and basic emergency medical supplies. These caches are restocked by Compact runners — T3 and T4 practitioners who make interior runs under cover of guide work or independent expeditions and do not formally announce what they are doing.

Crossing parties who have advance arrangement with the Compact receive sealed route documents: location markers for the caches along their planned route, authentication codes required to open the depot seals, and a post-crossing settlement account. The fee is paid on arrival at the far end, split between the cache fee and a deposit held against supplies consumed.

## Who Uses It

The Relay-Stage Crossing method is the Compact's primary client draw. It is suitable for parties who cannot sustain the full Flux load of an unassisted crossing but need to move cargo or information that cannot wait for a formal expedition season.

Typical clients:
- High-value commercial cargo that does not officially exist on any manifest (neither kingdom asks too many questions about goods that arrive from the other side)
- Academic researchers traveling independently of Corps or guild sponsorship, who cannot book a Cooperative guide during peak expedition season
- Information couriers carrying material that would be inspected on any official route
- The occasional overdetermined individual who simply does not want to be tracked

The Compact does not ask what is in the cargo. They ask whether the weight and volume description is accurate, because that affects cache consumption calculations.

## Canon Uncertainty

Neither [[Terravelle]] nor [[Auralis]] officially acknowledges the Compact's existence. Both kingdoms have used its services. The [[Advancement Corps]] has used it twice that Compact operators are aware of, both times for extracting ruins samples that were not in the official expedition manifest. The Corps paid and did not follow up, which the Compact interpreted as institutional approval of the arrangement continuing.

The Compact's founding is unclear even to its current operators. The oldest surviving cache was in place before the oldest current member joined. Someone built it. No one currently knows who, and the question is treated as less interesting than whether the seals hold.

## Risks

The Compact's single significant failure mode is cache compromise. A destroyed or looted cache along a planned relay route turns a survivable crossing into an emergency. The Compact's track record is good but not perfect — two cache compromises in the past decade, one from rift activity near the cache site and one from a crossing party that consumed beyond their contracted allocation and did not report it. The second party did not cross the desert again under Compact arrangement.
