[[Flux]] is a renewable dimensional energy that permeates all of [[Solumora]]. Though ever-present, it cannot be freely harnessed by just anyone. Individuals serve as conduits for Flux, but each person's capacity is finite and unique to them — and does not reliably follow bloodlines, wealth, or social standing.

The [[Sigils]] used to cast [[Spells]] are the precise frameworks that shape Flux into defined form. A caster's innate or trained ability — measured by [[Control Tier]] — determines how much Flux they can channel safely. Some individuals may never reach the threshold to cast even the simplest [[Spells|spells]], while others can channel amounts that defy normal understanding. The balance of power in [[Solumora]] is therefore as much about individual attunement to Flux as it is about the precision of the spellcraft.

Flux is abundant. Its use is defined entirely by the person wielding it.

Those who work with Flux are organized into a broad hierarchy detailed on the [[Flux Users]] page, ranging from [[Flux Users|Scribes]] who copy and distribute [[Grimoires]] to [[Flux Users|Conduits]] who operate beyond what the kingdom classification systems can meaningfully measure.

## Contemporary Research

Current [[Flux]] research is dominated by institutional networks in both kingdoms, each organized around distinct philosophical priorities and strategic orientations. **Contemporary military preparation is increasingly redirecting research agendas toward strategically-valuable applications**, creating institutional pressure on researchers to prioritize military-applicable work over pure theoretical investigation or research with only long-term practical application.

### Auralis Research Institutions

**[[Crestward Flux Institute]]** pursues cost reduction, parallel execution patterns, and methods to achieve higher-tier effects through coordinated low-tier practitioner teams. Leadership: [[Casten Miel]] ([[Control Tier|T4]] director) manages balancing strategic military research priorities against maintaining research quality and scholar autonomy. Strategic goal is expanding [[Flux]] access to practitioners currently constrained by individual high-tier casting requirements, which serves both humanitarian and military expansion objectives. Research emphasis is on force multiplication and institutional scalability. Additionally: [[Vestra Veln]] ([[Control Tier|T5]]) leads sigil miniaturization efforts, attempting to reduce physical footprint and tier requirements for complex spell patterns — research valuable for military equipment integration and civilian commercial applications.

**[[Emberfall]]** focuses on intensive, narrowly-specialized sigil development and theoretical advancement. Scholar-driven research pursues understanding of specific [[Sigils|sigils]], phenomena, or discipline-specific applications with resources and freedom to pursue long-term investigation. Research has practical (often military) applications but is organized around scholarly obsession rather than explicit strategic objectives. The [[Council of Auralis]] tolerates research that does not directly serve military priorities in exchange for occasional breakthrough discoveries that exceed strategic expectations.

### Terravelle Research Institutions

**[[Northern Terravelle Academy]]** emphasizes practical settlement-scale application and coordinated research on [[Rift Incursions|rift mechanics]]. The Academy operates independently of state control (through funding diversification) and explicitly avoids weapons-focused research despite increasing informal pressure to redirect rift mechanics work toward military applications. Research leadership: [[Meln Corst]] ([[Control Tier|T4]], senior rift mechanics researcher) grounds work in field observation and settlement applicability. Major research directions include rift formation prevention, [[Flux Demons|demon]] behavior study, and applied systems for communities. The Academy maintains formal boundary refusing military collaboration, though researchers recognize this boundary is increasingly under institutional challenge.

**[[Solhaven Flux Practical Research Collective]]** is an informal, distributed research network of 12-18 practitioners operating independently from guild and government structures. Unlike institutional hierarchies, the Collective maintains non-hierarchical governance and emphasizes research autonomy while sharing laboratory resources, collaborative peer review, and knowledge networks. Research areas span maritime applications, industrial efficiency, settlement-scale systems, and theoretical sigil notation work. Funding comes through merchant patronage and private research contracts, providing autonomy but creating economic precarity during wartime resource scarcity. Leadership is distributed without formal director role.

### Independent Scholars

**Independent scholars** operate throughout both kingdoms, conducting collaborative research that neither kingdom prioritizes internally. [[Kedn Rast]] ([[Control Tier|T3]]) exemplifies this institutional model, conducting cross-kingdom field system integration research and serving as bridge between [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] research traditions. This independent model is viable for scholars with portable expertise and successful funding negotiation, though it lacks the institutional security or resource scale of kingdom-based research. Funding precarity for independent researchers is increasing as institutional patrons redirect resources toward military applications.

### Open Research Questions

Current investigation focuses on several frontiers:

- **Cross-discipline synthesis:** Whether spells from different [[Discipline|disciplines]] can be combined reliably to produce novel effects, or whether such combinations are fundamentally incoherent. [[Crestward Flux Institute]] actively pursues this (partly for military force-multiplication potential). [[Emberfall]] has researchers investigating specific discipline interactions. [[Northern Terravelle Academy]] remains skeptical and focuses on single-discipline applications.
- **Rift mechanics:** Causes of [[Rift Incursions|rift formation]], prevention strategies, and whether rifts can be deliberately controlled or closed. This is the primary research focus of the [[Northern Terravelle Academy|Academy]]. [[Auralis]] observers view rift control research as potentially militarily valuable and have increased attempts to access Academy findings.
- **Flux capacity limits:** Whether individual [[Control Tier|tier]] capacity has theoretical maximum or only practical limits; whether sustained use produces permanent physiological change; whether capacity can be increased through training or is static.
- **Demon nature and communication:** What [[Flux Demons|demons]] are, whether they can be negotiated with or deterred, and whether demon behavior is predictable. [[Northern Terravelle Academy|Academy]] researchers pursue this; [[Auralis]] avoids publication in this area (though conducting internal research on demon weaponization).

### Institutional Differences

The two kingdoms' research traditions reflect different philosophical priorities:

- **Access vs. Depth:** [[Auralis]] institutions emphasize scaling research (broader access, more practitioners). [[Terravelle]] institutions emphasize depth (understanding specific domains thoroughly within resource constraints).
- **Strategic orientation:** [[Auralis]] research increasingly serves kingdom expansion and military capability. [[Terravelle]] research explicitly avoids such service and emphasizes community stability, though maintaining this boundary is increasingly difficult.
- **Publication vs. Implementation:** [[Auralis]] institutions produce high publication output and theoretical advancement. [[Terravelle]] institutions emphasize field-tested, practically implementable findings.
- **Competitive vs. Collaborative:** [[Auralis]] research operates in competitive resource-allocation environments (particularly between Crestward and Emberfall). [[Terravelle]] research emphasizes knowledge-sharing and collaborative problem-solving.

Neither approach is objectively superior. They produce different kinds of advancement and reflect different cultural values about what research is for.

### Wartime Pressure and Research Impacts

Contemporary military preparation is creating structural pressure across research community:

- **Practitioner conscription:** Skilled researchers are being conscripted for military [[Flux]] applications, creating knowledge loss and disrupting ongoing research. Particularly affected: mid-tier specialists (T2-T3) whose expertise translates directly to military applications. Loss is visible in independent research network and smaller institutions with less ability to protect researchers.
- **Funding redirection:** Institutional patrons are prioritizing funding toward military-applicable research. [[Auralis]] government is increasingly directing [[Crestward Flux Institute]] and [[Emberfall]] resources toward strategic priorities. Merchant patronage in [[Terravelle]] is similarly redirecting toward war-supporting applications. Independent researchers face particular funding scarcity.
- **Material scarcity:** Military procurement is monopolizing access to rare [[Flux]]-reactive materials, constraining civilian research requiring exotic materials. Temporary research deferrals are becoming common.
- **Research freedom tension:** Researchers in sensitive areas (rift mechanics, demon nature) face increasing institutional demands to restrict publication or provide exclusive access to findings. [[Northern Terravelle Academy]] has negotiated to maintain research independence but recognizes this is temporary arrangement dependent on continued political pressure resistance.
- **Institutional consolidation:** Smaller independent research operations (like the [[Solhaven Flux Practical Research Collective]]) face existential pressure as researcher conscription and funding scarcity threaten operational viability.

The sustainability of current institutional research approaches beyond 3-5 year horizon is increasingly questionable if military conflict significantly increases institutional demands on research resources and personnel.
