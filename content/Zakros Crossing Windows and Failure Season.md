# Zakros Crossing Windows and Failure Season

Desert crossing viability depends entirely on seasonal windows. The [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] does not become impassable predictably — it becomes deadly at specific times and merely dangerous at others. The difference between "marginally survivable with preparation" and "unsurvivable even with resources" is determined by [[Rift Incursions|rift]] activity patterns that follow seasonal cycles.

## Seasonal Rift Activity

Pre-collapse civilization research suggests rift activity follows patterns distinct from local weather. The [[Equatorial Desert]] experience constant low-level rift dimensional instability, but activity spikes cluster in specific months.

**Research Consensus (from [[Emberfall]] and [[Crestward Flux Institute]]):**

High-risk activity months: **Harvestfall, Coolward, Darkmoon** (autumn/winter transfer)

Moderate-risk months: **Frostfast, Colden, Thawmark** (peak winter), **Firstgreen** (spring recovery)

Low-risk months: **Plantmoon, Brightward, Highdry, Harvestfirst, Peaklite** (spring through summer)

The activity pattern correlates weakly with earthside weather and strongly with celestial cycles (sun angle, moon phase relative to equator). Current interpretation: rift formation relates to celestial alignment rather than ground temperature, suggesting dimensional stability fluctuates with planetary orientation.

This is not universally accepted — some researchers believe the correlation is coincidental — but operational practice follows the pattern because guides who ignore it die at higher rates than guides following it.

## Crossing Windows

### Primary Window (Brightward through Harvestfirst)

**Duration:** Approximately 12 weeks of relatively consistent low-rift-activity conditions.

**Conditions:**
- Desert surface temperatures moderate (35-45°C rather than 40-55°C)
- Rift activity predictable: major incursions rare, detectable in advance
- Fauna patterns most stable and documentable
- Wind patterns seasonal but not extreme

**Crossing Difficulty:** Standard hazard level. [[Standard Guided Crossing]] operates at normal casualty rate (5-10% loss on 100-person expedition typical). Competent guides produce 85-90% success rate.

**Expedition Planning:**
- 65-70% of annual planned crossings execute during this window
- Military expeditions concentrate here ([[The Council of Auralis|Council]] logistics planning coordinates with crossing seasons)
- Research expeditions prioritize this period
- Commercial trade crossings follow market demand (which also responds to season)

**Bottleneck Implications:**
Concentration of 65-70% of annual crossing volume into 12-week window creates severe infrastructure stress at launch points ([[Halveth]], [[Seln Watch]], [[Thel Reach]]). Expedition staging, guide assignment, supply provisioning — all bottleneck simultaneously.

Lead time for expedition planning is 4-6 weeks: expeditions must be organized by Plantmoon to launch by Brightward. This concentrates administrative and logistical work into spring months, adding recruiting, procurement, and training pressure to spring work cycles across [[Auralis]].

### Secondary Window (Plantmoon)

**Duration:** 4-5 weeks of moderate-risk-activity conditions (transition period between low and high activity).

**Conditions:**
- Rift activity increasing (rift frequency 2-3x seasonal average)
- Hazard level elevated but not prohibitive
- Recent winter had time to re-equilibrate conditions (minor advantage)

**Crossing Difficulty:** Elevated hazard. Expeditions experience 12-15% losses (double open-window rate). Guides require higher skill levels; casualty-intensive expeditions avoid this window if possible.

**expedition Planning:**
- Emergency crossings that cannot wait for summer window execute here
- Expeditions with high-tier practitioner support can risk it
- Poor-quality guides avoid this window (death rate too visibly connected to guide negligence)

**Conditions are bad enough that expeditions planning this period know they are taking increased risk. Some expeditions plan for it deliberately because avoiding the Harvestfall-Coolward window avoids peak-season infantry pressure and competition for guide resources. The tradeoff: higher mortality for reduced competition and earlier arrival at destination.**

### Marginal Window (Harvestfirst through Plantmoon of next calendar cycle)

**Harvestfirst (late summer before autumn rift surge):** Last 3-4 days of low-rift-activity window as conditions transition. Expeditions that launch here are racing against deteriorating conditions. Guides work accelerated schedules.

**Frostfast through Firstgreen (winter and early spring):** Strictly high-risk windows. Expeditions still execute (can't always wait) but accept documented 25-35% casualty rates. [[Express Route Crossing]] sometimes operates here (small teams moving fast, trading baggage/supply safety for speed, higher per-capita casualty rate acceptable when crossing is speed-critical).

**Coolward (early autumn):** Officially transitional, functionally high-risk. Expeditions here are unusual — either emergency/time-critical crossing, or expeditions with significant practitioner resources for defensive casting against rift incidents.

## Failure Season

Autumn Rift Surge (Harvestfall through Darkmoon) is when rift systems demonstrate maximum instability.

### Why Failure Rates Spike

**Rift Formation Probability:** Models from [[Flux Users|Flux researchers]] suggest rift formation probability increases 300-500% above baseline during autumn months. A zone that might experience one rift event per week during summer might experience 3-5 per week during autumn.

**Rift Stability:** Summer rifts tend toward lower-intensity degradation (dimensional tears heal within days). Autumn rifts show longer persistence (some remaining open or semi-open for weeks), larger geographic impact zones, and higher Flux contamination severity.

**Detection Degradation:** Large ambient Flux activity makes individual rift detection harder. T2-T3 practitioners attempting fine-scale rift location work must push into risky Flux-density zones themselves to maintain accurate sensing.

**Fauna Response:** Creature behavior becomes unpredictable during high-rift periods. Flux Scorpions and Flux Demons show feeding/movement pattern shifts. Regular prey creatures disappear or behave erratically (fleeing detected rift zones, disrupting water-source and shelter patterns guides depend on).

### Documented Failure Cascades

**The Autumn 621 FE Expedition Sequence:**

Three concurrent expeditions launched from [[Seln Watch]] in Harvestfall 621 with roughly 300 combined personnel.

- **Expedition One** (85 people, commercial crossing): Encountered unexpected rift cluster forming in previously-mapped stable zone. Rift expansion killed 12, injured 23. Expedition completed despite leadership disputes about continuing versus aborting. Final 3 casualties from improper field treatment during forced march.

- **Expedition Two** (120 people, military reconnaissance): Deliberately routed through documented rift-active zone assuming guides had recent intelligence on rift stability. Guides' intelligence was 2 weeks old. Major rift system had expanded twice since last scout report. 34 killed directly, 18 died from contamination sickness (delayed mortality). Environmental sickness affected 40-50 additional people (15-20% casualty rate from Flux exposure). Expedition aborted, salvage recovery required.

- **Expedition Three** (98 people, research expedition): Avoided documented high-rift zone, attempted northern alternate route assuming lower rift activity. Route proved unstable (unpredictable smaller rift formation). 8 killed, 12 major injuries requiring evacuation. Expedition diverted to rescue and support function for Expedition Two's evacuation.

These three expeditions, roughly 300 people, suffered roughly 115 total losses (38+). The season this occurred — high-rift autumn — was documented as unusual but within documented failure-mode range.

## Guide and Expediter Response

Experienced guides calibrate expedition strategies to seasonal windows:

**Summer Planning (May-June, Brightward-Highdry):** Guides take on maximum expedition commitment, knowing casualty probability is lowest. Earnings are highest when expedition success is most likely.

**Spring Planning (Plantmoon):** Guides selectively accept higher-risk contracts (emergency expeditions, wealthy parties able to pay hazard premium). Guides with less experience or less confidence avoid this season.

**Autumn Pragmatism (Harvestfall-Coolward):** Experienced guides typically **reduce expedition intake** or **accept only high-resource expeditions** with significant practitioner support enabling defensive casting. Casualty rates are documented as unsustainable long-term.

The economics are brutal: a guide's income depends on successful expeditions. Autumn expeditions pay 30-40% premium above summer rates (hazard compensation). But a guide losing half the expedition to rift casualties faces:

- Reputation damage (survivors know the guide contributed to losses even if not guide's fault)
- Reduced future booking rate (guides with autumn losses booked less frequently the following year)
- Psychological cost (guides haunted by seasons where they lost significant percentage of team)

Guides deal with this through three patterns:

1. **Specialization:** Some guides specialize in autumn/high-rift crossing (elite guides with proven success in challenging conditions). These guides command 50-100% premium over standard guide rates but book consistently in seasons others avoid.

2. **Rotation:** Guides who book heavily summer may go completely inactive autumn (accepting reduced annual income for reduced per-capita risk).

3. **Forced Acceptance:** Guides without capital leverage or alternative income must book autumn if offered opportunity, accepting elevated casualty rate as economic necessity.

## Calendar Pressure and Planning

The crossing window creates cascading planning requirements:

**Military Logistics:**
- Strategic objectives requiring desert-crossing intelligence or supply movement must be scheduled for low-rift window
- Expeditions planned in winter (Colden-Thawmark) must launch spring (Brightward) to execute summer (Highdry-Harvestfirst)
- Expeditions planned for winter execution must risk autumn window conditions or postpone to next year

**Research Institutions:**
- [[Emberfall]] expeditions prioritize summer window, pack schedule tightly to fit maximum research objectives
- Autumn expeditions are research-secondary (focus becomes risk mitigation rather than investigation optimization)
- Delayed research creates backlog: autumn failure has cascading effect on following year's field research timeline

**Commercial Crossing:**
- Peak crossing demand concentrated in spring (trade expeditions) responding to summer window
- Autumn crossing traffic is genuine-emergency traffic: time-critical deliveries that cannot wait for next cycle

## Implications for Ordinary Inhabitants

For people remote from desert operations, seasonal crossing windows seem irrelevant. In practice, window seasonality constrains the entire Solumora economy:

- **Trade Availability:** Goods that cross via desert (specialty materials, advanced Flux items) become available mid-summer (earliest expeditions return), peak autumn, drop winter (fewest crossings), resume spring
- **Supply Costs:** Scarce items have spring/summer premium pricing (low supply from minimal winter crossings), autumn discount (supply inflow peaks), winter extreme premium (supply drought)
- **Labor Availability:** Expedition labor and guide hiring concentrates spring-early summer; guides unavailable autumn (committed high-risk expeditions)

Ordinary people depending on seasonal trade access to specialized goods experience reliable access 8 months yearly, scarcity 4 months yearly.
