**Tessel Dran** is forty-seven years old, [[Control Tier|T4]], and has been a research scholar at the Reach Academy in [[Emberfall]] for fifteen years. Before Emberfall she spent eight years doing independent ruins survey work in the field, which is how she came to the Academy's attention: a site assessment report she submitted to the [[The Advancement Corps|Corps']] regional office contained an observation about a structural pattern in three separate sites' Flux geometry that a Corps reviewer passed to an Emberfall senior scholar, who wrote to her. She took the position the same year.

The move from field work to institutional scholarship clarified things she had not known were unclear. In the field, a site is a problem: what is this, where does it fit, what does it indicate. At the Academy, a site is a data point in a larger argument, and the argument has been partially pre-formed by the institutional positions of the people making it. She was not naive about this before she arrived. She is less naive now.

Her research centers on the construction methodology of [[Ancient Ruins|ruins sites]] — specifically what the physical structure of a site's Flux geometry indicates about how the people who built it understood casting. The argument she has been developing for eleven years is that the ancient civilization's approach to Flux was fundamentally architectural: not individual spells scaled up, but systems designed to interact with each other in ways that individual spells cannot. The ruins are not the remains of a culture that cast powerfully. They are the remains of a culture that cast *collectively*, in coordination, at a scale that neither current kingdom has a framework to understand.

She has submitted versions of this argument to the Academy's review committee three times. Each time the committee has noted the evidence as significant and requested additional documentation before endorsement. The documentation is never quite the right documentation. She has become, over eleven years, very good at identifying what kind of documentation will be requested and ensuring she has it before submission, and has found that this does not change the committee's behavior in any significant way.

She keeps research notes that are considerably more direct than anything she has submitted formally.
