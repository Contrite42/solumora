The relay-stage crossing is a method for traversing [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] using pre-positioned supply caches maintained by the [[Relay Compact]] at ruins sites across the desert interior. It occupies a practical middle ground: faster and less logistically heavy than the [[Standard Guided Crossing]], more survivable than the [[Express Route Crossing]] for practitioners who cannot sustain the Flux load of an unassisted run.

## Parameters

A relay-stage crossing runs 11 to 16 days depending on route and which cache sites are active on the planned corridor. The time varies more than a standard guided crossing because the route is constrained by cache placement — crossers move between known resupply points rather than on the theoretically optimal line, and cache sites cluster around ruins locations rather than the best geographic path.

Minimum practical capability is T2 with appropriate grimoire kit, provided the crosser has accurate cache location data and the caches along their route are confirmed active. The Relay Compact's advance arrangement process includes a current-status check on all cache sites the party will depend on. This is the critical verification step: crossing parties who skip the pre-arrangement process and rely on cache locations from older intelligence have encountered empty or compromised depots.

## Advance Arrangement

Using relay-stage crossing requires prior contact with the [[Relay Compact]]. The arrangement process covers:

- Route corridor selection and cache site confirmation
- Contracted supply quantities at each site (affecting cache consumption fees)
- Authentication codes for depot seals — the seals are designed to resist casual opening; they require the correct code or a T4+ forced opening that would be noisy enough to attract attention
- Post-crossing settlement account and holdback deposit

Arrangement is typically initiated through contacts in [[Halveth]] or through certain supply brokers in [[Solhaven]] who know what they are actually booking. The Compact does not operate a visible office.

## Failure Profile

The relay-stage method has one failure mode that the other methods do not: cache dependency. A practitioner who has relied on the relay-stage design — pacing their Flux expenditure around known resupply points — who arrives at a cache to find it compromised is in worse shape than a practitioner who was managing reserves for the full crossing from the start. The psychological and logistical recalibration required in that moment is genuinely difficult, and the Compact's incident record includes deaths that trace to parties who did not have a contingency protocol for empty caches.

The Compact's current cache integrity record is strong — two compromises in the past decade — but this is not a reason to not have a contingency protocol.

Outside of cache failure, the relay-stage method shares the standard failure modes: thermal reserve miscalculation and navigation error. Both are somewhat mitigated by the shorter between-cache legs, which allow more frequent rest and reserve checks, but neither is eliminated.

## Who Uses It

The relay-stage crossing attracts practitioners and parties for whom the standard guided crossing is either too slow or too public:

- Commercial cargo operators moving goods that do not appear on official manifests
- Academic researchers crossing outside formal expedition seasons
- Information couriers for whom documentation is an active liability
- Practitioners who need to cross at T2 or T3 without the overhead of a full guided expedition

The [[The Advancement Corps|Advancement Corps]] has used relay-stage crossings for specific operations, though this is not documented in any official Corps record. The Compact's operators know the Corps by the way their arrangements are structured: specific ruins site targeting, exact supply requirements with no negotiation, and post-settlement accounts that are paid precisely on schedule from accounts that do not have obvious institutional attribution.
