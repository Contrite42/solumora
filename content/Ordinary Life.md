Most people in [[Solumora]] are not involved in anything. They are not managing kingdoms, crossing deserts, or developing new casting techniques. They are feeding themselves, raising children, maintaining relationships, and trying to end each year in approximately the position they started it. This is the majority experience of a continent with a quarter billion people.

The texture of that life divides cleanly by kingdom. [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] arrived at different answers to the same questions — what work means, what tier means, what the shape of a life is for — and those answers are visible in how ordinary people live day to day.

- **[[Ordinary Life in Terravelle]]** — Guild culture, the Covenant of Measure, and the particular kind of person a practical society produces.
- **[[Ordinary Life in Auralis]]** — The Ascendant Path, the tier hierarchy in daily life, and what the gap between where you are and where you are supposed to want to be does to people over time.

---

Across both kingdoms, the Watt economy runs on [[Birth Runes|birth rune]] registration and [[Watts|distributed ledger]] transactions. Tier assessment between ages eight and twelve is the first formal event that tells most children what category the world has decided they occupy. Basic food, rough shelter, and unskilled labor wages are cheap enough to survive on throughout [[Solumora]]. Everything above survival is where the two kingdoms diverge.

## Education Trajectory Summary

Most families encounter the education system as a sequence of decisions under financial pressure:

1. childhood learning quality (household and neighborhood instruction);
2. tier assessment and initial placement;
3. choice between immediate wages, apprenticeship, and institutional tracks;
4. completion or dropout based on household stability.

In [[Terravelle]], movement into skilled work is usually mediated by guild and local reputation networks. In [[Auralis]], movement is more visibly structured through [[Ascendant Path]] institutions and district hierarchy. Both systems sort early. Both lose capable students when transport, materials, and foregone wages exceed household tolerance.

The largest practical truth is simple: education outcomes are less about one assessment day and more about whether a household can sustain years of follow-through.

## Civilian Continuity Layer

Recent district stress across both kingdoms is increasingly managed through continuity operations rather than emergency declarations.

- [[Queue Management Offices]] structure service access under persistent load.
- [[Ration Substitution Practices]] preserve meal continuity while reducing nutritional quality margins.
- [[Care Triage Workflows]] protect acute capacity by deferring lower-priority cases.
- [[Temporary Housing Pressure]] and [[Shelter Rotation Contracts]] convert shelter scarcity into administratively managed cycles.
- [[District Continuity Coordinators]] synchronize these systems so households are not forced to restart every queue after each disruption.

The result is a society that remains operational without appearing stable in the older sense of the term.
