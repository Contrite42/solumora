Informal intelligence in Solumora is only useful when communities can estimate reliability quickly. Reliability scoring is the practical method workers, brokers, and administrators use to judge whether a claim should affect immediate decisions.

The process is usually implicit, not documented. People ask: who said this, where did they hear it, how often have they been right before, and what do they gain if we believe them?

## The Five-Check Heuristic

Across [[Ashford]], [[Halveth]], and [[Solhaven]], experienced operators apply a similar five-check model:

- Source history: past accuracy across comparable events.
- Proximity: how close the source was to the observed event.
- Timing: whether the claim arrived early enough to be predictive rather than retrospective.
- Motive load: financial, political, or personal gain tied to claim adoption.
- Cross-confirmation: independent overlap from at least one unrelated channel.

No single check is definitive. A low-credibility source can still be right if proximity is high and confirmation appears quickly.

## Score Tiers in Practice

Many teams use informal tier labels rather than numbers:

- Green claim: use for immediate low-regret adjustments (shift timing, route delay, documentation double-check).
- Amber claim: monitor and prepare contingencies; do not commit major resources yet.
- Red claim: archive only; no action unless upgraded by confirmation.

Merchant coordinators and route supervisors often maintain private ledgers marking repeat sources by tier category. These ledgers are guarded because they expose both trusted channels and vulnerability points.

## Why Scoring Matters

Without scoring, rumor saturation causes decision paralysis. With scoring, imperfect information can still produce disciplined action. Teams avoid both extremes: blind trust and total dismissal.

Scoring also limits manipulation. A deliberate misinformation attempt can spread quickly, but if source history and motive load are poor, the claim stalls at amber or red instead of triggering costly overreaction.

## Failure Modes

Reliability scoring breaks under two conditions:

- Institutional panic: leadership treats all adverse claims as green and overloads operations.
- Social capture: one charismatic broker receives permanent green status regardless of evidence.

Both failure modes appear in pre-crisis periods documented in [[The Three Near-Wars]] and are recurring warning signs in current border environments.

## Bridge to Formal Analysis

[[Political Intelligence]] desks increasingly import informal scoring discipline into formal collection triage. Reports with strong five-check profiles are escalated first; low-score reports are retained but deprioritized.

This does not make informal intelligence "official." It makes formal analysis less vulnerable to noise floods when surveillance volume spikes.
