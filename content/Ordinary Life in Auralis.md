In [[Auralis]], the gap between where you are and where you could be is not a private concern. It is the organizing principle of the culture. Every institution reflects it. The social calendar is structured around it. The kingdom's religion says it is the central spiritual fact of a person's existence. The result is a society that is genuinely motivating for some of its population and a permanent, ambient source of pressure for the rest.

The rest is most of the population.

## Work and the Economy

The guild structure runs the Auralis economy much the way it runs Terravelle's, with one difference that changes most of what follows: Auralis guilds are formally tier-stratified. The tracks available to you, the positions you can hold, the wages you can negotiate — all of these are shaped by your certified [[Control Tier]]. A T2 finds steady work in the trades that do not require casting. A T3 in the right specialty earns significantly more. A T4 with formal certification has access to positions that make the gap between T3 and T4 feel much larger than the number suggests.

Day labor in Auralis concentrates at the bottom of this structure — harbor work, agricultural crews in the engineered low basin, maintenance. The work that does not select for tier is where the people the tier system has limited use for end up. The pay is adequate for survival. The conditions reflect what the economy has decided it would prefer to minimize.

## Food and Shelter

Auralis's engineered low basin grows rice and leafy plants that prefer warmth and moisture. The western and eastern coasts supply saltwater fish. The result is a diet with more variety than [[Terravelle]]'s river valleys produce — concentrated at the top of the price range where variety lives and simplified considerably by the time it reaches the harbor district. Preserved food is the baseline across all tiers. The difference is what you add to it.

Housing in Auralis major cities is organized by tier in ways that are officially geographic and practically social. Where you can afford to rent is where people at your tier level work, which is where people at your tier level live, which reinforces itself until the geography feels like nature. The harbor district is where T0 and T1 workers live because the harbor district is where they can afford to be. Most of them have not framed it as a choice in quite some time.

## Control Tier in Daily Life

Tier assessment in Auralis is not a quiet formality. The [[Religion and Belief|Ascendant Path]] registers assessments, not guild halls, and the ceremony is witnessed. For T0 and T1, the registration is the end of the event. The child leaves knowing their category, which the city's visible structure has been communicating since before they were old enough to read it.

For T3 and above, the assessment is the beginning of something. Institution representatives arrive. The family's social position shifts. The child enters a trajectory that was not precisely chosen.

T0 in Auralis carries social weight that T0 in Terravelle does not. The legal framework does not treat T0 citizens as lesser. It does not need to. The soft hierarchy of where people live, what work they do, which social events they are genuinely welcome at, and what the Ascendant Path says about the spiritual meaning of [[Flux]] mastery communicates something clearly enough that official policy does not need to state it. Most T0 Auralis residents understand their position in the kingdom before they are twelve. The understanding does not come from anyone saying it directly. That is part of what makes it hard to argue with.

## The Ascendant Path

The Ascendant Path is Auralis's dominant religious and cultural framework, and its theology maps conveniently onto the social structure: Flux mastery is the highest spiritual pursuit, the [[Control Tier|Control Tiers]] represent genuine states of advancement, and the ambition to develop and exceed your current capacity is a form of devotion. This is not a coincidence. The Path and the social hierarchy have been reinforcing each other for long enough that most Auralis residents cannot identify where one ends and the other begins, and do not find the question interesting.

The Path's temples are the ceremonial sites for every significant life event. Birth rune inscription, tier certification, marriage, death — all formal, all witnessed, all recorded. Ordinary participation for a working Auralis household means attendance at significant ceremonies and a general background assumption that the universe is organized the way the Path says it is. Most people's theology is this thorough and no more.

A dockworker in [[Solhaven]]'s harbor district who attends the tier certification ceremonies and believes, in some general way, that Flux mastery is spiritually significant is not living a spiritually examined life. She is participating in a culture. The culture happens to have strong opinions about where she ranks within it.
