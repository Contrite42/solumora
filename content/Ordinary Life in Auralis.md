[[Auralis]] is organized around ambition, which means it is organized around the gap between where you are and where you could be. This is a productive arrangement for people who are well positioned to close that gap. For everyone else it is a permanent reminder of the distance, framed by a religious and social structure that has decided the distance is meaningful.

## Work and the Tier Hierarchy

The Auralis economy runs through guild structures similar to [[Terravelle]]'s, with one difference that changes most of what follows: Auralis guilds are formally tier-stratified. The tracks available to you, the positions you can hold, the wages you can negotiate — these are shaped by your certified [[Control Tier]] in ways that Terravelle would consider straightforward discrimination and Auralis considers appropriate sorting.

A T2 finds steady work in trades that do not require casting. A T3 in the right specialty earns significantly more. A T4 or T5 with formal certification has access to positions and wages that make the gap between T3 and T4 feel much larger than the number suggests. A T0 in the Auralis labor market does the work the economy has decided it would prefer to minimize, and earns accordingly.

Day labor at the bottom of this structure — harbor dockworkers, agricultural crews in the engineered low basin, maintenance workers — is T0-heavy by consequence rather than design. The work that does not select for tier concentrates the people the tier system has no particular use for. The pay covers food and shared accommodation. The conditions are roughly what you would expect from work that the economy has decided it would prefer to minimize.

## Food

Auralis's engineered low basin grows rice and leafy plants that prefer warmth and moisture. The western and eastern coasts supply saltwater fish. The result is a diet with more variety than [[Terravelle]]'s river valleys, concentrated at the top of the price range where variety lives and simplified considerably by the time it reaches the harbor district.

Fresh food in the middle-tier markets is good and priced for the people who can afford it. Fresh food in the harbor district is whatever was left over at day's end, marked down. Preserved food is the baseline across all tiers; the difference is what you add to it.

## Shelter

Housing in Auralis major cities is organized by terrace in [[Solhaven]] and by district elsewhere, with the underlying logic the same: where you live communicates what tier you occupy, and the two have been reinforcing each other for long enough that the geography feels like nature. The harbor district is where T0 and T1 workers live because the harbor district is where they work because the harbor district is where they can afford to rent. This is experienced as geography.

In smaller Auralis cities and towns the tier-to-neighborhood correlation loosens, but the general principle holds: money and tier move together in Auralis, and where you can afford to live says something about both.

## Control Tier

Tier assessment in Auralis is not a quiet formality. The [[Religion and Belief|Ascendant Path]] registers assessments, not the guild halls, and the ceremony is formal and witnessed. For T0 and T1, the registration is the end of the event. The child leaves knowing what category they occupy and approximately what that means for their future, which the city's visible structure has been communicating since before they were old enough to read it.

For T3 and above, the assessment is the beginning of something. Institution representatives arrive. The family's social position shifts. The child enters a trajectory that is not precisely voluntary.

T0 in Auralis carries social weight that T0 in Terravelle does not. The legal framework does not treat T0 citizens as lesser. It does not need to. The soft hierarchy of where people live, what work they do, which social events they are genuinely welcome at, and what the Ascendant Path says about the meaning of [[Flux]] mastery communicates something clearly enough that official policy does not need to state it.

## The Ascendant Path

The Ascendant Path is Auralis's dominant religious and cultural framework, and its theology maps conveniently onto the social structure: Flux mastery is the highest spiritual pursuit, the Control Tiers represent genuine states of advancement, and the ambition to develop and exceed your current capacity is a form of devotion. This is not a coincidence. The Path and the social hierarchy have been reinforcing each other for long enough that most Auralis residents cannot identify where one ends and the other begins.

The Path's temples are the ceremonial sites for every significant life event. Birth rune inscription, tier certification, marriage, death — all formal, all witnessed, all recorded. Ordinary participation for a working Auralis household means attendance at the significant ceremonies, the formal observance days, and a general background assumption that the universe is organized the way the Path says it is. Most people's theology is this thorough and no more.

## What Ordinary Success Looks Like

The Auralis version of doing well involves visible tier advancement if possible and visible financial advancement if not. Tier certification events are social occasions. A household whose children test progressively higher than their parents has stories to tell. A household whose children test at the same tier or lower has a different set of social occasions and tells different stories about why.

For most working Auralis households, success means staying inside the comfortable range of the tier hierarchy — not at the bottom, not visibly aspirational in ways that make people uncomfortable, managing the gap between where you are and where you are supposed to want to be with enough grace that the management does not show.

The people who do this well look, from outside, like people who have made peace with something. Some of them have.
