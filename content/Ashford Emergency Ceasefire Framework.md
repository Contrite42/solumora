# Ashford Emergency Ceasefire Framework

The Ashford Emergency Ceasefire Framework is the provisional bilateral operating agreement established after the first joint stabilization operations in the corridor crisis that followed the [[Kellvost Incursion]] and the public [[High Demons|High Demon]] revelation by [[Teren Voss|Verath]].

It is not a peace treaty. It is an emergency structure designed to prevent renewed corridor escalation while both kingdoms renegotiate military posture under active dimensional risk.

## Core Purpose

The framework exists to solve a narrow but existential problem: both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] can still trigger a linked rift cascade through routine military movement if high-tier operations resume under pre-crisis assumptions.

The framework therefore prioritizes:
- preventing corridor reactivation under unstable load conditions,
- preserving verified communication channels between commands,
- enabling joint technical intervention without requiring full political settlement.

## Operational Clauses

### 1. Movement Suspension Window
- Immediate suspension of all high-tier military movement through designated red-band corridor sectors.
- Initial duration: 72 hours, renewable in 72-hour increments by joint technical review.
- Emergency exceptions require dual sign-off from both kingdom command liaisons and technical cell confirmation.

### 2. Technical Verification Requirement
- No major movement order in corridor-adjacent sectors may execute without prior dimensional load assessment from the joint monitoring cell.
- Orders issued without technical verification are classified as structural-risk violations.

### 3. Joint Incident Reporting
- Both kingdoms must submit real-time rift and micro-distortion observations to shared Ashford registry.
- Suppression or unilateral redaction of corridor-adjacent dimensional events is a ceasefire breach.

### 4. Command Relay Continuity
- Three independent relay channels maintained continuously: military, civic, and technical.
- Any channel interruption exceeding 90 minutes triggers automatic hold on non-defensive movement orders.

### 5. Personnel Immunity (Technical)
- Temporary cross-border immunity for listed stabilization personnel engaged in corridor preservation tasks.
- Includes non-human technical participants under explicit exception language.

## Governance Structure

The framework is managed through a tri-layer oversight arrangement:
- **Command Layer:** military liaisons from both kingdoms
- **Civic Layer:** civil authorities from Ashford and one rotating Auralis municipal delegate
- **Technical Layer:** stabilization specialists, logistics analysts, and ruins interpreters

No single layer can unilaterally terminate the framework while the technical layer classifies corridor conditions as unstable.

## Political Limits

The framework does not resolve:
- war accountability for Kellvost,
- constitutional authority disputes inside Auralis,
- legitimacy conflicts within expansion-aligned command blocs,
- long-term status of High Demons in public governance.

It is intentionally procedural and temporary. Its legitimacy is practical: it has prevented renewed first contact while maintaining corridor integrity under stress.

## Current Status

As of the current timeline, the framework remains active under rolling renewals. Compliance is imperfect but sufficient to prevent immediate re-escalation. Both kingdoms continue rhetorical hardline positions in public channels while adhering to most operational restrictions in practice.

The framework's central paradox remains unchanged: it is most politically fragile when most technically necessary.
