**Renna Molt** is thirty-nine years old and owns one of the larger textile dye operations in [[Solhaven]]'s [[The Bluff District|Bluff District]]. [[Control Tier|T1]]. She inherited the operation from her mother — who inherited it from her grandmother — and has expanded it over the fourteen years she has managed it. The workshop now employs twelve people full-time and supports seasonal contracts with several major cloth merchants.

Renna is not a particularly talented caster, but she is a meticulous dyer who understands color chemistry and the precise temperament required to produce consistent results. Her dyes are expensive because they are expensive to make and because they hold color better than cheaper alternatives. Her clients trust her to execute complex commissions that lesser dyers cannot manage.

She is married to Calen, who handles the business administration and logistics while Renna concentrates on craft. They have two young children, ages five and eight, and are already estimating the costs for their education and tier assessment. Both parents are T1; children from that genetic background have roughly a 15% chance of assessing T3 or higher, lower chance of T2, and substantial risk of T0.

Renna is politically pragmatic regarding tier hierarchy. She sees the Ascendant Path's theology as reasonable generally and finds no contradiction between believing that tier capacity matters and believing that the work a person does well is spiritually valuable. She attends temple ceremonies because it is expected and because some of the sermons about craft as practice genuinely speak to her understanding of her work.

She maintains knowledge of which upper-terrace merchants prefer her work and occasionally receives requests for specialized commissions from Crestward clients. These commissions pay extremely well and come with high time pressure and exacting standards. She accepts them selectively — enough to not offend important clients but not so many that they overwhelm her regular work. The balance is delicate and requires political sense as much as craft skill.

Renna has no ambition to move to the Crestward. She has assets, stability, and the genuine autonomy that merchant ownership provides. Her children may move up; if they demonstrate sufficient tier capacity they may find opportunities in elite institutions. She would be proud of them for achieving that, and also content if they chose to continue the family trade.
