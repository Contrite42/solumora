Grimoire Commissioning is the process of hiring a scrivener to produce a custom [[Grimoires|grimoire]] copy, either from an existing source text or as a personalized selection of [[Spells]]. Most grimoires in circulation are commissioned works rather than mass-produced inventory. Commissioning allows buyers to specify content, format, binding quality, and delivery timeline, but comes at higher cost than purchasing pre-made copies or using [[Grimoire Lending and Rental|rental]] access.

## How Commissioning Works

A buyer approaches a scrivener — either through the [[Guild Scrivener Network]] or as an [[Independent Scrivener]] — with a request. The simplest commissions are direct copies: "I want a copy of this specific grimoire." The scrivener inspects the source, confirms they have the [[Control Tier]] necessary to reproduce it accurately, and quotes a price based on page count, tier complexity, and timeline.

More complex commissions involve selection: "I want a grimoire containing these eight spells, bound together." The scrivener must source each spell from authenticated texts, copy them individually, and compile them into a single volume. This increases cost due to sourcing time and layout work, but allows the buyer to own a grimoire tailored to their specific needs.

Custom grimoires are the most expensive commissions. A buyer might request a grimoire organized by discipline rather than alphabetically, or one with margin space for personal annotations, or one bound in specific materials for durability or presentation. Some buyers commission grimoires as gifts, with decorative elements or personalized inscriptions.

## Pricing Structure

Pricing is per-page for [[Common Grimoire]] and [[Uncommon Grimoire]] texts, with multipliers for higher tiers. Typical guild rates in [[Terravelle]] are:
- Common tier (T1-T2): 5-8 [[Watts]] per page
- Uncommon tier (T3-T4): 15-25W per page
- Rare tier (T5-T6): 50-100W per page, restricted availability

[[Independent Scrivener|Independent scriveners]] often charge less but carry higher risk of [[Grimoire Authentication|authentication issues]]. Rush orders, custom bindings, and specialized materials add surcharges. A complete [[Common Grimoire]] compilation (20-30 spells, ~40 pages) might cost 200-320W through guild channels, or 150-250W from an independent.

Higher-tier commissions are rare and usually handled through institutional arrangements. A [[Rare Grimoire]] commission might require months of negotiation, authentication, and approval before work begins. The buyer must demonstrate legitimate need and often agree to restrictions on further copying.

## Sourcing and Authentication

The scrivener's first responsibility is sourcing: locating authenticated versions of the requested spells. Guild scriveners have access to institutional source libraries in [[Valdenmoor]], [[Ashford]], or [[Solhaven]], which simplifies this step. Independent scriveners must either maintain their own authenticated sources or borrow texts from other practitioners, which increases risk and time.

Buyers can provide their own source texts, but the scrivener must verify authenticity before copying. A buyer handing over a grimoire and saying "copy this" creates liability for the scrivener if the source contains errors. Most scriveners refuse to work from unverified sources unless the buyer signs a waiver acknowledging the risk.

## Timeline

Standard commissions take 1-3 weeks for [[Common Grimoire]] texts and 3-6 weeks for [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]]. Rush orders (1 week or less) are possible but come with surcharges (commonly 50-100% markup). Higher-tier commissions can take months due to sourcing, authentication, and approval delays.

Timelines lengthen if the buyer requests spells from multiple sources or custom compilation. A scrivener producing a 15-spell custom grimoire must source each spell individually, copy them without error, and bind them into a coherent volume. This is why custom grimoires cost significantly more than direct copies of existing texts.

## Cross-Border Commissions

Cross-border commissions are possible but complicated. A buyer in [[Terravelle]] commissioning a grimoire from an [[Auralis]]-based scrivener must arrange payment transfer (usually through merchant intermediaries), specify delivery logistics, and accept longer timelines due to courier delays along routes like the [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] or [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]].

Authentication is harder to verify cross-border. A buyer commissioning from a foreign scrivener relies entirely on that scrivener's reputation, with limited recourse if the grimoire is defective. Some buyers use intermediaries — merchants with cross-border connections who vet scriveners and handle disputes — but this adds cost and delays.

## Risks

The primary risk for buyers is receiving a defective grimoire after paying full price. Guild commissions carry lower risk due to inspection protocols, but disputes still occur. If a buyer claims a grimoire is defective, the guild investigates, which can take weeks. If the scrivener is found at fault, they must refund or replace the work. If the buyer is wrong, they may owe compensation.

For scriveners, the risk is non-payment or late payment. Guild contracts usually require deposit (30-50% upfront) to mitigate this, with full payment on delivery. Independent scriveners have less formal protection and rely on reputation pressure. Some independents require full payment upfront, which shifts risk entirely to the buyer.
