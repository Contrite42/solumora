# The High Demon Incident and Public Revelation

The event that triggered the [[The Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] occurred approximately 395 years before the [[Rift Catastrophe Revelation|current era's first casualty]]. Historical sources remain fragmentary, but consensus accounts describe:

A High Demon's presence in the [[Auralis]] capital was discovered during routine institutional records audit. The immortal being—identity sources disagree but several reference "Kel'mantis" or variants—had been living in [[Auralis]] continuously for at least eighty years without institutional knowledge.

When discovered, the reaction was not measured institutional response. It was panic.

Within three days, both [[Auralis]] Council and [[Terravelle]] leadership released simultaneous public declarations describing the situation as existential threat. Both kingdoms began immediate mobilization for what they framed as defensive action against "hidden invasion."

The High Demon made no aggressive movement. This was the critical point: threat assessment was based on the *existence* of an immortal being, not on any documented harm.

Within weeks, systematic hunts began.

## What Made the Revelation Possible

Before the incident, High Demons maintained absolute anonymity. The specific mechanism of discovery remains unclear—theories include:
- Administrative record inconsistency noticed by unusually thorough clerk
- Romantic relationship exposure forcing disclosure
- Voluntary partial revelation to a trusted confidant who later reported it

What matters is that once one was found, institutional response assumed more existed.

They were correct. Over the following decade, four additional High Demon presences were verified or strongly suspected. This confirmation of persistence drove persecution intensity far beyond single-target response.

## Institutional Justification Framework

Both kingdoms rapidly developed official rationales:
- "Hidden immortal beings represent unprecedented power concentration"
- "Authority that does not age cannot be subject to normal succession constraints"
- "Any immortal has motivation to subvert mortal governance for permanent advantage"

None of these required documented immortal wrongdoing. Threat was inherent in existence.

This framework became the legal foundation for 200 years of organized persecution.
