_(Historical Period, Persecution of Immortals)_

**The Ancient Persecution Era** (approximately 475-520 years ago) was a 45-year period during which [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] institutional authorities conducted systematic identification, hunting, and execution of [[High Demons|High Demon]] immortals hiding within human populations. The persecution was triggered by discovery that supernatural entities were living undetected in human settlements, proceeded through institutional identification protocols that transformed citizens against each other, and concluded when evidence emerged that persecuted demons were neither supernatural threats nor the cause of institutional failures that authorities had blamed on demon presence. The era represents the primary historical precedent for what happens when human populations learn about [[High Demons]] living among them — systematic persecution, institutional violence, profound social fracture, and trauma sufficient to shape cultural attitudes about immortals for centuries.

The Persecution Era is critical context for understanding why [[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] face such extreme danger from demon revelation during the current crisis. The institutional memory of what persecution looks like remains embedded in [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] historical consciousness. The revelation that demons are real and have been hiding in human settlements will trigger that historical memory — and the pattern that follows that memory triggers is persecution.

## Pre-Persecution Era Context

Before systematic persecution began, [[High Demons|High Demons]] existed in both kingdoms essentially unmolested. Historical record suggests 8-12 immortals maintained human-like existence across [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] combined around 475 years ago. Some maintained genuine friendships with human populations (like [[Fennick]] would later do). Some maintained strategic isolation. Most avoided creating institutional footprints that would draw attention.

The coexistence wasn't perfect — some humans had suspicions about individuals who didn't age, couldn't eat normal food, or displayed unusual emotional distance. But suspicion didn't translate to institutional action. Demons were outlier anomalies, not identified threat category.

Persecution emerged not from spontaneous discovery but from systematic investigation following institutional failure.

## The Institutional Failure

Around 475 years ago, both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] experienced patterns of institutional dysfunction that authorities attributed to demonic interference:

- Economic disruption without clear cause: commodity prices fluctuating beyond normal market variation
- Political conflicts that seemed to arise from nowhere: Council members having sudden bitter disputes over resolvable disagreements
- Social instability: communities fractturing into hostile factions over minor disputes
- Military operations failing inexplicably: battles lost despite superior positioning, generals making catastrophic tactical errors

The failures accumulated over 5-year period (475-470 years ago) and created genuine institutional panic. Leadership couldn't identify cause. Couldn't implement solutions. The system was breaking down.

Concurrent with institutional failures, isolated incidents of unusual deaths and disappearances occurred. People sometimes just vanished. Bodies were occasionally found showing signs of violence inconsistent with human combat capability. Livestock predation increased with apparent supernatural efficiency.

Institutional authorities hypothesized connection: demons hidden in human populations were causing institutional disruption and predatory violence. The demons were a category of threat that hadn't been previously identified or accounted for.

## The Persecution Doctrine

Authorities developed formal persecution framework:

**Demon Identification Protocol**: Training programs to help populations identify potential demons based on observable characteristics:
- Unusual age consistency (not aging visibly)
- Behavioral oddities (difficulty consuming normal food, emotional distances from normal human attachments)
- Knowledge anomalies (awareness of historical events from centuries past, appearing to remember things that happened "before they were born")
- Physical anomalies (unusual strength, resistance to injury, healing without visible scarring)
- Association patterns (sometimes spending extensive time observing human populations without consistent friendship ties)

**Institutional Vetting**: Religious organizations received authority to conduct interviews and assessment of suspicious individuals. Questions designed to identify whether suspects had access to forbidden knowledge or displayed awareness suggesting non-human perspective.

**Authorized Execution**: Individuals identified as demons were subject to immediate execution without trial. Justification: demons represented security threat sufficient to suspend normal due process. Religious authorities had veto power over execution recommendations, but veto power was rarely used (religious institutions were aligned with persecution agenda).

**Property Confiscation**: Executed demon-identified individuals' property transferred to kingdom administrative authority (ostensibly to compensate for administrative investigation costs, practically enriching state treasury).

**Social Obligation**: Populations were encouraged to report suspected demons to authorities. Rewards offered for information leading to execution. Social pressure applied to community members who were reluctant to report friends or acquaintances.

The framework created systematic identification and execution machinery. Estimated 80-120 executions occurred across both kingdoms during 45-year persecution period.

## The Actual Victims

Investigation centuries later (during historical scholarship review in Auralis, ~250 years ago) revealed most persecution victims had been ordinary humans, not demons. Some victims:

**"Demon" Identified as Albino Woman** (executed 468 years ago): Woman with albinism executed after neighbors reported her pale skin and light-sensitivity symptoms as demonic characteristics. Post-hoc investigation: medical condition, not immortality marker.

**"Demon" Identified as Elderly Scholar**: Scholar identified as demon due to knowledge of historical events and apparent age consistency. Executed at age 83. Investigation: normal human, lived full life and was unusually educated - not supernatural knowledge.

**"Demon" Identified as Merchant Traveling Extensively**: Merchant whose business required cross-kingdom travel and absence from community was identified and executed for behavioral oddities and lack of consistent social ties. Investigation: successful businessman focused on work rather than social integration, not immortal.

**Actual Demons Executed (approximately 3-5)**: Some individuals identified were likely actual [[High Demons|High Demons]], though historical records are insufficient for certainty. If demons were among executed, persecution apparatus accidentally identified actual threats while executing 16-39 false positives for every genuine demon.

**High Demons Who Avoided Execution**: Small number of immortals (estimated 3-4) survived persecution through concealment strategies. Some went completely dormant (stopped all human social contact, isolated completely). Some emigrated to regions outside [[Auralis]]-[[Terravelle]] kingdoms. Some managed to avoid identification through exceptional social caution.

## The Persecution Mechanism

Why did execution momentum continue despite false-positive rate approaching 95%?

**Institutional Investment**: Religious authorities conducting identifications became personally invested in persecution framework. Continuing examination and execution validated their institutional role as demon-hunting specialists. Stopping persecution would mean admitting their identification protocols were fundamentally flawed.

**Economic Incentives**: Property confiscations enriched state treasuries. Military commanders conducting executions were promoted and rewarded. Continuing persecution meant continuing resource flow.

**Social Momentum**: Once persecution began, it produced backlash justifying further persecution. Innocent people executed sometimes had friends/family who blamed investigative apparatus, creating conflict authorities attributed to demonic instigation. "See, demons are causing social conflict" — when conflict was actually caused by persecution itself.

**Institutional Failure Attribution**: Original institutional failures (economic disruption, political conflicts) didn't resolve despite persecution continuing. Rather than acknowledging persecution wasn't addressing underlying causes, authorities increased persecution intensity. If institutional failures persisted, demons must be more numerous/hidden than originally thought.

**Fear Dynamics**: Once persecution began, populations became paranoid about neighbors. Anyone with unusual characteristics, behavioral oddities, or unexplained wealth was vulnerable to accusation. Social systems broke down. The fear itself became institutional crisis that persecution apparatus attributed to ongoing demon activity.

Persecution continued not because it was working but because institutions were invested in maintaining it. Stopping would mean confronting failure.

## The End of Persecution

Persecution ended not through institutional acknowledgment of failure but through external pressure:

Around 430 years ago, both [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] faced external military threats requiring institutional unity and resources. Continuing internal persecution was becoming counterproductive — communities were divided, populations were paranoid, institutional trust was eroding.

More importantly, religious scholars in both kingdoms conducted historical research comparing persecution records against pre-persecution era documentation. Evidence emerged that institutional failures (economic disruption, political conflict) had natural explanations unrelated to demon activity. The failures had been real, but their causes were economic pressure, normal political disagreements, and social stress — not supernatural interference.

Simultaneously, physical evidence of persecution itself mounting: bones of executed individuals could be studied. Characteristics that got people identified as demons were identified as medical conditions, genetic variations, educational background, personality traits — nothing supernatural.

Religious authority (both kingdoms) issued formal statements that persecution had likely been based on false premises, execution protocols had executed primarily innocent individuals, and persecution era should be treated as historical error and institutional disgrace.

Persecution ended not because it had solved problems but because institutions could no longer maintain framework once evidence of its invalidity became inescapable.

## The Aftermath

Post-persecution institutional landscape:

**Legal Complications**: Centuries of disputes about property rights for executed individuals' families. Were confiscated properties legitimate state seizure or criminal theft? If victims were innocent, do heirs have restitution claims? Different legal frameworks developed across kingdoms and time periods. Many disputes never definitively resolved.

**Social Trauma**: Communities that had participated in neighbor identification and execution faced moral reckoning. How could they have participated? Were they complicit in murder? Coping mechanisms varied: some denied persecution happened (revised historical narratives), some normalized it as necessary security measure, some maintained honest shame about institutional failure.

**Institutional Accountability Failure**: Religious authorities that conducted identifications never faced prosecution (they were the institutions responsible for prosecution, creating conflict of interest). State authorities that conducted executions were essentially amnestied (acknowledging crimes would have damaged institutional legitimacy during vulnerable period).

**Persecution Survivors (Actual Demons)**: High Demons who survived persecution by concealment faced permanent institutional paranoia. Some remained in complete isolation indefinitely. Some emigrated permanently. Some reentered human populations decades later with extreme caution, never again trusting human institutions.

**Cultural Memory**: Persecution era became reference point for centuries describing institutional violence and collective persecution. Every authoritarian crackdown, every resource-driven prosecution, every social persecution of minority groups got compared to Persecution Era and judged historically shameful.

## Why This Matters Currently

The Persecution Era represents established historical pattern: **When human populations learn about [[High Demons]] living among them, institutional persecution follows.**

The pattern doesn't require coordinated conspiracy. It doesn't require extraordinary malice from individual actors. It requires:
1. Institutional authorities identifying demon-presence-as-threat
2. Identification protocols creating false positives at high rates
3. Economic/institutional incentives maintaining persecution despite evidence of failure
4. Social momentum amplifying once persecution begins
5. Inability to reverse once populations are mobilized

Every element of Persecution Era pattern could repeat if [[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] attempt demon revelation without careful institutional framework preventing persecution pathway.

## Current Institutional Susceptibility

Modern [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] institutions appear more rational and cautious than Persecution Era counterparts. But institutional review of persecution era demonstrates it wasn't deliberate evil — it was institutional rationalization, fear-driven decision-making, and institutional investment in frameworks once begun.

Current crisis conditions (constitutional breakdown, military mobilization, civil society uncertainty) actually increase institutional susceptibility to persecution:

- **Authority Legitimacy**: Both institutions are defending governance legitimacy through visible action. Demon-hunting could serve as demonstration of protective authority.
- **Social Panic**: Military conflict and [[The Communication Severance|communication severing]] create population fear. Persecution frameworks thrive during panic.
- **External Threat**: Military mobilization creates "us vs them" mentality. Demons as external threat category could unite populations against common enemy.
- **Economic Strain**: War costs and institutional disruption create pressure for rapid resource mobilization. Demon property confiscation could serve as emergency funding.

Modern institutional leaders would likely claim they're too sophisticated for Persecution Era repetition. Yet every mechanism that enabled Persecution Era is present in current institutional structure.

[[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] attempting demon revelation without institutional framework preventing persecution would trigger exact pattern that destroyed 120 lives and traumatized kingdoms 450 years ago.

The historical precedent isn't warning they should avoid revelation. It's evidence that revelation requires carefully designed institutional framework that prevents persecution pathway from activating.

That framework doesn't currently exist. Building it is prerequisite for demon revelation being anything other than catastrophic.
