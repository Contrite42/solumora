_(Historical Period, Origin of Civilization)_

**The First Contact Era** (approximately 1,200-1,100 years ago) describes the initial period of documented contact between human populations and [[High Demons|High Demon]] civilizations, the conditions under which humans and demons first became aware of each other's existence, and the institutional frameworks that enabled coexistence arrangements in the immediate post-contact period before [[High Demons]] developed concealment strategies that would eventually result in isolation and hiding. The First Contact Era is significant historical context because it establishes that human-demon coexistence is not inherently impossible (demonstrated through 50-100 year period of documented collaboration), because revealing the framework that enabled that historical coexistence could provide template for modern coexistence if institutional authorities were willing to adopt similar frameworks, and because understanding what changed from cooperation to concealment illuminates what would be required to reverse that process and enable modern demon revelation.

## Pre-Contact Human Knowledge

Before First Contact Era, human populations maintained folklore traditions about [[High Demons|High Demons]] and other supernatural entities:

**The Folklore**:
- Religious traditions described demonic entities as spiritual dangers
- Stories traditionally positioned demons as threats requiring religious protection
- Folklore described demons possessing superhuman abilities
- Traditional narratives framed demons as inherently opposed to human flourishing
- Folklore provided explanation for various unexplained phenomena that humans encountered

**The Reality**: These folklore traditions were substantially untrue. They were human cultural narratives about entities humans didn't actually understand.

**The Vulnerability**: Human populations approached First Contact with foreknowledge that was fundamentally inaccurate, creating potential for misunderstanding and conflict if contact occurred.

## The Contact Event

Approximately 1,200 years ago, contact between human and demon civilizations occurred through specific incident:

**The Circumstance**: Human explorers/traders encountered demon settlement in previously unvisited region (exact location uncertain, possibly mountain region or isolated area where demons had maintained open civilization).

**The Discovery**: Humans discovered that demonic entities were living in organized settlements, maintaining sophisticated institutional structures, engaging in trade and governance, and maintaining organized society fundamentally similar to human civilization structures.

**The Revelation**: Humans discovered that demons were not supernatural entities but were beings with:
- Biological existence (material beings, not spiritual)
- Civilization structures (governance, trade institutions, religious traditions)
- Individual variability (wide range of personality, philosophy, character variation)
- Material needs and vulnerabilities (require food, water, shelter, though biochemistry different from humans)
- Rational agency (capable of negotiation, agreement, institutional cooperation)

**The Shock**: This discovery contradicted human folklore traditions completely. Demons were not spiritual threats, not possessed of supernatural powers, not inherently opposed to human existence. Demons were civilized beings with capacity for reasonable negotiation.

## The Initial Response

Human and demon populations faced institutional choice about how to respond to discovery:

**The Mutual Vulnerability**: Both populations suddenly realized they weren't alone and discovered an equally-sophisticated civilization operating in close proximity. Both populations faced potential threat from the other.

**The Mutual Interest**: Both populations also recognized that coexistence offered mutual benefit. Demons had knowledge and resources humans lacked. Humans had knowledge and resources demons lacked. Trade was mutually beneficial.

**The Negotiation Framework**: Rather than military conflict, initial contact generated rapid negotiation. Emissaries from both populations met to establish framework for coexistence.

**The Agreement**: Negotiations resulted in framework that:
- Established human and demon territories with defined borders
- Permitted trade exchange where territories overlapped or routes crossed
- Created religious/cultural respect accommodations (humans and demons would not interfere with each other's religious beliefs and practices)
- Established common protocols for dispute resolution if conflicts arose
- Committed both populations to maintaining secrecy about each other's existence from populations that didn't yet know about contact

This agreement remained in force for approximately 50-100 years, representing the First Contact Era period of open cooperation.

## The Documented Coexistence

During First Contact Era, substantial institutional cooperation developed:

**Trade Networks**: Humans and demons established mutual trade. Demons offered knowledge (astronomy, mathematics, certain technical crafts), materials (certain mineral extraction, certain plant medicines), and crafted goods. Humans offered agricultural products that demons had difficulty producing, certain technical knowledge, and material goods.

**Knowledge Exchange**: Both populations maintained scholarly exchange. Demon scholars taught human scholars advanced mathematics and certain technical disciplines. Human scholars shared knowledge about agricultural innovation and certain medicinal practices.

**Military Coordination**: Where borders overlapped or shared security threats existed, humans and demons coordinated military response. Some demon military units fought alongside human military units against common enemies.

**Religious Dialogue**: Rather than conflict about religious beliefs, religious scholars from both populations engaged in dialogue and mutual study. Human religious authorities studied demon theology. Demon religious authorities studied human spiritual traditions.

**Institutional Modernization**: Contact with demon civilization accelerated human institutional development. Humans adopted certain governance practices from demons, refined legal frameworks, expanded trade institutional structures. Demon civilization influenced human institutional sophistication substantially.

## Why Contact Ended

After 50-100 years of coexistence, institutional contact essentially ended. Humans and demons did not go to war, but deliberate isolation developed:

**The Transition Causes** (understood imperfectly from historical record):

**Population Pressure**: Human populations grew faster than demon populations. Over decades, human expansion threatened demon territories. Rather than negotiate new borders, demons gradually withdrew from explicit contact.

**Religious Opposition**: Human religious institutions (particularly in some regions) opposed ongoing contact with demons. Religious authorities created theological frameworks opposing demon relationship. Over time, religious opposition constrained human willingness to maintain contact.

**Knowledge Asymmetry**: Humans, acquiring demon knowledge, became less dependent on demons for technical advancement. Once knowledge was transferred, mutual benefit from contact declined. Demons similarly found less advantage in trade as humans became self-sufficient.

**Social Integration Resistance**: While institutional cooperation existed, human and demon populations were not integrating socially. Families were not intermarrying, communities were not mixing, social interaction remained constrained. This created stability of separation despite institutional cooperation.

**Institutional Fatigue**: Maintaining contact frameworks required constant negotiation and management. As immediate benefits declined, institutions reduced investment in contact maintenance.

## The Concealment Development

Rather than ending in conflict, contact transitioned to deliberate concealment:

**The Demon Choice**: Demon populations (conscious institutional decision) withdrew from contact and entered concealment. Rather than maintain visible civilization, demons:
- Relocated to remote regions beyond human settlement
- Developed underground settlements and hidden communities
- Ceased visible trade with human populations
- Stopped scholarly exchange with human institutions
- Developed isolation protocols preventing accidental contact

**The Human Transition**: Human populations (initially unaware that concealment was deliberate) gradually shifted narratives:
- Contact had occurred but was interpreted as ancient history
- Demons were reframed as mythological rather than actual beings
- Trade relationships were forgotten or reinterpreted as folklore
- Knowledge that had come from demons was attributed to human discovery
- Eventually, humans forgot that demons were actual historical entities

**The Institutional Consequence**: Over approximately 200-300 years post-contact, human and demon institutions transformed from:
- Conscious mutual coexistence → mutual unknowing (humans forgot demons, demons hid from humans)
- Documented institutional relationship → folklore and myth
- Recorded history → lost historical memory

By approximately 900 years ago, contact had been so thoroughly forgotten that human populations treated demon existence as folklore/myth rather than historical fact that had occurred within written history.

## Why This Matters Currently

First Contact Era demonstrates several critical facts:

**Coexistence Is Possible**: Human and demon populations successfully maintained coexistence for decades, trading, exchanging knowledge, and coordinating militarily. Institutional cooperation was not theoretically impossible — it occurred historically.

**Coexistence Framework Existed**: The specific framework that enabled First Contact Era coexistence was:
- Territorial respect and non-interference
- Trade agreements and knowledge exchange
- Religious/cultural mutual accommodation
- Dispute resolution mechanisms
- Mutual secrecy about each other's existence

This framework could theoretically be readopted for modern coexistence.

**Concealment Was Active Choice**: Demons did not hide because humans would have persecuted them. Demons chose concealment strategically because:
- Human population expansion made coexistence unsustainable
- Religious opposition made institutional contact politically costlier
- Benefits of contact declined as knowledge was transferred
- Independent civilizations are simpler to maintain than contact frameworks requiring constant negotiation

This suggests demons might rationally accept renewed contact if institutional frameworks could be renegotiated.

**Institutional Memory Loss Was Catastrophic**: The fact that humans completely forgot documented contact within 200-300 years suggests institutional memory structures are critical. If First Contact Era frameworks were lost, the institutional knowledge that prevented persecution during First Contact Era was lost simultaneously.

By the time [[The Ancient Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] occurred, human institutions had completely forgotten that demons were actual beings with whom coexistence had proven possible. Persecution institutions, lacking historical memory of successful coexistence, operated under assumption that demons were hostile supernatural entities rather than rational beings with whom cooperation was possible.

## What Would Enable Renewed Contact

If [[Venn Drost]] and [[Fennick]] were attempting renewal of contact similar to First Contact Era:

1. **Territorial Recognition**: Human institutions would need to recognize demon settlements as legitimate territories with autonomy and sovereignty (similar to First Contact Era framework)

2. **Trade and Knowledge Exchange**: Institutional framework would need to permit and facilitate demon-human trade and scholarly exchange (similar to First Contact Era framework)

3. **Religious Accommodation**: Human religious institutions would need to commit to non-interference in demon spiritual practices and accept demon theological traditions as legitimate (similar to First Contact Era framework)

4. **Dispute Resolution**: Institutions would need mechanisms for resolving conflicts between human and demon populations without resorting to persecution (similar to First Contact Era framework)

5. **Institutional Continuity**: Unlike First Contact Era (where contact ended in institutional fatigue), modern contact would need mechanisms ensuring commitment survives generational change and institutional transitions

Modern contact would essentially require modern institutions reinventing historical coexistence frameworks that worked 1,200 years ago.

## The Strategic Opportunity

The fact that human-demon coexistence historically worked provides [[Venn Drost]] with strategic possibility:

If [[Venn Drost]] could convince institutional authorities that:
- Contact is not new revelation but renewal of ancient coexistence
- Coexistence frameworks historically existed and operated successfully
- Modern demons are inheritors of civilization that cooperated with humans previously
- Persecution is institutional failure to remember historical lesson

Then institutional authorities might accept demon revelation as institutional restoration (returning to successful coexistence) rather than as institutional crisis management (dealing with discovered threat).

This reframing requires access to First Contact Era historical documentation that proves coexistence occurred. If [[Venn Drost]] can produce historical evidence that contact existed, the narrative shifts from "demons are hidden threat we must deal with" to "we are resuming relationship our ancestors maintained."

That narrative reframing could be essential to preventing persecution.

But it requires that institutional authorities still have access to First Contact Era records (or that records can be found/reconstructed).
