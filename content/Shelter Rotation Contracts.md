# Shelter Rotation Contracts

**Shelter Rotation Contracts** are time-bounded agreements used by districts to distribute limited temporary beds across high-demand households without fully excluding repeat-need cases.

They are common in high-churn zones where monthly housing stability cannot be guaranteed but continuous displacement would overwhelm neighborhood systems.

## Contract Model

A standard rotation contract defines:

- fixed shelter nights per cycle
- re-entry interval rules
- proof requirements for continuity eligibility
- override criteria for medical or dependent-care priority

The model accepts cyclical instability and tries to make it administratively navigable.

## Why Districts Use It

Districts use rotation contracts when ordinary first-come shelter rules fail to balance fairness and continuity.

Without rotation, new entrants crowd out vulnerable repeat households. With rigid retention, bed turnover collapses. Rotation contracts create a middle path at the cost of higher paperwork and coordination overhead.

## Interface With Other Systems

Rotation contracts are tied to:

- token sequencing in [[Queue Management Offices]]
- overflow metrics in [[Temporary Housing Pressure]]
- case escalation through [[District Continuity Coordinators]]
- care-priority exceptions from [[Care Triage Workflows]]

This coupling means shelter policy is no longer isolated housing policy; it is part of whole-district continuity management.

## Common Disputes

Frequent disputes involve:

- alleged unfair re-entry denials
- incomplete documentation after household moves
- conflict between work-proof rules and care obligations

Disputes are usually resolved administratively, but unresolved cases can cascade into ration and clinic access failures.

## Effectiveness and Cost

Rotation contracts improve aggregate bed access and reduce same-household permanent lock-in.

They also impose hidden costs:

- more time spent in administrative compliance
- reduced predictability for children and elders
- increased dependence on coordinator discretion

The system is effective at throughput and weak on dignity.

## Current Role in Civilian Continuity

In stressed districts, rotation contracts are now baseline infrastructure rather than emergency measures.

That shift shows how ordinary life adapts under sustained pressure: not by solving scarcity, but by making scarcity schedulable.
