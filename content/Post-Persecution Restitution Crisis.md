_(Legal Consequence, Property Rights Aftermath)_

**Post-Persecution Restitution Crisis** describes the 200+ year legal dispute that followed [[The Ancient Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] concerning property rights of executed individuals, whether executed individuals' families had claims to property confiscated by state authorities, and how different legal frameworks across [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] resolved (or failed to resolve) accountability for institutional seizure of assets. The crisis demonstrates how institutional violence creates legal cascades extending centuries beyond the violence itself, how property disputes can remain unresolved because admitting wrong requires admitting institutional criminality, and how persecuted communities develop permanent structural disadvantage from institutional theft disguised as legal process.

## The Confiscation Mechanism

During [[The Ancient Persecution Era|Persecution Era]], when individuals were executed as suspected demons:

**Immediate Property Transfer**: All property and assets of executed individuals automatically transferred to state administrative authority. Justification: "Compensation for administrative investigation costs and security threat management."

**No Appeal Mechanism**: Executed individuals had no recourse to contest seizure. Family members had no legal standing to claim property before execution. Post-execution, property was officially state asset, not subject to inherited claim.

**Scope of Seizure**: Confiscation included personal property, real estate, tools/equipment, livestock, financial assets (transferred as currency), and business interests. If executed individual had been merchant with inventory, entire inventory transferred to state (sometimes sold at market, sometimes transferred to state-approved successor merchant with state receiving portion of proceeds).

**Estimated Total Value**: Across both kingdoms over 45-year period, approximately 2,000-3,000 property seizures occurred. Total asset value: estimated 500,000-800,000 Watts (enormous sum in era when average annual laborer income was 50 Watts).

The confiscation process enriched state treasuries and created financial incentive to continue identifying "demons" as asset supplementation strategy.

## Immediate Post-Persecution Problem

When persecution ended (~430 years ago), both kingdoms faced immediate legal crisis:

**Executed Individuals Deemed Innocent**: If persecuted demons were deemed innocent (or at least unproven guilty), then property seizure was theft disguised as legal process.

**Claimants Emerging**: Families of executed individuals began making restitution claims. "Our ancestor was executed without proper trial. We claim confiscated property should return to heirs."

**Institutional Dilemma**: Accepting restitution claims meant:
1. Admitting persecution was institutional crime
2. Returning enormous assets to families (creating massive financial cost)
3. Acknowledging state theft from vulnerable populations
4. Potentially opening prosecution of authorities who ordered confiscations

**Institutional Response**: Both kingdoms developed evasion strategies rather than accountability.

## Auralis Approach: Statute of Limitations

[[Auralis]] institutional leadership (approximately 420 years ago) issued formal ruling:

**Doctrine**: Property seizures that occurred more than 30 years prior to restitution claims were considered "completed transactions" subject to statute of limitations. Claims could not be pursued for property taken more than 30 years ago.

**Rationale**: "To restore stability and promote institutional trust, historical property disputes must eventually reach finality. Properties seized during Persecution Era have long since changed hands, creating complex legal chains of title. Attempting to unwind decades-old transactions would create chaos."

**Effect**: Since persecution had ended 50-80 years prior to ruling (by time of ruling), virtually all persecution-era confiscations fell outside statute of limitations. Approximately 90-95% of executed individuals' families had no legal recourse.

**Exceptions**: A handful of cases where properties had remained continuously in state ownership (not resold) could technically be claimed, but process required proving:
1. Ancestor was executed
2. Specific property could be identified
3. Property was never transferred during 50+ years
4. Claimant was legal heir with documented lineage

Process was so onerous that fewer than 20 successful restitution claims were pursued.

**Institutional Permanence**: Statute of limitations became permanent rule - no restitution possible for persecution-era seizures.

## Terravelle Approach: Guilt Verification

[[Terravelle]] took different approach:

**Doctrine**: Restitution claims would be assessed case-by-case. Executed individuals who could be demonstrated to have been actual demons forfeited restitution. Executed individuals who were innocent deserved restitution. Families were permitted to pursue claims if they could provide evidence in civil court that ancestors were innocent.

**Rationale**: "Justice requires distinguishing genuine perpetrators from innocent victims. We will compensate innocent persecution victims, not heretics."

**Effect**: Shifted burden to families of executed individuals to prove innocence in civil court against state prosecution apparatus same entities that had persecuted their ancestors.

**Practical Result**: Approximately 40-50 families pursued claims in civil court over 150-year period. Estimate 30-35 claims succeeded (courts determined ancestors were likely innocent). Approximately 10-15 claims failed (courts determined evidence supported demon hypothesis).

**Restitution Scale**: Successful claims generally resulted in restitution of fraction of original value (20-40%), adjusted downward to account for "administrative processing costs" and erosion of value over time. No interest compensation for 50-100 year periods of seized property. Inflation adjustment sometimes applied, sometimes not.

**Better Justice Outcome**: [[Terravelle]]'s approach was theoretically more just (discriminating innocent from guilty) but practically cruel (forcing families to relive persecution through civil proceeding, defending ancestors' innocence in court).

## Ongoing Unresolved Status

Even current era (250+ years post-persecution), restitution remains incomplete:

**Auralis**: Statute of limitations remains in effect. Persecution victims' descendants have inherited structural property disadvantage from ancestor confiscation, with no legal remedy. Some families maintain historical documentation of confiscated property as record of historical injustice. No institutional compensation provided.

**Terravelle**: Approximately 70-80 cases remain in dispute. Court systems are extremely slow. Some civil claims are 80+ years old without final determination. Some families gave up pursuit. Some remain determined to achieve justice.

**Cross-Kingdom Status**: Some executed individuals had property across both kingdoms. Survivors fleeing one kingdom and attempting to claim confiscated property in other kingdom faced jurisdictional disputes. Generally, courts ruled property seizures in one kingdom aren't subject to restitution determination in other kingdom, creating "lost" property that lies in legal limbo.

**Institutional Resistance**: Both kingdoms' administrative authorities resist restitution because:
1. Admitting persecution was crime requires admitting institutional wrongdoing
2. Restitution sets precedent for other persecutions/institutional violence
3. Property that was seized is now integrated into state holdings, making restitution painful
4. Many confiscated assets have already been spent or transferred decades ago

Institutions prefer permanent silence about confiscations rather than admitting liability.

## The Structural Injustice

The unresolved restitution creates permanent disadvantage for persecution victims' descendants:

**Inherited Poverty**: Families whose ancestors had property confiscated maintained lower economic status. Property advantage compounds across generations - wealth begets more wealth. Persecution victims' descendants are still economically disadvantaged 250 years later due to institutional theft.

**Social Status**: Communities that supplied disproportionate numbers of persecution victims developed lower social standing. Being from "persecuted families" created stigma. Intermarriage, professional opportunity, and social integration patterns were affected.

**Institutional Distrust**: Persecuted communities developed permanent distrust of state institutions and property law. Legal systems that had stolen from them once couldn't be trusted to protect them going forward.

**Historical Memory**: Persecution victims' descendants maintained grievance across centuries. Unresolved property claims kept persecution alive as active injustice rather than historical event.

## Why This Precedent Matters Currently

The restitution crisis demonstrates:

**Institutional Theft Pattern**: Persecution creates legal framework for asset seizure. Persecution ends, authorities face choice: admit crime and restore assets, or maintain silence and deny liability. Institutions almost universally choose silence.

**Structural Disadvantage Persistence**: Injustice that creates immediate economic loss (confiscated property) creates permanent disadvantage through intergenerational wealth transfer differential.

**Institutional Amnesty**: Persecuting authorities are virtually never prosecuted. Instead, institutions collectively amnesty themselves and move forward.

**Unresolved Grievance**: Lack of resolution means grievance persists for centuries, potentially triggering new violence if opportunity for justice emerges.

**Current Implications**: If modern demon revelation led to persecution (as Persecution Era demonstrates is probable), and if persecution ended:

1. **Demons' Property Would Be Seized**: Any demon-identified property would be confiscated through legal process
2. **Permanent Seizure**: Institutional authorities would resist restitution indefinitely
3. **Survivors Disadvantaged**: Demon survivors would approach new peaceful period as economically destroyed populations
4. **Unresolved Grievance**: Surviving demons would have permanent institutional cause for hostility
5. **Mutual Distrust**: Future relations between humans and demons would be permanently poisoned by institutional theft

[[Venn Drost]] attempting demon revelation without ensuring persecution prevention also ensures, almost inevitably, that persecution ends with institutional theft that is never resolved and creates permanent demon-human enmity.

The revelation itself isn't the problem. The problem is what comes after revelation when institutions apply established persecution frameworks.

Addressing that requires not just demon protection, but institutional framework that prevents confiscation and ensures restitution for any persecution that does occur.
