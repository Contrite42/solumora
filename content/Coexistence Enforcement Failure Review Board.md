# Coexistence Enforcement Failure Review Board

The **Coexistence Enforcement Failure Review Board** is a joint oversight mechanism formed two years after the [[Stories/The First Casualty|High Demon revelation crisis]] following a high-profile enforcement breakdown in a Full Integration Zone. It exists to investigate when coexistence enforcement actions themselves produce systemic harm, public backlash, or legal overreach severe enough to threaten framework legitimacy.

## Formation Trigger

The Board was created after the so-called "Aldrane Sweep Incident," where a rapid-response operation coordinated by local compliance officers and deputized civic guards produced mass detentions later found to include compliant residents, visiting researchers, and registered Monitoring Cell staff.

While the [[Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal]] invalidated most detentions within forty-eight hours, the incident caused:
- Public confidence collapse in Full Integration enforcement credibility
- Coordinated non-cooperation campaigns in multiple districts
- Political pressure to suspend Tribunal field authority in core cities

The [[Ashford Coexistence Oversight Commission]] argued that ad hoc internal review would be viewed as institutional self-protection. A structurally independent review body was authorized instead.

## Mandate

The Board does not replace existing institutions. It reviews failures crossing threshold criteria:

- **Scope Threshold:** 50+ individuals affected by a single enforcement action
- **Legitimacy Threshold:** documented procedural violations across at least two agencies
- **Destabilization Threshold:** measurable compliance drop across multiple districts within thirty days

When thresholds are met, the Board may issue:
- Public factual chronology with evidentiary annex
- Binding remediation directives for Commission and Tribunal field protocols
- Temporary suspension recommendations for specific enforcement practices
- Successor oversight requirements (audits, dual-signature approvals, independent observer presence)

## Composition

To avoid single-faction capture, seats are allocated across role classes:
- 2 Commission governance operators
- 2 Tribunal legal operators
- 2 cross-border civil systems delegates (one [[Auralis]], one [[Terravelle]])
- 1 Monitoring Cell technical delegate
- 1 rotating survivor-lineage observer from persecution-memory communities

No active field commander from an implicated incident can sit on the review panel for that case.

## Strategic Role

The Board institutionalizes an uncomfortable premise: coexistence frameworks can fail by overreaction as easily as by neglect. Post-reveal governance no longer treats enforcement errors as local embarrassment; it treats them as system-level risk events.

[[Merra Veld]] describes the Board as "an emergency brake for institutions convinced they are morally right and therefore operationally infallible."

[[Kess Tarren]] accepted the Board after initial opposition, framing it as necessary to preserve Tribunal authority through visible accountability rather than closed-door correction.

## Early Results

Within the first two review cycles, the Board produced:
- Standardized detention-verification gates before mass actions
- Mandatory independent observer presence for high-risk sweeps
- Public "false-positive index" reporting to track overreach rates

Critics still argue the Board adds bureaucratic drag during real threats. Supporters counter that the drag is precisely the point: slowing institutions before they convert fear into legally sanctioned error.
