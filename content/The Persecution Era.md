The Persecution Era is the informal name for a period of approximately two centuries, beginning roughly four hundred years ago, during which community-level violence against people suspected of extremely high-tier Flux capacity was widespread across both kingdoms. It was not coordinated, not officially sanctioned, and not effective at achieving any coherent goal. It burned itself out rather than being ended, and it is remembered, where it is remembered at all, as a general era of suspicion rather than a specific campaign.

## The Incident

The period began with an incident in a small northern settlement — now in [[Terravelle]]'s territory, though the kingdom did not yet exist in its current form. A practitioner of unknown tier cast an uncontrolled Force effect that killed one person and a horse and severely injured a third. The caster was identified and killed. This was not unusual for the period; violent response to dangerous magical failure was not uncommon.

What was unusual was what followed. The killing triggered a response in the community that was not directed at the specific caster — who was dead — but at anyone who might have similar capacity. The logic, as best as historical scholarship can reconstruct it, was something like: if this was possible once, anyone who can do it is a threat. The response spread to neighboring settlements, mutated as it traveled, and within a generation had become a diffuse cultural pattern rather than a response to a specific event.

The connection to [[High Demons|dimensional incursion]] was never made at the time. Current scholarship has not definitively made it either, though the circumstantial case is strong: uncontrolled Force effects of the type described, occurring in a population with no systematic Flux management, in a period when the ruins sites in the desert were not yet systematically monitored, has the profile of what a rift incursion would produce at a small scale. Whether the original incident was caused by an incursion or simply by a practitioner who overcasted beyond their control is unknown. What is known is that the community's response became generalized in ways that went far beyond anything the original incident justified.

## The Pattern

The Persecution Era's violence was decentralized and inconsistent. In some settlements, it took the form of formal exclusion — high-tier practitioners denied guild membership, denied land rights, denied marriage within the community. In others, it was more direct. The records that survive are incomplete and often written by people with reason to present events in particular ways.

What is consistent across surviving records is the absence of any systematic attempt to distinguish between practitioners who had harmed or threatened anyone and those who simply had high capacity. The target was capacity itself — the potential to do harm — rather than demonstrated harm. This is the logic of preventive persecution rather than punitive response, and it is what gave the period its particular character: the harm was often speculative and the targets were people whose only transgression was being what they were.

## The End

The Persecution Era did not end with a decision or a reversal. It ran out of momentum. By approximately two hundred years ago — coinciding roughly with the beginning of the [[First Contact]] period, though the connection is probably circumstantial rather than causal — the active violence had subsided in most of the documented regions. The cultural residue persisted longer: suspicion of high-tier capacity, particularly in rural communities, remained elevated for decades afterward.

What replaced it was not acceptance but institutional management. The guild certification systems in [[Terravelle]] and the [[Ascendant Path]]'s tier advancement frameworks in [[Auralis]] both provided structures through which high-tier practitioners could be integrated into social and economic life on terms that existing communities could manage. The institutions made high-tier capacity legible in ways that made it less threatening — a T5 with a guild certification is a known quantity in a way that a T5 with no institutional affiliation was not.

## The Long Shadow

The Persecution Era is why [[High Demons|High Demons]] in [[Solumora]] learned to hide. The conditions that produced the era — decentralized communities, no systematic Flux monitoring, a cultural environment where high capacity was treated as inherently dangerous — no longer exist in the same form. But the memory of what communities are capable of doing to people who seem too different or too powerful is part of the world's texture in ways that are not always conscious.

[[Selvane]], [[Mave]], [[Fennick]], [[Wren]], [[Sorath]]: none of them knew the Persecution Era personally. All of them exist in a world it shaped.
