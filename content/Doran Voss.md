_(Voss Line — Wolfpoint)_

**Doran Voss** is nineteen years old, the younger of [[Sera Voss|Sera Voss's]] two children, and the person in [[Wolfpoint]] whose Hypertext work most closely resembles the Voss family tradition at its best. He is precise, methodical, and has his mother's quality of attention. He is also, like his mother, deeply difficult to surprise.

## Character

Doran does not perform capability. He does not need to — the work speaks clearly enough, and in Wolfpoint, where the work is the only measure that matters, there is no reason to say more than the work says. He produces Hypertext sequences that are technically clean, logically sound, and entirely within the Voss family's established framework. His teacher at sixteen said he was the most promising student since his grandmother. Doran recorded this assessment and returned to the exercise.

He is not cold. He is contained. The people who have known him long enough to get past the containment find someone who is genuinely interested in them and remembers details of their lives and work with the same precision he applies to sequences. The relationship between his external presentation and his interior life is simply not one he discusses casually.

His [[Flux]] ability is developing in the way that Voss family ability develops — carefully, deliberately, without performance. He is probably T2, possibly beginning T3. He does not test formally and Wolfpoint does not test formally. He knows his range from the work.

## The Comparison He Does Not Make

Doran is nineteen, the same age as the Prince of [[Terravelle]]. He has read enough of the family's correspondence archives to know that the Terravellian succession situation is complicated in ways that the official account does not fully reflect, and that the current prince is the same age as him, and that the circumstances they were both born into have imposed expectations they did not choose.

He does not draw a comparison between them. He has noted the parallel and set it aside as an interesting datum that is not actionable. He is not incurious about the world outside Wolfpoint; he is simply confident that Wolfpoint is where he currently belongs and that this is enough.

## On the Succession

Doran is aware that he and [[Ysel Voss|Ysel]] are both being assessed for leadership. He has no strong position on who should succeed. He knows the formal criteria — demonstrated Hypertext mastery, ability to teach and lead — and he knows that Ysel's mastery is more creative and his is more precise, and that both qualities are needed, and that the family will reach the right conclusion through its process. He is not anxious about this.

He is, occasionally, curious about what Ysel is working on in her personal notes, which she does not share with him. He has not asked directly. He will, eventually. He is not in a hurry.
