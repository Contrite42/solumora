# Morra Keld

**Morra Keld** runs preservation operations at [[Mountain Game Collective]], overseeing the facility where fresh game becomes preserved meat that travels south through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. She is thirty-seven years old, [[Control Tier|Tier 2]], and has managed preservation for six years. Her job is quality control: determining which meat enters export inventory, which serves internal consumption, and what gets rejected entirely.

The Collective's export revenue depends on preservation quality. Morra makes the calls that determine whether Wolfpoint's preserved game  maintains its premium-pricing reputation or degrades into commodity-quality meat no different from coastal alternatives.

## The Preservation Process

Fresh game from upland hunts reaches Wolfpoint's central facility within 24 hours. Morra's team processes it through five stages:
1. Salt treatment (heavy application using imported sea salt).
2. Cold-wind drying (8-12 days in open-air racks where Wolfpoint's climate creates optimal conditions).
3. Quality inspection (Morra personally inspects every piece, removing any showing spoilage signs).
4. Sorting (export quality versus internal consumption versus rejection).
5. Packing (approved export meat sealed into transport crates).

Her constraint is **salt** availability. [[Wolfpoint]] has no local salt source. Every pound comes from [[Ashford]] through [[Ashford Supply Contract]] relationships managed by [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]]. When packet delays reduce salt supply, Morra operates at reduced capacity, focusing on highest-value cuts and deferring lower-priority work.

## Managing Salt Dependency

Morra communicates directly with [[Kess Rolv]] at [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] about salt needs. The Registry negotiates annual contracts approximately 20% above projected preservation requirements to buffer against maritime disruptions.

This buffer has worked for six years. It is also the minimum safety margin: if [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] experiences extended delays (major storm damage, political disruption, captain failures), preservation operations would face immediate shutdown risk within weeks.

Morra maintains contingency protocols:
- Priority preservation (highest-value export cuts processed first when salt runs low).
- Internal consumption redirection (meat that would normally be preserved goes to immediate consumption instead).
- Communication with [[Dren Vass]] about harvest adjustments (if salt shortage is extended, hunting operations slow to prevent waste of game the Collective cannot preserve).

She has never had to fully activate these protocols. She updates them every year anyway.

## Quality Standards

Morra's inspection standards are strict: any piece showing discoloration, unusual texture, or off-smell gets rejected from export inventory. These pieces either go to immediate internal consumption (if still safe to eat) or get discarded entirely.

This strictness costs revenue — rejection rates run 8-12% of total harvest volume. Morra accepts this because preservation quality is [[Wolfpoint]]'s commercial advantage. One batch of substandard meat reaching southern buyers damages reputation that took generations to build. Better to lose individual pieces than to risk market confidence.

[[Renn Taska]] at [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] supports this approach explicitly: premium pricing depends on consistent quality. Quality control is not optional.

## Six Years of Pattern Recognition

Morra has developed expertise in reading preservation failure modes:
- Salt contamination (impurities in imported salt that affect preservation chemistry).
- Temperature variance (unexpected warm periods that disrupt drying timelines).
- Processing timing errors (delays between field stations and central facility that start spoilage before preservation can succeed).

This expertise is embedded knowledge gained through six years of daily inspection work, not documented in written protocols. She trains preservation workers through direct instruction and by catching errors before they become batch failures.

## Daily Reality

Morra spends most days at the preservation facility: inspecting pieces in-progress, managing salt inventory allocations, training workers in quality standards, and making final approval decisions about what enters export manifests.

She coordinates constantly with Dren Vass about harvest timing and volumes, ensuring preservation capacity matches incoming game flow. The system works through sustained communication and mutual understanding of operational constraints.

She reviews the drying racks. She updates the salt inventory logs. She rejects three pieces showing early spoilage signs. The approved meat enters packing. The trade continues.
