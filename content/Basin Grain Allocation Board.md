The Basin Grain Allocation Board is Auralis's central food-allocation institution for dense urban districts, with authority to redirect grain flows during shortage or transport disruption.

Its existence reflects a practical truth: city stability starts with regular bread.

## Funding

The Board is funded by bulk-grain levies, storage licensing fees, and emergency reserve appropriations set by [[The Council of Auralis]].

## Recruitment

It recruits warehouse planners, route schedulers, and documentation clerks from port and inland markets across [[Auralis]].

## Rules

- reserve floors are fixed before commercial release
- district allotments publish weekly in shortage periods
- anti-hoarding inspections trigger automatic audits

## Opposition

Large speculators pressure allocation policy during unstable seasons, and some expansion-focused operators resent reserve constraints that limit short-term export profit.

## What It Trades In

It trades in predictability: households can plan, bakeries can stay open, and council panic stays lower.
