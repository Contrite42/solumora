_(Megastructure Program, Controlled Separation)_

**The Partition Works** refers to the late-era construction program believed to have separated unstable Flux domains from high-density population corridors during the final century of [[The Ancient Civilization]]. The works are inferred from repeated geometric signatures in ruin foundations and from inscriptions describing "zones of tolerated loss" and "containment boundaries."

## Purpose Hypothesis

Most scholars now treat the works as emergency infrastructure meant to:

- Limit spread of dimensional instability
- Keep transport arteries operational under rising anomaly frequency
- Protect archival and governance nodes long enough for managed transition

In this model, the works were not monuments. They were triage architecture.

## Why It Matters

The Partition Works imply ancient authorities prioritized system survival over territorial continuity. That strategic logic resembles modern emergency doctrine but at a much larger technical scale.

If true, then several active anomaly sites in [[Equatorial Desert]] may be remnants of containment boundaries still operating far past intended duration.

## Link to Modern Risk

Understanding partition logic could help explain why current [[Emberfall Probability Maps]] identify recurring stress corridors. Modern incidents may be distributed along ancient boundary lines rather than purely natural geography.
