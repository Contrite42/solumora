The Bone Road Consortium is a cross-border merchant network that maintains commercial relationships between [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] regardless of political climate. It is not a cartel, not a guild, and not politically aligned with either kingdom. It is a set of coordinated commercial relationships among merchants in both kingdoms who have found that maintaining inter-kingdom trade through periods of tension is both possible and profitable.

## The Name

The Bone Road is an informal name for the combined network of desert-edge routes that connect Terravelle's southern trade to Auralis's northern commercial infrastructure — the [[Ashford-Halveth Courier Road]], the [[Zakros Rim Caravan Loop]], and the desert crossing channels managed by the [[Halveth Cooperative]]. Merchants who work these routes have been calling them the Bone Road since before anyone formalized the arrangement. The name stuck to the network.

## What the Consortium Does

The Consortium's operational function is market intelligence and routing coordination. Members in both kingdoms share information about what is moving, at what price, and where bottlenecks are forming. This information sharing produces commercial advantages that independent operators cannot replicate: better pricing, earlier warning of disruptions, access to distribution relationships on the other side of the desert that would otherwise take years to build individually.

What this means in practice: a Terravelle merchant with Consortium connections can move goods into Auralis markets more efficiently and with better price certainty than a merchant without those connections. The reverse is equally true. The Consortium's value is the network itself — the years of established relationships, verified reliability, and shared commercial intelligence.

## Political Neutrality as Strategy

The Consortium is deliberately useful to both sides. Its members in [[Terravelle]] maintain relationships with the [[Trade Guilds]] and operate within guild norms. Its members in [[Auralis]] maintain relationships with the relevant commercial institutions and Scholar-network contacts. Neither group will say publicly that they are part of a cross-kingdom network; both groups behave in ways consistent with their own kingdom's commercial norms.

This neutrality is not ideological. It is commercial self-interest. The Consortium profits from inter-kingdom trade flowing. It profits from political tension that drives up the margin on goods that can only be obtained from the other kingdom. It does not profit from closed borders. This means the Consortium has a structural interest in ensuring that no political escalation goes so far as to actually cut the routes.

Members of the Consortium have, on specific occasions, provided information to both kingdoms' intelligence services through their normal commercial reporting relationships — and have never provided information that would damage the network's ability to operate on both sides. This is not espionage. It is commercial risk management.

## Current Concern

The [[Expansion Faction]]'s pressure campaign on Ashford-adjacent commerce is driving up friction costs faster than the Consortium can absorb through routing adjustments. The documentation delays that [[Orre Cavlt]] has been tracking, the changed inspection tone that [[Doss Varn]] has noticed — these are, from the Consortium's operational perspective, a deterioration of the environment that makes their business model work. The Consortium does not have a mechanism to resolve this politically, but it is tracking the deterioration carefully and is adjusting its routing assumptions.
