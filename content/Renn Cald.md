**Renn Cald** is a leatherworker and equipment repair specialist who has lived in [[Wolfpoint]] for twenty-three years. He is fifty-one, T1, and arrived as a young craftsperson following work opportunities and has not left.

## How He Ended Up There

Renn came to Wolfpoint at twenty-eight on a two-year contract to provide equipment maintenance for a Scholarium expedition preparing for a northern ruins survey. The cold-forge metalwork that Wolfpoint is known for required complementary leatherwork expertise — harness repair, cold-condition boot maintenance, the specific techniques for keeping leather functional in the temperature extremes of the northern terrain — and the settlement was importing this skill through short-term contracts rather than developing it internally.

He completed the two-year contract. He accepted a third year. By the time he was genuinely free to leave, he had built the equipment maintenance operation that Wolfpoint's research expeditions depend on, had established a workshop and a routine, and had become the person that the Scholarium's expedition planning assumed would be there.

Staying was not a dramatic decision. It was the accumulated weight of many small decisions to not leave yet.

## Life in Wolfpoint

Renn occupies a specific social category in Wolfpoint that the settlement does not have formal language for: permanent resident who is neither Voss family nor Scholarium researcher. The settlement's social structure is organized around the Voss-led research institution; everyone else is either a Scholarium participant (temporary, on access terms) or family/support staff. Renn is support staff who has been there long enough to be part of the social fabric but not long enough — there is no long enough — to be of the settlement in the way the Voss family is.

He does not find this distressing. Wolfpoint's practical culture — the Quiet Work ethic that treats competence as the primary social currency — works in his favor. He is very good at what he does, and twenty-three years of being the person the expedition teams depend on for working equipment has produced the particular kind of social standing that comes from consistent reliability.

He knows the Voss family well enough to have dinner at the family table on appropriate occasions. He knows [[Sera Voss]] as someone who has a precise understanding of what equipment she needs for specific work conditions and communicates it clearly, which he considers a professional virtue. He knows [[Mell Rask]] as one of the Scholarium researchers he has worked alongside on multiple expeditions over the past decade.

## What He Observes

Renn has not been told what the Scholarium is currently working on at the level of strategic significance, and he has not asked. He is aware, from the pattern of expedition preparation he supports, that the ruins-survey work has changed in character over the past three or four years — the surveys are going further north, the equipment specifications have been adjusted for extended deployment rather than the shorter forays that were standard before, and the composition of the teams has changed in ways that suggest the surveys are finding something worth sustained attention.

He does not know that [[Watts]] and the ancient civilization's construction are connected. He does not know about the Hypertext documentation project. He knows that something has changed in what Wolfpoint is doing, and he has concluded that this is not his business to pursue unless someone decides it should be.

This is the Quiet Work ethic applied to information: it is not his, it is not his role to obtain it, and his job is to make sure the people whose role it is have functional equipment when they go to find it.
