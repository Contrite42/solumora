Veln Hold is a river fishing and transport village on the lower [[Maren River]], thirty miles north of [[Valdenmoor]]. Population approximately 250. It supplies the city with fresh and preserved river fish, operates a seasonal ferry crossing, and provides stopover services for [[Maren Freight Chain]] barge crews.

## The Work

The Maren River at Veln Hold runs wide, slow, and deep — ideal for net fishing and barge traffic, poor for fast transport. The village economy divides between fishing operations (active year-round) and river transport support (seasonal, concentrated during the grain-shipping months).

Fishing crews work from small flat-bottomed boats, setting stake nets in the slow eddies where the river widens around the bend. Most fish caught are freshwater bass, pike, and seasonal migratory carp that move upriver in early spring. Sald Orvn, who has fished the Veln Hold reach for thirty years, knows every productive eddy and every seasonal shift in fish behavior that the river produces.

The catch moves two directions: fresh fish go downriver to Valdenmoor markets via daily runner boats, and preserved fish (salted, smoked, or dried) go to the village's communal storage for winter distribution and overland trade.

## The Ferry

Veln Hold operates the only reliable crossing point on the lower Maren for forty miles in either direction. The ferry is a flat barge pulled by cables anchored on both banks, operated by a rotating crew of village residents who maintain the cable system and coordinate crossing schedules.

The ferry is not a major revenue source — most traffic is local farmers and occasional travelers — but it is a service the village has maintained for generations because it keeps Veln Hold relevant to the surrounding communities. When the ferry is down, travelers add two days to their route. When the ferry is operational, Veln Hold is a stop on the way to somewhere else, which means trade, information, and connections that a purely fishing village would not maintain.

## The Barge Stopover

During the grain-shipping season (late summer through mid-autumn), [[Maren Freight Chain]] barges stop at Veln Hold for crew rotation, minor repairs, and resupply. The village provides meals, temporary housing, and equipment maintenance to barge operations in exchange for priority shipping slots for Veln Hold's fish deliveries and access to trade goods moving downriver from the interior.

Mira Cols coordinates stopover logistics. She schedules berth access, organizes provisioning, and maintains relationships with freight chain dispatch coordinators in Valdenmoor. The work is administrative more than physical, but it is critical — a barge stopover relationship that functions smoothly is worth more to Veln Hold than the immediate revenue, because it keeps the village integrated into the broader river economy.

## The Practitioners

Veln Hold has three active [[Flux Users|practitioners]], all [[Control Tier|Tier 1]], all fishers first and casters second. They pool their capacity during spring flood season to stabilize the ferry cable anchors and protect the village's fish-drying racks from storm surge. The casting is low-tier, low-cost, and effective — not because the practitioners are exceptional, but because they have been managing the same problems in the same location for years.

No one at Veln Hold treats Flux work as separate from fishing or ferry operations. It is part of the toolkit that keeps the village functional, used when needed and not otherwise.

## What the Village Notices

River traffic this season has been lighter than usual. The [[Maren Freight Chain]] confirmed reduced barge schedules due to upstream harvest delays, which means fewer stopover nights and reduced provisioning income for Veln Hold. The shortfall is not catastrophic — fishing continues regardless of barge traffic — but it is noticeable.

Mira Cols has been tracking stopover frequency for five years. This season's drop is the steepest on record. She does not know if it reflects a temporary disruption or a longer shift in freight patterns, but she is adjusting the village's provisioning contracts accordingly.

The village continues. The work continues. But margin is narrower than it was.
