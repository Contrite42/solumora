Grimoire Authentication is the process of verifying that a [[Grimoires|grimoire]] contains accurate, functional [[Sigils]] copied from a trusted source. Because [[Flux]] casting requires precise sigil geometry, a single transcription error can cause a spell to misfire, underperform, or fail entirely. Authentication protects buyers from purchasing defective or fraudulent texts.

## How Authentication Works

The [[Guild Scrivener Network]] maintains authenticated source libraries — grimoires known to contain correct sigils, verified through repeated successful casting and institutional lineage. When a scrivener produces a copy, the guild inspects the work by comparing it to the source text. Copies that pass inspection receive a guild mark — typically an embossed seal or ink stamp indicating the date, location, and inspecting authority.

Independent authentication is harder. A buyer commissioning a grimoire from an [[Independent Scrivener]] must either test the spells themselves or hire a third party to verify sigil accuracy. Some buyers use [[Copymark]] or [[Fluxread]] to detect obvious errors, but those spells only catch gross mistakes, not subtle geometry flaws that degrade performance over time.

## Fraud Patterns

The most common fraud is careless copying — a scrivener rushing work and introducing errors through inattention rather than intent. The result is the same: a buyer pays for a grimoire that doesn't function as expected.

Deliberate fraud is rarer but more damaging. Some fraudulent grimoires are sold as higher-tier texts than they actually contain. A [[Common Grimoire]] might be rebound with expensive materials and sold as [[Uncommon Grimoire|Uncommon]], exploiting buyers who can't cast the spells to verify tier accuracy.

Cross-border fraud is harder to detect. A grimoire sourced from [[Auralis]] and sold in [[Terravelle]] may contain correct sigils but come from an unauthenticated lineage. If the original source was flawed, every copy downstream inherits the error, and tracing the lineage back takes months of guild correspondence.

## Authentication Registry

The [[Guild Scrivener Network]] maintains a shared authentication registry listing verified source texts by title, author (if known), and holding institution. The registry is updated quarterly and distributed to guild halls across both kingdoms. It's not comprehensive — many private grimoires exist outside the registry — but it's the baseline for commercial trust.

Disputes over authenticity can take months to resolve. If a buyer claims a grimoire is fraudulent, the guild investigates by testing spells, examining sigil geometry, and tracing the copy's lineage. If fraud is confirmed, the scrivener faces financial penalties and potential suspension. If the buyer is wrong, they may owe compensation for the disruption.

## Reputation Infrastructure

In practice, authentication relies heavily on reputation. A scrivener with decades of clean work is trusted more than a new copyist, even if both produce technically identical output. An [[Independent Scrivener]] working in a rural community may never interact with the guild registry but still be trusted locally because their grimoires work and their neighbors vouch for them.

Reputation also protects against fraud claims. A scrivener with a strong track record can weather an accusation more easily than someone with thin documentation or recent disputes. This creates pressure to work conservatively — copying only well-known spells from well-documented sources — which slows the spread of new or experimental sigil designs.
