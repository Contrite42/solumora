# Council Orders Registry

The **Council Orders Registry** is the institutional link between [[The Council of Auralis]]'s deliberative votes and the actionable directives sent to district administrators, guild offices, and expedition coordinators across [[Auralis]]. When the council votes, the Registry translates intent into implementation language: budgets, timelines, responsible parties, compliance metrics, and the sequence of steps that makes abstract policy concrete.

The Registry's mandate is neutral coordination. Its reality is interpretation under pressure.

## What the Registry Does

The Registry receives council resolutions — often brief, sometimes vague, occasionally contradictory — and produces structured implementation orders. These orders specify:

- **Who** is responsible for execution (district administrators, guild coordinators, institutional heads).
- **What** must be delivered (infrastructure thresholds, resource allocations, regulatory shifts).
- **When** outcomes are due (phased timelines, milestone checkpoints, audit windows).
- **How** compliance will be verified (inspection schedules, reporting requirements, escalation triggers).

The Registry does not decide policy. It decides what policy means when someone has to act on it tomorrow.

## The Translation Problem

Council resolutions are political documents. They emerge from factional compromise, strategic ambiguity, and vote-counting logistics. [[The Culmination Faction]] wants expedition funding increased "substantially." [[Expansion Faction]] wants border infrastructure "secured appropriately." Cassia's faction wants transparency "where feasible."

The Registry translates these phrases into numbers, names, and dates. This is where politics becomes logistics — and where the original intent often shifts.

A resolution calling for "enhanced desert expedition capacity" can become:
- A 15% budget increase to [[Emberfall Expedition Registry]] (modest interpretation).
- Mandatory quarterly expedition quotas for research institutions (aggressive interpretation).
- Relaxed safety protocols to accelerate permit approvals (dangerous interpretation).

The Registry makes these calls daily. The council reviews outcomes, not the fine print.

## Staff and Process

The Registry operates from a three-story administrative building in [[Solhaven]], two blocks from the council chambers. Sixteen permanent staff handle order drafting, two senior clerks manage cross-institutional coordination, and [[Vessa Rolt]] serves as Registry Coordinator — the person who signs final orders and answers when implementation fails.

Orders pass through a multi-step review:
1. **Initial draft** by a junior clerk based on council session transcripts.
2. **Technical review** by the relevant domain specialist (expedition logistics, grain allocation, infrastructure timelines).
3. **Coordination check** to verify the order does not conflict with existing standing directives.
4. **Final approval** by Vessa Rolt, who has learned to read between the lines of what the council actually wants versus what they said in the chamber.

Most orders are routine. The difficult ones — where factional intent is unclear or where resource constraints make full compliance impossible — end up on Vessa's desk at the end of long days.

## The Institutional Position

The Registry sits at a structural fault line. The council expects their decisions to be executed as intended. District administrators and institutional heads expect orders to be realistic, coherent, and accompanied by the resources needed for compliance. These expectations do not always align.

When they don't, the Registry absorbs the friction. Orders get delayed for "technical clarification." Timelines get quietly extended. Compliance metrics get softened in ways that preserve formal accountability while reducing practical burden. The council notices when major failures occur. They do not notice when medium-sized disasters are prevented through careful mistranslation.

[[Vessa Rolt]] runs a log of every order that required substantive interpretation. She does not share this log. It is her insurance against the day someone decides the Registry is responsible for outcomes it was never resourced to control.

## Relationship with Other Institutions

The Registry coordinates directly with [[Solhaven Elevation Works Office]], [[Basin Grain Allocation Board]], [[Emberfall Expedition Registry]], and other operational bodies that execute council policy. These institutions see the Registry as a filter — sometimes helpful (when orders arrive with realistic timelines and adequate funding), sometimes obstructive (when Registry caution slows council-backed initiatives).

[[Council Compliance Directorate]] inspects the outcomes the Registry sets in motion. The relationship is tense by design: the Directorate evaluates whether orders were followed, the Registry ensures orders are followable. When compliance failures occur, both institutions claim the other should have caught the problem earlier.
