# Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum

The Intergenerational Coexistence Curriculum (ICC) is the standardized education framework introduced across participating districts one year after the first casualty to prevent memory drift about persecution dynamics, dimensional risk governance, and mixed-capacity crisis coordination.

It extends early emergency-era educational revisions into a full multi-year civic-learning sequence.

## Why It Was Created

First-year post-crisis surveys showed a widening generational interpretation gap:
- adults who lived through corridor standstill prioritized safeguards,
- younger cohorts treated coexistence constraints as abstract bureaucracy,
- political messaging increasingly reframed safeguards as temporary anomalies.

The ICC was designed to convert short-term crisis memory into durable civic competence.

## Curriculum Strands

### 1. Historical Mechanism Literacy
Students study how persecution emerges from procedure, not only ideology, using case modules on [[The Lantern Decrees]] and [[Ashford Witness Amnesty Records]].

### 2. Risk Communication and Verification
Students learn to interpret public risk bulletins, distinguish rumor from evidence, and understand why technical language precision matters.

### 3. Cross-Tier Coordination Practice
Scenario modules require mixed-role decision-making where logistics, interpretation, and restraint are scored alongside high-output capacity.

### 4. Governance Under Uncertainty
Learners evaluate tradeoffs between emergency speed and due-process safeguards through simulated tribunal and oversight hearings.

### 5. Coexistence Ethics
Framework for non-human civic participation: personhood, accountability, intervention limits, and institutional oversight obligations.

## Delivery Model

The ICC uses layered delivery:
- primary level: civic signals and safety basics,
- middle level: procedural fairness and risk reasoning,
- upper level: historical mechanism analysis and governance simulations,
- vocational/civic academies: advanced operational governance modules.

Teacher support includes annual update packets tied to JRMC/APCT precedent changes.

## Assessment Philosophy

The ICC avoids doctrine tests based on faction loyalty. Assessment focuses on:
- evidentiary reasoning,
- procedural integrity under stress,
- ability to identify recurrence patterns,
- coordination quality across capability differences.

## Early Effects

First implementation cycle reported:
- lower rumor-driven conflict in school-adjacent districts,
- higher public comprehension of bulletin states,
- improved municipal hearing participation quality among younger attendees.

Critics label the curriculum as politicized memory engineering. Defenders argue that forgetting persecution mechanics is itself political.

## Role in Long-Term Stability

The ICC is the educational counterpart to legal safeguards: tribunals can block bad directives, but only public competence can reduce demand for them.

Its long-run goal is not consensus. It is preventing ignorance from becoming an administrative weapon.
