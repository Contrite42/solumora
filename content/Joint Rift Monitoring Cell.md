# Joint Rift Monitoring Cell

The Joint Rift Monitoring Cell (JRMC) is the first standing bilateral technical body jointly operated by [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] since the emergency period following the [[Kellvost Incursion]]. It was created under the [[Ashford Emergency Ceasefire Framework]] to provide shared dimensional-risk intelligence and prevent corridor-level cascade triggers.

The JRMC is headquartered in [[Ashford]], with mirrored relay desks in one Terravelle and one Auralis command facility.

## Mandate

The cell is authorized to:
- collect, validate, and publish real-time rift and micro-distortion observations,
- model corridor load and trigger thresholds,
- classify movement requests as low, medium, or red-band risk,
- issue technical hold recommendations that automatically pause high-tier movement pending review.

The JRMC has no direct military command authority. Its leverage comes from ceasefire compliance clauses that link movement authorization to technical risk classification.

## Composition

The JRMC is structured as a mixed technical unit:
- rift instrumentation specialists,
- logistics modelers,
- ruins-structure interpreters,
- command liaison analysts,
- emergency medics trained in Flux contamination response.

Core named contributors in early operations include [[Jess Maren]] (logistics risk modeling), [[Kess Tarren]] (anchor interpretation and ruins thresholds), and rotating field consultation from [[Merra Veld]] when movement plans intersect expedition corridors.

[[Teren Voss|Verath]] is not a formal staff member but has provided high-load anomaly interpretation under exceptional clauses.

## Output Products

The JRMC publishes three primary outputs:

### 1. Corridor Risk Bulletin
Issued every 6 hours. Contains load state, instability trend, and movement constraints by sector.

### 2. Trigger Watch Notice
Issued when measured behavior approaches historical cascade signatures. Automatically activates elevated command relay protocols.

### 3. Incident Audit Packet
Post-event reconstruction package for any near-breach, used for accountability and procedure refinement.

## Friction Points

The JRMC's work is repeatedly constrained by:
- contested data sovereignty claims,
- delayed instrumentation handoffs,
- political attempts to reinterpret risk language for strategic flexibility,
- legal challenges around non-human technical input.

Despite this, it has established a shared evidentiary baseline that neither kingdom can fully replace with unilateral narratives.

## Strategic Impact

The JRMC shifted border crisis management from rumor and delayed diplomatic packets to near-real-time technical governance. This has not removed political conflict, but it has narrowed the window where either side can plausibly claim ignorance before escalation decisions.

Its deepest structural effect is cultural: both kingdoms now possess a living institution where cross-border cooperation is normal at working level even while public rhetoric remains adversarial.
