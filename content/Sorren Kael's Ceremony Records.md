**Advancement ceremony, mid-autumn**

*Candidate: T4 assessment, formal registration with chapter advancement track. Ceremony: standard third-tier acknowledgment. Result: completed.*

She cried during the third recitation, which is not unusual. She held the cast for forty-two seconds past the standard close, which I have not seen before and which I did not interrupt. Whatever she was doing with the extra forty-two seconds was not part of the formal ceremony. It was also the most significant thing that happened in the ceremony room today.

**Consolation ceremony, winter**

*Requesting party: family. Occasion: T0 assessment result, adult child. Ceremony: Path's standing consolation rite.*

The standing consolation rite for T0 assessment results has three formal components. I have performed it forty-one times. The third component, which centers on "contribution through witness and contemplation," works when the person receiving it is already at peace with the result. It does not work when they are not, and there is no mechanism in the rite to address the difference. I delivered the ceremony as written. The family thanked me. The young man in question did not look at me when he left.

I have submitted a request to the regional chapter authority twice to discuss revising the consolation ceremony for cases where the person is not at peace with the result. Both times I was told the ceremony reflects the Path's core teaching and that pastoral support outside the ceremonial framework is available through standard counseling scheduling. I offer standard counseling scheduling. I do not know how to make the gap between the ceremony and this particular kind of pain smaller through standard counseling scheduling.

**Private consultation, summer**

*Outside formal ceremony — not a ceremony record in the standard sense. Included here because I do not have another place to put it.*

A man came to the chapter house who had been managing his T5 casting without formal training for thirty years. Self-trained, rural origin, northern coast. He came because something in his practice had changed and he did not understand the change and he wanted to know if the Path had a name for it.

I listened for two hours. The Path does not have a name for what he described, or if it does, the name belongs to a category of practice that the formal curriculum does not address in straightforward terms. I told him what I knew, which was less than he needed. He was not disappointed. He seemed, at the end of the two hours, satisfied that he had found the edge of what the formal system could tell him, which was what he had come to find out.

He left me with the sense that the thirty years of self-directed practice had taught him something that the formal curriculum, at its best, might take fifty years to approach from a different direction. I am not sure what to do with this. I wrote it down here because the ceremony records are the place I write things down.

**T0 reassessment consultation, winter**

*Not a ceremony — a consultation, included because I do not know where else to record it.*

A woman came to ask whether a T0 assessment result can be wrong. The short answer is: rarely, but occasionally. The longer answer involves explaining what the assessment actually measures and what it does not, which takes more time than most people want to spend on information that does not change their situation.

She wanted the longer answer. I gave it.

At the end she asked whether the [[Ascendant Path]]'s teaching that T0 individuals remain part of the Path's community was a belief the chapter actually held or a formal position maintained for social reasons.

I told her the chapter held it. I told her I held it. I told her the T0 consolation ceremony is not adequate to the experience it is supposed to address, and that I had been trying to change this for twelve years, and that if she was willing to come back I would try to do better than the ceremony.

She came back. We have been meeting once a month for the past three years. I still do not know if I have given her anything useful. She keeps coming back.
