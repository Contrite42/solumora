# High Demon Planning and Strategic Organization

[[High Demons|High Demons]] operate according to planning horizons that have no human parallel. Even the youngest have existed longer than kingdoms. The oldest have watched the current world take shape across centuries. Their strategic thinking reflects time scales that create the illusion of inaction — because plans that measure decades are invisible year-to-year and plans measured in centuries are invisible across lifetimes.

## The Scale Problem

Human strategic planning typically operates at three scales: immediate (days to weeks), medium-term (seasonal or annual), and long-term (5-20 year projections). This is the natural limit of human careers, institution-building, and generational change. A [[Control Tier|T9]] scholar who achieves unusual longevity might shape institutional policy over 40 years. A kingdom might maintain a border strategy for a century. These are treated as extreme long-term planning in human political contexts.

High Demons do not experience these scales as "long-term." A two-hundred-year institutional project is an incidental commitment alongside other ongoing work. A five-hundred-year strategic goal is a moderate ambition.

The youngest High Demon in Solumora has been present for at least 140 years. The oldest has been here since before the current kingdoms consolidated. When they plan, the temporal frame of reference is simply not shared with any human institution. The result is that High Demon strategic projects are nearly invisible to all institutions attempting to observe them, because institutions look for patterns that repeat in human timescales. A strategy that will mature in 300 years looks like absence of activity.

## Individual Planning vs. Coordination

High Demons do not coordinate centrally. There is no council, no consensus building, no shared agenda from which individual strategies diverge. Each demon pursues goals that reflect their own centuries of experience in Solumora and their own understanding of the world.

What limits coordination is not animosity but incompatibility. Five beings with genuinely independent goals developed over centuries find common actionable projects rarely. When they become aware of each other's presence — which is always, given their [[High Demons|Flux detection]] capability — what they typically do is acknowledge the other's existence and adjust their own activities to avoid direct conflict. This is not alliance. It is not even formal communication in most cases. It is two entities with sufficient power to damage each other simply deciding that damage is not a worthwhile outcome.

The closest the five have come to strategic coordination is a shared understanding that their continued secrecy is essential to their survival. If the kingdoms became aware that High Demons exist, the response would be similar to the [[High Demons#The Hunting Period|historical hunting period]] — decentralised, undirected, but inevitable. This shared constraint creates a form of practical coordination: all five have incentive structures that reward individual secrecy and cooperation-through-silence. None of them have incentive to announce their existence.

This is as close to a coordinated High Demon agenda as currently exists. It is not a plan they jointly executed. It is five independent beings reaching the same conclusion.

## Planning Timeframes

The oldest High Demons operate using the following timeframe structure:

**Immediate horizon (1-10 years):** These are tactical adjustments. A [[Sorath|particular demon]] notices that a path they have been using is becoming more heavily monitored. They adjust routes. Or they implement a minor manipulation of local events to make the monitoring target something else. Or they address an immediate opportunity that has appeared. These plans are granular, responsive, and adjusted as conditions change.

**Medium horizon (10-150 years):** These are the actual long-term projects by human standards. An example would be gradually embedding a false identity within an institutional structure over decades, establishing credibility and position such that the identity can influence policy in subtle ways. Or positioning themselves within geographical locations that will matter when larger changes occur. Or acquiring specific knowledge or capability that will become relevant only much later.

**Extended horizon (150-600+ years):** These are strategic goals. The plans here are often incompletely specified, containing major decision points that cannot be addressed until the actual circumstances in the extended future become clearer. They often amount to "position myself such that when X becomes possible, I will be ready to act." What X is, and what the action will entail, is often unknown even to the demon who has been positioning toward that goal. The planning is fundamentally about maintaining optionality across centuries.

Demons who have been in Solumora for less than 600 years operate using the first two timeframes primarily. [[Selvane]], [[Mave]], and [[Wren]] are strategically young and are mostly engaged in immediate and medium-horizon planning. They have not yet reached the age where extended-horizon goals become the actual center of focus.

## Planning Methods

High Demons rely on three primary planning tools: **Integration**, **Positioning**, and **Knowledge Development**.

### Integration

Rather than external manipulation, a High Demon plans by becoming embedded within existing structures. They take on an identity — usually forged but sometimes legitimate — and develop actual position within institutions. They become a scrivener, a scholar, a merchant, occasionally a guild administrator. Over years or decades, they build credibility.

The advantage of this approach is that it creates continuity. A demon who has been a particular person in a particular position for 25 years has genuine institutional relationships. They understand the institution from the inside. They can recognize opportunities and constraints that are invisible to outside observers.

The consolidation of genuine position and genuine relationships means that the demon can influence institutional outcomes without it appearing coordinated. When they argue for a policy change in a council meeting, they are arguing based on demonstrated expertise and history. When they recommend a particular contract, they are making a choice that seems normal. No manipulation is required because the position itself creates influence.

### Positioning

A High Demon maintains multiple locations of residence or operation simultaneously, often separated geographically and functionally. One identity might be embedded in an [[Auralis]] institution. Another might operate as an independent scholar in [[Terravelle]]. A third might maintain a comfortable withdrawal location in a remote settlement.

This is not operational redundancy in the common sense. It is strategic redundancy. If one position becomes compromised or untenable, the demon has already established others. But more importantly, maintaining multiple positions across different regions allows them to observe patterns that single-location observers cannot see. They become aware of institutional coordination, political shifts, and large-scale patterns much earlier than they would if embedded in a single location.

The oldest demons have moved through dozens of positions over centuries. Each position teaches them something about how human institutions function, about what constraints shape behavior, about what possibilities remain overlooked. The positioning strategy is not about current influence, though influence may result. It is about continuous learning across geological timescales of human activity.

### Knowledge Development

A High Demon with centuries available will develop expertise in specific domains that no human institution can match simply through persistence and repetition. An example would be a demon who has spent 300 years focused on understanding how economic systems function. They have watched multiple kingdoms, multiple trade crises, multiple shifts in commodity availability and demand. They have participated as a merchant or economist in multiple of these instances.

What they understand as a result is not merely deeper but structurally different from institutional knowledge. An institution has turnover. People die. Documents are lost. New administrators re-learn patterns that previous ones understood. But an individual demon with continuity across centuries develops understanding that includes the full history directly, without institutional filtering.

This knowledge development often has no immediate application. A High Demon might spend a century developing expertise in siege engineering not because they intend to conduct a siege, but because understanding how siege systems function and fail is interesting and potentially useful. The knowledge sits, accumulated, until circumstances make it relevant.

## Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

The primary challenge High Demons face in planning is that their goals often span timeframes so long that major environmental factors are unpredictable. What will kingdoms look like in 300 years? Will [[Flux]] user density increase, decrease, or stabilize? Will new institutions emerge? Will the political relationship between Terravelle and Auralis remain adversarial?

High Demons address this through **adaptive framing** — planning goals in ways that are insensitive to specific outcomes but robust to general categories of future. Rather than planning "I will create X specific outcome in 300 years," they plan "I will position myself such that I can exploit opportunities in any of the following scenarios..." The scenarios are usually something like:

- Continued stability and institutional consolidation
- Significant disruption and institutional collapse
- Major resource crisis or environmental shift
- Flux user population increase or decrease
- Political integration or separation between kingdoms

A demon designs their 150-year medium plans such that regardless of which scenario materializes, they will be in position to act effectively. This requires maintaining flexibility and avoiding commitments that are sensitive to specific outcomes.

## Relationship to Institutional Planning

The five High Demons embedded in Solumora do not compete directly with kingdom institutions for long-term influence. Their goals are orthogonal. A kingdom's 100-year strategic plan aims to achieve specific political, military, or economic outcomes. A High Demon's 100-year project is usually aimed at achieving position, knowledge, or optionality that serves some extended-horizon goal.

This orthogonality means that High Demon planning and institutional planning have low intersection. Occasionally they coincide — a demon might advocate for a policy choice that also serves institutional interest — but this is coincidence rather than coordination. The demon is not attempting to manipulate the institution toward shared goals. They are using their position within the institution to pursue independent objectives. When those coincide with institutional interests, it appears normal. When they diverge, the demon is careful enough that divergence is not visible.

The oldest demons have learned that institution-shaping power is more efficiently achieved by allowing institutions to pursue their own interests while positioning oneself to benefit from predictable institutional behavior, rather than attempting to manipulate institutions directly. This has nothing to do with ethics and everything to do with cost-benefit analysis. Direct manipulation creates risks that vigilant observation and strategic positioning do not.

## Planning Failure Modes

High Demon plans fail in two primary ways: **Assumption Invalidation** and **Visibility Threshold**.

**Assumption Invalidation** occurs when a core assumption about how human institutions or the world operates turns out to be wrong over the course of centuries. A demon might have planned to use a specific institution as leverage in the extended future, but the institution transforms or collapses before the plan matures. Or they might have assumed a particular geographical location would remain stable and valuable, but resource discovery or political upheaval makes it irrelevant.

This is not fatal. High Demons who have lived for centuries have experienced assumption invalidation before. The response is to not treat individual plans as precious. When an assumption fails, the specific plan is released and the underlying goal is re-approached from the new actual circumstances. This flexibility is a luxury of long lifespans.

**Visibility Threshold** is more serious. If a High Demon's activities cross from "seemingly normal human behavior" into "demonstrably anomalous," investigation becomes likely. A demon who makes too many precise predictions, or who shows too perfect awareness of information they should not have, or who takes actions that require knowledge of future events, risks becoming visible as something other than human. This is a true failure mode because visibility invokes the historical response.

Sophisticated High Demons spend moderate effort continuously checking whether their behavior remains within the visibility threshold. They make small errors intentionally. They use [[Flux]] in ways that appear consistent with human casting. They maintain explanations for how they came by information. The goal is to remain legible as an unusually capable but essentially human agent.
