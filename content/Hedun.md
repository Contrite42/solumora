**Hedun** is a mid-sized port city on [[Auralis|Auralis's]] western coast, several days' travel south of [[Solhaven]] and well removed from both the capital's political gravity and [[Emberfall|Emberfall's]] academic seriousness. It has a population somewhere above two hundred thousand, a commercial economy built heavily on hospitality and maritime trade, and a reputation in respectable circles that ranges from fond to concerned depending on how closely those circles pay attention.

It is, among its other qualities, the city that [[Fennick]] has called home for the better part of forty years.

## Character

Hedun does not enforce [[Auralis|Auralis's]] soft [[Control Tier]] hierarchy with the same consistency as [[Solhaven]]. The city's economics are too horizontal for it — entertainment and hospitality depend on volume, and a business model that discourages lower-tier clients in favor of prestige guests is not viable when your competitor across the square has no such policy. The result, over generations, is a city that has developed a practical culture of tier indifference in public spaces that coexists awkwardly with the official Auralis social framework.

This does not make Hedun egalitarian. Money and charm still move things. The difference is that the thing money and charm move is access to experiences rather than social standing, which is a different economy with different winners.

The city's architecture is lower and wider than [[Solhaven]] — not the vertical ambition of the coast's prestige cities but the dense, layered sprawl of a place that has been adding onto itself for two centuries. The harbor district is the commercial core. Above it, the entertainment quarter runs for several miles along the bluffs, facing west toward the ocean. Evenings in this part of the city are, depending on who you ask, either what [[Auralis]] should aspire to or what it should be embarrassed by.

## What It Trades In

Hedun's primary industries are hospitality, maritime commerce, and the particular commerce of situations that people want handled at a remove from their home city. The city has a significant population of working [[Flux Users|Formulists]] offering services that require no questions and create no documentation trail, a financial services district known for the discretion of its Scribe-tier transaction work, and several establishments that have been functioning under the same name and general premise for longer than their current owners have been alive.

None of this is illegal by Auralis law in any precise sense. The legal framework of a kingdom organized around ambition and [[Flux]] mastery does not have strong opinions about what people do with their evenings, provided they do not damage property or injure the formally registered. Hedun has stayed well inside this tolerance for a long time and has developed institutional knowledge about exactly where the line is.

## Fennick's Position

[[Fennick]] has maintained an identity in Hedun — a name, a residence, a set of social connections that are reset periodically as human memories and human lives cycle through — for four decades. The city suits him in the ways that matter most: confidence reads as charm rather than suspicion here, unusual appearances are processed as personality, and the social mixing means he has access to a genuine cross-section of Auralis life in a single evening's work.

His current cover involves the role of a well-traveled, independently wealthy man of obscure previous occupation who has apparently always been in Hedun whenever anyone checks. He has been this person for eleven years. He expects to be this person for another decade before the edges begin to show.
