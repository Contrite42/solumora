[[Solumora]] is a single vast supercontinent, the only known landmass in the world. It runs roughly north to south, wider at its middle than at either end, with coastlines on all sides. No significant islands have been mapped. No land has been found beyond the horizon in any direction that expeditions have reached.

The continent is divided into three broad zones by climate and geography:

## The Northern Reaches
The continent rises as you move north. Coming out of the desert's low elevation, the terrain climbs steadily through [[Terravelle]]'s settled belt and does not stop. The north is defined by dry, rocky upland — not dramatic alpine peaks in the middle latitudes, but a slow, persistent rise in elevation that makes the air thinner and the winters longer the further north you travel. By the time the settled belt gives way to the far north, the landscape is open mountain country: sparse vegetation, constant wind, cold that arrives early and leaves late.

[[Wolfpoint]], at the extreme end of what most people consider reachable, is built into the rock of a northern mountain formation — high, dramatic, and difficult to approach in any season. The terrain between [[Greyveil]] and Wolfpoint is the hardest travel in the kingdom.

[[Terravelle]] occupies the northern settled belt — the band of temperate, workable land that sits several hundred miles north of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]. The terrain here is varied: river valleys, grassland, coastal cliffs, and forested upland. It is productive landscape, which in Terravelle is considered the same thing as beautiful. The wide valleys of the interior feel open and horizontal — vast sky, long sight lines — in a way that strikes travelers from the south as almost alien after the condensed urban density of [[Auralis]].

## Desert Zakros
See [[Equatorial Desert]].

The desert runs the full width of the continent coast to coast. It is not narrow. The fastest known crossing route takes several weeks under favorable conditions. There is no way around it.

## The Southern Reaches
South of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] the climate inverts rapidly and the elevation drops. The desert's southern edge gives way to dry scrubland that descends into the low basin where [[Auralis]] has built its civilization. The interior of southern Solumora sits at or near sea level in places — some of it below — and the combination of low elevation, warmth, and managed water has produced a landscape dense with agriculture and permanent settlement. [[Auralis]] has used [[Flux]] practice extensively to engineer its terrain: drainage, land stabilization, the kind of large-scale environmental work that allows an urban civilization to exist at the density it does. The result is warm, humid, heavily settled, and deeply unlike anything north of the desert.

The far south, beyond Auralis's settled territory, becomes increasingly dense and difficult. Expeditions have gone further south than the kingdom's current border and returned with accounts of deep forest, extreme humidity, and terrain that resists organized travel. Auralis funds occasional exploratory expeditions in this direction as part of its general expansion policy, but the south has not yet yielded anything as interesting as the desert ruins.

## Coastlines
Both kingdoms have significant coastlines. [[Terravelle]]'s coasts are primarily on the west, with a long eastern coastline that has historically been the site of territorial friction — the expansion campaign in which Eddan's elder son died was in the eastern coastal region. [[Auralis]] has both eastern and western coasts, and its port cities are among its most economically active.

The ocean has been sailed but not thoroughly mapped. There are no confirmed accounts of land beyond the horizon. This is not considered evidence that no such land exists.
