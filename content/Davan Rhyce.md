_(Council Member, Auralis — Expansion Faction)_

**Davan Rhyce** has been mapping [[Terravelle|Terravelle's]] eastern vulnerabilities for eleven years. He has not shared most of what he has found with the rest of the Expansion Faction, because [[Castor Helme]] is loud and [[Yara Venn]] is imprecise and the work he is doing requires neither quality in a collaborator. He does his planning privately and presents conclusions when they are ready. He considers himself close to something actionable.

## Character

Davan is forty-three, careful, and operates at a different speed than most of the Expansion Faction around him. Where Castor is theatrical and Yara is impatient, Davan is simply deliberate — not because he is cautious by nature, but because he has spent enough time thinking about what successful expansion actually requires to know that the people calling for speed have not done the same work.

He is also aware that his patience reads, in a faction oriented around ambition and forward movement, as a kind of drag. He does not change his pace to accommodate this perception. He considers the gap between his timeline and the faction's impatience to be the gap between people who understand the problem and people who want the problem solved.

His [[Flux]] ability is T4, with a Sense-oriented application — he has developed his detection work to a degree that makes him excellent at reading terrain, identifying sigil infrastructure, and gauging the Flux density characteristics of locations at a distance. These skills have practical applications in what he considers the most interesting current question: the eastern coastal territory that [[Terravelle]] claims but does not fully control.

## The Eastern Work

[[Terravelle|Eddan's]] eastern annexation campaign — the one in which his elder son died — absorbed coastal territory that was never fully integrated. The communities there operate in a grey zone: technically Terravellian, economically ambivalent, and politically underrepresented in [[Valdenmoor]]. Davan has been cultivating relationships in those communities through commercial intermediaries for eight years.

What he has found: several significant port towns whose primary trade relationships are with [[Auralis]] shipping routes, organized through [[Orla Fest|Orla Fest's]] network, and whose administrative loyalty to Terravelle is transactional rather than genuine. The calculation he is close to completing is whether those relationships could be formalized in a way that converts them from grey-zone commercial arrangements into something with territorial implications.

He has discussed none of this with Castor. He has discussed the general direction with [[Brennan Solce]], who knows the border geography better than anyone, and has noted that Brennan's reaction was careful rather than dismissive.
