_(Desert Expedition Leader)_

**Rett Soln** has led six crossings of [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]] under Auralis commission, and five of them returned. He is thirty-eight years old, T5, and has spent more time in the desert interior than anyone currently alive who is also employed to go back there. He stages out of [[Halveth]] during expedition season and spends his off-months in [[Emberfall]] at the Reach Academy, where the fragments he recovers are studied by people he does not supervise and does not ask questions of.

## Character

Rett is the product of desert crossing, which is a specific kind of education. It teaches you to assess things accurately or stop being alive, and after six crossings the assessment has become automatic — he enters a room and exits with a functional picture of what it contains and what it costs. He applies this to terrain, to weather, to the Flux anomaly readings near the ruin sites, and to people.

He is not warm and not cold. He is calibrated. He does not trust people who have not demonstrated themselves under conditions that matter, and he does not hold this against people who have not yet had the opportunity — he simply does not rely on them yet. The regulars he brings when they are available have demonstrated themselves. New specialists hired for specific crossings know going in that they are not yet trusted, and the ones who take this as an insult instead of a reasonable professional standard are the ones he screens out before departure.

His discipline is Heat — the practical choice for desert work. Thermal regulation at T5 means the crossing conditions that kill unprepared travelers are, for Rett, managed variables rather than survival crises. He is not casual about them. He is experienced.

## The One That Didn't Return

The crossing that did not return was his second. He was twenty-five and led a six-person team into the central desert toward one of the anomalous sites. The route they had planned was accurate. The event that stopped them was not a route problem. The detail of what happened — an unexpected rift opening very close to a campsite, the speed of the emergence, the decision he made about who stayed and who ran — is something he has reviewed often enough that it no longer produces specific feelings, only the flat awareness of which decisions were correct and which were not.

He did not lead his third crossing until three months after the second. He considered not leading a third crossing at all. He concluded that the calculation was still positive, which was not a comfortable conclusion to reach and is not a comfortable thing to hold, and has led a crossing every year since.

## Halveth and Wren

Rett has a functional relationship with [[Wren]], built on the recognition that she knows things about the [[The Northern Narrows|Northern Narrows corridors]] that he cannot know from crossing records alone. He speaks to her before every expedition and takes her assessments seriously, which she reciprocates by being straightforwardly useful rather than cryptically advisory. He is aware that she knows more about the rift activity near the anomalous sites than he does. He is aware that she is not going to share all of it. He works with what she provides and does not push.

He does not know what she is. He has noticed that she has not aged in ten years of their acquaintance in the way that a person his mother's age should age, and has filed this in the section of his mind where he puts things he cannot act on and does not need to understand to work with.
