# Vera Kolt

Vera Kolt is a senior researcher at the [[Crestward Flux Institute]], approximately 50 years old, [[Control Tier|T5]], and specializes in cost reduction through parallel execution patterns.

## Current Project: Distributed Load Casting

Vera's primary research focuses on understanding how a team of low-tier practitioners ([[Control Tier|T1-T2]]) can execute effects that would normally require a single higher-tier practitioner. The core insight driving her work is that spell cost is not linear — some effects can be decomposed into parallel sub-effects, each cheaper to execute individually than the unified spell would be.

Her model uses a library metaphor: a large grimoire can be split across many readers, each holding portions. When the entire library's contents are needed simultaneously, the readers coordinate to assemble meaning. Applied to Flux, this means a complex spell can sometimes be split across a team in ways that reduce total Watts required while increasing coordination complexity.

This research is strategically valuable to [[Auralis]] because it would allow the council to build larger magical capacity from its existing pool of T1-T2 practitioners without requiring development of higher-tier casters. It is also commercially valuable because it would make Flux-based systems viable for institutions and merchant houses operating with limited tier-4 staff.

## Constraints

The research has not yet produced generalizable methods. Vera has identified three spell categories where distributed execution works (certain filtering and sensing effects, some protective barriers, and a subset of binding operations), but the patterns that define which spells can be decomposed and how remain inconsistent. She is searching for underlying structural principles that might explain when decomposition is possible and when it is not.

The work generates political friction within Crestward because her results threaten the established models that justify continued funding for individual high-tier practitioner training. If all effects can eventually be decomposed across teams, the institutional value of developing T5+ casters becomes questionable.
