**Bren Ossve** is sixty-one years old and lives on the land where she was born, in the upland farming country between [[Greyveil]] and the northern range.

She tested [[Control Tier|T7]] at fourteen. The regional guild sent assessors three more times over the following decade, as if expecting the number to have been an error or expecting her to have changed her mind about the farm. She cooperated with each assessment. She did not change her mind.

What she does with the T7 is mostly healing. The surrounding settlements within a day's travel know where she lives and what she can do, and people come when they have something that the standard practitioners cannot resolve. She handles these cases with the same approach she brings to the farm work: what does the situation require, what of what she has will address it, and no more than that. The [[Covenant of Measure]] would provide a framework for this way of working if she used it as a framework, which she does not; she uses it the way most rural Terravelle people use it, as background water, the way the things you were raised with become the way things are.

In forty years of practice she has had one case that required her full range. The patient was a young man from the eastern holdings with a structural collapse in the Flux pathways that the formal medical chapter in Greyveil had assessed as unresolvable. She spent three days and told no one afterward exactly what the three days involved. The young man recovered. He is now forty and farms with his family and she sees him twice a year at the seasonal market.

The guild offered her a teaching position in Greyveil when she was thirty-two. She declined. They have not asked again.

Living with T7 in upland farming country means living with a gap between what is possible and what is necessary, and finding the gap unremarkable. Most of what a farm requires does not need a T7 to accomplish. The soil work, the weather reading, the small daily maintenance of structures and animals — she uses what the situation requires, which is usually within T2 range. The higher capacity sits available most of the time. She does not experience this as waste. It is more like having a sense for structural collapse and spending most of your life in buildings that don't collapse. The capacity is what it is. The work is what it is. They do not have to match.

The pressure from outside has not stopped, but it has changed form over the decades. Early on it arrived as reassessment requests — the regional guild convinced there must be an error, or convinced she would reconsider if the number was confirmed by a different examiner. After the fourth visit that approach ended. Since then it has been more diffuse: a practitioner from Greyveil who wants to observe her technique, always politely asked and politely declined; a delegation from a Flux medical chapter two cities south looking for a consulting relationship; letters from Valdenmoor whose nominal subject is certification renewal but whose structure invites conversations she has no interest in having. She responds to all of these. She is not hostile in her responses. The people receiving them understand clearly that she is not going to change her position, and most of them, eventually, stop writing.

The [[Covenant of Measure]] comes up in some of these exchanges and she uses it in that capacity — it gives the other party something to engage with rather than a closed door. She does not experience herself as operating within the Covenant. She operates within her own understanding of what the work requires, which predates the Covenant's language in her life and would exist without it.

The settlements within a day's travel have had forty years to establish a working relationship with what she is and what she will do. There are things they ask for — the cases the local practitioners cannot resolve, the occasional diagnostic that requires range a T2 can't provide, the structural assessments after a bad storm. There are things they don't ask for, because the community knowledge of her limits is old knowledge now. The people who remember the early years of her practice remember when that knowledge was being established, before anyone understood her well enough to know where the edges were. Their children inherited the understanding already complete.

There are things she has learned from forty years of diagnostic work that she has not written down. Case knowledge — specific Flux pathologies she has encountered, the resolutions that worked, the patterns she has seen repeat and how they resolve differently the second time. A practitioner studying healing who had access to forty years of her case records would be substantially more capable than one who didn't. She has considered this. She has not changed her practice. Her reasons are not entirely about the guild or the institutional arrangements or the people who would use the records in ways she finds distasteful. There is something else in it that she has not resolved and has stopped expecting to resolve.

She is sixty-one. T7 range includes applications that touch on physical decline — not reversal, nothing that contradicts the basic biology, but management, the extension of function past the point where ordinary years would have reduced it. She knows this and has not used it. This is a choice made by not choosing, the way that deferring something year after year becomes a position without a moment of decision. She is not opposed to it in principle. She is not certain what she would be doing it for. The farm runs. The cases come. The young man from the eastern holdings is at the seasonal market with his family. She is not sure what she is preserving function for that requires it to be extended, and she is not certain that is the right question to be asking.
