**Valdenmoor** is the capital of [[Terravelle]] and the seat of King Eddan's rule. It sits in the broad river valley of the Maren River, roughly central in Terravelle's settled belt, far enough from the [[Equatorial Desert]] to be temperate and close enough to the kingdom's geographic heart to function as its administrative center.

It is not a beautiful city. It is an extraordinarily functional one.

## Layout and Architecture

Valdenmoor follows the Terravellian architectural instinct without compromise — the city spreads outward rather than upward, a sprawl of single and double-story buildings connected by wide roads designed to move goods efficiently. The oldest districts near the river center have narrow streets and dense construction from an era before the current civic philosophy fully settled in, but everything built in the last century reflects the doctrine of horizontal expansion.

The palace is larger than it is tall. The administrative districts are organized by function rather than prestige. The market quarter runs along the river and has done so for as long as anyone has kept records.

## Population and Character

Valdenmoor is the largest city in [[Terravelle]] and one of the largest in [[Solumora]], with a population somewhere above half a million. It draws from every region of the kingdom — merchants, [[Flux Users|Scribes]], craftspeople, [[Flux Users|Formulists]] with guild contracts, and the permanent administrative class that keeps a kingdom running regardless of who sits on the throne.

The city has a particular culture around [[Flux]] use that is more pronounced than the rest of Terravelle — not hostile to high-tier casting, but genuinely indifferent to displays of it. A [[Flux Users|Channeler]] in Valdenmoor is not celebrated. They are noticed, assessed for usefulness, and then treated like anyone else who does skilled work. This is considered polite.

## The Maren River

The Maren River runs through the center of the city and has been the reason for Valdenmoor's location since the original settlement. River trade along the Maren connects the capital to the eastern and western coasts, and the river's management — flood control, navigation, distribution of water rights — is one of the oldest functions of the city's administrative apparatus. [[Sigils]] maintaining the river walls and flood infrastructure are some of the oldest continuously maintained spellwork in the kingdom.

## Relevance to Current Events

[[Cael]] grew up in Valdenmoor and spends most of his time here. The parties, the sympathetic [[Flux Users|Formulists]] he questions about channeling cost, the social world he moves through — all of it is Valdenmoor. Eddan has governed from here for decades.

The trade gala where [[Cael]] first saw [[Cassia]] was held in Valdenmoor's formal reception district, in the hall that handles diplomatic functions when the palace itself is considered too pointed a venue.

## Districts

Valdenmoor's internal organization follows the logic of its functions. [[The Dock Wards]] run along the Maren's western bank — the commercial waterfront where river trade arrives, is stored, and moves on. Behind them, [[Guild Quarter]] is the city's institutional and social center, where the trade guilds maintain their halls and run the certification systems that govern most of Valdenmoor's professional life. The residential belt beyond is [[The Outer Wards]] — neighborhoods where most of the city's population lives, organized by the specific trades and long-term community relationships that characterize settled urban life.

## Hinterland and Supply

Valdenmoor's supply base is [[Maren Valley]] — the agricultural and river-transport communities within several days' travel of the capital. The valley's grain and agricultural output reaches the city through the [[Maren Freight Chain]]'s river logistics. Valley communities also provide seasonal labor to the city's dock market, with workers spending low-demand agricultural seasons in Valdenmoor before returning home for planting and harvest.

Dispatch schedules, bonded storage windows, and transfer pricing for river-borne bulk cargo are coordinated in Valdenmoor's administrative offices before volume is pushed east toward [[Ashford]] for export. This is not glamorous work, but it is one of the systems that allows Terravelle to keep trade moving when political conditions at the border are unstable.
