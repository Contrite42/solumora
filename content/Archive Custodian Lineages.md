_(Knowledge Continuity, Institutional Memory Failure)_

**Archive Custodian Lineages** describes the final-generation professional class tasked with preserving technical and legal knowledge as [[The Ancient Civilization]] entered systemic breakdown. Their role matters because modern ruins indicate preservation attempts were deliberate, selective, and only partially successful.

## Role of Custodians

Custodians were not passive librarians. They handled:

- Priority ranking of records under shrinking storage capacity
- Encryption and symbolic abstraction for long-term survival
- Multi-site redundancy planning across archive nodes
- Controlled destruction of records judged destabilizing if misunderstood

This means modern gaps in knowledge may reflect deliberate omission, not accidental loss.

## Failure Mode

Lineage records suggest two collapse pathways:

- Physical loss of archive nodes during instability events
- Semantic loss where surviving records remained undecipherable without oral instruction chains

By the time early human polities formed, most oral scaffolding was gone. Archives survived as artifacts without operating context.

## Strategic Consequence

Modern institutions repeating final-generation mistakes now prioritize stockpiling over interpretation. Without trained custodial traditions, recovered records can increase panic or be weaponized politically.

For the current arc, this reinforces why evidence-holders like [[Osten Vess]] and coordinators like [[Venn Drost]] matter: preservation without trusted interpretation can destabilize governance as much as suppression does.
