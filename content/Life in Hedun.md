[[Hedun]] operates on the understanding that people want things they cannot get at home and will travel to get them. Everything else about the city follows from this. The maritime commerce, the entertainment quarter, the particular culture of not asking what someone does or where they are from — all of it is downstream of a simple economic insight that the city has been executing on for two centuries: be the place people come to when they do not want to be where they usually are.

This makes Hedun good at several things and structurally disinterested in several others. It is very good at providing experiences. It is structurally disinterested in the kind of social coherence that most cities consider foundational. Most Hedun residents have learned to find this freeing rather than unsettling, because the alternative is to find it unsettling, and Hedun is not particularly sympathetic to that response.

## The Harbor and the Bluff

The city has two distinct economies that operate in parallel without much friction. The harbor handles the maritime trade — cargo in and out, boat maintenance, fish markets, the commercial apparatus of a significant port. The bluff district, running along the elevation above the harbor, handles everything else.

The harbor is a normal working port. It is rougher and louder than [[Valdenmoor]]'s river districts and cleaner and more commercial than the unstructured fishing communities further up the coast. People who work the harbor in Hedun work hard, earn adequately, and spend their leisure time in the bluff district on the days they have money and in the cheaper establishments closer to the water on the days they do not. The harbor's permanent workforce is small relative to the traffic it processes — Hedun supplements with transient maritime labor, workers who come in with the ships and leave with them.

The bluff district is what Hedun is actually known for. It runs several miles along the coastal elevation, facing west toward the ocean, and in the late afternoon when the light hits it directly it is genuinely beautiful in the way of places that are also professionally beautiful — places that understand their own effect and have organized themselves around it. The establishments that line the bluff range from simple to elaborate. All of them are in the business of providing an evening, or a week, or however long you have, that was better than the one that came before.

## Tier on the Bluff

[[Auralis]] operates a soft hierarchy tied to [[Control Tier]], but Hedun's implementation of this hierarchy is the loosest in the kingdom. The economics of hospitality do not accommodate strict tier segregation — you cannot fill an establishment that only admits T4 and above in a city of two hundred thousand where T4 is rare. What the bluff district has instead is price-based differentiation that happens to correlate with tier in some establishments and deliberately does not correlate in others.

The most expensive venues are the ones that attract high-tier casters and wealthy merchants — not because low-tier people are excluded but because the price excludes them. The mid-range venues are genuinely mixed, in practice if not in stated policy. The cheaper establishments near the harbor end of the bluff are where the day laborers, the ship workers, and the harbor district residents drink, and they are T0-heavy and not subtle about it.

Sitting across from a T1 dockworker at a bluff-district table and having a conversation that neither party considers odd is unremarkable in Hedun. In [[Solhaven]] the same event would require explanation. Hedun has not solved the [[Auralis]] caste problem — it has simply made it less operationally relevant in the specific context of people having a good time, which is not nothing.

## The Economy of Discretion

Hedun has a significant business in handling situations that other cities cannot or will not. Transaction work that requires no documentation, disputes that need resolution without formal legal record, services that are technically legal under [[Auralis]] law's significant grey areas, and occasionally services that are not quite legal but are understood to exist and to have their own professional standards.

None of this is hidden. The Formulists who do no-documentation transaction facilitation work out of offices with signs on them. The establishments that offer extended privacy for extended stays advertise in the same publications as the restaurants. The people who know how to make certain things happen without the things being formally traceable are known quantities in the city's social infrastructure, and can be found by asking in the right direction.

This economy works because Hedun has maintained a consistent institutional understanding with [[Auralis]]'s administrative apparatus: we stay well inside the line of what causes formal problems, and you stay well outside the line of examining us closely. The arrangement has held for decades. The city has developed significant expertise in where the line actually is, which is a skill that has to be practiced continuously to be maintained.

[[Fennick]] has found the arrangement congenial. His own cover — a well-traveled man of obscure previous occupation who has apparently always been in Hedun — is exactly the kind of person the city is structured to accommodate without question.

## What People Do Here

A full-time Hedun resident with a stable position is most likely working in hospitality, maritime trade, or one of the service industries adjacent to both. The work is seasonal in character even for permanent staff — the city breathes in and out with the flow of visitors, and the months of heavy traffic require extended hours and the months of low traffic allow recovery.

The people who work the bluff district long-term develop a particular professional calm. They have seen the range of what people want when they are away from their normal lives and do not have to perform normality, and it does not surprise them. The ability to be present, pleasant, and unreadable is a genuine skill that the best bluff workers have and that is worth significant [[Watts]] to the establishments that employ them.

## What Hedun Produces

Children who grow up in Hedun grow up knowing how to read a room and how to manage expectations that are not going to be met. They grow up understanding that the same person can be someone different in different contexts, because they have watched this all their lives. They grow up knowing which questions to ask and which to not ask, and the difference between not asking because you do not care and not asking because you already know.

This is useful knowledge. It is also, in certain configurations, isolating. Hedun produces people who are socially capable in ways that other cities do not match, and who occasionally find, when they leave, that the directness of [[Valdenmoor]] or the transparent hierarchy of [[Solhaven]] feels strange and slightly too simple.

Most of them come back.
