**Fifteen Sigils for the Young Practitioner** is a T1 teaching text published by the [[Valdenmoor]] [[Trade Guilds]] as a standardized introduction to [[Flux]] casting. The text presents fifteen fundamental spells in pedagogical sequence, beginning with safety-oriented effects and progressing through utility applications to more refined techniques. The copy examined here contains extensive handwritten annotations from an experienced instructor.

The guild committee that produced this text prioritized safety and practical application over theoretical completeness. Each spell was selected for its reliability, clear failure modes, and relevance to common trade applications. The progression assumes no prior casting experience and builds complexity gradually through the sequence.

The annotated copy shows evidence of years of classroom use. The instructor's notes reference specific students, common mistakes, and practical teaching insights that supplement the formal text. One student — referred to variously as "the Massen boy" and later simply as "Massen" — appears repeatedly in the annotations, suggesting either exceptional difficulty or exceptional promise.

## Teaching Sequence

**1. Warmstone** — Transfers heat into objects for comfort and utility.
_Margin note: Perfect starter spell. Safe failure mode, immediate feedback, impossible to hurt yourself with. Still gets the Massen boy excited every time._

**2. Glowmark** — Creates persistent light markers on surfaces.
_Margin note: Watch for overcharging — new students always try to make it brighter than necessary. Teach restraint early._

**3. Hearthlight** — Produces combined warmth and illumination.
_Margin note: First multi-output spell. Explain why it costs more than Warmstone + Glowmark separately. The girl from the textile district grasped this immediately._

**4. Dryseal** — Removes moisture from surfaces.
_Margin note: Practical applications obvious to everyone. Good confidence builder. Massen used this to dry his boots every morning for a month._

**5. Heatcheck** — Provides temperature readings through contact.
_Margin note: Essential safety skill. Emphasize that this prevents burns, not causes them. Some students need this clarified._

**6. Inkmark** — Creates permanent marks for identification and pairing.
_Margin note: Binding discipline introduction. Explain permanence carefully — several students have marked things they shouldn't have._

**7. Breathclean** — Filters air for improved breathing conditions.
_Margin note: Popular with workshop students. Chemical discipline basics. The Massen boy asked if it works on cooking smoke. It does._

**8. Nudge** — Applies gentle kinetic force to small objects.
_Margin note: Force discipline introduction. Stress "gentle" — overenthusiastic students have broken things._

**9. Copymark** — Duplicates surface impressions between objects.
_Margin note: Advanced Binding work. Requires steady hands and patience. Not suitable for all students._

**10. Chargecheck** — Detects electrical charge in objects and systems.
_Margin note: Electric discipline introduction. Essential for anyone working with mechanisms. Dry explanation in text — demonstrate with workshop equipment._

**11. Dustsweep** — Removes accumulated particles from surfaces.
_Margin note: Combination of Force and Chemical principles. Good preparation for multi-discipline thinking._

**12. Shove** — Produces directed kinetic bursts for utility applications.
_Margin note: Stronger Force work. Some students struggle with control. Practice on soft targets first._

**13. Slowfall** — Reduces falling velocity through sustained upward force.
_Margin note: Sustained persistence introduction. Expensive to maintain — teach resource management alongside technique._

**14. Lift** — Levitates objects through continuous kinetic application.
_Margin note: Most complex Force application in sequence. Requires concentration and steady [[Flux]] output. The textile girl mastered this in two days._

**15. Marksense** — Locates marked objects within detection range.
_Margin note: Advanced Binding + detection combination. Capstone spell for the sequence. If they can do this cleanly, they're ready for journeyman assessment._

_Additional instructor note at bottom of page: Massen completed the full sequence in eight months. Recommended for advanced placement. The textile district girl finished in five months but declined guild apprenticeship — family obligations. Exceptional natural ability._
