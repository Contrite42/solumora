

## Field Cost Recollections

The assessment near Halveth three years back required T4-range casting in desert conditions. 400-600W sustained for the barrier work. What that cost the team physically: Meren couldn't hold a sigil steady for two days after. Coss needed assistance walking back to the staging point. I managed the casting but felt it for a week. Desert amplifies everything - the heat, the Flux resistance, the recovery time. Young practitioners don't know what 1,500W feels like until they've done it. Not as complaint. Just observation about what you can't know until you've carried it.

The T3 practitioners I see in Hedun now work clean, efficient. Good technique. But they cap out around 300W and think that's the ceiling. In the field, under pressure, with lives depending on precision, you learn what your actual limits are. Usually higher than you expected. Always more expensive than you planned for.
