**Ossal Meln's Workshop Notes** document a development project undertaken by [[Ossal Meln]], a T3 [[Flux]]-embedded objects craftsperson in [[Valdenmoor]]. The project attempted to combine thermal regulation and light emission in a single embedded object — a technical challenge that required solving the fundamental problem of multi-discipline [[Sigils]] interaction.

The notes span twelve days of systematic experimentation, recorded in Meln's characteristic technical style. Each entry documents specific configurations, cost calculations, and test results without editorial commentary. The progression shows the methodical approach typical of experienced embedded-object craftspeople: hypothesis, test, failure analysis, revised approach.

## Development Log

**Day 1**
Initial attempt: combined Heat+Light sigil. Two disciplines, single shape configuration. Triangle base, dual discipline specification.
Result: Casting failure. Cannot apply multiple disciplines to single sigil — fundamental constraint of [[Shape]] system. Each sigil accepts exactly one discipline specification.
Note: Correct principle confirmed. Multi-output requires multi-sigil approach.

**Day 4**
Revised approach: Two separate sigils embedded in adjacent positions. Configuration: Triangle Heat (3W × 2 = 6W) + Triangle Light (3W × 1 = 3W) = 9W total embedded cost.
Test results: Functional activation of both effects. Unexpected [[Flux]] interference between sigils at close proximity. Heat output fluctuates when light sigil activates. Light output dims when heat sigil cycles.
Analysis: Embedded sigils create overlapping Flux fields. Interference pattern destabilizes both effects.

**Day 9**
Spacing solution implemented. Minimum separation distance eliminates interference between embedded sigils. Final working configuration: Triangle Light (3W × 1 = 3W) + Triangle Heat (3W × 2 = 6W) = 9W total. Both sigils T1 individually. Stable operation achieved.
Performance: Clean light output, consistent thermal regulation. No cross-interference detected. Embedded successfully in test container.

**Day 12**
Scaling documentation for larger applications. Pentagon configuration: Pentagon Light (20W × 1 = 20W) + Pentagon Heat (20W × 2 = 40W) = 60W total embedded cost. Both sigils T2 individually. Requires T2 practitioner for embedding process.
Cost comparison: T1 dual-sigil (9W total) suitable for small objects. T2 dual-sigil (60W total) required for larger applications. Spacing requirements scale proportionally with sigil complexity.
