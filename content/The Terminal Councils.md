_(Ancient Governance, End-State Deliberation)_

**The Terminal Councils** were the final documented deliberative bodies of [[The Ancient Civilization Final Generation]], convened when ordinary governance shifted into emergency continuity mode. Fragmentary inscriptions suggest these councils were not ceremonial; they made explicit triage decisions about which populations, systems, and archives could still be protected as instability accelerated.

## Decision Scope

Terminal Council directives appear to have prioritized three objectives:

- Preserve minimal civil continuity in selected corridors
- Protect core knowledge vaults and trained custodians
- Delay cascading failure long enough for controlled relocation

This indicates a governing class that understood collapse risk and chose managed contraction over denial.

## Political Pattern

Council marks imply contested votes between two schools:

- **Containment bloc**: preserve infrastructure and hold territory
- **Transfer bloc**: abandon fixed territory and prioritize mobile survival

The transfer bloc appears to have won late-phase decisions, matching evidence for widespread route restructuring and archive migration.

## Modern Relevance

Current leadership in [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] still frames crisis governance as temporary exception. Terminal Council evidence suggests that once systems cross certain thresholds, emergency governance becomes structural and must be redesigned deliberately or it hardens into permanent coercion.
