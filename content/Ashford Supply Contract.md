# Ashford Supply Contract

The **Ashford Supply Contract** is the standing trade agreement between [[Wolfpoint]] and [[Ashford]]-based suppliers that provides the northern settlement with grain, salt, tools, and other essential goods that Wolfpoint's cold-zone location cannot produce internally. It is not a single contract. It is a portfolio of overlapping agreements, annual negotiations, and sustained relationships with multiple Ashford merchant houses that collectively ensure Wolfpoint's supply continuity.

The Contract system is managed by [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] and represents Wolfpoint's single most important economic dependency: without reliable southern supply access, the settlement cannot survive.

## What Wolfpoint Must Import

Wolfpoint's constant 45-degree cold and wind-exposed terrain create agricultural limitations that trade must compensate for:

**Grain** (wheat, rye, oats) — Wolfpoint cannot grow grain in quantities sufficient for its population. [[Upland Herding Network]] families also depend on imported grain. Total annual requirement: approximately 40,000-50,000 pounds across Wolfpoint's direct population and Network trading relationships.

**Salt** — No local salt source exists. Salt is essential for [[Mountain Game Collective]]'s preservation operations and for food preservation more broadly. Annual requirement: 8,000-10,000 pounds, 60% allocated to Mountain Game Collective, 40% to internal food preservation.

**Replacement tools and metal stock** — While [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] produces specialty metalwork, it does not produce bulk tools (axes, saws, agricultural implements) or raw metal stock for repairs. These must be imported.

**Textiles and finished cloth** — [[Upland Herding Network]] produces wool, but Wolfpoint lacks the capacity to process all textile needs internally. Finished cloth for clothing, blankets, and workshop materials must be imported.

**Specialty goods** — Scribing materials, medicinal compounds, glass, ceramics, and other manufactured goods that Wolfpoint's small population and limited production capacity cannot generate.

Without these imports, Wolfpoint's economy collapses within months. The settlement's export revenue from metalwork, preserved game, and wool textiles exists primarily to fund the import flow that sustains basic survival.

## How the Contract System Works

Multiple Ashford merchant houses maintain standing relationships with [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]], each specializing in different supply categories. The Registry does not rely on a single supplier — diversification prevents any one merchant from having unilateral control over Wolfpoint's supply access.

**Annual negotiation cycle:**
1. **Winter assessment** (late winter) — Registry reviews previous year's supply performance, consumption patterns, and export revenue projections.
2. **Spring contracting** (early spring) — Registry negotiates next year's supply commitments with Ashford merchants: volume guarantees, pricing, delivery schedules, and payment terms.
3. **Execution phase** (spring through autumn) — Supplies travel north through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] according to negotiated schedules.
4. **Settlement and adjustment** (late autumn) — Final payments, dispute resolution, and preliminary discussions for next cycle's contracts.

**Payment structure:**
Wolfpoint pays partially in watts (currency transferred through Ashford banking relationships) and partially through direct commodity exchange (Wolfpoint's export goods delivered south in exchange for specific import allocations). The commodity exchange portion reduces Wolfpoint's currency requirements and gives Ashford merchants direct access to premium cold-forge metalwork and preserved game for their own resale operations.

This hybrid payment model benefits both parties: Wolfpoint preserves limited currency reserves, Ashford merchants gain access to goods that command premium pricing in southern markets.

## Key Supply Relationships

**Grain Contracts:**
Three Ashford merchant houses supply grain through rotating annual contracts. [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] maintains relationships with all three, never committing more than 60% of annual grain requirements to a single supplier. This diversification protects against individual merchant failure, political disruption affecting specific supply chains, or opportunistic price manipulation.

Grain arrives in bulk shipments during spring and summer (maritime conditions most favorable for heavy cargo transport). Wolfpoint maintains underground storage capacity for 6-8 months of grain reserves — enough to buffer against one failed growing season in southern supply regions or extended maritime transport disruptions.

**Salt Contracts:**
Salt comes from coastal extraction operations near [[Hedun]], transported to Ashford, then north through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. [[Morra Keld]] at [[Mountain Game Collective]] communicates directly with [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] about preservation-driven salt needs, and Registry negotiates annual salt contracts 20% above projected requirements to buffer against packet-line delays.

Salt storage is simpler than grain (indefinite shelf life, lower volume) but equally critical. If salt runs out, preservation operations stop immediately and Wolfpoint loses export revenue from its most reliable physical commodity.

**Tool and Metal Stock:**
Multiple Ashford metalworking suppliers provide replacement tools and raw metal stock. These relationships are more flexible than grain/salt contracts — tools degrade on multi-year timelines rather than annual consumption cycles, allowing for opportunistic purchasing when Ashford suppliers offer favorable terms or when [[Wolfpoint Cold-Forge Works]] requires specific alloy stock for commissioned projects.

## Who Manages This

**[[Kess Rolv]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 4]], 46 years old) is [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]]'s Ashford relations coordinator. She manages the Contract system: negotiating with merchant houses, balancing supply diversification against relationship maintenance, tracking payment obligations, and ensuring delivery schedules align with Wolfpoint's seasonal needs.

Kess spends approximately one month per year in Ashford conducting face-to-face contract negotiations. The rest of the year, she manages relationships through correspondence carried by [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] packet ships, responding to price adjustments, delivery delays, and merchant inquiries about expanding supply categories or adjusting terms.

Her constraint is **trust distance**. Ashford merchants are profit-seeking entities operating in competitive southern markets. Wolfpoint is a remote northern settlement with limited options if contracts fail. Kess maintains leverage by ensuring no single merchant controls critical supply categories and by building multi-year relationships with merchants who demonstrate consistent performance.

When merchants violate contract terms (late deliveries, short quantities, quality substitutions), Kess does not terminate relationships immediately. She adjusts future contract allocations, gradually shifting business to more reliable partners. This measured approach prevents sudden supply disruptions while creating long-term accountability pressure.

## Strategic Vulnerabilities

The Ashford Supply Contract system has three structural vulnerabilities:

**Maritime dependence:** All imports travel through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. If packet operations degrade significantly (storm damage to vessels, political disruption of coastal access, or economic failure of packet captains), Wolfpoint's supply continuity faces immediate risk.

**Ashford concentration:** All grain, salt, and tool supplies funnel through Ashford before traveling north. If Ashford itself faces supply constraints (failed harvests affecting grain availability, coastal disruption affecting salt access), Wolfpoint's import flow suffers regardless of how well individual merchant contracts perform.

**Payment capacity:** Wolfpoint must generate sufficient export revenue to fund import costs. If cold-forge metalwork, preserved game, or wool textile markets weaken, Wolfpoint's purchasing power declines and supply contracts become harder to sustain.

[[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] monitors all three vulnerabilities but cannot eliminate them. Wolfpoint's geographic isolation and population scale make supply independence structurally impossible. The settlement survives by managing dependency carefully, not by escaping it.

## Why Ashford Merchants Cooperate

Ashford merchants maintain Wolfpoint supply contracts because:
- **Premium goods access** — Wolfpoint's export products command above-market rates in southern resale.
- **Reliable payment** — Seven generations of consistent contract performance establish Wolfpoint as low-risk trade partner.
- **Strategic positioning** — Wolfpoint relationships provide market intelligence about far-northern conditions, maritime route performance, and [[Hypertext]] development that some merchants value beyond immediate profit margins.

The relationship is mutually beneficial but asymmetric. Ashford merchants have multiple trade partners and can survive Wolfpoint's failure. Wolfpoint has no alternative to Ashford-coordinated southern supply access.

Kess Rolv knows this. She manages contracts with the understanding that Wolfpoint's independence depends on being valuable enough to trade with, not threatening enough to conquer, and reliable enough to trust across multi-year relationships.

She reviews the contract portfolio. She drafts next year's proposals. She confirms the spring shipment schedules. The grain flows north. Wolfpoint continues.
