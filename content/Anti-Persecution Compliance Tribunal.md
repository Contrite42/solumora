# Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal

The Anti-Persecution Compliance Tribunal (APCT) is a bilateral adjudication body established during treaty codification to enforce civil safeguards against persecution-pattern governance.

It was created 171 days after the first casualty and received binding authority under the [[Corridor Stabilization Treaty]] for defined classes of rights-risk violations.

## Mandate

The APCT reviews, adjudicates, and sanctions:
- rumor-based detention proposals,
- identity-screening programs targeting non-human indicators,
- association-chain enforcement that reproduces historical persecution dynamics,
- suppression or falsification of technical evidence in coexistence governance.

Its central design principle: detect mechanism-level replication early, before mass harm scales.

## Jurisdiction

The Tribunal has concurrent jurisdiction over:
- municipal emergency directives in corridor-adjacent regions,
- cross-border compliance disputes tied to treaty safeguards,
- appeals from oversight determinations involving persecution-pattern tests.

Military operational decisions remain outside direct APCT command authority but can trigger mandatory compliance review if civil safeguards are implicated.

## Structure

The APCT uses a mixed bench model:
- 2 Auralis jurists,
- 2 Terravelle jurists,
- 1 rotating historical-mechanisms expert,
- 1 rotating technical-governance expert,
- 1 presiding neutral chair selected by joint civic councils.

Decisions require supermajority for high-impact sanctions.

## Procedural Safeguards

Key procedural rules include:
- rapid filing path for imminent-risk cases (decision target: 48 hours),
- public docket with narrow redaction scope,
- explicit burden on filing authority to show evidence beyond rumor convergence,
- mandatory historical-pattern comparison in written decisions.

Tribunal opinions must identify whether challenged policy maps onto documented patterns from [[The Lantern Decrees]] or [[The Identification Protocols]].

## Remedies and Sanctions

Available remedies:
- injunction against unlawful directive,
- mandatory policy rewrite with compliance monitor,
- restitution orders for unlawful detention harm,
- administrative censure and funding restriction for repeat offenders.

In treaty context, severe non-compliance findings can trigger corridor-operation penalties through coordinated sanction tiers.

## Early Case Pattern

First-quarter APCT docket shows three dominant risk categories:
- municipal panic ordinances drafted with vague threat language,
- covert data-sharing programs intended to build informal identity lists,
- procedural delays used to preserve de facto detention after formal denial.

Early rulings have favored narrow emergency powers, strict evidence standards, and aggressive transparency requirements.

## Strategic Role

The APCT is not designed to eliminate political fear. It is designed to prevent fear from converting into durable coercive machinery.

Its success criterion is procedural: whether potentially persecutory mechanisms are stopped at design stage rather than after casualty stage.
