Sorath is the oldest [[High Demons|High Demon]] currently present in [[Solumora]], somewhere in his eighth century of existence. He arrived before either [[Terravelle]] or [[Auralis]] existed as coherent kingdoms, before the current political geography of the continent had taken its shape, before most of what either kingdom considers ancient history had happened yet. He has watched all of it. He is no longer particularly interested in it.

## Appearance

Sorath presents as a tall man in apparent late middle age — weathered, unhurried, with the particular quality of stillness that comes from having nothing left to prove. His horns are broad and curved, swept back close to the skull, and he keeps them covered without exception: hoods, wide-brimmed hats, wrapped cloth in the desert heat. He has been doing this long enough that the habit is seamless. No one who has met him in the last century has seen his face uncovered in full light.

His eyes are notably pale — not an unusual color exactly, but with a quality that people remember without being able to describe. Several travelers who have encountered him have noted, independently, that looking at him directly feels like trying to look at something that isn't quite where it appears to be.

This is not accidental.

## Discipline

Sorath's spell is Light — not fire, not heat, but the behavior of light itself. At T8 and closing on eight centuries of practice, his application of it has no meaningful ceiling within the discipline. He does not produce dramatic displays. He has not needed to for a very long time. What he does is quieter: he adjusts what is seen, what is remembered seeing, what registers as present or absent in a room. He has walked through crowded cities in full daylight without being observed. He has stood in front of people and been perceived as someone else entirely. He has, on at least three occasions that he is aware of, been present at significant historical events without anyone in attendance knowing he was there.

The concealment of his horns requires, for him, essentially no effort. It is the first thing he learned and the thing he has practiced longest.

## History

Sorath spent his first two centuries embedded in human society, cycling through identities as each one aged past plausibility. He was a scholar in a city that no longer exists, an advisor to a ruler whose dynasty has been forgotten, a merchant in a trade network that collapsed before [[Terravelle]] had a name. He learned what human civilization is, how it works, what it wants, and what it costs.

Somewhere in his fourth or fifth century he stopped. Not dramatically — he did not withdraw suddenly or make a decision he could point to. He simply found that the next identity was less interesting than the last, and the one after that less still, and eventually the effort of maintaining a position in a society he had already seen the shape of stopped being worth what it gave him.

He has been traveling since. Not toward anything. Not investigating. Moving through the world at his own pace, passing through settlements long enough to eat and rest and occasionally exchange a few words with someone who will never know what they were talking to, then moving on.

He is not unhappy. He is not much of anything, by human emotional standards. He has simply run out of things that surprise him, and he is waiting, without urgency, to find something that does.

## Current Situation

Sorath is somewhere on the road. He has no fixed location, no cover identity, no agenda. He passes through [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] territory interchangeably, unremarked. Innkeepers remember a quiet traveler who paid in full and left early. Border guards remember processing someone unremarkable. Nobody remembers the horns.

He is aware that [[Fennick]] exists and finds him exhausting. He is aware that [[Selvane]] and [[Mave]] are both operating in [[Auralis]] and considers their collision inevitable and not his problem. He is aware of [[Wren]] and thinks the village post is either very patient or very naive. He has not sought out any of them. If their paths cross he will be civil. He will not stay long.

The one thing that has come close to interesting him in recent decades is the increased rift activity near the [[Equatorial Desert|desert]] sites. Not the demons coming through — he has seen that before, across multiple periods of elevated activity — but the quality of the Flux anomalies at the [[Ancient Ruins|ruin sites]] themselves. Something about them has changed. He has not investigated. He has noted it, filed it in the long mental record he keeps of things that might eventually matter, and kept walking.

## Current Movement

Sorath has come north from the desert interior and entered [[Hedun]]. This is unusual for Sorath — his pattern for the past several decades has been to avoid the major port cities, preferring smaller settlements and overland routes that require less sustained interaction with concentrated populations. His presence in Hedun suggests an errand that cannot be conducted elsewhere.

From the outside, what Sorath does in Hedun appears routine: someone buying supplies, making a quiet inquiry at a harbor counting house, lingering near the docks without obvious purpose. He moves through the city like someone who has been in worse places — not [[Fennick]]'s smoothness, not [[Selvane]]'s prominence. Simply present. The conversations he has are brief and forgettable. The transactions he conducts are unremarkable. What the errand actually concerns is not visible to anyone Sorath interacts with.

Sorath's presence in the harbor district has the quality of someone passing through rather than settling. He does not establish routines. He does not return to the same establishments repeatedly. He does not seek out social contact beyond what his immediate needs require. This is consistent with his general approach to populated areas — sufficient engagement to accomplish what he came to do, no more than that.

[[Fennick]] and Sorath have not crossed paths. The harbor district is large enough and busy enough that two people can operate in the same general area without encountering each other, particularly when one of them — Sorath — is actively avoiding the kind of social disruption that tends to follow in Fennick's wake. The proximity will not last. Sorath's errands in Hedun are finite. When they are complete, he will leave.

[[Tolla Rend]], the harbor supervisor, has seen Sorath twice — once near the counting house, once walking the main dock approach. She noted something was wrong but cannot name what. The wrongness was not dramatic enough to act on, not specific enough to report. Simply the feeling that the person she observed did not quite fit the context they were moving through. She has filed this in the category of things that are probably nothing but that she will remember if they become relevant later.
