A Grimoire is a bound collection of [[Sigils]] — a reference work that allows any caster of sufficient [[Control Tier]] to access, reproduce, and cast [[Spells|spells]] they did not design themselves. Grimoires are the backbone of distributed [[Flux]] use across [[Solumora]]. Without them, most of the population would have access only to spells they personally developed or memorized.

## How Grimoires Work

Each [[Sigils|sigil]] in a grimoire is drawn with full precision. A caster who meets the [[Control Tier]] requirement for a given spell can activate it directly from the page, or copy it onto another surface for single use. The quality of the grimoire matters — a poorly copied sigil may misfire, underperform, or fail entirely.

[[Flux Users|Scribes]] are responsible for the production of most grimoires in circulation. High-quality Scribe work is commercially valuable and socially respected, particularly in [[Terravelle]].

## Grimoire Tiers

Grimoires are categorized by the rarity of the [[Spells|spells]] they contain, reflecting the [[Control Tier]] required to use them:

| Tier                   | Control Tier | Typical Holder              |
| ---------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| [[Common Grimoire]]    | T1–T2        | Most households             |
| [[Uncommon Grimoire]]  | T3–T4        | Skilled [[Flux Users        | Formulists]]                   |
| [[Rare Grimoire]]      | T5–T6        | [[Flux Users                | Channelers]], restricted       |
| [[Legendary Grimoire]] | T7–T8        | Institutional holdings only |
| [[Mythic Grimoire]]    | T9           | [[Flux Users                | Conduits]], documentation only |
| [[Pale Grimoire]]      | T9           | Restricted scholarly record |

The full catalogue of documented spells across all tiers is maintained in the [[All Grimoire]].

## Cultural Attitudes

In [[Terravelle]], grimoires are treated as practical tools — functional, affordable at the Common tier, and widely distributed. High-tier grimoires exist but are held in institutional libraries rather than private hands.

In [[Auralis]], grimoires are also status objects. The tier of grimoire a household owns signals something about their position in society. [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] and above texts circulate through [[Flux Users|Scholar]] networks, private patronage arrangements, and the occasional very expensive sale.

## The Grimoire Economy

Grimoires are the mechanism by which [[Flux]] capability is distributed broadly rather than confined to high-tier practitioners. An entire economy exists around their production, authentication, distribution, and trade. The [[Grimoire Economy]] page covers the full system.

**Production:** [[Grimoire Copyists]] (Scribes) produce most grimoires in circulation. In [[Terravelle]], copying work is certified through the [[Trade Guilds]]; in [[Auralis]], quality signals run through Scholar-network vouching. [[Grimoire Licensing]] covers originator attribution and what rights actually mean in practice.

**Markets:** The [[Common Grimoire Market]] drives most economic activity — high volume, competitive, accessible to most households. [[High-Tier Grimoire Access]] covers how Rare and above texts actually move: institutional control, patronage channels, private arrangement. [[Grimoire Distribution]] covers the supply chain from Scribe to end user, including traveling merchant circuits and cross-kingdom movement.

**Access and lending:** [[Grimoire Lending]] covers temporary access — institutional library loans, commercial rental, and private lending in rural areas where purchase is not viable. [[Grimoire Commissioning]] covers custom-copy orders: specific spell selections, compiled collections, and cross-border commissions.

**Fraud and underground:** [[Grimoire Fraud]] covers mis-tier presentation, degraded copies, false attribution. [[Black Market Grimoires]] covers what goes underground — out-of-tier texts, restricted disciplines, cross-kingdom access. [[Grimoire Regulation]] covers how both kingdoms handle the trade differently.

**Long-term:** [[Grimoire Inheritance]] covers what a practitioner's library is worth when they die and the complications of settling an estate with originator declarations. [[Grimoire Repair and Preservation]] covers keeping texts functional through heavy use.

**Personal vs. published:** [[Personal Working Grimoire]] covers the distinction between a practitioner's annotated working text and a commercial copy.
