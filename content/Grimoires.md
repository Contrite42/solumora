A Grimoire is a bound collection of [[Sigils]] — a reference work that allows any caster of sufficient [[Control Tier]] to access, reproduce, and cast [[Spells|spells]] they did not design themselves. Grimoires are the backbone of distributed [[Flux]] use across [[Solumora]]. Without them, most of the population would have access only to spells they personally developed or memorized.

## How Grimoires Work

Each [[Sigils|sigil]] in a grimoire is drawn with full precision. A caster who meets the [[Control Tier]] requirement for a given spell can activate it directly from the page, or copy it onto another surface for single use. The quality of the grimoire matters — a poorly copied sigil may misfire, underperform, or fail entirely.

[[Flux Users|Scribes]] are responsible for the production of most grimoires in circulation. High-quality Scribe work is commercially valuable and socially respected, particularly in [[Terravelle]].

## Grimoire Tiers

Grimoires are categorized by the rarity of the [[Spells|spells]] they contain, reflecting the [[Control Tier]] required to use them:

| Tier | Control Tier | Typical Holder |
|---|---|---|
| [[Common Grimoire]] | T1–T2 | Most households |
| [[Uncommon Grimoire]] | T3–T4 | Skilled [[Flux Users\|Formulists]] |
| [[Rare Grimoire]] | T5–T6 | [[Flux Users\|Channelers]], restricted |
| [[Legendary Grimoire]] | T7–T8 | Institutional holdings only |
| [[Mythic Grimoire]] | T9 | [[Flux Users\|Conduits]], documentation only |
| [[Pale Grimoire]] | T9 | Restricted scholarly record |

The full catalogue of documented spells across all tiers is maintained in the [[All Grimoire]].

## Cultural Attitudes

In [[Terravelle]], grimoires are treated as practical tools — functional, affordable at the Common tier, and widely distributed. High-tier grimoires exist but are held in institutional libraries rather than private hands.

In [[Auralis]], grimoires are also status objects. The tier of grimoire a household owns signals something about their position in society. [[Rare Grimoire|Rare]] and above texts circulate through [[Flux Users|Scholar]] networks, private patronage arrangements, and the occasional very expensive sale.

## The Grimoire Economy

Grimoires are valuable, portable knowledge objects, and an entire economy exists around their production, distribution, authentication, and trade. Most [[Flux Users]] depend on grimoires they did not author, which creates markets for copying, lending, commissioning, and repair services. The grimoire economy spans both kingdoms and operates through guild networks, independent practitioners, and underground channels.

### Production and Distribution

The [[Guild Scrivener Network]] handles most commercial grimoire production in both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]. Guild scriveners work from authenticated source texts, produce copies under standardized contracts, and face quality inspections before release. Pricing is structured per-page with tier multipliers, and guild certification limits which scriveners can copy higher-tier texts.

[[Independent Scrivener|Independent scriveners]] operate outside guild infrastructure, serving rural markets, niche buyers, or clients seeking lower prices or faster work. They rely on personal reputation rather than institutional backing and face higher authentication scrutiny but retain more flexibility in what they copy and how they price their work.

### Authentication and Fraud

[[Grimoire Authentication]] is the process of verifying that a grimoire contains accurate sigils copied from trusted sources. The guild network maintains authentication registries and inspects copies before release, but independent and cross-border grimoires are harder to verify. Fraud ranges from careless copying errors to deliberate mis-tier sales, and buyers without [[Flux]] expertise often can't detect defects until spells fail.

[[Black Market Grimoire Circulation]] handles unauthorized copies, restricted texts, and higher-tier grimoires sold outside institutional channels. Most black market activity involves routine copying at below-market prices, but restricted or rare texts also circulate through trusted intermediaries. Authentication is a persistent problem, and buyers have limited recourse if grimoires are defective.

### Access and Rights

[[Grimoire Rights and Restrictions]] govern who may copy, own, and distribute specific grimoires. Institutional libraries control higher-tier texts through conditional copying permissions. Authorial rights protect new spell documentation for a set period, and both kingdoms restrict certain grimoires by law (destructive spells in [[Terravelle]], mind-affecting and politically sensitive texts in [[Auralis]]).

[[Grimoire Lending and Rental]] provides temporary access without permanent ownership. Institutional libraries lend texts to members at low or no cost; commercial rental shops charge by the week and require collateral. Private lending is common in rural areas and relies on social capital. Copying from borrowed texts is legally ambiguous and hard to enforce.

[[Grimoire Commissioning]] is the process of hiring a scrivener to produce a custom copy. Buyers can request direct copies of existing grimoires or custom compilations selecting specific spells. Pricing depends on page count, tier complexity, timeline, and binding quality. Cross-border commissions are possible but add transport costs, customs delays, and authentication complexity.

### Trade and Regulation

[[Cross-Border Grimoire Trade]] moves grimoires between [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]], exploiting each kingdom's production specialties. [[Terravelle]] produces standardized low-tier texts optimized for commerce and agriculture; [[Auralis]] produces experimental and higher-tier research outputs. Cross-border trade is legal but regulated through customs inspections, requires transport via courier networks, and costs significantly more than domestic acquisition.

[[Scrivener Regulation]] enforces quality standards, tier certification, and pricing guidelines within the [[Guild Scrivener Network]]. Guild members face inspection protocols, penalties for poor work, and must maintain documented source text lineage. [[Terravelle]] relies on guild self-regulation; [[Auralis]] adds [[Council of Auralis|Council]] oversight for higher-tier work. Independent scriveners operate outside guild regulation but remain subject to general fraud law.

### Maintenance

[[Grimoire Repair and Preservation]] extends grimoire lifespan through ink restoration, binding repair, and environmental protection. Grimoires degrade from repeated use, and faded sigils lose reliability or fail entirely. Restoration specialists re-ink sigils, replace bindings, and use preservation spells for long-term storage. Higher-tier grimoire restoration requires matching [[Control Tier]] capability and is often institutional work.
