The Ancient Ruins are the scattered surface remnants of a civilization that predates both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] by an unknown but significant margin. They are found throughout [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]], never clustered, never in defensible positions, never with any clear logic to their placement that surviving expeditions have been able to map.

They are the most important unsolved question in [[Solumora]].

## What Has Been Found

Surface ruins only. Broken foundations, partially buried structural elements, fragments of inscription on materials of unknown composition. The buildings, where they can be reconstructed from their remnants, were large and complex — multi-level in places, with internal geometries that suggest sophisticated construction. No complete structure has been found intact. No bodies. No personal effects. No evidence of rapid abandonment or violence.

The inscriptions are the primary object of study. They do not match any known [[Sigils|sigil]] standard. The script appears intentional and systematic — there are recurring elements, clear patterns of repetition, what appear to be hierarchical structures in longer inscriptions — but two centuries of [[Flux Users|Scholar]] effort in [[Emberfall]] and elsewhere have not produced a working translation of more than fragmentary phrases.

What those fragments suggest, to the researchers who have spent careers on them, is a [[Flux]] system of significantly greater complexity than the current one. Not more powerful in the sense of higher [[Control Tier]], but more expressive — capable of encoding interactions and behaviors that the current [[Spell Variables]] framework has no vocabulary for. The sigil geometry recovered from two confirmed ruins sites includes shapes with more outer points than a Circle. What those additional points controlled is unknown. 

What the expedition researchers have not yet recognized is that the most significant artifact the ancient civilization left behind is not in the desert at all. The [[Watts|Watts ledger]] — the distributed Flux network that both kingdoms use as the foundation of their currency — is their work. Inscription fragments at confirmed ruin sites include ledger architecture. The ruins are not the only thing they built that survived.

## What Has Not Been Found

No migration evidence. No record of where the population went. No graves. The absence of bodies is considered by most researchers to be the most significant single fact about the ruins — a civilization this large and this established should have left human remains somewhere in the desert, and it has not.

The leading theories are: rapid departure by unknown means, a catastrophic event that consumed the physical remains, or something that does not fit neatly into either category. The third option is the one that the Culmination Faction within [[The Council of Auralis]] finds most interesting.

## The Three Anomalous Sites

Among the dozens of ruin sites catalogued across the desert, three are categorically different from the rest. They are not simply broken-down structures — they are areas where the [[Flux]] field itself behaves abnormally. Sigils malfunction or produce unexpected effects within these zones. [[Flux Users|Channelers]] report disorientation and difficulty maintaining precision. Ambient Flux readings, where they can be taken, show densities that do not correspond to any known natural or artificial cause.

These three sites are believed to be the locations of Tier 9 [[Control Tier|casting failures]] — places where the ancient civilization attempted something at or beyond the apex of Flux manipulation and the attempt went wrong in ways that have not normalized in the centuries since.

This interpretation is not universally accepted. The alternative — that the sites are not failure sites but success sites, and the anomalous Flux behavior is the *result* of what was accomplished there rather than the aftermath of what went wrong — is a minority position in academic circles and the majority position within [[Drest|Drest's]] faction. What neither interpretation accounts for is the correlation between the anomalous sites and rift formation: all three sites are active rift zones, and the [[Flux Demons]] that emerge through them range outward into inhabited territory. Whether the rifts are a consequence of the original Flux event or a separate phenomenon enabled by the ongoing field distortion is an open question that [[Auralis]] expedition researchers have not yet formally posed, because doing so would require acknowledging the incursion problem in academic documentation.

## Expedition History

[[Auralis]] has funded formal expeditions to the ruins for approximately eighty years. [[Terravelle]] has funded two, both early in the period and neither returning much of value. Independent expeditions exist but are rare — the desert is dangerous enough that survival requires resources that most independent scholars cannot access.

The attrition rate for Auralis expeditions is high. Approximately one in three does not return. The ones that do return rarely bring back more than fragments, and the fragments rarely yield immediate insight. The research progress is real but slow — incremental advances in partial translation, improved understanding of the structural geography, better maps of the anomalous sites.

[[Drest]] funds expeditions beyond the official council budget. The Reach Academy in [[Emberfall]] trains expedition members. The work continues.
