# The Lantern Decrees - Persecution Legal Framework

The [[The Lantern Decrees]] were a series of coordinated legal instruments enacted across [[Auralis]] and [[Terravelle]] starting year 5 of the [[The Persecution Era|Persecution Era]] and continuously amended through year 180. They established the legal framework for systematic persecution and institutionalized paranoia about High Demon presence.

## Core Decrees (Chronological)

**First Decree (Year 5): Mandatory Reporting**
- All citizens required to report suspected immortal beings to magistrates
- Failure to report classified as conspiracy in perpetuity
- No statute of limitations on retroactive prosecution for non-reporting

This decree created pervasive surveillance culture. Communities learned to distrust neighbors. Incentives were created to produce false reports to prove loyalty.

**Second Decree (Year 12): Identity Verification Requirements**
- All citizens required to maintain documentation of lineage and age
- Individuals whose age exceeded reasonable biological limits subject to institutional interrogation
- Family records made public to magistrates for cross-verification

This decree eliminated privacy in genealogy. Families destroyed records to protect future members from scrutiny. Oral tradition replaced documented history in many communities.

**Third Decree (Year 25): Sympathizer Prosecution**
- Providing shelter, food, or aid to suspected immortals classified as treason
- Punishment: confiscation of property, exile, execution (jurisdiction-dependent)
- Suspicion sufficient for prosecution; proof of actual aid not required

This decree criminalized compassion. Helping refugees became high crime. Many communities responded by claiming ignorance rather than admitting protection they'd provided.

**Fourth Decree (Year 60): Institutional Purging**
- Government and guild positions required background verification for familial immortal association
- Individuals with questioned lineage banned from administrative roles
- Entire family lines could be barred from institutional participation based on rumors

This decree destroyed institutional continuity. Experienced administrators were purged for association suspicion. Institutional capacity declined. Magistrates became younger and less competent.

**Fifth Decree (Year 100): Record Destruction Authority**
- Magistrates authorized to destroy birth/death records of suspected immortal lineages
- Institutional erasure of entire family lines from official history
- Survivors of erased families could not legally prove prior existence

This decree created administrative non-persons. Refugee populations grew because families with destroyed records had no legal recourse to reintegration.

## Humanitarian Impact

By Year 150, Decrees had produced:
- Population-scale paranoia about institutional record-keeping
- Hundreds of thousands with destroyed or forged documentation
- Communities split between those who reported suspect individuals and those who harbored them
- Institutional collapse in magistrate effectiveness (too much time spent purging, too little on actual governance)

## Decline and Formal Repeal

The Decrees were never formally repealed. They were abandoned as persecution waned:
- By Year 180, magistrates stopped prosecuting sympathizer cases because sympathizer population exceeded magistrate capacity
- By Year 200, [[Five Survivors Concord]] negotiations implicitly superseded Decree enforcement
- After Concord failed, Decrees weren't revived because populations wouldn't tolerate renewed intensity

Instead, they remained on books as legal language no longer operationally enforced—a pattern that persisted for 200+ years.

## Modern Relevance

The Lantern Decrees explain institutional paranoia about records in current [[Protected Observation Zone|Protected Observation zones]]:
- Resistance to mandatory reporting
- Distrust of population registers
- Preference for oral communication over documented policies
- Reluctance to create institutional paper trails

These aren't oppositional ideology. They're trauma responses inherited from communities whose legal documentation was weaponized.
