The Wolfpoint Scholarium is the formal institutional structure of [[Wolfpoint]] — the body through which Wolfpoint controls access to [[Hypertext]] research, manages the scholars who study there, and maintains Wolfpoint's position as a third pole in Solumoran politics. It is less an academy and more a gatekeeper: an institution whose primary function is deciding who may engage with what [[Wolfpoint]] knows, and on what terms.

## Structure

The Scholarium is administered by the [[The Voss Line|Voss family]], with [[Sera Voss]] currently holding the senior administrative role in addition to her research position. The administrative separation between Voss family governance and the Scholarium's scholarly operations is partly real — research decisions are generally made by researchers — and partly nominal, because Wolfpoint's political independence depends on the Voss family's continued ability to control the institution.

The Scholarium's working scholars are a small community: researchers who have been invited to Wolfpoint, passed the Voss family's evaluation process, and agreed to the terms of access. These terms include limits on what they can publish without Scholarium review, restrictions on teaching Hypertext outside Wolfpoint without explicit authorization, and an understanding that their continued access depends on remaining in good standing with the institution.

Most scholars find these terms reasonable given what access to Hypertext research represents. Some find them constraining. A few have left under difficult circumstances.

## What It Controls

The Scholarium holds the primary archive of [[Hypertext]] documentation — the systematic record of how the Hypertext system works, what it can do that standard [[Sigils|sigil]] casting cannot, and where its limits lie. This archive is not secret; scholars working with authorization have access to it. It is controlled in the sense that the Scholarium decides who gets authorization.

[[Sera Voss]] is writing what she intends to be the first formal documentation of Hypertext at scale — a text that would, if published broadly, allow practitioners with the right tier to learn the system without coming to Wolfpoint. She has not decided what to do with it. The Scholarium does not have a formal position on publication; the decision is effectively Sera's to make, and she has not made it.

## Political Position

Wolfpoint's independence rests on being more valuable as an independent resource than as a territory belonging to either kingdom. [[Terravelle]] could theoretically claim Wolfpoint — it sits in the far north, nominally within Terravelle's geographic sphere — but has never pressed the claim because Wolfpoint under Voss family administration is more useful than Wolfpoint as a contested territory. [[Auralis]] wants access to Hypertext and knows it cannot take it by force without triggering a level of conflict that would cost more than the access is worth.

The Scholarium exploits this balance carefully. It maintains research relationships with scholars from both kingdoms. It charges for access in ways that feel like academic fees rather than tribute. It shares enough of its findings — on ruins, on Flux mechanics, on Hypertext theory — to remain scientifically relevant without giving away the core advantage that keeps it independent.

[[Mira Solv]], the Scholarium's theory specialist, is aware that she holds information the Council of Auralis would pay significantly for. She has not provided it. Her relationship with [[Drest]] is managed with care: she shares enough to maintain the access arrangements that benefit the Scholarium, and not enough to shift the strategic balance she believes Wolfpoint currently holds.
