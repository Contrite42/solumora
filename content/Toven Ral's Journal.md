Morning journal of [[Toven Ral]], [[Hedun]]. Begun in the first year of retirement.

---

The network's language for what we did in Kessvar province was "environmental stabilization with community integration." I wrote that language. I spent an afternoon on it. The phrase needed to be accurate enough to survive a review and vague enough to describe what we actually did, which was occupy a settlement for six weeks while running experiments with a new Force application on their building stock, their food preservation infrastructure, and two volunteers we recruited with promises about what participation would do for their tier assessments.

I don't know what happened to the two volunteers. I left before the assessment results came back. The mission report closed the loop: "volunteer integration outcomes: satisfactory." I wrote that too.

I am writing this down because the phrase has been in my head since I left the network and I want to see if externalizing it changes anything about it. It does not.

---

The question I keep coming back to is whether I knew.

The answer is yes and also no and also that the distinction stopped mattering somewhere in year twelve or thirteen when knowing and not-knowing had collapsed into something that functioned like neither. I understood what the reports were describing. I understood what they were not describing. I wrote language that satisfied both requirements simultaneously and moved to the next assignment.

I was good at the work. This is still true. It remains true regardless of what else is true, which is one of the things I am working out.

---

There was a woman in Valdenmoor district — this was the seventeenth assignment, the boundary survey — who asked me directly what we were doing there. Not the cover version. She had already heard the cover version and was asking what was actually happening.

I told her we were conducting flux density surveys for the Council's expansion planning. This was true. It was also not the complete answer to the question she was asking. She knew this. I knew she knew. She thanked me and went back inside.

I think about her with some regularity. I don't know why that one and not others. Possibly because she asked and I answered in a way that we both understood and she thanked me anyway, which required more grace than I had any right to expect.

---

I sleep differently now. Not badly. Differently. There are mornings I wake up and the specific smell of the Zakros approach road is in the room with me, which is strange because I was never in Zakros — that was Coss's territory, not mine. I think what I'm smelling is some kind of aggregate. Nineteen years of field work compressing into a single smell that the sleeping brain substitutes for the specific one it can't locate.

Or the smell is from somewhere I was and I have forgotten which assignment it corresponds to.

I have checked my mission logs. There are forty-three entries. I have been through all of them twice. I know where I was on every assignment. The smell is not from any of them.

---

The network was not wrong about everything. The [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] work produced real knowledge. The flux mapping that came out of the Zakros perimeter surveys — not Coss's crossings, the perimeter work we did on the Auralis side — gave the scholars information they needed and would not have gotten otherwise. Some of what I helped accomplish has made the world more legible.

I believe this. I also believe the other things. Both continue to be true in the mornings.
