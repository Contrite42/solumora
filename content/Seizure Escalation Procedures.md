# Seizure Escalation Procedures

**Seizure Escalation Procedures** are the stepwise rules customs and enforcement teams use when grimoire shipments move from routine inspection concern to high-risk containment response.

The core objective is to prevent evidence loss while avoiding unnecessary full-corridor shutdown.

## Escalation Tiers

Most offices use a four-tier seizure ladder:

- Tier 1: packet hold and document reconciliation
- Tier 2: batch isolation and expanded provenance check
- Tier 3: chain-linked seizure (associated packets, broker review)
- Tier 4: corridor advisory and inter-office alert posture

The shift from Tier 2 to Tier 3 is where operations become strategic rather than administrative.

## Trigger Conditions

Common escalation triggers include:

- repeated verification anomalies in the same route window
- mismatch between declared tier class and structural spell profile
- authentication signatures linked to known counterfeit clusters
- broker recurrence in prior unresolved seizure events

These triggers are designed to map laundering chains, not just confiscate one shipment.

## Procedure Design Constraint

A seizure protocol that is too narrow misses networks. A protocol that is too broad destabilizes legal trade.

Offices therefore use bounded escalation windows with mandatory review checkpoints to decide whether to expand or de-escalate action.

## Failure Patterns

Escalation procedures fail when:

- public narrative is released before technical adjudication
- chain mapping data is not shared across offices
- local teams remain in prolonged Tier 2 loops without resolution

This produces volume without clarity, increasing both market panic and smuggling adaptation.
