Osvin Brack has assessed guild candidates in [[Valdenmoor]] for twenty-nine years. He is fifty-five, [[Control Tier|T3]], and took the assessment position at twenty-six because the guild office was hiring and he needed income while completing a journeyman certification in cartographic drafting that he never finished. Assessment suited him in ways drafting had not, and the drafting certification is somewhere in a box he has not opened in two decades.

The work is specific: a candidate demonstrates competency in their claimed trade, Osvin records what he observes, and issues a pass or conditional pass or failure. He does this twelve to twenty times a week depending on the season. Autumn is the busiest period, when the year's apprentice cohorts reach certification age. He has assessed approximately nine thousand candidates since he started. He has passed roughly seven thousand of them.

What the ledgers record is competency, failure, and marginal cases — the candidates who pass the standard comfortably but have visible gaps, the ones who fail on a technicality they will correct and return with in six months, the ones who are clearly doing the work of a higher certification than they are presenting for. Over twenty-nine years these observations have accumulated into something that Osvin has not thought to describe as a portrait of the city's labor population, but which functions as one. Which training masters consistently send underprepared candidates. Which trades are producing fewer qualified practitioners than a decade ago. Which candidates return for a second certification in a different trade and why.

He keeps the ledgers because the guild requires records. He re-reads old entries occasionally when a name comes up again. He is sometimes surprised by what he noticed at the time that he did not act on.

He lives alone in a moderate apartment near the guild offices. At quarterly review meetings he raises concerns about certification standards with more frequency than his colleagues prefer.
