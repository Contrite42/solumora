_(Council Member, Auralis — Culmination Faction)_

**Corda Lenn** is the oldest serving member of [[The Council of Auralis]] and the only one who has been there since the beginning. She was present in the room when [[Drest]] assembled the council in the weeks after the old queen died. She has been present in every room since. She is seventy-three years old, she shows no signs of leaving, and the people who have been assuming she will retire soon have been making that assumption for approximately eight years.

## Character

Corda is an archivist by nature. She does not think in arguments or positions — she thinks in records. Everything that happens in a council session is filed somewhere inside her, organized by date and speaker and what was actually said rather than what was officially recorded. She has a memory for the gap between those two things that she has never fully shared with anyone.

She is not warm in the way [[Maren Sollis]] is warm. She is accurate, which is a different thing. She asks questions that have already been answered — apparently — and waits to see whether the new answer matches the old one. It usually does. When it doesn't, she notes the difference and files it without comment.

Her [[Flux]] ability is T3, unremarkable, never relevant. She has not cast a spell in a council session in twenty-three years. She does not need to.

## What She Knows

Corda knows where everything is buried. The phrase is sometimes literal — she has a complete record of every formal [[Ancient Ruins|expedition]] funded by the council, including the undocumented ones, including the ones that didn't return and were not officially acknowledged as lost. She knows what the Culmination Faction said about the desert sites in the early years, before the language was refined into its current careful form. She knows which votes were changed between the informal discussion and the formal record and who asked for those changes.

She also knows things that are not in any record. Things that were said before sessions formally opened. Things that were decided in corridors and ratified in chambers. Things about individual council members that most of those members believe are not known.

She has not used most of this. The question of why — whether it is loyalty, patience, or something closer to the satisfaction of simply knowing — is one she has not answered for anyone, including herself.

## Relationship with Drest

Corda knew [[Drest]] before any of this. Not well — they were not close — but she was present in [[Solhaven]]'s political circles when he was still a man of conviction without a platform. She remembers the specific quality of his belief in the early days: genuine, urgent, and uncomplicated by two decades of managing a council whose members hold the goal for many different reasons.

She has watched that change. She has not said anything about it. [[Maren Sollis]] thinks about what Drest became and grieves it. Corda thinks about it and catalogs it, and the distinction matters in ways neither of them could easily explain.
