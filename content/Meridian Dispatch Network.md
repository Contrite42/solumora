# Meridian Dispatch Network

The Meridian Dispatch Network is the primary official inter-kingdom communication channel, a licensed courier service operating under joint [[Trade Guilds|guild]] authority in both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]]. It provides guaranteed message delivery for diplomatic correspondence, legal documents, and administrative orders with specified deadlines and chain-of-custody documentation.

The network claims official neutrality. This is partially true. It is more accurate to say that both kingdoms trust it equally because it is equally compromised to both. Guild auditors from both sides periodically verify routing and timing, creating the impression of oversight. In practice, the arrangement means that both [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] maintain separate intelligence access to message traffic — neither kingdom is truly ignorant of what the other is communicating, and the Meridian system's value lies not in secrecy but in formal accountability and plausible deniability.

## Structure

The network employs approximately 120 permanent couriers distributed across four major stations (Ashford, Valdenmoor, Solhaven, Halveth) and a dozen smaller relays. Each station maintains storage for pending dispatches, redundant copies of time-sensitive materials, and rotation schedules that ensure continuous operation even during seasonal disruptions.

Message classes are formally organized:
- **Priority Class A**: Diplomatic dispatches, emergency orders, crisis-related communications. 4-day maximum delivery window across the full kingdom boundary.
- **Standard Class B**: Administrative correspondence, legal notices, trade permits. 8-day delivery window.
- **Archive Class C**: Historical records, certified copies, non-urgent administrative materials. 2-week delivery, lower reliability standard.

## How It Actually Works

The formal structure matches the official descriptions. The informal structure runs parallel to it.

Couriers are typically mid-tier [[Control Tier|Tier 1-2]] [[Flux Users]] with legitimate guild certification and a personal interest in remaining employed. They are not spies. They do, however, remember things. A diplomatic dispatch that arrives sealed and leaves sealed may have been read by a courier during the brief window when it was handed from one specialist to another. A permission slip for an expedition into restricted territory becomes useful intelligence when it reaches ears other than its intended recipients.

Both kingdoms tacitly understand this. The Meridian system's security is based on the assumption that message traffic will be partially compromised, and the formal sealing and routing procedures are designed to make it impossible to prove how, when, or by whom. The system works because nobody is officially expected to be able to prove anything.

## Unreliability at Scale

The Meridian system is reliable for specific, high-value communications. It is not reliable for coordinating across multiple weeks or for communicating information that both sides need to act on simultaneously. When the kingdom boundary begins to feel like open conflict, communication-at-scale breaks down because both sides begin to assume that the other side is manipulating the timing of delivery to gain advantage. The system persists in peacetime because delays of a few days do not matter much. In crisis, those delays become politically meaningful.
