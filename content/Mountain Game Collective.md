# Mountain Game Collective

The **Mountain Game Collective** is [[Wolfpoint]]'s hunting and preservation operation, responsible for converting the northern hunting grounds' seasonal game into preserved meat that reaches [[Ashford]] markets in better condition than coastal alternatives. It is not a large-scale commercial enterprise. It is a tightly coordinated operation that turns Wolfpoint's climate disadvantage — constant 45-degree cold with relentless wind — into a preservation advantage no southern settlement can match.

The Collective produces approximately 25-30% of Wolfpoint's physical export revenue and provides the bulk of the settlement's internal meat supply.

## The Hunting Grounds

North of Wolfpoint, beyond the wind-break ridges that protect the settlement's core buildings, lies open upland terrain where game populations remain stable year-round: mountain elk, cold-adapted deer variants, and seasonal migratory fowl that use the northern belt as stopover habitat.

The hunting grounds are not wilderness. They are managed range — the Collective tracks population health, enforces seasonal harvest limits, and rotates hunting pressure across territories to prevent local depletion. This management protocol has been refined over seven generations and is one reason Wolfpoint's game supply remains consistent while other northern settlements experience boom-bust hunting cycles.

Three designated hunting teams operate year-round, each consisting of 4-6 hunters working week-long rotations. Teams camp in the uplands, process fresh kills at field stations, and transport preserved quarters back to Wolfpoint's central processing facility. The rotation system maintains continuous harvest without exhausting any single team.

## Preservation Method

Wolfpoint's preservation advantage is climate-based. Fresh meat processed in 45-degree constant cold and sustained wind dries faster and more uniformly than equivalent work in warmer, more humid coastal zones. The result is preserved game that:
- **Retains better texture** (faster drying prevents the mushiness that occurs when meat sits too long at intermediate temperatures).
- **Stores longer** (lower moisture content extends shelf-life by months).
- **Travels better** (less susceptible to spoilage during maritime transport through variable temperature zones).

The process:
1. **Field processing** — kills are quartered and initial blood/moisture removal happens at field stations within hours of the hunt.
2. **Transport to central facility** — quarters reach Wolfpoint's processing building within 24 hours.
3. **Salt treatment** — meat is heavily salted using imported sea salt (one of Wolfpoint's necessary supply imports from [[Ashford]]).
4. **Cold-wind drying** — quarters hang in open-air drying racks where Wolfpoint's constant wind and cold create optimal drying conditions. Typical drying time: 8-12 days depending on cut thickness and seasonal humidity variation.
5. **Final inspection and packing** — [[Morra Keld]] oversees quality control, removing any pieces that show spoilage signs and packing approved meat into sealed transport crates.

## Operations and Staffing

**[[Dren Vass]]** ([[Control Tier|Tier 3]], 44 years old) coordinates the Collective. He is not a hunter — he is a logistics manager. His job is ensuring continuous flow: rotating hunting teams stay supplied, field stations maintain processing capacity, transport timing prevents bottlenecks, and preservation capacity matches harvest volume without creating waste or shortfall.

Dren tracks population data, adjusts harvest quotas seasonally, and manages the Collective's relationship with [[Upland Herding Network]] (which shares some of the same range territory and requires coordination to prevent resource conflict).

**Morra Keld** ([[Control Tier|Tier 2]], 37 years old) runs preservation operations. She oversees the central drying facility, manages the salt inventory (critical resource with no local source), trains preservation workers in quality standards, and makes final approval decisions on which meat enters export inventory versus internal consumption supply.

Morra's constraint is salt. Wolfpoint has no local salt source. Every pound used in preservation must come south from [[Ashford]] through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]]. This means preservation capacity is limited by trade-route reliability and [[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]]'s ability to negotiate consistent salt deliveries. When packet ships experience delays, preservation operations slow to preserve salt inventory for highest-value cuts.

Eighteen hunters rotate through field operations. Six preservation workers staff the central facility during peak seasons (autumn and early winter, when game populations are highest and harvest volume peaks).

## Market Position

Preserved game from Wolfpoint commands premium pricing in [[Ashford]] and selective [[Terravelle]] inland markets because:
- **Superior preservation quality** (documented through repeat buyer experience over multiple years).
- **Supply consistency** (managed harvests mean Wolfpoint can deliver predictable volumes, unlike settlements dependent on unpredictable hunting success).
- **Limited competition** (few northern settlements have both reliable game access and climate-based preservation advantages).

The Collective does not attempt to compete with bulk meat suppliers in southern markets. Volume is too low, transport costs too high. Instead, it targets buyers who value quality and consistency over lowest-per-unit cost: guild commissaries, expedition supply coordinators, and preservation-focused merchants who resell to buyers requiring long-storage meat for extended travel or remote operations.

Average pricing: 3-5 watts per preserved quarter (roughly 8-12 pounds of finished meat). This is 30-40% higher than bulk-market equivalents, justified by extended shelf-life and superior travel resilience.

## Relationship with Wolfpoint's Internal Economy

The Collective serves dual function:
- **Export production** — generates revenue that supports Wolfpoint's trade independence.
- **Internal food supply** — provides most of the settlement's meat consumption through harvest allocation before export packaging.

Allocation split is roughly 60% export / 40% internal consumption, adjusted seasonally based on hunting success and settlement needs. If harvest volume drops due to weather disruption or population decline, internal consumption takes priority. Export commitments are reduced rather than compromising the settlement's food security.

This dual-function model means the Collective is operationally critical beyond its revenue contribution. Wolfpoint cannot import sufficient meat through [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] to sustain its population. The Collective must succeed at its primary ecological function — turning local game into reliable food supply — before it can function as export industry.

## Supply Dependencies

The Collective's blocking dependency is **salt**. Without consistent salt access, preservation operations collapse and the export model fails.

[[Wolfpoint Trade Registry]] manages salt contracts with [[Ashford]] suppliers, treating salt as strategic-priority import alongside grain and essential tool replacements. Salt contracts are negotiated annually with built-in excess capacity (Registry orders 20% more salt than preservation operations historically require) to buffer against maritime transport disruptions.

When packet delays occur, Morra Keld operates at reduced preservation capacity, focusing on highest-value cuts and deferring lower-priority work until salt inventory recovers. The system has worked for seven generations. It is also fragile: if [[Outer Coast Packet Line]] operations degrade significantly or [[Ashford]] suppliers face their own supply constraints, Wolfpoint's preservation operations — and by extension, its export economy — face immediate risk.

## What Buyers Know

Buyers know Wolfpoint produces superior preserved game. Most buyers do not know the specific population management protocols, field-processing timelines, or climate-dependent drying techniques that create the quality advantage. The Collective does not teach these methods externally.

Some [[Terravelle]] and [[Auralis]] hunters have attempted to replicate the quality results using Wolfpoint-purchased game as reference standard. Attempts generally fail not because the techniques are secret, but because other settlements lack both Wolfpoint's specific climate conditions and the seven-generation refinement of coordinated population management, field processing, and preservation timing.

Technique is partially transferable. Climate advantage is not.
