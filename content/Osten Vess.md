_(Records Analyst, Auralis)_

**Osten Vess** is a T3 records analyst for the [[Council Orders Registry]], age 38, who has spent fourteen years translating ambiguous council resolutions into actionable directives. He is exceptionally good at reading what's not written, tracking what's not tracked, and noticing when documentation patterns change in ways that suggest someone is hiding something. When he discovers that [[The Council of Auralis]] had advance warning of the [[Thel Reach]] rift incursion and chose not to act, he faces the choice every institutional worker dreads: stay quiet and complicit, or document everything and become a problem.

## Background

Osten came to the [[Council Orders Registry]] straight from [[Crestward Flux Institute]], where he'd been training as a sigil optimization researcher until he realized he was better at documentation than innovation. [[Vessa Rolt]] hired him for precisely that reason — the Registry needs people who can turn political language into implementable instructions without losing critical nuance or creating legal exposure.

He's worked under three different registry coordinators. He's watched factional power shift in [[The Council of Auralis]] twice. He's seen resolutions that were clearly impossible get issued anyway, then watched institutions pretzel themselves trying to execute them, then documented the compliance failures when reality won. He stopped expecting the council to make sense years ago. He did not stop expecting basic procedural integrity.

He lives in [[The Terraces]] district of [[Solhaven]], walks to work, and has a reputation as someone who never makes transcription errors and always flags ambiguities before they become disasters. Boring, reliable, invisible. Exactly the kind person who sees things that shouldn't be seen because no one notices he's there.

## The Discovery

On the day after the [[Thel Reach]] incursion, Osten is processing routine [[Emberfall]] expedition reports for archive integration when he notices a gap. [[Emberfall]] submits rift monitoring data quarterly — standard institutional reporting that goes into the archive without anyone reading it unless there's an incident investigation. The most recent submission, dated three weeks before the incursion, is missing seventeen pages from the appendix.

Missing pages are unusual but not unprecedented. Osten submits an administrative query requesting the complete document. He receives, instead, a notation that the appendix was redacted under Council executive discretion and is not available to registry staff.

Osten has worked in the Registry for fourteen years. He has never seen [[Emberfall]] data redacted. Research data doesn't get classified — it's too technical for political sensitivity. He flags the discrepancy for [[Vessa Rolt]]'s review before moving on to other files.

Two days later, [[Vessa Rolt|Vessa]] tells him quietly to drop the query. The redaction came from [[Drest]] personally, through emergency authority channels, and pushing it will create problems for both of them. Osten drops the query. He does not stop thinking about it.

He spends his evening at home going through six years of archived [[Emberfall]] rift monitoring reports, cross-referencing publication dates against known incursion events. The pattern is obvious once he's looking for it: elevated probability flagged 12-20 days before every major incursion in the archive. The missing seventeen pages would have been a probability map. [[Thel Reach]] was in that map. The [[Council of Auralis|Council]] knew.

Osten makes three copies of everything he's found. One goes into his personal storage. One goes into a dead-drop location he's maintained since his second year at the Registry, when he first realized institutional documentation could become a liability. The third he keeps in his work satchel, because he's not sure yet what he's going to do with it but he knows he has to do something.

## What He Knows

Beyond the immediate discovery about [[Thel Reach]], Osten has fourteen years of institutional memory:

- He knows how [[Council Orders Registry]] tracks compliance failures, and he knows which failures get reported versus which get quietly archived
- He knows [[Drest]] almost never invokes executive authority, which means the redaction was important enough to risk procedural exposure
- He knows emergency powers doctrine has been invoked exactly twice in forty years, both times for resource allocation crises, never for military authorization
- He knows [[Vessa Rolt]] is conflicted about something — she's usually direct with him, and "drop it" is not her usual mode
- He knows institutional workers like him are complicit in council decisions they implement, even when they don't make the decisions themselves

Most critically, he knows how to document everything in ways that are legally defensible, procedurally clean, and catastrophically damaging to anyone who tries to bury it.

## Motivation

Osten is not a revolutionary. He believes institutions should function properly, not be dismantled. But he also believes that when institutions fail in ways that kill people, the people responsible should be held accountable through the very documentation systems designed to ensure accountability exists.

The war starting around him is being conducted through emergency powers — which means normal documentation oversight is suspended. If he doesn't preserve evidence now, it will disappear into the post-war reconstruction where everything gets recategorized as "operational necessity." The cover-up won't be a conspiracy, it will be standard archival practice.

He wants to stop the war if possible. Failing that, he wants to ensure that when the war ends, there's an unimpeachable record of how it started and who knew what when. Someone needs to be the person who kept the receipts.

## Skills

- **Documentation Expertise**: Fourteen years creating legally precise records
- **Institutional Knowledge**: Knows [[Auralis]] bureaucratic systems intimately
- **Pattern Recognition**: Can spot anomalies in data flows and reporting structures
- **Legal Procedure**: Understands what evidence is admissible and defensible
- **Archival Systems**: Knows how to preserve information against deliberate erasure
- **Flux Capability**: T3 Chemical discipline (developed for laboratory precision work, not combat)
- **Strategic Discretion**: Knows when to be visible and when to be invisible

## Personal Stakes

Osten's younger brother [[Tess Vess]] is a T1 supply coordinator stationed at [[Halveth]]. If the war escalates, [[Halveth]] becomes a strategic military position instead of a research outpost, and supply coordinators become targets. Tess has two children, ages six and nine, living with their mother in [[Valdenmoor]].

Osten's parents died when he was nineteen. He raised Tess himself while finishing his research training. Tess calls him twice a month for advice about logistics problems. The most recent call was Tess asking whether he should volunteer for military supply service or wait to be conscripted — military service pays better, but volunteers go to active fronts.

Osten told him to wait. Now he's not sure that was the right answer. If there's a way to stop the war, maybe Tess never has to make that choice.

## Current Status

Osten continues working at [[Council Orders Registry]], processing wartime mobilization orders and watching documentation patterns shift into emergency mode. He's made no public move with the evidence he's gathered. [[Vessa Rolt]] has noticed he's been quiet lately; she hasn't asked why.

He's been watching for someone who might be positioned to do something with evidence the Council cannot easily dismiss. A council member would be ideal but unlikely — they're all complicit to some degree. A military officer might work, but military chain of command complicates things. Someone from [[Terravelle]] would have standing but no easy access.

What he needs is someone who was directly harmed, has their own evidence, and has nothing to lose by going public. He's been casually monitoring incident reports from [[Thel Reach]], looking for survivor names and testimony patterns. There's a logistics coordinator named [[Merra Teld]] who's been interviewed six times and keeps providing the same precise timeline with supporting timestamps.

Osten thinks she might be the right person. He just needs to figure out how to reach her without creating a communication trail that implicates [[Vessa Rolt]] or exposes his access to restricted archives.

He's working on it.
