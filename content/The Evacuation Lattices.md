_(Population Logistics, Final-Generation Survival)_

**The Evacuation Lattices** were route matrices used in the final-generation crisis of [[The Ancient Civilization]] to move civilians, skilled workers, and archive staff through unstable regions under strict timing windows. The lattices are reconstructed from marker patterns near transit ruins and from partial route-logic inscriptions.

## Design Principles

Unlike standard roads, lattice corridors appear to have been scheduled systems, not fixed paths. Movement rights changed by cycle conditions:

- Open windows tied to Flux variance thresholds
- Priority classes for medical, technical, and archival personnel
- Redundant branches to absorb corridor failure
- Hard closure rules when instability indices crossed limits

This looks less like migration and more like emergency network operations.

## Institutional Implication

Final-generation authorities seem to have accepted sustained displacement as normal governance. People were managed as moving populations, not settled tax units. That resembles modern crisis logistics in [[Border Security Crisis]] conditions, but with far better coordination tools.

## Present-Day Use

If lattice reconstruction advances, it could support modern evacuation doctrine for rift-prone settlements and reduce dependence on improvised corridor decisions that currently drive casualty spikes.
