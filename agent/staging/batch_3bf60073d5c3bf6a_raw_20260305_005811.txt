The Harbor Guilds manage port facilities and maritime trade. The Inland Guilds coordinate river and road transport. The Provisioners Guilds handle supply chains and bulk goods. The Specialty Guilds focus on particular trades — metalwork, textiles, preserved foods, [[Flux]] materials. Each guild maintains its own membership, sets its own standards, and negotiates its own agreements with other organizations.\n\nThe guilds are not hierarchical in the way that [[Terravelle]]'s administrative structure is hierarchical. They are networked. A merchant might hold membership in multiple guilds depending on their business scope. A settlement might host chapters of several different guild types. The relationships between guilds are negotiated rather than mandated, which creates flexibility but also creates opportunities for influence and manipulation.\n\nGuild membership is not automatic. A merchant must demonstrate competence, pay dues, and be sponsored by existing members. This creates a barrier to entry that maintains quality but also maintains exclusivity. The guilds protect their members' interests, but they also control who gets to have interests worth protecting.\n\n## Economic Power\n\nThe merchant guilds collectively control most of [[Auralis]]'s internal trade. They set shipping rates, determine priority for scarce goods, negotiate bulk purchase agreements, and maintain the [[Watts]] ledger relationships that enable large-scale commerce. A guild's decision to support or boycott a particular settlement can determine that settlement's economic viability.\n\nThis power is exercised subtly. The guilds do not issue proclamations or make dramatic gestures. They adjust rates. They modify delivery schedules. They recommend certain suppliers over others. A settlement that finds itself gradually isolated from guild networks may not understand why its prosperity is declining until the process is well advanced.\n\nThe [[Council of Auralis]] understands this dynamic and maintains formal relationships with the major guilds. Council members often hold honorary guild positions. Guild leaders are consulted on economic policy. The relationship is cooperative rather than adversarial, but it is also a relationship between equals rather than between authority and subject.\n\n## Guild Politics and Internal Dynamics\n\nWithin each guild, advancement follows a combination of competence, seniority, and political skill. Guild officers are elected by membership vote, but the elections are influenced by the same factors that influence any political process — personal relationships, economic leverage, and the ability to build coalitions.\n\nThis creates opportunities for patient, skilled operators to accumulate influence over time. A person who consistently provides value to other guild members, who builds relationships across multiple organizations, and who positions themselves as indispensable to important decisions can gradually become central to the guild's operation without ever holding its highest formal office.\n\n[[Selvane]] has exploited this dynamic masterfully in [[Korthaven]]. Rather than seeking guild leadership directly, he has made himself the person other leaders consult when they need expertise, connections, or problem-solving capability. This gives him influence over guild decisions without the visibility and accountability that come with formal authority.\n\n## People in Motion\n\n**Gareth Ironhold** — Master of the Southern Metalworkers Guild, based in [[Solhaven]], currently negotiating a major contract to supply [[Sigils|sigil]]-inscribed tools to [[Council of Auralis|Council]]-sponsored expeditions.
