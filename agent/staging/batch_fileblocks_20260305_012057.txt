The precision demands of [[Flux Expenditure]] follow predictable patterns based on [[Discipline]] baseline costs, [[Shape]] multipliers, and [[Spell Variables|variable]] specifications. This reference provides approximate tier requirements for common combinations.

## Discipline Base Costs

**Physical Disciplines (Low Baseline)**
- **Force**: Baseline +0 tiers. The cheapest discipline to channel.
- **Heat**: Baseline +0.5 tiers. Nearly as efficient as Force.

**Physical-Complex Disciplines (Moderate Baseline)**
- **Electric**: Baseline +1 tier. Requires precise pathway control.
- **Chemical**: Baseline +1 tier. Molecular interaction demands refinement.
- **Sound**: Baseline +1 tier. Frequency control adds complexity.
- **Light**: Baseline +1 tier. Wavelength precision required.

**Structural Disciplines (Elevated Baseline)**
- **Binding**: Baseline +1.5 tiers. Relational interactions cost more than physical.

**Consciousness Disciplines (High Baseline)**
- **Mind**: Baseline +2 tiers. Consciousness targeting requires extreme precision.
- **Soul**: Baseline +2.5 tiers. The most expensive discipline baseline.

## Shape Multipliers

**Simple Shapes**
- **Triangle** (3 outer points): ×1.0 multiplier
- **Square** (4 outer points): ×1.2 multiplier

**Complex Shapes**
- **Pentagon** (5 outer points): ×1.5 multiplier
- **Hexagon** (6 outer points): ×1.8 multiplier
- **Circle** (6 outer + center): ×2.0 multiplier

## Variable Specification Costs

**[[Reach]] Specifications**
- Self: +0 tiers
- Touch: +0.5 tiers
- Short: +0.5 tiers
- Medium: +1 tier
- Long: +1.5 tiers
- Line-of-Sight: +2 tiers
- Linked: +2.5 tiers

**[[Persistence]] Specifications**
- Immediate: +0 tiers
- Timed Short: +0.5 tiers
- Timed Medium: +1 tier
- Timed Long: +1.5 tiers
- Sustained: +1 tier (ongoing)
- Permanent: +3 tiers (during cast only)

**[[Geometry Pattern]] Specifications**
- Point: +0 tiers
- Beam: +0.5 tiers
- Cone: +1 tier
- Ring: +1 tier
- Sphere: +1.5 tiers
- Cylinder: +1.5 tiers
- Field: +2 tiers

**[[Target Spec]] Specifications**
- Written: +0 tiers
- Self: +0 tiers
- Individual: +0.5 tiers
- Object: +0.5 tiers
- Group: +1 tier
- Filter: +2 tiers

**[[Output Mode]] Cross-Discipline Penalties**
- Natural pairing (Heat→Thermal, Force→Kinetic): +0 tiers
- Compatible pairing (Electric→Kinetic, Chemical→Constraint): +0.5 tiers
- Incompatible pairing (Mind→Kinetic, Soul→Constraint): +1.5 tiers

## Common Spell Examples

**Basic Working Spells (T1-T2 Range)**

*Triangle Force, all defaults*
- Base: Force (0) × Triangle (1.0) = T0
- Common in basic [[Grimoires|grimoires]]

*Square Heat with Touch reach*
- Base: Heat (0.5) × Square (1.2) + Touch (0.5) = T1.1
- Standard warming sigil

*Triangle Binding with Medium reach, Timed Medium persistence*
- Base: Binding (1.5) × Triangle (1.0) + Medium (1) + Timed Medium (1) = T3.5
- Typical restraint spell

**Advanced Applications (T4-T6 Range)**

*Pentagon Mind with Short reach, Individual target*
- Base: Mind (2) × Pentagon (1.5) + Short (0.5) + Individual (0.5) = T4
- Basic thought reading

*Hexagon Chemical with Long reach, Group target, Timed Long persistence*
- Base: Chemical (1) × Hexagon (1.8) + Long (1.5) + Group (1) + Timed Long (1.5) = T6.8
- Mass chemical effect

*Circle Soul with Line-of-Sight reach, Filter target*
- Base: Soul (2.5) × Circle (2.0) + Line-of-Sight (2) + Filter (2) = T9
- Advanced soul manipulation

## Efficiency Factors

**Experience Reduction**
Practitioners with extensive experience in specific spell combinations can reduce effective costs by 0.5-1 tier through refined channeling technique.

**Prepared Sigil Reduction**
Pre-inscribed [[Sigils]] reduce activation cost by approximately 1 tier compared to improvised casting, as the geometric framework absorbs part of the precision demand.

**Environmental Factors**
- High ambient [[Flux]] areas: -0.5 tier reduction
- Flux-dead zones: +1 tier penalty
- Dimensional boundary proximity: +0.5 tier penalty
- Prepared casting circles: -0.5 tier reduction

## Practical Applications

**[[Flux Users|Formulist]] Work Planning**
A T3 practitioner can safely execute spells with total costs up to T3. Working at T2.5 or below allows sustained casting without exhaustion.

**[[Control Tier|Tier]] Assessment Implications**
Children assessed at specific tiers can expect to handle spell combinations within these cost ranges:
- T1: Basic utility spells only
- T2: Standard working magic
- T3: Intermediate applications
- T4+: Advanced and specialized casting

**Combat Considerations**
High-stress casting typically adds +0.5 tier penalty due to reduced precision under pressure. Combat-viable spells should be at least 1 tier below practitioner maximum.

===WRITE: content/Places/Life in Halveth.md===
[[Halveth]] transforms twice each year. In expedition season, when [[Auralis]]-funded crossings and independent groups converge on the settlement, the population swells from eighty permanent residents to several hundred temporary ones. The village becomes a staging ground — busy, profitable, and tense with the particular energy of people preparing to risk their lives crossing [[Equatorial Desert|Desert Zakros]]. When expedition season ends, the temporary population disperses north, and Halveth contracts back into something quieter, more sustainable, and more honest about what it actually is: a place where people live because they have made their peace with proximity to danger.

## The Rhythm of Seasons

**Expedition season** runs roughly from late spring through early fall, when desert conditions are most survivable and [[Auralis]] funding flows most freely toward [[Ancient Ruins|ruins research]]. The village's three inns fill completely. Temporary camps spring up on the eastern flat. The supply shops that normally serve the permanent population expand their stock to include specialized desert gear — thermal [[Sigils|sigil]] sets, navigation equipment, preserved foods rated for extreme temperature variance. Prices rise accordingly. Nobody argues with the markup who understands what the desert does to unprepared travelers.

During this period, Halveth operates at a pace that would be unsustainable year-round. The permanent residents work longer hours, take on temporary help, and tolerate the disruption because expedition season funds the rest of the year. The temporary population brings money, but it also brings noise, competition for resources, and the constant undercurrent of people who are treating the village as a waypoint rather than a place where people actually live.

**Off-season** begins when the last expedition departs and the weather turns. The temporary camps are struck. The expanded inventory is sold off or stored. The village contracts back to its permanent population, and the rhythm shifts to something more sustainable — maintenance work, preparation for the next season, the quieter business of a community that knows it will spend the winter together.

This is when Halveth's actual character becomes visible. The permanent residents are people who have chosen to live here, not people who are passing through. They have developed the particular competencies and social structures that make life sustainable in a place where the nearest major settlement is weeks away and [[Flux Demons]] occasionally wander up from the desert rifts.

## The Permanent Population

The core residents fall into several categories, most of them connected to the desert crossing trade in some way.

**Former expedition members** make up a significant portion of the permanent population. These are people who crossed the desert once, understood what they had survived, and decided that supplying future crossings was more sustainable than attempting another one. They bring expertise that cannot be sourced elsewhere — practical knowledge of what works in the desert, what fails, and what kills. Their advice is part of what Halveth sells, and it commands a premium because it is based on direct experience rather than theory.

**Guides and suppliers** form the village's economic backbone. The guides are people who have made multiple crossings and survived them, which is a rare enough combination to make their services valuable. The suppliers stock goods that are difficult to source anywhere else in the south — replacement grimoire covers treated against wind abrasion, water storage systems designed for extreme temperature variance, the specialized equipment that makes desert crossing possible rather than merely suicidal.

**Service providers** — innkeepers, cooks, equipment maintenance — serve both the permanent population and the seasonal influx. These are often people who came to Halveth for other reasons and stayed because they found a niche that worked. The village's isolation means that anyone who can provide a necessary service has less competition than they would in a larger settlement.

**Families** who have been in the area long enough that leaving seems like more effort than staying. These are typically second or third-generation residents whose parents or grandparents came to Halveth during an earlier period of desert crossing activity and established roots. They provide continuity and institutional memory — knowledge of how the village has handled previous crises, where resources can be found, which approaches to common problems have worked and which have not.

## [[Wren]]'s Integration

[[Wren]] has been in Halveth for thirty years, which makes her neither the newest nor the oldest resident, but certainly the most influential. Her role in the community is not formal — there is no mayor, no council, no official authority structure — but it is comprehensive.

Her [[Binding]] discipline, refined through decades of practice on the road approaches to the village, means that she is aware of everyone who comes and goes. This is not surveillance in the intrusive sense — she does not monitor private conversations or track personal business — but she knows when someone is on the road, roughly who they are, and whether they are approaching with good or ill intent. This early warning system has proven valuable enough times that the permanent residents have come to rely on it.

More directly, Wren's control over the road conditions means that she can make life easier or harder for people based on her assessment of them. Expeditions that take the time to speak with her honestly, that treat the village as something other than a supply depot to be used, find the road south notably smoother than it has any right to be. Groups that are dismissive, demanding, or careless find the road more difficult — cart wheels that develop problems, supply seals that won't hold, guides who become inexplicably reluctant to honor contracts.

This is not arbitrary. Wren's judgments are based on thirty years of watching expeditions depart and observing which approaches to the desert tend to result in people coming back alive. She favors caution, preparation, and respect for the environment over confidence, haste, and the assumption that superior [[Flux]] ability will compensate for poor planning.

## Social Structure

Halveth operates without formal government, but it is not anarchic. The permanent residents have developed informal mechanisms for handling disputes, allocating resources, and making collective decisions. These mechanisms work because the population is small enough that everyone knows everyone else, and because the village's isolation means that social reputation has direct practical consequences.

**Consensus decision-making** handles issues that affect the entire community — how to respond to increased [[Rift Incursions|demon activity]], whether to expand the village's infrastructure, how to manage conflicts between permanent residents and seasonal visitors. These discussions happen in the common room of the largest inn, and they continue until a solution emerges that everyone can live with. This process is slower than formal authority structures, but it produces decisions that have genuine community support.

**Reputation-based enforcement** handles individual disputes and violations of community standards. A person who cheats in business dealings, who fails to contribute to collective work, or who creates unnecessary conflict finds themselves gradually excluded from the social and economic networks that make life in Halveth sustainable. This is not formal punishment — no one is exiled or fined — but it is effective because the village is too small to function without cooperation.

**Resource sharing** during crises is expected rather than negotiated. When demon incursions threaten the village, when severe weather damages buildings, or when someone is injured or ill, the permanent residents respond collectively. This is not altruism — it is practical necessity in a place where individual survival depends on community resilience.

## The Desert's Presence

Living in Halveth means living with constant awareness of the desert's proximity. The [[Equatorial Desert|desert]] is not just a geographic feature — it is an active presence that shapes daily life in ways that residents of more settled areas do not experience.

**Weather patterns** are more extreme than in the northern settlements. Temperature swings are wider, storms are more violent, and the wind carries fine particulate that damages exposed [[Sigils]] over time. Buildings in Halveth are constructed to withstand these conditions, with reinforced shutters, sealed storage areas, and structural sigils that are renewed more frequently than would be necessary elsewhere.

**Demon incursions** are a regular occurrence rather than an emergency. Lower-order [[Flux Demons]] wander north from the rift zones with enough frequency that the permanent residents have developed standard procedures for handling them. Most demons that reach the village are disoriented, non-aggressive, and can be redirected back toward the desert without violence. The ones that cannot be redirected are dealt with by the residents who have the appropriate [[Control Tier|tier]] and experience.

**Supply considerations** are more complex than in connected settlements. Everything that cannot be produced locally must be brought in from the north, and the cost of transport is reflected in prices. The permanent residents have learned to be self-sufficient in areas where other communities rely on trade — food preservation, equipment maintenance, basic medical care. This self-sufficiency is not complete, but it is sufficient to handle most situations that arise between supply runs.

## What Halveth Produces

The village's primary export is expertise. The knowledge that its permanent residents have accumulated about desert crossing, demon behavior, and survival in extreme conditions cannot be sourced elsewhere. Expedition leaders who spend time in Halveth before attempting the crossing have significantly better survival rates than those who do not, and this reputation draws continued business despite the village's remote location.

**Practical knowledge** about desert conditions, route selection, equipment requirements, and emergency procedures is transmitted through conversation rather than formal instruction. The former expedition members who live in Halveth share their experience freely with anyone who asks genuine questions and listens to the answers. This knowledge transfer is informal, but it is systematic — the same critical information is conveyed to every group that takes the time to engage with it.

**Specialized equipment** modified for desert use is produced by the village's craftspeople. Standard gear does not perform reliably under desert conditions, and the modifications required are not obvious to people who have not experienced those conditions directly. Halveth's suppliers have learned through trial and error what works and what fails, and their products reflect that accumulated knowledge.

**Route intelligence** about current desert conditions, recent demon activity, and seasonal variations is maintained by the guides who make regular trips to the desert's edge. This information is valuable because it is current — conditions change, and what was true last season may not be true this season. Expeditions that depart with current intelligence have better outcomes than those that rely on outdated information.

## The Children

Children born in Halveth grow up with a different relationship to risk and self-sufficiency than children in more settled areas. They learn early that survival requires competence, that help may not be available when needed, and that the community's well-being depends on everyone contributing according to their ability.

**[[Control Tier|Tier]] assessment** happens at the usual age, but the implications are different than in the cities. A high-tier child in Halveth is not automatically destined for advancement — the village needs people with various skills, and [[Flux]] ability is only one of them. A low-tier child is not disadvantaged in the same way they would be in [[Solhaven]] — practical competence and community contribution matter more than tier ranking.

**Education** is practical and community-based. Children learn by participating in the work that keeps the village functioning — equipment maintenance, food preservation, basic defense procedures. They learn about the desert by listening to the stories and advice of people who have crossed it. They learn about demons by observing how the adults handle incursions.

**Coming of age** often involves a choice about whether to stay in Halveth or seek opportunities elsewhere. Some children leave for larger settlements when they reach adulthood, seeking education or careers that the village cannot provide. Others stay, finding purpose in the community that raised them. Both choices are respected, and children who leave are welcome to return.
