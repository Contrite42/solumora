It is large enough to matter — a population of roughly twelve thousand, a harbor that can accommodate deep-water vessels, warehouses that store goods bound for the capital — but small enough that individual influence can reshape its entire character over time. The river provides access to the inland settlements of northern [[Auralis]], while the harbor connects to the broader coastal trade network that runs the length of the kingdom's western shore. Korthaven processes timber from the northern forests, grain from the river valley settlements, and manufactured goods from [[Solhaven]] — a position that has made it prosperous without making it politically significant.\n\nThe harbor district sprawls along the waterfront in the practical way of working ports. Warehouses, docks, shipyards, and the businesses that serve them occupy the flat ground near the water. The residential areas climb the low hills inland, with the wealthier merchant families building higher up where the air is cleaner and the view extends over the harbor to the sea beyond.\n\n## The Merchant Guild Structure\n\nKorthaven's economy runs through its [[Merchant Guilds]] — the Harbor Guild that manages dock space and shipping schedules, the Inland Guild that coordinates river trade, and the Provisioners Guild that handles the supply chains feeding both. These are not ceremonial organizations. They set prices, resolve disputes, determine which ships get priority berthing, and collectively control more of the settlement's daily function than any formal government structure.\n\nThe guilds operate through a council system where each organization sends representatives to negotiate shared decisions. In theory, this creates balanced representation. In practice, it creates a structure where a person with sufficient patience and skill can gradually accumulate influence across multiple guilds simultaneously — which is exactly what [[Selvane]] has been doing since he arrived.\n\n## Selvane's Integration\n\nWhen [[Selvane]] first appeared in Korthaven, he presented as a merchant from the southern settlements with capital to invest and connections to develop. This was not entirely false — he had spent his first decades in [[Solumora]] learning how trade worked, and he had accumulated both resources and relationships during that time. What was false was the implication that he was human, temporary, or operating with anything resembling normal commercial motivations.\n\nHe joined the Provisioners Guild first, as a supplier of specialized goods — rare materials, high-quality preserved foods, items that were difficult to source elsewhere but that he could somehow acquire reliably. His [[High Demons|Flux]] ability, focused on detection and analysis, allowed him to identify opportunities and assess quality in ways that appeared to be exceptional business instinct. Within five years, he was a guild officer. Within ten, he was indispensable.\n\nThe pattern repeated with the other guilds. He provided services they needed. He solved problems they could not solve themselves. He never pushed for formal authority, but he gradually became the person other guild leaders consulted when difficult decisions needed to be made. By the time anyone might have questioned how one merchant had become so central to the settlement's operation, questioning him would have meant questioning the foundation of their own prosperity.\n\n## People in Motion\n\n**Mira Kortheld** — Harbor Guild leader, mid-fifties, has worked with Selvane for twenty years without ever quite understanding how he manages to anticipate market shifts so accurately. Currently negotiating a major expansion of the harbor facilities that Selvane has quietly suggested would position Korthaven as the primary northern port for [[Council of Auralis|Council]]-sponsored expeditions.\n\n**Jorik Sandmere** — Young merchant, recently returned from training in [[Solhaven]], increasingly suspicious of how much influence Selvane wields and how little anyone knows about his background. The harbor expansion that Mira Kortheld is negotiating would position the settlement as the primary staging point for northern [[Auralis]] expeditions to the [[Equatorial Desert]]. This is not coincidental. Selvane has been working toward this outcome for years, understanding that control over expedition logistics would give him access to information about [[Ancient Ruins|ruins]] discoveries and [[Rift Incursions|rift]] activity that the [[Council of Auralis]] does not share publicly.\n\nThe expansion would also create opportunities for him to influence which expeditions receive support and which face unexpected difficulties — a capability that aligns with his broader agenda of positioning himself to benefit from whatever the Council's ruins research ultimately discovers.\n\nJorik Sandmere's growing suspicion represents the first serious threat to Selvane's position in decades. Trade disputes that might have escalated are resolved quietly. Supply shortages that might have created hardship are somehow avoided. The settlement's reputation for reliable business practices has attracted merchants from across northern [[Auralis]], creating a positive feedback loop that has made Selvane's position seem like the natural result of good management rather than careful manipulation.\n\nThe residents are not unhappy. Most of them are better off than they would be without Selvane's influence. But they have also become dependent on systems they do not fully understand, managed by a person whose motivations they have never questioned. This dependency is Selvane's primary asset and his primary vulnerability — it gives him enormous leverage, but it also means that any serious disruption to his position would destabilize the entire settlement.\n\n*See also: [[Selvane]], [[High Demons]], [[Auralis]], [[Merchant Guilds]], [[Coastal Trade]], [[Guild Politics]], [[Trade Routes]], [[Solhaven]], [[Council of Auralis]], [[Desert Crossing]], [[Ancient Ruins]]*"

      "content": "The merchant guilds of [[Auralis]] are the practical backbone of the kingdom's economy — the organizations that move goods between settlements, negotiate trade agreements, and maintain the commercial relationships that keep the southern kingdom fed and supplied. They operate with more day-to-day authority than most formal government structures, and they have been quietly infiltrated by [[High Demons]] who understand that economic influence translates directly into political power.\n\n## Structure and Function\n\nMerchant guilds organize around specialization and geography. The Harbor Guilds manage port facilities and maritime trade. The Inland Guilds coordinate river and road transport. The Provisioners Guilds handle supply chains and bulk goods. The Specialty Guilds focus on particular trades — metalwork, textiles, preserved foods, [[Flux]] materials. Each guild maintains its own membership, sets its own standards, and negotiates its own agreements with other organizations.\n\nThe guilds are not hierarchical in the way that [[Terravelle]]'s administrative structure is hierarchical. They are networked. A merchant might hold membership in multiple guilds depending on their business scope. A settlement might host chapters of several different guild types. The relationships between guilds are negotiated rather than mandated, which creates flexibility but also creates opportunities for influence and manipulation.\n\nGuild membership is not automatic. A merchant must demonstrate competence, pay dues, and be sponsored by existing members. This creates a barrier to entry that maintains quality but also maintains exclusivity. The guilds protect their members' interests, but they also control who gets to have interests worth protecting.\n\n## Economic Power\n\nThe merchant guilds collectively control most of [[Auralis]]'s internal trade. They set shipping rates, determine priority for scarce goods, negotiate bulk purchase agreements, and maintain the [[Watts]] ledger relationships that enable large-scale commerce. A guild's decision to support or boycott a particular settlement can determine that settlement's economic viability.\n\nThis power is exercised subtly. The guilds do not issue proclamations or make dramatic gestures. They adjust rates. They modify delivery schedules. They recommend certain suppliers over others. A settlement that finds itself gradually isolated from guild networks may not understand why its prosperity is declining until the process is well advanced.\n\nThe [[Council of Auralis]] understands this dynamic and maintains formal relationships with the major guilds. Council members often hold honorary guild positions. Guild leaders are consulted on economic policy. The relationship is cooperative rather than adversarial, but it is also a relationship between equals rather than between authority and subject.\n\n## Guild
