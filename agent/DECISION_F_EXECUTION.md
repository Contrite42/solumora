# DECISION F — Execution Plan

## Creator Selections (2026-03-07)

**Narrative Approach:** Frieren-style organic storytelling
- Right people at right places at right time
- No prophecy/chosen-one mechanics
- Quiet competence over grand heroics
- History woven naturally into present through flashbacks and discoveries

---

## Selected Components

### War Trigger: **OPTION A + C (COMBINED)**

**Cascading Crisis:**
1. Rift incursion at Terravelle border causes 40-60 casualties
2. Evidence surfaces that Auralis Council knew rift patterns, delayed warning for research access
3. Council attempts to exploit crisis, calls vote to authorize military response
4. Cassia refuses royal seal — first time she's publicly opposed Council
5. Council invokes emergency powers doctrine, proceeds without her seal
6. Constitutional crisis and war begin simultaneously

**Result:** 
- Dual legitimacy crisis in Auralis (who actually governs?)
- Moral justification for Terravelle (defensive response to deaths)
- Rift mechanics become plot-critical for resolution
- Cassia becomes fugitive or prisoner depending on Council's speed

### Protagonist: **NEW CHARACTERS**

Build fresh ensemble naturally positioned for story needs:
- Mix of tiers, backgrounds, kingdoms
- Discover each other organically through events rather than quest assembly
- Characters appear when story requires them (Frieren model)

**Core Character Types Needed:**
1. Desert-edge settlement resident (witnesses rift incursion firsthand)
2. Auralis institutional worker (sees constitutional crisis from inside)
3. Terravelle courier/trader (cross-kingdom connections)
4. Ruins site worker (understands ancient infrastructure hands-on)
5. Medical/logistics specialist (managing casualty response, sees war's reality)
6. Someone who's been unknowingly interacting with a High Demon

### Resolution: **LAYERED SEQUENCE (B + C + D + E)**

Not "pick one solution" but cascading phases where each resolution reveals the need for the next:

**Phase 1: Practical Collapse (C)**
- First major engagement across desert
- Neither kingdom can sustain logistics (supply lines, casualties, coordination)
- Military failure forces operational pause
- Buys time but doesn't resolve underlying crisis
- Characters witness institutional incompetence firsthand

**Phase 2: Cassia's Legitimacy Play (E)**
- Cassia surfaces with documentation of Council manipulation
- Makes public declaration to both kingdoms' populations
- Splits Auralis internally (some support her, some support Council)
- Eddan recognizes "legitimate" Auralis queen
- Council still controls military apparatus
- Creates negotiation window but war threat remains

**Phase 3: High Demon Forced Revelation (B)**
- War's rift cascade reaches dimensional crisis threshold
- High Demons must intervene publicly or risk catastrophic breach
- Five immortals revealed to human society for first time in 400 years
- Existential context shift: kingdoms' conflict suddenly trivial against bigger reality
- Cooperation becomes survival necessity, not political choice
- Historical Persecution Era context becomes immediately relevant

**Phase 4: Watts System Understanding (D)**
- In aftermath, characters realize ancient Watts ledger can be modified
- Discovery that "economic natural law" is actually constructed infrastructure
- Not deployed as weapon, but understanding creates foundation for lasting peace
- Economic interdependence becomes structural, not just policy
- Connects to Ancient Civilization's original purpose

## Prior Generations: **ALL (A + B + C + D)**

Develop all four historical periods in interwoven fashion as Frieren does with flashbacks:

### A: Persecution Era (~400-200 years ago)
**12-15 historical notes**
- Two-century hunt after High Demon public incident
- Community-level persecution mechanics (how villages turned on neighbors)
- How Sorath/Fennick survived centuries of hiding
- Survivor lineage memories that become plot-relevant in present
- Sets up emotional/political stakes for Phase 3 revelation
- Answer: "What happens when society learns about High Demons?"

### B: First Contact Era (~200 years ago)
**10-12 historical notes**
- When desert crossing became viable through thermal sigil development
- Early diplomatic failures and successes
- Cultural clash patterns still echoing in present day
- Precedents for peace-making that present characters can draw on
- Shows kingdoms CAN learn to coexist given right conditions
- Economic integration history that informs Phase 4

### C: Ancient Civilization's Final Generation
**15-18 historical notes**
- Last 20-50 years before civilization disappeared
- Personal stories: specific people with names, goals, failures
- What they were actually trying to build (did they succeed or fail?)
- Watts system original purpose and architecture
- Ruins become places where real people lived and worked
- Essential for understanding what Phase 4 discovery means

### D: Ashford Blockade Generation (~45 years ago)
**8-10 historical notes**
- Living memory for current leaders (Drest, Eddan)
- Formative experiences that shaped their current thinking
- Shows how near-war barely avoided shapes next generation's choices
- Parents of current characters and their decisions during crisis
- Economic disruption patterns that apply to current war
- Demonstrates institutional memory and learning (or failure to learn)

**Historical Integration Method:**
- Flashbacks triggered by present discoveries
- Documents found during main arc
- Older characters' memories
- Ruins that connect to ancient civilization stories
- Family histories that echo Persecution Era
- Institutional records showing pattern repetition
