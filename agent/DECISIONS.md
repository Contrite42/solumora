#### **Component 1: War Trigger Mechanism**

**Option A — Rift Catastrophe Revelation**
- Major incursion at Terravelle border; 40-60 casualties in settled area
- Evidence surfaces that Auralis Council knew rift patterns and delayed warning for research access
- Eddan has domestic pressure + moral justification for military response
- **Tradeoffs:** Clear villain (Council), preserves Cassia as sympathetic, makes High Demons relevant to resolution
- **Development cost:** 6-8 incident notes, new rift mechanics detail, casualty documentation

**Option B — Ancient Site Military Fortification**
- Auralis sends 200-soldier expedition with fortification supplies to major anomalous site
- Framed as "research protection" but infrastructure clearly military staging post
- Terravelle reads as territorial annexation; 45-day diplomatic standoff fails
- **Tradeoffs:** Echoes Ruins Claim Incident precedent, avoids casualties-as-trigger, slower escalation
- **Development cost:** 5-7 fortification/diplomatic notes, new ancient site detail, military logistics

**Option C — Cassia's Constitutional Break**
- Council votes to authorize military action; Cassia refuses royal seal
- Council proceeds with emergency powers doctrine; constitutional crisis becomes military crisis
- Cassia flees or is confined; legitimacy question splits Auralis internally
- **Tradeoffs:** Makes Cassia active protagonist early, creates Auralis civil dimension, highest political complexity
- **Development cost:** 8-10 constitutional/political notes, Auralis internal faction warfare, refugee mechanics

**Option D — Desert Crossing Blockade**
- Auralis deploys military forces to control all viable desert crossing points
- Cuts Terravelle's southern access completely; economic siege without direct combat
- Terravelle must break blockade militarily or accept economic collapse
- **Tradeoffs:** Slow burn, economic warfare focus, follows Ashford Blockade precedent, avoids casualties initially
- **Development cost:** 4-6 blockade logistics notes, economic impact cascade documentation

#### **Component 4: Resolution Mechanism**

**Option A — Ancient Mechanism Completion**
- Party reaches ruins core; discovers civilization's "completion" was choosing transcendence/departure
- Mechanism can be activated but requires permanent commitment (death? dimensional departure?)
- Someone from party must make choice: trigger mechanism to force peace, or let kingdoms decide own fate
- **Tradeoffs:** Echoes ancient civilization mystery, requires ultimate sacrifice, philosophically complex
- **Development cost:** 10-12 ruins core notes, mechanism operation detail, metaphysical Flux theory

**Option B — High Demon Forced Revelation**
- War threatens to open uncontrolled rift cascade; High Demons must intervene publicly or risk dimensional collapse
- Revelation of five immortals creates new existential context; kingdoms forced to recognize bigger picture
- Post-war: High Demons known, new threat requires cooperation, magical understanding revolutionized
- **Tradeoffs:** Paradigm shift for entire world, High Demons become public, massive downstream implications
- **Development cost:** 8-10 revelation/response notes, societal reaction framework, new political equilibrium

**Option C — Practical Collapse + Treaty**
- Both kingdoms mobilize; first major engagement reveals neither can sustain desert logistics militarily
- Casualties catastrophic, supply lines break, institutional capacity collapses faster than modeled
- Cassia + Eddan broker emergency ceasefire; party provides neutral ground/secure communication
- **Tradeoffs:** Realistic, preserves existing world structure, avoids magical solutions, leaves tensions unresolved
- **Development cost:** 6-8 military collapse notes, treaty negotiation detail, post-war reconstruction framework

**Option D — Watts System Override**
- Party discovers ancient Watts ledger can be modified; someone triggers economic basis change
- Both kingdoms' war funding structures collapse simultaneously; forced negotiation from mutual weakness
- Post-war: Understanding that economic reality is constructed, not natural - philosophical revolution
- **Tradeoffs:** Economic rather than military resolution, requires completely new game-theory, unprecedented
- **Development cost:** 12-15 Watts system notes, economic theory framework, implementation mechanics

**Option E — Cassia's Legitimacy Play**
- Party secures Cassia with credible evidence of Council manipulation + protection force
- She makes public declaration with both kingdoms' populations as witnesses; splits Auralis internally
- Council cannot prosecute war while defending legitimacy at home; Eddan accepts ceasefire with "real" Auralis queen
- **Tradeoffs:** Makes Cassia central hero, requires her character development, creates Auralis civil aftermath
- **Development cost:** 7-9 legitimacy/propaganda notes, Auralis internal conflict, public opinion mechanics
